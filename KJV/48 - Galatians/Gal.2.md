<big><big>[[Gal.2.1 The Council at Jerusalem|⥃]]</big></big>***The Council at Jerusalem***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Acts.15|Acts 15:5–21]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then fourteen<small><small>[^2]</small></small> years after I went<small><small>[^3]</small></small> up again to Jerusalem with Barnabas<small><small>[^4]</small></small>, and took Titus<small><small>[^5]</small></small> with \[me\] also. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I went up by<small><small>[^6]</small></small> revelation, and communicated<small><small>[^7]</small></small> unto them that gospel which I preach among the Gentiles, but privately<small><small>[^8]</small></small> to them which<small><small>[^9]</small></small> were of reputation, lest by any means I should<small><small>[^10]</small></small> run, or had run, in vain. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But neither Titus, who was with me, being a Greek, was compelled to be circumcised:<small><small>[^11]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And that because<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of false brethren unawares<small><small>[^13]</small></small> brought in, who came in privily to spy out our liberty<small><small>[^14]</small></small> which we have in Christ Jesus, that they might bring<small><small>[^15]</small></small> us into bondage: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To whom we<small><small>[^16]</small></small> gave place by subjection, no, not for an hour; that<small><small>[^17]</small></small> the truth of the gospel might continue with you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But of these who<small><small>[^18]</small></small> seemed to be somewhat, (whatsoever they were, it maketh<small><small>[^19]</small></small> no matter to me: God<small><small>[^20]</small></small> accepteth no man’s person:) for they who seemed \[to be somewhat\] in<small><small>[^21]</small></small> conference added nothing to me: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But contrariwise, when<small><small>[^22]</small></small> they saw that the gospel of the uncircumcision<small><small>[^23]</small></small> was committed unto me, as \[the gospel\] of the circumcision \[was\] unto Peter; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> (For he<small><small>[^24]</small></small> that wrought effectually in Peter to the apostleship of the circumcision, the same<small><small>[^25]</small></small> was mighty in me toward the Gentiles:) <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when James<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, Cephas, and John, who seemed to be pillars<small><small>[^27]</small></small>, perceived the grace<small><small>[^28]</small></small> that was given unto me, they gave to me and Barnabas the right hands of fellowship<small><small>[^29]</small></small>; that we \[should<small><small>[^30]</small></small> go\] unto the heathen, and they unto the circumcision. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Only \[they would\] that<small><small>[^31]</small></small> we should remember the poor; the same which I also was forward to do.

<big><big>[[Gal.2.11 Paul Confronts Cephas|⥃]]</big></big>***Paul Confronts Cephas***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But when Peter was come to Antioch<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, I withstood<small><small>[^33]</small></small> him to the face, because<small><small>[^34]</small></small> he was to be blamed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For before that certain<small><small>[^35]</small></small> came from James, he did<small><small>[^36]</small></small> eat with the Gentiles: but when they were come, he withdrew<small><small>[^37]</small></small> and separated himself, fearing<small><small>[^38]</small></small> them which were of the circumcision. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the other<small><small>[^39]</small></small> Jews dissembled likewise with him; insomuch that Barnabas also was carried<small><small>[^40]</small></small> away with their dissimulation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But when I saw that they walked<small><small>[^41]</small></small> not uprightly according to the truth<small><small>[^42]</small></small> of the gospel, I said<small><small>[^43]</small></small> unto Peter before \[them\] all, If thou<small><small>[^44]</small></small>, being a Jew, livest after the manner of Gentiles, and not as do the Jews, why<small><small>[^45]</small></small> compellest thou the Gentiles to live as do the Jews? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> We \[who are\] Jews<small><small>[^46]</small></small> by nature, and not sinners<small><small>[^47]</small></small> of the Gentiles, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Knowing that<small><small>[^48]</small></small> a man is not justified by the works of the law, but<small><small>[^49]</small></small> by the faith of Jesus Christ, even we have<small><small>[^50]</small></small> believed in Jesus Christ, that we might be justified by the faith of Christ, and not by the works of the law: for<small><small>[^51]</small></small> by the works of the law shall no flesh be justified. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if, while<small><small>[^52]</small></small> we seek to be justified by Christ, we ourselves also are found<small><small>[^53]</small></small> sinners, \[is<small><small>[^54]</small></small>\] therefore Christ the minister of sin? God<small><small>[^55]</small></small> forbid. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For if I build again the things which I destroyed, I make myself a transgressor.<small><small>[^56]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I through<small><small>[^57]</small></small> the law am dead<small><small>[^58]</small></small> to the law, that<small><small>[^59]</small></small> I might live unto God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I am crucified<small><small>[^60]</small></small> with Christ: nevertheless<small><small>[^61]</small></small> I live; yet not I, but<small><small>[^62]</small></small> Christ liveth in me: and the life<small><small>[^63]</small></small> which I now<small><small>[^64]</small></small> live in the flesh I live by the faith of the Son<small><small>[^65]</small></small> of God, who<small><small>[^66]</small></small> loved me, and gave himself for me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gal.2.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I do not<small><small>[^67]</small></small> frustrate the grace of God: for if righteousness<small><small>[^68]</small></small> \[come\] by the law, then Christ<small><small>[^69]</small></small> is dead in vain. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Gal.1|<< Galatians 1]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Gal.3|Galatians 3 >>]]</td></tr></table>
