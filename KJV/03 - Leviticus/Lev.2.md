***Laws for Grain Offerings***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Lev.6|Leviticus 6:14–23]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb273

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when any will offer a meat offering<small><small>[^2]</small></small> unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, his offering shall be \[of\] fine flour<small><small>[^3]</small></small>; and he shall pour oil<small><small>[^4]</small></small> upon it, and put frankincense<small><small>[^5]</small></small> thereon: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he shall bring it to Aaron’s sons the priests: and he shall take thereout his handful of the flour thereof, and of the oil thereof, with all the frankincense thereof; and the priest shall burn the memorial<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of it upon the altar, \[to be\] an offering made by fire, of a sweet savour unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the remnant<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of the meat offering \[shall be\] Aaron’s and his sons’: \[it is\] a thing most holy<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of the offerings of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> made by fire. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And if thou bring an oblation of a meat offering<small><small>[^9]</small></small> baken in the oven<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, \[it shall be\] unleavened cakes of fine flour mingled with oil, or unleavened wafers<small><small>[^11]</small></small> anointed with oil. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And if thy oblation \[be\] a meat offering \[baken\] in a pan<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, it shall be \[of\] fine flour unleavened, mingled with oil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt part it in pieces, and pour oil thereon: it \[is\] a meat offering.<small><small>[^13]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And if thy oblation \[be\] a meat offering \[baken\] in the fryingpan<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, it shall be made \[of\] fine<small><small>[^15]</small></small> flour with oil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt bring the meat offering that is made of these things unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: and when it is presented unto the priest, he shall bring it unto the altar. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the priest shall take from the meat offering a memorial<small><small>[^16]</small></small> thereof, and shall burn \[it\] upon the altar: \[it is\] an offering<small><small>[^17]</small></small> made by fire, of a sweet savour unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And that which is left of the meat offering \[shall be\] Aaron’s and his sons’: \[it is\] a thing most holy of the offerings of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> made by fire.<small><small>[^18]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> No meat offering, which ye shall bring unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, shall be made with leaven: for ye shall burn no leaven<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, nor any honey<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, in any offering of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> made by fire. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]As for the oblation<small><small>[^21]</small></small> of the firstfruits, ye shall offer them unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: but they shall not be burnt<small><small>[^22]</small></small> on the altar for a sweet savour. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And every oblation of thy meat offering shalt thou season with salt<small><small>[^23]</small></small>; neither shalt thou suffer the salt<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of the covenant of thy God to be lacking from thy meat offering: with all thine<small><small>[^25]</small></small> offerings thou shalt offer salt. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if thou offer a meat offering<small><small>[^26]</small></small> of thy firstfruits unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, thou shalt offer for the meat offering of thy firstfruits green ears of corn dried by the fire, \[even\] corn beaten<small><small>[^27]</small></small> out of full ears. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt put oil upon it, and lay frankincense thereon: it \[is\] a meat offering.<small><small>[^28]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.2.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the priest shall burn the memorial of it, \[part\] of the beaten corn thereof, and \[part\] of the oil thereof, with all the frankincense thereof: \[it is\] an offering made by fire unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <small><small>[^29]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Lev.1|<< Leviticus 1]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Lev.3|Leviticus 3 >>]]</td></tr></table>
