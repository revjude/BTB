<big><big>[[Lev.10.1 The Sin of Nadab and Abihu|⥃]]</big></big>***The Sin of Nadab and Abihu***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Num.3|Numbers 3:1–4]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Nadab<small><small>[^2]</small></small> and Abihu, the sons of Aaron, took either of them his censer<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, and put fire therein, and put incense<small><small>[^4]</small></small> thereon, and offered strange<small><small>[^5]</small></small> fire before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, which<small><small>[^6]</small></small> he commanded them not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there went out fire<small><small>[^7]</small></small> from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and devoured them, and they died<small><small>[^8]</small></small> before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^9]</small></small><small><small>[^10]</small></small><small><small>[^11]</small></small><small><small>[^12]</small></small><small><small>[^13]</small></small><small><small>[^14]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Moses said unto Aaron, This \[is it\] that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> spake, saying, I will be<small><small>[^15]</small></small> sanctified in them that come nigh me, and before<small><small>[^16]</small></small> all the people I will be glorified. And Aaron<small><small>[^17]</small></small> held his peace. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses called Mishael and Elzaphan, the sons of Uzziel<small><small>[^18]</small></small> the uncle of Aaron, and said unto them, Come near, carry<small><small>[^19]</small></small> your brethren from before the sanctuary out of the camp. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So they went near, and carried them in their coats out of the camp; as Moses had said. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses said unto Aaron, and unto Eleazar and unto Ithamar, his sons, Uncover<small><small>[^20]</small></small> not your heads, neither rend your clothes; lest ye die, and lest wrath<small><small>[^21]</small></small> come upon all the people: but let your brethren, the whole house of Israel, bewail the burning which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath kindled. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And ye shall<small><small>[^22]</small></small> not go out from the door of the tabernacle of the congregation, lest ye die: for the anointing<small><small>[^23]</small></small> oil of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] upon you. <big><big>[[Lev.10.8 Restrictions for Priests|⥃]]</big></big>***Restrictions for Priests***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> spake unto Aaron, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Do not<small><small>[^24]</small></small> drink wine nor strong drink<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, thou, nor thy sons with thee, when ye go into the tabernacle of the congregation, lest ye die: \[it shall be<small><small>[^26]</small></small>\] a statute for ever throughout your generations: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And that ye may put difference between holy and unholy, and between unclean and clean;<small><small>[^27]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And that ye may teach the children of Israel all the statutes which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath spoken unto them by the hand of Moses.<small><small>[^28]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses spake unto Aaron, and unto Eleazar and unto Ithamar, his sons that were left, Take<small><small>[^29]</small></small> the meat offering that remaineth of the offerings of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> made by fire, and eat it without leaven beside the altar: for it \[is\] most<small><small>[^30]</small></small> holy: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And ye shall<small><small>[^31]</small></small> eat it in the holy place, because it \[is\] thy due, and thy sons’ due, of the sacrifices of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> made by fire: for so I<small><small>[^32]</small></small> am commanded. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the wave breast and heave shoulder shall ye eat in a clean place; thou, and thy sons, and thy daughters with thee: for \[they be\] thy due, and thy sons’ due, \[which\] are given out of the sacrifices of peace offerings of the children of Israel.<small><small>[^33]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The heave shoulder<small><small>[^34]</small></small> and the wave breast shall they bring with the offerings made by fire of the fat, to wave \[it for\] a wave offering before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and it shall be thine, and thy sons’ with thee, by a statute for ever<small><small>[^35]</small></small>; as the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath commanded. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And Moses diligently sought the goat<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of the sin offering, and, behold, it was burnt: and he was angry<small><small>[^37]</small></small> with Eleazar and Ithamar, the sons of Aaron \[which were\] left \[alive\], saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore<small><small>[^38]</small></small> have ye not eaten the sin offering in the holy place, seeing it \[is\] most holy, and \[God\] hath given it you to bear<small><small>[^39]</small></small> the iniquity of the congregation, to make atonement for them before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, the blood<small><small>[^40]</small></small> of it was not brought in within the holy \[place\]: ye should indeed have eaten it in the holy \[place\], as I commanded<small><small>[^41]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lev.10.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Aaron said unto Moses, Behold, this day<small><small>[^42]</small></small> have they offered their sin offering and their burnt offering before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and such things have befallen me: and \[if\] I had eaten the sin offering to day, should<small><small>[^43]</small></small> it have been accepted in the sight of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>? [^25]: The Hebrew ***shecher***, Arabic ***sakar***, or ***sukr***, Greek <span class="asgreek"><span class='asgreek'>σικερα</span></span>, from ***shachar***, to inebriate, signifies any kind of fermented and inebriating liquor beside wine. So St. Jerome informs us, that ***sicera*** in Hebrew denotes any inebriating liquor, whether made of corn, the juice of apples, honey, dates, or any other fruit. These different kinds of liquors are described by Pliny, who calls then ***vina factitia***. One of the four prohibited drinks among the Mohammedans in India is called ***sakar***, which denotes inebriating liquor in general, but especially date wine.
