***The Mountain of the House of the LORD***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Isa.2|Isaiah 2:1–4]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb2027

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mic.4.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But in the last<small><small>[^2]</small></small> days it shall come to pass, \[that\] the mountain<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall be established in the top of the mountains, and it shall be exalted above the hills; and people<small><small>[^4]</small></small> shall flow unto it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mic.4.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And many nations shall come, and say<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, Come, and let us go up to the mountain of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and to the house of the God of Jacob; and he<small><small>[^6]</small></small> will teach us of his ways, and we will walk in his paths: for<small><small>[^7]</small></small> the law shall go forth of Zion, and the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> from Jerusalem. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mic.4.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And he shall judge<small><small>[^8]</small></small> among many people, and rebuke<small><small>[^9]</small></small> strong nations afar off; and they shall<small><small>[^10]</small></small> beat their swords into plowshares, and their spears into pruninghooks<small><small>[^11]</small></small>: nation shall not lift up a sword against nation, neither<small><small>[^12]</small></small> shall they learn war any more. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mic.4.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But<small><small>[^13]</small></small> they<small><small>[^14]</small></small> shall sit every man under his vine and under his fig tree; and none<small><small>[^15]</small></small> shall make \[them\] afraid: for<small><small>[^16]</small></small> the mouth of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts hath spoken \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mic.4.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For all<small><small>[^17]</small></small> people will walk every one in the name of his god, and we<small><small>[^18]</small></small> will walk in the name<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> our God for ever and ever.

***The Restoration of Zion***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Zech.8|Zechariah 8:1–23]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb2028

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mic.4.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In that day, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, will I<small><small>[^20]</small></small> assemble her that halteth, and I<small><small>[^21]</small></small> will gather her that is driven out, and her that I have afflicted; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mic.4.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will<small><small>[^22]</small></small> make her that halted a remnant, and her that was cast far off a strong nation: and the<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall reign over them in mount Zion from henceforth, even for ever. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mic.4.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou, O tower<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of the flock<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, the strong<small><small>[^26]</small></small> hold of the daughter of Zion, unto thee shall it come, even the first<small><small>[^27]</small></small> dominion; the kingdom shall come to the daughter of Jerusalem. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mic.4.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now why<small><small>[^28]</small></small> dost thou cry out aloud? \[is there<small><small>[^29]</small></small>\] no king in thee? is thy counsellor perished? for<small><small>[^30]</small></small> pangs have taken thee as a woman in travail. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mic.4.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be in pain, and labour<small><small>[^31]</small></small> to bring forth, O daughter of Zion, like a woman in travail: for now shalt thou<small><small>[^32]</small></small> go forth out of the city, and thou shalt dwell in the field, and thou shalt go \[even\] to Babylon; there shalt<small><small>[^33]</small></small> thou be delivered; there the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall redeem<small><small>[^34]</small></small> thee from the hand of thine enemies. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mic.4.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Now also many<small><small>[^35]</small></small> nations are gathered against thee, that say, Let her be defiled, and let our<small><small>[^36]</small></small> eye look upon Zion. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mic.4.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But they know<small><small>[^37]</small></small> not the thoughts of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, neither understand they his counsel: for he shall<small><small>[^38]</small></small> gather them as the sheaves into the floor. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mic.4.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Arise and thresh<small><small>[^39]</small></small>, O daughter of Zion: for I will make thine horn iron, and I will make thy hoofs<small><small>[^40]</small></small> brass: and thou shalt<small><small>[^41]</small></small> beat in pieces many people: and I will consecrate<small><small>[^42]</small></small> their gain unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and their substance unto the Lord of<small><small>[^43]</small></small> the whole earth. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Mic.3|<< Micah 3]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Mic.5|Micah 5 >>]]</td></tr></table>
