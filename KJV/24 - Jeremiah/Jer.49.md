<big><big>[[Jer.49.1 Judgment on the Ammonites|⥃]]</big></big>***Judgment on the Ammonites***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Concerning<small><small>[^2]</small></small> the Ammonites<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; Hath Israel no sons? hath he no heir? why \[then\] doth their king<small><small>[^4]</small></small> inherit Gad, and his people dwell in his cities<small><small>[^5]</small></small>?<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore, behold, the days come, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that I will<small><small>[^7]</small></small> cause an alarm of war to be heard in Rabbah<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of the Ammonites; and it shall be a desolate heap, and her daughters<small><small>[^9]</small></small> shall be burned with fire: then shall Israel<small><small>[^10]</small></small> be heir unto them that were his heirs, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Howl<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, O Heshbon, for Ai is spoiled: cry, ye daughters of Rabbah, gird<small><small>[^12]</small></small> you with sackcloth; lament, and run<small><small>[^13]</small></small> to and fro by the hedges; for their king<small><small>[^14]</small></small> shall go<small><small>[^15]</small></small> into captivity, \[and\] his priests and his princes together. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore gloriest<small><small>[^16]</small></small> thou in the valleys, thy flowing valley<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, O backsliding<small><small>[^18]</small></small> daughter? that trusted<small><small>[^19]</small></small> in her treasures, \[saying\], Who<small><small>[^20]</small></small> shall come unto me? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, I will<small><small>[^21]</small></small> bring a fear upon thee, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span> of hosts, from all those that be about thee; and ye shall<small><small>[^22]</small></small> be driven out every man right forth; and none<small><small>[^23]</small></small> shall gather up him that wandereth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And afterward I will bring again the captivity of the children of Ammon, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^24]</small></small>

<big><big>[[Jer.49.7 Judgment on Edom|⥃]]</big></big>***Judgment on Edom***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Obad.1|Obadiah 1:1–14]] ]</sup></small>

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Concerning Edom<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts; \[Is\] wisdom<small><small>[^26]</small></small> no more in Teman<small><small>[^27]</small></small>? is counsel perished from the prudent? is their wisdom vanished? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Flee<small><small>[^28]</small></small> ye, turn back<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, dwell<small><small>[^30]</small></small> deep, O inhabitants of Dedan<small><small>[^31]</small></small>; for<small><small>[^32]</small></small> I will bring the calamity of Esau upon him, the time \[that\] I will visit him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If grapegatherers<small><small>[^33]</small></small> come to thee, would they not leave \[some\] gleaning grapes? if thieves by night, they will destroy till they have enough<small><small>[^34]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But I have made<small><small>[^35]</small></small> Esau bare, I have uncovered his secret<small><small>[^36]</small></small> places, and he shall not be able to hide himself: his seed<small><small>[^37]</small></small> is spoiled, and his brethren, and his neighbours, and he \[is\] not<small><small>[^38]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Leave thy fatherless<small><small>[^39]</small></small> children, I will preserve \[them\] alive; and let thy<small><small>[^40]</small></small> widows trust in me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; Behold, they whose<small><small>[^41]</small></small> judgment \[was\] not to drink of the cup have assuredly drunken; and \[art\] thou he \[that\] shall altogether go unpunished? thou shalt not go unpunished, but thou shalt surely drink \[of it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I have<small><small>[^42]</small></small> sworn by myself, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that Bozrah<small><small>[^43]</small></small> shall become a desolation<small><small>[^44]</small></small>, a reproach, a waste, and a curse; and all the cities thereof shall be perpetual wastes. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have heard<small><small>[^45]</small></small> a rumour from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and an ambassador<small><small>[^46]</small></small> is sent unto the heathen, \[saying\], Gather<small><small>[^47]</small></small> ye together, and come against her, and rise up to the battle. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For, lo, I will make thee small among the heathen, \[and\] despised among men.<small><small>[^48]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy terribleness<small><small>[^49]</small></small> hath deceived thee, \[and\] the pride of thine heart, O thou that dwellest<small><small>[^50]</small></small> in the clefts of the rock, that holdest the height of the hill: though<small><small>[^51]</small></small> thou shouldest make thy nest as high as the eagle, I will bring thee down from thence, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also Edom<small><small>[^52]</small></small> shall be a desolation: every<small><small>[^53]</small></small> one that goeth by it shall be astonished, and shall hiss<small><small>[^54]</small></small> at all the plagues thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As in the<small><small>[^55]</small></small> overthrow of Sodom and Gomorrah and the neighbour \[cities\] thereof, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, no man<small><small>[^56]</small></small> shall abide there, neither shall a son of man dwell in it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, he shall come<small><small>[^57]</small></small> up like a lion from the swelling<small><small>[^58]</small></small> of Jordan against the habitation of the strong: but I will suddenly make him run away from her: and who \[is\] a chosen \[man\], \[that\] I may appoint over her? for who \[is\] like<small><small>[^59]</small></small> me? and who will appoint me the time<small><small>[^60]</small></small>? and who \[is\] that shepherd<small><small>[^61]</small></small> that will stand before me? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore hear the counsel<small><small>[^62]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that he hath taken against Edom; and his purposes, that he hath purposed against the inhabitants of Teman<small><small>[^63]</small></small>: Surely<small><small>[^64]</small></small> the least of the flock shall draw them out: surely he shall make<small><small>[^65]</small></small> their habitations desolate with them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The earth<small><small>[^66]</small></small> is moved at the noise of their fall, at the cry the noise thereof was heard in the Red sea<small><small>[^67]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, he shall<small><small>[^68]</small></small> come up and fly as the eagle, and spread his wings over Bozrah<small><small>[^69]</small></small>: and at that day shall the heart of the<small><small>[^70]</small></small> mighty men of Edom be as the heart of a woman in her pangs.

<big><big>[[Jer.49.23 Judgment on Damascus|⥃]]</big></big>***Judgment on Damascus***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Isa.17|Isaiah 17:1–14]] ]</sup></small>

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Concerning Damascus<small><small>[^71]</small></small>. Hamath<small><small>[^72]</small></small> is confounded, and Arpad: for they have heard evil tidings: they are fainthearted<small><small>[^73]</small></small>; \[there is\] sorrow<small><small>[^74]</small></small> on the sea<small><small>[^75]</small></small>; it cannot be quiet. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Damascus is waxed feeble, \[and\] turneth herself to flee, and fear hath seized on \[her\]: anguish<small><small>[^76]</small></small> and sorrows have taken her, as a woman in travail. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> How is the city of praise not left, the city of my joy!<small><small>[^77]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore her young men shall fall in her streets, and all the men of war shall be cut off in that day, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts.<small><small>[^78]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will<small><small>[^79]</small></small> kindle a fire in the wall of Damascus, and it shall consume the palaces of Ben–hadad<small><small>[^80]</small></small>.

<big><big>[[Jer.49.28 Judgment on Kedar and Hazor|⥃]]</big></big>***Judgment on Kedar and Hazor***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Concerning Kedar<small><small>[^81]</small></small>, and concerning the kingdoms of Hazor<small><small>[^82]</small></small>, which Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon shall smite, thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; Arise<small><small>[^83]</small></small> ye, go up to Kedar, and spoil<small><small>[^84]</small></small> the men of the east. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Their tents<small><small>[^85]</small></small> and their flocks shall they take away: they shall take to themselves their curtains<small><small>[^86]</small></small>, and all their vessels, and their camels<small><small>[^87]</small></small>; and they shall cry unto them, Fear<small><small>[^88]</small></small> \[is\] on every side. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Flee, get you far off<small><small>[^89]</small></small>, dwell<small><small>[^90]</small></small> deep, O ye inhabitants of Hazor, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; for<small><small>[^91]</small></small> Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon hath taken counsel against you, and hath conceived a purpose against you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Arise, get you up unto the wealthy nation<small><small>[^92]</small></small>, that<small><small>[^93]</small></small> dwelleth without care, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, which have neither gates nor bars, \[which\] dwell<small><small>[^94]</small></small> alone. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And their camels<small><small>[^95]</small></small> shall be a booty, and the multitude of their cattle a spoil: and I will scatter<small><small>[^96]</small></small> into all winds them \[that are\] in the utmost corners<small><small>[^97]</small></small>; and I will bring their calamity from all sides thereof, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Hazor<small><small>[^98]</small></small> shall be a dwelling<small><small>[^99]</small></small> for dragons, \[and\] a desolation for ever: there shall no man abide there, nor \[any\] son of man dwell in it.

<big><big>[[Jer.49.34 Judgment on Elam|⥃]]</big></big>***Judgment on Elam***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.34|34]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> that came to Jeremiah the prophet against Elam<small><small>[^100]</small></small> in the beginning of the reign of Zedekiah king of Judah, saying,<small><small>[^101]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.35|35]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts; Behold, I will break<small><small>[^102]</small></small> the bow<small><small>[^103]</small></small> of Elam, the chief of their might. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.36|36]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And upon Elam will I bring the four winds<small><small>[^104]</small></small> from the four quarters of heaven, and will scatter<small><small>[^105]</small></small> them toward all those winds; and there shall be no nation whither the outcasts<small><small>[^106]</small></small> of Elam shall not come. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.37|37]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I will cause Elam to be<small><small>[^107]</small></small> dismayed before their enemies<small><small>[^108]</small></small>, and before them that seek their life: and I will bring evil upon them, \[even\] my fierce anger, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and I will send the sword<small><small>[^109]</small></small> after them, till I have consumed them: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.49.38|38]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will set my throne in Elam, and will destroy from thence the king and the princes, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^110]</small></small>
