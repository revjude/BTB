***The Restoration of Israel and Judah***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Ezek.28|Ezekiel 28:25–26]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1763

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The word that came to Jeremiah from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, saying,<small><small>[^2]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus speaketh the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of Israel, saying, Write thee all the words that I have spoken unto thee in a book.<small><small>[^3]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For, lo, the days<small><small>[^4]</small></small> come, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that I<small><small>[^5]</small></small> will bring again the captivity of my people Israel and Judah, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: and I<small><small>[^6]</small></small> will cause them to return to the land that I gave to their fathers, and they shall possess it. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And these \[are\] the words that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> spake concerning Israel and concerning Judah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; We have heard a voice<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of trembling, of fear, and not of peace<small><small>[^8]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ask ye now, and see whether a man<small><small>[^9]</small></small> doth travail with child? wherefore do I see every<small><small>[^10]</small></small> man with his hands on his loins, as a woman in travail, and all faces are turned into paleness<small><small>[^11]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Alas! for<small><small>[^12]</small></small> that day \[is\] great, so<small><small>[^13]</small></small> that none \[is\] like it: it \[is<small><small>[^14]</small></small>\] even the time of Jacob’s trouble; but<small><small>[^15]</small></small> he shall be saved out of it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For i<small><small>[^16]</small></small>t shall come to pass in that day, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, \[that\] I will break his yoke from off thy neck, and will burst thy bonds, and strangers shall no more serve<small><small>[^17]</small></small> themselves of him: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But they shall serve the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> their God, and David their king, whom I will raise up unto them.<small><small>[^18]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore fear<small><small>[^19]</small></small> thou not, O my servant Jacob, sai<small><small>[^20]</small></small>th the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; neither be dismayed, O Israel: for, lo, I will save thee from afar, and thy seed from the land of their captivity; and Jacob shall return, and shall<small><small>[^21]</small></small> be in rest, and be quiet, and none shall make \[him\] afraid. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I \[am<small><small>[^22]</small></small>\] with thee, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, to save thee: though<small><small>[^23]</small></small> I make a full end of all nations whither I have scattered thee, yet will I not make a full end of thee: but I<small><small>[^24]</small></small> will correct thee in measure, and will not leave thee altogether unpunished. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, Thy bruise \[is\] incurable, \[and\] thy wound \[is\] grievous.<small><small>[^25]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] none<small><small>[^26]</small></small> to plead thy cause, that<small><small>[^27]</small></small> thou mayest be bound up: thou hast<small><small>[^28]</small></small> no healing medicines. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All thy lovers<small><small>[^29]</small></small> have forgotten thee; they seek thee not; for I<small><small>[^30]</small></small> have wounded thee with the wound of an enemy, with the chastisement of a cruel one, for the multitude of thine iniquity; \[because<small><small>[^31]</small></small>\] thy sins were increased. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Why<small><small>[^32]</small></small> criest thou for thine affliction? thy sorrow<small><small>[^33]</small></small> \[is\] incurable for the<small><small>[^34]</small></small> multitude of thine iniquity: \[because\] thy sins were increased, I have done these things unto thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore all they that devour thee shall be devoured; and all thine adversaries, every one of them, shall go into captivity; and they that spoil thee shall be a spoil, and all that prey upon thee will I give for a prey.<small><small>[^35]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I<small><small>[^36]</small></small> will restore health unto thee, and I will heal thee of thy wounds, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; because they<small><small>[^37]</small></small> called thee an Outcast, \[saying\], This \[is\] Zion, whom no man seeketh after. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; Behold<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, I will bring again the captivity of Jacob’s tents, and have mercy on his dwellingplaces; and the city<small><small>[^39]</small></small> shall be builded upon her own heap<small><small>[^40]</small></small>, and the palace<small><small>[^41]</small></small> shall remain after the manner thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And out<small><small>[^42]</small></small> of them shall proceed thanksgiving and the voice of them that make merry: and I<small><small>[^43]</small></small> will<small><small>[^44]</small></small> multiply them, and they shall not be few; I will also glorify them, and they shall not be small. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Their children<small><small>[^45]</small></small> also shall be as aforetime, and their congregation shall be established before me, and I<small><small>[^46]</small></small> will punish all that oppress them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And their nobles<small><small>[^47]</small></small> shall be of themselves, and their governor<small><small>[^48]</small></small> shall proceed from the midst of them; and I<small><small>[^49]</small></small> will cause him to draw near, and he shall approach unto me: for<small><small>[^50]</small></small> who \[is\] this that engaged<small><small>[^51]</small></small> his heart to approach unto me? saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And ye shall be my people, and I will be your God.<small><small>[^52]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, the whirlwind<small><small>[^53]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> goeth forth with fury, a continuing<small><small>[^54]</small></small> whirlwind: it shall fall<small><small>[^55]</small></small> with pain upon the head of the wicked. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.30.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The fierce<small><small>[^56]</small></small> anger of the <divin<small><small>[^57]</small></small>eName>Lord</divineName> shall not return, until he have done \[it\], and until he have performed the intents of his heart: in the latter days ye shall consider it. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Jer.29|<< Jeremiah 29]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Jer.31|Jeremiah 31 >>]]</td></tr></table>
