<big><big>[[Jer.18.1 The Potter and the Clay|⥃]]</big></big>***The Potter and the Clay***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The word which came to Jeremiah from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Arise, and go<small><small>[^2]</small></small> down to the potter’s house, and there I will cause<small><small>[^3]</small></small> thee to hear my words.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then I went<small><small>[^5]</small></small> down to the potter’s house, and, behold, he wrought a work on the wheels<small><small>[^6]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the vessel that he made of clay was marred in<small><small>[^7]</small></small> the hand of the potter: so he made it again another vessel, as seemed good to the potter to make \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came to me, saying,<small><small>[^8]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O house of Israel, cannot I do with you as this potter? saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. Behold, as the clay \[is\] in the potter’s hand, so \[are\] ye in mine hand, O house of Israel.<small><small>[^9]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[At what\] instant I shall speak concerning a nation, and concerning a kingdom, to pluck<small><small>[^10]</small></small> up, and to pull down, and to destroy \[it\]; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If that nation<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, against whom I have pronounced, turn from their evil, I will<small><small>[^12]</small></small> repent of the evil that I thought to do unto them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And \[at what\] instant I shall speak concerning a nation, and concerning a kingdom, to build<small><small>[^13]</small></small> and to plant \[it\]; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If it do<small><small>[^14]</small></small> evil in my sight, that it obey not my voice, then<small><small>[^15]</small></small> I will repent of the good, wherewith I said I would benefit them. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now therefore go to<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, speak to the men of Judah, and to the inhabitants of Jerusalem, saying, Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; Behold, I frame evil against you, and devise<small><small>[^17]</small></small> a device against you: return<small><small>[^18]</small></small> ye now every one from his evil way, and make your ways and your doings good. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they said, There<small><small>[^19]</small></small> is no hope: but we will walk<small><small>[^20]</small></small> after our own devices, and we will every one do the imagination of his evil heart. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; Ask<small><small>[^21]</small></small> ye now among the heathen, who<small><small>[^22]</small></small> hath heard such things: the virgin<small><small>[^23]</small></small> of Israel hath done a very<small><small>[^24]</small></small> horrible thing. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Will<small><small>[^25]</small></small> \[a man\] leave the snow<small><small>[^26]</small></small> of Lebanon \[which cometh\] from the rock of the field? \[or\] shall the cold flowing waters that come from another place be forsaken? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because my people<small><small>[^27]</small></small> hath forgotten me, they have burned<small><small>[^28]</small></small> incense to vanity, and they have caused<small><small>[^29]</small></small> them to stumble in their ways \[from\] the ancient<small><small>[^30]</small></small> paths, to walk<small><small>[^31]</small></small> in paths, \[in\] a way not cast up; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To make<small><small>[^32]</small></small> their land desolate, \[and\] a perpetual<small><small>[^33]</small></small> hissing; every one that passeth thereby shall be<small><small>[^34]</small></small> astonished, and wag his head. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will scatter<small><small>[^35]</small></small> them as with an east wind before the enemy; I will shew<small><small>[^36]</small></small> them the back, and not the face, in the day<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of their calamity.

Come, and let us smite<small><small>[^41]</small></small> him with<small><small>[^42]</small></small> the tongue, and let us not<small><small>[^43]</small></small> give heed to any of his words. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Give<small><small>[^44]</small></small> heed to me, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and hearken<small><small>[^45]</small></small> to the voice of them that contend with me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Shall evil<small><small>[^46]</small></small> be recompensed for good? for they have digged<small><small>[^47]</small></small> a pit for my soul. Remember<small><small>[^48]</small></small> that I stood before thee to speak good for them, \[and\] to turn away thy wrath from them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore deliver<small><small>[^49]</small></small> up their children to the famine, and pour out their \[blood<small><small>[^50]</small></small>\] by the force of the sword; and let their wives<small><small>[^51]</small></small> be bereaved of their children, and \[be\] widows; and let their men be put to death; \[let\] their young<small><small>[^52]</small></small> men \[be\] slain by the sword in battle. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let a cry<small><small>[^53]</small></small> be heard from their houses, when thou shalt bring a troop suddenly upon them: for<small><small>[^54]</small></small> they have digged a pit to take me, and hid<small><small>[^55]</small></small> snares for my feet. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.18.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet, <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, thou<small><small>[^56]</small></small> knowest all their counsel against me to slay \[me<small><small>[^57]</small></small>\]: forgive<small><small>[^58]</small></small> not their iniquity, neither blot out their sin from thy sight, but let them be overthrown before thee; deal \[thus\] with them in the<small><small>[^59]</small></small> time of thine anger. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Jer.17|<< Jeremiah 17]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Jer.19|Jeremiah 19 >>]]</td></tr></table>
