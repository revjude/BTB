<big><big>[[Jer.48.1 Judgment on Moab|⥃]]</big></big>***Judgment on Moab***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Isa.15|Isaiah 15:1–9]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Against Moab<small><small>[^2]</small></small> thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, the God of Israel; Woe unto Nebo<small><small>[^3]</small></small>! for it is spoiled: Kiriathaim is confounded \[and\] taken: Misgab<small><small>[^4]</small></small> is confounded and dismayed.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There shall be\] no more<small><small>[^6]</small></small> praise of Moab: in Heshbon<small><small>[^7]</small></small> they have devised evil against it; come<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, and let us cut it off from \[being\] a nation. Also thou shalt<small><small>[^9]</small></small> be cut down<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, O Madmen; the sword shall pursue thee<small><small>[^11]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A voice<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of crying \[shall be\] from Horonaim<small><small>[^13]</small></small>, spoiling and great destruction. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moab<small><small>[^14]</small></small> is destroyed; her<small><small>[^15]</small></small> little ones have caused a cry to be heard. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For in the going up of Luhith<small><small>[^16]</small></small> continual weeping<small><small>[^17]</small></small> shall go up; for in the going down of Horonaim the enemies have heard a cry of destruction. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Flee<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, save your lives, and be like<small><small>[^19]</small></small> the heath<small><small>[^20]</small></small> in the wilderness. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]For because<small><small>[^21]</small></small> thou hast trusted in thy works and in thy treasures, thou shalt also be taken: and Chemosh<small><small>[^22]</small></small> shall go forth into captivity \[with\] his priests<small><small>[^23]</small></small> and his princes together. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the spoiler<small><small>[^24]</small></small> shall come upon every city, and no<small><small>[^25]</small></small> city shall escape: the valley also shall perish, and the plain shall be destroyed, as the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath spoken. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Give wings<small><small>[^26]</small></small> unto Moab, that it may flee and get away: for the cities<small><small>[^27]</small></small> thereof shall be desolate, without any to dwell therein. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Cursed<small><small>[^28]</small></small> \[be\] he that doeth the work of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> deceitfully<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, and cursed \[be\] he that keepeth back his sword from blood. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Moab hath been<small><small>[^30]</small></small> at ease from his youth, and he hath<small><small>[^31]</small></small> settled on his lees, and hath not been emptied<small><small>[^32]</small></small> from vessel to vessel, neither hath he gone into captivity: therefore his taste remained<small><small>[^33]</small></small> in him, and his scent is not changed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore, behold, the days come, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that I will send unto him wanderers<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, that shall cause him to wander, and shall empty<small><small>[^35]</small></small> his vessels, and break their bottles. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moab shall be ashamed<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of Chemosh, as the<small><small>[^37]</small></small> house of Israel was ashamed of Beth–el their confidence. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> How<small><small>[^38]</small></small> say ye, We<small><small>[^39]</small></small> \[are\] mighty and strong men for the war? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moab is spoiled<small><small>[^40]</small></small>, and gone up \[out of\] her cities, and his chosen<small><small>[^41]</small></small> young men are gone<small><small>[^42]</small></small> down to the slaughter, saith<small><small>[^43]</small></small> the King, whose<small><small>[^44]</small></small> name \[is\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The calamity of Moab \[is\] near<small><small>[^45]</small></small> to come, and his affliction hasteth fast. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All ye that are about him, bemoan<small><small>[^46]</small></small> him; and all ye that know his name, say, How<small><small>[^47]</small></small> is the strong staff broken, \[and\] the beautiful rod! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou daughter<small><small>[^48]</small></small> that dost inhabit Dibon<small><small>[^49]</small></small>, come down from \[thy\] glory, and sit<small><small>[^50]</small></small> in thirst; for the spoiler<small><small>[^51]</small></small> of Moab shall come upon thee, \[and\] he shall destroy thy strong holds. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O inhabitant<small><small>[^52]</small></small> of Aroer<small><small>[^53]</small></small>, stand by the way, and espy; ask<small><small>[^54]</small></small> him that fleeth, and her that escapeth, \[and\] say, What is done? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moab is confounded<small><small>[^55]</small></small>; for it is broken down: howl and cry; tell ye it in Arnon<small><small>[^56]</small></small>, that Moab is spoiled, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And judgment is come upon the plain<small><small>[^57]</small></small> country; upon Holon, and upon Jahazah<small><small>[^58]</small></small>, and upon Mephaath, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And upon Dibon<small><small>[^59]</small></small>, and upon Nebo, and upon Beth–diblathaim<small><small>[^60]</small></small>, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And upon Kiriathaim<small><small>[^61]</small></small>, and upon Beth–gamul, and upon Beth–meon<small><small>[^62]</small></small>, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And upon Kerioth<small><small>[^63]</small></small>, and upon Bozrah<small><small>[^64]</small></small>, and upon all the cities of the land of Moab, far or near. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The horn<small><small>[^65]</small></small> of Moab is cut off, and his<small><small>[^66]</small></small> arm is broken, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Make ye him<small><small>[^67]</small></small> drunken: for he<small><small>[^68]</small></small> magnified \[himself\] against the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: Moab also shall wallow<small><small>[^69]</small></small> in his vomit, and he \[it<small><small>[^79]</small></small>\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore will I howl<small><small>[^80]</small></small> for Moab, and I will cry out for all Moab; \[mine heart\] shall mourn for the men of Kir–heres<small><small>[^81]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.32|32]]**<span sea, they reach \[even\] to the sea of Jazer: the spoiler<small><small>[^84]</small></small> is fallen upon thy summer fruits and upon thy vintage. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And joy<small><small>[^85]</small></small> and gladness is taken from the plentiful field, and from the land of Moab; and I have caused<small><small>[^86]</small></small> wine to fail from the winepresses: none shall tread with shouting; \[their\] shouting \[shall be\] no shouting. <span Elealeh<small><small>[^88]</small></small>, \[and even\] unto Jahaz<small><small>[^89]</small></small>, have they uttered their voice, from Zoar<small><small>[^90]</small></small> \[even\] unto <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.35|35]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover I will cause to cease in Moab, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, him that offereth<small><small>[^94]</small></small> in the high places, and him that burneth incense to his gods. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.36|36]]**<span Kir–heres<small><small>[^96]</small></small>: because the riches<small><small>[^97]</small></small> \[that\] he hath gotten are perished. <span and in the streets thereof: for I have broken<small><small>[^103]</small></small> Moab like a vessel wherein \[is\] no pleasure, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span the back<small><small>[^105]</small></small> with shame! so shall Moab be a derision<small><small>[^106]</small></small> and a dismaying to all them about him. <span fly as an eagle, and shall spread<small><small>[^108]</small></small> his wings over Moab. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.41|41]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Kerioth<small><small>[^109]</small></small> is taken, and the strong holds are surprised, and the mighty men’s hearts in Moab at that day shall be as the heart<small><small>[^110]</small></small> of a woman in her pangs. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.42|42]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moab<small><small>[^111]</small></small> shall be destroyed from<small><small>[^112]</small></small> \[being\] a people, because he hath magnified<small><small>[^113]</small></small> \[himself\] against the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.43|43]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Fear, and the pit, and the snare, \[shall be\] upon thee, O inhabitant of Moab, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^114]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.44|44]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that year<small><small>[^116]</small></small> of their visitation, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.48.45|45]]**<span the midst of Sihon, and shall devour<small><small>[^118]</small></small> the corner of Moab, and the crown of the head of the tumultuous ones<small><small>[^119]</small></small>. <span of Chemosh perisheth: for thy sons are taken captives<small><small>[^122]</small></small>, and thy daughters captives.
