***A Prophecy against Zedekiah*** ^bsb1772

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The word<small><small>[^2]</small></small> which came unto Jeremiah from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, when<small><small>[^3]</small></small> Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon, and all his army, and all the kingdoms<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of the earth of his dominion<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, and all the people, fought against Jerusalem, and against all the cities thereof, saying,<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, the Go<small><small>[^7]</small></small>d of Israel; Go and speak to Zedekiah king of Judah, and tell him, Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; Behold<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, I will give this city into the hand of the king of Babylon, and he shall burn it with fire: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou<small><small>[^9]</small></small> shalt not escape out of his hand, but shalt surely be taken, and delivered into his hand; and thine<small><small>[^10]</small></small> eyes shall behold the eyes of the king of Babylon, and he shall speak with thee mouth to mouth<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, and thou shalt go to Babylon. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet hear the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, O Zedekiah king of Judah; Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of thee, Thou shalt not die by the sword: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[But\] thou<small><small>[^12]</small></small> shalt die in peace: and with<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the burnings of thy fathers, the former kings which were before thee, so<small><small>[^14]</small></small> shall they burn \[odours\] for thee; and they<small><small>[^15]</small></small> will lament thee, \[saying\], Ah lord! for I have pronounced the word, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Jeremiah the prophet spake all these words unto Zedekiah king of Judah in Jerusalem,<small><small>[^16]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When the king of Babylon’s army fought against<small><small>[^17]</small></small> Jerusalem, and against all the cities of Judah that were left, against Lachish<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, and against Azekah: for<small><small>[^19]</small></small> these defenced cities remained of the cities of Judah.

***Freedom for Hebrew Slaves*** ^bsb1773

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[This is\] the word that came unto Jeremiah from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, after that the king Zedekiah had<small><small>[^20]</small></small> made a covenant with all the people which \[were\] at Jerusalem, to proclaim<small><small>[^21]</small></small> liberty unto them; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That every man should let his manservant, and every man his maidservant, \[being\] an Hebrew<small><small>[^22]</small></small> or an Hebrewess, go free; that none should serve<small><small>[^23]</small></small> himself of them, \[to wit\], of a Jew his brother. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now when<small><small>[^24]</small></small> all the princes, and all the people, which had entered into the covenant, heard that every one should let his manservant, and every one his maidservant, go free, that none should serve themselves of them any more, then<small><small>[^25]</small></small> they obeyed, and let \[them\] go. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But afterward they turned, and caused the servants and the handmaids, whom they had let go free, to return, and brought them into subjection for servants and for handmaids.<small><small>[^26]</small></small>

and when he hath served thee six years, thou shalt let him go free from thee: but<small><small>[^32]</small></small> your fathers hearkened not unto me, neither inclined their ear. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And ye<small><small>[^33]</small></small> were now<small><small>[^34]</small></small> turned, and had done right in my sight, in proclaiming<small><small>[^35]</small></small> liberty every man to his neighbour; Ye have not hearkened unto me, in proclaiming liberty, every one to his brother, and every man to his neighbour: behold<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, I proclaim a liberty for you, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, to the sword<small><small>[^42]</small></small>, to the pestilence, and to the famine; and I will<small><small>[^43]</small></small> make you to be removed<small><small>[^44]</small></small> into all the kingdoms of the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will give the men that have transgressed<small><small>[^45]</small></small> my covenant, which have not performed the words of the covenant which they had made before me, when<small><small>[^46]</small></small> they cut the calf in twain, and passed between the parts thereof, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The princes<small><small>[^47]</small></small> of Judah, and the princes of Jerusalem, the eunuchs<small><small>[^48]</small></small>, and the priests, and all the people of the land, which passed between the parts of the calf; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will even give them into the hand of their enemies, and into<small><small>[^49]</small></small> the hand of them that seek their life: and their<small><small>[^50]</small></small> dead bodies shall be for meat unto the fowls of the heaven, and to the beasts of the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Zedekiah<small><small>[^51]</small></small> king of Judah and his princes will I give into the hand of their enemies, and into the hand of them that seek their life, and into the hand of the king of Babylon’s army, which are<small><small>[^52]</small></small> gone up from you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.34.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, I will command<small><small>[^53]</small></small>, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and cause<small><small>[^54]</small></small> them to return to this city;
