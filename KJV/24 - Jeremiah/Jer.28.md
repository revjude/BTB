***Hananiah’s False Prophecy*** ^bsb1760

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass the same<small><small>[^2]</small></small> year, in the beginning of the reign of Zedekiah king of Judah, in the fourth year, \[and\] in the fifth month, \[that\] Hananiah<small><small>[^3]</small></small> the son of Azur the prophet<small><small>[^4]</small></small>, which \[was\] of Gibeon, spake unto me in the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, in the presence of the priests and of all the people, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus speaketh the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, the God of I<small><small>[^5]</small></small>srael, saying, I have broken the yoke of the king of Babylon. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Within two full years<small><small>[^6]</small></small> will I bring again into this place all the<small><small>[^7]</small></small> vessels of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> house, that<small><small>[^8]</small></small> Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon took away from this place, and carried them to Babylon: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will bring<small><small>[^9]</small></small> again to this place Jeconiah<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the son of Jehoiakim king of Judah, with all the captives<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of Judah, that went into Babylon, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: for I will break<small><small>[^12]</small></small> the yoke of the king of Babylon. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then the prophet Jeremiah said unto the prophet Hananiah in the presence of the priests, and in the presence of all the people that stood in the house<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Even the prophet Jeremiah said, Amen<small><small>[^14]</small></small>: the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> do so: the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> perform thy words which thou hast prophesied, to bring again the vessels of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> house, and all that is carried away captive, from Babylon into this place.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nevertheless hear thou now this word that I speak in thine ears, and in the ears of all the people;<small><small>[^16]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The prophets<small><small>[^17]</small></small> that have been before me and before thee of old prophesied<small><small>[^18]</small></small> both against many countries, and against great kingdoms, of war, and of evil, and of pestilence. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The prophet which<small><small>[^19]</small></small> prophesieth of peace, when the word of the prophet shall come to pass, \[then<small><small>[^20]</small></small>\] shall the prophet be known, that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath truly sent him. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Hananiah the prophet took<small><small>[^21]</small></small> the yoke from off the prophet Jeremiah’s neck, and brake it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Hananiah spake in the presence of all the people, saying, Thus<small><small>[^22]</small></small> saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; Even<small><small>[^23]</small></small> so will I break the yoke of Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon from the neck of all nations within the space of two full years. And the prophet Jeremiah went his way. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto Jeremiah \[the prophet\], after that Hananiah the prophet had broken the yoke from off the neck of the prophet Jeremiah, saying,<small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Go and tell Hananiah, saying, Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; Thou hast<small><small>[^25]</small></small> broken the yokes of wood; but thou shalt<small><small>[^26]</small></small> make for them yokes of iron. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, the God of Israel; I have put<small><small>[^27]</small></small> a yoke of iron upon the neck of all these nations, that they<small><small>[^28]</small></small> may serve Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon; and they shall serve him: and I<small><small>[^29]</small></small> have given him the beasts of the field also. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then said the prophet Jeremiah unto Hananiah the prophet, Hear now, Hananiah; The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath not sent thee; but thou makest this people to trust in a lie.<small><small>[^30]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; Behold, I will<small><small>[^31]</small></small> cast thee from off the face of the earth: this year<small><small>[^32]</small></small> thou shalt die, because<small><small>[^33]</small></small> thou hast taught rebellion<small><small>[^34]</small></small> against the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.28.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So Hananiah<small><small>[^35]</small></small> the prophet died the same year in the seventh month<small><small>[^36]</small></small>. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Jer.27|<< Jeremiah 27]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Jer.29|Jeremiah 29 >>]]</td></tr></table>

The death of Hananiah, thus predicted, was God's seal to the words of His prophet, and must have gained his other predictions great credit among the people; though it is evident that it did not induce them to forsake their sins and return to the God of Israel.
