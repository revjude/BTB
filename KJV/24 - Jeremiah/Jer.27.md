***The Yoke of Nebuchadnezzar*** ^bsb1759

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the beginning<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of the reign of Jehoiakim the son of Josiah king of Judah came this word unto Jeremiah from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, saying,<small><small>[^3]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> to me; Make<small><small>[^4]</small></small> thee bonds and yokes, and put<small><small>[^5]</small></small> them upon thy neck,<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And send them to the king of Edom<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, and to the king of Moab, and to the king of the Ammonites, and to the king of Tyrus, and to the king of Zidon, by the hand of the messengers<small><small>[^8]</small></small> which come to Jerusalem unto Zedekiah king of Judah; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And command them to say unto their masters, Thus<small><small>[^9]</small></small> saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, the God of Israel; Thus<small><small>[^10]</small></small> shall ye say unto your masters; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have made<small><small>[^11]</small></small> the earth, the man and the beast that \[are\] upon the ground, by my great power and by my outstretched arm, and have<small><small>[^12]</small></small> given it unto whom it seemed meet unto me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And now have I given all<small><small>[^13]</small></small> these lands into the hand of Nebuchadnezzar the king of Babylon, my<small><small>[^14]</small></small> servant; and<small><small>[^15]</small></small> the beasts of the field have I given him also to serve him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all<small><small>[^16]</small></small> nations shall serve him, and his son, and his son’s son, until<small><small>[^17]</small></small> the very time of his land come: and then many<small><small>[^18]</small></small> nations and great kings shall serve themselves of him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it<small><small>[^19]</small></small> shall come to pass, \[that\] the nation and kingdom which will not serve the same Nebuchadnezzar the king of Babylon, and that will not put their neck under the yoke of the king of Babylon, that nation<small><small>[^20]</small></small> will I punish, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, with the sword<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, and with the famine, and with the pestilence, until I have consumed them by his hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore hearken<small><small>[^22]</small></small> not ye to your prophets, nor to your diviners, nor to your dreamers<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, nor to your enchanters, nor to your sorcerers, which speak unto you, saying, Ye shall not serve the king of Babylon: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For they<small><small>[^24]</small></small> prophesy a lie unto<small><small>[^25]</small></small> you, to remove you far from your land; and that I<small><small>[^26]</small></small> should drive you out, and ye should perish. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the nations that bring their neck under the yoke of the king of Babylon, and serve him, those<small><small>[^27]</small></small> will I let remain still in their own land, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and they shall till it, and dwell therein.<small><small>[^28]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I spake also to Zedekiah<small><small>[^29]</small></small> king of Judah according to all these words, saying, Bring<small><small>[^30]</small></small> your necks under the yoke of the king of Babylon, and serve him and his people, and live. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Why<small><small>[^31]</small></small> will ye die, thou and thy people, by the sword<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, by the famine, and by the pestilence, as the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath spoken against the nation that will not serve the king of Babylon? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore hearken<small><small>[^33]</small></small> not unto the words of the prophets that speak unto you, saying, Ye shall not serve the king of Babylon: for they<small><small>[^34]</small></small> prophesy a lie unto you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I have not sent them, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, yet they prophesy a lie<small><small>[^35]</small></small> in my name; that I<small><small>[^36]</small></small> might drive you out, and that ye<small><small>[^37]</small></small> might perish, ye, and the prophets that prophesy unto you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also I spake to the priests and to all this people, saying, Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; Hearken not to the words of your prophets that prophesy unto you, saying, Behold<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, the vessels of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> house shall now shortly be brought again from Babylon: for<small><small>[^39]</small></small> they prophesy a lie unto you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hearken not unto them; serve<small><small>[^40]</small></small> the king of Babylon, and live: wherefore<small><small>[^41]</small></small> should this city be laid waste? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.27.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if they<small><small>[^42]</small></small> \[be\] prophets, and if the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> be with them, let them<small><small>[^43]</small></small> now make intercession to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, that the vessels which are left in the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and \[in\] the house of the king of Judah, and at Jerusalem, go not to Babylon.

[^19]: There is a peculiar grandeur, as well as propriety, in this method of predicting Nebuchadnezzar's rapid successes. The God of Israel, declaring himself to be the Lord of armies, and the Creator and Owner of the whole earth, with all its inhabitants and productions, and claiming full sovereignty over his creatures, avows his determination, for reasons he does not deign to assign, to give all the countries of the world to the king of Babylon, whom he calls his servant, because he would accomplish an important part of his most righteous designs. They, therefore, who would escape the most dreadful judgments, must submit to the God of Israel, by submitting to Nebuchadnezzar; they must hearken to the prophets of Israel, and not to their own diviners; and they must observe, that Nebuchadnezzar, his son, and his grandson, would whatever opposition should be made, possess the full dominion of all these countries, till the appointed time was expired; and then, these haughty conquerors would in their turn become the prey of other powerful conquerors; all of which was most exactly fulfilled.
