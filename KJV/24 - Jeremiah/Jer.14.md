<big><big>[[Jer.14.1 Drought, Famine, Sword, and Plague|⥃]]</big></big>***Drought, Famine, Sword, and Plague***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The word<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> that came to Jeremiah concerning the dearth<small><small>[^3]</small></small>.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Judah mourneth<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, and the gates<small><small>[^6]</small></small> thereof languish; they<small><small>[^7]</small></small> are black unto the ground; and the cry<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of Jerusalem is gone up. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And their nobles<small><small>[^9]</small></small> have sent their little ones to the waters: they came to the pits<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, \[and\] found no water; they returned with their vessels empty; they were<small><small>[^11]</small></small> ashamed and confounded, and covered<small><small>[^12]</small></small> their heads. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because the ground<small><small>[^13]</small></small> is chapt, for there was no rain in the earth, the plowmen<small><small>[^14]</small></small> were ashamed, they covered their heads. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea, the hind also calved in the field, and forsook \[it\], because there was no grass.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the wild<small><small>[^16]</small></small> asses did stand in the high places, they<small><small>[^17]</small></small> snuffed up the wind like dragons; their<small><small>[^18]</small></small> eyes did fail, because \[there was\] no grass. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, though<small><small>[^19]</small></small> our iniquities testify against us, do<small><small>[^20]</small></small> thou \[it\] for thy name’s sake: for our<small><small>[^21]</small></small> backslidings are many; we have sinned against thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O the hope<small><small>[^22]</small></small> of Israel, the saviour<small><small>[^23]</small></small> thereof in time<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of trouble, why<small><small>[^25]</small></small> shouldest thou be as a stranger in the land, and as a wayfaring<small><small>[^26]</small></small> man \[that\] turneth aside to tarry for a night? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Why shouldest thou be as a man astonied, as a mighty man \[that\] cannot<small><small>[^27]</small></small> save? yet thou, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[art<small><small>[^28]</small></small>\] in the midst of us, and we are called by thy name<small><small>[^29]</small></small>; leave<small><small>[^30]</small></small> us not. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> unto this people, Thus have they<small><small>[^31]</small></small> loved to wander, they have not refrained<small><small>[^32]</small></small> their feet, therefore the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^33]</small></small></span> doth not accept them; he will<small><small>[^34]</small></small> now remember their iniquity, and visit their sins. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> unto me, Pray not for this people for \[their\] good.<small><small>[^35]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When they fast<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, I will not hear their cry; and when<small><small>[^37]</small></small> they offer burnt offering and an oblation, I will not accept them: but<small><small>[^38]</small></small> I will consume them by the sword, and by the famine, and by the pestilence. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then said I, Ah<small><small>[^39]</small></small>, Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>! behold<small><small>[^40]</small></small>, the prophets say unto them, Ye shall not see the sword, neither shall ye have famine; but I will give you assured peace<small><small>[^41]</small></small> in this place. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto me, The prophets<small><small>[^42]</small></small> prophesy lies in my name: I sent<small><small>[^43]</small></small> them not, neither have I commanded them, neither spake unto them: they prophesy unto you a false vision and divination<small><small>[^44]</small></small>, and a thing of nought, and the<small><small>[^45]</small></small> deceit of their heart. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> concerning the prophets that prophesy in my name, and I sent them not, yet they say, Sword and famine shall not<small><small>[^46]</small></small> be in this land; By sword and famine shall those prophets be consumed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the people<small><small>[^47]</small></small> to whom they prophesy shall be cast<small><small>[^48]</small></small> out in the streets of Jerusalem because of the famine and the sword; and they shall have none to bury them, them, their wives, nor their sons, nor their daughters: for<small><small>[^49]</small></small> I will pour their wickedness upon them. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thou shalt say this word unto them; Let mine<small><small>[^50]</small></small> eyes run down with tears night and day, and let them not cease: for<small><small>[^51]</small></small> the virgin daughter of my people is broken with a great breach, with a very<small><small>[^52]</small></small> grievous blow. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.14.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If I go forth<small><small>[^53]</small></small> into the field, then behold the slain with the sword! and if I enter into the city, then behold them that are sick with famine! yea<small><small>[^54]</small></small>, both the prophet and the priest go about<small><small>[^55]</small></small> into a land that they know not.
