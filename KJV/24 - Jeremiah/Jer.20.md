<big><big>[[Jer.20.1 Pashhur Persecutes Jeremiah|⥃]]</big></big>***Pashhur Persecutes Jeremiah***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now Pashur the son of Immer<small><small>[^2]</small></small> the priest, who \[was\] also chief<small><small>[^3]</small></small> governor in the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, heard that Jeremiah prophesied these things. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Pashur smote<small><small>[^4]</small></small> Jeremiah the prophet, and put him in the stocks<small><small>[^5]</small></small> that \[were\] in the high<small><small>[^6]</small></small> gate of Benjamin, which \[was\] by the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass on the morrow, that Pashur<small><small>[^7]</small></small> brought forth Jeremiah out of the stocks. Then said Jeremiah unto him, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath<small><small>[^8]</small></small> not called thy name Pashur, but Magor–missabib<small><small>[^9]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, Behold, I will make<small><small>[^10]</small></small> thee a terror to thyself, and to all thy friends: and they shall fall by the sword of their enemies, and thine<small><small>[^11]</small></small> eyes shall behold \[it\]: and I will give<small><small>[^12]</small></small> all Judah into the hand of the king of Babylon, and he shall carry them captive into Babylon, and shall slay them with the sword. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover I will deliver<small><small>[^13]</small></small> all the strength of this city, and all the labours<small><small>[^14]</small></small> thereof, and all the precious things thereof, and all the treasures of the kings of Judah will I give into the hand of their enemies, which shall spoil them, and take them, and carry them to Babylon. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou, Pashur<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, and all that dwell in thine house shall go into captivity: and thou shalt come to Babylon, and there thou shalt die, and shalt be buried there, thou, and all thy friends<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, to whom thou hast<small><small>[^17]</small></small> prophesied lies.

<big><big>[[Jer.20.7 Jeremiah’s Complaint|⥃]]</big></big>***Jeremiah’s Complaint***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, thou hast deceived<small><small>[^18]</small></small> me, and I was deceived: thou art<small><small>[^19]</small></small> stronger than I, and hast prevailed: I am<small><small>[^20]</small></small> in derision daily, every one mocketh me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For since I spake, I cried<small><small>[^21]</small></small> out, I cried violence and spoil; because the word<small><small>[^22]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> was made a reproach unto me, and a derision, daily. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then I said, I will<small><small>[^23]</small></small> not make mention of him, nor speak any more in his name. But \[his word\] was in<small><small>[^24]</small></small> mine heart as a burning fire shut up in my bones, and I was weary with forbearing, and I could not \[stay\]. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]For I heard<small><small>[^25]</small></small> the defaming of many, fear<small><small>[^26]</small></small> on every side. Report<small><small>[^27]</small></small>, \[say they\], and we will report it. All my familiars<small><small>[^28]</small></small> watched for my halting, \[saying\], Peradventure he will be enticed, and we shall<small><small>[^29]</small></small> prevail against him, and we shall take our revenge on him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^30]</small></small></span> \[is\] with me as a mighty<small><small>[^31]</small></small> terrible one: therefore my<small><small>[^32]</small></small> persecutors shall stumble, and they<small><small>[^33]</small></small> shall not prevail: they shall be greatly ashamed; for they shall not prosper: \[their\] everlasting<small><small>[^34]</small></small> confusion shall never be forgotten. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, that<small><small>[^35]</small></small> triest the righteous, \[and\] seest the reins and the heart, let me<small><small>[^36]</small></small> see thy vengeance on them: for<small><small>[^37]</small></small> unto thee have I opened my cause. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Sing unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, praise ye the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: for<small><small>[^38]</small></small> he hath delivered the soul of the poor from the hand of evildoers. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Cursed \[be\] the day wherein I was born: let not the day wherein my mother bare me be blessed.<small><small>[^39]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Cursed \[be\] the man who brought tidings to my father, saying, A man<small><small>[^40]</small></small> child is born unto thee; making him very glad. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And let that man be as<small><small>[^41]</small></small> the cities which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> overthrew, and repented<small><small>[^42]</small></small> not: and let him<small><small>[^43]</small></small> hear the cry in the morning, and the shouting at noontide; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because he slew<small><small>[^44]</small></small> me not from the womb; or that my mother might have been my grave, and her womb \[to be\] always great \[with me\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.20.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore came<small><small>[^45]</small></small> I forth out of the womb to see<small><small>[^46]</small></small> labour and sorrow, that my days should be consumed with<small><small>[^47]</small></small> shame? <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Jer.19|<< Jeremiah 19]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Jer.21|Jeremiah 21 >>]]</td></tr></table>
