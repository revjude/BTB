<big><big>[[Jer.35.1 The Obedience of the Rechabites|⥃]]</big></big>***The Obedience of the Rechabites***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The word<small><small>[^2]</small></small> which came unto Jeremiah from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> in the<small><small>[^3]</small></small> days of Jehoiakim the son of Josiah king of Judah, saying,<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Go unto the house<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of the Rechabites, and speak unto them, and bring them into the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, into one<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of the chambers, and give them wine to drink. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then I took Jaazaniah the son of Jeremiah, the son of Habaziniah, and his brethren, and all his sons, and the whole house of the Rechabites; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I brought them into the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, into the chamber<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of the sons of Hanan, the son of Igdaliah, a man<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of God, which \[was\] by the chamber of the princes<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, which \[was\] above the chamber of Maaseiah the son of Shallum, the keeper<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of the door<small><small>[^11]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I set before the sons of the house of the Rechabites pots full of wine, and cups, and I said unto them, Drink<small><small>[^12]</small></small> ye wine. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But they said, We will drink no wine: for Jonadab<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the son of Rechab our father commanded us, saying, Ye shall<small><small>[^14]</small></small> drink no wine, \[neither\] ye, nor your sons for ever: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Neither shall<small><small>[^15]</small></small> ye build house, nor sow seed, nor plant vineyard, nor have \[any\]: but all your days ye shall dwell in tents; that ye may live many days in the land where ye \[be\] strangers. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus have we obeyed the voice of Jonadab the son of Rechab our father in all that he hath charged us, to drink no wine all our days, we, our wives, our sons, nor our daughters;<small><small>[^16]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nor to build houses for us to dwell in: neither have we vineyard, nor field, nor seed:<small><small>[^17]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But we have dwelt in tents, and have obeyed, and done according to all that Jonadab our father commanded us.<small><small>[^18]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But it came to pass, when<small><small>[^19]</small></small> Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon came up into the land, that we said, Come<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, and let us go to Jerusalem for fear of the army of the Chaldeans, and for fear of the army of the Syrians: so we dwell at Jerusalem.

<big><big>[[Jer.35.12 Judah Rebuked|⥃]]</big></big>***Judah Rebuked***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then came the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> unto Jeremiah, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, the God of Israel; Go and tell the men of Judah and the inhabitants of Jerusalem, Will<small><small>[^21]</small></small> ye not receive instruction to hearken to my words? saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The words<small><small>[^22]</small></small> of Jonadab the son of Rechab, that he commanded his sons not to drink wine, are performed; for unto this day they drink none, but obey their father’s commandment: notwithstanding I have spoken unto you, rising<small><small>[^23]</small></small> early and speaking; but ye<small><small>[^24]</small></small> hearkened not unto me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have<small><small>[^25]</small></small> sent also unto you all my servants the prophets, rising up early and sending \[them\], saying, Return<small><small>[^26]</small></small> ye now every man from his evil way, and amend your doings, and go not after other gods to serve them, and ye shall dwell<small><small>[^27]</small></small> in the land which I have given to you and to your fathers: but ye have<small><small>[^28]</small></small> not inclined your ear, nor hearkened unto me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because the sons of Jonadab the son of Rechab have performed the commandment of their father, which he commanded them; but this people hath not hearkened unto me:<small><small>[^29]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.35.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore<small><small>[^30]</small></small> thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of hosts, the God of Israel; Behold<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, I will bring upon Judah and upon all the inhabitants of Jerusalem all the evil that I have pronounced against them: because<small><small>[^32]</small></small> I have spoken unto them, but they have not heard; and I have called unto them, but they have not answered.

[^14]: Jonadab, a man of fervent zeal for the pure worship of God, and who lived about three hundred years before this time ([[2Kgs.10|2Kgs 10:15-16]], etc.) had probably practised these rules himself; and having trained up his children to habits of abstemiousness, he enjoined them and their posterity to adhere to them. In these regulations he seems to have had no religious, but merely a prudential view, as is intimated in the reason annexed to them "that ye may live many days in the land where ye be strangers." And this would be the natural consequence of observing these rules; for their temperate mode of living would very much contribute to preserve health and prolong life; and they would avoid giving umbrage, or exciting the jealousy or envy of the Jews, who might have been provoked, by their engaging and succeeding in the principal business in which they themselves were engaged, agriculture and vine-dressing to expel them their country; by which they would have been deprived of the religious advantages they enjoyed. In [[1Chr.2|1Chr 2:55]], they are termed scribes, which intimates that they were engaged in some kind of literary employments.
