***Mourning Turned to Joy***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Matt.2|Matthew 2:16–18]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1764

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> At the same<small><small>[^2]</small></small> time, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, will<small><small>[^3]</small></small> I be the God of<small><small>[^4]</small></small> all the families of Israel, and they shall be my people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, The people<small><small>[^5]</small></small> \[which were\] left of the sword found<small><small>[^6]</small></small> grace in the wilderness; \[even\] Israel, when<small><small>[^7]</small></small> I went to cause him to rest. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath appeared of old<small><small>[^8]</small></small> unto me, \[saying\], Yea, I have<small><small>[^9]</small></small> loved thee with an<small><small>[^10]</small></small> everlasting love: therefore with lovingkindness have I drawn<small><small>[^11]</small></small> thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Again I will build<small><small>[^12]</small></small> thee, and thou shalt be built, O<small><small>[^13]</small></small> virgin of Israel: thou shalt again<small><small>[^14]</small></small> be adorned with thy tabrets<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, and shalt go forth in the dances of them that make merry. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt yet<small><small>[^16]</small></small> plant vines upon the mountains<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of Samaria: the planters shall plant, and shall eat<small><small>[^18]</small></small> \[them\] as common things. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For there shall be a day<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, \[that\] the watchmen upon<small><small>[^20]</small></small> the mount Ephraim shall cry, Arise<small><small>[^21]</small></small> ye, and let us go up to Zion unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> our God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; Sing<small><small>[^22]</small></small> with gladness for Jacob, and shout among the chief of the nations: publish ye, praise ye, and say, O<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, save thy people, the remnant<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, I<small><small>[^25]</small></small> will bring them from the north country, and gather them from the coasts<small><small>[^26]</small></small> of the earth, \[and\] with them the<small><small>[^27]</small></small> blind and the lame, the woman with child and her that travaileth with child together: a great company shall return thither. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They shall come<small><small>[^28]</small></small> with weeping, and with supplications<small><small>[^29]</small></small> will I lead them: I will<small><small>[^30]</small></small> cause them to walk by the rivers of waters in a<small><small>[^31]</small></small> straight way, wherein they shall not stumble: for I<small><small>[^32]</small></small> am a father to Israel, and Ephraim<small><small>[^33]</small></small> \[is\] my firstborn. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, O ye nations, and declare<small><small>[^34]</small></small> \[it\] in the isles afar off, and say, He<small><small>[^35]</small></small> that scattered Israel will gather him, and keep<small><small>[^36]</small></small> him, as a shepherd \[doth\] his flock. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath redeemed<small><small>[^37]</small></small> Jacob, and ransomed him from the hand of \[him that was\] stronger<small><small>[^38]</small></small> than he. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore<small><small>[^39]</small></small> they shall come and sing in the height<small><small>[^40]</small></small> of Zion, and shall<small><small>[^41]</small></small> flow together to the goodness of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, for wheat<small><small>[^42]</small></small>, and for wine, and for oil, and for the young of the flock and of the herd: and their<small><small>[^43]</small></small> soul shall be as a watered garden; and they<small><small>[^44]</small></small> shall not sorrow any more at all. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shall<small><small>[^45]</small></small> the virgin rejoice in the dance, both young men and old together: for<small><small>[^46]</small></small> I will turn their mourning into joy, and will comfort them, and make them rejoice from their sorrow. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will satiate<small><small>[^47]</small></small> the soul of the priests with fatness, and my people<small><small>[^48]</small></small> shall be satisfied with my goodness, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; A<small><small>[^49]</small></small> voice was heard in Ramah<small><small>[^50]</small></small>, lamentation, \[and\] bitter weeping; Rahel weeping for her children refused<small><small>[^51]</small></small> to be comforted for her children, because<small><small>[^52]</small></small> they \[were\] not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; Refrain<small><small>[^53]</small></small> thy voice from weeping, and thine eyes from tears: for<small><small>[^54]</small></small> thy work shall be rewarded, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and they<small><small>[^55]</small></small> shall come again from the land of the enemy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there is hope in thine end, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that thy children shall come again to their own border.<small><small>[^56]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have surely<small><small>[^57]</small></small> heard Ephraim<small><small>[^58]</small></small> bemoaning himself \[thus\]; Thou hast<small><small>[^59]</small></small> chastised me, and I was chastised, as a<small><small>[^60]</small></small> bullock unaccustomed \[to the yoke\]: turn<small><small>[^61]</small></small> thou me, and I shall be turned; for<small><small>[^62]</small></small> thou \[art\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> my God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Surely after<small><small>[^63]</small></small> that I was turned, I repented; and after that I was instructed, I smote<small><small>[^64]</small></small> upon \[my\] thigh: I was ashamed<small><small>[^65]</small></small>, yea, even confounded, because I did<small><small>[^66]</small></small> bear the reproach of my youth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Is\] Ephraim<small><small>[^67]</small></small> my dear son? \[is he\] a pleasant child? for<small><small>[^68]</small></small> since I spake against him, I do earnestly remember him still: therefore my bowels<small><small>[^69]</small></small> are troubled<small><small>[^70]</small></small> for him; I will<small><small>[^71]</small></small> surely have mercy upon him, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Set thee<small><small>[^72]</small></small> up waymarks, make thee high heaps: set thine<small><small>[^73]</small></small> heart toward the highway, \[even\] the way \[which\] thou wentest: turn<small><small>[^74]</small></small> again, O<small><small>[^75]</small></small> virgin of Israel, turn again to these thy cities. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]How<small><small>[^76]</small></small> long wilt thou go about, O thou backsliding<small><small>[^77]</small></small> daughter? for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath created<small><small>[^78]</small></small> a new thing in the earth, A woman<small><small>[^79]</small></small> shall compass a man. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, the God of Israel; As<small><small>[^80]</small></small> yet they shall use this speech in the land of Judah and in the cities thereof, when I shall bring again their captivity; The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> bless thee, O<small><small>[^81]</small></small> habitation of justice, \[and\] mountain<small><small>[^82]</small></small> of holiness.<small><small>[^83]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there shall dwell in Judah itself, and in all the cities thereof together, husbandmen, and they \[that\] go forth with flocks.<small><small>[^84]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I have satiated the weary soul, and I have replenished every sorrowful soul.<small><small>[^85]</small></small>

***The New Covenant***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Heb.8|Hebrews 8:6–13]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1765

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Upon this I awaked, and beheld; and my sleep was sweet unto me.<small><small>[^86]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, the days<small><small>[^87]</small></small> come, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that I<small><small>[^88]</small></small> will sow the house of Israel and the house of Judah with the seed of man, and with the seed of beast. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it shall come to pass, \[that\] like<small><small>[^89]</small></small> as I have watched over them, to pluck<small><small>[^90]</small></small> up, and to break down, and to throw down, and to destroy, and to afflict; so<small><small>[^91]</small></small> will I watch over them, to build, and to plant, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In those days they shall say no more, The fathers have eaten a sour grape, and the children’s teeth are set on edge.<small><small>[^92]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But every one shall die for his own iniquity: every man that eateth the sour grape, his teeth shall be set on edge.<small><small>[^93]</small></small>

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.35|35]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, which giveth<small><small>[^108]</small></small> the sun for a light by day, \[and\] the ordinances of the moon and of the stars for a light by night, which divideth<small><small>[^109]</small></small> the sea when<small><small>[^110]</small></small> the waves thereof roar; The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts \[is\] his name:<small><small>[^111]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.36|36]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If those<small><small>[^112]</small></small> ordinances depart from before me, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[then\] the seed of Israel also shall cease<small><small>[^113]</small></small> from being a nation before me for ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.31.37|37]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; If<small><small>[^114]</small></small> heaven above can be measured, and the foundations of the earth searched out beneath, I<small><small>[^115]</small></small> will also cast off all the seed of Israel for all that they have done, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.
