<big><big>[[Jer.5.1 No One Is Just|⥃]]</big></big>***No One Is Just***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Run ye<small><small>[^2]</small></small> to and fro through the streets of Jerusalem, and see now, and know, and seek<small><small>[^3]</small></small> in the broad places thereof, if ye can<small><small>[^4]</small></small> find a man, if there<small><small>[^5]</small></small> be \[any\] that executeth judgment, that seeketh<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the truth; and I will pardon it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And though<small><small>[^7]</small></small> they say, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> liveth; surely they swear falsely. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[are\] not thine<small><small>[^8]</small></small> eyes upon the truth? thou hast stricken<small><small>[^9]</small></small> them, but they have not grieved; thou hast consumed them, \[but\] they have refused to receive correction: they have made<small><small>[^10]</small></small> their faces harder than a rock; they have refused to return. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore I said, Surely these \[are\] poor; they are foolish: for they know not the way of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[nor\] the judgment of their God.<small><small>[^11]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will get me<small><small>[^12]</small></small> unto the great men, and will speak unto them; for they have known the way of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[and\] the judgment of their God: but these<small><small>[^13]</small></small> have altogether broken the yoke, \[and\] burst the bonds. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore a lion<small><small>[^14]</small></small> out of the forest shall slay them, \[and\] a wolf<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of the evenings<small><small>[^16]</small></small> shall spoil them, a leopard<small><small>[^17]</small></small> shall watch over their cities: every one that goeth out thence shall be torn in pieces: because<small><small>[^18]</small></small> their transgressions are many, \[and\] their backslidings are increased<small><small>[^19]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]How shall<small><small>[^20]</small></small> I pardon thee for this? thy children have forsaken me, and sworn by<small><small>[^21]</small></small> \[them that are\] no gods<small><small>[^22]</small></small>: when I had fed<small><small>[^23]</small></small> them to the full, they then<small><small>[^24]</small></small> committed adultery, and assembled themselves by troops<small><small>[^25]</small></small> in the harlots’ houses. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They were \[as\] fed horses in the morning: every one<small><small>[^26]</small></small> neighed after his neighbour’s wife. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Shall I not visit<small><small>[^27]</small></small> for these \[things\]? saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: and shall<small><small>[^28]</small></small> not my soul be avenged on such a nation as this? 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Go ye up<small><small>[^29]</small></small> upon her walls, and destroy; but make<small><small>[^30]</small></small> not a full end: take away her battlements; for they \[are\] not<small><small>[^31]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the house<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of Israel and the house of Judah have dealt very treacherously against me, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They have belied<small><small>[^33]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and said, \[It is\] not he; neither<small><small>[^34]</small></small> shall evil come upon us; neither shall we see sword nor famine: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the prophets<small><small>[^35]</small></small> shall become wind, and the word \[is\] not in them: thus shall it be done unto them.

<big><big>[[Jer.5.14 Judgment Proclaimed|⥃]]</big></big>***Judgment Proclaimed***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of hosts, Because ye speak this word, behold, I will make<small><small>[^36]</small></small> my words in thy mouth fire, and this people wood, and it shall devour them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lo, I will<small><small>[^37]</small></small> bring a nation upon you from far, O house<small><small>[^38]</small></small> of Israel, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: it \[is\] a mighty<small><small>[^39]</small></small> nation, it \[is\] an ancient nation, a nation<small><small>[^40]</small></small> whose language thou knowest not, neither understandest what they say. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Their quiver \[is\] as an open sepulchre, they \[are\] all mighty men.<small><small>[^41]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they<small><small>[^42]</small></small> shall eat up thine harvest, and thy bread, \[which\] thy sons and thy daughters should eat: they shall eat up thy flocks and thine herds: they shall eat up thy vines and thy fig trees: they shall impoverish<small><small>[^43]</small></small> thy fenced cities, wherein thou trustedst, with the sword. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nevertheless in those days, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, I will not make<small><small>[^44]</small></small> a full end with you. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And it shall come to pass, when ye shall say, Wherefore<small><small>[^45]</small></small> doeth the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> our God all these \[things\] unto us? then shalt thou answer them, Like as<small><small>[^46]</small></small> ye have forsaken me, and served strange gods in your land, so shall ye serve strangers in a land \[that is\] not yours. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Declare this in the house of Jacob, and publish it in Judah, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear now this, O foolish<small><small>[^47]</small></small> people, and without understanding<small><small>[^48]</small></small>; which have eyes, and see not; which have ears, and hear not: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Fear ye not<small><small>[^49]</small></small> me? saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: will ye not tremble<small><small>[^50]</small></small> at my presence, which have placed<small><small>[^51]</small></small> the sand \[for\] the bound of the sea by a perpetual decree, that it cannot pass it: and though the waves thereof toss themselves, yet can they not prevail; though they roar, yet can they not pass over it? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But this people hath a revolting<small><small>[^52]</small></small> and a rebellious heart; they are revolted and gone. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Neither say they in their heart, Let us now<small><small>[^53]</small></small> fear the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> our God, that giveth<small><small>[^54]</small></small> rain, both the former and the latter, in his season: he reserveth unto us the appointed<small><small>[^55]</small></small> weeks of the harvest. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Your iniquities have turned away these \[things\], and your sins have withholden good \[things\] from you.<small><small>[^56]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^57]</small></small> among my people are found wicked \[men\]: they lay wait<small><small>[^58]</small></small>, as he that setteth snares; they set a trap, they catch<small><small>[^59]</small></small> men. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As a cage<small><small>[^60]</small></small> is full of birds, so \[are<small><small>[^61]</small></small>\] their houses full of deceit: therefore they are become great, and waxen rich. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They are waxen<small><small>[^62]</small></small> fat, they shine: yea, they overpass<small><small>[^63]</small></small> the deeds of the wicked: they judge<small><small>[^64]</small></small> not the cause, the cause of the fatherless, yet<small><small>[^65]</small></small> they prosper; and the right of the needy do they not judge. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.5.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Shall I not visit for these \[things\]? saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: shall not my soul be avenged on such a nation as this?<small><small>[^66]</small></small>
