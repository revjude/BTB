<big><big>[[Jer.19.1 The Broken Jar|⥃]]</big></big>***The Broken Jar***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, Go<small><small>[^2]</small></small> and get a potter’s earthen bottle, and \[take\] of the ancients of the people<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, and of the ancients of the priests;<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And go forth unto the valley<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of the son of Hinnom, which \[is\] by the entry of the east gate<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, and proclaim<small><small>[^7]</small></small> there the words that I shall tell thee, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And say, Hear<small><small>[^8]</small></small> ye the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, O kings of Judah, and inhabitants of Jerusalem; Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, the God of Israel; Behold, I will bring evil upon this place, the which whosoever heareth, his ears<small><small>[^9]</small></small> shall tingle. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because they have<small><small>[^10]</small></small> forsaken me, and have estranged<small><small>[^11]</small></small> this place, and have burned<small><small>[^12]</small></small> incense in it unto other gods, whom neither they nor their fathers have known, nor the kings of Judah, and have filled<small><small>[^13]</small></small> this place with the blood of innocents; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They have built also the high<small><small>[^14]</small></small> places of Baal, to burn<small><small>[^15]</small></small> their sons with fire \[for\] burnt offerings unto Baal, which<small><small>[^16]</small></small> I commanded not, nor spake \[it\], neither<small><small>[^17]</small></small> came \[it\] into my mind: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore, behold, the days come, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that this<small><small>[^18]</small></small> place shall no more be called Tophet, nor The valley of the son of Hinnom, but The valley of slaughter. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will make<small><small>[^19]</small></small> void the counsel of Judah and Jerusalem in this place; and I will cause<small><small>[^20]</small></small> them to fall by the sword before their enemies, and by the hands of them that seek their lives: and their<small><small>[^21]</small></small> carcases will I give to be meat for the fowls of the heaven, and for the beasts of the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will make this city desolate, and an hissing; every one that passeth thereby shall be astonished and hiss because of all the plagues thereof.<small><small>[^22]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will cause them to eat the<small><small>[^23]</small></small> flesh of their sons and the flesh of their daughters, and they shall eat every one the flesh of his friend in the siege and straitness, wherewith their enemies, and they that seek their lives, shall straiten them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shalt thou break the bottle in the sight of the men that go with thee,<small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And shalt say unto them, Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts; Even<small><small>[^25]</small></small> so will I break this people and this city, as \[one\] breaketh a potter’s vessel, that cannot be made whole<small><small>[^26]</small></small> again: and they shall bury<small><small>[^27]</small></small> \[them\] in Tophet, till \[there be\] no place to bury. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus will I do unto this place, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and to the inhabitants thereof, and \[even\] make<small><small>[^28]</small></small> this city as Tophet: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the houses of Jerusalem, and the houses of the kings of Judah, shall be defiled<small><small>[^29]</small></small> as the place of Tophet, because of all the houses upon<small><small>[^30]</small></small> whose roofs they have burned incense unto all the host of heaven, and have poured<small><small>[^31]</small></small> out drink offerings unto other gods. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then came Jeremiah from<small><small>[^32]</small></small> Tophet, whither the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had sent him to prophesy; and he stood<small><small>[^33]</small></small> in the court of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> house; and said to all the people, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.19.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, the God of Israel; Behold, I will bring upon this city and upon all her towns all the evil that I have pronounced against it, because<small><small>[^34]</small></small> they have hardened<small><small>[^35]</small></small> their necks, that they<small><small>[^36]</small></small> might not hear my words. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Jer.18|<< Jeremiah 18]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Jer.20|Jeremiah 20 >>]]</td></tr></table>
