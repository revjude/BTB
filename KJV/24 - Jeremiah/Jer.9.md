<big><big>[[Jer.9.1 A Lament over Zion|⥃]]</big></big>***A Lament over Zion***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Oh that my head were waters, and mine eyes a fountain of tears, that I might weep<small><small>[^2]</small></small> day and night for the slain of the daughter<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of my people!<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Oh that I had<small><small>[^5]</small></small> in the wilderness a lodging place of wayfaring men; that I might leave my people, and go from them! for<small><small>[^6]</small></small> they \[be\] all adulterers, an assembly<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of treacherous men. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they bend<small><small>[^8]</small></small> their tongues \[like\] their bow \[for\] lies: but they are not valiant<small><small>[^9]</small></small> for the truth upon the earth; for they<small><small>[^10]</small></small> proceed from evil to evil, and they know<small><small>[^11]</small></small> not me, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Take ye heed<small><small>[^12]</small></small> every one of his neighbour<small><small>[^13]</small></small>, and trust ye not in any brother: for every brother<small><small>[^14]</small></small> will utterly supplant, and every neighbour will walk<small><small>[^15]</small></small> with slanders. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they will<small><small>[^16]</small></small> deceive<small><small>[^17]</small></small> every one his neighbour, and will not speak the truth: they have taught<small><small>[^18]</small></small> their tongue to speak lies, \[and\] weary<small><small>[^19]</small></small> themselves to commit iniquity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thine habitation<small><small>[^20]</small></small> \[is\] in the midst of deceit; through deceit they refuse<small><small>[^21]</small></small> to know me, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, Behold, I will<small><small>[^22]</small></small> melt them, and try them; for how shall<small><small>[^23]</small></small> I do for the daughter of my people? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Their tongue<small><small>[^24]</small></small> \[is as\] an arrow shot out; it speaketh deceit: \[one<small><small>[^25]</small></small>\] speaketh peaceably to his neighbour with his mouth, but in heart<small><small>[^26]</small></small> he layeth his wait<small><small>[^27]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Shall I not visit them for these \[things\]? saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: shall not my soul be avenged on such a nation as this?<small><small>[^28]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the mountains<small><small>[^29]</small></small> will I take up a weeping and wailing, and for the habitations<small><small>[^30]</small></small> of the wilderness a lamentation, because<small><small>[^31]</small></small> they are burned up<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, so<small><small>[^33]</small></small> that none can pass through \[them\]; neither can \[men\] hear the voice of the cattle; both<small><small>[^34]</small></small> the fowl of the heavens and the beast are fled; they are gone. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will make Jerusalem<small><small>[^35]</small></small> heaps, \[and\] a den<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of dragons; and I will make the cities<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of Judah desolate<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, without an inhabitant. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who \[is\] the wise<small><small>[^39]</small></small> man, that may understand this? and \[who is he\] to whom the mouth of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath spoken, that he may declare it, for<small><small>[^40]</small></small> what the land perisheth \[and\] is burned up like a wilderness, that none passeth through? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> saith, Because they have forsaken my law which I set before them, and have not obeyed my voice, neither walked therein;<small><small>[^41]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But have walked<small><small>[^42]</small></small> after the imagination<small><small>[^43]</small></small> of their own heart, and after Baalim, which<small><small>[^44]</small></small> their fathers taught them: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, the God of Israel; Behold, I will<small><small>[^45]</small></small> feed them, \[even\] this people, with wormwood, and give them water of gall to drink. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will scatter<small><small>[^46]</small></small> them also among the heathen, whom neither they nor their fathers have known: and I<small><small>[^47]</small></small> will send a sword after them, till I have consumed them. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, Consider ye, and call<small><small>[^48]</small></small> for the mourning women<small><small>[^49]</small></small>, that they may come; and send for cunning \[women\], that they may come: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And let them make haste, and take<small><small>[^50]</small></small> up a wailing for us, that our eyes<small><small>[^51]</small></small> may run down with tears, and our eyelids gush out with waters. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For a voice<small><small>[^52]</small></small> of wailing is heard out of Zion, How are we spoiled! we are<small><small>[^53]</small></small> greatly confounded, because we have forsaken the land, because our<small><small>[^54]</small></small> dwellings have cast \[us\] out. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet hear<small><small>[^55]</small></small> the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, O ye women, and let your ear receive<small><small>[^56]</small></small> the word of his mouth, and teach<small><small>[^57]</small></small> your daughters wailing, and every one her neighbour lamentation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For death is come up into our windows, \[and\] is entered into our palaces, to cut off the children from without, \[and\] the young men from the streets.<small><small>[^58]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Speak, Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, Even the carcases of men shall fall<small><small>[^59]</small></small> as dung upon the open field, and as the handful after the harvestman, and none shall gather \[them\]. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, Let not the wise<small><small>[^60]</small></small> \[man\] glory in his wisdom, neither<small><small>[^61]</small></small> let the mighty \[man\] glory in his might, let not the rich<small><small>[^62]</small></small> \[man\] glory in his riches: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But let him<small><small>[^63]</small></small> that glorieth glory in this, that he understandeth and knoweth<small><small>[^64]</small></small> me, that I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> which exercise lovingkindness<small><small>[^65]</small></small>, judgment, and righteousness, in the earth: for<small><small>[^66]</small></small> in these \[things\] I delight, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Behold, the days come, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that<small><small>[^67]</small></small> I will punish<small><small>[^68]</small></small> all \[them which are\] circumcised with the uncircumcised; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.9.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Egypt<small><small>[^69]</small></small>, and Judah<small><small>[^70]</small></small>, and Edom, and the children of Ammon, and Moab, and all \[that are\] in the utmost corners<small><small>[^71]</small></small>, that dwell in the wilderness: for all \[these\] nations \[are\] uncircumcised, and all the house of Israel \[are\] uncircumcised in<small><small>[^72]</small></small> the heart. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Jer.8|<< Jeremiah 8]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Jer.10|Jeremiah 10 >>]]</td></tr></table>
