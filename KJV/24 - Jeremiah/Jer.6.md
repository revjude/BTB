<big><big>[[Jer.6.1 Jerusalem’s Final Warning|⥃]]</big></big>***Jerusalem’s Final Warning***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O ye<small><small>[^2]</small></small> children of Benjamin, gather<small><small>[^3]</small></small> yourselves to flee out of the midst of Jerusalem, and blow<small><small>[^4]</small></small> the trumpet in Tekoa<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, and set up a sign of fire in Beth–haccerem<small><small>[^6]</small></small>: for evil<small><small>[^7]</small></small> appeareth out of the north, and great destruction. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have likened the daughter<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of Zion to a comely and delicate \[woman<small><small>[^9]</small></small>\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The shepherds<small><small>[^10]</small></small> with their flocks shall come unto her; they shall<small><small>[^11]</small></small> pitch \[their\] tents against her round about; they shall feed every one in his place. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Prepare<small><small>[^12]</small></small> ye war against her; arise, and let us go up at noon<small><small>[^13]</small></small>. Woe unto us! for the<small><small>[^14]</small></small> day goeth away, for the shadows<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of the evening are stretched out. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Arise, and let us go by night, and let us destroy<small><small>[^16]</small></small> her palaces. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]For thus hath the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts said, Hew ye down trees, and cast a mount against Jerusalem: this \[is\] the city to be visited; she \[is\] wholly oppression in the midst of her. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As a fountain<small><small>[^17]</small></small> casteth out her waters, so she casteth out her wickedness: violence<small><small>[^18]</small></small> and spoil is heard in her; before me continually \[is\] grief and wounds. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be thou<small><small>[^19]</small></small> instructed, O Jerusalem, lest<small><small>[^20]</small></small> my soul depart<small><small>[^21]</small></small> from thee; lest I<small><small>[^22]</small></small> make thee desolate, a land not inhabited. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, They shall<small><small>[^23]</small></small> thoroughly glean the remnant of Israel as a vine: turn back thine hand as a grapegatherer into the baskets. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To whom<small><small>[^24]</small></small> shall I speak, and give<small><small>[^25]</small></small> warning, that they may hear? behold, their ear<small><small>[^26]</small></small> \[is\] uncircumcised, and they cannot hearken: behold, the word<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> is unto them a reproach; they have no delight<small><small>[^28]</small></small> in it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore<small><small>[^29]</small></small> I am full of the fury of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; I am weary with holding in: I will<small><small>[^30]</small></small> pour it out upon the children abroad, and upon the assembly of young men together: for even<small><small>[^31]</small></small> the husband with the wife shall be taken, the aged with \[him that is\] full of days. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And their<small><small>[^32]</small></small> houses shall be turned unto others, \[with their\] fields and wives together: for I will<small><small>[^33]</small></small> stretch out my hand upon the inhabitants of the land, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^34]</small></small> from the least of them even unto the greatest of them every one \[is\] given to covetousness; and<small><small>[^35]</small></small> from the prophet even unto the priest every one dealeth falsely. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They have healed<small><small>[^36]</small></small> also the hurt<small><small>[^37]</small></small> \[of the daughter\] of my people slightly, saying, Peace<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, peace; when \[there is\] no peace. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Were<small><small>[^39]</small></small> they ashamed when they had committed abomination? nay, they were not at all ashamed, neither could they blush<small><small>[^40]</small></small>: therefore<small><small>[^41]</small></small> they shall fall among them that fall: at the time<small><small>[^42]</small></small> \[that\] I visit them they shall be cast down, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, Stand<small><small>[^43]</small></small> ye in the ways, and see, and ask for the old paths, where \[is\] the good way, and walk<small><small>[^44]</small></small> therein, and ye shall<small><small>[^45]</small></small> find rest for your souls. But they said, We will<small><small>[^46]</small></small> not walk \[therein\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also I<small><small>[^47]</small></small> set watchmen over you, \[saying\], Hearken<small><small>[^48]</small></small> to the sound of the trumpet. But they said, We<small><small>[^49]</small></small> will not hearken. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore hear<small><small>[^50]</small></small>, ye nations, and know, O congregation, what \[is\] among them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear, O earth<small><small>[^51]</small></small>: behold, I will bring evil upon this people, \[even<small><small>[^52]</small></small>\] the fruit of their thoughts, because they have not hearkened unto my words, nor to<small><small>[^53]</small></small> my law, but rejected it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To what<small><small>[^54]</small></small> purpose cometh there to me incense from Sheba<small><small>[^55]</small></small>, and the sweet cane<small><small>[^56]</small></small> from a far country? your burnt<small><small>[^57]</small></small> offerings \[are\] not acceptable, nor your sacrifices sweet unto me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, Behold, I will<small><small>[^58]</small></small> lay stumblingblocks before this people, and the fathers<small><small>[^59]</small></small> and the sons together shall fall upon them; the neighbour and his friend shall perish.

<big><big>[[Jer.6.22 An Invasion from the North|⥃]]</big></big>***An Invasion from the North***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, Behold, a people<small><small>[^60]</small></small> cometh from the north country, and a great nation shall be raised from the sides of the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They shall<small><small>[^61]</small></small> lay hold on bow and spear; they \[are\] cruel<small><small>[^62]</small></small>, and have no mercy; their<small><small>[^63]</small></small> voice roareth like the sea; and they ride upon horses, set in array as men for war against thee, O daughter of Zion. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> We have<small><small>[^64]</small></small> heard the fame thereof: our hands wax feeble: anguish<small><small>[^65]</small></small> hath taken hold of us, \[and\] pain, as of a woman in travail. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jer.6.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Go not<small><small>[^66]</small></small> forth into the field, nor walk by the way; for the sword<small><small>[^67]</small></small> of the enemy \[and\] fear \[is\] on every side.
