<big><big>[[Job.24.1 Job：Judgment for the Wicked|⥃]]</big></big>***Job: Judgment for the Wicked***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Why, seeing<small><small>[^2]</small></small> times are not hidden from the Almighty, do they that know<small><small>[^3]</small></small> him not see<small><small>[^4]</small></small> his days? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Some\] remove the landmarks<small><small>[^5]</small></small>; they violently<small><small>[^6]</small></small> take away flocks, and feed \[thereof<small><small>[^7]</small></small>\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They drive<small><small>[^8]</small></small> away the ass of the fatherless, they take the widow’s ox for a pledge. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They turn<small><small>[^9]</small></small> the needy out of the way: the poor of the earth hide<small><small>[^10]</small></small> themselves together. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, \[as\] wild asses<small><small>[^11]</small></small> in the desert, go they forth to their work; rising<small><small>[^12]</small></small> betimes for a prey: the wilderness<small><small>[^13]</small></small> \[yieldeth\] food for them \[and\] for \[their\] children. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They reap<small><small>[^14]</small></small> \[every one\] his corn<small><small>[^15]</small></small> in the field: and they gather<small><small>[^16]</small></small> the vintage of the wicked. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They cause the naked<small><small>[^17]</small></small> to lodge without clothing, that \[they have\] no covering<small><small>[^18]</small></small> in the cold. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They are wet<small><small>[^19]</small></small> with the showers of the mountains, and embrace<small><small>[^20]</small></small> the rock for want of a shelter. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They pluck the fatherless from the breast, and take a pledge of the poor.<small><small>[^21]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They cause \[him\] to go naked without clothing, and they take away<small><small>[^22]</small></small> the sheaf \[from\] the hungry; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Which\] make oil within their walls, \[and\] tread \[their\] winepresses, and suffer thirst.<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Men groan<small><small>[^24]</small></small> from out of the city, and the soul of the wounded<small><small>[^25]</small></small> crieth out: yet God<small><small>[^26]</small></small> layeth not folly \[to them\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They are of those that rebel<small><small>[^27]</small></small> against the light; they know<small><small>[^28]</small></small> not the ways thereof, nor abide<small><small>[^29]</small></small> in the paths thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The murderer<small><small>[^30]</small></small> rising with the light killeth the poor and needy, and in the night<small><small>[^31]</small></small> is as a thief. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The eye<small><small>[^32]</small></small> also of the adulterer waiteth for the twilight, saying, No eye<small><small>[^33]</small></small> shall see me: and disguiseth \[his\] face<small><small>[^34]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the dark<small><small>[^35]</small></small> they dig through houses, \[which\] they had marked for themselves in the daytime: they know<small><small>[^36]</small></small> not the light. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the morning \[is\] to them even as the shadow of death: if \[one\] know \[them\], \[they are in\] the terrors<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of the shadow of death. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He \[is\] swift<small><small>[^38]</small></small> as the waters; their portion<small><small>[^39]</small></small> is cursed in the earth: he beholdeth not the way of the vineyards. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Drought<small><small>[^40]</small></small> and heat consume<small><small>[^41]</small></small> the snow waters: \[so doth<small><small>[^42]</small></small>\] the grave \[those which\] have sinned. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The womb shall forget him; the worm<small><small>[^43]</small></small> shall feed sweetly on him; he shall be<small><small>[^44]</small></small> no more remembered; and wickedness<small><small>[^45]</small></small> shall be broken as a tree. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He evil<small><small>[^46]</small></small> entreateth the barren \[that\] beareth not: and doeth not<small><small>[^47]</small></small> good to the widow. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He draweth<small><small>[^48]</small></small> also the mighty with his power: he riseth up, and no \[man\] is sure of life<small><small>[^49]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Though\] it be given<small><small>[^50]</small></small> him \[to be\] in safety, whereon<small><small>[^51]</small></small> he resteth; yet his eyes<small><small>[^52]</small></small> \[are\] upon their ways. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They are exalted<small><small>[^53]</small></small> for a little while, but are gone<small><small>[^54]</small></small> and brought low; they are taken out<small><small>[^55]</small></small> of the way as all \[other\], and cut off<small><small>[^56]</small></small> as the tops of the ears of corn. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.24.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if \[it be\] not \[so\] now, who will make<small><small>[^57]</small></small> me a liar, and make my speech nothing worth? <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Job.23|<< Job 23]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Job.25|Job 25 >>]]</td></tr></table>
