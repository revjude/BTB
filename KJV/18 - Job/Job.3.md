<big><big>[[Job.3.1 Job Laments His Birth|⥃]]</big></big>***Job Laments His Birth***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> After<small><small>[^2]</small></small> this opened<small><small>[^3]</small></small> Job his mouth, and cursed<small><small>[^4]</small></small> his day<small><small>[^5]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Job spake<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, and said, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let the day<small><small>[^7]</small></small> perish wherein I was born, and the night \[in which\] it was said, There is a man child conceived. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let that day be darkness<small><small>[^8]</small></small>; let not God regard<small><small>[^9]</small></small> it from above, neither let the light shine upon it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let darkness and the shadow<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of death stain it<small><small>[^11]</small></small>; let it not be joined unto the days<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of the year, let it not come into the number of the months. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lo, let that night be solitary<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, let no joyful voice come therein. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let them curse it that curse the day, who are ready<small><small>[^16]</small></small> to raise up their mourning<small><small>[^17]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let the stars of the twilight thereof be dark; let it look for light<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, but \[have\] none; neither let it see the dawning of the day<small><small>[^19]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because it shut not<small><small>[^20]</small></small> up the doors of my \[mother’s\] womb, nor hid<small><small>[^21]</small></small> sorrow from mine eyes. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Why died I<small><small>[^22]</small></small> not from the womb? \[why\] did I \[not\] give up the ghost when I came<small><small>[^23]</small></small> out of the belly? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Why did the knees<small><small>[^24]</small></small> prevent me? or why the breasts that I should suck? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For now should I have lain still and been quiet, I should have slept: then had I been at rest<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> With kings<small><small>[^26]</small></small> and counsellors of the earth, which built<small><small>[^27]</small></small> desolate places for themselves; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Or with princes that had gold, who filled their houses<small><small>[^28]</small></small> with silver: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Or as an hidden<small><small>[^29]</small></small> untimely birth I had not been; as infants \[which\] never saw light. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There the wicked<small><small>[^30]</small></small> cease \[from\] troubling; and there the weary<small><small>[^31]</small></small> be at rest<small><small>[^32]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There\] the prisoners rest together; they<small><small>[^33]</small></small> hear not the voice of the oppressor. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The small<small><small>[^34]</small></small> and great are there; and the servant<small><small>[^35]</small></small> \[is\] free from his master. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore<small><small>[^36]</small></small> is light<small><small>[^37]</small></small> given to him that is in misery, and life unto the bitter<small><small>[^38]</small></small> \[in\] soul; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Which long<small><small>[^39]</small></small> for death, but it \[cometh\] not; and dig<small><small>[^40]</small></small> for it more than for hid treasures; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Which rejoice exceedingly, \[and\] are glad, when they can find the grave? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Why is light given\] to a man whose way<small><small>[^41]</small></small> is hid, and whom God hath hedged in<small><small>[^42]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For my sighing<small><small>[^43]</small></small> cometh before I eat<small><small>[^44]</small></small>, and my roarings<small><small>[^45]</small></small> are poured out like the waters. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the thing<small><small>[^46]</small></small> which I greatly feared is come upon me, and that which I was afraid of is come unto me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.3.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I was not in safety, neither had I rest, neither was I quiet;
