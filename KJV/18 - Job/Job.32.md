<big><big>[[Job.32.1 Elihu Rebukes Job’s Friends|⥃]]</big></big>***Elihu Rebukes Job’s Friends***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So these three men ceased to answer<small><small>[^2]</small></small> Job, because he \[was\] righteous<small><small>[^3]</small></small> in his own eyes. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then was kindled<small><small>[^4]</small></small> the wrath of Elihu the son of Barachel the Buzite<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, of the kindred of Ram: against Job was his wrath kindled, because<small><small>[^6]</small></small> he justified himself<small><small>[^7]</small></small> rather than God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also against his three friends was his wrath kindled, because<small><small>[^8]</small></small> they had found no answer, and \[yet<small><small>[^9]</small></small>\] had condemned Job. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now Elihu had waited till Job had spoken<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, because they \[were\] elder<small><small>[^11]</small></small> than he. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When Elihu saw that \[there was\] no answer in the mouth of \[these\] three men, then his wrath<small><small>[^12]</small></small> was kindled. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Elihu the son of Barachel the Buzite answered and said, I \[am<small><small>[^13]</small></small>\] young<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, and ye \[are<small><small>[^15]</small></small>\] very old; wherefore I was afraid, and durst not<small><small>[^16]</small></small> shew you mine opinion. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But \[there is\] a spirit in man: and the inspiration<small><small>[^18]</small></small> of the Almighty giveth them understanding. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Great<small><small>[^19]</small></small> men are not \[always\] wise: to me; I also will shew mine opinion.<small><small>[^21]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, I waited for<small><small>[^22]</small></small> your words; I gave ear to your reasons<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, whilst<small><small>[^24]</small></small> ye searched out what to say<small><small>[^25]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea, I attended unto you<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, and, behold<small><small>[^27]</small></small>, \[there was\] none of you that convinced Job, \[or\] that answered his words: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lest<small><small>[^28]</small></small> ye should say, We<small><small>[^29]</small></small> have found out wisdom: God<small><small>[^30]</small></small> thrusteth him down, not man. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now he hath not directed<small><small>[^31]</small></small> \[his\] words against me: neither will I answer him with your speeches. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They were amazed<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, they answered no more: they left off speaking<small><small>[^33]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When I had waited, (for they spake not, but stood still, \[and\] answered no more;)<small><small>[^34]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[I said\], I will answer also my part, I also will shew mine opinion.<small><small>[^35]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I am full of matter<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, the spirit<small><small>[^37]</small></small> within me<small><small>[^38]</small></small> constraineth me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, my belly \[is\] as wine \[which\] hath no vent<small><small>[^39]</small></small>; it is ready to burst like new<small><small>[^40]</small></small> bottles. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will speak<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, that I may be refreshed<small><small>[^42]</small></small>: I will open<small><small>[^43]</small></small> my lips and answer. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let me not, I pray you, accept<small><small>[^44]</small></small> any man’s person, neither let me give flattering<small><small>[^45]</small></small> titles unto man. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.32.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I know
