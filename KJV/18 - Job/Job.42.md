***Job Submits Himself to the LORD*** ^bsb1291

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.42.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Job answered the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and said, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.42.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I know that thou<small><small>[^2]</small></small> canst do every \[thing\], and \[that\] no<small><small>[^3]</small></small> thought can be withholden from thee<small><small>[^4]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.42.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who<small><small>[^5]</small></small> \[is\] he that hideth counsel without knowledge? therefore have I uttered that I understood not; things<small><small>[^6]</small></small> too wonderful for me, which I knew not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.42.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, I beseech thee, and I will<small><small>[^8]</small></small> speak: I will demand of thee, and declare thou unto me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.42.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have heard<small><small>[^9]</small></small> of thee by the hearing of the ear: but now mine<small><small>[^10]</small></small> eye seeth thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.42.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore I<small><small>[^11]</small></small> abhor \[myself\], and repent<small><small>[^12]</small></small> in dust and ashes.

***The LORD Rebukes Job’s Friends*** ^bsb1292

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.42.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it was \[so\], that after the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had spoken these words unto Job, the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said to Eliphaz<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the Temanite, My<small><small>[^14]</small></small> wrath is kindled against thee, and against thy two friends: for ye have<small><small>[^15]</small></small> not spoken of me \[the thing that is\] right, as my servant Job \[hath\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.42.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore<small><small>[^16]</small></small> take unto you now seven bullocks<small><small>[^17]</small></small> and seven rams, and go<small><small>[^18]</small></small> to my servant Job, and offer<small><small>[^19]</small></small> up for yourselves a burnt offering; and my servant Job shall<small><small>[^20]</small></small> pray for you: for him<small><small>[^21]</small></small> will I accept: lest<small><small>[^22]</small></small> I deal with you \[after your\] folly, in that ye have not spoken of me \[the thing which is\] right, like my servant Job. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Job.42.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So Eliphaz the Temanite and Bildad the Shuhite \[and\] Zophar the Naamathite went, and did<small><small>[^23]</small></small> according as the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> commanded them: the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> also accepted Job<small><small>[^24]</small></small>.
