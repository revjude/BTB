***A Good Name*** ^bsb1470

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A \[good\] name<small><small>[^1]</small></small> \[is\] rather to be chosen than great riches, \[and\] loving favour rather than<small><small>[^2]</small></small> silver and gold. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The rich<small><small>[^3]</small></small> and poor meet together: the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^4]</small></small></span> \[is\] the maker of them all. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A prudent<small><small>[^5]</small></small> \[man\] foreseeth the evil, and hideth himself: but the simple<small><small>[^6]</small></small> pass on, and are punished. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> By<small><small>[^7]</small></small> humility \[and\] the fear of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[are\] riches, and honour, and life. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thorns<small><small>[^8]</small></small> \[and\] snares \[are\] in the<small><small>[^9]</small></small> way of the froward: he that doth keep his soul shall be far from them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Train up<small><small>[^10]</small></small> a child<small><small>[^11]</small></small> in the way<small><small>[^12]</small></small> he should go: and when<small><small>[^13]</small></small> he is old, he will not depart from it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The rich<small><small>[^14]</small></small> ruleth over the poor, and the borrower<small><small>[^15]</small></small> \[is\] servant to the lender<small><small>[^16]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^17]</small></small> soweth iniquity shall reap vanity: and the rod of his anger shall fail<small><small>[^18]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that hath a bountiful eye<small><small>[^19]</small></small> shall be blessed; for he giveth of his bread to the poor. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Cast out the scorner, and contention shall go out; yea, strife and reproach shall cease.<small><small>[^20]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^21]</small></small> loveth pureness of heart, \[for\] the grace of his lips<small><small>[^22]</small></small> the king<small><small>[^23]</small></small> \[shall be\] his friend. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The eyes<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of the<small><small>[^25]</small></small> <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> preserve knowledge, and he overthroweth the words<small><small>[^26]</small></small> of the transgressor. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The slothful<small><small>[^27]</small></small> \[man\] saith, \[There is\] a lion without, I shall be slain in the streets. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The mouth<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of strange women \[is\] a deep pit<small><small>[^29]</small></small>: he that is abhorred<small><small>[^30]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall fall therein. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Foolishness<small><small>[^31]</small></small> \[is\] bound in the heart of a child; \[but<small><small>[^32]</small></small>\] the rod of correction shall drive it far from him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that oppresseth<small><small>[^33]</small></small> the poor to increase his \[riches\], \[and\] he that giveth<small><small>[^34]</small></small> to the rich, \[shall\] surely \[come\] to want.

*Saying 1* ^bsb1471

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Bow<small><small>[^35]</small></small> down thine ear, and hear<small><small>[^36]</small></small> the words of the wise, and apply<small><small>[^37]</small></small> thine heart unto my knowledge. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For \[it is<small><small>[^38]</small></small>\] a pleasant thing if thou keep them within thee<small><small>[^39]</small></small>; they shall withal be fitted<small><small>[^40]</small></small> in thy lips. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That thy<small><small>[^41]</small></small> trust may be in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, I have made known to thee this day, even to thee<small><small>[^42]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Have not I written to thee excellent things in counsels and knowledge,<small><small>[^43]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That I<small><small>[^44]</small></small> might make thee know the certainty of the words of truth; that thou mightest answer<small><small>[^45]</small></small> the words of truth to them that send unto thee<small><small>[^46]</small></small>?

*Saying 2* ^bsb1472

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Rob<small><small>[^47]</small></small> not the poor, because he \[is\] poor: neither oppress<small><small>[^48]</small></small> the afflicted in the gate<small><small>[^49]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^50]</small></small></span> will plead their cause, and spoil<small><small>[^51]</small></small> the soul of those that spoiled them.

*Saying 3* ^bsb1473

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Make no friendship with an angry man; and with a furious man thou shalt not go:<small><small>[^52]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lest thou learn his ways, and get a snare to thy soul.<small><small>[^53]</small></small>

*Saying 4* ^bsb1474

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be not thou \[one\] of them that strike hands, \[or\] of them that are sureties for debts.<small><small>[^54]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If thou hast nothing to pay, why should he take away thy bed from under thee?<small><small>[^55]</small></small>

*Saying 5* ^bsb1475

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.22.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Remove<small><small>[^56]</small></small> not the ancient landmark<small><small>[^57]</small></small>, which thy fathers have set.
