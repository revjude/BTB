<big><big>[[Prov.3.1 Trust in the LORD with All Your Heart|⥃]]</big></big>***Trust in the LORD with All Your Heart***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My son, forget<small><small>[^2]</small></small> not my law; but let<small><small>[^3]</small></small> thine heart keep my commandments: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For length<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of days, and long life<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, and peace<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, shall they add to thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let not mercy<small><small>[^7]</small></small> and truth forsake thee: bind<small><small>[^8]</small></small> them about thy neck; write<small><small>[^9]</small></small> them upon the table of thine heart: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So shalt<small><small>[^10]</small></small> thou find favour and good understanding<small><small>[^11]</small></small> in the sight of God and man. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Trust<small><small>[^12]</small></small> in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> with all thine heart; and<small><small>[^13]</small></small> lean not unto thine own understanding. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In<small><small>[^14]</small></small> all thy ways acknowledge him, and<small><small>[^15]</small></small> he shall direct thy paths. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be<small><small>[^16]</small></small> not wise in thine own eyes: fear<small><small>[^17]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and depart from evil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> It shall<small><small>[^18]</small></small> be health<small><small>[^19]</small></small> to thy<small><small>[^20]</small></small> navel, and marrow<small><small>[^21]</small></small> to thy bones. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Honour the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> with thy substance, and with the firstfruits of all thine increase:<small><small>[^22]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So shall thy barns be filled with plenty, and thy presses shall burst out with new wine.<small><small>[^23]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]My<small><small>[^24]</small></small> son, despise not the chastening of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; neither<small><small>[^25]</small></small> be weary of his correction: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For whom the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> loveth he correcteth; even as a father the son \[in whom\] he delighteth.<small><small>[^26]</small></small>

<big><big>[[Prov.3.13 The Blessings of Wisdom|⥃]]</big></big>***The Blessings of Wisdom***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Happy \[is\] the<small><small>[^27]</small></small> man \[that\] findeth wisdom, and the man \[that\] getteth<small><small>[^28]</small></small> understanding. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the merchandise of it \[is\] better than the merchandise of silver, and the gain thereof than fine gold.<small><small>[^29]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She \[is\] more<small><small>[^30]</small></small> precious than rubies: and all<small><small>[^31]</small></small> the things thou canst desire are not to be compared unto her. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Length<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of days \[is\] in her right hand<small><small>[^33]</small></small>; \[and\] in her left hand riches and honour. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Her ways \[are\] ways of<small><small>[^34]</small></small> pleasantness, and all<small><small>[^35]</small></small> her paths \[are\] peace. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She \[is\] a tree of life to them that lay hold upon her: and happy \[is every one\] that retaineth her.<small><small>[^36]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^37]</small></small></span> by wisdom hath founded the earth; by understanding hath he established<small><small>[^38]</small></small> the heavens. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> By his knowledge the depths<small><small>[^39]</small></small> are broken up, and the clouds<small><small>[^40]</small></small> drop down the dew. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My son, let<small><small>[^41]</small></small> not them depart from thine eyes: keep<small><small>[^42]</small></small> sound wisdom and discretion: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So shall they be life<small><small>[^43]</small></small> unto thy soul, and grace<small><small>[^44]</small></small> to thy neck. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shalt thou walk in thy way safely, and thy foot shall not stumble.<small><small>[^45]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When thou liest<small><small>[^46]</small></small> down, thou shalt not be afraid: yea, thou shalt lie down, and<small><small>[^47]</small></small> thy sleep shall be sweet. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be<small><small>[^48]</small></small> not afraid of sudden fear, neither<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of the desolation of the wicked, when it cometh. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^50]</small></small></span> shall be thy confidence, and shall keep<small><small>[^51]</small></small> thy foot from being taken. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Withhold<small><small>[^52]</small></small> not good from them to whom it is due<small><small>[^53]</small></small>, when it is in the<small><small>[^54]</small></small> power of thine hand to do \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Say not unto thy neighbour, Go, and come again, and to morrow I will give; when thou hast it by thee.<small><small>[^55]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Devise not evil<small><small>[^56]</small></small> against thy neighbour, seeing he dwelleth securely by thee. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Strive not with a man without cause, if he have done thee no harm.<small><small>[^57]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Envy<small><small>[^58]</small></small> thou not the oppressor<small><small>[^59]</small></small>, and choose<small><small>[^60]</small></small> none of his ways. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.3.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the froward<small><small>[^61]</small></small> \[is\] abomination to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: but his<small><small>[^62]</small></small> secret \[is\] with the righteous.
