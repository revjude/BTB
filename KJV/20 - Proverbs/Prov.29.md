***The Flourishing of the Righteous*** ^bsb1506

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He, that being often reproved<small><small>[^2]</small></small> hardeneth<small><small>[^3]</small></small> \[his\] neck, shall<small><small>[^4]</small></small> suddenly be destroyed, and that without remedy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When the righteous<small><small>[^5]</small></small> are in authority<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, the people rejoice: but when the wicked<small><small>[^7]</small></small> beareth rule, the people mourn. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso loveth<small><small>[^8]</small></small> wisdom rejoiceth his fathe<small><small>[^9]</small></small>r: but he that keepeth company with harlots spendeth \[his\] substance. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The king<small><small>[^10]</small></small> by judgment establisheth the land: but he that receiveth gifts<small><small>[^11]</small></small> overthroweth it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A man that<small><small>[^12]</small></small> flattereth his neighbour spreadeth<small><small>[^13]</small></small> a net for his feet. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the transgression<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of an evil man \[there is\] a snare: but<small><small>[^15]</small></small> the righteous doth sing and rejoice. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The righteous considereth<small><small>[^16]</small></small> the cause of the poor: \[but<small><small>[^17]</small></small>\] the wicked regardeth not to know \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Scornful<small><small>[^18]</small></small> men bring a city into a snare<small><small>[^19]</small></small>: but wise<small><small>[^20]</small></small> \[men\] turn away wrath. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[If\] a wise man contendeth with a foolish man, whether he rage or laugh, \[there is\] no rest.<small><small>[^21]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The bloodthirsty<small><small>[^22]</small></small> hate the upright: but<small><small>[^23]</small></small> the just seek his soul. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A fool uttereth all his mind: but a wise \[man\] keepeth it in till afterwards.<small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If a ruler hearken to lies, all his servants \[are\] wicked.<small><small>[^25]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The poor and the deceitful man<small><small>[^26]</small></small> meet<small><small>[^27]</small></small> together: the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^28]</small></small></span> lighteneth both their eyes. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The king<small><small>[^29]</small></small> that faithfully judgeth the poor, his throne<small><small>[^30]</small></small> shall be established for ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The rod and reproof give wisdom: but a child<small><small>[^31]</small></small> left \[to himself\] bringeth his mother to shame.<small><small>[^32]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When the wicked<small><small>[^33]</small></small> are multiplied, transgression increaseth: but<small><small>[^34]</small></small> the righteous shall see their fall. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Correct thy son, and he shall give thee rest; yea, he shall give delight unto thy soul.<small><small>[^35]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Where \[there<small><small>[^36]</small></small> is\] no vision, the people perish<small><small>[^37]</small></small>: but<small><small>[^38]</small></small> he that keepeth the law, happy \[is\] he. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A servant<small><small>[^39]</small></small> will not be corrected by words: for though<small><small>[^40]</small></small> he understand he will not answer. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Seest<small><small>[^41]</small></small> thou a man \[that is\] hasty in his words<small><small>[^42]</small></small>? \[there is\] more<small><small>[^43]</small></small> hope of a fool than of him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that delicately bringeth up his servant from a child shall have him become \[his\] son at the length. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> An angry<small><small>[^44]</small></small> man stirreth up strife, and a furious<small><small>[^45]</small></small> man aboundeth in transgression. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A man’s pride shall bring him low: but honour<small><small>[^46]</small></small> shall uphold the humble in spirit.<small><small>[^47]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso is partner<small><small>[^48]</small></small> with a thief hateth<small><small>[^49]</small></small> his own soul: he<small><small>[^50]</small></small> heareth cursing, and bewrayeth \[it\] not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The fear<small><small>[^51]</small></small> of man bringeth a snare: but whoso<small><small>[^52]</small></small> putteth his trust in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall be safe<small><small>[^53]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Many seek<small><small>[^54]</small></small> the ruler’s favour; but \[every\] man’s judgment \[cometh\] from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^55]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.29.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> An unjust man \[is\] an abomination to the just: and \[he that is\] upright in the way \[is\] abomination to the wicked. <small><small>[^56]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td
