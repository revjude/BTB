***Dishonest Scales***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Deut.25|Deuteronomy 25:13–16]] <big>†</big> [[Ezek.45|Ezekiel 45:10–12]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1459

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A false balance \[is<small><small>[^1]</small></small>\] abomination to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: but a just weight<small><small>[^2]</small></small> \[is\] his delight. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[When\] pride<small><small>[^3]</small></small> cometh, then cometh shame: but<small><small>[^4]</small></small> with the lowly \[is\] wisdom. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The integrity<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of the upright shall guide them: but the perverseness<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of transgressors shall destroy them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Riches<small><small>[^7]</small></small> profit not in the day of wrath: but<small><small>[^8]</small></small> righteousness delivereth from death. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The righteousness of the perfect shall direct<small><small>[^9]</small></small> his way: but the wicked shall fall by his own wickedness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The righteousness<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of the upright shall deliver them: but<small><small>[^11]</small></small> transgressors shall be taken in \[their own\] naughtiness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When a wicked man dieth, \[his\] expectation shall perish: and the hope of unjust \[men\] perisheth.<small><small>[^12]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The righteous is delivered out of trouble, and the wicked cometh in his stead.<small><small>[^13]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> An hypocrite<small><small>[^14]</small></small> with \[his\] mouth destroyeth his neighbour: but through<small><small>[^15]</small></small> knowledge shall the just be delivered. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When it goeth<small><small>[^16]</small></small> well with the righteous, the city rejoiceth: and when<small><small>[^17]</small></small> the wicked perish, \[there is\] shouting. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> By the blessing<small><small>[^18]</small></small> of the upright the cit<small><small>[^19]</small></small>y is exalted: but it is overthrown by the mouth of the wicked. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^20]</small></small> is void of wisdom<small><small>[^21]</small></small> despiseth his neighbour: but a man<small><small>[^22]</small></small> of understanding holdeth his peace. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A talebearer revealeth<small><small>[^23]</small></small> secrets: but he<small><small>[^24]</small></small> that is of a faithful spirit concealeth the matter.<small><small>[^25]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Where no counsel \[is\], the people fall: but in the multitude of counsellors \[there is\] safety.<small><small>[^26]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that is surety<small><small>[^27]</small></small> for a stranger shall smart<small><small>[^28]</small></small> \[for it\]: and he that hateth suretiship<small><small>[^29]</small></small> is sure. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A gracious<small><small>[^30]</small></small> woman retaineth honour: and<small><small>[^31]</small></small> strong \[men\] retain riches. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The merciful<small><small>[^32]</small></small> man doeth good to his own soul: but<small><small>[^33]</small></small> \[he that is\] cruel troubleth his own flesh. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wicked<small><small>[^34]</small></small> worketh a deceitful work: but<small><small>[^35]</small></small> to him that soweth righteousness \[shall be\] a sure reward. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As righteousness<small><small>[^36]</small></small> \[tendeth\] to life: so he<small><small>[^37]</small></small> that pursueth evil \[pursueth it\] to his own death. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They that are of<small><small>[^38]</small></small> a froward heart \[are\] abomination to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: but \[such as are\] upright<small><small>[^39]</small></small> in \[their\] way \[are\] his delight. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Though\] hand<small><small>[^40]</small></small> \[join\] in hand, the wicked shall not be unpunished: but the seed<small><small>[^41]</small></small> of the righteous shall be delivered. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[As\] a jewel<small><small>[^42]</small></small> of gold in a swine’s snout, \[so is\] a fair woman which is without<small><small>[^43]</small></small> discretion. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The desire<small><small>[^44]</small></small> of the righteous \[is\] only good: \[but\] the expectation<small><small>[^45]</small></small> of the wicked \[is\] wrath. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There is that scattereth<small><small>[^46]</small></small>, and yet increaseth; and \[there is\] that withholdeth more than is meet, but<small><small>[^47]</small></small> \[it tendeth\] to poverty. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The liberal soul<small><small>[^48]</small></small> shall be made fat: and he that watereth shall be watered also himself. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that withholdeth<small><small>[^49]</small></small> corn, the people shall curse him: but blessing<small><small>[^50]</small></small> \[shall be\] upon the head of him that selleth \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that diligently<small><small>[^51]</small></small> seeketh good procureth favour: but he that seeketh<small><small>[^52]</small></small> mischief, it shall come unto him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^53]</small></small> trusteth in his riches shall fall: but<small><small>[^54]</small></small> the righteous shall flourish as a branch. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^55]</small></small> troubleth his own house shall inherit<small><small>[^56]</small></small> the wind: and the fool \[shall be\] servant to the wise of heart. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The fruit<small><small>[^57]</small></small> of the righteous \[is\] a tree of life; and<small><small>[^58]</small></small> he that winneth<small><small>[^59]</small></small> souls \[is\] wise. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.11.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, the righteous shall be recompensed in the earth: much more the wicked and the sinner. <small><small>[^60]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td
