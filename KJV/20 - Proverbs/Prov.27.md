***Do Not Boast about Tomorrow***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Jas.4|James 4:13–17]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1504

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Boast<small><small>[^2]</small></small> not thyself of to morrow<small><small>[^3]</small></small>; for thou<small><small>[^4]</small></small> knowest not what a day may bring forth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let another man praise thee, and not thine own mouth; a stranger, and not thine own lips.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A stone \[is\] heavy<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, and the sand weighty; but<small><small>[^7]</small></small> a fool’s wrath \[is\] heavier than them both. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wrath \[is\] cruel, and anger \[is\] outrageous<small><small>[^8]</small></small>; but<small><small>[^9]</small></small> who \[is\] able to stand before envy<small><small>[^10]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Open rebuke \[is\] better than secret love.<small><small>[^11]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Faithful \[are\] the wounds<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of a friend; but the kisses<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of an enemy \[are\] deceitful<small><small>[^14]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The full<small><small>[^15]</small></small> soul loatheth<small><small>[^16]</small></small> an honeycomb; but to<small><small>[^17]</small></small> the hungry soul every bitter thing is sweet. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As a bird<small><small>[^18]</small></small> that wandereth from her nest, so \[is\] a man<small><small>[^19]</small></small> that wandereth from his place. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ointment<small><small>[^20]</small></small> and perfume rejoice the heart: so<small><small>[^21]</small></small> \[doth\] the sweetness of a man’s friend by hearty counsel<small><small>[^22]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thine own<small><small>[^23]</small></small> friend, and thy father’s friend, forsake not; neither<small><small>[^24]</small></small> go into thy brother’s house in the day of thy calamity: \[for\] better<small><small>[^25]</small></small> \[is\] a neighbour \[that is\] near than a brother far off. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My son, be wise<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, and make my heart glad, that I<small><small>[^27]</small></small> may answer him that reproacheth me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A prudent \[man\] foreseeth the evil, \[and\] hideth himself; \[but\] the simple pass on, \[and\] are punished.<small><small>[^28]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Take his garment that is surety for a stranger, and take a pledge of him for a strange woman.<small><small>[^29]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^30]</small></small> blesseth his friend with a loud voice, rising early in the morning, it shall be counted a curse to him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A continual<small><small>[^31]</small></small> dropping in a very rainy day and a contentious woman are alike. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whosoever hideth her hideth the wind, and the ointment<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of his right hand, \[which\] bewrayeth \[itself\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Iron<small><small>[^33]</small></small> sharpeneth iron; so<small><small>[^34]</small></small> a man sharpeneth the countenance of his friend. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso keepeth<small><small>[^35]</small></small> the fig tree shall eat the fruit thereof: so<small><small>[^36]</small></small> he that waiteth on his master shall be<small><small>[^37]</small></small> honoured. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As in<small><small>[^38]</small></small> water face \[answereth\] to face, so<small><small>[^39]</small></small> the heart of man to man. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hell<small><small>[^40]</small></small> and destruction are never<small><small>[^41]</small></small> full; so<small><small>[^42]</small></small> the eyes of man are never satisfied. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[As\] the fining<small><small>[^43]</small></small> pot for silver, and the furnace for gold; so<small><small>[^44]</small></small> \[is\] a man to his praise. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Though thou shouldest bray a fool in a mortar among wheat with a pestle, \[yet\] will not his foolishness depart from him.<small><small>[^45]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be thou diligent<small><small>[^46]</small></small> to know the state of thy flocks, \[and\] look well<small><small>[^47]</small></small> to thy herds. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^48]</small></small> riches<small><small>[^49]</small></small> \[are\] not for ever: and doth<small><small>[^50]</small></small> the crown \[endure\] to every generation<small><small>[^51]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The hay<small><small>[^52]</small></small> appeareth, and the tender grass sheweth itself, and herbs of the mountains are gathered. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The lambs \[are\] for thy clothing, and the goats \[are\] the price of the field.<small><small>[^53]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.27.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And \[thou shalt have\] goats’ milk enough<small><small>[^54]</small></small> for thy food, for the food of thy household, and \[for\] the maintenance<small><small>[^55]</small></small> for thy maidens. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Prov.26|<< Proverbs 26]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Prov.28|Proverbs 28 >>]]</td></tr></table>
