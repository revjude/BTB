<big><big>[[Prov.13.1 A Father’s Discipline|⥃]]</big></big>***A Father’s Discipline***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A wise<small><small>[^1]</small></small> son \[heareth\] his father’s instruction: but<small><small>[^2]</small></small> a scorner heareth not rebuke. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A man shall eat<small><small>[^3]</small></small> good by the fruit of \[his\] mouth: but the soul<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of the transgressors \[shall eat\] violence. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that keepeth his mouth keepeth his life: \[but\] he that openeth wide his lips shall have destruction.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The soul of the sluggard desireth<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, and \[hath\] nothing: but<small><small>[^7]</small></small> the soul of the diligent shall be made<small><small>[^8]</small></small> fat. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A righteous<small><small>[^9]</small></small> \[man\] hateth lying: but a wicked \[man\] is<small><small>[^10]</small></small> loathsome, and<small><small>[^11]</small></small> cometh to shame. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Righteousness<small><small>[^12]</small></small> keepeth \[him that is\] upright in the way: but wickedness<small><small>[^13]</small></small> overthroweth the sinner<small><small>[^14]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There is that maketh himself rich<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, yet \[hath\] nothing: \[there is\] that maketh himself poor<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, yet \[hath\] great riches. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The ransom<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of a man’s life \[are\] his riches: but the poor<small><small>[^18]</small></small> heareth not rebuke. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The light<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of the righteous rejoiceth: but the lamp<small><small>[^20]</small></small> of the wicked shall be put out. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Only<small><small>[^21]</small></small> by pride cometh contention: but with<small><small>[^22]</small></small> the well advised \[is\] wisdom. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wealth<small><small>[^23]</small></small> \[gotten\] by vanity shall be diminishe<small><small>[^24]</small></small>d: but he that gathereth by labour<small><small>[^25]</small></small> shall increase. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hope<small><small>[^26]</small></small> deferred maketh the heart sick: but \[when<small><small>[^27]</small></small>\] the desire cometh, \[it is\] a tree<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of life. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso despiseth<small><small>[^29]</small></small> the<small><small>[^30]</small></small> word shall be destroyed: but he that feareth the commandment shall be rewarded<small><small>[^31]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The law<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of the wise \[is\] a fountain of life, to<small><small>[^33]</small></small> depart from the snares of death. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Good<small><small>[^34]</small></small> understanding giveth favour: but<small><small>[^35]</small></small> the way of transgressors \[is\] hard. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Every prudent<small><small>[^36]</small></small> \[man\] dealeth with knowledge: but a fool<small><small>[^37]</small></small> layeth<small><small>[^38]</small></small> open \[his\] folly. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A wicked<small><small>[^39]</small></small> messenger falleth into mischief: but<small><small>[^40]</small></small> a faithful ambassador<small><small>[^41]</small></small> \[is\] health. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Poverty<small><small>[^42]</small></small> and shame \[shall be to\] him that refuseth instruction: but he<small><small>[^43]</small></small> that regardeth reproof shall be honoured. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The desire<small><small>[^44]</small></small> accomplished is sweet to the soul: but \[it is<small><small>[^45]</small></small>\] abomination to fools to depart<small><small>[^46]</small></small> from evil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^47]</small></small> walketh with wise \[men\] shall be wise: but<small><small>[^48]</small></small> a companion of fools shall be destroyed<small><small>[^49]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Evil pursueth<small><small>[^50]</small></small> sinners: but to the righteous<small><small>[^51]</small></small> good shall be repayed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A good \[man\] leaveth<small><small>[^52]</small></small> an inheritance to his children’s children: and the wealth<small><small>[^53]</small></small> of the sinner \[is\] laid up for the just. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Much food<small><small>[^54]</small></small> \[is in\] the tillage of the poor: but there is \[that is\] destroyed<small><small>[^55]</small></small> for want of judgment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that spareth his rod hateth his son: but he that loveth him chasteneth him betimes.<small><small>[^56]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.13.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The righteous<small><small>[^57]</small></small> eateth to the satisfying of his soul: but the belly<small><small>[^58]</small></small> of the wicked shall want. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%"
