***Avoiding Immorality***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Lev.20|Leviticus 20:10–21]] <big>†</big> [[1Cor.5|1 Corinthians 5:1–8]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1451

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My son, attend<small><small>[^2]</small></small> unto my wisdom, \[and\] bow<small><small>[^3]</small></small> thine ear to my understanding: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That thou mayest regard discretion, and \[that\] thy lips<small><small>[^4]</small></small> may keep knowledge. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]For the lips<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of a strange woman drop \[as\] an honeycomb, and her mouth<small><small>[^6]</small></small> \[is\] smoother<small><small>[^7]</small></small> than oil: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But her<small><small>[^8]</small></small> end is bitter as wormwood, sharp<small><small>[^9]</small></small> as a twoedged sword. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Her feet go down to death; her steps take hold on hell.<small><small>[^10]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lest thou shouldest ponder<small><small>[^11]</small></small> the path<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of life, her<small><small>[^13]</small></small> ways are moveable, \[that\] thou canst not know \[them\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear<small><small>[^14]</small></small> me now therefore, O ye children, and depart<small><small>[^15]</small></small> not from the words of my mouth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Remove thy way far from her, and come not nigh the door of her house:<small><small>[^16]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lest thou give thine honour unto others, and thy years unto the cruel:<small><small>[^17]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lest strangers<small><small>[^18]</small></small> be filled with thy wealth<small><small>[^19]</small></small>; and thy labours \[be\] in the house of a stranger; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou<small><small>[^20]</small></small> mourn at the last, when<small><small>[^21]</small></small> thy flesh and thy body are consumed, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And say, How<small><small>[^22]</small></small> have I hated instruction, and my<small><small>[^23]</small></small> heart despised reproof; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And have not obeyed the voice of my teachers, nor inclined mine ear to them that instructed me!<small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I was almost in all evil in the midst of the congregation and assembly.<small><small>[^25]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Drink waters out of thine own cistern, and running waters out of thine own well.<small><small>[^26]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let thy<small><small>[^27]</small></small> fountains be dispersed<small><small>[^28]</small></small> abroad, \[and\] rivers of waters in the streets. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let them be only thine own, and not strangers’ with thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let thy fountain be blessed: and rejoice<small><small>[^29]</small></small> with the wife of thy youth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Let her be as\] the<small><small>[^30]</small></small> loving hind and pleasant roe; let her breasts satisfy thee<small><small>[^31]</small></small> at all times; and be thou ravished always with her love<small><small>[^32]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And why wilt thou, my son, be ravished with<small><small>[^33]</small></small> a strange woman, and embrace the bosom of a stranger? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the ways of man \[are\] before the eyes of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and he pondereth all his goings.<small><small>[^34]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]His<small><small>[^35]</small></small> own iniquities shall take the wicked himself, and he shall be holden<small><small>[^36]</small></small> with the cords of his sins<small><small>[^37]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.5.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He shall die<small><small>[^38]</small></small> without instruction; and in the<small><small>[^39]</small></small> greatness of his folly he shall go astray. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Prov.4|<< Proverbs 4]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Prov.6|Proverbs 6 >>]]</td></tr></table>
