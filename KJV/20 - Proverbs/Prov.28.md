***The Boldness of the Righteous*** ^bsb1505

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wicked<small><small>[^2]</small></small> flee when no man pursueth: but the righteous<small><small>[^3]</small></small> are bold as a lion. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the transgression<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of a land many \[are\] the princes thereof: but<small><small>[^5]</small></small> by a man<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of understanding \[and\] knowledge the state \[thereof\] shall be prolonged. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A poor man<small><small>[^7]</small></small> that oppresseth the poor \[is like\] a sweeping rain which leaveth no food<small><small>[^8]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They that<small><small>[^9]</small></small> forsake the law praise the wicked: but<small><small>[^10]</small></small> such as keep the law contend with them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Evil men understand not judgment: but they that seek the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> understand all \[things\].<small><small>[^11]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Better \[is\] the poor that walketh in his uprightness, than \[he that is\] perverse \[in his\] ways, though he \[be\] rich.<small><small>[^12]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso keepeth<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the law \[is\] a wise son: but<small><small>[^14]</small></small> he that is a companion of riotous \[men<small><small>[^15]</small></small>\] shameth his father. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that by<small><small>[^16]</small></small> usury and unjust gain<small><small>[^17]</small></small> increaseth his substance, he shall gather it for him that will pity<small><small>[^18]</small></small> the poor. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that turneth<small><small>[^19]</small></small> away his ear from hearing the law, even<small><small>[^20]</small></small> his prayer \[shall be\] abomination. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso causeth<small><small>[^21]</small></small> the righteous to go astray in an evil way, he shall<small><small>[^22]</small></small> fall himself into his own pit: but<small><small>[^23]</small></small> the upright shall have good \[things\] in possession. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The rich<small><small>[^24]</small></small> man \[is\] wise in his own conceit<small><small>[^25]</small></small>; but the poor<small><small>[^26]</small></small> that hath understanding searcheth him out. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When righteous<small><small>[^27]</small></small> \[men\] do rejoice, \[there is\] great glory: but<small><small>[^28]</small></small> when the wicked rise, a man is hidden<small><small>[^29]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^30]</small></small> covereth his sins shall not prosper: but whoso<small><small>[^31]</small></small> confesseth and forsaketh<small><small>[^32]</small></small> \[them\] shall have mercy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Happy<small><small>[^33]</small></small> \[is\] the man that feareth alway: but<small><small>[^34]</small></small> he that hardeneth his heart shall fall into mischief. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[As\] a roaring<small><small>[^35]</small></small> lion, and a ranging<small><small>[^36]</small></small> bear; \[so<small><small>[^37]</small></small> is\] a wicked ruler over the poor people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The prince<small><small>[^38]</small></small> that wanteth understanding \[is\] also a great oppressor: \[but\] he that<small><small>[^39]</small></small> hateth covetousness shall prolong \[his\] days. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A man that doeth violence to the blood of \[any\] person shall flee to the pit; let no man stay him.<small><small>[^40]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso walketh<small><small>[^41]</small></small> uprightly shall be saved: but<small><small>[^42]</small></small> \[he that is\] perverse \[in his\] ways shall fall at once. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that tilleth<small><small>[^43]</small></small> his land shall have plenty of bread: but<small><small>[^44]</small></small> he that followeth after vain \[persons\] shall have poverty enough. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A faithful<small><small>[^45]</small></small> man shall abound with blessings: but<small><small>[^46]</small></small> he that maketh haste to be rich shall not be innocent<small><small>[^47]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To have respect<small><small>[^48]</small></small> of persons \[is\] not good: for<small><small>[^49]</small></small> for a piece of bread \[that\] man will transgress. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that hasteth<small><small>[^50]</small></small> to be rich \[hath\] an evil<small><small>[^51]</small></small> eye, and<small><small>[^52]</small></small> considereth not that poverty shall come upon him.<small><small>[^53]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that rebuketh a man afterwards shall find more favour than he that flattereth with the tongue.<small><small>[^54]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso robbeth<small><small>[^55]</small></small> his father or his mother, and saith, \[It is\] no transgression; the same<small><small>[^56]</small></small> \[is\] the companion of a destroyer<small><small>[^57]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that is<small><small>[^58]</small></small> of a proud heart stirreth up strife: but he that putteth<small><small>[^59]</small></small> his trust in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall be made<small><small>[^60]</small></small> fat. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^61]</small></small> trusteth in his own heart is a fool: but<small><small>[^62]</small></small> whoso walketh wisely, he shall be delivered. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that giveth<small><small>[^63]</small></small> unto the poor shall not lack: but he that hideth<small><small>[^64]</small></small> his eyes shall<small><small>[^65]</small></small> have many a curse. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.28.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When the wicked<small><small>[^66]</small></small> rise, men hide<small><small>[^67]</small></small> themselves: but when they perish<small><small>[^68]</small></small>, the righteous increase. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%"
