***The King’s Heart***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Ps.21|Psalms 21:1–13]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1469

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The king’s<small><small>[^1]</small></small> heart \[is\] in the hand of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[as<small><small>[^2]</small></small>\] the rivers of water: he turneth it whithersoever he will. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Every way of a man \[is\] right<small><small>[^3]</small></small> in his own eyes: but the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^4]</small></small></span> pondereth the hearts. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To do justice and judgment \[is\] more acceptable to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> than sacrifice.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> An high look<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, and a proud heart, \[and\] the<small><small>[^7]</small></small> plowing of the wicked<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, \[is\] sin. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The thoughts<small><small>[^9]</small></small> of the diligent \[tend\] only to plenteousness; but of every<small><small>[^10]</small></small> one \[that is\] hasty only to want. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The getting<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of treasures by a lying tongue \[is\] a vanity tossed to and fro of them that seek<small><small>[^12]</small></small> death. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The robbery<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of the wicked shall destroy them<small><small>[^14]</small></small>; because<small><small>[^15]</small></small> they refuse to do judgment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The way<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of man \[is\] froward and strange: but<small><small>[^17]</small></small> \[as for\] the pure, his work \[is\] right. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[It is\] better<small><small>[^18]</small></small> to dwell in a corner of the housetop, than with a brawling woman in a wide house<small><small>[^19]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The soul<small><small>[^20]</small></small> of the wicked desireth evil: his neighbour findeth no favour<small><small>[^21]</small></small> in his eyes. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When the scorner<small><small>[^22]</small></small> is punished, the simple is made wise: and when the wise<small><small>[^23]</small></small> is instructed, he receiveth knowledge. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The righteous \[man\] wisely<small><small>[^24]</small></small> considereth the house of the wicked: \[but God\] overthroweth<small><small>[^25]</small></small> the wicked for \[their\] wickedness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso stoppeth<small><small>[^26]</small></small> his ears at<small><small>[^27]</small></small> the cry of the poor, he also shall cry himself<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, but shall not be heard. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A gift<small><small>[^29]</small></small> in secret<small><small>[^30]</small></small> pacifieth anger: and a reward in the bosom strong wrath. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[It is\] joy<small><small>[^31]</small></small> to the just to do judgment: but destruction<small><small>[^32]</small></small> \[shall be\] to the workers of iniquity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The man that wandereth<small><small>[^33]</small></small> out of the way of understanding shall remain<small><small>[^34]</small></small> in the congregation of the dead. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that loveth<small><small>[^35]</small></small> pleasure<small><small>[^36]</small></small> \[shall be\] a poor man: he that loveth wine and oil shall not be rich. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wicked<small><small>[^37]</small></small> \[shall be\] a ransom for the righteous, and the transgressor for the upright. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[It is\] better<small><small>[^38]</small></small> to dwell in the wilderness<small><small>[^39]</small></small>, than with a contentious and an angry woman. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] treasure<small><small>[^40]</small></small> to be desired and oil<small><small>[^41]</small></small> in the dwelling of the wise; but<small><small>[^42]</small></small> a foolish man spendeth it up. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^43]</small></small> followeth after righteousness and mercy findeth<small><small>[^44]</small></small> life, righteousness, and honour. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A wise \[man\] scaleth the city of the mighty, and casteth down the strength of the confidence thereof.<small><small>[^45]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso keepeth his mouth and his tongue keepeth his soul from troubles.<small><small>[^46]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Proud \[and\] haughty<small><small>[^47]</small></small> scorner \[is\] his name, who dealeth in proud wrath<small><small>[^48]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The desire of the slothful killeth him; for his hands refuse to labour.<small><small>[^49]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He coveteth<small><small>[^50]</small></small> greedily all the day long: but the righteous<small><small>[^51]</small></small> giveth and spareth not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The sacrifice<small><small>[^52]</small></small> of the wicked \[is\] abomination: how much more, \[when\] he bringeth it with a wicked mind<small><small>[^53]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A false witness<small><small>[^54]</small></small> shall perish: but the man<small><small>[^55]</small></small> that heareth speaketh constantly. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A wicked man hardeneth<small><small>[^56]</small></small> his face: but \[as for\] the upright, he directeth<small><small>[^57]</small></small> his way. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] no wisdom nor understanding nor counsel against the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^58]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.21.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The horse<small><small>[^59]</small></small> \[is\] prepared against the day of battle: but<small><small>[^60]</small></small> safety<small><small>[^61]</small></small> \[is\] of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Prov.20|<< Proverbs 20]]</td><td align="center"
