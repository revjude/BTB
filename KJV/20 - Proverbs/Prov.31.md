***The Sayings for King Lemuel*** ^bsb1508

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The words of king Lemuel, the prophecy<small><small>[^2]</small></small> that his<small><small>[^3]</small></small> mother taught him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What, my son? and what, the son of my womb<small><small>[^4]</small></small>? and what, the son of my vows<small><small>[^5]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Give not thy strength<small><small>[^6]</small></small> unto women, nor thy ways to that<small><small>[^7]</small></small> which destroyeth kings. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[It is\] not for kings, O Lemuel, \[it is\] not for kings to drink wine; nor for princes strong drink:<small><small>[^8]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lest they drink, and forget the law, and pervert<small><small>[^9]</small></small> the judgment of any of the afflicted<small><small>[^10]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Give strong<small><small>[^11]</small></small> drink unto him that is ready to perish, and wine unto those that be of heavy hearts<small><small>[^12]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let him drink, and forget his poverty, and remember his misery no more.<small><small>[^13]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Open<small><small>[^14]</small></small> thy mouth for the dumb in the cause of all such<small><small>[^15]</small></small> as are appointed to destruction. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Open thy mouth, judge righteously, and plead the cause of the poor and needy.<small><small>[^16]</small></small>

***The Virtues of a Noble Woman*** ^bsb1509

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who<small><small>[^17]</small></small> can<small><small>[^18]</small></small> find a virtuous woman? for her<small><small>[^19]</small></small> price \[is\] far above rubies. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The heart of her husband doth safely trust in her, so that he shall have no need of spoil.<small><small>[^20]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She will do him good and not evil all the days of her life.<small><small>[^21]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She seeketh wool, and flax, and worketh<small><small>[^22]</small></small> willingly with her hands. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She is like the merchants’ ships; she bringeth her food from afar.<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She riseth<small><small>[^24]</small></small> also while it is yet night, and giveth<small><small>[^25]</small></small> meat to her household, and a portion to her maidens. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She considereth<small><small>[^26]</small></small> a field, and buyeth<small><small>[^27]</small></small> it: with the fruit of her hands she planteth a vineyard. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She girdeth<small><small>[^28]</small></small> her loins with strength, and strengtheneth<small><small>[^29]</small></small> her arms. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She perceiveth<small><small>[^30]</small></small> that her merchandise \[is\] good: her candle<small><small>[^31]</small></small> goeth not out by night. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She layeth her hands to the spindle, and her hands hold the distaff.<small><small>[^32]</small></small><small><small>[^33]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She stretcheth<small><small>[^34]</small></small> out her hand to the poor; yea, she reacheth<small><small>[^35]</small></small> forth her hands to the needy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She is not afraid<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of the snow for her household: for all her household \[are\] clothed with scarlet<small><small>[^37]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She maketh herself coverings<small><small>[^38]</small></small> of tapestry; her clothing<small><small>[^39]</small></small> \[is\] silk<small><small>[^40]</small></small> and purple. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Her husband<small><small>[^41]</small></small> is known in the<small><small>[^42]</small></small> gates, when he sitteth among the elders of the land. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She maketh fine linen, and selleth \[it\]; and delivereth girdles unto the merchant.<small><small>[^43]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Strength<small><small>[^44]</small></small> and honour \[are\] her clothing; and she<small><small>[^45]</small></small> shall rejoice in time to come. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She openeth<small><small>[^46]</small></small> her mouth with wisdom; and in her<small><small>[^47]</small></small> tongue \[is\] the law of kindness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She looketh well to the ways of her household, and eateth not the bread of idleness.<small><small>[^48]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Her children<small><small>[^49]</small></small> arise up, and call her blessed; her husband<small><small>[^50]</small></small> \[also\], and he praiseth her. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Many daughters have done virtuously<small><small>[^51]</small></small>, but thou<small><small>[^52]</small></small> excellest them all. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Favour<small><small>[^53]</small></small> \[is\] deceitful, and beauty \[is\] vain: \[but\] a woman<small><small>[^54]</small></small> \[that\] feareth the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, she<small><small>[^55]</small></small> shall be praised. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.31.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Give her of the<small><small>[^56]</small></small> fruit of her hands;
