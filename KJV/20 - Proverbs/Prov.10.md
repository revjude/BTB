<big><big>[[Prov.10.1 Solomon’s Proverbs：The Wise Son|⥃]]</big></big>***Solomon’s Proverbs: The Wise Son***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The proverbs<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of Solomon. A wise<small><small>[^3]</small></small> son maketh a glad father: but a foolish son \[is\] the heaviness of his mother. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Treasures<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of wickedness profit nothing: but<small><small>[^5]</small></small> righteousness delivereth from death. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> will<small><small>[^6]</small></small> not suffer the soul of the righteous to famish: but<small><small>[^7]</small></small> he casteth away the substance of the wicked<small><small>[^8]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He becometh<small><small>[^9]</small></small> poor that dealeth \[with\] a slack hand<small><small>[^10]</small></small>: but<small><small>[^11]</small></small> the hand of the diligent maketh rich. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that gathereth<small><small>[^12]</small></small> in summer \[is\] a wise son: \[but\] he that sleepeth in harvest \[is\] a son<small><small>[^13]</small></small> that causeth shame. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Blessings<small><small>[^14]</small></small> \[are\] upon the head of the just: but violence<small><small>[^15]</small></small> covereth the mouth of the wicked. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The memory<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of the just \[is\] blessed: but the name<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of the wicked shall rot. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wise<small><small>[^18]</small></small> in heart will receive commandments: but<small><small>[^19]</small></small> a prating fool<small><small>[^20]</small></small> shall fall<small><small>[^21]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that walketh<small><small>[^22]</small></small> uprightly walketh surely: but<small><small>[^23]</small></small> he that perverteth his ways shall be known. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^24]</small></small> winketh with the eye causeth sorrow: but<small><small>[^25]</small></small> a prating fool shall fall<small><small>[^26]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The mouth of a<small><small>[^27]</small></small> righteous \[man is\] a well of life: but<small><small>[^28]</small></small> violence covereth the mouth of the wicked. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hatred<small><small>[^29]</small></small> stirreth up strifes: but love<small><small>[^30]</small></small> covereth all sins. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the lips<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of him that hath understanding wisdom is found: but a rod<small><small>[^32]</small></small> \[is\] for the back of him that is void of understanding<small><small>[^33]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wise \[men\] lay<small><small>[^34]</small></small> up knowledge: but the mouth<small><small>[^35]</small></small> of the foolish \[is\] near destruction. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The rich<small><small>[^36]</small></small> man’s wealth \[is\] his strong city: the destruction<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of the poor \[is\] their poverty. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The labour<small><small>[^38]</small></small> of the righteous \[tendeth\] to life: the fruit<small><small>[^39]</small></small> of the wicked to sin. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He \[is in\] the way<small><small>[^40]</small></small> of life that keepeth instruction: but he that<small><small>[^41]</small></small> refuseth reproof erreth<small><small>[^42]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that hideth<small><small>[^43]</small></small> hatred \[with\] lying lips, and he that uttereth<small><small>[^44]</small></small> a slander, \[is\] a fool. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the multitude<small><small>[^45]</small></small> of words there wanteth not sin: but<small><small>[^46]</small></small> he that refraineth his lips \[is\] wise. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The tongue<small><small>[^47]</small></small> of the just \[is as\] choice silver: the heart<small><small>[^48]</small></small> of the wicked \[is\] little worth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The lips of the righteous feed<small><small>[^49]</small></small> many: but fools<small><small>[^50]</small></small> die for want of wisdom<small><small>[^51]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The blessing of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, it<small><small>[^52]</small></small> maketh rich, and he<small><small>[^53]</small></small> addeth no sorrow with it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[It is\] as sport to a fool to do mischief: but a man of understanding hath wisdom.<small><small>[^54]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The fear<small><small>[^55]</small></small> of the wicked, it shall come upon him: but the desire<small><small>[^56]</small></small> of the righteous shall be granted. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As the whirlwind<small><small>[^57]</small></small> passeth, so \[is\] the wicked no \[more\]: but the righteous \[is\] an<small><small>[^58]</small></small> everlasting foundation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As vinegar<small><small>[^59]</small></small> to the teeth, and as smoke<small><small>[^60]</small></small> to the eyes, so<small><small>[^61]</small></small> \[is\] the sluggard to them that send him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The fear<small><small>[^62]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> prolongeth<small><small>[^63]</small></small> days: but the years<small><small>[^64]</small></small> of the wicked shall be shortened. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The hope<small><small>[^65]</small></small> of the righteous \[shall be\] gladness: but<small><small>[^66]</small></small> the expectation of the wicked shall perish. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The way<small><small>[^67]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] strength to the upright: but<small><small>[^68]</small></small> destruction \[shall be\] to the workers of iniquity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The righteous shall never<small><small>[^69]</small></small> be removed: but the wicked<small><small>[^70]</small></small> shall not inhabit the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The mouth<small><small>[^71]</small></small> of the just bringeth forth wisdom: but the froward<small><small>[^72]</small></small> tongue shall be cut out. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.10.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The lips of the righteous know<small><small>[^73]</small></small> what is acceptable: but<small><small>[^74]</small></small> the mouth of the wicked \[speaketh\] frowardness<small><small>[^75]</small></small>. <table
