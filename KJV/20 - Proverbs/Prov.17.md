***Better a Dry Morsel in Quietness*** ^bsb1465

house<small><small>[^2]</small></small> full of sacrifices<small><small>[^3]</small></small> \[with<small><small>[^4]</small></small>\] strife. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A wise<small><small>[^5]</small></small> servant shall have rule over a son that<small><small>[^6]</small></small> causeth shame, and shall have part of the inheritance among the brethren. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The fining pot \[is\] for silver, and the furnace for gold: but the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> trieth the hearts.<small><small>[^7]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A wicked doer giveth heed to false lips; \[and\] a liar giveth ear to a naughty tongue.<small><small>[^8]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso mocketh<small><small>[^9]</small></small> the poor class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Childrenâ€™s<small><small>[^12]</small></small> children \[are\] the crown of old men; and speech<small><small>[^14]</small></small> becometh not a fool: much<small><small>[^15]</small></small> less do lying lips<small><small>[^16]</small></small> a prince. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A gift<small><small>[^17]</small></small> \[is as\] a precious stone<small><small>[^18]</small></small> in the eyes of him that hath it: whithersoever<small><small>[^19]</small></small> it turneth, it prospereth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that covereth<small><small>[^20]</small></small> a transgression seeketh<small><small>[^21]</small></small> love; but<small><small>[^22]</small></small> he that repeateth a matter separateth \[very\] fool.<small><small>[^23]</small></small><small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> An evil \[man\] seeketh only rebellion: therefore a cruel messenger shall be sent against him.<small><small>[^25]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.12|12]]**<span <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The beginning<small><small>[^29]</small></small> of strife \[is as\] when one letteth out water: therefore leave<small><small>[^30]</small></small> off contention, before it be meddled with. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He loveth<small><small>[^38]</small></small> transgression that loveth strife: \[and\] he that<small><small>[^39]</small></small> exalteth his gate seeketh destruction. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that hath a froward fool hath<small><small>[^43]</small></small> no joy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A merry<small><small>[^44]</small></small> heart doeth good \[like\] a medicine<small><small>[^45]</small></small>: but a broken<small><small>[^46]</small></small> spirit drieth the bones. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A wicked \[man\] taketh a gift out of the bosom to pervert the ways of judgment.<small><small>[^47]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wisdom \[is\] before<small><small>[^48]</small></small> him that hath understanding; but the eyes<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of a fool \[are\] in the ends of the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A foolish son \[is\] a grief to his father, and bitterness to her that bare him.<small><small>[^50]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.17.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also to punish<small><small>[^51]</small></small> the just \[is\] not good, \[nor\] to
