***The Words of Agur*** ^bsb1507

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The words of Agur<small><small>[^2]</small></small> the son of Jakeh, \[even<small><small>[^3]</small></small>\] the prophecy: the man spake unto Ithiel, even unto Ithiel and Ucal, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Surely I \[am<small><small>[^4]</small></small>\] more brutish<small><small>[^5]</small></small> than \[any\] man, and have not the understanding of a man. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I neither<small><small>[^6]</small></small> learned wisdom, nor<small><small>[^7]</small></small> have<small><small>[^8]</small></small> the knowledge of the holy<small><small>[^9]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who hath ascended<small><small>[^10]</small></small> up into heaven, or descended? who hath gathered<small><small>[^11]</small></small> the wind in his fists? who hath bound the waters in a garment? who hath established all the ends of the earth? what \[is\] his name<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, and what<small><small>[^13]</small></small> \[is\] his son’s name, if thou canst tell? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Every word<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of God \[is\] pure<small><small>[^15]</small></small>: he \[is\] a shield unto them that put their trust in him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Add<small><small>[^16]</small></small> thou not unto his words, lest he reprove thee, and<small><small>[^17]</small></small> thou be found a liar. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Two \[things\] have<small><small>[^18]</small></small> I required of thee; deny me \[them\] not<small><small>[^19]</small></small> before I die: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Remove<small><small>[^20]</small></small> far from me vanity and lies: give me neither poverty nor riches; feed<small><small>[^21]</small></small> me with food convenient for me<small><small>[^22]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lest I be full<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, and deny \[thee<small><small>[^24]</small></small>\], and say, Who<small><small>[^25]</small></small> \[is\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lor<small><small>[^26]</small></small>d</span>? or lest I be poor, and steal, and take the name<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of my God \[in vain\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Accuse not<small><small>[^28]</small></small> a servant unto his master, lest<small><small>[^29]</small></small> he curse thee, and thou be found guilty. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] a generation<small><small>[^30]</small></small> \[that\] curseth<small><small>[^31]</small></small> their father, and doth<small><small>[^32]</small></small> not bless their mother. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] a generation \[that are<small><small>[^33]</small></small>\] pure in their own eyes, and \[yet\] is not<small><small>[^34]</small></small> washed from their filthiness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] a generation, O how lofty are their eyes! and their eyelids are lifted up.<small><small>[^35]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] a generation, whose<small><small>[^36]</small></small> teeth \[are as\] swords, and their jaw teeth \[as\] knives, to devour<small><small>[^37]</small></small> the poor from off the earth, and the needy from \[among\] men. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The horseleach<small><small>[^38]</small></small> hath two daughters, \[crying\], Give<small><small>[^39]</small></small>, give. There<small><small>[^40]</small></small> are three \[things that\] are never satisfied, \[yea\], four \[things\] say not, \[It is\] enough<small><small>[^41]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The grave; and the barren womb; the earth \[that\] is not filled with water; and the fire \[that\] saith not, \[It is\] enough.<small><small>[^42]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The eye<small><small>[^43]</small></small> \[that\] mocketh at \[his\] father, and despiseth to obey \[his\] mother, the ravens<small><small>[^44]</small></small> of the valley<small><small>[^45]</small></small> shall pick it out, and the young eagles shall eat it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There be three \[things which\] are too<small><small>[^46]</small></small> wonderful for me, yea, four which I know not: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The way of an<small><small>[^47]</small></small> eagle in the air; the way of a serpent upon a rock; the way of a ship in the midst<small><small>[^48]</small></small> of the sea; and the<small><small>[^49]</small></small> way of a man with a maid. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Such \[is\] the way of an adulterous woman; she eateth, and wipeth her mouth, and saith, I have done no wickedness.<small><small>[^50]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For three \[things\] the earth is disquieted, and for four \[which\] it cannot bear: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For a servant<small><small>[^51]</small></small> when he reigneth; and a fool<small><small>[^52]</small></small> when he is filled with meat; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For an odious<small><small>[^53]</small></small> \[woman\] when she is married; and an handmaid<small><small>[^54]</small></small> that is heir to her mistress. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There be four \[things which are\] little<small><small>[^55]</small></small> upon the earth, but they \[are\] exceeding wise<small><small>[^56]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The ants \[are\] a people not strong, yet they prepare their meat in the summer;<small><small>[^57]</small></small><small><small>[^58]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The conies \[are but\] a feeble folk, yet make they their houses in the rocks;<small><small>[^59]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The locusts<small><small>[^60]</small></small> have no king, yet go they forth all of them by bands<small><small>[^61]</small></small>; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The spider taketh hold with her hands, and is in kings’ palaces. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There be three \[things\] which go well, yea, four are comely in going: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A lion \[which is\] strongest among beasts, and turneth not away for any;<small><small>[^62]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A greyhound<small><small>[^63]</small></small>; an he goat also; and a king, against<small><small>[^64]</small></small> whom \[there is\] no rising up. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If thou hast done<small><small>[^65]</small></small> foolishly in lifting up thyself, or if thou hast thought evil, \[lay<small><small>[^66]</small></small>\] thine hand upon thy mouth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.30.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Surely the churning of milk bringeth forth butter, and the wringing of the nose bringeth forth blood: so<small><small>[^67]</small></small> the forcing of wrath bringeth forth strife. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Prov.29|<< Proverbs 29]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Prov.31|Proverbs 31 >>]]</td></tr></table>
