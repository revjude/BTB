<big><big>[[Prov.15.1 A Gentle Answer Turns Away Wrath|⥃]]</big></big>***A Gentle Answer Turns Away Wrath***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A soft<small><small>[^1]</small></small> answer turneth away wrath: but grievous<small><small>[^2]</small></small> words stir up anger. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The tongue<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of the wise useth knowledge aright: but the mouth of fools poureth<small><small>[^4]</small></small> out foolishness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The eyes of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[are\] in every place, beholding the evil and the good.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A wholesome<small><small>[^6]</small></small> tongue \[is\] a tree<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of life: but perverseness therein \[is\] a breach<small><small>[^8]</small></small> in the spirit. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A fool<small><small>[^9]</small></small> despiseth his father’s instruction: but<small><small>[^10]</small></small> he that regardeth reproof is prudent. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the house<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of the righteous \[is\] much treasure: but in the revenues<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of the wicked is trouble. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The lips<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of the wise disperse knowledge: but the heart<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of the foolish \[doeth\] not so. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The sacrifice<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of the wicked \[is\] an abomination to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: but the prayer<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of the upright \[is\] his delight. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The way<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of the wicked \[is\] an abomination unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: but he loveth<small><small>[^18]</small></small> him that followeth after righteousness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Correction<small><small>[^19]</small></small> \[is\] grievous<small><small>[^20]</small></small> unto him that forsaketh the way: \[and\] he<small><small>[^21]</small></small> that hateth reproof shall die. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hell<small><small>[^22]</small></small> and destruction \[are\] before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: how much more then the hearts<small><small>[^23]</small></small> of the children of men? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A scorner<small><small>[^24]</small></small> loveth not one that reproveth him: neither<small><small>[^25]</small></small> will he go unto the wise. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A merry<small><small>[^26]</small></small> heart maketh a cheerful countenance: but by<small><small>[^27]</small></small> sorrow of the heart the spirit is broken. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The heart<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of him that hath understanding seeketh knowledge: but the mouth<small><small>[^29]</small></small> of fools feedeth on foolishness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All<small><small>[^30]</small></small> the days of the afflicted \[are\] evil: but<small><small>[^31]</small></small> he that is of a merry heart \[hath\] a continual feast. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Better \[is\] little<small><small>[^32]</small></small> with the fear of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> than great<small><small>[^33]</small></small> treasure and trouble therewith. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Better \[is\] a dinner of herbs where love is, than a stalled ox and hatred therewith.<small><small>[^34]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A wrathful<small><small>[^35]</small></small> man stirreth up strife: but \[he<small><small>[^36]</small></small> that is\] slow to anger appeaseth strife. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The way of the slothful<small><small>[^37]</small></small> \[man is\] as an hedge of thorns: but the way of the righteous<small><small>[^38]</small></small> \[is\] made plain<small><small>[^39]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A wise<small><small>[^40]</small></small> son maketh a glad father: but a foolish man despiseth<small><small>[^41]</small></small> his mother. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Folly \[is\] joy<small><small>[^42]</small></small> to \[him that is\] destitute of wisdom<small><small>[^43]</small></small>: but a man<small><small>[^44]</small></small> of understanding walketh uprightly. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Without counsel purposes are disappointed: but in the multitude of counsellors they are established.<small><small>[^45]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A man hath joy<small><small>[^46]</small></small> by the answer of his mouth: and a word \[spoken\] in due season<small><small>[^47]</small></small>, how<small><small>[^48]</small></small> good \[is it\]! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The way<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of life \[is\] above<small><small>[^50]</small></small> to the wise, that<small><small>[^51]</small></small> he may depart from hell beneath. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> will destroy<small><small>[^52]</small></small> the house of the proud: but<small><small>[^53]</small></small> he will establish the border of the widow. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The thoughts<small><small>[^54]</small></small> of the wicked \[are\] an abomination to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: but<small><small>[^55]</small></small> \[the words\] of the pure \[are\] pleasant words<small><small>[^56]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that is<small><small>[^57]</small></small> greedy of gain troubleth his own house; but<small><small>[^58]</small></small> he that hateth gifts shall live. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The heart<small><small>[^59]</small></small> of the righteous studieth to answer: but the mouth<small><small>[^60]</small></small> of the wicked poureth out evil things. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] far<small><small>[^61]</small></small> from the wicked: but he heareth<small><small>[^62]</small></small> the prayer of the righteous. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The light<small><small>[^63]</small></small> of the eyes rejoiceth the heart: \[and\] a good<small><small>[^64]</small></small> report maketh the bones<small><small>[^65]</small></small> fat. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The ear<small><small>[^66]</small></small> that heareth the reproof of life abideth<small><small>[^67]</small></small> among the wise. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that refuseth<small><small>[^68]</small></small> instruction<small><small>[^69]</small></small> despiseth his own soul: but he that heareth<small><small>[^70]</small></small> reproof getteth understanding<small><small>[^71]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.15.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The fear<small><small>[^72]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] the instruction of wisdom; and<small><small>[^73]</small></small> before honour \[is\] humility. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td
