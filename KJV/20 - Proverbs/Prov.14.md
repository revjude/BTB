<big><big>[[Prov.14.1 The Wise Woman|⥃]]</big></big>***The Wise Woman***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Every wise<small><small>[^1]</small></small> woman buildeth her house: but the foolish<small><small>[^2]</small></small> plucketh it down with her hands. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that walketh<small><small>[^3]</small></small> in his uprightness feareth the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: but<small><small>[^4]</small></small> \[he that is\] perverse in his ways despiseth him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the mouth<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of the foolish \[is\] a rod of pride: but<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the lips of the wise shall preserve them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Where no oxen \[are\], the crib \[is\] clean<small><small>[^7]</small></small>: but<small><small>[^8]</small></small> much increase \[is\] by the strength of the ox. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A faithful witness will not lie: but a false witness will utter lies.<small><small>[^9]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A scorner<small><small>[^10]</small></small> seeketh wisdom, and \[findeth it\] not: but knowledge<small><small>[^11]</small></small> \[is\] easy unto him that understandeth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Go from the presence of a foolish man, when thou perceivest not \[in him\] the lips of knowledge.<small><small>[^12]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wisdom<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of the prudent \[is\] to understand his way: but the folly<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of fools \[is\] deceit. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Fools<small><small>[^15]</small></small> make a mock at sin: but among<small><small>[^16]</small></small> the righteous \[there is\] favour. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The heart<small><small>[^17]</small></small> knoweth his<small><small>[^18]</small></small> own bitterness; and<small><small>[^19]</small></small> a stranger doth not intermeddle with his joy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The house<small><small>[^20]</small></small> of the wicked shall be overthrown: but the tabernacle<small><small>[^21]</small></small> of the upright shall flourish. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There is a way which seemeth right unto a man, but the end thereof \[are\] the ways of death.<small><small>[^22]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Even in laughter the heart is sorrowful; and the end of that mirth \[is\] heaviness.<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The backslider<small><small>[^24]</small></small> in heart shall be filled<small><small>[^25]</small></small> with his own ways: and a good<small><small>[^26]</small></small> man \[shall be satisfied\] from himself. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The simple<small><small>[^27]</small></small> believeth every word: but the prudent<small><small>[^28]</small></small> \[man\] looketh well to his going. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A wise \[man\] feareth<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, and departeth from evil: but the fool<small><small>[^30]</small></small> rageth, and is confident. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[He that<small><small>[^31]</small></small> is\] soon angry dealeth foolishly: and a man<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of wicked devices is hated. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The simple inherit<small><small>[^33]</small></small> folly: but the prudent<small><small>[^34]</small></small> are crowned with knowledge. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The evil bow before the good; and the wicked at the gates of the righteous.<small><small>[^35]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The poor<small><small>[^36]</small></small> is hated even of his own neighbour: but<small><small>[^37]</small></small> the rich \[hath\] many friends<small><small>[^38]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that despiseth<small><small>[^39]</small></small> his neighbour sinneth: but he that hath<small><small>[^40]</small></small> mercy on the poor, happy \[is\] he. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Do they not err<small><small>[^41]</small></small> that devise evil? but<small><small>[^42]</small></small> mercy and truth \[shall be\] to them that devise<small><small>[^43]</small></small> good. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In all<small><small>[^44]</small></small> labour there is profit: but<small><small>[^45]</small></small> the talk of the lips \[tendeth\] only to penury. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The crown<small><small>[^46]</small></small> of the wise \[is\] their riches: \[but\] the foolishness<small><small>[^47]</small></small> of fools \[is\] folly. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A true witness delivereth souls: but a deceitful \[witness\] speaketh lies.<small><small>[^48]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the fear<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] strong confidence: and his<small><small>[^50]</small></small> children shall have a place of refuge. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The fear of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] a fountain<small><small>[^51]</small></small> of life, to<small><small>[^52]</small></small> depart from the snares of death. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the multitude of people \[is\] the king’s honour: but in the want of people \[is\] the destruction of the prince.<small><small>[^53]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[He that is\] slow<small><small>[^54]</small></small> to wrath \[is\] of great understanding: but<small><small>[^55]</small></small> \[he that is\] hasty<small><small>[^56]</small></small> of spirit exalteth<small><small>[^57]</small></small> folly. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A sound<small><small>[^58]</small></small> heart \[is\] the life of the flesh: but envy<small><small>[^59]</small></small> the rottenness<small><small>[^60]</small></small> of the bones. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that oppresseth<small><small>[^61]</small></small> the poor reproacheth his Maker: but<small><small>[^62]</small></small> he that honoureth him hath mercy on the poor. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wicked is driven<small><small>[^63]</small></small> away in his wickedness: but the righteous<small><small>[^64]</small></small> hath hope in his death. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wisdom resteth in the heart of him that hath understanding: but \[that which is\] in the midst of fools is made known.<small><small>[^65]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.34|34]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Righteousness<small><small>[^66]</small></small> exalteth a nation: but<small><small>[^67]</small></small> sin \[is\] a reproach to any people<small><small>[^68]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.14.35|35]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The king’s<small><small>[^69]</small></small> favour \[is\] toward a wise servant: but his wrath is \[against\] him<small><small>[^70]</small></small> that causeth
