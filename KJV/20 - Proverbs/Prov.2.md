<big><big>[[Prov.2.1 The Benefits of Wisdom|â¥ƒ]]</big></big>***The Benefits of Wisdom***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My son, if<small><small>[^2]</small></small> thou wilt receive my words, and hide<small><small>[^3]</small></small> my commandments with thee; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So that thou<small><small>[^4]</small></small> incline thine ear unto wisdom, \[and\] apply<small><small>[^5]</small></small> thine heart to understanding; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea, if<small><small>[^6]</small></small> thou criest after knowledge, \[and\] liftest up thy voice<small><small>[^7]</small></small> for understanding; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If thou<small><small>[^8]</small></small> seekest her as silver, and searchest<small><small>[^9]</small></small> for her as \[for\] hid treasures; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shalt<small><small>[^10]</small></small> thou understand the fear<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and find<small><small>[^12]</small></small> the knowledge of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^13]</small></small></span> giveth wisdom: out<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of his mouth \[cometh\] knowledge and understanding. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He layeth<small><small>[^15]</small></small> up sound wisdom for the righteous: \[he is\] a buckler<small><small>[^16]</small></small> to them that walk uprightly. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He keepeth<small><small>[^17]</small></small> the paths of judgment, and<small><small>[^18]</small></small> preserveth the way of his saints. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shalt thou understand righteousness, and judgment, and equity; \[yea\], every good path.<small><small>[^19]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When wisdom entereth into thine heart, and knowledge is pleasant unto thy soul;<small><small>[^20]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Discretion shall preserve thee, understanding shall keep thee:<small><small>[^21]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To deliver<small><small>[^22]</small></small> thee from the way of the evil \[man\], from the man<small><small>[^23]</small></small> that speaketh froward things; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who leave<small><small>[^24]</small></small> the paths of uprightness, to walk<small><small>[^25]</small></small> in the ways of darkness; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who rejoice<small><small>[^26]</small></small> to do evil, \[and<small><small>[^27]</small></small>\] delight in the frowardness of the wicked; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whose ways \[are\] crooked, and \[they\] froward in their paths:<small><small>[^28]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To deliver<small><small>[^29]</small></small> thee from the strange woman, \[even\] from the stranger \[which\] flattereth<small><small>[^30]</small></small> with her words; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Which forsaketh the guide<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of her youth, and forgetteth<small><small>[^32]</small></small> the covenant of her God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For her house inclineth unto death, and her paths unto the dead.<small><small>[^33]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> None<small><small>[^34]</small></small> that go unto her return again, neither take<small><small>[^35]</small></small> they hold of the paths of life. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That thou mayest walk in the way of good \[men\], and keep the paths of the righteous.<small><small>[^36]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the upright shall dwell in the land, and the perfect shall remain in it.<small><small>[^37]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.2.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the wicked<small><small>[^38]</small></small> shall be cut off from the earth, and the transgressors shall be rooted<small><small>[^39]</small></small> out of it. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Prov.1|<< Proverbs 1]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Prov.3|Proverbs 3 >>]]</td></tr></table>
