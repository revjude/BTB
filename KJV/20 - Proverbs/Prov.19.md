<big><big>[[Prov.19.1 The Man of Integrity|⥃]]</big></big>***The Man of Integrity***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Better<small><small>[^1]</small></small> \[is\] the poor that walketh in his integrity, than \[he that is\] perverse<small><small>[^2]</small></small> in his lips, and is a fool. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also, \[that\] the<small><small>[^3]</small></small> soul \[be\] without knowledge, \[it is\] not good; and<small><small>[^4]</small></small> he that hasteth with \[his\] feet sinneth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The foolishness<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of man perverteth his way: and his heart fretteth<small><small>[^6]</small></small> against the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wealth maketh<small><small>[^7]</small></small> many friends; but the poor<small><small>[^8]</small></small> is separated from his neighbour. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A false<small><small>[^9]</small></small> witness shall not be unpunished<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, and \[he that\] speaketh lies shall not escape. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Many will<small><small>[^11]</small></small> intreat the favour of the prince: and<small><small>[^12]</small></small> every man \[is\] a friend to him that giveth gifts<small><small>[^13]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All the<small><small>[^14]</small></small> brethren<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of the poor do hate him: how much more do his friends go far from him? he pursueth \[them with\] words, \[yet<small><small>[^16]</small></small>\] they \[are\] wanting \[to him\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that getteth wisdom<small><small>[^17]</small></small> loveth<small><small>[^18]</small></small> his own soul: he that keepeth<small><small>[^19]</small></small> understanding shall find good. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A false<small><small>[^20]</small></small> witness shall not be unpunished, and<small><small>[^21]</small></small> \[he that\] speaketh lies shall perish. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Delight<small><small>[^22]</small></small> is not seemly for a fool; much<small><small>[^23]</small></small> less for a servant to have rule over princes. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The discretion<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of a man deferreth<small><small>[^25]</small></small> his anger; and<small><small>[^26]</small></small> \[it is\] his glory to pass over a transgression. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The king’s<small><small>[^27]</small></small> wrath \[is\] as the roaring of a lion; but his<small><small>[^28]</small></small> favour \[is\] as dew upon the grass. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A foolish<small><small>[^29]</small></small> son \[is\] the calamity of his father: and the contentions<small><small>[^30]</small></small> of a wife \[are\] a continual dropping. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> House and riches \[are\] the inheritance<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of fathers: and a<small><small>[^32]</small></small> prudent wife \[is\] from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Slothfulness casteth<small><small>[^33]</small></small> into a deep sleep; and<small><small>[^34]</small></small> an idle soul shall suffer hunger. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that keepeth the<small><small>[^35]</small></small> commandment keepeth his<small><small>[^36]</small></small> own soul; \[but\] he that despiseth<small><small>[^37]</small></small> his ways shall die. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that hath<small><small>[^38]</small></small> pity upon the poor lendeth<small><small>[^39]</small></small> unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and that which he hath given<small><small>[^40]</small></small> will he pay him again. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Chasten<small><small>[^41]</small></small> thy son while there is hope, and let not thy soul spare for his crying<small><small>[^42]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A man<small><small>[^43]</small></small> of great wrath shall suffer punishment: for if thou deliver \[him\], yet thou must do it again<small><small>[^44]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear counsel, and receive<small><small>[^45]</small></small> instruction, that thou mayest be<small><small>[^46]</small></small> wise in thy latter end. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There are\] many<small><small>[^47]</small></small> devices in a man’s heart; nevertheless<small><small>[^48]</small></small> the counsel of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that shall stand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The desire<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of a man \[is\] his kindness: and<small><small>[^50]</small></small> a poor man \[is\] better than a liar. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The fear<small><small>[^51]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[tendeth\] to life: and \[he that hath it\] shall abide<small><small>[^52]</small></small> satisfied; he shall<small><small>[^53]</small></small> not be visited with evil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A slothful \[man\] hideth his hand in \[his\] bosom, and will not so much as bring it to his mouth again.<small><small>[^54]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Smite<small><small>[^55]</small></small> a scorner, and the simple will beware<small><small>[^56]</small></small>: and reprove<small><small>[^57]</small></small> one that hath understanding, \[and\] he will understand knowledge. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that wasteth<small><small>[^58]</small></small> \[his\] father, \[and\] chaseth away \[his\] mother, \[is\] a son<small><small>[^59]</small></small> that causeth shame, and bringeth reproach. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Cease, my son, to hear the instruction \[that causeth\] to err from the words of knowledge.<small><small>[^60]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> An ungodly witness<small><small>[^61]</small></small> scorneth<small><small>[^62]</small></small> judgment: and the<small><small>[^63]</small></small> mouth of the wicked devoureth iniquity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.19.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Judgments<small><small>[^64]</small></small> are prepared for scorners, and<small><small>[^65]</small></small> stripes for the back of fools. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Prov.18|<< Proverbs 18]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Prov.20|Proverbs 20 >>]]</td></tr></table>
