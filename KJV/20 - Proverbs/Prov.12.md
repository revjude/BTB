<big><big>[[Prov.12.1 Loving Discipline and Knowledge|⥃]]</big></big>***Loving Discipline and Knowledge***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso loveth<small><small>[^1]</small></small> instruction loveth knowledge: but he that<small><small>[^2]</small></small> hateth reproof \[is\] brutish. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A good<small><small>[^3]</small></small> \[man\] obtaineth favour of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: but a man<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of wicked devices will he condemn. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A man shall not be established<small><small>[^5]</small></small> by wickedness: but the root<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of the righteous shall not be moved. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A virtuous<small><small>[^7]</small></small> woman \[is\] a crown to her husband: but she<small><small>[^8]</small></small> that maketh as<small><small>[^9]</small></small>hamed \[is\] as rottenness in his bones. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The thoughts<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of the righteous \[are\] right: \[but\] the counsels<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of the wicked \[are\] deceit. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The words<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of the wicked \[are\] to lie in wait for blood: but the mouth<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of the upright shall deliver them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wicked<small><small>[^14]</small></small> are overthrown, and \[are\] not: but the house<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of the righteous shall stand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A man shall be commended<small><small>[^16]</small></small> according to his wisdom: but he<small><small>[^17]</small></small> that is of a perverse heart<small><small>[^18]</small></small> shall be despised. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[He that is<small><small>[^19]</small></small>\] despised<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, and hath a servant, \[is\] better than he that honoureth himself, and lacketh bread. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A righteous<small><small>[^21]</small></small> \[man\] regardeth the life of his beast: but<small><small>[^22]</small></small> the tender mercies<small><small>[^23]</small></small> of the wicked \[are\] cruel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that tilleth<small><small>[^24]</small></small> his land shall be satisfied with bread: but he that followeth<small><small>[^25]</small></small> vain \[persons is\] void of understanding. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wicked desireth<small><small>[^26]</small></small> the net<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of evil \[men\]: but the root<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of the righteous yieldeth \[fruit\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wicked is snared by the transgression of \[his\] lips<small><small>[^29]</small></small>: but<small><small>[^30]</small></small> the just shall come out of trouble. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A man shall be satisfied<small><small>[^31]</small></small> with good by the fruit of \[his\] mouth: and<small><small>[^32]</small></small> the recompence of a man’s hands shall be rendered unto him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The way<small><small>[^33]</small></small> of a fool \[is\] right in his own eyes: but<small><small>[^34]</small></small> he that hearkeneth unto counsel \[is\] wise. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A fool’s<small><small>[^35]</small></small> wrath is presently<small><small>[^36]</small></small> known: but<small><small>[^37]</small></small> a prudent \[man\] covereth shame. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[He that<small><small>[^38]</small></small>\] speaketh truth sheweth forth righteousness: but<small><small>[^39]</small></small> a false witness deceit. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There is that<small><small>[^40]</small></small> speaketh like<small><small>[^41]</small></small> the piercings of a sword: but<small><small>[^42]</small></small> the tongue of the wise \[is\] health. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The lip<small><small>[^43]</small></small> of truth shall be established for ever: but<small><small>[^44]</small></small> a lying tongue \[is\] but for a moment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Deceit<small><small>[^45]</small></small> \[is\] in the heart of them that imagine evil: but<small><small>[^46]</small></small> to the counsellors of peace \[is\] joy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There shall no<small><small>[^47]</small></small> evil happen to the just: but the wicked shall be filled<small><small>[^48]</small></small> with mischief. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lying<small><small>[^49]</small></small> lips \[are\] abomination to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: but<small><small>[^50]</small></small> they that deal truly \[are\] his delight. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A prudent<small><small>[^51]</small></small> man concealeth knowledge: but<small><small>[^52]</small></small> the heart of fools proclaimeth foolishness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The hand<small><small>[^53]</small></small> of the diligent shall bear rule: but<small><small>[^54]</small></small> the slothful<small><small>[^55]</small></small> shall be under tribute. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Heaviness<small><small>[^56]</small></small> in the heart of man maketh it stoop: but<small><small>[^57]</small></small> a good word maketh it glad. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The righteous<small><small>[^58]</small></small> \[is\] more excellent<small><small>[^59]</small></small> than his neighbour: but<small><small>[^60]</small></small> the way of the wicked seduceth them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The slothful<small><small>[^61]</small></small> \[man\] roasteth not that which he took in hunting: but<small><small>[^62]</small></small> the substance of a diligent man \[is\] precious. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.12.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the way of righteousness \[is\] life; and \[in\] the pathway \[thereof there is\] no death. <small><small>[^63]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;"
