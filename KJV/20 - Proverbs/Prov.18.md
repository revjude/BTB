<big><big>[[Prov.18.1 The Selfishness of the Unfriendly|⥃]]</big></big>***The Selfishness of the Unfriendly***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Through<small><small>[^1]</small></small> desire a man, having separated himself, seeketh<small><small>[^2]</small></small> \[and\] intermeddleth<small><small>[^3]</small></small> with all wisdom. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A fool<small><small>[^4]</small></small> hath no delight in understanding, but<small><small>[^5]</small></small> that his heart may discover itself. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When the wicked cometh, \[then\] cometh also contempt, and with ignominy reproach.<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The words<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of a man’s mouth \[are as\] deep waters, \[and\] the wellspring<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of wisdom \[as\] a flowing brook. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[It is\] not<small><small>[^9]</small></small> good to accept the person of the wicked, to overthrow<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the righteous in judgment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A fool’s<small><small>[^11]</small></small> lips enter into contention, and his<small><small>[^12]</small></small> mouth calleth for strokes. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A fool’s mouth \[is\] his destruction<small><small>[^13]</small></small>, and his lips<small><small>[^14]</small></small> \[are\] the snare of his soul. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The words<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of a talebearer<small><small>[^16]</small></small> \[are\] as wounds<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, and they go down into the innermost parts<small><small>[^18]</small></small> of the belly. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He also that is slothful<small><small>[^19]</small></small> in his work is brother<small><small>[^20]</small></small> to him that is a great waster. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The name<small><small>[^21]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] a strong<small><small>[^22]</small></small> tower: the righteous<small><small>[^23]</small></small> runneth into it, and is safe<small><small>[^24]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The rich man’s wealth \[is\] his strong city, and as an high wall in his own conceit.<small><small>[^25]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Before destruction<small><small>[^26]</small></small> the heart of man is haughty, and<small><small>[^27]</small></small> before honour \[is\] humility. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^28]</small></small> answereth a matter<small><small>[^29]</small></small> before he heareth \[it\], it \[is\] folly and shame unto him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The spirit<small><small>[^30]</small></small> of a man will sustain his infirmity; but<small><small>[^31]</small></small> a wounded spirit who can bear? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The heart of the prudent getteth knowledge; and the ear of the wise seeketh knowledge.<small><small>[^32]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A man’s gift maketh room for him, and bringeth him before great men.<small><small>[^33]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[He that is\] first in his own cause \[seemeth\] just; but his neighbour cometh and searcheth him.<small><small>[^34]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The lot causeth contentions to cease, and parteth between the mighty.<small><small>[^35]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A brother<small><small>[^36]</small></small> offended \[is harder to be won\] than<small><small>[^37]</small></small> a strong city: and \[their\] contentions \[are\] like the bars of a castle. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A man’s belly shall be satisfied with the fruit of his mouth; \[and\] with the increase of his lips shall he be filled.<small><small>[^38]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Death<small><small>[^39]</small></small> and<small><small>[^40]</small></small> life \[are\] in the power of the tongue: and they that love it shall eat the fruit thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Whoso\] findeth a wife<small><small>[^41]</small></small> findeth a good \[thing\], and<small><small>[^42]</small></small> obtaineth favour of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The poor<small><small>[^43]</small></small> useth intreaties; but the rich<small><small>[^44]</small></small> answereth roughly. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Prov.18.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A man \[that hath<small><small>[^45]</small></small>\] friends must shew himself friendly: and there<small><small>[^46]</small></small> is a friend \[that\] sticketh closer than a brother. <table width="100%"
