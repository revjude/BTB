<big><big>[[Titus.3.1 Heirs of Grace|⥃]]</big></big>***Heirs of Grace***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Put<small><small>[^2]</small></small> them in mind to be subject<small><small>[^3]</small></small> to principalities and powers, to obey magistrates, to be ready<small><small>[^4]</small></small> to every good work, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To speak<small><small>[^5]</small></small> evil of no<small><small>[^6]</small></small> man, to be no brawlers, \[but\] gentle<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, shewing all meekness unto all men<small><small>[^8]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For we<small><small>[^9]</small></small> ourselves also were sometimes foolish<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, disobedient<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, deceived<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, serving<small><small>[^13]</small></small> divers lusts and pleasures, living<small><small>[^14]</small></small> in malice and envy, hateful<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, \[and\] hating one another. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But after that the kindness<small><small>[^16]</small></small> and love<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of God<small><small>[^18]</small></small> our Saviour toward man appeared<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Not by works<small><small>[^20]</small></small> of righteousness which we have done, but according<small><small>[^21]</small></small> to his mercy he saved us, by the washing<small><small>[^22]</small></small> of regeneration, and renewing<small><small>[^23]</small></small> of the Holy Ghost; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Which he shed<small><small>[^24]</small></small> on us abundantly<small><small>[^25]</small></small> through<small><small>[^26]</small></small> Jesus Christ our Saviour; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That being<small><small>[^27]</small></small> justified by his grace, we should be made<small><small>[^28]</small></small> heirs according to the hope<small><small>[^29]</small></small> of eternal life. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[This is\] a faithful<small><small>[^30]</small></small> saying, and these things I will that thou<small><small>[^31]</small></small> affirm constantly, that they which<small><small>[^32]</small></small> have be<small><small>[^33]</small></small>lieved in God might be careful to maintain good<small><small>[^34]</small></small> works. These things are good and profitable unto men.

<big><big>[[Titus.3.9 Avoid Divisions|⥃]]</big></big>***Avoid Divisions***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Rom.16|Romans 16:17–20]] ]</sup></small>

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But avoid<small><small>[^35]</small></small> foolish questions, and genealogies, and contentions, and strivings about the law; for they are unprofitable<small><small>[^36]</small></small> and vain. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A man that is an heretic<small><small>[^37]</small></small>k after<small><small>[^38]</small></small> the first and second admonition reject<small><small>[^39]</small></small>; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Knowing that he that is such is subverted<small><small>[^40]</small></small>, and sinneth, being<small><small>[^41]</small></small> condemned of himself.

<big><big>[[Titus.3.12 Final Remarks and Greetings|⥃]]</big></big>***Final Remarks and Greetings***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When I shall send Artemas unto thee, or Tychicus<small><small>[^42]</small></small>, be<small><small>[^43]</small></small> diligent to come unto me to Nicopolis: for<small><small>[^44]</small></small> I have determined there to winter. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Bring Zenas the lawyer<small><small>[^45]</small></small> and Apollos<small><small>[^46]</small></small> on<small><small>[^47]</small></small> their journey diligently, that nothing be wanting unto them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And let ours also learn<small><small>[^48]</small></small> to maintain good works<small><small>[^49]</small></small> for necessary uses, that<small><small>[^50]</small></small> they be not unfruitful. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Titus.3.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All that are with me<small><small>[^51]</small></small> salute thee. Greet<small><small>[^52]</small></small> them that love<small><small>[^53]</small></small> us in the faith. Grace<small><small>[^54]</small></small> \[be\] with you all.
