***Elijah Flees from Jezebel*** ^bsb868

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Ahab<small><small>[^2]</small></small> told Jezebel all that Elijah had done, and withal how he had slain<small><small>[^3]</small></small> all the prophets with the sword. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Jezebel sent a messenger unto Elijah, saying, So let<small><small>[^4]</small></small> the gods do \[to me\], and more also, if I<small><small>[^5]</small></small> make not thy life as the life of one of them by to morrow<small><small>[^6]</small></small> about this time. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when he saw \[that\], he arose<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, and went for his life, and came to Beer–sheba, which \[belongeth\] to Judah, and left his servant there.<small><small>[^8]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]But he himself went a day’s journey into the wilderness, and came and sat down<small><small>[^9]</small></small> under a juniper tree: and he requested<small><small>[^10]</small></small> for himself<small><small>[^11]</small></small> that he might die; and said, It is enough; now, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, take away my life; for I \[am\] not better<small><small>[^12]</small></small> than my fathers. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And as he lay<small><small>[^13]</small></small> and slept under a juniper tree, behold, then an angel<small><small>[^14]</small></small> touched him, and said unto him, Arise \[and\] eat. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he looked, and, behold, \[there was\] a cake<small><small>[^15]</small></small> baken on the coals, and a cruse of water at his head<small><small>[^16]</small></small>. And he did eat and drink, and laid him down again. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the angel<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came again the second time, and touched him, and said, Arise \[and\] eat; because the journey<small><small>[^18]</small></small> \[is\] too great for thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he arose, and did eat and drink, and went in the strength<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of that meat forty days<small><small>[^20]</small></small> and forty nights unto Horeb<small><small>[^21]</small></small> the mount of God.

***The LORD Speaks to Elijah at Horeb*** ^bsb869

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he came thither unto a cave<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, and lodged there; and, behold, the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[came\] to him, and he said unto him, What doest thou<small><small>[^23]</small></small> here, Elijah? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, I have been very jealous<small><small>[^24]</small></small> for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of hosts: for the children of Israel have forsaken thy covenant, thrown down<small><small>[^25]</small></small> thine altars, and slain thy prophets with the sword; and I, \[even\] I only<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, am left; and they seek my life<small><small>[^27]</small></small>, to take it away. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, Go forth, and stand upon the mount<small><small>[^28]</small></small> before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. And, behold, the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> passed by, and a great<small><small>[^29]</small></small> and strong wind rent the mountains, and brake in pieces the rocks before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; \[but\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[was\] not in the wind: and after the wind an earthquake<small><small>[^30]</small></small>; \[but\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[was\] not in the earthquake:<small><small>[^31]</small></small><small><small>[^32]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And after the earthquake a fire<small><small>[^33]</small></small>; \[but\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[was\] not in the fire: and after the fire a still<small><small>[^34]</small></small> small voice. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it was \[so\], when Elijah heard \[it\], that he wrapped his face<small><small>[^35]</small></small> in his mantle, and went out, and stood in the entering in of the cave. And, behold, \[there came\] a voice unto him, and said, What doest<small><small>[^36]</small></small> thou here, Elijah? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, I have been<small><small>[^37]</small></small> very jealous for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of hosts: because the children of Israel have forsaken<small><small>[^38]</small></small> thy covenant, thrown down thine altars, and slain thy prophets with the sword; and I, \[even\] I only, am left; and they seek my life, to take it away. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto him, Go, return on thy way to the wilderness of Damascus<small><small>[^39]</small></small>: and when thou comest, anoint<small><small>[^40]</small></small> Hazael<small><small>[^41]</small></small> \[to be\] king over Syria: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jehu<small><small>[^42]</small></small> the son of Nimshi shalt thou anoint \[to be\] king over Israel: and Elisha<small><small>[^43]</small></small> the son of Shaphat of Abel–meholah shalt thou anoint \[to be\] prophet in thy room.<small><small>[^44]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it shall come to pass, \[that\] him that escapeth<small><small>[^45]</small></small> the sword of Hazael<small><small>[^46]</small></small> shall Jehu slay: and him that escapeth from the sword of Jehu<small><small>[^47]</small></small> shall Elisha slay<small><small>[^48]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet I have left<small><small>[^49]</small></small> \[me\] seven thousand in Israel, all the knees<small><small>[^50]</small></small> which have not bowed unto Baal, and every mouth<small><small>[^51]</small></small> which hath not kissed him.

***The Call of Elisha*** ^bsb870

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So he departed thence, and found Elisha<small><small>[^52]</small></small> the son of Shaphat, who \[was\] plowing \[with\] twelve yoke \[of oxen\] before him, and he with<small><small>[^53]</small></small> the twelfth: and Elijah passed by him, and cast his mantle<small><small>[^54]</small></small> upon him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he left<small><small>[^55]</small></small> the oxen, and ran after Elijah, and said, Let me, I pray<small><small>[^56]</small></small> thee, kiss my father and my mother, and \[then\] I will follow thee. And he said unto him, Go back again<small><small>[^57]</small></small>: for what have I done to thee? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.19.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he returned back from him, and took a yoke of oxen, and slew them, and boiled their flesh<small><small>[^58]</small></small> with the instruments of the oxen, and gave unto<small><small>[^59]</small></small> the people, and they did eat.
