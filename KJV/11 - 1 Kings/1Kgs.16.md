***Jehu’s Prophecy against Baasha*** ^bsb856

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came to Jehu<small><small>[^2]</small></small> the son of Hanani<small><small>[^3]</small></small> against Baasha, saying,<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Forasmuch as I exalted thee<small><small>[^5]</small></small> out of the dust, and made thee prince over my people Israel; and thou hast walked<small><small>[^6]</small></small> in the way of Jeroboam, and hast made my people<small><small>[^7]</small></small> Israel to sin, to provoke me to anger with their sins; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, I will take away the posterity of Baasha, and the posterity of his house; and will make thy house<small><small>[^8]</small></small> like the house of Jeroboam the son of Nebat. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Him that dieth of Baasha in the city shall the dogs eat<small><small>[^9]</small></small>; and him that dieth of his in the fields shall the fowls of the air eat. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now the rest<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of the acts of Baasha, and what he did, and his might, \[are\] they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel?<small><small>[^11]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So Baasha<small><small>[^12]</small></small> slept with his fathers, and was buried in Tirzah<small><small>[^13]</small></small>: and Elah<small><small>[^14]</small></small> his son reigned in his stead.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And also by the hand<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of the prophet Jehu the son of Hanani came the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> against Baasha, and against his house<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, even for all the evil that he did in the sight of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, in provoking<small><small>[^18]</small></small> him to anger with the work<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of his hands, in being like the house of Jeroboam; and because he killed him<small><small>[^20]</small></small>.

***Elah Reigns in Israel*** ^bsb857

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]In the twenty<small><small>[^21]</small></small> and sixth year of Asa king of Judah began Elah the son of Baasha to reign over Israel in Tirzah, two years.<small><small>[^22]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And his servant<small><small>[^23]</small></small> Zimri, captain of half \[his\] chariots, conspired<small><small>[^24]</small></small> against him, as he was in Tirzah, drinking<small><small>[^25]</small></small> himself drunk in the house of Arza steward of<small><small>[^26]</small></small> \[his\] house in Tirzah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Zimri<small><small>[^27]</small></small> went in and smote him, and killed him, in the twenty and seventh year of Asa king of Judah, and reigned<small><small>[^28]</small></small> in his stead. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And it came to pass, when he began to reign, as soon as he sat on his throne, \[that\] he slew<small><small>[^29]</small></small> all the house of Baasha: he left him<small><small>[^30]</small></small> not one that pisseth against a wall, neither of his kinsfolks, nor of his friends<small><small>[^31]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus did Zimri destroy all the house of Baasha, according<small><small>[^32]</small></small> to the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, which he spake against Baasha by Jehu the prophet<small><small>[^33]</small></small>, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For all the sins of Baasha, and the sins of Elah his son, by which they sinned, and by which they made Israel to sin, in provoking<small><small>[^34]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of Israel to anger with their vanities<small><small>[^35]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now the rest of the acts of Elah, and all that he did, \[are\] they not written<small><small>[^36]</small></small> in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel?

***Zimri Reigns in Israel*** ^bsb858

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the twenty and seven<small><small>[^37]</small></small>th year of Asa king of Judah did Zimri reign seven days in Tirzah. And the people \[were\] encamped<small><small>[^38]</small></small> against Gibbethon, which \[belonged\] to the Philistines. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the people \[that were\] encamped heard say, Zimri hath conspired, and hath also slain the king: wherefore all Israel made Omri<small><small>[^39]</small></small>, the captain of the host, king over Israel that day in the camp. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Omri went up from Gibbethon, and all Israel with him, and they besieged Tirzah<small><small>[^40]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, when Zimri saw that the city was taken, that he went into the palace of the king’s house, and burnt the king’s house<small><small>[^41]</small></small> over him with fire, and died, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For his sins which he sinned in doing<small><small>[^42]</small></small> evil in the sight of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, in walking in the way of Jeroboam, and in his<small><small>[^43]</small></small> sin which he did, to make Israel to sin. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now the rest<small><small>[^44]</small></small> of the acts of Zimri, and his treason that he wrought, \[are\] they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel?

***Omri Reigns in Israel*** ^bsb859

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then were the people of Israel divided<small><small>[^45]</small></small> into two parts: half of the people followed Tibni the son of Ginath, to make him king; and half followed Omri. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the people that followed Omri prevailed against the people that followed Tibni the son of Ginath: so Tibni died, and Omri reigned. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]In the thirty<small><small>[^46]</small></small> and first year of Asa king of Judah began Omri to reign over Israel, twelve years<small><small>[^47]</small></small>: six years reigned he in Tirzah.<small><small>[^48]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he bought the hill Samaria of Shemer for two talents of silver, and built on the hill, and called the name of the city<small><small>[^49]</small></small> which he built, after the name of Shemer, owner of the hill, Samaria<small><small>[^50]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]But Omri wrought evil in the eyes of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and did worse<small><small>[^51]</small></small> than all that \[were\] before him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he walked<small><small>[^52]</small></small> in all the way of Jeroboam the son of Nebat, and in his sin wherewith he made Israel to sin, to provoke the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of Israel to anger with their vanities<small><small>[^53]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now the rest<small><small>[^54]</small></small> of the acts of Omri which he did, and his might that he shewed, \[are\] they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So Omri slept<small><small>[^55]</small></small> with his fathers, and was buried in Samaria: and Ahab his son reigned in his stead.

***Ahab Reigns in Israel, Marries Jezebel*** ^bsb860

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And in the thirty and eighth year of Asa king of Judah began Ahab the son of Omri to reign over Israel: and Ahab the son of Omri reigned over Israel in Samaria<small><small>[^56]</small></small> twenty and two years.<small><small>[^57]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Ahab the son of Omri did evil in the sight of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> above<small><small>[^58]</small></small> all that \[were\] before him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, as if it had been a light thing<small><small>[^59]</small></small> for him to walk in the sins of Jeroboam the son of Nebat, that he took to wife<small><small>[^60]</small></small> Jezebel<small><small>[^61]</small></small> the daughter of Ethbaal king of the Zidonians<small><small>[^62]</small></small>, and went<small><small>[^63]</small></small> and served Baal<small><small>[^64]</small></small>, and worshipped him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he reared up an altar for Baal in the house of Baal<small><small>[^65]</small></small>, which he had built in Samaria. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Kgs.16.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Ahab made a grove<small><small>[^66]</small></small>; and Ahab did more to provoke<small><small>[^67]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of Israel to anger than all the kings of Israel that were before him.
