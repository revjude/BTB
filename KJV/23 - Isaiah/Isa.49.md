***The Servant and Light to the Gentiles***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Acts.13|Acts 13:42–52]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1664

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Listen<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, O isles, unto me; and hearken<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, ye people, from far; The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^4]</small></small></span> hath called me from the womb; from the bowels of my mother hath he made mention of my name. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he hath made<small><small>[^5]</small></small> my mouth like a sharp sword; in the<small><small>[^6]</small></small> shadow of his hand hath he hid me, and made me<small><small>[^7]</small></small> a polished shaft; in his quiver hath he hid me; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And said unto me, Thou \[art\] my servant, O Israel, in whom I will be glorified.<small><small>[^8]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then I said, I have laboured<small><small>[^9]</small></small> in vain, I have spent<small><small>[^10]</small></small> my strength for nought, and in vain: \[yet<small><small>[^11]</small></small>\] surely my judgment \[is\] with the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and my work<small><small>[^12]</small></small> with my God. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And now, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> that formed<small><small>[^13]</small></small> me from the womb \[to be\] his servant, to bring<small><small>[^14]</small></small> Jacob again to him, Though<small><small>[^15]</small></small> Israel<small><small>[^16]</small></small> be not gathered, yet<small><small>[^17]</small></small> shall I be glorious in the eyes of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and my God shall be my strength. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, It is a light thing that<small><small>[^18]</small></small> thou shouldest be my servant to raise up the tribes of Jacob, and to restore the preserved<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of Israel: I will also<small><small>[^20]</small></small> give thee for a light to the Gentiles, that thou mayest<small><small>[^21]</small></small> be my salvation unto the end of the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, the Redeemer<small><small>[^22]</small></small> of Israel, \[and\] his Holy One, to him whom man despiseth<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, to him whom the nation abhorreth, to a<small><small>[^24]</small></small> servant of rulers, Kings<small><small>[^25]</small></small> shall see and arise, princes also shall worship, because of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> that is faithful, \[and\] the Holy One of Israel, and he<small><small>[^26]</small></small> shall choose thee. <span They shall feed<small><small>[^34]</small></small> in the ways, and their pastures \[shall be\] in all high<small><small>[^35]</small></small> places. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They shall not<small><small>[^36]</small></small> hunger nor thirst; neither<small><small>[^37]</small></small> shall the heat nor sun smite them: for he that<small><small>[^38]</small></small> hath mercy on them shall lead them, even by the springs of water shall he guide them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will make all my mountains a way, and my highways shall be exalted.<small><small>[^39]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, these shall<small><small>[^40]</small></small> come from far: and, lo, these from the north and from the west; and these from the land of Sinim. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Sing, O heavens<small><small>[^41]</small></small>; and be joyful, O earth; and break forth into singing, O mountains: for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^42]</small></small></span> hath comforted his people, and will have mercy upon his afflicted. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But Zion said, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^43]</small></small></span> hath forsaken me, and my Lord<small><small>[^44]</small></small> hath forgotten me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Can a woman<small><small>[^45]</small></small> forget her sucking child, that she should not have compassion<small><small>[^46]</small></small> on the son of her womb? yea, they may<small><small>[^47]</small></small> forget, yet<small><small>[^48]</small></small> will I not forget thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, I have<small><small>[^49]</small></small> graven thee upon the palms of \[my\] hands; thy walls<small><small>[^50]</small></small> \[are\] continually before me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.49.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy children<small><small>[^51]</small></small> shall make haste<small><small>[^52]</small></small>; thy destroyers<small><small>[^53]</small></small> and they that made thee waste shall go forth of thee.
