<big><big>[[Isa.30.1 The Worthless Treaty with Egypt|⥃]]</big></big>***The Worthless Treaty with Egypt***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Woe to the rebellious<small><small>[^2]</small></small> children, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that take<small><small>[^3]</small></small> counsel, but not of me; and that cover<small><small>[^4]</small></small> with a covering, but not of my spirit, that they may add<small><small>[^5]</small></small> sin to sin:<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That walk<small><small>[^7]</small></small> to go down into Egypt, and have<small><small>[^8]</small></small> not asked at my mouth; to strengthen themselves in the strength of Pharaoh, and to trust in the shadow<small><small>[^9]</small></small> of Egypt! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore shall the strength<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of Pharaoh be your shame, and the trust in the shadow of Egypt \[your\] confusion<small><small>[^11]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For his princes<small><small>[^12]</small></small> were at Zoan<small><small>[^13]</small></small>, and his ambassadors came to Hanes<small><small>[^14]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They were all ashamed of a people \[that\] could not profit them, nor be an help nor profit, but a shame, and also a reproach.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The burden<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of the beasts<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of the south: into the land<small><small>[^18]</small></small> of trouble and anguish, from whence \[come\] the young and old lion, the viper<small><small>[^19]</small></small> and fiery flying serpent, they will carry their riches<small><small>[^20]</small></small> upon the shoulders of young asses, and their treasures upon the bunches of camels, to a people \[that\] shall not profit \[them\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the Egyptians<small><small>[^21]</small></small> shall help in vain, and to no purpose: therefore have I cried concerning this<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, Their<small><small>[^23]</small></small> strength \[is\] to sit still. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now go, write<small><small>[^24]</small></small> it before them in a table, and note it in a book, that it may be for the time to come<small><small>[^25]</small></small> for ever and ever: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That this \[is<small><small>[^26]</small></small>\] a rebellious people, lying<small><small>[^27]</small></small> children, children \[that\] will not<small><small>[^28]</small></small> hear the law of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Which say<small><small>[^29]</small></small> to the seers, See not; he shall not<small><small>[^40]</small></small> spare: so that<small><small>[^41]</small></small> there shall not be found in the bursting of it a sherd to take fire from the hearth, or to take water \[withal\] out of the pit. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>, the Holy One<small><small>[^42]</small></small> of Israel; In returning<small><small>[^43]</small></small> and rest shall ye be saved; in quietness and in confidence shall be your strength: and ye<small><small>[^44]</small></small> would not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But ye said, No; for we will<small><small>[^45]</small></small> flee upon horses; therefore<small><small>[^46]</small></small> shall ye flee: and, We will ride upon the swift; therefore shall they that pursue you be swift. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> One thousand<small><small>[^47]</small></small> \[shall flee\] at the rebuke of one; at the rebuke of five shall ye flee: till ye<small><small>[^48]</small></small> be left as a beacon<small><small>[^49]</small></small> upon the top of a mountain, and as an ensign on an hill.

<big><big>[[Isa.30.18 God Will Be Gracious|⥃]]</big></big>***God Will Be Gracious***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And therefore<small><small>[^50]</small></small> will the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> wait<small><small>[^51]</small></small>, that he may be gracious unto you, and therefore will he be<small><small>[^52]</small></small> exalted, that he may have mercy upon you: for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^53]</small></small></span> \[is\] a God of judgment: blessed<small><small>[^54]</small></small> \[are\] all they that wait for him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the people shall dwell<small><small>[^55]</small></small> in Zion at Jerusalem: thou shalt<small><small>[^56]</small></small> weep no more: he will<small><small>[^57]</small></small> be very gracious unto thee at the voice of thy cry; and bread of the increase of the earth, and it shall<small><small>[^69]</small></small> be fat and plenteous: in that day shall thy cattle<small><small>[^70]</small></small> feed in large pastures. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The oxen<small><small>[^71]</small></small> likewise and the young asses that ear the ground<small><small>[^72]</small></small> shall eat clean<small><small>[^73]</small></small> provender, which hath been winnowed with the shovel and with the fan. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there shall be upon every high<small><small>[^74]</small></small><small><small>[^75]</small></small> mountain, and upon every high hill, rivers \[and\] streams of waters in the day<small><small>[^76]</small></small> of the great slaughter, when<small><small>[^77]</small></small> the towers fall. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover the light of the moon<small><small>[^78]</small></small> shall be as the light of the sun, and the light of the sun shall be sevenfold, as the light of seven days, in the day that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> bindeth<small><small>[^79]</small></small> up the breach of his people, and healeth the stroke of their wound. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, the name of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> cometh from far, burning<small><small>[^80]</small></small> \[with\] his anger, and the burden \[thereof<small><small>[^81]</small></small> is\] heavy<small><small>[^82]</small></small>: his lips are full of indignation, and his tongue as a devouring fire: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And his breath<small><small>[^83]</small></small>, as an overflowing<small><small>[^84]</small></small> stream, shall reach to the midst of the neck, to sift<small><small>[^85]</small></small> the nations with the sieve of vanity: and \[there shall be\] a bridle<small><small>[^86]</small></small> in the jaws of the people, causing<small><small>[^87]</small></small> \[them\] to err. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.30.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ye shall<small><small>[^88]</small></small> have a song, as in the night<small><small>[^89]</small></small> \[when\] a holy solemnity is kept;
