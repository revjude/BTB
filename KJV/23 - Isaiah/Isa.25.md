 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.25.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, thou \[art<small><small>[^1]</small></small>\] my God; I will exalt thee, I will praise thy name; for thou hast<small><small>[^2]</small></small> done wonderful \[things\]; \[thy\] counsels<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of old \[are\] faithfulness \[and\] truth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.25.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^4]</small></small> thou hast made of a city an heap; \[of\] a defenced city a ruin: a palace<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of strangers to be no city; it shall never be built. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.25.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore shall the strong people glorify thee, the city of the terrible nations shall fear thee.<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.25.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thou hast<small><small>[^7]</small></small> been a strength to the poor, a strength to the needy in his distress, a refuge<small><small>[^8]</small></small> from the storm, a shadow from the heat, when<small><small>[^9]</small></small> the blast of the terrible ones \[is\] as a storm \[against\] the wall. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.25.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt bring<small><small>[^10]</small></small> down the noise of strangers, as the heat<small><small>[^11]</small></small> in a dry place; \[even\] the heat with the shadow of a cloud: the branch<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of the terrible ones shall be brought low. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.25.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And in this<small><small>[^13]</small></small> mountain shall the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts make<small><small>[^14]</small></small> unto all people<small><small>[^15]</small></small> a feast of fat things, a feast of wines<small><small>[^16]</small></small> on the lees, of fat things full of marrow, of wines on the lees well refined. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.25.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he will<small><small>[^17]</small></small> destroy<small><small>[^18]</small></small> in this mountain the face of the covering cast<small><small>[^19]</small></small> over all people, and the vail that is spread over all nations. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.25.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He<small><small>[^20]</small></small> will swallow up death in victory; and the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God<small><small>[^21]</small></small></span> will wipe away tears from off all faces; and the rebuke<small><small>[^22]</small></small> of his people shall he take away from off<small><small>[^23]</small></small> all the earth: for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath spoken \[it\]. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.25.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it shall<small><small>[^24]</small></small> be said in that day, Lo<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, this \[is\] our God; we have waited for him, and he will save us: this \[is\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; we have waited for him, we will<small><small>[^26]</small></small> be glad and rejoice in his salvation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.25.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For in this<small><small>[^27]</small></small> mountain shall the hand of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> rest, and Moab<small><small>[^28]</small></small> shall be trodden down<small><small>[^29]</small></small> under him, even<small><small>[^30]</small></small> as straw is trodden down for the dunghill<small><small>[^31]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.25.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he shall spread<small><small>[^32]</small></small> forth his hands in the midst of them, as he that swimmeth spreadeth forth \[his hands\] to swim: and he shall bring<small><small>[^33]</small></small> down their pride together with the spoils of their hands. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.25.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the fortress<small><small>[^34]</small></small> of the high fort of thy walls shall he bring down, lay low, \[and\] bring to the ground, \[even\] to the dust<small><small>[^35]</small></small>. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Isa.24|<< Isaiah 24]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Isa.26|Isaiah 26 >>]]</td></tr></table>
