***Woe to Those Who Rely on Egypt*** ^bsb1630

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.31.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Woe to them<small><small>[^2]</small></small> that go down to Egypt for help; and stay on horses<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, and trust in chariots, because \[they are\] many; and in horsemen, because they are very strong; but they look not unto the Holy One of Israel, neither<small><small>[^4]</small></small> seek the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.31.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet he also<small><small>[^5]</small></small> \[is\] wise, and will bring<small><small>[^6]</small></small> evil, and will not<small><small>[^7]</small></small> call back<small><small>[^8]</small></small> his words: but will arise<small><small>[^9]</small></small> against the house of the evildoers, and against the help<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of them that work iniquity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.31.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now the Egyptians<small><small>[^11]</small></small> \[are\] men, and not God; and their horses<small><small>[^12]</small></small> flesh, and not spirit. When the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall stretch<small><small>[^13]</small></small> out his hand, both<small><small>[^14]</small></small> he that helpeth shall fall, and he that is holpen shall fall down, and they all shall fail together. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.31.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thus hath the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> spoken unto me, Like as<small><small>[^15]</small></small> the lion and the young lion roaring on his prey, when a multitude of shepherds is called forth against him, \[he\] will not be afraid of their voice, nor abase himself for the noise<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of them: so shall<small><small>[^17]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts come down to fight for mount Zion, and for the hill thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.31.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As birds<small><small>[^18]</small></small> flying, so will the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts defend Jerusalem; defending<small><small>[^19]</small></small> also he will deliver \[it\]; \[and\] passing<small><small>[^20]</small></small> over he will preserve \[it\]. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.31.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Turn<small><small>[^21]</small></small> ye unto \[him from\] whom the children of Israel have deeply<small><small>[^22]</small></small> revolted. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.31.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For in that<small><small>[^23]</small></small> day every man shall cast away his idols of silver, and his idols of gold<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, which your own hands have made unto you \[for\] a sin<small><small>[^25]</small></small>.
