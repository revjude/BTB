<big><big>[[Isa.46.1 Babylon’s Idols|⥃]]</big></big>***Babylon’s Idols***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.46.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Bel<small><small>[^2]</small></small> boweth down, Nebo stoopeth, their idols were upon the beasts, and upon the cattle: your carriages \[were\] heavy loaden; \[they are\] a burden<small><small>[^3]</small></small> to the weary \[beast\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.46.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They stoop, they bow down together; they could<small><small>[^4]</small></small> not deliver the burden, but<small><small>[^5]</small></small> themselves are<small><small>[^6]</small></small> gone into captivity. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.46.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Hearken<small><small>[^7]</small></small> unto me, O house of Jacob, and all the remnant<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of the house of Israel, which are borne<small><small>[^9]</small></small> \[by me\] from the belly, which are carried from the womb: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.46.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And \[even\] to \[your<small><small>[^10]</small></small>\] old age I \[am\] he; and \[even\] to hoar<small><small>[^11]</small></small> hairs will I carry \[you\]: I have made, and I will bear; even I will carry, and will deliver \[you\]. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.46.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To whom will ye liken me, and make \[me\] equal, and compare me, that we may be like?<small><small>[^12]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.46.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They lavish<small><small>[^13]</small></small> gold out of the bag, and weigh silver in the balance, \[and\] hire a goldsmith; and he maketh it a god: they fall<small><small>[^14]</small></small> down, yea, they worship. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.46.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They bear him upon the shoulder, they carry him<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, and set him in his place, and he standeth; from his place shall he not remove: yea, \[one\] shall cry<small><small>[^16]</small></small> unto him, yet can he not answer, nor save him out of his trouble. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.46.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Remember<small><small>[^17]</small></small> this, and shew yourselves men: bring<small><small>[^18]</small></small> \[it\] again to mind, O ye transgressors. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.46.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Remember the former<small><small>[^19]</small></small> things of old: for I \[am\] God<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, and \[there is\] none else; \[I am\] God, and \[there is\] none like<small><small>[^21]</small></small> me, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.46.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Declaring the end<small><small>[^22]</small></small> from the beginning, and from ancient times \[the things\] that are not \[yet\] done, saying, My counsel<small><small>[^23]</small></small> shall stand, and I will do all my pleasure: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.46.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Calling<small><small>[^24]</small></small> a ravenous bird<small><small>[^25]</small></small> from the east, the man<small><small>[^26]</small></small> that executeth my counsel<small><small>[^27]</small></small> from a far country: yea, I have spoken<small><small>[^28]</small></small> \[it\], I will also bring it to pass; I have purposed \[it\], I will also do it. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.46.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Hearken<small><small>[^29]</small></small> unto me, ye stouthearted<small><small>[^30]</small></small>, that<small><small>[^31]</small></small> \[are\] far from righteousness: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.46.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I bring<small><small>[^32]</small></small> near my righteousness; it shall not be far off, and my salvation shall not tarry<small><small>[^33]</small></small>: and I will place salvation<small><small>[^34]</small></small> in Zion for Israel<small><small>[^35]</small></small> my glory. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Isa.45|<< Isaiah 45]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Isa.47|Isaiah 47 >>]]</td></tr></table>
