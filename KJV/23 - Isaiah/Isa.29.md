<big><big>[[Isa.29.1 Woe to David’s City|⥃]]</big></big>***Woe to David’s City***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Luke.19|Luke 19:41–44]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Woe<small><small>[^2]</small></small> to Ariel, to Ariel, the city<small><small>[^3]</small></small> \[where\] David dwelt! add<small><small>[^4]</small></small> ye year to year; let them kill<small><small>[^5]</small></small> sacrifices.<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet I will<small><small>[^7]</small></small> distress Ariel, and there shall be heaviness and sorrow: and it shall<small><small>[^8]</small></small> be unto me as Ariel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will camp against thee round about, and will lay siege against thee with a mount, and I will raise forts against thee.<small><small>[^9]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt<small><small>[^10]</small></small> be brought down, \[and\] shalt speak out of the ground, and thy speech shall be low out of the dust, and thy voice shall be, as of one that hath a familiar spirit, out of the ground, and thy speech shall whisper<small><small>[^11]</small></small> out of the dust. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover the multitude<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of thy strangers shall be like small dust, and the multitude of the terrible ones \[shall be\] as chaff<small><small>[^13]</small></small> that passeth away: yea, it shall be at an<small><small>[^14]</small></small> instant suddenly. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt be visited of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts with thunder, and with earthquake, and great noise, with storm and tempest, and the flame of devouring fire.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And the multitude<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of all the nations that fight against Ariel, even all that fight against her and her munition, and that distress<small><small>[^17]</small></small> her, shall be as a dream<small><small>[^18]</small></small> of a night vision. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> It shall even be as when<small><small>[^19]</small></small> an hungry \[man\] dreameth, and, behold<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, he eateth; but he awaketh, and his soul is empty: or as when a thirsty man dreameth, and, behold, he drinketh; but he awaketh, and, behold, \[he is\] faint, and his soul hath appetite: so shall the multitude of all the nations be, that fight against mount Zion. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Stay yourselves, and wonder<small><small>[^21]</small></small>; cry ye out, and cry<small><small>[^22]</small></small>: they are<small><small>[^23]</small></small> drunken, but not with wine; they stagger, but not with strong drink. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^24]</small></small></span> hath poured out upon you the spirit of deep sleep, and hath closed<small><small>[^25]</small></small> your eyes: the prophets and your rulers<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, the seers<small><small>[^27]</small></small> hath he covered. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the vision of all is become unto you as the words of a book<small><small>[^28]</small></small> that is sealed<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, which \[men\] deliver to one that is learned, saying, Read this, I pray thee: and he saith, I cannot<small><small>[^30]</small></small>; for it \[is\] sealed: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the book is delivered to him that is not learned, saying, Read this, I pray thee: and he saith, I am not<small><small>[^31]</small></small> learned. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Wherefore the Lord said, Forasmuch<small><small>[^32]</small></small> as this people draw near \[me\] with their mouth, and with their lips do honour me, but have removed their heart far from me, and their fear<small><small>[^33]</small></small> toward me is taught by the precept of men: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore, behold, I will<small><small>[^34]</small></small> proceed<small><small>[^35]</small></small> to do a marvellous work among this people, \[even\] a marvellous work and a wonder: for the wisdom<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of their wise \[men\] shall perish, and the understanding of their prudent \[men\] shall be hid. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Woe unto them that seek<small><small>[^37]</small></small> deep to hide their counsel from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and their works<small><small>[^38]</small></small> are in the dark, and they say, Who seeth<small><small>[^39]</small></small> us? and who knoweth us? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.29.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Surely your turning<small><small>[^40]</small></small> of things upside down shall be esteemed as the potter’s<small><small>[^41]</small></small> clay: for shall<small><small>[^42]</small></small> the work say of him that made it, He made me not? or shall the thing framed say of him that framed it, He had no understanding?
