***Hezekiah Shows His Treasures***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[2Kgs.20|2 Kings 20:12–19]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1645

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.39.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> At that time Merodach–baladan<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, the son of Baladan, king<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of Babylon, sent letters<small><small>[^4]</small></small> and a present to Hezekiah: for he had heard that he had been sick, and was recovered.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.39.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Hezekiah was glad<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of them, and shewed<small><small>[^7]</small></small> them the house of his precious things<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, the silver, and the gold, and the spices, and the precious ointment, and all the house of his armour<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, and all that was found in his treasures: there was<small><small>[^10]</small></small> nothing in his house, nor in all his dominion, that Hezekiah shewed them not. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.39.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then came Isaiah<small><small>[^11]</small></small> the prophet unto king Hezekiah, and said unto him, What said these men? and from whence came they unto thee? And Hezekiah said, They are<small><small>[^12]</small></small> come from a far country unto me, \[even\] from Babylon. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.39.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said he, What have they seen in thine house? And Hezekiah answered, All that<small><small>[^13]</small></small> \[is\] in mine house have they seen: there is nothing among my treasures that I have not shewed them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.39.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said Isaiah to Hezekiah, Hear<small><small>[^14]</small></small> the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.39.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, the days come, that all<small><small>[^15]</small></small> that \[is\] in thine house, and \[that\] which thy fathers have laid up in store until this day, shall be carried to Babylon: nothing shall be left, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.39.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And of thy sons<small><small>[^16]</small></small> that shall issue from thee, which thou shalt beget, shall they take away; and they shall be<small><small>[^17]</small></small> eunuchs in the palace of the king of Babylon. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.39.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said Hezekiah to Isaiah, Good<small><small>[^18]</small></small> \[is\] the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> which thou hast spoken. He said moreover, For<small><small>[^19]</small></small> there shall be peace and truth in my days. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Isa.38|<< Isaiah 38]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Isa.40|Isaiah 40 >>]]</td></tr></table>
