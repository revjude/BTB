***A Sign against Egypt and Cush*** ^bsb1612

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.20.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the year that Tartan<small><small>[^2]</small></small> came unto Ashdod<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, (when Sargon the king of Assyria sent him,) and fought against Ashdod, and took<small><small>[^4]</small></small> it; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.20.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> At the same time spake the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> by Isaiah<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the son of Amoz, saying, Go<small><small>[^6]</small></small> and loose the sackcloth<small><small>[^7]</small></small> from off thy loins, and put<small><small>[^8]</small></small> off thy shoe from thy foot. And he did so, walking naked<small><small>[^9]</small></small> and barefoot. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.20.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said, Like as my servant Isaiah hath walked naked and barefoot three<small><small>[^10]</small></small> years \[for\] a sign<small><small>[^11]</small></small> and wonder upon Egypt<small><small>[^12]</small></small> and upon Ethiopia; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.20.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So shall<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the king of Assyria lead away the Egyptians<small><small>[^14]</small></small> prisoners, and the Ethiopians captives, young and old, naked and barefoot, even with \[their<small><small>[^15]</small></small>\] buttocks uncovered, to the shame<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of Egypt. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.20.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they shall be afraid<small><small>[^17]</small></small> and ashamed of Ethiopia their expectation, and of Egypt their glory<small><small>[^18]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.20.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the inhabitant of this isle<small><small>[^19]</small></small> shall say in that day, Behold, such \[is\] our expectation, whither<small><small>[^20]</small></small> we flee for help to be delivered from the king of Assyria: and how<small><small>[^21]</small></small> shall we escape? <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Isa.19|<< Isaiah 19]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Isa.21|Isaiah 21 >>]]</td></tr></table>
