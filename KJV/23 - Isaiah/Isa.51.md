***Salvation for Zion*** ^bsb1667

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hearken<small><small>[^2]</small></small> to me, ye that follow<small><small>[^3]</small></small> after righteousness, ye that seek<small><small>[^4]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: look<small><small>[^5]</small></small> unto the rock \[whence\] ye are hewn, and to the hole of the pit \[whence\] ye are digged. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Look unto Abraham<small><small>[^6]</small></small> your father, and unto Sarah \[that\] bare you: for<small><small>[^7]</small></small> I called him alone, and blessed him, and increased him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall<small><small>[^8]</small></small> comfort Zion: he will comfort all her waste places; and he will make<small><small>[^9]</small></small> her wilderness like Eden, and her desert like the<small><small>[^10]</small></small> garden of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; joy<small><small>[^11]</small></small> and gladness shall be found therein, thanksgiving, and the voice of melody.<small><small>[^12]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hearken unto me, my people; and give ear unto me, O my<small><small>[^13]</small></small> nation: for a law<small><small>[^14]</small></small> shall proceed from me, and I will make<small><small>[^15]</small></small> my judgment to rest for a light of the people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My righteousness<small><small>[^16]</small></small> \[is\] near; my salvation<small><small>[^17]</small></small> is gone forth, and mine<small><small>[^18]</small></small> arms shall judge the people; the isles<small><small>[^19]</small></small> shall wait upon me, and on mine arm shall they trust. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lift up<small><small>[^20]</small></small> your eyes to the heavens<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, and look upon the earth beneath: for the heavens shall vanish away like smoke, and the earth shall wax old like a garment, and they that dwell therein shall die in like manner: but my salvation<small><small>[^22]</small></small> shall be for ever, and my righteousness shall not be abolished. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Hearken<small><small>[^23]</small></small> unto me, ye that<small><small>[^24]</small></small> know righteousness, the people in whose<small><small>[^25]</small></small> heart \[is\] my law; fear<small><small>[^26]</small></small> ye not the reproach of men, neither be ye afraid of their revilings. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the moth<small><small>[^27]</small></small> shall eat them up like a garment, and the worm shall eat them like wool: but my righteousness<small><small>[^28]</small></small> shall be for ever, and my salvation from generation to generation. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Awake<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, awake, put<small><small>[^30]</small></small> on strength, O arm<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; awake, as in<small><small>[^32]</small></small> the ancient days, in the generations of old. \[Art\] thou<small><small>[^33]</small></small> not it that hath cut Rahab, \[and\] wounded the dragon<small><small>[^34]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Art\] thou not it which hath dried<small><small>[^35]</small></small> the sea, the waters of the great deep; that hath made the depths of the sea a way for the ransomed to pass over? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore the redeemed<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall return, and come with singing unto Zion; and everlasting<small><small>[^37]</small></small> joy \[shall be\] upon their head: they shall obtain gladness and joy; \[and\] sorrow<small><small>[^38]</small></small> and mourning shall flee away. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I, \[even\] I, \[am\] he<small><small>[^39]</small></small> that comforteth you: who \[art\] thou, that thou<small><small>[^40]</small></small> shouldest be afraid of a man \[that\] shall die, and of the son of man \[which<small><small>[^41]</small></small>\] shall be made \[as\] grass; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And forgettest<small><small>[^42]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy maker, that hath<small><small>[^43]</small></small> stretched forth the heavens, and laid the foundations of the earth; and hast feared<small><small>[^44]</small></small> continually every day because of the fury of the oppressor, as if he were ready<small><small>[^45]</small></small> to destroy? and where \[is<small><small>[^46]</small></small>\] the fury of the oppressor? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The captive<small><small>[^47]</small></small> exile hasteneth that he may be loosed, and that he should not die<small><small>[^48]</small></small> in the pit, nor that his bread should fail. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, that divided<small><small>[^49]</small></small> the sea, whose waves roared: The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts \[is\] his name.<small><small>[^50]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I have put<small><small>[^51]</small></small> my words in thy mouth, and I have covered<small><small>[^52]</small></small> thee in the shadow of mine hand, that I may plant<small><small>[^53]</small></small> the heavens, and lay<small><small>[^54]</small></small> the foundations of the earth, and say unto Zion, Thou \[art<small><small>[^55]</small></small>\] my people.

***God’s Fury Removed*** ^bsb1668

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Awake, awake<small><small>[^56]</small></small>, stand up, O Jerusalem, which hast<small><small>[^57]</small></small> drunk at the hand of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> the cup of his fury; thou hast drunken the dregs of the cup of trembling, \[and\] wrung \[them\] out. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] none<small><small>[^58]</small></small> to guide her among all the sons \[whom\] she hath brought forth; neither \[is there any\] that taketh<small><small>[^59]</small></small> her by the hand of all the sons \[that\] she hath brought up. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> These two \[things<small><small>[^60]</small></small>\] are come<small><small>[^61]</small></small> unto thee; who shall<small><small>[^62]</small></small> be sorry for thee? desolation, and destruction<small><small>[^63]</small></small>, and the famine, and the sword: by whom<small><small>[^64]</small></small> shall I comfort thee? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.51.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy sons<small><small>[^65]</small></small> have fainted, they lie at the head of all the streets, as a wild<small><small>[^66]</small></small> bull in a net: they are full<small><small>[^67]</small></small> of the fury of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, the rebuke of thy God.
