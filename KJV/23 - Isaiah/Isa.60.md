***Future Glory for Zion*** ^bsb1683

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Arise<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, shine; for thy light is come<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, and the glory<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> is risen upon thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For, behold, the darkness<small><small>[^5]</small></small> shall cover the earth, and gross darkness the people: but the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall arise upon thee, and his glory shall be seen upon thee.<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the Gentiles<small><small>[^7]</small></small> shall come to thy light, and kings<small><small>[^8]</small></small> to the brightness of thy rising. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lift<small><small>[^9]</small></small> up thine eyes round about, and see: all they gather themselves together, they come<small><small>[^10]</small></small> to thee: thy sons shall come from far, and thy daughters shall be nursed at \[thy\] side. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then thou shalt see<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, and flow together, and thine heart shall fear, and be enlarged<small><small>[^12]</small></small>; because the abundance of the sea shall be converted unto thee<small><small>[^13]</small></small>, the forces<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of the Gentiles shall come unto thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The multitude<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of camels shall cover thee, the dromedaries of Midian<small><small>[^16]</small></small> and Ephah; all<small><small>[^17]</small></small> they from Sheba shall come: they shall bring<small><small>[^18]</small></small> gold and incense; and they shall shew forth the praises of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^19]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All the flocks<small><small>[^20]</small></small> of Kedar shall be gathered together unto thee, the rams of Nebaioth shall minister unto thee: they shall<small><small>[^21]</small></small> come up with acceptance on mine altar, and I will<small><small>[^22]</small></small> glorify the house of my glory. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who \[are\] these \[that\] fly<small><small>[^23]</small></small> as a cloud<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, and as the<small><small>[^25]</small></small> doves to their windows? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Surely the isles<small><small>[^26]</small></small> shall wait for me, and the ships<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of Tarshish first, to bring thy sons<small><small>[^28]</small></small> from far, their silver and their gold with them, unto<small><small>[^29]</small></small> the name of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, and to the Holy One of Israel, because<small><small>[^30]</small></small> he hath glorified thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the sons<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of strangers shall build up thy walls, and their kings<small><small>[^32]</small></small> shall minister unto thee: for in my wrath<small><small>[^33]</small></small> I smote thee, but in my favour have I had mercy on thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore<small><small>[^34]</small></small> thy gates<small><small>[^35]</small></small> shall be open continually; they shall not be shut day nor night; that \[men\] may bring unto thee the forces<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of the Gentiles, and \[that\] their kings \[may be\] brought. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the nation and kingdom that will not serve thee shall perish; yea, \[those\] nations shall be utterly wasted.<small><small>[^37]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The glory<small><small>[^38]</small></small> of Lebanon shall come unto thee, the fir tree, the pine tree, and the box together, to beautify<small><small>[^39]</small></small> the place<small><small>[^40]</small></small> of my sanctuary; and I will make the place of my feet glorious. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The sons<small><small>[^41]</small></small> also of them that afflicted thee shall come bending unto thee; and all they that despised thee shall bow themselves down at the soles of thy feet; and they shall call thee, The city<small><small>[^42]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, The Zion of the Holy One of Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whereas thou<small><small>[^43]</small></small> hast been forsaken and hated, so that no man went through \[thee\], I will make thee an eternal excellency, a joy<small><small>[^44]</small></small> of many generations. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt also suck the milk<small><small>[^45]</small></small> of the Gentiles, and shalt suck the breast of kings: and thou shalt know<small><small>[^46]</small></small> that I the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[am\] thy Saviour and thy Redeemer, the mighty One of Jacob. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For brass<small><small>[^47]</small></small> I will bring gold, and for iron I will bring silver, and for wood brass, and for stones iron: I will also make<small><small>[^48]</small></small> thy officers peace, and thine exactors righteousness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Violence<small><small>[^49]</small></small> shall no more be heard in thy land, wasting nor destruction within thy borders; but<small><small>[^50]</small></small> thou shalt call thy walls Salvation, and thy gates Praise. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The sun<small><small>[^51]</small></small> shall be no more thy light by day; neither for brightness shall the moon give light unto thee: but the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall be unto thee an everlasting light, and thy God<small><small>[^52]</small></small> thy glory. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy sun<small><small>[^53]</small></small> shall no more go down; neither shall thy moon withdraw itself: for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall be thine everlasting light, and the days<small><small>[^54]</small></small> of thy mourning shall be ended. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy people<small><small>[^55]</small></small> also \[shall be\] all righteous: they shall inherit<small><small>[^56]</small></small> the land for ever, the branch<small><small>[^57]</small></small> of my planting, the work of my hands, that I<small><small>[^58]</small></small> may be glorified. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.60.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A little<small><small>[^59]</small></small> one shall become a thousand, and a small one a strong nation: I the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> will hasten it in his time. [^34]: The subject of this chapter, says Bp. Lowth, is the great increase and flourishing state of the church of God, by the conversion and accession of the heathen nations to it; which is set forth in such ample and exalted terms, as plainly shew that the full completion of this prophecy is reserved for future times. This subject is displayed in the most splendid colours, under a great variety of images highly poetical, designed to give a general idea of the glories of that perfect state of the church of God, which we are taught to expect in the latter times; when the fulness of the Gentiles shall come in, and the Jews shall be converted and gathered from their dispersions, and "the kingdom of this world shall become the kingdom of our Lord and of His Christ."
