<big><big>[[Isa.17.1 The Burden against Damascus|⥃]]</big></big>***The Burden against Damascus***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Jer.49|Jeremiah 49:23–27]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The burden<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of Damascus<small><small>[^3]</small></small>. Behold, Damascus is<small><small>[^4]</small></small> taken away from \[being\] a city, and it shall be a ruinous<small><small>[^5]</small></small> heap.<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The cities of Aroer<small><small>[^7]</small></small> \[are\] forsaken: they shall<small><small>[^8]</small></small> be for flocks, which shall lie down, and none<small><small>[^9]</small></small> shall make \[them\] afraid. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The fortress<small><small>[^10]</small></small> also shall cease from Ephraim, and the kingdom from Damascus, and the remnant of Syria: they shall<small><small>[^11]</small></small> be as the glory of the children of Israel, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And in that day it shall come to pass, \[that\] the glory<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of Jacob shall be made thin, and the fatness<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of his flesh shall wax lean. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it shall be as when<small><small>[^14]</small></small> the harvestman gathereth the corn, and reapeth the ears with his arm; and it shall be as he that gathereth ears in the valley<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of Rephaim. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet gleaning grapes shall be left in it, as the shaking of an olive tree, two \[or\] three berries in the top of the uppermost bough, four \[or\] five in the outmost fruitful branches thereof, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of Israel.<small><small>[^16]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> At that day shall a man look to his Maker, and his eyes shall have respect to the Holy One of Israel.<small><small>[^17]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he shall<small><small>[^18]</small></small> not look to the altars, the work<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of his hands, neither shall respect \[that\] which his fingers have made, either the groves, or the images<small><small>[^20]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In that day shall his strong cities be as a forsaken bough, and an uppermost branch, which they left because of the children of Israel: and there shall be desolation.<small><small>[^21]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because thou hast<small><small>[^22]</small></small> forgotten the God<small><small>[^23]</small></small> of thy salvation, and hast not been mindful of the rock<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of thy strength, therefore shalt thou<small><small>[^25]</small></small> plant pleasant plants, and shalt set it with strange slips: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the day shalt thou make thy plant to grow, and in the morning shalt thou make thy seed to flourish: \[but\] the harvest<small><small>[^26]</small></small> \[shall be\] a heap in the day of grief and of desperate sorrow<small><small>[^27]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Woe to the multitude<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of many people, \[which\] make a noise<small><small>[^29]</small></small> like the noise of the seas; and to the rushing of nations, \[that\] make a rushing like the rushing of mighty<small><small>[^30]</small></small> waters! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The nations shall rush like the rushing of many waters: but<small><small>[^31]</small></small> \[God\] shall rebuke<small><small>[^32]</small></small> them, and they shall flee far off, and shall be<small><small>[^33]</small></small> chased as the chaff of the mountains before the wind, and like a rolling thing<small><small>[^34]</small></small> before the whirlwind. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.17.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And behold at eveningtide<small><small>[^35]</small></small> trouble; \[and\] before the morning he \[is\] not. This \[is\] the portion<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of them that spoil us, and the lot of them that rob us. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Isa.16|<< Isaiah 16]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Isa.18|Isaiah 18 >>]]</td></tr></table>
