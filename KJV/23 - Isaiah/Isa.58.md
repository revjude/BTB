***True Fasts and Sabbaths*** ^bsb1680

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Cry aloud<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, spare<small><small>[^3]</small></small> not, lift up<small><small>[^4]</small></small> thy voice like<small><small>[^5]</small></small> a trumpet, and shew my people their transgression, and the house of Jacob their sins. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet they seek<small><small>[^6]</small></small> me daily, and delight to know my ways, as a nation that did righteousness, and forsook not the ordinance of their God: they ask<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of me the ordinances of justice; they take delight in approaching to God. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore have we fasted<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, \[say they\], and thou seest not? \[wherefore\] have we afflicted<small><small>[^9]</small></small> our soul, and thou takest no knowledge? Behold, in<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the day of your fast ye find pleasure, and exact<small><small>[^11]</small></small> all your labours<small><small>[^12]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, ye fast<small><small>[^13]</small></small> for strife and debate, and to smite<small><small>[^14]</small></small> with the fist of wickedness: ye shall not fast as \[ye do this\] day<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, to make your voice to be heard on high. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Is it such<small><small>[^16]</small></small> a fast that I have chosen? a day for a man to afflict his soul<small><small>[^17]</small></small>? \[is it\] to bow down his head as a bulrush, and to spread<small><small>[^18]</small></small> sackcloth and ashes \[under him\]? wilt thou call this a fast, and an acceptable<small><small>[^19]</small></small> day to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Is\] not this the fast that I have chosen? to loose<small><small>[^20]</small></small> the bands of wickedness, to undo the heavy burdens<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, and to let the oppressed<small><small>[^22]</small></small> go free, and that ye break every yoke? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Is it\] not to deal<small><small>[^23]</small></small> thy bread to the hungry, and that thou bring<small><small>[^24]</small></small> the poor that are cast out<small><small>[^25]</small></small> to thy house? when thou seest the naked<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, that thou cover him; and that thou hide not thyself from thine own<small><small>[^27]</small></small> flesh? 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shall thy light<small><small>[^28]</small></small> break forth as the morning, and thine<small><small>[^29]</small></small> health shall spring forth speedily: and thy<small><small>[^30]</small></small> righteousness shall go before thee; the glory<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall be thy rereward<small><small>[^32]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shalt thou<small><small>[^33]</small></small> call, and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall answer; thou shalt cry, and he shall say, Here<small><small>[^34]</small></small> I \[am\]. If thou take away from the midst of thee the yoke<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, the putting<small><small>[^36]</small></small> forth of the finger, and speaking<small><small>[^37]</small></small> vanity; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And \[if\] thou draw<small><small>[^38]</small></small> out thy soul<small><small>[^39]</small></small> to the hungry, and satisfy the afflicted soul; then<small><small>[^40]</small></small> shall thy light rise in obscurity, and thy darkness \[be\] as the noonday: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall guide thee continually, and satisfy<small><small>[^41]</small></small> thy soul in drought<small><small>[^42]</small></small>, and make fat<small><small>[^43]</small></small> thy bones: and thou shalt be like<small><small>[^44]</small></small> a watered garden, and like a spring of water, whose waters fail<small><small>[^45]</small></small> not.<small><small>[^46]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And \[they that shall be\] of thee shall build<small><small>[^47]</small></small> the old waste<small><small>[^48]</small></small> places: thou shalt raise up the foundations of many generations; and thou shalt be called, The repairer<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of the breach, The restorer of paths to dwell in. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If thou turn<small><small>[^50]</small></small> away thy foot from the sabbath, \[from\] doing thy pleasure on my holy day; and call<small><small>[^51]</small></small> the sabbath a delight, the holy of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, honourable; and shalt honour him, not doing thine own ways, nor finding thine own pleasure, nor speaking \[thine own\] words: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.58.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shalt thou delight<small><small>[^52]</small></small> thyself in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and I will cause thee to ride<small><small>[^53]</small></small> upon the high places of the earth, and feed<small><small>[^54]</small></small> thee with the heritage of Jacob thy father: for the mouth<small><small>[^55]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath spoken \[it\]. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Isa.57|<< Isaiah 57]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Isa.59|Isaiah 59 >>]]</td></tr></table>
