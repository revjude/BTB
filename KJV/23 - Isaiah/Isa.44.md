<big><big>[[Isa.44.1 The LORD Has Chosen Israel|⥃]]</big></big>***The LORD Has Chosen Israel***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet now<small><small>[^2]</small></small> hear, O Jacob<small><small>[^3]</small></small> my servant; and Israel, whom I have chosen: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> that made<small><small>[^4]</small></small> thee, and formed<small><small>[^5]</small></small> thee from the womb, \[which\] will help thee; Fear<small><small>[^6]</small></small> not, O Jacob, my servant; and thou, Jesurun<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, whom I have chosen. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I will pour water<small><small>[^8]</small></small> upon him that is thirsty, and floods<small><small>[^9]</small></small> upon the dry ground<small><small>[^10]</small></small>: I will pour my<small><small>[^11]</small></small> spirit upon thy seed, and my blessing upon thine offspring: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they shall spring<small><small>[^12]</small></small> up \[as\] among the grass, as willows<small><small>[^13]</small></small> by the water courses. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> One shall say, I \[am<small><small>[^14]</small></small>\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span>; and another shall call \[himself\] by the name of Jacob; and another shall subscribe<small><small>[^15]</small></small> \[with\] his hand unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and surname \[himself\] by the name<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> the King<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of Israel, and his redeemer<small><small>[^18]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts; I \[am\] the first<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, and I \[am\] the last; and beside<small><small>[^20]</small></small> me \[there is\] no God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And who<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, as I, shall call, and shall declare it, and set it in order for me, since<small><small>[^22]</small></small> I appointed the ancient people? and the things that are coming, and shall come, let them shew unto them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Fear ye not, neither<small><small>[^23]</small></small> be afraid: have not I told thee from that time, and have declared<small><small>[^24]</small></small> \[it\]? ye \[are<small><small>[^25]</small></small>\] even my witnesses. Is there<small><small>[^26]</small></small> a God beside me? yea, \[there is\] no God<small><small>[^27]</small></small>; I know not \[any\]. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They that make<small><small>[^28]</small></small> a graven image \[are\] all of them vanity; and their<small><small>[^29]</small></small> delectable<small><small>[^30]</small></small> things shall not profit; and they \[are\] their own<small><small>[^31]</small></small> witnesses; they see not, nor know; that they may be ashamed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who hath formed a god, or molten a graven image \[that\] is profitable for nothing?<small><small>[^32]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, all his<small><small>[^33]</small></small> fellows shall be ashamed: and the workmen, they \[are\] of men: let them all<small><small>[^34]</small></small> be gathered together, let them stand up; \[yet\] they shall fear, \[and\] they shall be ashamed together. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The smith<small><small>[^35]</small></small> with the tongs<small><small>[^36]</small></small> both worketh in the coals, and fashioneth it with hammers, and worketh it with the strength of his arms: yea, he is<small><small>[^37]</small></small> hungry, and his strength faileth: he drinketh no water, and is faint. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The carpenter stretcheth out \[his\] rule; he marketh<small><small>[^38]</small></small> it out with a line; he fitteth it with planes, and he marketh it out with the compass, and maketh it after the figure of a man, according to the beauty of a man; that it may<small><small>[^39]</small></small> remain in the house. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He heweth<small><small>[^40]</small></small> him down cedars, and taketh the cypress and the oak, which he strengtheneth<small><small>[^41]</small></small> for himself among the trees of the forest: he planteth an ash, and the rain doth nourish \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shall it be for a man to burn: for he will take thereof, and warm himself; yea, he kindleth \[it\], and baketh bread; yea, he maketh a god<small><small>[^42]</small></small>, and worshippeth \[it\]; he maketh it a graven image, and falleth down thereto. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He burneth part thereof in the fire; with part thereof he eateth flesh; he roasteth roast, and is satisfied: yea, he warmeth \[himself\], and saith, Aha, I am warm, I have seen the fire: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the residue thereof he maketh a god, \[even\] his graven image: he falleth down unto it, and worshippeth \[it\], and prayeth unto it, and saith, Deliver me<small><small>[^43]</small></small>; for thou \[art\] my god. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They have not<small><small>[^44]</small></small> known nor understood: for he hath<small><small>[^45]</small></small> shut<small><small>[^46]</small></small> their eyes, that they cannot<small><small>[^47]</small></small> see; \[and\] their hearts, that they cannot understand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And none considereth in his heart<small><small>[^48]</small></small>, neither \[is there\] knowledge nor understanding to say, I have burned part of it in the fire; yea, also I have baked bread upon the coals thereof; I have roasted flesh, and eaten \[it\]: and shall I make the residue thereof an abomination<small><small>[^49]</small></small>? shall I fall down to the stock of a tree<small><small>[^50]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He feedeth<small><small>[^51]</small></small> on ashes: a deceived<small><small>[^52]</small></small> heart hath turned him aside, that he cannot deliver his soul, nor say, \[Is there<small><small>[^53]</small></small>\] not a lie in my right hand?

<big><big>[[Isa.44.21 Jerusalem to Be Restored|⥃]]</big></big>***Jerusalem to Be Restored***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Remember<small><small>[^54]</small></small> these, O Jacob and Israel; for thou \[art<small><small>[^55]</small></small>\] my servant: I have formed thee; thou \[art\] my servant: O Israel, thou shalt<small><small>[^56]</small></small> not be forgotten of me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have blotted<small><small>[^57]</small></small> out, as a thick<small><small>[^58]</small></small> cloud, thy transgressions, and, as a cloud, thy sins: return<small><small>[^59]</small></small> unto me; for I have redeemed thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Sing<small><small>[^60]</small></small>, O ye heavens; for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath done \[it\]: shout, ye lower parts of the earth: break forth into singing, ye mountains, O forest, and every tree therein: for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath redeemed Jacob, and glorified<small><small>[^61]</small></small> himself in Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, thy redeemer<small><small>[^62]</small></small>, and he<small><small>[^63]</small></small> that formed thee from the womb, I \[am<small><small>[^64]</small></small>\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> that maketh all \[things\]; that stretcheth forth the heavens alone; that spreadeth abroad the earth by myself<small><small>[^65]</small></small>; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That frustrateth<small><small>[^66]</small></small> the tokens of the liars, and maketh<small><small>[^67]</small></small> diviners mad; that turneth<small><small>[^68]</small></small> wise \[men\] backward, and maketh their knowledge foolish; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That confirmeth<small><small>[^69]</small></small> the word of his servant, and performeth the counsel of his messengers; that saith<small><small>[^70]</small></small> to Jerusalem, Thou shalt be inhabited; and to the cities of Judah, Ye shall be built, and I will<small><small>[^71]</small></small> raise up the decayed places<small><small>[^72]</small></small> thereof: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That saith to the deep, Be dry<small><small>[^73]</small></small>, and I will dry up thy rivers: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.44.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That saith of Cyrus<small><small>[^74]</small></small>, \[He is\] my shepherd<small><small>[^75]</small></small>, and shall perform all my pleasure: even saying<small><small>[^76]</small></small> to Jerusalem, Thou shalt be built;
