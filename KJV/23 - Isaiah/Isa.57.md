<big><big>[[Isa.57.1 The Blessed Death of the Righteous|⥃]]</big></big>***The Blessed Death of the Righteous***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The righteous<small><small>[^2]</small></small> perisheth, and no man<small><small>[^3]</small></small> layeth \[it\] to heart: and merciful men<small><small>[^4]</small></small> \[are\] taken away, none considering that the righteous<small><small>[^5]</small></small> is taken away from the evil \[to come<small><small>[^6]</small></small>\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He shall<small><small>[^7]</small></small> enter into<small><small>[^8]</small></small> peace: they shall rest<small><small>[^9]</small></small> in their beds, \[each one\] walking \[in\] his uprightness<small><small>[^10]</small></small>.

<big><big>[[Isa.57.3 God Condemns Idolatry|⥃]]</big></big>***God Condemns Idolatry***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But draw<small><small>[^11]</small></small> near hither, ye sons<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of the sorceress, the seed of the adulterer and the whore. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Against<small><small>[^13]</small></small> whom do ye sport<small><small>[^14]</small></small> yourselves? against whom make<small><small>[^15]</small></small> ye a wide mouth, \[and\] draw<small><small>[^16]</small></small> out the tongue? \[are\] ye<small><small>[^17]</small></small> not children of transgression, a seed of falsehood, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Enflaming<small><small>[^18]</small></small> yourselves with idols<small><small>[^19]</small></small> under<small><small>[^20]</small></small> every green tree, slaying<small><small>[^21]</small></small> the children in the valleys under the clifts of the rocks? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Among the smooth<small><small>[^22]</small></small> \[stones\] of the stream \[is\] thy portion; they, they \[are\] thy lot: even to them<small><small>[^23]</small></small> hast thou poured a drink offering, thou hast offered a meat offering. Should<small><small>[^24]</small></small> I receive comfort in these? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Upon a lofty and high mountain hast thou set thy bed: even thither wentest thou up to offer sacrifice.<small><small>[^25]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behind the doors<small><small>[^26]</small></small> also and the posts hast thou set up thy remembrance: for<small><small>[^27]</small></small> thou hast discovered \[thyself to another\] than me, and art gone up; thou hast enlarged thy bed, and made thee \[a covenant\] with them<small><small>[^28]</small></small>; thou lovedst<small><small>[^29]</small></small> their bed where thou sawest \[it<small><small>[^30]</small></small>\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou wentest to the king<small><small>[^31]</small></small> with ointment, and didst increase thy perfumes<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, and didst send thy messengers far off, and didst debase<small><small>[^33]</small></small> \[thyself even\] unto hell. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou art wearied<small><small>[^34]</small></small> in the greatness of thy way; \[yet\] saidst thou not, There is<small><small>[^35]</small></small> no hope: thou hast found the life<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of thine hand; therefore<small><small>[^37]</small></small> thou wast not grieved. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And of whom<small><small>[^38]</small></small> hast thou been afraid or feared, that thou<small><small>[^39]</small></small> hast lied, and hast<small><small>[^40]</small></small> not remembered me, nor<small><small>[^41]</small></small> laid \[it\] to thy heart? have not<small><small>[^42]</small></small> I held my peace even of old, and thou fearest me not? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will declare thy righteousness, and thy works; for they shall not profit thee.<small><small>[^43]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When thou criest, let<small><small>[^44]</small></small> thy companies deliver thee; but the<small><small>[^45]</small></small> wind shall carry them all away; vanity shall take \[them\]: but he<small><small>[^46]</small></small> that putteth his trust in me shall possess the land, and shall inherit my holy<small><small>[^47]</small></small> mountain;

<big><big>[[Isa.57.14 Healing for the Repentant|⥃]]</big></big>***Healing for the Repentant***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And shall say, Cast<small><small>[^48]</small></small> ye up, cast ye up, prepare the way, take<small><small>[^49]</small></small> up the stumblingblock out of the way of my people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thus saith the high<small><small>[^50]</small></small> and lofty One that inhabiteth<small><small>[^51]</small></small> eternity, whose<small><small>[^52]</small></small> name \[is\] Holy; I dwell<small><small>[^53]</small></small> in the high and holy \[place\], with<small><small>[^54]</small></small> him also \[that is\] of a contrite and humble spirit, to revive the spirit<small><small>[^55]</small></small> of the humble, and to revive the heart of the contrite ones. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I will not<small><small>[^56]</small></small> contend for ever, neither will I be always wroth: for the spirit should fail before me, and the souls<small><small>[^57]</small></small> \[which\] I have made. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the iniquity<small><small>[^58]</small></small> of his covetousness was I wroth, and smote him: I hid<small><small>[^59]</small></small> me, and was wroth, and he<small><small>[^60]</small></small> went on frowardly<small><small>[^61]</small></small> in the<small><small>[^62]</small></small> way of his heart. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have<small><small>[^63]</small></small> seen his ways, and will heal<small><small>[^64]</small></small> him: I will lead<small><small>[^65]</small></small> him also, and restore<small><small>[^66]</small></small> comforts unto him and to his<small><small>[^67]</small></small> mourners. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I create<small><small>[^68]</small></small> the fruit<small><small>[^69]</small></small> of the lips; Peace<small><small>[^70]</small></small>, peace to \[him that is\] far off, and to \[him that is\] near, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and I will heal him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the wicked \[are\] like<small><small>[^71]</small></small> the troubled sea, when it cannot rest, whose waters cast up mire and dirt. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.57.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] no peace, saith my God, to the wicked. <small><small>[^72]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Isa.56|<< Isaiah 56]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Isa.58|Isaiah 58 >>]]</td></tr></table>
