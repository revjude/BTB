<big><big>[[Isa.10.1 Woe to Tyrants|⥃]]</big></big>***Woe to Tyrants***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Woe<small><small>[^2]</small></small> unto them<small><small>[^3]</small></small> that decree unrighteous decrees, and that write grievousness<small><small>[^4]</small></small> \[which\] they have prescribed;<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To turn aside<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the needy from judgment, and to take away the right from the poor of my people, that widows<small><small>[^7]</small></small> may be their prey, and \[that\] they may rob the fatherless! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And what<small><small>[^8]</small></small> will ye do in the day<small><small>[^9]</small></small> of visitation, and in the desolation<small><small>[^10]</small></small> \[which\] shall come from far? to whom<small><small>[^11]</small></small> will ye flee for help? and where<small><small>[^12]</small></small> will ye leave your glory? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Without me<small><small>[^13]</small></small> they shall bow down under the prisoners, and they shall fall under the slain. For all this<small><small>[^14]</small></small> his anger is not turned away, but his hand \[is\] stretched out still.

<big><big>[[Isa.10.5 Judgment on Assyria|⥃]]</big></big>***Judgment on Assyria***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O Assyrian<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, the rod<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of mine anger, and<small><small>[^17]</small></small> the staff in their hand is mine indignation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will send him against<small><small>[^18]</small></small> an hypocritical nation, and against the people of my wrath will I give<small><small>[^19]</small></small> him a charge, to take the spoil, and to take the prey, and to tread them<small><small>[^20]</small></small> down like the mire of the streets. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Howbeit he meaneth<small><small>[^21]</small></small> not so, neither doth his heart think so; but \[it is\] in his heart<small><small>[^22]</small></small> to destroy and cut off nations not a few. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he saith, \[Are\] not my princes altogether kings?<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Is\] not Calno<small><small>[^24]</small></small> as Carchemish<small><small>[^25]</small></small>? \[is\] not Hamath<small><small>[^26]</small></small> as Arpad? \[is\] not Samaria<small><small>[^27]</small></small> as Damascus? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As my hand hath found the kingdoms<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of the idols, and whose graven images did excel them of Jerusalem and of Samaria; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Shall I not, as I have<small><small>[^29]</small></small> done unto Samaria and her idols, so do to Jerusalem and her idols? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore it shall come to pass, \[that\] when the Lord<small><small>[^30]</small></small> hath performed his whole work upon mount Zion and on Jerusalem, I will<small><small>[^31]</small></small> punish the fruit of the stout heart<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of the king of Assyria, and the glory<small><small>[^33]</small></small> of his high looks. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he saith<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, By the strength of my hand I have done \[it\], and by my wisdom; for I am prudent: and I have removed<small><small>[^35]</small></small> the bounds of the people, and have robbed<small><small>[^36]</small></small> their treasures, and I have put down the inhabitants like a valiant \[man<small><small>[^37]</small></small>\]: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And my<small><small>[^38]</small></small> hand hath found as a nest the riches of the people: and as one gathereth eggs \[that are\] left, have I gathered all the earth; and there was none that moved the wing, or opened the mouth, or peeped<small><small>[^39]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Shall the ax<small><small>[^40]</small></small>e boast itself against him that heweth therewith? \[or\] shall the saw magnify itself against him that shaketh it? as if the rod should shake \[itself\] against them<small><small>[^41]</small></small> that lift it up, \[or\] as if the staff should lift up \[itself\], \[as if it were\] no wood. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore shall the Lord, the Lord of hosts<small><small>[^42]</small></small>, send among his fat ones leanness; and under<small><small>[^43]</small></small> his glory he shall kindle a burning like the burning of a fire. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the light<small><small>[^44]</small></small> of Israel shall be for a fire, and his Holy One for a flame<small><small>[^45]</small></small>: and it shall burn and devour<small><small>[^46]</small></small> his thorns and his briers in one day; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And shall consume<small><small>[^47]</small></small> the glory of his forest, and of his fruitful field, both soul and body<small><small>[^48]</small></small>: and they shall be as when a standardbearer fainteth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.10.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the rest of the trees of his forest shall be few<small><small>[^49]</small></small>, that a child may write them.
