***Israel’s Stubbornness*** ^bsb1662

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear ye this, O house of Jacob, which are<small><small>[^2]</small></small> called by the name of Israel, and are come<small><small>[^3]</small></small> forth out of the waters of Judah, which swear<small><small>[^4]</small></small> by the name of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and make mention<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of the God of Israel, \[but\] not in truth<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, nor in righteousness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For they call<small><small>[^7]</small></small> themselves of the holy city, and stay<small><small>[^8]</small></small> themselves upon the God of Israel; The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^9]</small></small></span> of hosts \[is\] his name. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have declared<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the former things from the beginning; and they went forth out of my mouth, and I<small><small>[^11]</small></small> shewed them; I did \[them\] suddenly, and they came to pass. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because I knew<small><small>[^12]</small></small> that thou \[art\] obstinate<small><small>[^13]</small></small>, and thy neck \[is\] an iron sinew, and thy brow<small><small>[^14]</small></small> brass; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have even<small><small>[^15]</small></small> from the beginning declared \[it\] to thee; before it came to pass I shewed \[it\] thee: lest thou shouldest say, Mine idol<small><small>[^16]</small></small> hath done them, and my graven image, and my molten image, hath commanded them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou hast heard<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, see all this; and will<small><small>[^18]</small></small> not ye declare \[it\]? I have shewed<small><small>[^19]</small></small> thee new things from this time, even hidden things, and thou didst not know them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They are created now, and not from the beginning; even before the day when thou heardest them not; lest thou shouldest say, Behold, I knew them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea, thou heardest<small><small>[^20]</small></small> not; yea, thou knewest not; yea, from that time \[that\] thine ear<small><small>[^21]</small></small> was not opened: for I knew<small><small>[^22]</small></small> that thou wouldest deal very treacherously, and wast called a transgressor<small><small>[^23]</small></small> from the womb. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For my name’s<small><small>[^24]</small></small> sake will I defer<small><small>[^25]</small></small> mine anger, and for my praise will I refrain for thee, that I cut thee not off. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, I have refined<small><small>[^26]</small></small> thee, but not with silver<small><small>[^27]</small></small>; I have chosen<small><small>[^28]</small></small> thee in the furnace of affliction. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For mine own<small><small>[^29]</small></small> sake, \[even\] for mine own sake, will I do \[it\]: for how<small><small>[^30]</small></small> should \[my name<small><small>[^31]</small></small>\] be polluted? and I will not<small><small>[^32]</small></small> give my glory unto another.

***Deliverance Promised to Israel*** ^bsb1663

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Hearken<small><small>[^33]</small></small> unto me, O Jacob<small><small>[^34]</small></small> and Israel, my called<small><small>[^35]</small></small>; I \[am\] he<small><small>[^36]</small></small>; I \[am\] the first, I also \[am\] the last. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Mine hand<small><small>[^37]</small></small><small><small>[^38]</small></small> also hath laid the foundation of the earth, and my right hand hath spanned<small><small>[^39]</small></small> the heavens: \[when<small><small>[^40]</small></small>\] I call unto them, they stand up together. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All ye, assemble<small><small>[^41]</small></small> yourselves, and hear; which among them<small><small>[^42]</small></small> hath declared these \[things\]? The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^43]</small></small></span> hath loved him: he will do<small><small>[^44]</small></small> his pleasure on Babylon, and his arm \[shall be on\] the Chaldeans. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I, \[even\] I, have spoken; yea, I have called him: I have brought him, and he shall make his way prosperous.<small><small>[^45]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Come ye near unto me, hear ye this; I have not<small><small>[^46]</small></small> spoken in secret from the beginning; from the time that it was, there \[am\] I: and now the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God<small><small>[^47]</small></small></span>, and his Spirit, hath sent me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^48]</small></small></span>, thy Redeemer, the Holy One of Israel; I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God which teacheth<small><small>[^49]</small></small> thee to profit, which leadeth<small><small>[^50]</small></small> thee by the way \[that\] thou shouldest go. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O that thou<small><small>[^51]</small></small> hadst hearkened to my commandments! then had<small><small>[^52]</small></small> thy peace been as a river, and thy righteousness as the waves of the sea: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy seed<small><small>[^53]</small></small> also had been as the sand, and the offspring of thy bowels like the gravel thereof; his name<small><small>[^54]</small></small> should not have been cut off nor destroyed from before me. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Go ye forth<small><small>[^55]</small></small> of Babylon, flee ye from the Chaldeans, with a voice<small><small>[^56]</small></small> of singing declare ye, tell this, utter it \[even<small><small>[^57]</small></small>\] to the end of the earth; say ye, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath redeemed his servant Jacob. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they thirsted<small><small>[^58]</small></small> not \[when\] he led them through the deserts: he caused<small><small>[^59]</small></small> the waters to flow out of the rock for them: he clave the rock also, and the waters gushed out. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.48.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] no peace, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, unto the wicked. <small><small>[^60]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Isa.47|<< Isaiah 47]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Isa.49|Isaiah 49 >>]]</td></tr></table>
