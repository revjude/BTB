<big><big>[[Isa.63.1 God’s Vengeance on the Nations|⥃]]</big></big>***God’s Vengeance on the Nations***

I that speak<small><small>[^8]</small></small> in righteousness, mighty<small><small>[^9]</small></small> to save. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.63.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore \[art thou\] red in thine apparel, and thy garments like him that treadeth in the winefat? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.63.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have trodden<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the winepress alone; and of the people<small><small>[^11]</small></small> \[there was\] none with me: for I will tread them in mine anger, and trample<small><small>[^12]</small></small> them in my fury; and their blood shall be sprinkled upon my garments, and I will stain all my raiment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.63.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the day of vengeance \[is\] in mine heart, and the year of my redeemed is come.<small><small>[^13]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.63.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I looked<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, and \[there was\] none to help; and I wondered that \[there was\] none to uphold: therefore mine own<small><small>[^15]</small></small> arm brought salvation unto me; and my fury<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, it upheld me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.63.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will tread down the people in mine anger, and make<small><small>[^17]</small></small> them drunk in my fury, and I will bring<small><small>[^18]</small></small> down their strength to the earth.

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.63.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]But they rebelled<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, and vexed<small><small>[^30]</small></small> his holy Spirit: therefore he was<small><small>[^31]</small></small> turned to be their enemy, \[and\] he fought against them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.63.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then he remembered<small><small>[^32]</small></small> the days of old, Moses, \[and\] his people, \[saying\], Where \[is\] he that brought<small><small>[^33]</small></small> them up out of the sea with the shepherd<small><small>[^34]</small></small> of his flock? where \[is\] he that put<small><small>[^35]</small></small> his holy Spirit within him? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.63.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That led \[them\] by the right hand of Moses with<small><small>[^36]</small></small> his glorious arm, dividing<small><small>[^37]</small></small> the water before them, to make<small><small>[^38]</small></small> himself an everlasting name? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.63.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That led them through the deep, as an horse in the wilderness, \[that\] they should not stumble?<small><small>[^39]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.63.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As a beast goeth down into the valley, the Spirit<small><small>[^40]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> caused him to rest: so didst thou lead thy people, to make<small><small>[^41]</small></small> thyself a glorious name.

Though, as Bp. Lowth observes, this prophecy must have its accomplishment, there is no necessity for supposing that it has been already accomplished. There are prophecies which intimate a great slaughter of the enemies of God and his people, which remain to be fulfilled. Those in [[Ezek.38|Ezek 38:1-23]], and [[Rev.20|Rev 20:1-15]] are called Gog and Magog. This prophecy of Isaiah may possibly refer to the same, or the like event.
