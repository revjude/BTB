<big><big>[[Isa.55.1 Invitation to the Needy|⥃]]</big></big>***Invitation to the Needy***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.55.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ho<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, every<small><small>[^3]</small></small> one that thirsteth, come ye to the waters, and<small><small>[^4]</small></small> he that hath no money; come ye, buy, and eat; yea, come, buy wine<small><small>[^5]</small></small> and milk<small><small>[^6]</small></small> without money<small><small>[^7]</small></small> and without price. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.55.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore do ye<small><small>[^8]</small></small> spend<small><small>[^9]</small></small> money for \[that which is\] not bread? and your labour for \[that which\] satisfieth not? hearken<small><small>[^10]</small></small> diligently unto me, and eat<small><small>[^11]</small></small> ye \[that which is\] good, and let your soul delight itself in fatness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.55.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Incline<small><small>[^12]</small></small> your ear, and come<small><small>[^13]</small></small> unto me: hear<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, and your soul shall live; and I will<small><small>[^15]</small></small> make an everlasting covenant with you, \[even\] the sure<small><small>[^16]</small></small> mercies of David. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.55.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, I have<small><small>[^17]</small></small> given him \[for\] a witness to the people, a leader<small><small>[^18]</small></small> and commander to the people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.55.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, thou shalt<small><small>[^19]</small></small> call a nation<small><small>[^20]</small></small> \[that\] thou knowest not, and nations \[that\] knew not the<small><small>[^21]</small></small>e shall run unto thee because of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, and for the Holy One of Israel; for he hath glorified thee. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.55.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Seek<small><small>[^22]</small></small> ye the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> while he may be found, call ye upon him while he is near<small><small>[^23]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.55.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let the wicked<small><small>[^24]</small></small> forsake his way, and the unrighteous man<small><small>[^25]</small></small> his thoughts<small><small>[^26]</small></small>: and let him return unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and he will have mercy upon him; and to our God, for<small><small>[^27]</small></small> he will abundantly<small><small>[^28]</small></small> pardon. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.55.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]For my thoughts \[are\] not your thoughts, neither \[are\] your ways my ways, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^29]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.55.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For \[as\] the heavens are higher than the earth, so are my ways higher than your ways, and my thoughts than your thoughts.<small><small>[^30]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.55.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For as the rain<small><small>[^31]</small></small> cometh down, and the snow from heaven, and returneth not thither, but watereth the earth, and maketh it bring forth and bud, that it may give<small><small>[^32]</small></small> seed to the sower, and bread to the eater: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.55.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So shall my<small><small>[^33]</small></small> word be that goeth forth out of my mouth: it shall not return unto me void, but it shall accomplish<small><small>[^34]</small></small> that which I please, and it shall prosper \[in the thing\] whereto I sent it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.55.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For ye shall<small><small>[^35]</small></small> go out with joy, and be led forth with peace: the mountains<small><small>[^36]</small></small> and the hills shall break forth before you into singing, and all the trees of the field shall clap<small><small>[^37]</small></small> \[their\] hands. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.55.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Instead of the thorn<small><small>[^38]</small></small> shall come up the fir tree, and instead of the brier shall come up the myrtle tree: and it shall be to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> for a<small><small>[^39]</small></small> name, for an everlasting<small><small>[^40]</small></small> sign \[that\] shall not be cut off. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td
