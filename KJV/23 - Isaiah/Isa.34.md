<big><big>[[Isa.34.1 Judgment on the Nations|⥃]]</big></big>***Judgment on the Nations***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Come<small><small>[^2]</small></small> near, ye nations, to hear; and hearken, ye people: let the<small><small>[^3]</small></small> earth hear, and all that is therein<small><small>[^4]</small></small>; the world, and all things that come forth of it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the indignation<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] upon all nations, and \[his<small><small>[^6]</small></small>\] fury upon all their armies: he hath utterly destroyed them, he hath delivered them to the slaughter. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Their slain<small><small>[^7]</small></small> also shall be cast out, and their stink shall come up out of their carcases, and the mountains<small><small>[^8]</small></small> shall be melted with their blood. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all the<small><small>[^9]</small></small> host of heaven shall be dissolved, and the heavens shall be rolled together as a scroll: and all their host shall fall down, as the leaf falleth off from the vine, and as a falling \[fig\] from the fig tree.

<big><big>[[Isa.34.5 Judgment on Edom|⥃]]</big></big>***Judgment on Edom***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Isa.21|Isaiah 21:11–12]] ]</sup></small>

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For my sword<small><small>[^10]</small></small> shall be bathed in heaven: behold, it shall come down upon Idumea<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, and upon the people<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of my curse, to judgment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The sword of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> is filled<small><small>[^13]</small></small> with blood, it is made fat with fatness, \[and\] with the blood of lambs and goats, with the fat<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of the kidneys of rams: for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath<small><small>[^15]</small></small> a sacrifice in Bozrah, and a great slaughter in the land of Idumea. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the unicorns<small><small>[^16]</small></small> shall come down with them, and the bullocks<small><small>[^17]</small></small> with the bulls; and their land shall be soaked<small><small>[^18]</small></small> with blood, and their dust made fat with fatness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For \[it is\] the day of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> vengeance, \[and\] the year of recompences for the controversy of Zion.<small><small>[^19]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the streams thereof shall be turned into pitch, and the dust thereof into brimstone, and the land thereof shall become burning pitch.<small><small>[^20]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> It shall not<small><small>[^21]</small></small> be quenched night nor day; the smoke<small><small>[^22]</small></small> thereof shall go up for ever: from<small><small>[^23]</small></small> generation to generation it shall lie waste; none shall pass through it for ever and ever. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]But the cormorant<small><small>[^24]</small></small> and the bittern shall possess it; the owl also and the raven shall dwell in it: and he shall stretch<small><small>[^25]</small></small> out upon it the line of confusion, and the stones of emptiness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They shall call<small><small>[^26]</small></small> the nobles thereof to the kingdom, but none \[shall be\] there, and all her princes shall be nothing<small><small>[^27]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thorns<small><small>[^28]</small></small> shall come up in her palaces, nettles and brambles in the fortresses thereof: and it shall be an habitation<small><small>[^29]</small></small> of dragons, \[and\] a court for owls<small><small>[^30]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wild beasts of the desert<small><small>[^31]</small></small> shall also meet with the wild beasts of the island<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, and the satyr shall cry to his fellow; the screech owl<small><small>[^33]</small></small> also shall rest there, and find for herself a place of rest. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There shall the great owl make her nest, and lay, and hatch, and gather under her shadow: there shall the vultures also be gathered, every one with her mate. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Seek<small><small>[^34]</small></small> ye out of the book of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and read: no one of these shall fail, none shall want her mate: for my mouth<small><small>[^35]</small></small> it hath commanded, and his spirit it hath gathered them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.34.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he hath cast<small><small>[^36]</small></small> the lot for them, and his hand hath divided it unto them by line: they shall<small><small>[^37]</small></small> possess it for ever, from generation to generation shall they dwell therein. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Isa.33|<< Isaiah 33]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Isa.35|Isaiah 35 >>]]</td></tr></table>


[^1]:  [[Isa.34|Isa 34:1]], The judgments wherewith God revenges his church; [[Isa.34|Isa 34:11]], The desolation of her enemies; [[Isa.34|Isa 34:16]], The certainty of the prophecy.

[^2]: This and the following chapter, as Bp. Lowth observes, form one distinct prophecy; an entire, regular, and beautiful poem, consisting of two parts; the first containing a denunciation of Divine vengeance against the enemies of the people or church of God; the second describing the flourishing state of that church consequent upon those judgments. The event foretold is represented as of the highest importance, and of universal concern; all nations are called upon to attend to the declaration of it; and the wrath of God is denounced against all the nations who had provoked to anger the Defender of the cause of Zion. By a figure frequently occurring in the prophetical writings, the cities and people mentioned here, who were remarkably distinguished as the enemies of the people of God, are put for those enemies in general.
