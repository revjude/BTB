<big><big>[[Isa.28.1 The Captivity of Ephraim|⥃]]</big></big>***The Captivity of Ephraim***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Woe to the crown<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of pride, to the drunkards<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of Ephraim, whose<small><small>[^4]</small></small> glorious beauty \[is\] a fading flower, which \[are\] on the head of the fat valleys of them that are overcome<small><small>[^5]</small></small> with wine!<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, the Lord<small><small>[^7]</small></small> hath a mighty and strong one, \[which\] as a tempest<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of hail \[and\] a destroying storm, as a flood of mighty waters overflowing, shall cast down to the earth with the hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The crown<small><small>[^9]</small></small> of pride, the drunkards of Ephraim, shall<small><small>[^10]</small></small> be trodden under feet<small><small>[^11]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the glorious beauty, which \[is\] on the head of the fat valley, shall be<small><small>[^12]</small></small> a fading flower, \[and\] as the hasty<small><small>[^13]</small></small> fruit before the summer; which \[when\] he that looketh upon it seeth, while it is yet in his hand he eateth<small><small>[^14]</small></small> it up. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]In that day shall the<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts be for a crown of glory, and for a diadem<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of beauty, unto the residue<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of his people, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And for a spirit<small><small>[^18]</small></small> of judgment to him that sitteth in judgment, and for strength<small><small>[^19]</small></small> to them that turn the battle to the gate. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]But they also have erred<small><small>[^20]</small></small> through wine, and through strong drink are out of the way; the priest and the prophet have erred through strong drink, they are swallowed<small><small>[^21]</small></small> up of wine, they are out of the way through strong drink; they err in<small><small>[^22]</small></small> vision, they stumble \[in\] judgment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For all tables are full of vomit \[and\] filthiness, \[so that there is\] no place \[clean\].<small><small>[^23]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whom shall he teach<small><small>[^24]</small></small> knowledge? and whom shall he make to understand doctrine<small><small>[^25]</small></small>? \[them that are\] weaned<small><small>[^26]</small></small> from the milk, \[and\] drawn from the breasts. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For precept<small><small>[^27]</small></small> \[must be<small><small>[^28]</small></small>\] upon precept, precept upon precept; line upon line, line upon line; here a little, \[and\] there a little: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For with<small><small>[^29]</small></small> stammering lips<small><small>[^30]</small></small> and another tongue will he speak<small><small>[^31]</small></small> to this people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To whom he said, This<small><small>[^32]</small></small> \[is\] the rest \[wherewith\] ye may cause the weary to rest; and this \[is\] the refreshing: yet<small><small>[^33]</small></small> they would not hear. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> was unto them precept upon precept<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, precept upon precept; line upon line, line upon line; here a little, \[and\] there a little; that<small><small>[^35]</small></small> they might go, and fall backward, and be broken, and snared, and taken.

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>, Behold<small><small>[^40]</small></small>, I lay in Zion for a foundation a stone, a tried stone, a precious corner \[stone\], a sure foundation: he that<small><small>[^41]</small></small> believeth shall not make haste. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Judgment<small><small>[^42]</small></small> also will I lay to the line, and righteousness to the plummet: and the hail<small><small>[^43]</small></small> shall sweep away the refuge of lies, and the waters<small><small>[^44]</small></small> shall overflow the hiding place. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And your covenant<small><small>[^45]</small></small> with death shall be disannulled<small><small>[^46]</small></small>, and your agreement with hell shall not stand; when<small><small>[^47]</small></small> the overflowing scourge shall pass through, then ye shall be trodden down by it<small><small>[^48]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> From the time<small><small>[^49]</small></small> that it goeth forth it shall take you: for morning by morning shall it pass over, by day and by night: and it<small><small>[^50]</small></small> shall be a vexation only \[to\] understand the report<small><small>[^51]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the bed<small><small>[^52]</small></small> is shorter than that \[a man\] can stretch himself \[on it\]: and the covering narrower than that he can wrap himself \[in it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall rise up as \[in\] mount Perazim<small><small>[^53]</small></small>, he shall be wroth as \[in\] the valley<small><small>[^54]</small></small> of Gibeon, that he may do his work, his strange<small><small>[^55]</small></small> work; and bring to pass his act, his strange act. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.28.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now therefore be ye<small><small>[^56]</small></small> not mockers, lest<small><small>[^57]</small></small> your bands be made strong: for I have heard from the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span> of hosts a consumption<small><small>[^58]</small></small>, even determined upon the whole earth.
