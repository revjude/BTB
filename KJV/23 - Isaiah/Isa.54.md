***Future Blessings for Zion*** ^bsb1673

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Sing, O barren<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, thou \[that\] didst not bear; break<small><small>[^3]</small></small> forth into singing, and cry aloud, thou \[that\] didst not travail with child: for more<small><small>[^4]</small></small> \[are\] the children of the desolate than the children of the married wife, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Enlarge the place of thy tent, and let them stretch forth the curtains of thine habitations: spare not, lengthen thy cords, and strengthen thy stakes;<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thou shalt<small><small>[^6]</small></small> break forth on the right hand and on the left; for thou shalt not be ashamed: neither be thou confounded; for thou shalt not be put to shame: for thou shalt forget<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the shame of thy youth, and shalt not remember the reproach of thy widowhood any more. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thy Maker<small><small>[^11]</small></small> \[is\] thine husband; the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^12]</small></small></span> of hosts \[is\] his name; and thy Redeemer the Holy One of Israel; The God<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of the whole earth shall he be called. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath called thee as a woman<small><small>[^14]</small></small> forsaken and grieved in spirit, and a wife<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of youth, when thou wast refused, saith thy God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For a small<small><small>[^16]</small></small> moment have I forsaken thee; but with<small><small>[^17]</small></small> great mercies will I gather thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In a little<small><small>[^18]</small></small> wrath I hid<small><small>[^19]</small></small> my face from thee for a moment; but<small><small>[^20]</small></small> with everlasting kindness will I have mercy on thee, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^21]</small></small></span> thy Redeemer. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For this \[is as\] the waters of Noah unto me: for \[as\] I have sworn that the waters of Noah should no more go over the earth; so have I sworn that I would not be wroth with thee, nor rebuke thee.<small><small>[^22]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the mountains<small><small>[^23]</small></small> shall depart, and the hills be removed; but my kindness shall not depart from thee, neither shall the covenant<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of my peace be removed, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> that hath<small><small>[^25]</small></small> mercy on thee. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O thou afflicted<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, tossed<small><small>[^27]</small></small> with tempest, \[and\] not comforted<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, behold, I will lay<small><small>[^29]</small></small> thy stones with fair colours, and lay thy foundations with sapphires<small><small>[^30]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will make thy windows of agates, and thy gates of carbuncles, and all thy borders of pleasant stones. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all<small><small>[^31]</small></small> thy children \[shall be\] taught of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and great<small><small>[^32]</small></small> \[shall be\] the peace of thy children. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In righteousness<small><small>[^33]</small></small> shalt thou be established: thou shalt be<small><small>[^34]</small></small> far from oppression; for thou<small><small>[^35]</small></small> shalt not fear: and from terror; for it shall not come near thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, they shall<small><small>[^36]</small></small> surely gather together, \[but\] not by me: whosoever shall gather together against thee shall fall<small><small>[^37]</small></small> for thy sake. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.54.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, I have<small><small>[^38]</small></small> created the smith that bloweth the coals in the fire, and that bringeth forth an instrument for his work; and I have created the waster to destroy.
