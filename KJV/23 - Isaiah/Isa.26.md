***A Song of Salvation*** ^bsb1621

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In that day<small><small>[^1]</small></small> shall this song<small><small>[^2]</small></small> be sung in the land<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of Judah; We have a strong city; salvation<small><small>[^4]</small></small> will \[God\] appoint \[for\] walls and bulwarks. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Open<small><small>[^5]</small></small> ye the gates, that the righteous<small><small>[^6]</small></small> nation which keepeth the truth<small><small>[^7]</small></small> may enter in. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou wilt<small><small>[^8]</small></small> keep \[him\] in perfect peace<small><small>[^9]</small></small><small><small>[^10]</small></small>, \[whose\] mind \[is\] stayed<small><small>[^11]</small></small> \[on thee\]: because<small><small>[^12]</small></small> he trusteth in thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Trust<small><small>[^13]</small></small> ye in the<small><small>[^14]</small></small> <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> for ever: for in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> <span class="smallcaps">Jehovah</span> \[is\] everlasting strength<small><small>[^15]</small></small>: 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he bringeth<small><small>[^16]</small></small> down them that dwell on high; the lofty<small><small>[^17]</small></small> city, he layeth it low; he layeth it low, \[even\] to the ground; he bringeth it \[even\] to the dust. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The foot shall tread it down, \[even\] the feet of the poor, \[and\] the steps of the needy.<small><small>[^18]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The way<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of the just \[is\] uprightness: thou, most<small><small>[^20]</small></small> upright, dost weigh the path of the just. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea, in<small><small>[^21]</small></small> the way of thy judgments, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, have we<small><small>[^22]</small></small> waited for thee; the desire<small><small>[^23]</small></small> of \[our\] soul \[is\] to thy name, and to the remembrance of thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> With my soul have I<small><small>[^24]</small></small> desired thee in the night; yea, with my spirit<small><small>[^25]</small></small> within me will I seek thee early: for<small><small>[^26]</small></small> when thy judgments \[are\] in the earth, the inhabitants of the world will learn righteousness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let favour<small><small>[^27]</small></small> be shewed to the wicked, \[yet\] will he not learn righteousness: in the<small><small>[^28]</small></small> land of uprightness will he deal unjustly, and will not<small><small>[^29]</small></small> behold the majesty of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[when<small><small>[^30]</small></small>\] thy hand is lifted up, they will<small><small>[^31]</small></small> not see: \[but\] they shall<small><small>[^32]</small></small> see, and be<small><small>[^33]</small></small> ashamed for \[their\] envy at the<small><small>[^34]</small></small> people; $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, thou wilt ordain<small><small>[^36]</small></small> peace for<small><small>[^37]</small></small> us: for thou also hast wrought all our works in us<small><small>[^38]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> our God, \[other<small><small>[^39]</small></small>\] lords beside thee have had dominion over us: \[but\] by thee<small><small>[^40]</small></small> only will we make mention of thy name. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[They are\] dead<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, they shall not live; \[they are\] deceased, they shall not rise: therefore hast thou visited and destroyed them, and made<small><small>[^42]</small></small> all their memory to perish. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou hast increased<small><small>[^43]</small></small> the nation, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, thou hast increased the nation: thou art<small><small>[^44]</small></small> glorified: thou hadst<small><small>[^45]</small></small> removed \[it\] far \[unto\] all the ends of the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, in trouble<small><small>[^46]</small></small> have they visited thee, they poured out a prayer<small><small>[^47]</small></small> \[when\] thy chastening \[was\] upon them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Like as a woman with child, \[that\] draweth near the time of her delivery, is in pain, \[and\] crieth out in her pangs; so have we been in thy sight, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^48]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> We have been with child, we have been in<small><small>[^49]</small></small> pain, we have as it were brought forth wind; we have not<small><small>[^50]</small></small> wrought any deliverance in the earth; neither have the inhabitants<small><small>[^51]</small></small> of the world fallen. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.26.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy dead \[men<small><small>[^52]</small></small>\] shall live, \[together with\] my dead<small><small>[^53]</small></small> body shall they arise. Awake<small><small>[^54]</small></small> and sing, ye that dwell in dust: for thy dew<small><small>[^55]</small></small> \[is as\] the dew of herbs, and the earth<small><small>[^56]</small></small> shall cast out the dead.
