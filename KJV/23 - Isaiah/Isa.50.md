<big><big>[[Isa.50.1 Israel’s Sin|⥃]]</big></big>***Israel’s Sin***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.50.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, Where \[is\] the bill<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of your mother’s divorcement, whom I have put away? or which<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of my creditors \[is it\] to whom I have sold you? Behold<small><small>[^4]</small></small>, for your iniquities<small><small>[^5]</small></small> have ye sold yourselves, and for your transgressions is your mother put away. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.50.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore, when I came<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, \[was there\] no man? when I called, \[was there\] none to answer? Is my<small><small>[^7]</small></small> hand shortened at all, that it cannot redeem? or have I<small><small>[^8]</small></small> no power to deliver? behold, at my<small><small>[^9]</small></small> rebuke I dry<small><small>[^10]</small></small> up the sea, I make the rivers a wilderness: their fish<small><small>[^11]</small></small> stinketh, because \[there is\] no water, and dieth for thirst. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.50.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I clothe the heavens with blackness, and I make sackcloth their covering.<small><small>[^12]</small></small>

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.50.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The Lord <span class="smallcaps">God<small><small>[^13]</small></small></span> hath given me the tongue of the learned, that I should know how to speak a word<small><small>[^14]</small></small> in season to \[him that is\] weary: he wakeneth morning by morning, he wakeneth mine ear to hear as the<small><small>[^15]</small></small> learned. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.50.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]The Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span> hath opened mine ear, and I was not rebellious, neither turned away back.<small><small>[^16]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.50.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I gave<small><small>[^17]</small></small> my back to the smiters, and my cheeks<small><small>[^18]</small></small> to them that plucked<small><small>[^19]</small></small> off the hair: I hid<small><small>[^20]</small></small> not my face from shame and spitting. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.50.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]For the Lord<small><small>[^21]</small></small> <span class="smallcaps">God</span> will help me; therefore shall I not be confounded: therefore have I set<small><small>[^22]</small></small> my face like a flint, and I know that I shall not be ashamed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.50.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[He is\] near that<small><small>[^23]</small></small> justifieth me; who will contend with me? let us<small><small>[^24]</small></small> stand together: who \[is\] mine adversary<small><small>[^25]</small></small>? let him come near to me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.50.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span> will help me; who \[is\] he \[that\] shall condemn me? lo, they all<small><small>[^26]</small></small> shall wax old as a garment; the moth shall eat them up. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.50.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who \[is\] among<small><small>[^27]</small></small> you that feareth the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that obeyeth<small><small>[^28]</small></small> the voice of his servant, that walketh<small><small>[^29]</small></small> \[in\] darkness, and hath no light? let<small><small>[^30]</small></small> him trust in the name of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and stay upon his God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.50.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, all ye<small><small>[^31]</small></small> that kindle a fire, that compass \[yourselves\] about with sparks: walk<small><small>[^32]</small></small> in the light of your fire, and in the sparks \[that\] ye have kindled.
