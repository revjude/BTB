***Sin Separates Us from God***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Ps.14|Psalms 14:1–7]] <big>†</big> [[Ps.53|Psalms 53:1–6]] <big>†</big> [[Rom.3|Romans 3:9–20]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1681

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s<small><small>[^2]</small></small></span> hand is not shortened, that it cannot save<small><small>[^3]</small></small>; neither his ear<small><small>[^4]</small></small> heavy, that it cannot hear: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But your iniquities<small><small>[^5]</small></small> have separated between you and your God, and your sins have hid<small><small>[^6]</small></small> \[his\] face from you, that he will not hear. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For your hands<small><small>[^7]</small></small> are defiled with blood, and your fingers with iniquity; your lips<small><small>[^8]</small></small> have spoken lies, your tongue hath muttered perverseness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> None calleth<small><small>[^9]</small></small> for justice, nor \[any\] pleadeth for truth: they trust<small><small>[^10]</small></small> in vanity, and speak<small><small>[^11]</small></small> lies; they conceive<small><small>[^12]</small></small> mischief, and bring forth iniquity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They hatch cockatrice’<small><small>[^13]</small></small> eggs, and weave the spider’s web: he that eateth of their eggs dieth, and that which is crushed breaketh out into a viper<small><small>[^14]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Their webs<small><small>[^15]</small></small> shall not become garments, neither<small><small>[^16]</small></small> shall they cover themselves with their works<small><small>[^17]</small></small>: their works \[are\] works of iniquity, and the act of violence \[is\] in their hands. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Their feet<small><small>[^18]</small></small> run to evil, and they<small><small>[^19]</small></small> make haste to shed innocent blood: their thoughts<small><small>[^20]</small></small> \[are\] thoughts of iniquity; wasting<small><small>[^21]</small></small> and destruction<small><small>[^22]</small></small> \[are\] in their paths. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The way<small><small>[^23]</small></small> of peace they kno<small><small>[^24]</small></small>w not; and \[there is\] no judgment<small><small>[^25]</small></small> in their goings: they have made them crooked<small><small>[^26]</small></small> paths: whosoever<small><small>[^27]</small></small> goeth therein shall not know peace. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore is judgment<small><small>[^28]</small></small> far from us, neither doth justice overtake us: we wait<small><small>[^29]</small></small> for light, but behold obscurity; for brightness, \[but\] we walk in darkness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> We grope<small><small>[^30]</small></small> for the wall like the blind, and we grope as if \[we had\] no eyes: we stumble at noonday as in the night; \[we are\] in desolate<small><small>[^31]</small></small> places as dead \[men\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> We roar<small><small>[^32]</small></small> all like bears, and mourn<small><small>[^33]</small></small> sore like doves: we look for judgment, but \[there is\] none; for salvation<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, \[but\] it is far off from us. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For our transgressions<small><small>[^35]</small></small> are multiplied before thee, and our sins<small><small>[^36]</small></small> testify against us: for our transgressions \[are\] with us; and \[as for\] our iniquities, we know<small><small>[^37]</small></small> them; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In transgressing and lying<small><small>[^38]</small></small> against the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and departing<small><small>[^39]</small></small> away from our God, speaking<small><small>[^40]</small></small> oppression and revolt, conceiving and uttering from the heart words of falsehood. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And judgment is turned away backward, and justice standeth afar off: for truth is fallen in the street, and equity cannot enter.<small><small>[^41]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea, truth<small><small>[^42]</small></small> faileth; and he \[that<small><small>[^43]</small></small>\] departeth from evil maketh himself a prey<small><small>[^44]</small></small>: and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> saw \[it\], and it displeased him<small><small>[^45]</small></small> that \[there was\] no judgment. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he saw<small><small>[^46]</small></small> that \[there was\] no man, and wondered that \[there was\] no intercessor: therefore<small><small>[^47]</small></small> his arm brought salvation unto him; and his righteousness, it sustained him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he put on righteousness<small><small>[^48]</small></small> as a breastplate, and an helmet of salvation upon his head; and he put on the garments<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of vengeance \[for\] clothing, and was clad with zeal<small><small>[^50]</small></small> as a cloke.

***The Covenant of the Redeemer*** ^bsb1682

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> According<small><small>[^51]</small></small> to \[their\] deeds<small><small>[^52]</small></small>, accordingly he will repay, fury<small><small>[^53]</small></small> to his adversaries, recompence to his enemies; to the islands he will repay recompence. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.59.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So shall they<small><small>[^54]</small></small> fear the name of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> from the west, and his glory from the rising of the sun. When<small><small>[^55]</small></small> the enemy<small><small>[^56]</small></small> shall come in like a flood, the Spirit<small><small>[^57]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall lift up a standard against him<small><small>[^58]</small></small>.
