<big><big>[[Isa.47.1 The Humiliation of Babylon|⥃]]</big></big>***The Humiliation of Babylon***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Come down<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, and sit in the dust, O virgin<small><small>[^3]</small></small> daughter<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of Babylon, sit on the ground: \[there is<small><small>[^5]</small></small>\] no throne, O daughter of the Chaldeans: for thou shalt<small><small>[^6]</small></small> no more be called tender and delicate. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Take the millstones<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, and grind meal: uncover thy locks, make bare<small><small>[^8]</small></small> the leg, uncover the thigh, pass over the rivers. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy nakedness shall be uncovered, yea, thy shame shall be seen: I will take<small><small>[^9]</small></small> vengeance, and I will not meet \[thee as\] a man. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[As for\] our redeemer<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts \[is\] his name, the Holy One of Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Sit thou silent<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, and get thee into darkness, O daughter of the Chaldeans: for<small><small>[^12]</small></small> thou shalt no more be called, The lady of kingdoms. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]I was wroth<small><small>[^13]</small></small> with my people, I have polluted<small><small>[^14]</small></small> mine inheritance, and given them into thine hand: thou didst<small><small>[^15]</small></small> shew them no mercy; upon<small><small>[^16]</small></small> the ancient hast thou very heavily laid thy yoke. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou saidst<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, I shall be a lady for ever: \[so\] that<small><small>[^18]</small></small> thou didst not lay these \[things\] to thy heart, neither didst remember the latter end of it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore hear now this, \[thou that art\] given<small><small>[^19]</small></small> to pleasures, that dwellest carelessly, that sayest in thine heart, I \[am<small><small>[^20]</small></small>\], and none else beside me; I shall not<small><small>[^21]</small></small> sit \[as\] a widow, neither shall I know the loss of children: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But these two<small><small>[^22]</small></small> \[things\] shall come to thee in a moment<small><small>[^23]</small></small> in one day, the loss of children, and widowhood: they shall come<small><small>[^24]</small></small> upon thee in their perfection for the multitude<small><small>[^25]</small></small> of thy sorceries, \[and\] for the great abundance of thine enchantments. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]For thou hast trusted<small><small>[^26]</small></small> in thy wickedness: thou hast said<small><small>[^27]</small></small>, None seeth me. Thy wisdom<small><small>[^28]</small></small> and thy knowledge, it hath perverted thee<small><small>[^29]</small></small>; and thou hast said in thine heart, I \[am<small><small>[^30]</small></small>\], and none else beside me. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Therefore shall evil come upon thee; thou shalt not know<small><small>[^31]</small></small> from whence it riseth<small><small>[^32]</small></small>: and mischief shall fall upon thee; thou shalt not be<small><small>[^33]</small></small> able to put it off<small><small>[^34]</small></small>: and desolation shall come upon thee suddenly, \[which\] thou shalt not know. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Stand now with thine enchantments, and with the multitude of thy sorceries, wherein thou hast laboured from thy youth; if so be thou shalt be able to profit, if so be thou mayest prevail.<small><small>[^35]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou art wearied<small><small>[^36]</small></small> in the multitude of thy counsels. Let now<small><small>[^37]</small></small> the astrologers, the stargazers<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, the monthly prognosticators, stand up, and save thee from \[these things\] that shall come upon thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, they shall<small><small>[^39]</small></small> be as stubble; the fire shall burn them; they shall not deliver themselves from the power of the flame: \[there shall<small><small>[^40]</small></small>\] not \[be\] a coal to warm at, \[nor\] fire to sit before it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.47.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus shall they be unto thee with whom thou hast laboured, \[even\] thy merchants<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, from thy youth: they shall<small><small>[^42]</small></small> wander every one to his quarter; none shall save thee. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Isa.46|<< Isaiah 46]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Isa.48|Isaiah 48 >>]]</td></tr></table>
