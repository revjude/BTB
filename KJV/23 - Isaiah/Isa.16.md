***Moab’s Destruction***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Zeph.2|Zephaniah 2:8–11]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1607

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Send ye the lamb<small><small>[^2]</small></small> to the ruler of the land from<small><small>[^3]</small></small> Sela<small><small>[^4]</small></small> to the wilderness, unto the mount<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of the daughter of Zion. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For it shall be, \[that\], as<small><small>[^6]</small></small> a wandering bird cast out of the nest<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, \[so\] the daughters of Moab shall be at the fords<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of Arnon. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Take<small><small>[^9]</small></small> counsel, execute<small><small>[^10]</small></small> judgment; make<small><small>[^11]</small></small> thy shadow as the night in the midst of the noonday; hide<small><small>[^12]</small></small> the outcasts; bewray not him that wandereth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let mine<small><small>[^13]</small></small> outcasts dwell with thee, Moab; be thou a covert to them from the face of the spoiler: for<small><small>[^14]</small></small> the extortioner<small><small>[^15]</small></small> is at an end, the spoiler ceaseth, the oppressors<small><small>[^16]</small></small> are consumed out of the land. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And in mercy<small><small>[^17]</small></small> shall the throne be established<small><small>[^18]</small></small>: and he shall sit upon it in truth in the<small><small>[^19]</small></small> tabernacle of David, judging<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, and seeking judgment, and hasting<small><small>[^21]</small></small> righteousness. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> We have<small><small>[^22]</small></small> heard of the pride of Moab; \[he is\] very proud: \[even\] of his haughtiness, and his pride, and his wrath: \[but<small><small>[^23]</small></small>\] his lies \[shall\] not \[be\] so. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore shall Moab<small><small>[^24]</small></small> howl for Moab, every one shall howl: for the foundations of Kir–hareseth shall ye mourn<small><small>[^25]</small></small>; surely \[they are\] stricken.<small><small>[^26]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the fields<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of Heshbon languish, \[and\] the vine<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of Sibmah<small><small>[^29]</small></small>: the lords<small><small>[^30]</small></small> of the heathen have broken down the principal plants thereof, they are come \[even\] unto Jazer<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, they wandered \[through\] the wilderness: her branches are stretched out<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, they are gone over the sea. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Therefore I will bewail<small><small>[^33]</small></small> with the weeping of Jazer the vine of Sibmah: I will water thee with my tears, O Heshbon<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, and Elealeh: for<small><small>[^35]</small></small> the shouting for<small><small>[^36]</small></small> thy summer fruits and for thy harvest is fallen. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And gladness is taken away, and joy out of the plentiful field; and in the vineyards there shall be no singing, neither shall there be shouting: the treaders shall tread out no wine in \[their\] presses; I have made \[their vintage\] shouting to cease.<small><small>[^37]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore my<small><small>[^38]</small></small> bowels shall sound like an harp for Moab, and mine inward parts for Kir–haresh.<small><small>[^39]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And it shall come to pass, when<small><small>[^40]</small></small> it is seen that Moab is weary on the high place, that he shall<small><small>[^41]</small></small> come to his sanctuary to pray; but<small><small>[^42]</small></small> he shall not prevail. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> This \[is\] the word that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath spoken concerning Moab since<small><small>[^43]</small></small> that time. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.16.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But now the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath spoken, saying, Within three<small><small>[^44]</small></small> years, as the years of an hireling, and the glory<small><small>[^45]</small></small> of Moab shall be contemned, with all that great multitude; and the remnant<small><small>[^46]</small></small> \[shall be\] very small \[and\] feeble<small><small>[^47]</small></small>. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Isa.15|<< Isaiah 15]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Isa.17|Isaiah 17 >>]]</td></tr></table>
