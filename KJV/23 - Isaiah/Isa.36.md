<big><big>[[Isa.36.1 Sennacherib Threatens Jerusalem|⥃]]</big></big>***Sennacherib Threatens Jerusalem***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[2Kgs.18|2 Kings 18:13–37]] <big>†</big> [[2Chr.32|2 Chronicles 32:1–8]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now it came<small><small>[^2]</small></small> to pass in the fourteenth year of king Hezekiah, \[that\] Sennacherib<small><small>[^3]</small></small> king of Assyria came up against all the defenced cities of Judah, and took them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king of Assyria sent<small><small>[^4]</small></small> Rab–shakeh from Lachish to Jerusalem unto king Hezekiah with a great army. And he stood by the conduit<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of the upper pool in the highway of the fuller’s field.<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then came forth unto him Eliakim<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, Hilkiah’s son, which was over the house, and Shebna<small><small>[^8]</small></small> the scribe<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, and Joah, Asaph’s son, the recorder. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And Rab–shakeh said unto them, Say ye now to Hezekiah, Thus saith<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the great king, the king of Assyria<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, What<small><small>[^12]</small></small> confidence \[is\] this wherein thou trustest? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I say, \[sayest thou\], (but \[they are but\] vain words<small><small>[^13]</small></small>) \[I have\] counsel and strength for war: now on whom dost thou trust, that<small><small>[^14]</small></small> thou rebellest against me? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lo, thou trustest in the staff of this broken reed, on Egypt; whereon if a man lean, it will go into his hand, and pierce it: so \[is\] Pharaoh king of Egypt to all that trust in him.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if thou say to me, We trust<small><small>[^16]</small></small> in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> our God: \[is it\] not<small><small>[^17]</small></small> he, whose high places and whose altars Hezekiah hath taken away, and said to Judah and to Jerusalem, Ye shall worship before this altar? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now therefore give pledges<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, I pray thee, to my master the king of Assyria, and I<small><small>[^19]</small></small> will give thee two thousand horses, if thou be able on thy part to set riders upon them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> How then wilt thou turn away the face of one captain of the least<small><small>[^20]</small></small> of my master’s servants, and put<small><small>[^21]</small></small> thy trust on Egypt for chariots and for horsemen? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And am I now come up without the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> against this land to destroy it? the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto me, Go up against this land, and destroy it.<small><small>[^22]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said Eliakim and Shebna and Joah unto Rab–shakeh, Speak, I pray thee, unto thy servants in the Syrian<small><small>[^23]</small></small> language; for we understand \[it\]: and speak not to us in the Jews’ language, in the ears of the people that \[are\] on the wall. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]But Rab–shakeh said, Hath my master sent me to thy master and to thee to speak these words? \[hath he\] not \[sent me\] to the men that sit upon the wall, that they may<small><small>[^24]</small></small> eat their own dung, and drink their own piss with you? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Rab–shakeh stood, and cried<small><small>[^25]</small></small> with a loud voice in the Jews’ language, and said, Hear<small><small>[^26]</small></small> ye the words of the great king, the king of Assyria. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the king, Let not Hezekiah deceive you: for he shall not be able to deliver you.<small><small>[^27]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Neither let Hezekiah make you trust in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, saying, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> will surely deliver us: this city shall not be delivered into the hand of the king of Assyria.<small><small>[^28]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hearken not to Hezekiah: for thus saith the king of Assyria, Make \[an agreement\] with me \[by\] a present<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, and come out<small><small>[^30]</small></small> to me: and eat ye<small><small>[^31]</small></small> every one of his vine, and every one of his fig tree, and drink ye every one the waters of his own cistern; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Until I come<small><small>[^32]</small></small> and take you away to a land like your own land, a land of corn<small><small>[^33]</small></small> and wine, a land of bread and vineyards. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Beware\] lest<small><small>[^34]</small></small> Hezekiah persuade you, saying, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> will deliver us. Hath<small><small>[^35]</small></small> any of the gods of the nations delivered his land out of the hand of the king of Assyria? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Where \[are\] the gods of Hamath<small><small>[^36]</small></small> and Arphad<small><small>[^37]</small></small>? where \[are\] the gods of Sepharvaim<small><small>[^38]</small></small>? and have<small><small>[^39]</small></small> they delivered Samaria out of my hand? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who \[are they\] among all the gods of these lands, that have delivered their land out of my hand, that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^40]</small></small></span> should deliver Jerusalem out of my hand? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But they held their peace, and answered him not a word: for the king’s commandment was, saying, Answer him not.<small><small>[^41]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Isa.36.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then came Eliakim<small><small>[^42]</small></small>, the son of Hilkiah, that \[was\] over the household, and Shebna the scribe, and Joah, the son of Asaph, the recorder, to Hezekiah with \[their<small><small>[^43]</small></small>\] clothes rent, and told him the words of Rab–shakeh. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Isa.35|<< Isaiah 35]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Isa.37|Isaiah 37 >>]]</td></tr></table>
