***Sanballat’s Conspiracy*** ^bsb1204

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.6.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now it came to pass, when Sanballat<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, and Tobiah, and Geshem<small><small>[^3]</small></small> the Arabian, and the rest of our enemies, heard that I had builded the wall, and \[that\] there was no breach<small><small>[^4]</small></small> left therein; (though at that time<small><small>[^5]</small></small> I had not set up the doors upon the gates;) <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.6.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That Sanballat and Geshem sent unto me, saying, Come<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, let us meet together in \[some one of\] the villages in the plain of Ono<small><small>[^7]</small></small>. Come now<small><small>[^20]</small></small> therefore, and let us take counsel together. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.6.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then I sent unto him, saying, There are<small><small>[^21]</small></small> no such things done as thou sayest, but thou feignest<small><small>[^22]</small></small> them out of thine own heart. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.6.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For they<small><small>[^23]</small></small> all made us afraid, saying, Their hands<small><small>[^24]</small></small> shall be weakened from the work, that it be not done. I will not go in. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.6.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And, lo, I perceived<small><small>[^34]</small></small> that God had<small><small>[^35]</small></small> not sent him; but that he pronounced this prophecy against me: for Tobiah and Sanballat had hired him<small><small>[^36]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.6.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore \[was\] he hired, that I should<small><small>[^37]</small></small> be afraid, and do so, and sin<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, and \[that\] they<small><small>[^39]</small></small> might have \[matter\] for an evil report, that<small><small>[^40]</small></small> they might reproach me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.6.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My God<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, think thou<small><small>[^42]</small></small> upon Tobiah and Sanballat according to these their works, and on the prophetess<small><small>[^43]</small></small> Noadiah, and the rest of the prophets, that would have put me in fear.

***Completion of the Wall*** ^bsb1205

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.6.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So the wall<small><small>[^44]</small></small> was finished in the twenty and fifth \[day\] of \[the month\] Elul, in fifty<small><small>[^45]</small></small> and two days. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.6.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, that when all our enemies<small><small>[^46]</small></small> heard \[thereof\], and all the heathen that \[were\] about us saw \[these things\], they were much cast down in their own eyes: for they perceived<small><small>[^47]</small></small> that this work was wrought of our God.
