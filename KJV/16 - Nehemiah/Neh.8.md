<big><big>[[Neh.8.1 Ezra Reads the Law|⥃]]</big></big>***Ezra Reads the Law***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Deut.31|Deuteronomy 31:9–13]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all the people<small><small>[^2]</small></small> gathered themselves together as one man<small><small>[^3]</small></small> into the street that \[was\] before<small><small>[^4]</small></small> the water gate; and they spake unto Ezra<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the scribe to bring<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the book of the law of Moses, which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had commanded to Israel.<small><small>[^7]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Ezra the priest<small><small>[^8]</small></small> brought the law before the congregation<small><small>[^9]</small></small> both of men and women, and all that could hear with understanding<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, upon the first<small><small>[^11]</small></small> day of the seventh month. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he read<small><small>[^12]</small></small> therein before the street that \[was\] before the water gate from the morning<small><small>[^13]</small></small> until midday, before the men and the women, and those that could understand; and the ears<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of all the people \[were attentive\] unto the book of the law. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Ezra the scribe stood upon a pulpit<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of wood, which they had made for the purpose; and beside him stood Mattithiah, and Shema, and Anaiah, and Urijah, and Hilkiah, and Maaseiah<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, on his right hand; and on his left hand, Pedaiah, and Mishael, and Malchiah<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, and Hashum<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, and Hashbadana, Zechariah, \[and\] Meshullam<small><small>[^19]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Ezra opened<small><small>[^20]</small></small> the book in the sight<small><small>[^21]</small></small> of all the people; And all the people answered, Amen<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, Amen, with lifting<small><small>[^25]</small></small> up their hands: and they bowed<small><small>[^26]</small></small> their heads, and worshipped the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> with \[their\] faces<small><small>[^27]</small></small> to the ground. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also Jeshua<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, and Bani<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, and Sherebiah<small><small>[^30]</small></small>, Jamin, Akkub<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, Shabbethai, Hodijah<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, Maaseiah<small><small>[^33]</small></small>, Kelita<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, Azariah<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, Jozabad<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, Hanan<small><small>[^37]</small></small>, Pelaiah<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, and the Levites, caused<small><small>[^39]</small></small> the people to understand the law: and the people \[stood\] in their place. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So they read in the book in the law of God distinctly, and gave the sense<small><small>[^40]</small></small>, and caused \[them\] to understand the reading. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Nehemiah<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, which \[is\] the Tirshatha<small><small>[^42]</small></small>, and Ezra<small><small>[^43]</small></small> the priest the scribe, and the Levites<small><small>[^44]</small></small> that taught the people, said unto all the people, This day<small><small>[^45]</small></small> \[is\] holy unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> your God; mourn not<small><small>[^46]</small></small>, nor weep. For all the people<small><small>[^47]</small></small> wept, when they heard the words of the law. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then he said unto them, Go your way<small><small>[^48]</small></small>, eat<small><small>[^49]</small></small> the fat, and drink the sweet, and send<small><small>[^50]</small></small> portions unto them for whom nothing is prepared: for \[this\] day \[is\] holy unto our Lord: neither be ye sorry; for the joy<small><small>[^51]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> is your strength. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So the Levites stilled<small><small>[^52]</small></small> all the people, saying, Hold your peace, for the day \[is\] holy; neither be ye grieved. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all the people went their way to eat, and to drink, and to send<small><small>[^53]</small></small> portions, and to make<small><small>[^54]</small></small> great mirth, because<small><small>[^55]</small></small> they had understood the words that were declared unto them.

<big><big>[[Neh.8.13 The Feast of Tabernacles|⥃]]</big></big>***The Feast of Tabernacles***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Lev.23|Leviticus 23:33–44]] <big>†</big> [[Zech.14|Zechariah 14:16–21]] ]</sup></small>

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And on the second<small><small>[^56]</small></small> day were gathered together the chief of the fathers of all the people, the priests, and the Levites, unto Ezra the scribe, even to understand the words of the law<small><small>[^57]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they found written in the law which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had commanded by<small><small>[^58]</small></small> Moses, that the children of Israel should dwell<small><small>[^59]</small></small> in booths<small><small>[^60]</small></small> in the feast<small><small>[^61]</small></small> of the seventh month: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And that<small><small>[^62]</small></small> they should publish and proclaim in all their cities, and in Jerusalem<small><small>[^63]</small></small>, saying, Go forth unto the mount<small><small>[^64]</small></small>, and fetch<small><small>[^65]</small></small> olive<small><small>[^66]</small></small> branches, and pine branches, and myrtle branches, and palm<small><small>[^67]</small></small> branches, and branches of thick trees, to make booths, as \[it is\] written. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]So the people went forth, and brought \[them\], and made themselves booths, every one upon the roof<small><small>[^68]</small></small> of his house, and in their courts, and in the courts<small><small>[^69]</small></small> of the house of God, and in the street of the water gate<small><small>[^70]</small></small>, and in the street of the gate of Ephraim<small><small>[^71]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all the congregation of them that were come again out of the captivity made booths, and sat under<small><small>[^72]</small></small> the booths: for since the days of Jeshua<small><small>[^73]</small></small> the son of Nun unto that day had not<small><small>[^74]</small></small> the children of Israel done so<small><small>[^75]</small></small>. And there was<small><small>[^76]</small></small> very great gladness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Neh.8.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also day by day<small><small>[^77]</small></small>, from the first day unto the last day, he read in the book of the law of God.
