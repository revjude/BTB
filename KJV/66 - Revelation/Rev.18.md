<big><big>[[Rev.18.1 Babylon Is Fallen|⥃]]</big></big>***Babylon Is Fallen***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Isa.21|Isaiah 21:1–10]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And after these things I saw<small><small>[^2]</small></small> another angel come down from heaven, having great power; and the<small><small>[^3]</small></small> earth was lightened with his glory. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he cried<small><small>[^4]</small></small> mightily with a strong voice, saying, Babylon<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the great is fallen, is fallen, and is become<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the habitation of devils, and the hold of every foul spirit, and a cage of every unclean and hateful bird. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For all<small><small>[^7]</small></small> nations have drunk of the wine of the wrath of her fornication, and the kings of the earth have committed fornication with her, and the merchants<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of the earth are waxed rich through the abundance<small><small>[^9]</small></small> of her delicacies<small><small>[^10]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I heard another voice from heaven, saying, Come<small><small>[^11]</small></small> out of her, my people, that ye be not partakers<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of her sins, and that ye receive not of her plagues. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For her sins have reached<small><small>[^13]</small></small> unto heaven, and<small><small>[^14]</small></small> God hath remembered her iniquities. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Reward<small><small>[^15]</small></small> her even as she rewarded you, and double unto<small><small>[^16]</small></small> her double according to her works: in the cup<small><small>[^17]</small></small> which she hath filled fill to her double. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> How much she<small><small>[^18]</small></small> hath glorified herself, and lived deliciously, so much torment and sorrow give her: for she saith in her heart, I sit<small><small>[^19]</small></small> a queen, and am no widow<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, and shall see no sorrow. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore shall her<small><small>[^21]</small></small> plagues come in one day, death, and mourning, and famine; and she<small><small>[^22]</small></small> shall be utterly burned with fire: for<small><small>[^23]</small></small> strong \[is\] the Lord God who judgeth her.

<big><big>[[Rev.18.9 Lament over Babylon|⥃]]</big></big>***Lament over Babylon***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the kings<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of the earth, who have committed fornication and lived deliciously with her, shall bewail<small><small>[^25]</small></small> her, and lament for her, when they shall see the smoke<small><small>[^26]</small></small> of her burning, <span Alas<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, alas, that great<small><small>[^29]</small></small> city Babylon, that mighty city! for<small><small>[^30]</small></small> in one hour is thy judgment come. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the merchants<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of the earth shall weep and mourn over her; for no man buyeth<small><small>[^32]</small></small> their merchandise any more: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The merchandise<small><small>[^33]</small></small> of gold, and silver, and precious stones, and of pearls, and fine linen, and purple, and silk, and scarlet, and all thyine<small><small>[^34]</small></small> wood, and all manner vessels of ivory, and all manner vessels of most precious wood, and of brass, and iron, and marble, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And cinnamon<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, and odours, and ointments, and frankincense, and wine, and oil, and fine flour, and wheat, and beasts, and sheep, and horses, and chariots, and slaves<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, and souls<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of men. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the fruits that thy soul<small><small>[^38]</small></small> lusted after are departed<small><small>[^39]</small></small> from thee, and all things which were dainty and goodly are departed from thee, and thou shalt find them no more at all. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The merchants of these things, which<small><small>[^40]</small></small> were made rich by her, shall<small><small>[^41]</small></small> stand afar off for the fear of her torment, weeping and wailing, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And saying, Alas<small><small>[^42]</small></small>, alas, that great city, that was clothed in fine linen, and purple, and scarlet, and decked with gold, and precious stones, and pearls! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For in one<small><small>[^43]</small></small> hour so great riches is come to nought. And every<small><small>[^44]</small></small> shipmaster, and all the company in ships, and sailors, and as many as trade by sea, stood afar off, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And cried when<small><small>[^45]</small></small> they saw the smoke of her burning, saying, What<small><small>[^46]</small></small> \[city is\] like unto this great city! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they cast<small><small>[^47]</small></small> dust on their heads, and cried, weeping<small><small>[^48]</small></small> and wailing, saying, Alas, alas, that great city, wherein were made rich all that had ships in the sea by reason of her costliness! for<small><small>[^49]</small></small> in one hour is she made desolate. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rev.18.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Rejoice<small><small>[^50]</small></small> over her, \[thou\] heaven, and \[ye<small><small>[^51]</small></small>\] holy apostles and prophets; for God<small><small>[^52]</small></small> hath avenged you on her.
