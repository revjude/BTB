<big><big>[[Jas.3.1 Taming the Tongue|⥃]]</big></big>***Taming the Tongue***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Ps.64|Psalms 64:1–10]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My brethren, be<small><small>[^2]</small></small> not many masters, knowing<small><small>[^3]</small></small> that we shall receive the greater condemnation<small><small>[^4]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For in<small><small>[^5]</small></small> many things we offend all. If<small><small>[^6]</small></small> any man offend not in word, the same \[is\] a perfect<small><small>[^7]</small></small> man, \[and\] able also to bridle<small><small>[^8]</small></small> the whole body. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, we put bits in the horses’ mouths, that they may obey us; and we turn about their whole body.<small><small>[^9]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold also the ships, which though \[they be\] so great, and \[are\] driven<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of fierce winds, yet are they turned about with a very small helm, whithersoever the governor listeth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Even so<small><small>[^11]</small></small> the tongue is a little member, and boasteth great things. Behold, how great a matter<small><small>[^12]</small></small> a little fire kindleth! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the tongue<small><small>[^13]</small></small> \[is\] a fire, a world<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of iniquity: so is the tongue among our members, that it defileth the whole body, and setteth on fire the course of nature; and it is<small><small>[^15]</small></small> set on fire of hell. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For every kind<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of beasts, and of birds, and of serpents, and of things in the sea, is tamed<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, and hath been tamed of mankind<small><small>[^18]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the tongue can no man tame; \[it is\] an unruly<small><small>[^19]</small></small> evil, full<small><small>[^20]</small></small> of deadly poison. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therewith<small><small>[^21]</small></small> bless<small><small>[^22]</small></small> we God, even the Father; and therewith curse<small><small>[^23]</small></small> we men, which are made<small><small>[^24]</small></small> after the similitude of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Out of<small><small>[^25]</small></small> the same mouth proceedeth blessing and cursing. My brethren, these<small><small>[^26]</small></small> things ought not so to be. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Doth a fountain send forth at the same place<small><small>[^27]</small></small> sweet \[water\] and bitter? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Can the fig tree<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, my brethren, bear olive berries? either a vine, figs? so<small><small>[^29]</small></small> \[can\] no fountain both yield salt water and fresh.

<big><big>[[Jas.3.13 The Wisdom from Above|⥃]]</big></big>***The Wisdom from Above***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who \[is\] a<small><small>[^30]</small></small> wise man and endued<small><small>[^31]</small></small> where<small><small>[^42]</small></small> envying and strife \[is\], there<small><small>[^43]</small></small> \[is\] confusion<small><small>[^44]</small></small> and every<small><small>[^45]</small></small> evil work. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the wisdom<small><small>[^46]</small></small> that is from above is first<small><small>[^47]</small></small> pure, then peaceable<small><small>[^48]</small></small>, gentle<small><small>[^49]</small></small>, \[and\] easy to be intreated, hypocrisy<small><small>[^53]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.3.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the fruit<small><small>[^54]</small></small> of
