<big><big>[[Jas.2.1 A Warning against Favoritism|⥃]]</big></big>***A Warning against Favoritism***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My brethren, have not the faith<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of our Lord Jesus Christ, \[the Lord<small><small>[^3]</small></small>\] of glory, with<small><small>[^4]</small></small> respect of persons. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For if there come unto your assembly<small><small>[^5]</small></small> a man with a gold<small><small>[^6]</small></small> ring, in goodly<small><small>[^7]</small></small> apparel, and there come in also a poor man in vile<small><small>[^8]</small></small> raiment; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And ye<small><small>[^9]</small></small> have respect to him that weareth the gay clothing, and say unto him, Sit thou here in a good place<small><small>[^10]</small></small>; and say to the<small><small>[^11]</small></small> poor, Stand thou there, or sit here under my footstool: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Are ye not then partial<small><small>[^12]</small></small> in yourselves, and are become judges<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of evil thoughts? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hearken<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, my beloved brethren, Hath not<small><small>[^15]</small></small> God chosen the poor of this world rich<small><small>[^16]</small></small> in faith, and heirs<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of the<small><small>[^18]</small></small> kingdom which he hath promised to them that love him? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But ye<small><small>[^19]</small></small> have despised the poor. Do<small><small>[^20]</small></small> not rich men oppress you, and<small><small>[^21]</small></small> draw you before the judgment seats? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Do not they blaspheme<small><small>[^22]</small></small> that worthy<small><small>[^23]</small></small> name by<small><small>[^24]</small></small> the which ye are called? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If ye fulfil the royal<small><small>[^25]</small></small> law according to the scripture, Thou<small><small>[^26]</small></small> shalt love thy neighbour as thyself, ye do<small><small>[^27]</small></small> well: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if<small><small>[^28]</small></small> ye have respect to persons, ye commit sin, and are<small><small>[^29]</small></small> convinced of the law as transgressors<small><small>[^30]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^31]</small></small> whosoever<small><small>[^32]</small></small> shall keep the whole law, and yet offend in one \[point\], he is guilty of all. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he that said, Do not commit<small><small>[^33]</small></small> adultery, said also, Do not kill. Now<small><small>[^34]</small></small> if thou commit no adultery, yet if thou kill, thou art become a transgressor of the law.<small><small>[^35]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So speak<small><small>[^36]</small></small> ye, and so do, as the<small><small>[^37]</small></small>y that shall be judged by the law of liberty. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he<small><small>[^38]</small></small> shall have judgment without mercy, that hath shewed no mercy; and<small><small>[^39]</small></small> mercy rejoiceth<small><small>[^40]</small></small> against judgment.

<big><big>[[Jas.2.14 Faith and Works|⥃]]</big></big>***Faith and Works***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Gal.3|Galatians 3:1–9]] ]</sup></small>

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What<small><small>[^41]</small></small> \[doth it\] profit, my brethren, though<small><small>[^42]</small></small> a man say he hath faith, and have not works? can<small><small>[^43]</small></small> faith save him? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If a brother or sister be naked, and destitute of daily food,<small><small>[^44]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And one<small><small>[^45]</small></small> of you say unto them, Depart in peace, be \[ye\] warmed and filled; notwithstanding ye give them not those things which are needful to the body; what<small><small>[^46]</small></small> \[doth it\] profit? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Even so<small><small>[^47]</small></small> faith, if it hath not works, is dead, being alone<small><small>[^48]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea, a man may say, Thou<small><small>[^49]</small></small> hast faith, and I have works: shew me thy faith without thy works<small><small>[^50]</small></small>, and I will<small><small>[^51]</small></small> shew thee my faith by my works. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou believest that there is one God; thou doest<small><small>[^52]</small></small> well: the<small><small>[^53]</small></small> devils also believe, and tremble.<small><small>[^54]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But wilt thou know, O vain<small><small>[^55]</small></small> man, that<small><small>[^56]</small></small> faith without works is dead? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Was not Abraham<small><small>[^57]</small></small> our father justified<small><small>[^58]</small></small> by works, when<small><small>[^59]</small></small> he had offered Isaac his son upon the altar? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Seest thou<small><small>[^60]</small></small> how faith<small><small>[^61]</small></small> wrought with his works, and by works was faith made<small><small>[^62]</small></small> perfect? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the scripture<small><small>[^63]</small></small> was fulfilled which saith, Abraham<small><small>[^64]</small></small> believed God, and it was imputed unto him for righteousness: and he was called the Friend<small><small>[^65]</small></small> of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ye see then how that by works a man is justified, and not by faith only.<small><small>[^66]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Likewise also was<small><small>[^67]</small></small> not Rahab the harlot<small><small>[^68]</small></small> justified<small><small>[^69]</small></small> by works, when<small><small>[^70]</small></small> she had received the messengers, and had sent \[them\] out another way? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.2.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For as<small><small>[^71]</small></small> the body without the spirit<small><small>[^72]</small></small> is dead, so<small><small>[^73]</small></small> faith without works is dead also. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Jas.1|<< James 1]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Jas.3|James 3 >>]]</td></tr></table>
