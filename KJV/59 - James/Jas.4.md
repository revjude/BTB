<big><big>[[Jas.4.1 A Warning against Pride|⥃]]</big></big>***A Warning against Pride***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> From whence<small><small>[^2]</small></small> \[come\] wars and fightings<small><small>[^3]</small></small> among you? \[come they<small><small>[^4]</small></small>\] not hence, \[even\] of your lusts<small><small>[^5]</small></small> that war in<small><small>[^6]</small></small> your members? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ye lust<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, and have not: ye kill<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, and desire to have, and cannot obtain: ye fight and war, yet ye have not, because<small><small>[^9]</small></small> ye ask not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ye ask, and<small><small>[^10]</small></small> receive not, because ye ask amiss, that ye may<small><small>[^11]</small></small> consume \[it\] upon your lusts<small><small>[^12]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ye adulterers<small><small>[^13]</small></small> and adulteresses, know ye not that the friendship<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of the world is enmity<small><small>[^15]</small></small> with God? whosoever<small><small>[^16]</small></small> therefore will be a friend of the world is the<small><small>[^17]</small></small> enemy of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Do ye think that the scripture<small><small>[^18]</small></small> saith in vain, The spirit<small><small>[^19]</small></small> that dwelleth in us lusteth to envy<small><small>[^20]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But he giveth more grace. Wherefore he saith, God<small><small>[^21]</small></small> resisteth the proud, but giveth grace<small><small>[^22]</small></small> unto the humble.

<big><big>[[Jas.4.7 Drawing Near to God|⥃]]</big></big>***Drawing Near to God***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Submit<small><small>[^23]</small></small> yourselves therefore to God. Resist<small><small>[^24]</small></small> the devil, and he will flee from you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Draw nigh to God<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, and he will draw nigh to you. Cleanse<small><small>[^26]</small></small> \[your\] hands, \[ye\] sinners; and purify<small><small>[^27]</small></small> \[your\] hearts, \[ye\] double<small><small>[^28]</small></small> minded. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be afflicted<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, and mourn, and weep: let<small><small>[^30]</small></small> your laughter be turned to mourning, and \[your\] joy to heaviness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Humble<small><small>[^31]</small></small> yourselves in the<small><small>[^32]</small></small> sight of the Lord, and he shall lift you up. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Speak<small><small>[^33]</small></small> not evil one of another, brethren. He that speaketh evil of \[his\] brother, and judgeth<small><small>[^34]</small></small> his brother, speaketh evil of the law<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, and judgeth the law: but if thou judge the law, thou art not a doer<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of the law, but a judge. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There is one lawgiver<small><small>[^37]</small></small>, who is able<small><small>[^38]</small></small> to save and to destroy: who<small><small>[^39]</small></small> art thou that judgest another?

<big><big>[[Jas.4.13 Do Not Boast about Tomorrow|⥃]]</big></big>***Do Not Boast about Tomorrow***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Prov.27|Proverbs 27:1]] ]</sup></small>

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Go to<small><small>[^40]</small></small> now, ye that say, To day<small><small>[^41]</small></small> or to morrow we will go into such a city, and continue there a year, and buy<small><small>[^42]</small></small> and sell, and get gain: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whereas ye know not what \[shall be\] on the morrow. For what \[is\] your life? It is<small><small>[^43]</small></small> even a vapour<small><small>[^44]</small></small>, that appeareth for a little time, and then vanisheth away. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For that ye \[ought\] to say, If<small><small>[^45]</small></small> the Lord will, we shall live, and do this, or that. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But now ye rejoice in your boastings: all such rejoicing is evil.<small><small>[^46]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jas.4.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore to him that knoweth to do good, and doeth \[it\] not, to him it is sin. <small><small>[^47]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Jas.3|<< James 3]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Jas.5|James 5 >>]]</td></tr></table>
