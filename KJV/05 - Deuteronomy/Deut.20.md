***Laws of Warfare*** ^bsb511

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When thou goest out<small><small>[^2]</small></small> to battle against thine enemies, and seest horses<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, and chariots, \[and\] a people more than thou, be not afraid of them: for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God \[is\] with thee, which brought thee up out of the land of Egypt.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it shall be, when ye are come nigh unto the battle, that the priest shall approach and speak unto the people,<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And shall say unto them, Hear, O Israel, ye approach this day unto battle against your enemies: let not<small><small>[^6]</small></small> your hearts faint<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, fear not, and do not tremble<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, neither be ye terrified<small><small>[^9]</small></small> because of them; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> your God \[is\] he that goeth with you, to fight<small><small>[^10]</small></small> for you against your enemies, to save you. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And the officers<small><small>[^11]</small></small> shall speak unto the people, saying, What man \[is there\] that hath built a new house, and hath not dedicated<small><small>[^12]</small></small> it? let him go and return to his house, lest he die in the battle, and another man dedicate it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And what man \[is he\] that hath planted a vineyard, and hath not \[yet\] eaten of it<small><small>[^13]</small></small>? let him \[also\] go and return unto his house, lest he die<small><small>[^14]</small></small> in the battle, and another man eat of it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And what man \[is there\] that hath betrothed a wife<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, and hath not taken her? let him go and return unto his house, lest he die<small><small>[^16]</small></small> in the battle, and another man take her. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the officers shall speak further unto the people, and they shall say, What man \[is there that is\] fearful<small><small>[^17]</small></small> and faint<small><small>[^18]</small></small>hearted? let him go and return unto his house, lest his brethren’s heart faint as well as his heart.<small><small>[^19]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it shall be, when the officers have made an end of speaking unto the people, that they shall make captains of the armies to lead the people<small><small>[^20]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When thou comest nigh unto a city to fight against it, then proclaim<small><small>[^21]</small></small> peace unto it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it shall be, if it make thee answer of peace, and open unto thee, then it shall be, \[that\] all the people \[that is\] found therein shall be tributaries<small><small>[^22]</small></small> unto thee, and they shall serve thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if it will make no peace with thee, but will make war against thee, then thou shalt besiege it: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God hath delivered it into thine hands, thou shalt smite<small><small>[^23]</small></small> every male thereof with the edge of the sword: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the women<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, and the little ones, and the cattle, and all that is in the city, \[even\] all the spoil thereof, shalt thou take unto thyself<small><small>[^25]</small></small>; and thou shalt eat<small><small>[^26]</small></small> the spoil of thine enemies, which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God hath given thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus shalt thou do unto all the cities \[which are\] very far off from thee, which \[are\] not of the cities of these nations. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But of the cities of these people, which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God doth give thee \[for\] an inheritance, thou shalt save alive nothing that breatheth:<small><small>[^27]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But thou shalt<small><small>[^28]</small></small> utterly destroy them; \[namely\], the Hittites<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, and the Amorites, the Canaanites, and the Perizzites, the Hivites, and the Jebusites; as the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God hath commanded thee: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That they teach you not to do after all their abominations, which they have done unto their gods; so should ye sin against the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> your God.<small><small>[^30]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]When thou shalt besiege a city a long time, in making war against it to take it, thou shalt not<small><small>[^31]</small></small> destroy the trees thereof by forcing an axe against them: for thou mayest eat of them, and thou shalt not cut them down (for the tree<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of the field \[is\] man’s \[life\]) to employ<small><small>[^33]</small></small> \[them\] in the siege: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.20.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Only the trees which thou knowest that they \[be\] not trees for meat, thou shalt destroy and cut them down; and thou shalt build<small><small>[^34]</small></small> bulwarks against the city that maketh war with thee, until it be subdued<small><small>[^35]</small></small>. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Deut.19|<< Deuteronomy 19]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Deut.21|Deuteronomy 21 >>]]</td></tr></table>

[^32]: etc. or, for, O man, the tree of the field is to be employed in the siege, The original is exceedingly difficult. The LXX has it, "Is the tree in the field a man, to enter the trench before thee?" The Latin Vulgate: "For it is a tree, and not a man, neither can it increase the number of those who war against thee;" Onkelos, "For the tree of the field is not as a man, that it should come against thee in the siege;" and to the same purpose the Arabic, Philo, and Josephus who say, "If trees could speak, they would cry out, that it is unjust that they, who were no cause of the war, should suffer the miseries of it." However rendered, the sense is sufficiently clear, and it is a merciful provision to spare all the fruit trees for the support of both the besieged and besiegers. [[Deut.26|Deut 26:6]]
