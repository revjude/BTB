class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee \[for\] an inheritance, and possessest it, and dwellest therein;<small><small>[^2]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.26.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That thou shalt<small><small>[^3]</small></small> take of the first of all the fruit of the earth, which thou shalt bring of thy land that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee, and shalt put \[it\] in a basket, and shalt go unto<small><small>[^4]</small></small> the place which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God shall choose to place his name there. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.26.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt go unto class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.26.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when we cried<small><small>[^14]</small></small> unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of our fathers, the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> heard our voice, and looked<small><small>[^15]</small></small> on our affliction, and our labour, and our oppression: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.26.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^16]</small></small></span> brought us forth out of Egypt with a mighty hand, and with an outstretched arm, and with great<small><small>[^17]</small></small> terribleness, and with signs, and with wonders: <span \[even\] a land<small><small>[^19]</small></small> that floweth with milk and honey. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.26.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And now, behold, $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.26.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When thou hast made an end of tithing all the tithes<small><small>[^25]</small></small> of thine increase the third<small><small>[^26]</small></small> year, \[which is\] the year of tithing, and hast given \[it<small><small>[^27]</small></small>\] unto the Levite, the stranger, the fatherless, and the widow, that they may eat within thy gates, and be filled; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.26.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then thou shalt say before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, I have brought away the hallowed things out of \[mine\] house, and also have given them unto the Levite<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, and unto the stranger, to the fatherless, and to the widow, according to all thy commandments which thou hast commanded me: I have not<small><small>[^29]</small></small> transgressed thy commandments, neither have I forgotten<small><small>[^30]</small></small> \[them\]: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.26.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have not eaten<small><small>[^31]</small></small> thereof in my mourning, neither have I taken away \[ought\] thereof for \[any\] unclean \[use\], nor given \[ought\] thereof for the dead<small><small>[^32]</small></small>: \[but\] I have hearkened to the voice of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> my God, \[and\] have done according to all that thou hast commanded me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.26.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Look down<small><small>[^33]</small></small> from thy holy habitation, from heaven, and bless thy<small><small>[^34]</small></small> people Israel, and the land which thou hast given us, as thou<small><small>[^35]</small></small> swarest unto our fathers, a land that floweth with milk and honey.
