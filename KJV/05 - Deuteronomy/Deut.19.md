***Cities of Refuge***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Num.35|Numbers 35:9–34]] <big>†</big> [[Deut.4|Deuteronomy 4:41–43]] <big>†</big> [[Josh.20|Joshua 20:1–9]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb509

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.19.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God hath cut<small><small>[^2]</small></small> off the nations, whose land the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee, and thou succeedest<small><small>[^3]</small></small> them, and dwellest in their cities, and in their houses; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.19.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt separate three cities<small><small>[^4]</small></small> for thee in the midst of thy land, which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee to possess it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.19.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt prepare thee a way, and divide the coasts of thy land, which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee to inherit, into three parts, that every slayer may flee thither.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.19.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And this \[is\] the case of the slayer<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, which shall flee thither, that he may live: Whoso killeth his neighbour ignorantly, whom he hated not in time past<small><small>[^7]</small></small>; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.19.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As when a man goeth into the wood with his neighbour to hew wood, and his hand fetcheth a stroke with the axe to cut down the tree, and the head<small><small>[^8]</small></small> slippeth from the helve<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, and lighteth<small><small>[^10]</small></small> upon his neighbour, that he die; he shall flee<small><small>[^11]</small></small> unto one of those cities, and live: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.19.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lest the avenger<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of the blood pursue the slayer, while his heart is hot, and overtake him, because the way is long, and slay him<small><small>[^13]</small></small>; whereas he \[was\] not worthy<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of death, inasmuch as he hated him not in time past<small><small>[^15]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.19.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore I command thee, saying, Thou shalt separate three cities for thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.19.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God enlarge thy coast, as he hath sworn unto thy fathers, and give thee all the land which he promised to give unto thy fathers;<small><small>[^16]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.19.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If thou shalt<small><small>[^17]</small></small> keep all these commandments to do them, which I command thee this day, to love the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, and to walk ever in his ways; $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.19.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]But if any<small><small>[^20]</small></small> man hate his neighbour, and lie in wait for him, and rise up against him, and smite him mortally<small><small>[^21]</small></small> that he die, and fleeth into one of these cities: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.19.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the elders of his city shall send and fetch him thence, and deliver him into the hand of the avenger of blood, that he may die.<small><small>[^22]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.19.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thine eye<small><small>[^23]</small></small> shall not pity him, but thou<small><small>[^24]</small></small> shalt put away \[the guilt of\] innocent blood from Israel, that it may go well with thee. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.19.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt not remove<small><small>[^25]</small></small> thy neighbour’s landmark, which they of old time have set in thine inheritance, which thou shalt inherit in the land that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee to possess it.

[^25]: Before the extensive use of fences, landed property was marked out by stones or posts, set up so as to ascertain the divisions of family estates. It was easy to remove one of these landmarks, and set it in a different place; and thus a dishonest man might enlarge his own estate by contracting that of his neighbour. Hence it was a matter of considerable importance to prevent this crime among the Israelites; among whom, removing them would be equivalent to forging, altering, destroying, or concealing the title-deeds of an estate among us. Accordingly, by the Mosaic law, it was not only prohibited in the commandment against covetousness, but we find a particular curse expressly annexed to it in [[Deut.27|Deut 27:17]]. Josephus considers this law a general prohibition, intended not only to protect private property, but also to preserve the boundaries of kingdoms and countries inviolable.
