***The Altar on Mount Ebal***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Josh.8|Joshua 8:30–35]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb530

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.27.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses with the elders of Israel commanded the people, saying, Keep all<small><small>[^2]</small></small> the commandments which I command you this day. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.27.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it shall be on the day<small><small>[^3]</small></small> when ye shall pass over Jordan unto the<small><small>[^4]</small></small> land which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee, that thou shalt set thee up great stones<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, and plaister<small><small>[^6]</small></small> them with plaister: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.27.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt<small><small>[^7]</small></small> write upon them all the words of this law<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, when thou art passed over, that thou mayest go in unto the land which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee, a land<small><small>[^9]</small></small> that floweth with milk and honey; as the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of thy fathers hath promised thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.27.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore it shall be when ye be gone over Jordan, \[that\] ye shall set up these stones, which I command you this day, in mount Ebal<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, and thou shalt plaister them with plaister. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.27.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there<small><small>[^11]</small></small> shalt thou build an altar unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, an altar of stones: thou shalt not<small><small>[^12]</small></small> lift up \[any\] iron \[tool\] upon them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.27.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt build the altar of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God of whole stones: and thou shalt offer burnt offerings<small><small>[^13]</small></small> thereon unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.27.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt offer peace offerings<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, and shalt eat there, and rejoice<small><small>[^15]</small></small> before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.27.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt<small><small>[^16]</small></small> write upon the stones all the words of this law very plainly<small><small>[^17]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.27.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And Moses and the priests the Levites spake unto all Israel, saying, Take heed, and hearken, O Israel; this day<small><small>[^18]</small></small> thou art become the people of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.27.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt therefore obey the voice of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, and do his commandments and his statutes, which I command thee this day.<small><small>[^19]</small></small>

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.27.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses charged the people the same day, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.27.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> These shall stand upon mount Gerizim<small><small>[^20]</small></small> to bless the people, when ye are come over Jordan; Simeon<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, and Levi, and Judah, and Issachar, and Joseph, and Benjamin: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.27.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And these shall stand upon mount Ebal<small><small>[^22]</small></small> to curse<small><small>[^23]</small></small>; Reuben<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, Gad, and Asher, and Zebulun, Dan, and Naphtali.

[^31]: To each of the curses the people were to say Amen, as well as to the blessings; to denote a profession of their faith in the truth of them, that they were the real declarations of the wrath of God; and an acknowledgment of the equity of these curses. It was such an imprecation upon themselves, as strongly obliged them to have nothing to do with those evil practices on which the curse is entailed. We read of those who entered a curse to walk in God's law. [[Neh.10|Neh 10:29]]. All the people, by saying this Amen, became bound one for another, that they would observe God's laws, by which every man was obliged, as far as he could, to prevent his neighbour from breaking these laws, and to reprove those that had offended, lest they should bear sin and the curse for them.
