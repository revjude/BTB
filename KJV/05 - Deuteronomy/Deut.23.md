***Exclusion from the Congregation*** ^bsb519

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that is wounded<small><small>[^2]</small></small> in the stones, or hath his privy member cut off, shall not enter<small><small>[^3]</small></small> into the congregation of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A bastard shall not enter into the congregation of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; even to his tenth generation shall he not enter into the congregation of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> An Ammonite<small><small>[^5]</small></small> or Moabite shall not enter into the congregation of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; even to their tenth generation shall they not enter into the congregation of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> for ever: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because they met<small><small>[^6]</small></small> you not with bread and with water in the way, when ye came forth out of Egypt; and because they hired<small><small>[^7]</small></small> against thee Balaam the son of Beor of Pethor of Mesopotamia, to curse thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nevertheless<small><small>[^8]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God would not hearken unto Balaam; but the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God turned the curse into a blessing unto thee, because the<small><small>[^9]</small></small> <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God loved thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt<small><small>[^10]</small></small> not seek their peace nor their prosperity<small><small>[^11]</small></small> all thy days for ever. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Thou shalt not abhor an Edomite; for he \[is\] thy<small><small>[^12]</small></small> brother: thou shalt not abhor an Egyptian; because thou<small><small>[^13]</small></small> wast a stranger in his land. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The children that are begotten of them shall enter into<small><small>[^14]</small></small> the congregation of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> in their third generation<small><small>[^15]</small></small>.

***Uncleanness in the Camp***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Lev.15|Leviticus 15:1–12]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb520

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When the host goeth forth against thine enemies, then keep thee from every wicked thing.<small><small>[^16]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]If there be among you any man, that is not clean by reason of uncleanness that chanceth him by night, then shall he go abroad out of the camp, he shall not come within the camp:<small><small>[^17]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But it shall be, when evening<small><small>[^18]</small></small> cometh on<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, he shall wash \[himself<small><small>[^20]</small></small>\] with water: and when the sun is down, he shall come into the camp \[again\]. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Thou shalt have a place also without the camp, whither thou shalt go forth abroad:<small><small>[^21]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt have a paddle upon thy weapon; and it shall be, when thou wilt ease thyself<small><small>[^22]</small></small> abroad, thou shalt dig therewith, and shalt turn back and cover that<small><small>[^23]</small></small> which cometh from thee: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God walketh<small><small>[^24]</small></small> in the midst of thy camp, to deliver thee, and to give up thine enemies before thee; therefore shall thy camp be holy: that he see no unclean thing<small><small>[^25]</small></small> in thee, and turn away from thee.

***Miscellaneous Laws*** ^bsb521

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt not deliver unto his master the servant which is escaped from his master unto thee:<small><small>[^26]</small></small><small><small>[^27]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He shall dwell<small><small>[^28]</small></small> with thee, \[even\] among you, in that place which he shall choose in one of thy gates, where it liketh him best<small><small>[^29]</small></small>: thou shalt not<small><small>[^30]</small></small> oppress him. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]There shall be<small><small>[^31]</small></small> no whore<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of the daughters of Israel, nor a sodomite<small><small>[^33]</small></small> of the sons of Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt not bring the hire<small><small>[^34]</small></small> of a whore, or the price of a dog<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, into the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God for any vow<small><small>[^36]</small></small>: for even both these \[are\] abomination unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Thou shalt not lend upon usury to thy brother; usury of money, usury of victuals, usury of any thing that is lent upon usury:<small><small>[^37]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Unto a stranger<small><small>[^38]</small></small> thou mayest lend upon usury; but unto thy brother thou shalt not lend upon usury: that the<small><small>[^39]</small></small> <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God may bless thee in all that thou settest thine hand to in the land whither thou goest to possess it. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]When thou shalt vow a vow unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, thou shalt not slack to pay it: for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God will surely require it of thee; and it would be sin in thee.<small><small>[^40]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if thou shalt forbear to vow, it shall be no sin in thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That which<small><small>[^41]</small></small> is gone out of thy lips thou shalt keep and perform; \[even\] a freewill offering, according as thou hast vowed<small><small>[^42]</small></small> unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, which thou hast promised with thy mouth. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]When thou comest into thy neighbour’s vineyard, then thou mayest<small><small>[^43]</small></small> eat grapes thy fill at thine own pleasure; but thou shalt not put \[any\] in thy vessel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.23.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When thou comest into the standing corn of thy neighbour, then thou mayest<small><small>[^44]</small></small> pluck the ears with thine hand; but thou shalt not move a sickle unto thy neighbour’s standing corn. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Deut.22|<< Deuteronomy 22]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Deut.24|Deuteronomy 24 >>]]</td></tr></table>
