***Marriage and Divorce Laws***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Matt.5|Matthew 5:31–32]] <big>†</big> [[Luke.16|Luke 16:18]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb522

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When a man hath taken<small><small>[^2]</small></small> a wife, and married her, and it come to pass that she find no favour in his eyes, because he hath found some uncleanness<small><small>[^3]</small></small> in her: then let him<small><small>[^4]</small></small> write her a bill of divorcement<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, and give \[it\] in her hand, and send her<small><small>[^6]</small></small> out of his house. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when she is departed out of his house, she may go<small><small>[^7]</small></small> and be another man’s \[wife\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And \[if\] the latter husband hate her, and write her a bill of divorcement, and giveth \[it\] in her hand, and sendeth her out of his house; or if the latter husband die, which took her \[to be\] his wife; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Her former<small><small>[^8]</small></small> husband, which sent her away, may not take her again to be his wife, after that she is defiled; for that \[is\] abomination before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: and thou shalt<small><small>[^9]</small></small> not cause the land to sin, which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee \[for\] an inheritance. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When a man<small><small>[^10]</small></small> hath taken a new wife, he shall not go out to war, neither<small><small>[^11]</small></small> shall he be charged with any business: \[but\] he shall be free at home one year, and shall cheer up<small><small>[^12]</small></small> his wife which he hath taken.

***Additional Laws*** ^bsb523

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]No man shall take<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the nether or the upper millstone to pledge: for he taketh \[a man’s\] life<small><small>[^14]</small></small> to pledge. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]If a man be found<small><small>[^15]</small></small> stealing any of his brethren of the children of Israel, and maketh merchandise of him, or selleth him; then that<small><small>[^16]</small></small> thief shall die; and thou shalt<small><small>[^17]</small></small> put evil away from among you. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Take heed in the plague of leprosy, that thou observe diligently, and do according to all that the priests the Levites shall teach you: as I commanded them, \[so\] ye shall observe to do.<small><small>[^18]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Remember<small><small>[^19]</small></small> what the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God did unto Miriam<small><small>[^20]</small></small> by the way, after that ye were come forth out of Egypt. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]When<small><small>[^21]</small></small> thou dost lend thy brother any thing<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, thou shalt not go into his house to fetch his pledge. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt stand abroad, and the man to whom thou dost lend shall bring out the pledge abroad unto thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if the man \[be\] poor, thou shalt not sleep with his pledge:<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In any case thou shalt deliver<small><small>[^24]</small></small> him the pledge again when the sun<small><small>[^25]</small></small> goeth down, that he may sleep in his own raiment<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, and bless thee: and it shall be<small><small>[^27]</small></small> righteousness unto thee before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt not oppress an hired servant \[that is\] poor and needy, \[whether he be\] of thy brethren, or of thy strangers that \[are\] in thy land within thy gates:<small><small>[^28]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> At his<small><small>[^29]</small></small> day thou shalt give \[him\] his hire, neither shall the sun go down upon it; for he \[is\] poor, and setteth his heart upon it<small><small>[^30]</small></small>: lest he<small><small>[^31]</small></small> cry against thee unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and it be sin unto thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The fathers shall not be put to death for the children, neither shall the children be put to death for the fathers: every man shall be put to death for his own sin.<small><small>[^32]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Thou shalt not pervert<small><small>[^33]</small></small> the judgment of the stranger, \[nor\] of the fatherless; nor take<small><small>[^34]</small></small> a widow’s raiment to pledge: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But thou shalt remember that thou wast a bondman in Egypt, and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God redeemed thee thence: therefore I command thee to do this thing.<small><small>[^35]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]When thou<small><small>[^36]</small></small> cuttest down thine harvest in thy field, and hast forgot a sheaf in the field, thou shalt not go again to fetch it: it shall be<small><small>[^37]</small></small> for the stranger, for the fatherless, and for the widow: that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God may bless<small><small>[^38]</small></small> thee in all the work of thine hands. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When thou beatest thine olive tree, thou shalt not go over the boughs again<small><small>[^39]</small></small>: it shall be for the stranger, for the fatherless, and for the widow. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When thou gatherest<small><small>[^40]</small></small> the grapes of thy vineyard, thou shalt not glean \[it\] afterward<small><small>[^41]</small></small>: it shall be for the stranger, for the fatherless, and for the widow. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.24.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt remember that thou wast a bondman in the land of Egypt: therefore I command thee to do this thing. <small><small>[^42]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Deut.23|<< Deuteronomy 23]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Deut.25|Deuteronomy 25 >>]]</td></tr></table>
