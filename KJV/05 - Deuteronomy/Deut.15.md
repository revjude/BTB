<big><big>[[Deut.15.1 The Seventh Year|⥃]]</big></big>***The Seventh Year***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Exod.23|Exodus 23:10–13]] <big>†</big> [[Lev.25|Leviticus 25:1–7]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.15.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> At the end of \[every\] seven years thou shalt make a release.<small><small>[^2]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.15.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And this \[is\] the manner of the release: Every creditor that lendeth<small><small>[^3]</small></small> \[ought\] unto his neighbour shall release \[it\]; he shall not exact \[it<small><small>[^4]</small></small>\] of his neighbour, or of his brother; because it is called the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> release. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.15.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Of a foreigner thou mayest exact \[it again\]: but \[that\] which is thine with thy brother thine hand shall release;<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.15.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Save<small><small>[^6]</small></small> when there shall be no poor among you; for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall greatly bless<small><small>[^7]</small></small> thee in the land which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee \[for\] an inheritance to possess it: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.15.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Only if thou carefully hearken unto the voice of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, to observe to do all these commandments which I command thee this day.<small><small>[^8]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.15.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God blesseth thee, as he promised thee: and thou shalt lend<small><small>[^9]</small></small> unto many nations, but thou shalt not borrow; and thou shalt reign<small><small>[^10]</small></small> over many nations, but they shall not reign over thee.

<big><big>[[Deut.15.7 Generosity in Lending and Giving|⥃]]</big></big>***Generosity in Lending and Giving***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Matt.6|Matthew 6:1–4]] ]</sup></small>

[^36]: ***Tzevee***, in Arabic ***zaby***, Chaldee and Syriac ***tavya***, denotes the gazelle or antelope, so called from its stately beauty, as the word imports. In size it is smaller than the roe, of an elegant form, and it motions are light and graceful. It bounds seemingly without effort, and runs with such swiftness that few creatures can exceed it ([[2Sam.2|2Sam 2:18]]). Its fine eyes are so much celebrated as even to become a proverb; and its flesh is much esteemed for food among eastern nations, having a sweet, musky taste, which is highly agreeable to their palates ([[1Kgs.4|1Kgs 4:23]]). If to these circumstances we add, that they are gregarious, and common all over the East, whereas the roe is either not known at all, or else very rare in these countries, little doubt can remain that the gazelle and not the roe is intended by the original word.
