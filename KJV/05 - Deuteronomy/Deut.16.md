<big><big>[[Deut.16.1 Passover and the Feast of Unleavened Bread|⥃]]</big></big>***Passover and the Feast of Unleavened Bread***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Exod.12|Exodus 12:14–28]] <big>†</big> [[Lev.23|Leviticus 23:4–8]] <big>†</big> [[Num.28|Numbers 28:16–25]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Observe the month<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of Abib, and keep the passover<small><small>[^3]</small></small> unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God: for in<small><small>[^4]</small></small> the month of Abib the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God brought thee forth out of Egypt by night. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt therefore sacrifice<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the passover unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, of the flock and the herd, in the place which<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall choose to place his name there. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt eat no<small><small>[^7]</small></small> leavened bread with it; seven days shalt thou eat unleavened bread therewith, \[even\] the bread<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of affliction; for thou camest<small><small>[^9]</small></small> forth out of the land of Egypt in haste: that thou mayest<small><small>[^10]</small></small> remember the day when thou camest forth out of the land of Egypt all the days of thy life. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there shall<small><small>[^11]</small></small> be no leavened bread seen with thee in all thy coast seven days; neither<small><small>[^12]</small></small> shall there \[any thing\] of the flesh, which thou sacrificedst the first day at even, remain all night until the morning. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou mayest not sacrifice<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the passover within any of thy gates, which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But at the place which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God shall choose to place his name in, there thou shalt sacrifice the passover at even<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, at the going down of the sun, at the season that thou camest forth out of Egypt. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt roast<small><small>[^15]</small></small> and eat \[it\] in the place<small><small>[^16]</small></small> which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God shall choose: and thou shalt turn in the morning, and go unto thy tents. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Six days<small><small>[^17]</small></small> thou shalt eat unleavened bread: and on the seventh day \[shall be\] a solemn assembly<small><small>[^18]</small></small> to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God: thou shalt do no work \[therein\].

<big><big>[[Deut.16.9 The Feast of Weeks|⥃]]</big></big>***The Feast of Weeks***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Num.28|Numbers 28:26–31]] ]</sup></small>

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Seven weeks shalt thou number unto thee: begin to number the seven weeks from \[such time as\] thou beginnest \[to put\] the sickle to the corn.<small><small>[^19]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt keep the feast of weeks unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God with a tribute<small><small>[^20]</small></small> of a freewill offering of thine hand, which thou shalt give \[unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God\], according<small><small>[^21]</small></small> as the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God hath blessed thee: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt rejoice before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, thou, and thy son, and thy daughter, and thy manservant, and thy maidservant, and the Levite that \[is\] within thy gates, and the stranger, and the fatherless, and the widow, that \[are\] among you, in the place which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God hath chosen to place his name there.<small><small>[^22]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt remember that thou wast a bondman in Egypt: and thou shalt observe and do these statutes.<small><small>[^23]</small></small>

<big><big>[[Deut.16.13 The Feast of Tabernacles|⥃]]</big></big>***The Feast of Tabernacles***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Num.29|Numbers 29:12–40]] ]</sup></small>

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Thou shalt observe the feast<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of tabernacles seven days, after that thou hast gathered in thy corn and thy wine<small><small>[^25]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt rejoice in thy feast, thou, and thy son, and thy daughter, and thy manservant, and thy maidservant, and the Levite, the stranger, and the fatherless, and the widow, that \[are\] within thy gates.<small><small>[^26]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Seven days<small><small>[^27]</small></small> shalt thou keep a solemn feast unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God in the place which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall choose: because<small><small>[^28]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God shall bless thee in all thine increase, and in all the works of thine hands, therefore thou shalt surely rejoice. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Three times<small><small>[^29]</small></small> in a year shall all thy males appear before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God in the place which he shall choose; in the feast of unleavened bread, and in the feast of weeks, and in the feast of tabernacles: and they shall<small><small>[^30]</small></small> not appear before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> empty: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Every man \[shall give\] as he is able<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, according to the blessing of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God which he hath given thee.

<big><big>[[Deut.16.18 Judges and Justice|⥃]]</big></big>***Judges and Justice***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Judges<small><small>[^32]</small></small> and officers shalt thou make thee in all thy gates<small><small>[^33]</small></small>, which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee, throughout thy tribes: and they shall judge the people with just judgment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt not wrest<small><small>[^34]</small></small> judgment; thou shalt not respect<small><small>[^35]</small></small> persons, neither take a gift: for a gift doth blind the eyes of the wise, and pervert the words<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of the righteous. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.16.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That which<small><small>[^37]</small></small> is altogether just shalt thou follow, that thou mayest live<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, and inherit the land which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee.

[^3]: This word comes from the Hebrew verb ***pasach***, to pass, to leap or skip over. The destroying angel passed over the houses marked with the blood of the Paschal Lamb, so the wrath of God passes over those whose souls sprinkled with the blood of Christ. [[1Cor.5|1Cor 5:7]]. As the paschal lamb was killed before Israel was delivered, so by the death of Christ, we have redemption through his blood. It was killed before the tables of the law were delivered to Moses, or Aaron's sacrifices were enjoined; thus deliverance comes to men, not by the works of the law, but by the only true passover, the Lamb of God. [[Rom.3|Rom 3:25]]. [[Heb.9|Heb 9:14]]. It was killed the first month of the year, which prefigured that Christ should suffer death in that month. [[John.18|John 18:28]]. it was killed in the evening. [[Exod.12|Exod 12:6]]. Christ suffered at that time of the day. [[Matt.27|Matt 27:46]]. [[Heb.1|Heb 1:2]]. At even the sun sets; at Christ's passion, universal darkness was upon the whole earth. The passover was roasted with fire, denoting the sharp and dreadful pains that Christ should suffer, not only from men, but God also. It was to be eaten with bitter herbs, [[Exod.12|Exod 12:8]]; not only to put them in remembrance of their bitter bondage in Egypt, but also to testify our mortification to sin, and readiness to undergo afflictions for Christ, [[Col.1|Col 1:24]]; and likewise to teach us the absolute necessity of true repentance in all that would profitably feed by faith on Christ, the true paschal lamb.
