***Moses Blesses the Twelve Tribes*** ^bsb544

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And this \[is\] the blessing<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, wherewith Moses the man<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of God blessed the children of Israel before his death. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came from Sinai<small><small>[^4]</small></small>, and rose up from Seir unto them; he shined forth from mount Paran, and he came with ten thousands<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of saints: from his right hand \[went\] a fiery law<small><small>[^6]</small></small> for them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea, he loved<small><small>[^7]</small></small> the people; all his saints<small><small>[^8]</small></small> \[are\] in thy hand: and they sat<small><small>[^9]</small></small> down at thy feet; \[every one\] shall receive<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of thy words. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moses<small><small>[^11]</small></small> commanded us a law, \[even\] the inheritance<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of the congregation of Jacob. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he was king<small><small>[^13]</small></small> in Jeshurun<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, when the heads of the people \[and\] the tribes of Israel were gathered together. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let Reuben live, and not die; and let \[not\] his men be few.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And this \[is the blessing\] of Judah: and he said, Hear, <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, the voice of Judah, and bring<small><small>[^16]</small></small> him unto his people: let his hands<small><small>[^17]</small></small> be sufficient for him; and be thou<small><small>[^18]</small></small> an help \[to him\] from his enemies. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And of Levi he said, \[Let\] thy<small><small>[^19]</small></small> Thummim and thy Urim \[be\] with thy<small><small>[^20]</small></small> holy one, whom thou didst prove at<small><small>[^21]</small></small> Massah, \[and with\] whom thou didst strive at the waters of Meribah; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who said<small><small>[^22]</small></small> unto his father and to his mother, I have not<small><small>[^23]</small></small> seen him; neither did he acknowledge his brethren, nor knew his own children: for they<small><small>[^24]</small></small> have observed thy word, and kept thy covenant. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They shall teach<small><small>[^25]</small></small> Jacob thy judgments, and Israel thy law: they shall put incense<small><small>[^26]</small></small> before thee<small><small>[^27]</small></small>, and whole<small><small>[^28]</small></small> burnt sacrifice upon thine altar. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Bless, <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, his substance<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, and accept<small><small>[^30]</small></small> the work of his hands: smite<small><small>[^31]</small></small> through the loins of them that rise against him, and of them that hate him, that they rise not again. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[And\] of Benjamin he said, The beloved<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall dwell in safety by him; \[and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>\] shall cover him<small><small>[^33]</small></small> all the day long, and he shall dwell between his shoulders. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And of Joseph<small><small>[^34]</small></small> he said, Blessed of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[be\] his land, for the precious things of heaven, for the dew<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, and for the deep that coucheth beneath, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And for the precious<small><small>[^36]</small></small> fruits \[brought forth\] by the sun, and for the precious things put forth<small><small>[^37]</small></small> by the moon<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And for the chief things of the ancient mountains, and for the precious things of the lasting hills,<small><small>[^39]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And for the precious things of the earth<small><small>[^40]</small></small> and fulness thereof, and \[for\] the good<small><small>[^41]</small></small> will of him that dwelt in the bush: let \[the blessing\] come upon the head of Joseph, and upon the top<small><small>[^42]</small></small> of the head of him \[that was\] separated<small><small>[^43]</small></small> from his brethren. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> His glory \[is like\] the firstling<small><small>[^44]</small></small> of his bullock, and his horns<small><small>[^45]</small></small> \[are like\] the horns of unicorns<small><small>[^46]</small></small>: with them he shall push<small><small>[^47]</small></small> the people together to the ends of the earth: and they \[are\] the ten thousands<small><small>[^48]</small></small> of Ephraim, and they \[are\] the thousands of Manasseh. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And of Zebulun he said, Rejoice, Zebulun, in thy going out; and, Issachar, in thy tents.<small><small>[^49]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They shall call the people<small><small>[^50]</small></small> unto the mountain; there they shall<small><small>[^51]</small></small> offer sacrifices of righteousness: for they shall suck \[of<small><small>[^52]</small></small>\] the abundance of the seas, and \[of\] treasures hid in the sand. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And of Gad he said, Blessed<small><small>[^53]</small></small> \[be\] he that enlargeth Gad: he dwelleth<small><small>[^54]</small></small> as a lion, and teareth<small><small>[^55]</small></small> the arm with the crown of the head. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he provided the first part<small><small>[^56]</small></small> for himself, because there, \[in\] a portion<small><small>[^57]</small></small> of the lawgiver, \[was he\] seated<small><small>[^58]</small></small>; and he came<small><small>[^59]</small></small> with the heads of the people, he executed the justice of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and his judgments with Israel. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And of Dan he said, Dan \[is\] a lion’s whelp: he shall leap from Bashan.<small><small>[^60]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> <milesto<small><small>[^61]</small></small>ne marker="¶" subType="x-added" type="x-p"/>And of Naphtali he said, O Naphtali, satisfied with favour, and full with the blessing of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: possess<small><small>[^62]</small></small> thou the west and the south. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And of Asher he said, \[Let\] Asher \[be\] blessed<small><small>[^63]</small></small> with children; let him be<small><small>[^64]</small></small> acceptable to his brethren, and let him dip<small><small>[^65]</small></small> his foot in oil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy shoes<small><small>[^66]</small></small> \[shall be\] iron and brass; and as thy<small><small>[^67]</small></small> days, \[so shall\] thy strength \[be\]. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] none<small><small>[^68]</small></small> like unto the God of Jeshurun<small><small>[^69]</small></small>, \[who\] rideth<small><small>[^70]</small></small> upon the heaven in thy help, and in his excellency on the sky. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The eternal<small><small>[^71]</small></small> God \[is thy\] refuge<small><small>[^72]</small></small>, and underneath<small><small>[^73]</small></small> \[are\] the everlasting arms: and he shall thrust<small><small>[^74]</small></small> out the enemy from before thee; and shall say, Destroy \[them\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Israel<small><small>[^75]</small></small> then shall dwell in safety alone: the fountain<small><small>[^76]</small></small> of Jacob \[shall be\] upon a land of corn and wine; also his<small><small>[^77]</small></small> heavens shall drop down dew. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.33.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Happy<small><small>[^78]</small></small> \[art\] thou, O Israel: who \[is\] like unto thee, O people saved<small><small>[^79]</small></small> by the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, the shield<small><small>[^80]</small></small> of thy help, and who \[is\] the sword<small><small>[^81]</small></small> of thy excellency! and thine enemies shall be found liars<small><small>[^82]</small></small> unto thee; and thou shalt<small><small>[^83]</small></small> tread upon their high places. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Deut.32|<< Deuteronomy 32]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Deut.34|Deuteronomy 34 >>]]</td></tr></table>
