***Detestable Sacrifices*** ^bsb502

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.17.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt<small><small>[^2]</small></small> not sacrifice unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God \[any\] bullock, or sheep<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, wherein is blemish, \[or\] any evilfavouredness: for that<small><small>[^4]</small></small> \[is\] an abomination unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God.<small><small>[^5]</small></small>

***Purge the Idolater*** ^bsb503

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.17.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]If there be found among you, within any of thy gates<small><small>[^6]</small></small> which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee, man<small><small>[^7]</small></small> or woman, that hath wrought wickedness in the sight of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, in transgressing<small><small>[^8]</small></small> his covenant, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.17.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And hath gone and served other gods, and worshipped them, either the sun<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, or moon, or any of the host of heaven, which<small><small>[^10]</small></small> I have not commanded; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.17.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it be told thee, and thou hast heard \[of it\], and enquired<small><small>[^11]</small></small> diligently, and, behold, \[it be\] true, \[and\] the thing certain, \[that\] such abomination is wrought in Israel: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.17.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shalt thou bring forth that man or that woman, which have committed that wicked thing, unto thy gates, \[even\] that man or that woman, and shalt stone them<small><small>[^12]</small></small> with stones, till they die. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.17.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> At the mouth of two witnesses, or three witnesses, shall he that is worthy of death be put to death; \[but\] at the mouth of one witness he shall not be put to death.<small><small>[^13]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.17.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The hands of the witnesses<small><small>[^14]</small></small> shall be first upon him to put him to death, and afterward the hands of all the people. So thou<small><small>[^15]</small></small> shalt put the evil away from among you.

***Courts of Law*** ^bsb504

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.17.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If there arise<small><small>[^16]</small></small> a matter too hard for thee in judgment, between blood<small><small>[^17]</small></small> and blood, between plea and plea, and between stroke and stroke, \[being\] matters of controversy within thy gates: then shalt thou arise, and get thee up<small><small>[^18]</small></small> into the place which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God shall choose; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.17.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt come unto the priests<small><small>[^19]</small></small> the Levites, and unto the judge that shall be in those days, and enquire; and they shall<small><small>[^20]</small></small> shew thee the sentence of judgment: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.17.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt do according to the sentence, which they of that place which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall choose shall shew thee; and thou shalt observe to do according to all that they inform thee:<small><small>[^21]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.17.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> According to<small><small>[^22]</small></small> the sentence of the law which they shall teach thee, and according to the judgment which they shall tell thee, thou shalt do: thou shalt not decline from the sentence which they shall shew thee, \[to\] the right<small><small>[^23]</small></small> hand, nor \[to\] the left. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.17.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the man that will do<small><small>[^24]</small></small> presumptuously, and will not hearken<small><small>[^25]</small></small> unto the priest<small><small>[^26]</small></small> that standeth to minister there before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, or unto the judge, even that man<small><small>[^27]</small></small> shall die: and thou shalt<small><small>[^28]</small></small> put away the evil from Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.17.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all the people shall hear<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, and fear, and do no more presumptuously<small><small>[^30]</small></small>.

[^36]: Multiplying horses for chariots of war and cavalry, or for luxury, would increase the splendour of a monarch, and form a ground of confidence distinct from a proper confidence in God, and inconsistent with it, and with considering him as the glory of Israel. Egypt abounded in horses; and the desire of multiplying these would induce the prince to encourage a trade with that kingdom; and this might make way for the Israelites being again subjugated by the Egyptians, or at least corrupted by their idolatries and vices. Whereas, it was the command of God that they should no more return thither, but be totally detached from them.
