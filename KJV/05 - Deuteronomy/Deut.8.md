<big><big>[[Deut.8.1 Remember the LORD Your God|⥃]]</big></big>***Remember the LORD Your God***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All the commandments which I command thee this day shall ye observe to do, that ye may live, and multiply, and go in and possess the land which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> sware unto your fathers.<small><small>[^2]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt remember<small><small>[^3]</small></small> all the way which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God led thee<small><small>[^4]</small></small> these forty years in the wilderness, to humble<small><small>[^5]</small></small> thee, \[and\] to prove thee<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, to know<small><small>[^7]</small></small> what \[was\] in thine heart, whether thou wouldest keep his commandments, or no. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he humbled thee, and suffered thee to hunger, and fed thee<small><small>[^8]</small></small> with manna, which thou knewest not, neither did thy fathers know; that he might make thee know that man doth<small><small>[^9]</small></small> not live by bread only, but by every \[word\] that proceedeth out of the mouth of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> doth man live. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy raiment waxed not old upon thee, neither did thy foot swell, these forty years.<small><small>[^10]</small></small><small><small>[^11]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt also consider<small><small>[^12]</small></small> in thine heart, that, as a man<small><small>[^13]</small></small> chasteneth his son, \[so\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God chasteneth thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thou shalt keep the commandments of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, to walk<small><small>[^14]</small></small> in his ways, and to fear him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God bringeth thee into a good land, a land of brooks of water, of fountains and depths that spring out of valleys and hills;<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A land of wheat<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, and barley<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, and vines<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, and fig trees, and pomegranates; a land of oil olive<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, and honey; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A land wherein thou shalt eat bread without scarceness, thou shalt not lack any \[thing\] in it; a land whose stones<small><small>[^20]</small></small> \[are\] iron, and out of whose hills thou mayest dig brass. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When thou hast<small><small>[^21]</small></small> eaten and art full, then thou<small><small>[^22]</small></small> shalt bless the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God for the good land which he hath given thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Beware that thou forget not the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, in not keeping his commandments, and his judgments, and his statutes, which I command thee this day:<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lest \[when<small><small>[^24]</small></small>\] thou hast eaten and art full, and hast built<small><small>[^25]</small></small> goodly houses, and dwelt \[therein\]; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And \[when\] thy herds and thy flocks multiply, and thy silver and thy gold is multiplied, and all that thou hast is multiplied;<small><small>[^26]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then thine heart<small><small>[^27]</small></small> be lifted up, and thou forget<small><small>[^28]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, which brought thee forth out of the land of Egypt, from the house of bondage; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who led thee<small><small>[^29]</small></small> through that great and terrible wilderness, \[wherein were\] fiery serpents<small><small>[^30]</small></small>, and scorpions, and drought, where \[there was\] no water; who brought<small><small>[^31]</small></small> thee forth water out of the rock of flint; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who fed thee<small><small>[^32]</small></small> in the wilderness with manna, which thy fathers knew not, that he might<small><small>[^33]</small></small> humble thee, and that he might prove thee, to do thee<small><small>[^34]</small></small> good at thy latter end; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou say<small><small>[^35]</small></small> in thine heart, My power<small><small>[^36]</small></small> and the might of \[mine\] hand hath gotten me this wealth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But thou shalt remember the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God: for \[it is\] he that<small><small>[^37]</small></small> giveth thee power to get wealth, that he may<small><small>[^38]</small></small> establish his covenant which he sware unto thy fathers, as \[it is\] this day. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it shall be, if thou do at all forget the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, and walk after other gods, and serve them, and worship them, I testify against<small><small>[^39]</small></small> you this day that ye shall surely perish. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.8.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As the nations which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> destroyeth before your face, so shall ye perish<small><small>[^40]</small></small>; because ye would not be obedient unto the voice of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> your God. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Deut.7|<< Deuteronomy 7]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Deut.9|Deuteronomy 9 >>]]</td></tr></table>

[^10]: Many have attempted to give the following meaning to this text - "God so amply provided for them all the necessaries of life, that they never were obliged to wear tattered garments, nor were their feet injured for lack of shoes or sandals." Now, though the Israelites doubtless brought out of Egypt more raiment than what they had upon them; and they might manufacture the fleeces of their flocks in the wilderness; and also might be favoured by Providence with other supplies from the neighbouring nations or travelling hordes of Arabs; yet, when we consider their immense numbers, their situation and long continuance in the wilderness, and the very strong expressions made use of in the text, why should we question the extraordinary and miraculous interposition of God in this respect, as well as in others, not less stupendous in their nature, or constant in their supply?
