***Clean and Unclean Animals***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Lev.11|Leviticus 11:1–47]] <big>†</big> [[Acts.10|Acts 10:9–16]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb491

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ye \[are\] the children<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> your God: ye shall not<small><small>[^3]</small></small> cut yourselves, nor make any baldness between your eyes for the dead. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thou \[art\] an holy people unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath chosen thee to be a peculiar people unto himself, above all the nations that \[are\] upon the earth.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Thou shalt not eat any abominable thing.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> These \[are\] the beasts which ye shall eat: the ox, the sheep, and the goat,<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The hart, and the roebuck, and the fallow deer, and the wild goat<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, and the pygarg<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, and the wild ox<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, and the chamois. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And every beast that parteth the hoof, and cleaveth the cleft into two claws, \[and\] cheweth the cud among the beasts, that ye shall eat.<small><small>[^10]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nevertheless these ye shall not eat of them that chew the cud, or of them that divide the cloven hoof; \[as\] the camel, and the hare, and the coney: for they chew the cud, but divide not the hoof; \[therefore\] they \[are\] unclean unto you.<small><small>[^11]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the swine<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, because it divideth the hoof, yet cheweth not the cud, it \[is\] unclean unto you: ye shall not eat of their flesh, nor touch<small><small>[^13]</small></small> their dead carcase. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]These ye shall eat of all that \[are\] in the waters: all that have fins and scales shall ye eat:<small><small>[^14]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And whatsoever hath not fins and scales ye may not eat; it \[is\] unclean unto you. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]\[Of\] all clean birds ye shall eat. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But these \[are they\] of which ye shall not eat: the eagle, and the ossifrage, and the ospray,<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the glede<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, and the kite, and the vulture after his kind, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And every raven after his kind, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the owl, and the night<small><small>[^17]</small></small> hawk, and the cuckow, and the hawk after his kind,<small><small>[^18]</small></small><small><small>[^19]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The little owl, and the great owl, and the swan<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the pelican, and the gier<small><small>[^21]</small></small> eagle, and the cormorant<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the stork, and the heron after her kind, and the lapwing<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, and the bat. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And every creeping thing that flieth \[is\] unclean unto you: they shall not be eaten.<small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[But of\] all clean fowls ye may eat. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Ye shall not eat \[of\] any thing<small><small>[^25]</small></small> that dieth of itself: thou shalt give it unto the stranger<small><small>[^26]</small></small> that \[is\] in thy gates, that he may eat it; or thou mayest sell it unto an alien: for thou \[art\] an holy<small><small>[^27]</small></small> people unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God. Thou shalt<small><small>[^28]</small></small> not seethe a kid in his mother’s milk.

***Giving Tithes***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Lev.27|Leviticus 27:30–34]] <big>†</big> [[Deut.26|Deuteronomy 26:1–15]] <big>†</big> [[Neh.13|Nehemiah 13:10–14]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb492

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt truly tithe all the increase of thy seed, that the field bringeth forth year by year.<small><small>[^29]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt eat before<small><small>[^30]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, in the place which he shall choose to place his name there, the tithe of thy corn, of thy wine, and of thine oil, and the firstlings<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of thy herds and of thy flocks; that thou mayest learn to fear the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God always. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if the way be too long for thee, so that thou art not able to carry it; \[or\] if the place<small><small>[^32]</small></small> be too far from thee, which<small><small>[^33]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God shall choose to set his name there, when the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God hath blessed thee: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shalt thou turn \[it\] into money, and bind up the money in thine hand, and shalt go unto the place which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God shall choose: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt bestow<small><small>[^34]</small></small> that money for whatsoever thy soul<small><small>[^35]</small></small> lusteth after, for oxen, or for sheep, or for wine, or for strong drink, or for whatsoever thy soul desireth<small><small>[^36]</small></small>: and thou shalt eat<small><small>[^37]</small></small> there before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, and thou shalt rejoice<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, thou, and thine household, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the Levite<small><small>[^39]</small></small> that \[is\] within thy gates; thou shalt not forsake him; for he hath no<small><small>[^40]</small></small> part nor inheritance with thee. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]At the end<small><small>[^41]</small></small> of three years thou shalt bring<small><small>[^42]</small></small> forth all the tithe of thine increase the same year, and shalt lay \[it\] up within thy gates:<small><small>[^43]</small></small><small><small>[^44]</small></small><small><small>[^45]</small></small><small><small>[^46]</small></small><small><small>[^47]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Deut.14.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the Levite, (because he hath<small><small>[^48]</small></small> no part nor inheritance with thee,) and the stranger<small><small>[^49]</small></small>, and the fatherless, and the widow, which \[are\] within thy gates, shall come, and shall eat and be satisfied; that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God may bless thee in all the work of thine hand which thou doest. <small><small>[^50]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Deut.13|<< Deuteronomy 13]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Deut.15|Deuteronomy 15 >>]]</td></tr></table>
