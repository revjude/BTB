<big><big>[[Hag.2.1 The Coming Glory of God’s House|⥃]]</big></big>***The Coming Glory of God’s House***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the seventh<small><small>[^2]</small></small> \[month\], in the one and twentieth \[day\] of the month, came the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> by the prophet<small><small>[^3]</small></small> Haggai, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Speak now to Zerubbabel the son of Shealtiel, governor<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of Judah, and to Joshua the son of Josedech, the high priest, and to the residue of the people, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who \[is\] left<small><small>[^5]</small></small> among you that saw this house in her first glory<small><small>[^6]</small></small>? and how do ye see it now? \[is it\] not in your eyes in comparison of it as nothing? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet now<small><small>[^7]</small></small> be strong, O Zerubbabel, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and be strong, O Joshua, son of Josedech, the high priest; and be strong, all ye people of the land, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and work: for<small><small>[^8]</small></small> I \[am\] with you, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[According to\] the<small><small>[^9]</small></small> word that I covenanted with you when ye came out of Egypt, so<small><small>[^10]</small></small> my spirit remaineth among you: fear<small><small>[^11]</small></small> ye not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts; Yet<small><small>[^12]</small></small> once, it \[is<small><small>[^13]</small></small>\] a little while, and I<small><small>[^14]</small></small> will shake the heavens, and the earth, and the sea, and the dry \[land\]; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will shake<small><small>[^15]</small></small> all nations, and the<small><small>[^16]</small></small> desire of all nations shall come: and I will fill<small><small>[^17]</small></small> this house with glory, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The silver \[is\] mine, and the gold \[is\] mine, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts.<small><small>[^18]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The glory<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of this latter house shall be greater than of the former, saith<small><small>[^20]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts: and in this place will I give<small><small>[^21]</small></small> peace, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts.

<big><big>[[Hag.2.10 Blessings for a Defiled People|⥃]]</big></big>***Blessings for a Defiled People***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the four and twentieth \[day\] of the ninth \[month\], in the second year of Darius, came the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> by Haggai the prophet, saying,<small><small>[^22]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts; Ask now the priests \[concerning\] the law, saying,<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If one bear holy flesh in the skirt of his garment, and with his skirt do touch bread, or pottage, or wine, or oil, or any meat, shall it be holy? And the priests answered and said, No.<small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said Haggai, If \[one that is\] unclean by a dead body touch any of these, shall it be unclean? And the priests answered and said, It shall be unclean.<small><small>[^25]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then answered Haggai, and said, So \[is\] this people<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, and so \[is\] this nation before me, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and so \[is\] every work of their hands; and that<small><small>[^27]</small></small> which they offer there \[is\] unclean. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And now, I pray you, consider<small><small>[^28]</small></small> from this day and upward, from before<small><small>[^29]</small></small> a stone was laid upon a stone in the temple of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Since those \[days\] were, when \[one\] came to an<small><small>[^30]</small></small> heap of twenty \[measures\], there were \[but\] ten: when \[one\] came to the pressfat for to draw out fifty \[vessels\] out of the press, there were \[but\] twenty. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I smote you with blasting<small><small>[^31]</small></small> and with mildew and with hail<small><small>[^32]</small></small> in all<small><small>[^33]</small></small> the labours of your hands; yet<small><small>[^34]</small></small> ye \[turned\] not to me, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Consider<small><small>[^35]</small></small> now from this day and upward, from the four and twentieth day of the ninth \[month\], \[even<small><small>[^36]</small></small>\] from the day that the foundation of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> temple was laid, consider \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hag.2.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Is the seed yet in the barn? yea, as<small><small>[^37]</small></small> yet the vine, and the fig tree, and the pomegranate, and the olive tree, hath not brought forth: from<small><small>[^38]</small></small> this day will I bless \[you\].

[^20]: Whoever compares the description of the temple of Solomon, in the first book of Kings, with the most splendid accounts of the second temple, however adorned with costly stones and other magnificent decorations in after ages, must perceive that the former, being wholly overlaid with pure gold, was incomparably more glorious than the latter in its greatest magnificence; and the Jews themselves allow that the ark of the covenant, fire from heaven, the ***Urim*** and ***Thummim*** the anointing oil, the ***Shechinah*** or visible glory, and the spirit of prophecy, which distinguished the former temple, were wanting in this. In nothing, in fact, could the second temple excel the first in glory, except in the personal presence of "the Desire of all nations," He who is "the glory of the Lord," and the true temple, "in whom dwells all the fulness of the Godhead bodily," and who was the true ***Shechinah*** of which that of Solomon's temple was merely a type. And if it be admitted that the presence of the promised Messiah was intended, then it will follow that "Jesus of Nazareth" was He; for the second temple, in which as the "Prince of peace" he preached peace and reconciliation with God, has been utterly destroyed for upwards of seventeen hundred years.
