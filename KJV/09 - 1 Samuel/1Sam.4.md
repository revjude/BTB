***The Philistines Capture the Ark*** ^bsb665

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the word of Samuel came<small><small>[^2]</small></small> to all Israel. Now Israel went out against the Philistines to battle, and pitched beside Eben–ezer: and the Philistines pitched in Aphek<small><small>[^3]</small></small>.<small><small>[^4]</small></small><small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the Philistines put<small><small>[^6]</small></small> themselves in array against Israel: and when they joined battle<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, Israel<small><small>[^8]</small></small> was smitten before the Philistines: and they<small><small>[^9]</small></small> slew of the army<small><small>[^10]</small></small> in the field about four thousand men. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And when the people were come into the camp, the elders of Israel said, Wherefore<small><small>[^11]</small></small> hath the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> smitten us to day before the Philistines? Let us<small><small>[^12]</small></small> fetch<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the ark<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of the covenant of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> out of Shiloh unto us, that, when it cometh among us, it may save<small><small>[^15]</small></small> us out of the hand of our enemies. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So the people sent to Shiloh, that they might bring from thence the ark of the covenant of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, which dwelleth<small><small>[^16]</small></small> \[between\] the cherubims<small><small>[^17]</small></small>: and the two sons of Eli, Hophni<small><small>[^18]</small></small> and Phinehas, \[were\] there with the ark<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of the covenant of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when the ark of the covenant of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came into the camp, all Israel<small><small>[^20]</small></small> shouted with a great shout, so that the earth rang again. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when the Philistines heard the noise of the shout, they said, What \[meaneth<small><small>[^21]</small></small>\] the noise of this great shout in the camp of the Hebrews? And they understood that the ark of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> was come into the camp. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the Philistines were afraid<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, for they said, God is come into the camp. And they said, Woe unto us! for there hath not been such a thing heretofore<small><small>[^23]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Woe unto us! who shall deliver us out of the hand of these mighty Gods? these \[are\] the Gods that smote<small><small>[^24]</small></small> the Egyptians with all the plagues in the wilderness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be strong<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, and quit yourselves like men, O ye Philistines, that ye be not servants unto the Hebrews, as they have<small><small>[^26]</small></small> been to you: quit yourselves like men<small><small>[^27]</small></small>, and fight. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the Philistines fought, and Israel<small><small>[^28]</small></small> was smitten, and they fled every man<small><small>[^29]</small></small> into his tent: and there was a very great<small><small>[^30]</small></small> slaughter; for there fell of Israel thirty thousand footmen. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the ark<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of God was taken; and the two sons<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of Eli, Hophni and Phinehas, were slain<small><small>[^33]</small></small>.

***The Death of Eli*** ^bsb666

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there ran a man of Benjamin out of the army, and came to Shiloh the same day with his clothes rent<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, and with earth<small><small>[^35]</small></small> upon his head. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when he came, lo, Eli sat upon<small><small>[^36]</small></small> a seat by the wayside watching: for his heart<small><small>[^37]</small></small> trembled for the ark of God. And when the man came into the city, and told \[it\], all the city cried out. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when Eli heard the noise of the crying, he said, What \[meaneth<small><small>[^38]</small></small>\] the noise of this tumult? And the man came in hastily, and told Eli. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now Eli was ninety<small><small>[^39]</small></small> and eight years old; and his eyes<small><small>[^40]</small></small> were dim<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, that he could not see. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the man said unto Eli, I \[am\] he that came out of the army, and I fled to day out of the army. And he said, What is there done<small><small>[^42]</small></small>, my son<small><small>[^43]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the messenger answered and said, Israel<small><small>[^44]</small></small> is fled before the Philistines, and there hath been also a great slaughter among the people, and thy two sons also, Hophni and Phinehas, are dead, and the ark of God is taken. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.4.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, when he made<small><small>[^45]</small></small> mention of the ark of God, that he fell from off the seat backward by the side of the gate, and his neck<small><small>[^46]</small></small> brake, and he died: for he was an old man, and heavy. And he had<small><small>[^47]</small></small> judged Israel forty years.
