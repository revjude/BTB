***Eli’s Wicked Sons*** ^bsb661

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.2.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Now the sons<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of Eli \[were\] sons of Belial<small><small>[^38]</small></small>; they knew<small><small>[^39]</small></small> not the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.2.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the priests’ custom with the people \[was\], \[that\], when any man offered sacrifice, the priest’s servant came, while the flesh was in seething, with a fleshhook of three teeth in his hand; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.2.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he struck \[it\] into the pan, or kettle, or caldron, or pot; all that the fleshhook<small><small>[^40]</small></small> brought up the priest took for himself. $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.2.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]But Samuel ministered<small><small>[^46]</small></small> before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[being\] a child, girded with a linen ephod<small><small>[^47]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.2.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover his mother made him a little coat<small><small>[^48]</small></small>, and brought \[it\] to him from year to year<small><small>[^49]</small></small>, when she came up with her husband to offer the yearly sacrifice. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.2.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And Eli blessed<small><small>[^50]</small></small> Elkanah and his wife, and said, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> give thee seed of this woman for the loan<small><small>[^51]</small></small> which is lent to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.2.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Now<small><small>[^55]</small></small> Eli was very old, and heard all that his sons did unto<small><small>[^56]</small></small> all Israel; and how they lay with the women<small><small>[^57]</small></small> that assembled<small><small>[^58]</small></small> \[at\] the door of the tabernacle of the congregation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.2.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said unto them, Why<small><small>[^59]</small></small> do ye such things? for I hear<small><small>[^60]</small></small> of your evil dealings by all<small><small>[^61]</small></small> this people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.2.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nay, my sons; for \[it is\] no good<small><small>[^62]</small></small> report that I hear: ye make<small><small>[^63]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> people to transgress<small><small>[^64]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.2.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If one man sin against<small><small>[^65]</small></small> another, the judge shall judge him: but if a man<small><small>[^66]</small></small> sin against the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, who shall<small><small>[^67]</small></small> intreat for him?
