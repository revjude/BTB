***Elkanah and His Wives***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Ps.113|Psalms 113:1–9]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb657

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.1.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now there was a certain man of Ramathaim–zophim, of mount<small><small>[^3]</small></small> Ephraim, and his name \[was\] Elkanah<small><small>[^4]</small></small>, the son of Jeroham, the son of Elihu, the son of Tohu, the son of Zuph<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, an Ephrathite<small><small>[^6]</small></small>:<small><small>[^7]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.1.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he had two<small><small>[^8]</small></small> wives; the name of the one \[was\] Hannah, and the name of the other Peninnah: and Peninnah had children, but<small><small>[^9]</small></small> Hannah had no children. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.1.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And this man went up out of his city yearly<small><small>[^10]</small></small> to worship<small><small>[^11]</small></small> and to sacrifice unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts in Shiloh<small><small>[^12]</small></small>. ***Hannah Prays for a Son*** ^bsb658

Now Eli the priest sat upon a seat by a post of the temple of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.1.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And she \[was\] in bitterness of soul<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, and prayed<small><small>[^26]</small></small> unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and wept sore<small><small>[^27]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.1.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And she vowed<small><small>[^28]</small></small> a vow, and said, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, if thou wilt indeed look<small><small>[^29]</small></small> on the affliction of thine handmaid, and remember<small><small>[^30]</small></small> me, and not forget thine handmaid, but wilt give unto thine handmaid a man child<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, then I will give<small><small>[^32]</small></small> him unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> all the days of his life, and there<small><small>[^33]</small></small> shall no razor come upon his head. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.1.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, as she continued praying<small><small>[^34]</small></small> before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that Eli marked her mouth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.1.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now Hannah, she spake<small><small>[^35]</small></small> in her heart; only her lips moved, but her voice was not heard: therefore Eli thought she had<small><small>[^36]</small></small> been drunken. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.1.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Eli said unto her, How long<small><small>[^37]</small></small> wilt thou be drunken? ***The Birth of Samuel*** ^bsb659

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.1.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they rose<small><small>[^49]</small></small> up in the morning early, and worshipped before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and returned, and came to their house to Ramah: and Elkanah knew<small><small>[^50]</small></small> Hannah his wife; and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> remembered her.<small><small>[^51]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.1.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore it came to pass, when the time was come about<small><small>[^52]</small></small> after Hannah had conceived, that she bare a son, and called his name Samuel, \[saying\], Because I have asked him of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.1.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the man Elkanah, and all his house, went up to offer unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> the yearly sacrifice, and his vow.<small><small>[^53]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.1.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But Hannah went not up; for she said unto her husband, \[I will not go up\] until the child be weaned, and \[then<small><small>[^54]</small></small>\] I will bring him, that he may appear before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and there<small><small>[^55]</small></small> abide for ever<small><small>[^56]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.1.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Elkanah her husband said unto her, Do what<small><small>[^57]</small></small> seemeth thee good; tarry until thou have weaned him; only the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> establish his word. So the woman abode, and gave her son suck<small><small>[^58]</small></small> until she weaned him.<small><small>[^59]</small></small>
