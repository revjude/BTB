***The Amalekites Raid Ziklag*** ^bsb721

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, when David and his men were come<small><small>[^2]</small></small> to Ziklag on the third<small><small>[^3]</small></small> day, that the Amalekites<small><small>[^4]</small></small> had invaded the south, and Ziklag, and smitten Ziklag, and burned it with fire; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And had taken the women captives, that \[were\] therein: they slew not<small><small>[^5]</small></small> any, either great or small, but carried \[them\] away, and went on their way. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]So David and his men came to the city, and, behold, \[it was\] burned<small><small>[^6]</small></small> with fire; and their wives, and their sons, and their daughters, were taken captives. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then David and the people that \[were\] with him lifted up<small><small>[^7]</small></small> their voice and wept, until they had no more power to weep. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And David’s two wives<small><small>[^8]</small></small> were taken captives, Ahinoam the Jezreelitess, and Abigail the wife of Nabal the Carmelite. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And David was greatly<small><small>[^9]</small></small> distressed; for the people<small><small>[^10]</small></small> spake of stoning him, because the soul of all the people was grieved<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, every man for his sons and for his daughters: but David<small><small>[^12]</small></small> encouraged himself in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> his God.

***David Destroys the Amalekites*** ^bsb722

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And David said to Abiathar<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the priest, Ahimelech’s son, I pray thee, bring me hither the ephod. And Abiathar brought thither the ephod to David. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And David enquired<small><small>[^14]</small></small> at the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, saying, Shall I pursue after this troop? shall I overtake them? And he answered him<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, Pursue: for thou shalt surely overtake \[them\], and without fail recover \[all\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So David went, he and the six hundred men that \[were\] with him, and came to the brook Besor, where those that were left behind stayed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But David pursued, he and four hundred men: for two hundred<small><small>[^16]</small></small> abode behind, which were so faint<small><small>[^17]</small></small> that they could not go over the brook Besor<small><small>[^18]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And they found an Egyptian in the field, and brought him to David, and gave him<small><small>[^19]</small></small> bread, and he did eat; and they made him drink water; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they gave him a piece of a cake of figs, and two clusters of raisins: and when he had eaten, his spirit<small><small>[^20]</small></small> came again to him: for he had eaten no bread, nor drunk \[any\] water, three days<small><small>[^21]</small></small> and three nights. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And David said unto him, To whom \[belongest\] thou? and whence \[art\] thou? $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And when he<small><small>[^28]</small></small> had brought him down, behold, \[they were\] spread abroad upon all the earth, eating<small><small>[^29]</small></small> and drinking, and dancing, because of all<small><small>[^30]</small></small> the great spoil that they had taken out of the land of the Philistines, and out of the land of Judah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And David smote them from the twilight even unto the evening of the next day<small><small>[^31]</small></small>: and there<small><small>[^32]</small></small> escaped not a man of them, save four hundred young men, which rode upon camels, and fled. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And David recovered all that the Amalekites had carried away: and David rescued his two wives. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there was nothing lacking to them, neither small nor great, neither sons nor daughters, neither spoil, nor any \[thing\] that they had taken to them: David recovered all.<small><small>[^33]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.30.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And David took all the flocks and the herds, \[which\] they drave before those \[other\] cattle, and said, This \[is\] David’s spoil<small><small>[^34]</small></small>.
