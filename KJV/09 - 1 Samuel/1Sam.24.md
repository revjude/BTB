***David Spares Saul*** ^bsb708

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, when Saul<small><small>[^2]</small></small> was returned from following<small><small>[^3]</small></small> the Philistines, that it was told<small><small>[^4]</small></small> him, saying, Behold, David \[is\] in the wilderness<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of En–gedi. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Saul took<small><small>[^6]</small></small> three thousand chosen men out of all Israel, and went<small><small>[^7]</small></small> to seek David and his men upon the rocks<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of the wild goats. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he came to the sheepcotes<small><small>[^9]</small></small> by the way, where \[was\] a cave; and Saul<small><small>[^10]</small></small> went in to cover<small><small>[^11]</small></small> his feet: and David<small><small>[^12]</small></small> and his men remained in the sides of the cave. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the men<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of David said unto him, Behold the day of which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto thee, Behold, I will deliver<small><small>[^14]</small></small> thine enemy into thine hand, that thou mayest do to him as it shall seem good unto thee. Then David arose, and cut off the skirt of Saul’s robe privily.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass afterward, that David’s heart smote him, because he had cut off Saul’s skirt.<small><small>[^16]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said unto his men, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> forbid that I should do this thing unto my master, the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> anointed, to stretch forth mine hand against him, seeing he \[is\] the anointed of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^17]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So David stayed<small><small>[^18]</small></small> his servants with these words, and suffered<small><small>[^19]</small></small> them not to rise against Saul. But Saul rose up out of the cave, and went on \[his\] way. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> David also arose afterward, and went out of the cave, and cried after Saul, saying, My lord<small><small>[^20]</small></small> the king. And when Saul looked behind him, David stooped<small><small>[^21]</small></small> with his face to the earth, and bowed himself. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And David said to Saul, Wherefore hearest thou men’s words, saying, Behold, David seeketh thy hurt?<small><small>[^22]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, this day thine eyes have seen how that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had delivered thee to day into mine hand in the cave: and \[some\] bade \[me<small><small>[^23]</small></small>\] kill thee: but \[mine eye\] spared thee; and I said, I will not put forth mine hand against my lord; for he \[is\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> anointed.<small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover, my father<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, see, yea, see the skirt of thy robe in my hand: for in that I cut off the skirt of thy robe, and killed thee not, know thou and see that \[there is\] neither evil<small><small>[^26]</small></small> nor transgression in mine hand, and I have not sinned against thee; yet thou huntest<small><small>[^27]</small></small> my soul to take it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> judge between me and thee, and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> avenge me of thee: but mine hand<small><small>[^28]</small></small> shall not be upon thee.<small><small>[^29]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As saith the proverb of the ancients, Wickedness<small><small>[^30]</small></small> proceedeth from the wicked: but mine hand shall not be upon thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> After whom is the king<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of Israel come out? after whom dost thou pursue? after a dead dog<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, after a flea<small><small>[^33]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Sam.24.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> therefore be judge<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, and judge between me and thee, and see, and plead<small><small>[^35]</small></small> my cause, and deliver<small><small>[^36]</small></small> me out of thine hand.

[^9]: Caves in the rocks, in which it is still common for shepherds and their flocks to lodge. Dr. Pococke observes, "Beyond the valley [of Tekoa], there is a very large grotto, which the Arabs call El-Maamah, a hiding place. The high rocks on each side of the valley are almost perpendicular; and the way to the grotto is by a terrace formed in the rock, which is very narrow. There are two entrances into it; we went by the farthest, which leads by a narrow passage into a very large grotto, the rock being supported by natural pillars; the top of it rises in several places like domes; the grotto is perfectly dry. There is a tradition, that the people of the country, to the number of 30,000, retired into this grotto, to avoid a bad air. This place is so strong, that one would imagine it to be one of the strong holds of En-gedi, to which David and his men fled from Saul, and possibly it may be that very cave in which he cut off Saul's skirt; for David and his men might, with good ease, lie hid there and not be seen by him." Travels, vol. ii. P. 1. p. 41.
