***Jonah Flees from the LORD***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Nah.1|Nahum 1:1–15]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb2015

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto Jonah<small><small>[^2]</small></small> the son of Amittai, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Arise, go to Nineveh<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, that great city, and cry<small><small>[^4]</small></small> against it; for<small><small>[^5]</small></small> their wickedness is come up before me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But Jonah rose up to flee<small><small>[^6]</small></small> unto Tarshish from<small><small>[^7]</small></small> the presence of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and went down to Joppa<small><small>[^8]</small></small>; and he found a ship going to Tarshish<small><small>[^9]</small></small>: so he paid the fare thereof, and went down into it, to go with them unto Tarshish from the presence of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.

***The Great Storm***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Acts.27|Acts 27:13–26]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb2016

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^10]</small></small></span> sent out<small><small>[^11]</small></small> a great wind into the sea, and there was a mighty tempest in the sea, so that the ship was like<small><small>[^12]</small></small> to be broken. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the mariners were afraid, and cried<small><small>[^13]</small></small> every man unto his god, and cast<small><small>[^14]</small></small> forth the wares that \[were\] in the ship into the sea, to lighten \[it\] of them. But Jonah was gone down into the sides<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of the ship; and he lay, and was<small><small>[^16]</small></small> fast asleep. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So the shipmaster came to him, and said unto him, What<small><small>[^17]</small></small> meanest thou, O sleeper? arise<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, call upon thy God, if<small><small>[^19]</small></small> so be that God will think upon us, that we perish not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they said every<small><small>[^20]</small></small> one to his fellow, Come, and let<small><small>[^21]</small></small> us cast lots, that we may know for<small><small>[^22]</small></small> whose cause this evil \[is\] upon us. So they cast lots, and the<small><small>[^23]</small></small> lot fell upon Jonah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said they unto him, Tell<small><small>[^24]</small></small> us, we pray thee, for whose cause this evil \[is\] upon us; What \[is\] thine<small><small>[^25]</small></small> occupation? and whence comest thou? what \[is\] thy country? and of what people \[art\] thou? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said unto them, I \[am<small><small>[^26]</small></small>\] an Hebrew; and I<small><small>[^27]</small></small> fear the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^28]</small></small></span>, the God<small><small>[^29]</small></small> of heaven, which<small><small>[^30]</small></small> hath made the sea and the dry \[land\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then were<small><small>[^31]</small></small> the men exceedingly afraid<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, and said unto him, Why<small><small>[^33]</small></small> hast thou done this? For the men knew that he fled<small><small>[^34]</small></small> from the presence of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, because he had told them.

***Jonah Cast into the Sea*** ^bsb2017

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said they unto him, What<small><small>[^35]</small></small> shall we do unto thee, that the sea may be calm unto us<small><small>[^36]</small></small>? for the sea wrought, and was tempestuous. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said unto them, Take<small><small>[^37]</small></small> me up, and cast me for<small><small>[^38]</small></small>th into the sea; so shall the sea be calm unto you: for I know that for my sake this great tempest \[is\] upon you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nevertheless the<small><small>[^39]</small></small> men rowed<small><small>[^40]</small></small> hard to bring \[it\] to the land; but<small><small>[^41]</small></small> they could not: for the sea wrought, and was tempestuous against them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore they<small><small>[^42]</small></small> cried unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and said, We beseech thee, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, we beseech thee, let<small><small>[^43]</small></small> us not perish for<small><small>[^44]</small></small> this man’s life, and lay not upon us innocent blood: for thou, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, hast done as it pleased thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So they<small><small>[^45]</small></small> took up Jonah, and cast him forth into the sea: and the<small><small>[^46]</small></small> sea ceased<small><small>[^47]</small></small> from her raging. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Jonah.1.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the men feared<small><small>[^48]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> exceedingly, and offered<small><small>[^49]</small></small> a sacrifice unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and made<small><small>[^50]</small></small> vows.
