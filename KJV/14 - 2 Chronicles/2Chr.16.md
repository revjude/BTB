<big><big>[[2Chr.16.1 War between Asa and Baasha|⥃]]</big></big>***War between Asa and Baasha***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Kgs.15|1 Kings 15:16–22]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the six<small><small>[^2]</small></small> and thirtieth year of the reign of Asa Baasha king of Israel came up against Judah, and built Ramah, to the intent<small><small>[^3]</small></small> that he might let none go out or come in to Asa king of Judah.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Asa brought out<small><small>[^5]</small></small> silver and gold out of the treasures of the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> and of the king’s house, and sent to Ben–hadad king of Syria, that dwelt at Damascus<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] a league<small><small>[^7]</small></small> between me and thee, as \[there was\] between my father and thy father: behold, I have sent thee silver and gold; go, break<small><small>[^8]</small></small> thy league with Baasha king of Israel, that he may depart from me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Ben–hadad hearkened<small><small>[^9]</small></small> unto king Asa, and sent the captains of his armies<small><small>[^10]</small></small> against the cities of Israel; and they smote Ijon<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, and Dan<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, and Abel–maim<small><small>[^13]</small></small>, and all the store cities<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of Naphtali. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, when Baasha heard \[it\], that he left off<small><small>[^15]</small></small> building of Ramah, and let his work cease. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Asa the king took all Judah; and they carried<small><small>[^16]</small></small> away the stones of Ramah, and the timber thereof, wherewith Baasha was building; and he built therewith Geba<small><small>[^17]</small></small> and Mizpah<small><small>[^18]</small></small>.

<big><big>[[2Chr.16.7 Hanani’s Message to Asa|⥃]]</big></big>***Hanani’s Message to Asa***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And at that time Hanani<small><small>[^19]</small></small> the seer came to Asa king of Judah, and said unto him, Because<small><small>[^20]</small></small> thou hast relied on<small><small>[^21]</small></small> the king of Syria, and not relied on the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, therefore is the host<small><small>[^22]</small></small> of the king of Syria escaped out of thine hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Were not the Ethiopians<small><small>[^23]</small></small> and the Lubims<small><small>[^24]</small></small> a huge host<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, with very many chariots and horsemen? yet, because<small><small>[^26]</small></small> thou didst rely on the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, he delivered them into thine hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the eyes<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> run to and fro throughout the whole earth, to shew himself<small><small>[^28]</small></small> strong in the behalf of \[them\] whose heart \[is\] perfect toward him. Herein<small><small>[^29]</small></small> thou hast done foolishly: therefore from henceforth<small><small>[^30]</small></small> thou shalt have wars. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Asa was wroth<small><small>[^31]</small></small> with the seer, and put him<small><small>[^32]</small></small> in a prison house; for \[he was\] in a rage with him because of this \[thing\]. And Asa oppressed<small><small>[^33]</small></small> \[some\] of the people the same time<small><small>[^34]</small></small>.

<big><big>[[2Chr.16.11 The Death and Burial of Asa|⥃]]</big></big>***The Death and Burial of Asa***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And, behold, the acts of Asa<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, first and last, lo, they \[are\] written in the book of the kings of Judah<small><small>[^36]</small></small> and Israel.<small><small>[^37]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Asa in the thirty and ninth year of his reign was diseased<small><small>[^38]</small></small> in his feet, until his disease \[was\] exceeding \[great\]: yet in his disease<small><small>[^39]</small></small> he sought not to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, but to the physicians<small><small>[^40]</small></small>.<small><small>[^41]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And Asa slept<small><small>[^42]</small></small> with his fathers, and died in the one and fortieth year of his reign.<small><small>[^43]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.16.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they buried him in his own sepulchres<small><small>[^44]</small></small>, which he had made<small><small>[^45]</small></small> for himself in the city of David, and laid him in the bed which was filled with sweet odours<small><small>[^46]</small></small> and divers kinds \[of spices\] prepared by the apothecaries’ art<small><small>[^47]</small></small>: and they made a very great<small><small>[^48]</small></small> burning for him. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[2Chr.15|<< 2 Chronicles 15]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[2Chr.17|2 Chronicles 17 >>]]</td></tr></table>
