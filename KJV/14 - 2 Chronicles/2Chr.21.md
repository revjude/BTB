***Jehoram Reigns in Judah***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[2Kgs.8|2 Kings 8:16–19]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1114

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now Jehoshaphat<small><small>[^2]</small></small> slept with his fathers, and was buried<small><small>[^3]</small></small> with his fathers in the city of David. And Jehoram<small><small>[^4]</small></small> his son reigned<small><small>[^5]</small></small> in his stead.<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he had brethren the sons of Jehoshaphat, Azariah, and Jehiel, and Zechariah, and Azariah, and Michael, and Shephatiah: all these \[were\] the sons of Jehoshaphat king of Israel<small><small>[^7]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And their father gave them<small><small>[^8]</small></small> great gifts of silver, and of gold, and of precious things, with fenced cities in Judah: but the kingdom<small><small>[^9]</small></small> gave he to Jehoram<small><small>[^10]</small></small>; because he \[was\] the firstborn. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now when Jehoram was risen up to the kingdom of his father, he strengthened himself, and slew all<small><small>[^11]</small></small> his brethren with the sword, and \[divers\] also of the princes of Israel. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Jehoram<small><small>[^12]</small></small> \[was\] thirty and two years old when he began to reign, and he reigned eight years in Jerusalem.<small><small>[^13]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he walked in the way<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of the kings of Israel, like as did the house of Ahab: for he had<small><small>[^15]</small></small> the daughter of Ahab to wife: and he wrought \[that which was\] evil in the eyes of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Howbeit<small><small>[^16]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> would not destroy the house of David, because<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of the covenant that he had made with David, and as he promised<small><small>[^18]</small></small> to give a light<small><small>[^19]</small></small> to him and to his sons for ever.

***Edom and Libnah Rebel***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[2Kgs.8|2 Kings 8:20–24]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1115

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]In his days the Edomites<small><small>[^20]</small></small> revolted from under the dominion<small><small>[^21]</small></small> of Judah, and made<small><small>[^22]</small></small> themselves a king.<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Jehoram went forth with his princes, and all his chariots with him: and he rose up by night, and smote the Edomites which compassed him in, and the captains of the chariots. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So the Edomites revolted from under the hand of Judah unto this day. The same time \[also\] did Libnah<small><small>[^24]</small></small> revolt from under his hand; because<small><small>[^25]</small></small> he had forsaken the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of his fathers. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover<small><small>[^26]</small></small> he made high places in the mountains of Judah, and caused<small><small>[^27]</small></small> the inhabitants of Jerusalem to commit fornication<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, and compelled<small><small>[^29]</small></small> Judah \[thereto\].

***Elijah’s Letter to Jehoram*** ^bsb1116

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there came a writing<small><small>[^30]</small></small> to him from Elijah the prophet<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, saying, Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of David thy father, Because thou hast not walked in the ways of Jehoshaphat<small><small>[^32]</small></small> thy father, nor in the ways of Asa<small><small>[^33]</small></small> king of Judah,<small><small>[^34]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But hast walked in the way<small><small>[^35]</small></small> of the kings of Israel, and hast made Judah and the inhabitants of Jerusalem to go a whoring<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, like to the whoredoms of the house of Ahab, and also hast slain<small><small>[^37]</small></small> thy brethren of thy father’s house, \[which were\] better than thyself: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, with a great plague<small><small>[^38]</small></small> will the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> smite thy people<small><small>[^39]</small></small>, and thy children<small><small>[^40]</small></small>, and thy wives, and all thy goods: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou \[shalt have\] great sickness by disease<small><small>[^41]</small></small> of thy bowels, until thy bowels fall<small><small>[^42]</small></small> out by reason of the sickness<small><small>[^43]</small></small> day by day.

***Jehoram’s Disease and Death*** ^bsb1117

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Moreover the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> stirred up against Jehoram the spirit of the Philistines<small><small>[^44]</small></small>, and of the Arabians, that \[were\] near the Ethiopians:<small><small>[^45]</small></small><small><small>[^46]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they came up into Judah, and brake into it, and carried away<small><small>[^47]</small></small> all the substance that was found in the king’s house, and his sons also<small><small>[^48]</small></small>, and his wives; so that there was never a son left him, save Jehoahaz<small><small>[^49]</small></small>, the youngest of his sons. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And after all<small><small>[^50]</small></small> this the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> smote him in his bowels with an incurable disease<small><small>[^51]</small></small>.<small><small>[^52]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, that in process of time, after the end of two years, his bowels fell out by reason of his sickness: so he died of sore diseases. And his people made no<small><small>[^53]</small></small> burning for him, like the burning of his fathers. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.21.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thirty and two<small><small>[^54]</small></small> years old was he when he began to reign, and he reigned in Jerusalem eight years, and departed without being desired<small><small>[^55]</small></small>.
