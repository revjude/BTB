<big><big>[[2Chr.10.1 Rebellion against Rehoboam|⥃]]</big></big>***Rebellion against Rehoboam***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Kgs.12|1 Kings 12:1–15]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Rehoboam<small><small>[^2]</small></small> went to Shechem<small><small>[^3]</small></small>: for to Shechem were all<small><small>[^4]</small></small> Israel come to make him king. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, when Jeroboam<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the son of Nebat, who \[was\] in Egypt, whither he had fled from the presence of Solomon the king, heard \[it\], that Jeroboam returned out of Egypt. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they sent<small><small>[^6]</small></small> and called him. So Jeroboam and all Israel came and spake to Rehoboam, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy father<small><small>[^7]</small></small> made our yoke grievous<small><small>[^8]</small></small>: now therefore ease thou somewhat the grievous servitude of thy father, and his heavy yoke that he put upon us, and we will serve thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said unto them, Come again<small><small>[^9]</small></small> unto me after three days. And the people departed. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And king Rehoboam took counsel<small><small>[^10]</small></small> with the old men that had stood before Solomon his father while he yet lived, saying, What counsel<small><small>[^11]</small></small> give ye \[me\] to return answer to this people? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they spake unto him, saying, If thou be kind<small><small>[^12]</small></small> to this people, and please them, and speak good<small><small>[^13]</small></small> words to them, they will be thy servants for ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But he forsook<small><small>[^14]</small></small> the counsel which the old men gave him, and took counsel with the young men<small><small>[^15]</small></small> that were brought up with him, that stood before him.<small><small>[^16]</small></small><small><small>[^17]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said unto them, What advice<small><small>[^18]</small></small> give ye that we may return answer to this people, which have spoken to me, saying, Ease<small><small>[^19]</small></small> somewhat the yoke that thy father did put upon us? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the young men that were brought up with him spake unto him, saying, Thus shalt<small><small>[^20]</small></small> thou answer the people that spake unto thee, saying, Thy father made our yoke heavy, but make thou \[it\] somewhat lighter for us; thus shalt thou say unto them, My little \[finger<small><small>[^21]</small></small>\] shall be thicker than my father’s loins. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For whereas my father<small><small>[^22]</small></small> put<small><small>[^23]</small></small> a heavy yoke upon you, I will put<small><small>[^24]</small></small> more to your yoke: my father chastised you with whips, but I \[will chastise you\] with scorpions<small><small>[^25]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So Jeroboam and all the people came to Rehoboam on the third day, as the king bade, saying, Come<small><small>[^26]</small></small> again to me on the third day. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king answered<small><small>[^27]</small></small> them roughly; and king Rehoboam forsook<small><small>[^28]</small></small> the counsel of the old men, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And answered them after the advice<small><small>[^29]</small></small> of the young men, saying, My father<small><small>[^30]</small></small> made your yoke heavy, but I will add thereto: my father chastised you with whips, but I \[will chastise you\] with scorpions. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So the king<small><small>[^31]</small></small> hearkened not unto the people: for the cause<small><small>[^32]</small></small> was of God, that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^33]</small></small></span> might perform his word, which he spake by the hand of Ahijah<small><small>[^34]</small></small> the Shilonite to Jeroboam the son of Nebat.

<big><big>[[2Chr.10.16 The Kingdom Divided|⥃]]</big></big>***The Kingdom Divided***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Kgs.12|1 Kings 12:16–19]] ]</sup></small>

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And when all Israel \[saw\] that the king would not hearken unto them, the people answered the king, saying, What portion<small><small>[^35]</small></small> have we in David? and \[we have\] none inheritance in the son<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of Jesse: every man to your tents, O Israel: \[and\] now, David<small><small>[^37]</small></small>, see to thine own house. So all Israel<small><small>[^38]</small></small> went to their tents. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But \[as for<small><small>[^39]</small></small>\] the children of Israel that dwelt in the cities of Judah, Rehoboam reigned over them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then king Rehoboam sent Hadoram<small><small>[^40]</small></small> that \[was\] over the tribute; and the children of Israel stoned him<small><small>[^41]</small></small> with stones, that he died. But king Rehoboam made speed<small><small>[^42]</small></small> to get him up to \[his\] chariot, to flee to Jerusalem. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.10.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Israel<small><small>[^43]</small></small> rebelled against the house of David unto this day<small><small>[^44]</small></small>. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[2Chr.9|<< 2 Chronicles 9]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[2Chr.11|2 Chronicles 11 >>]]</td></tr></table>
