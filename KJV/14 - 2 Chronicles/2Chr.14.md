***Asa Reigns in Judah***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Kgs.15|1 Kings 15:9–15]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1095

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So Abijah slept<small><small>[^2]</small></small> with his fathers, and they buried him in the city of David: and Asa<small><small>[^3]</small></small> his son reigned in his stead. In his days the land was quiet ten years.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Asa did \[that which was\] good and right<small><small>[^5]</small></small> in the eyes of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> his God:<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he took<small><small>[^7]</small></small> away the altars of the strange \[gods\], and the high places<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, and brake<small><small>[^9]</small></small> down the images<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, and cut down<small><small>[^11]</small></small> the groves: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And commanded<small><small>[^12]</small></small> Judah to seek<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of their fathers, and to do<small><small>[^14]</small></small> the law and the commandment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also he took away out of all the cities of Judah the high places and the images<small><small>[^15]</small></small>: and the kingdom was quiet before him. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And he built<small><small>[^16]</small></small> fenced cities in Judah: for the land<small><small>[^17]</small></small> had rest, and he had no war in those years; because the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^18]</small></small></span> had given him rest. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore<small><small>[^19]</small></small> he said unto Judah, Let us build these cities, and make about \[them\] walls, and towers, gates, and bars, \[while\] the land<small><small>[^20]</small></small> \[is\] yet before us; because we have sought<small><small>[^21]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> our God, we have sought \[him\], and he hath given<small><small>[^22]</small></small> us rest on every side. So they built and prospered. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Asa had an army \[of men\] that bare targets and spears, out of Judah<small><small>[^23]</small></small> three hundred thousand; and out of Benjamin, that bare shields and drew bows, two hundred and fourscore thousand: all these \[were\] mighty men of valour. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there came out against them Zerah<small><small>[^24]</small></small> the Ethiopian with an host of a thousand thousand, and three hundred chariots; and came unto Mareshah<small><small>[^25]</small></small>.<small><small>[^26]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Asa went out against him, and they set the battle in array in the valley of Zephathah<small><small>[^27]</small></small> at Mareshah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Asa cried unto<small><small>[^28]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> his God, and said, <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[it is\] nothing<small><small>[^29]</small></small> with thee to help, whether with many, or with them that<small><small>[^30]</small></small> have no power: help us, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> our God; for we rest on thee<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, and in thy name<small><small>[^32]</small></small> we go against this multitude. O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, thou \[art\] our God; let not man<small><small>[^33]</small></small> prevail against thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> smote the Ethiopians before Asa, and before Judah; and the Ethiopians fled.<small><small>[^34]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Asa and the people that \[were\] with him pursued them unto Gerar<small><small>[^35]</small></small>: and the Ethiopians were overthrown, that they could not recover themselves; for they were destroyed<small><small>[^36]</small></small> before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^37]</small></small></span>, and before his host<small><small>[^38]</small></small>; and they carried away very much spoil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they smote all the cities round about Gerar; for the fear<small><small>[^39]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came upon them: and they spoiled all the cities; for there was exceeding<small><small>[^40]</small></small> much spoil in them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.14.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They smote also the tents of cattle<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, and carried away<small><small>[^42]</small></small> sheep and camels in abundance, and returned to Jerusalem. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[2Chr.13|<< 2 Chronicles 13]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[2Chr.15|2 Chronicles 15 >>]]</td></tr></table>
