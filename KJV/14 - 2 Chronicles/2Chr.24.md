<big><big>[[2Chr.24.1 Joash Repairs the Temple|⥃]]</big></big>***Joash Repairs the Temple***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[2Kgs.12|2 Kings 12:1–16]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Joash<small><small>[^2]</small></small> \[was\] seven years old<small><small>[^3]</small></small> when he began to reign, and he reigned forty years in Jerusalem. His mother’s name also \[was\] Zibiah of Beer–sheba.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Joash<small><small>[^5]</small></small> did \[that which was\] right in the sight of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> all the days of Jehoiada<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the priest.<small><small>[^7]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jehoiada took for him<small><small>[^8]</small></small> two wives<small><small>[^9]</small></small>; and he begat sons and daughters. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And it came to pass after this, \[that\] Joash was minded to repair<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^11]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he gathered together the priests and the Levites, and said to them, Go out unto the cities of Judah, and gather of all Israel<small><small>[^12]</small></small> money to repair the house of your God from year to year, and see that ye hasten the matter. Howbeit<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the Levites hastened \[it\] not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king called for Jehoiada the chief, and said unto him, Why hast thou<small><small>[^14]</small></small> not required of the Levites to bring in out of Judah and out of Jerusalem the collection<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, \[according to the commandment\] of Moses the servant of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and of the congregation of Israel, for the tabernacle<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of witness? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the sons of Athaliah<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, that wicked<small><small>[^18]</small></small> woman, had broken up the house of God; and also all the dedicated<small><small>[^19]</small></small> things of the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> did they bestow<small><small>[^20]</small></small> upon Baalim. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And at the king’s<small><small>[^21]</small></small> commandment they made a chest, and set it without at the gate of the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they made a proclamation<small><small>[^22]</small></small> through Judah and Jerusalem, to bring in to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> the collection<small><small>[^23]</small></small> \[that\] Moses the servant of God \[laid\] upon Israel in the wilderness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all the princes and all the people rejoiced<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, and brought in, and cast into the chest, until they had made an end. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now it came to pass, that at what time<small><small>[^25]</small></small> the chest was brought unto the king’s office by the hand of the Levites, and when they saw that \[there was\] much money, the king’s scribe<small><small>[^26]</small></small> and the high priest’s officer came and emptied the chest, and took it, and carried it to his place again. Thus they did<small><small>[^27]</small></small> day by day, and gathered money in abundance. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king and Jehoiada gave it to such<small><small>[^28]</small></small> as did the work of the service of the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and hired masons<small><small>[^29]</small></small> and carpenters to repair the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and also such as wrought iron and brass to mend the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So the workmen wrought, and the work was perfected by them<small><small>[^30]</small></small>, and they set<small><small>[^31]</small></small> the house of God in his state<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, and strengthened it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when they had finished \[it\], they brought the rest of the money before the king and Jehoiada, whereof were made vessels for the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[even\] vessels to minister<small><small>[^33]</small></small>, and to offer \[withal<small><small>[^34]</small></small>\], and spoons, and vessels of gold and silver. And they offered<small><small>[^35]</small></small> burnt offerings in the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> continually all the days<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of Jehoiada.<small><small>[^37]</small></small>

<big><big>[[2Chr.24.15 Jehoiada’s Death and Burial|⥃]]</big></big>***Jehoiada’s Death and Burial***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But Jehoiada waxed old, and was full of days<small><small>[^38]</small></small> when he died; an hundred<small><small>[^39]</small></small> and thirty years old \[was he\] when he died.<small><small>[^40]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they buried him in the city<small><small>[^41]</small></small> of David among the kings, because<small><small>[^42]</small></small> he had done good in Israel, both toward God, and toward his house.

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now after<small><small>[^43]</small></small> the death of Jehoiada came the princes of Judah<small><small>[^44]</small></small>, and made obeisance to the king. Then the king<small><small>[^45]</small></small> hearkened unto them.<small><small>[^46]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they left<small><small>[^47]</small></small> the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of their fathers, and served groves and idols: and wrath<small><small>[^48]</small></small> came upon Judah and Jerusalem for this their trespass. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet he sent<small><small>[^49]</small></small> prophets to them, to bring them again unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and they testified against them: but they would<small><small>[^50]</small></small> not give ear. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the Spirit<small><small>[^51]</small></small> of God came upon<small><small>[^52]</small></small> Zechariah the son<small><small>[^53]</small></small> of Jehoiada the priest, which stood above the people, and said unto them, Thus saith God, Why transgress<small><small>[^54]</small></small> ye the commandments of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that ye cannot prosper? because<small><small>[^55]</small></small> ye have forsaken the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, he hath also forsaken you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they conspired<small><small>[^56]</small></small> against him, and stoned him<small><small>[^57]</small></small> with stones at the commandment of the king in the court of the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus Joash the king remembered<small><small>[^58]</small></small> not the kindness which Jehoiada his father had done to him, but slew his son<small><small>[^59]</small></small>. And when he died, he said, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^60]</small></small></span> look upon \[it\], and require \[it<small><small>[^61]</small></small>\].

So<small><small>[^69]</small></small> they executed judgment against Joash. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when they were departed from him, (for they left him in great diseases<small><small>[^70]</small></small>,) his own servants<small><small>[^71]</small></small> conspired against him for the blood<small><small>[^72]</small></small> of the sons of Jehoiada<small><small>[^73]</small></small> the priest, and slew him on his bed, and he died: and they buried him in the city of David, but they buried him not<small><small>[^74]</small></small> in the sepulchres of the kings. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.24.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And these are they that conspired against him; Zabad<small><small>[^75]</small></small> the son of Shimeath an Ammonitess, and Jehozabad the son of Shimrith<small><small>[^76]</small></small> a Moabitess.
