<big><big>[[2Chr.20.1 War against Jehoshaphat|⥃]]</big></big>***War against Jehoshaphat***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> It came to pass after this also<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, \[that\] the children of Moab<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, and the children of Ammon, and with them \[other\] beside the Ammonites, came against<small><small>[^4]</small></small> Jehoshaphat to battle. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then there came some that told Jehoshaphat, saying, There cometh a great multitude against thee from beyond the sea<small><small>[^5]</small></small> on this side Syria; and, behold, they \[be\] in Hazazon–tamar<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, which \[is\] En–gedi<small><small>[^7]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jehoshaphat feared<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, and set himself<small><small>[^9]</small></small> to seek the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^10]</small></small></span>, and proclaimed<small><small>[^11]</small></small> a fast throughout all Judah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Judah gathered themselves together, to ask \[help\] of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^12]</small></small></span>: even out of all the cities<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of Judah they came to seek the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.

<big><big>[[2Chr.20.5 Jehoshaphat’s Prayer|⥃]]</big></big>***Jehoshaphat’s Prayer***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And Jehoshaphat<small><small>[^14]</small></small> stood in the congregation of Judah and Jerusalem, in the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, before the new court, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And said, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^15]</small></small></span> God of our fathers, \[art\] not thou God in heaven<small><small>[^16]</small></small>? and rulest \[not<small><small>[^17]</small></small>\] thou over all the kingdoms of the heathen? and in thine hand<small><small>[^18]</small></small> \[is there not\] power and might, so that none is able<small><small>[^19]</small></small> to withstand thee? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Art\] not thou our God<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, \[who<small><small>[^21]</small></small>\] didst drive out<small><small>[^22]</small></small> the inhabitants of this land before thy people Israel, and gavest<small><small>[^23]</small></small> it to the seed of Abraham thy friend<small><small>[^24]</small></small> for ever? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they dwelt therein, and have built thee<small><small>[^25]</small></small> a sanctuary therein for thy name, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If, \[when\] evil<small><small>[^26]</small></small> cometh upon us, \[as\] the sword, judgment, or pestilence, or famine, we stand before this house, and in thy presence<small><small>[^27]</small></small>, (for thy name<small><small>[^28]</small></small> \[is\] in this house<small><small>[^29]</small></small>,) and cry unto thee in our affliction, then thou wilt hear and help. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And now, behold, the children of Ammon and Moab and mount Seir, whom thou<small><small>[^30]</small></small> wouldest not let Israel invade, when they came out of the land of Egypt, but they turned from them, and destroyed them not; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, \[I say\], \[how\] they reward us<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, to come to cast us<small><small>[^32]</small></small> out of thy possession, which thou hast given us to inherit. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O our God, wilt<small><small>[^33]</small></small> thou not judge them? for we have<small><small>[^34]</small></small> no might against this great company that cometh against us; neither<small><small>[^35]</small></small> know we what to do: but our eyes<small><small>[^36]</small></small> \[are\] upon thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all Judah<small><small>[^37]</small></small> stood before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, with their little ones, their wives, and their children.

<big><big>[[2Chr.20.14 The Prophecy of Jahaziel|⥃]]</big></big>***The Prophecy of Jahaziel***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then upon<small><small>[^38]</small></small> Jahaziel the son of Zechariah, the son of Benaiah, the son of Jeiel, the son of Mattaniah, a Levite of the sons of Asaph, came the Spirit<small><small>[^39]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> in the midst of the congregation; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, Hearken ye, all Judah, and ye inhabitants of Jerusalem, and thou king Jehoshaphat, Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> unto you, Be not afraid<small><small>[^40]</small></small> nor dismayed by reason of this great multitude; for the battle<small><small>[^41]</small></small> \[is\] not yours, but God’s. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To morrow go ye down against them: behold, they come up by the cliff<small><small>[^42]</small></small> of Ziz<small><small>[^43]</small></small>; and ye shall find them at the end of the brook<small><small>[^44]</small></small>, before the wilderness<small><small>[^45]</small></small> of Jeruel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ye shall not \[need<small><small>[^46]</small></small>\] to fight in this \[battle\]: set yourselves, stand ye \[still<small><small>[^47]</small></small>\], and see the salvation of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> with you, O Judah and Jerusalem: fear not, nor be dismayed; to morrow go out against them: for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^48]</small></small></span> \[will be\] with you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jehoshaphat bowed his head<small><small>[^49]</small></small> with \[his\] face to the ground: and all Judah and the inhabitants of Jerusalem fell before<small><small>[^50]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, worshipping the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the Levites<small><small>[^51]</small></small>, of the children of the Kohathites, and of the children of the Korhites<small><small>[^52]</small></small>, stood up to praise the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of Israel with a loud<small><small>[^53]</small></small> voice on high.

<big><big>[[2Chr.20.20 The Enemies Destroy Themselves|⥃]]</big></big>***The Enemies Destroy Themselves***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they rose early in the morning, and went forth into the wilderness of Tekoa<small><small>[^54]</small></small>: and as they went forth, Jehoshaphat stood and said, Hear me<small><small>[^55]</small></small>, O Judah, and ye inhabitants of Jerusalem; Believe in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^56]</small></small></span> your God, so shall ye be established; believe his<small><small>[^57]</small></small> prophets, so shall ye prosper. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when he had consulted<small><small>[^58]</small></small> with the people, he appointed<small><small>[^59]</small></small> singers unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and that should praise<small><small>[^60]</small></small> the beauty<small><small>[^61]</small></small> of holiness, as they went out before the army, and to say, Praise the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^62]</small></small></span>; for his mercy \[endureth\] for ever. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And when they<small><small>[^63]</small></small> began to sing and to<small><small>[^64]</small></small> praise, the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> set ambushments against the children of Ammon, Moab, and mount Seir, which were come against Judah; and they were smitten<small><small>[^65]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the children of Ammon and Moab stood up against the inhabitants of mount Seir<small><small>[^66]</small></small>, utterly to slay and destroy \[them\]: and when they had made an end of the inhabitants of Seir, every one helped to destroy another<small><small>[^67]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when Judah came toward the watch tower in the wilderness, they looked unto the multitude, and, behold, they \[were\] dead<small><small>[^68]</small></small> bodies fallen to the earth, and none escaped<small><small>[^69]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when Jehoshaphat and his people came to take away the spoil of them, they found<small><small>[^70]</small></small> among them in abundance both riches with the dead bodies<small><small>[^71]</small></small>, and precious jewels<small><small>[^72]</small></small>, which they stripped off for themselves, more than they could carry away: and they were three days in gathering of the spoil, it was so much<small><small>[^73]</small></small>.

<big><big>[[2Chr.20.26 The Joyful Return|⥃]]</big></big>***The Joyful Return***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And on the fourth day they assembled themselves in the valley of Berachah<small><small>[^74]</small></small>; for there they blessed<small><small>[^75]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: therefore the name<small><small>[^76]</small></small> of the same place was called, The valley of Berachah, unto this day<small><small>[^77]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then they returned, every man of Judah and Jerusalem, and Jehoshaphat in the forefront<small><small>[^78]</small></small> of them, to go again to Jerusalem with joy; for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^79]</small></small></span> had made them to rejoice over their enemies. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they came to Jerusalem with psalteries<small><small>[^80]</small></small> and harps and trumpets unto the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the fear<small><small>[^81]</small></small> of God was on all the kingdoms of \[those\] countries, when they had heard<small><small>[^82]</small></small> that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> fought against the enemies of Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So the realm of Jehoshaphat was quiet: for his God<small><small>[^83]</small></small> gave him rest round about.

And his mother’s name \[was\] Azubah the daughter of Shilhi.<small><small>[^85]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he walked<small><small>[^86]</small></small> in the way<small><small>[^87]</small></small> of Asa his father, and departed not<small><small>[^88]</small></small> from it, doing \[that which was\] right in the sight of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Howbeit the high places<small><small>[^89]</small></small> were not taken away: for as yet the people had not<small><small>[^90]</small></small> prepared their hearts unto the God of their fathers. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.20.34|34]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now the rest<small><small>[^91]</small></small> of the acts of Jehoshaphat, first and last, behold, they \[are\] written in the book<small><small>[^92]</small></small> of Jehu the son of Hanani<small><small>[^93]</small></small>, who \[is\] mentioned<small><small>[^94]</small></small> in the book of the kings of Israel.
