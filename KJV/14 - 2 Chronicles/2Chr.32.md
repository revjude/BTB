 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> After these things<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, and the establishment thereof, Sennacherib king of Assyria<small><small>[^3]</small></small> came, and entered into Judah, and encamped against the fenced cities, and thought to win them<small><small>[^4]</small></small> for himself. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when Hezekiah saw that Sennacherib was come, and that he was purposed to fight<small><small>[^5]</small></small> against Jerusalem, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He took counsel<small><small>[^6]</small></small> with his princes and his mighty men to stop<small><small>[^7]</small></small> the waters of the fountains which \[were\] without the city: and they did help him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So there was gathered much people together, who stopped<small><small>[^8]</small></small> all the fountains, and the brook<small><small>[^9]</small></small> that ran through the midst of<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the land, saying, Why should the kings<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of Assyria come, and find<small><small>[^12]</small></small> much water? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also he strengthened<small><small>[^13]</small></small> himself, and built up all the wall that was broken<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, and raised \[it\] up to the towers, and another wall<small><small>[^15]</small></small> without, and repaired Millo<small><small>[^16]</small></small> \[in\] the city of David, and made darts<small><small>[^17]</small></small> and shields in abundance. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he set captains<small><small>[^18]</small></small> of war over the people, and gathered them together to him in the street<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of the gate of the city, and spake comfortably to them<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be strong<small><small>[^21]</small></small> and courageous, be not afraid<small><small>[^22]</small></small> nor dismayed for the king of Assyria, nor for all the multitude that \[is\] with him: for \[there<small><small>[^23]</small></small> be\] more with us than with him: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> With him \[is\] an arm<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of flesh; but with us<small><small>[^25]</small></small> \[is\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> our God to help us, and to fight<small><small>[^26]</small></small> our battles. And the people rested<small><small>[^27]</small></small> themselves upon the words<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of Hezekiah king of Judah.

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> After this did Sennacherib<small><small>[^29]</small></small> king of Assyria send his servants to Jerusalem, (but he \[himself laid siege\] against Lachish<small><small>[^30]</small></small>, and all his power<small><small>[^31]</small></small> with him,) unto Hezekiah king of Judah, and unto all Judah that \[were\] at Jerusalem, saying,<small><small>[^32]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith<small><small>[^33]</small></small> Sennacherib king of Assyria, Whereon do ye trust, that ye abide in the siege<small><small>[^34]</small></small> in Jerusalem? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Know ye not what I and my<small><small>[^41]</small></small> fathers have done unto all the people of \[other\] lands? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And his servants spake yet<small><small>[^48]</small></small> \[more\] against<small><small>[^49]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God, and against his servant Hezekiah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He wrote<small><small>[^50]</small></small> also letters to rail<small><small>[^51]</small></small> on the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of Israel, and to speak against him, saying, As the gods<small><small>[^52]</small></small> of the nations of \[other\] lands have not delivered their people out of mine hand, so shall not the God of Hezekiah deliver his people out of mine hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then they cried<small><small>[^53]</small></small> with a loud voice in the Jews’ speech unto the people of Jerusalem that \[were\] on the wall, to affright<small><small>[^54]</small></small> them, and to trouble them; that they might take the city. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they spake<small><small>[^55]</small></small> against the God<small><small>[^56]</small></small> of Jerusalem, as against the gods of the people of the earth, \[which were\] the work<small><small>[^57]</small></small> of the hands of man.

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And for this \[cause\] Hezekiah<small><small>[^58]</small></small> the king, and the prophet<small><small>[^59]</small></small> Isaiah the son of Amoz, prayed<small><small>[^60]</small></small> and cried to heaven.

And Hezekiah<small><small>[^96]</small></small> prospered in all his works. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.32.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Howbeit in \[the business<small><small>[^97]</small></small> of\] the ambassadors<small><small>[^98]</small></small> of the princes of Babylon, who sent unto him to enquire of the wonder<small><small>[^99]</small></small> that was \[done\] in the land, God left him<small><small>[^100]</small></small>, to try him<small><small>[^101]</small></small>, that he might<small><small>[^102]</small></small> know all \[that was\] in his heart.<small><small>[^103]</small></small>
