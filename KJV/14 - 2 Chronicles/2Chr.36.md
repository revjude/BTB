***Jehoahaz Succeeds Josiah***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[2Kgs.23|2 Kings 23:31–35]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1162

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the people<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of the land took Jehoahaz<small><small>[^3]</small></small> the son of Josiah, and made him king in his father’s stead in Jerusalem. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Jehoahaz \[was\] twenty and three years old when he began to reign, and he reigned three months in Jerusalem. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king of Egypt put him down<small><small>[^4]</small></small> at Jerusalem, and condemned<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the land in an hundred talents of silver and a talent of gold. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king of Egypt made Eliakim<small><small>[^6]</small></small> his brother king over Judah and Jerusalem, and turned his name to Jehoiakim. And Necho<small><small>[^7]</small></small> took Jehoahaz his brother, and carried him to Egypt.

***Jehoiakim Reigns in Judah***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[2Kgs.23|2 Kings 23:36–37]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1163

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Jehoiakim<small><small>[^8]</small></small> \[was\] twenty and five years old when he began to reign, and he reigned eleven years in Jerusalem: and he did \[that which was\] evil in the sight of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> his God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Against him came up<small><small>[^9]</small></small> Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon, and bound him in fetters<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, to carry him to Babylon.<small><small>[^11]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nebuchadnezzar also carried of the vessels<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> to Babylon, and put them in his temple at Babylon.<small><small>[^13]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now the rest of the acts of Jehoiakim, and his abominations which he did, and that which was found in him, behold, they \[are\] written<small><small>[^14]</small></small> in the book of the kings of Israel and Judah: and Jehoiachin<small><small>[^15]</small></small> his son reigned in his stead.<small><small>[^16]</small></small>

***Jehoiachin Reigns in Judah***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[2Kgs.24|2 Kings 24:6–9]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1164

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Jehoiachin \[was\] eight years old<small><small>[^17]</small></small> when he began to reign, and he reigned three months and ten days in Jerusalem: and he did \[that which was\] evil in the sight of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^18]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when the year was expired<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, king Nebuchadnezzar sent, and brought him to Babylon, with the goodly vessels<small><small>[^20]</small></small> of the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and made Zedekiah<small><small>[^21]</small></small> his brother king over Judah and Jerusalem.

***Zedekiah Reigns in Judah***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[2Kgs.24|2 Kings 24:18–20]] <big>†</big> [[Jer.52|Jeremiah 52:1–3]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1165

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Zedekiah \[was\] one and twenty<small><small>[^22]</small></small> years old when he began to reign, and reigned eleven years in Jerusalem.<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he did \[that which was\] evil in the sight of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> his God, \[and\] humbled<small><small>[^24]</small></small> not himself before Jeremiah<small><small>[^25]</small></small> the prophet \[speaking\] from the mouth<small><small>[^26]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he also rebelled<small><small>[^27]</small></small> against king Nebuchadnezzar, who had<small><small>[^28]</small></small> made him swear by God: but he stiffened<small><small>[^29]</small></small> his neck, and hardened<small><small>[^30]</small></small> his heart from turning unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of Israel. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Moreover all the chief<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of the priests, and the people, transgressed very much<small><small>[^32]</small></small> after all the abominations of the heathen; and polluted<small><small>[^33]</small></small> the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> which he had hallowed in Jerusalem.

***The Fall of Jerusalem***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[2Kgs.25|2 Kings 25:1–7]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1166

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^34]</small></small></span> God of their fathers sent to them by his messengers<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, rising up betimes<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, and sending; because<small><small>[^37]</small></small> he had compassion on his people, and on his dwelling place: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But they mocked<small><small>[^38]</small></small> the messengers of God, and despised<small><small>[^39]</small></small> his words, and misused<small><small>[^40]</small></small> his prophets, until the wrath<small><small>[^41]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> arose against his people, till<small><small>[^42]</small></small> \[there was\] no remedy<small><small>[^43]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore he brought<small><small>[^44]</small></small> upon them the king<small><small>[^45]</small></small> of the Chaldees, who slew<small><small>[^46]</small></small> their young men with the sword in the house<small><small>[^47]</small></small> of their sanctuary, and had no compassion<small><small>[^48]</small></small> upon young man or maiden, old man, or him that stooped for age: he gave \[them\] all into his hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all the vessels<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of the house of God, great and small, and the treasures<small><small>[^50]</small></small> of the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and the treasures of the king, and of his princes; all \[these\] he brought to Babylon.<small><small>[^51]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they burnt<small><small>[^52]</small></small> the house of God, and brake down<small><small>[^53]</small></small> the wall of Jerusalem, and burnt all the palaces thereof with fire, and destroyed all the goodly vessels thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And them that had escaped from<small><small>[^54]</small></small> the sword carried he away to Babylon; where they were servants<small><small>[^55]</small></small> to him and his sons until the reign<small><small>[^56]</small></small> of the kingdom of Persia:<small><small>[^57]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To fulfil<small><small>[^58]</small></small> the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> by the mouth of Jeremiah, until the land<small><small>[^59]</small></small> had enjoyed her sabbaths: \[for\] as long as she lay desolate she kept sabbath, to fulfil threescore and ten years.

***The Proclamation of Cyrus***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Ezra.1|Ezra 1:1–4]] <big>†</big> [[Isa.45|Isaiah 45:1–25]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1167

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now in the first<small><small>[^60]</small></small> year of Cyrus<small><small>[^61]</small></small> king of Persia, that the word<small><small>[^62]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[spoken\] by the mouth of Jeremiah might be accomplished, the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> stirred<small><small>[^63]</small></small> up the spirit of Cyrus king of Persia, that he made a proclamation<small><small>[^64]</small></small> throughout all his kingdom, and \[put it\] also in writing, saying,<small><small>[^65]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.36.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith Cyrus king of Persia, All the kingdoms<small><small>[^66]</small></small> of the earth hath the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of heaven given me; and he hath charged<small><small>[^67]</small></small> me to build him an house in Jerusalem, which \[is\] in Judah. Who \[is there<small><small>[^68]</small></small>\] among you of all his people?
