<big><big>[[2Chr.18.1 Jehoshaphat Allies with Ahab|⥃]]</big></big>***Jehoshaphat Allies with Ahab***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Kgs.22|1 Kings 22:1–12]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now Jehoshaphat had riches<small><small>[^2]</small></small> and honour in abundance, and joined affinity<small><small>[^3]</small></small> with Ahab.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And after \[certain\] years<small><small>[^5]</small></small> he went<small><small>[^6]</small></small> down to Ahab<small><small>[^7]</small></small> to Samaria. And Ahab killed sheep and oxen for him in abundance, and for the people that \[he had\] with him, and persuaded<small><small>[^8]</small></small> him to go up \[with him\] to Ramoth–gilead<small><small>[^9]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Ahab king of Israel said unto Jehoshaphat king of Judah, Wilt thou go with me to Ramoth–gilead? And he answered him, I \[am\] as thou<small><small>[^10]</small></small> \[art\], and my people as thy people; and \[we will be\] with thee in the war. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jehoshaphat said unto the king of Israel, Enquire<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, I pray thee, at the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> to day. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore the king of Israel gathered together of prophets<small><small>[^12]</small></small> four hundred men, and said unto them, Shall we go<small><small>[^13]</small></small> to Ramoth–gilead to battle, or shall I forbear? And they said, Go up<small><small>[^14]</small></small>; for God will deliver \[it\] into the king’s hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But Jehoshaphat said, \[Is there\] not<small><small>[^15]</small></small> here a prophet of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> besides<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, that we might enquire of him? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king of Israel said unto Jehoshaphat, \[There is\] yet one man<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, by whom we may enquire of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: but I hate him<small><small>[^18]</small></small>; for he never prophesied good<small><small>[^19]</small></small> unto me<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, but always evil: the same \[is\] Micaiah the son of Imla. And Jehoshaphat said, Let not the<small><small>[^21]</small></small> king say so. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king of Israel called for one \[of his\] officers<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, and said, Fetch quickly<small><small>[^23]</small></small> Micaiah the son of Imla. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king of Israel and Jehoshaphat king of Judah sat either<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of them on his throne, clothed<small><small>[^25]</small></small> in \[their\] robes, and they sat in a void place<small><small>[^26]</small></small> at the entering in of the gate of Samaria; and all the prophets<small><small>[^27]</small></small> prophesied before them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Zedekiah the son of Chenaanah had made him horns of iron<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, and said, Thus<small><small>[^29]</small></small> saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, With these thou shalt push Syria until they be consumed<small><small>[^30]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all the prophets<small><small>[^31]</small></small> prophesied so, saying, Go up to Ramoth–gilead, and prosper: for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall deliver \[it\] into the hand of the king.

<big><big>[[2Chr.18.12 Micaiah Prophesies against Ahab|⥃]]</big></big>***Micaiah Prophesies against Ahab***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Kgs.22|1 Kings 22:13–28]] ]</sup></small>

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the messenger that went to call Micaiah spake to him, saying, Behold<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, the words of the prophets \[declare\] good to the king with one assent<small><small>[^33]</small></small>; let thy word therefore, I pray thee, be like one of theirs, and speak thou good. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Micaiah said, \[As\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> liveth, even what my God<small><small>[^34]</small></small> saith, that will I speak. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when he was come to the king, the king said unto him, Micaiah, shall we go to Ramoth–gilead to battle, or shall I forbear? And he said, Go ye up<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, and prosper, and they shall be delivered into your hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king said to him, How many times shall I adjure<small><small>[^36]</small></small> thee that thou say nothing but the truth to me in the name of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then he said<small><small>[^37]</small></small>, I did see all Israel scattered upon the mountains, as sheep<small><small>[^38]</small></small> that have no shepherd: and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said, These have no master<small><small>[^39]</small></small>; let them return \[therefore\] every man to his house in peace. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king of Israel said to Jehoshaphat, Did I not tell<small><small>[^40]</small></small> thee \[that\] he would not prophesy good unto me, but evil<small><small>[^41]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Again he said, Therefore hear the word<small><small>[^42]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; I saw<small><small>[^43]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> sitting upon his throne, and all the host<small><small>[^44]</small></small> of heaven standing on his right hand and \[on\] his left. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said, Who shall entice<small><small>[^45]</small></small> Ahab king of Israel, that he may go up<small><small>[^46]</small></small> and fall at Ramoth–gilead? And one spake saying after this manner, and another saying after that manner. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then there came<small><small>[^47]</small></small> out a spirit, and stood before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and said, I will entice him. And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto him, Wherewith? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, I will go out, and be a lying spirit<small><small>[^48]</small></small> in the mouth of all his prophets. And \[the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>\] said, Thou shalt<small><small>[^49]</small></small> entice \[him\], and thou shalt also prevail: go out, and do \[even\] so.<small><small>[^50]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now therefore, behold, the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath<small><small>[^51]</small></small> put a lying spirit in the mouth of these thy prophets, and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^52]</small></small></span> hath spoken evil against thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Zedekiah<small><small>[^53]</small></small> the son of Chenaanah came near, and smote Micaiah upon the cheek, and said, Which way<small><small>[^54]</small></small> went the Spirit of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> from me to speak unto thee? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Micaiah said, Behold<small><small>[^55]</small></small>, thou shalt see on that day when thou shalt go into an inner chamber<small><small>[^56]</small></small> to hide thyself. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the king of Israel said, Take ye Micaiah, and carry him back<small><small>[^57]</small></small> to Amon the governor of the city, and to Joash the king’s son; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And say, Thus saith the king, Put<small><small>[^58]</small></small> this \[fellow<small><small>[^59]</small></small>\] in the prison, and feed him with bread of affliction<small><small>[^60]</small></small> and with water of affliction, until I return<small><small>[^61]</small></small> in peace. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Micaiah said, If<small><small>[^62]</small></small> thou certainly return in peace, \[then\] hath not the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> spoken by me. And he said, Hearken<small><small>[^63]</small></small>, all ye people.

<big><big>[[2Chr.18.28 Ahab’s Defeat and Death|⥃]]</big></big>***Ahab’s Defeat and Death***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Kgs.22|1 Kings 22:29–40]] ]</sup></small>

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So the king<small><small>[^64]</small></small> of Israel and Jehoshaphat the king of Judah went up to Ramoth–gilead. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king of Israel said unto Jehoshaphat, I will disguise<small><small>[^65]</small></small> myself, and will go to the battle; but put thou on thy robes<small><small>[^66]</small></small>. So the king<small><small>[^67]</small></small> of Israel disguised himself; and they went to the battle. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now the king of Syria had commanded the captains of the chariots that \[were\] with him, saying, Fight ye<small><small>[^68]</small></small> not with small or great<small><small>[^69]</small></small>, save only with the king of Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, when the captains of the chariots saw Jehoshaphat<small><small>[^70]</small></small>, that they said, It \[is\] the king of Israel. Therefore they compassed about him to fight: but Jehoshaphat cried out, and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^71]</small></small></span> helped him; and God moved them<small><small>[^72]</small></small> \[to depart\] from him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For it came to pass, that, when the captains of the chariots perceived that it was not the king of Israel, they turned back again from pursuing him<small><small>[^73]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And a \[certain\] man<small><small>[^74]</small></small> drew a bow at a venture<small><small>[^75]</small></small>, and smote the king of Israel between the<small><small>[^76]</small></small> joints of the harness: therefore he said to his chariot man, Turn thine hand, that thou mayest carry me out of the host; for I am wounded<small><small>[^77]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.18.34|34]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the battle increased that day: howbeit the king of Israel stayed \[himself\] up in \[his\] chariot against the Syrians until the even: and about the time of the sun going down he died<small><small>[^78]</small></small>. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[2Chr.17|<< 2 Chronicles 17]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[2Chr.19|2 Chronicles 19 >>]]</td></tr></table>

The corslet was made of flax or of wool woven very thick, of ox-hide, of brass, or of iron. The metallic corslet consisted not of solid piece, but of scales, hooks, or rings, connected like the links of a chain, that the warrior might move with greater ease.
