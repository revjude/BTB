***Abijah Reigns in Judah***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Kgs.15|1 Kings 15:1–8]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1093

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now in the eighteenth<small><small>[^2]</small></small> year of king Jeroboam began Abijah to reign over Judah.<small><small>[^3]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He reigned three years in Jerusalem. His mother’s name also \[was\] Michaiah<small><small>[^4]</small></small> the daughter of Uriel of Gibeah<small><small>[^5]</small></small>. And there was<small><small>[^6]</small></small> war between Abijah and Jeroboam. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abijah set<small><small>[^7]</small></small> the battle in array with an army of valiant men of war, \[even\] four hundred<small><small>[^8]</small></small> thousand chosen men: Jeroboam also set the battle in array against him with eight hundred<small><small>[^9]</small></small> thousand chosen men, \[being\] mighty men of valour.

***Civil War against Jeroboam*** ^bsb1094

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And Abijah stood up upon mount Zemaraim<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, which \[is\] in mount Ephraim, and said, Hear me<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, thou Jeroboam, and all Israel; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ought ye not<small><small>[^12]</small></small> to know that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of Israel gave the kingdom over Israel to David<small><small>[^13]</small></small> for ever, \[even\] to him and to his sons by a covenant of salt<small><small>[^14]</small></small>?<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet Jeroboam the son of Nebat, the servant of Solomon the son of David, is risen up, and hath rebelled<small><small>[^16]</small></small> against his lord. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there are gathered unto him vain men<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, the children of Belial<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, and have strengthened themselves against Rehoboam the son of Solomon, when Rehoboam was young<small><small>[^19]</small></small> and tenderhearted, and could not<small><small>[^20]</small></small> withstand them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And now ye think to withstand the kingdom<small><small>[^21]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> in the hand of the sons of David; and ye \[be\] a great multitude<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, and \[there are\] with you golden<small><small>[^23]</small></small> calves, which Jeroboam made you for gods. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Have ye not cast out<small><small>[^24]</small></small> the priests of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, the sons of Aaron, and the Levites, and have made you priests<small><small>[^25]</small></small> after the manner of the nations of \[other\] lands? so that whosoever cometh to consecrate himself<small><small>[^26]</small></small> with a young<small><small>[^27]</small></small> bullock and seven rams, \[the same\] may be a priest of \[them that are\] no gods<small><small>[^28]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But as for us, the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] our God, and we have not forsaken him; and the priests<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, which minister unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[are\] the sons of Aaron, and the Levites \[wait\] upon \[their\] business:<small><small>[^30]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they burn<small><small>[^31]</small></small> unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> every morning and every evening burnt sacrifices and sweet incense<small><small>[^32]</small></small>: the shewbread also \[set they in order\] upon the pure table; and the candlestick<small><small>[^33]</small></small> of gold with the lamps thereof, to burn every evening: for we keep<small><small>[^34]</small></small> the charge of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> our God; but ye have forsaken him.<small><small>[^35]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And, behold, God<small><small>[^36]</small></small> himself \[is\] with us for \[our\] captain<small><small>[^37]</small></small>, and his priests<small><small>[^38]</small></small> with sounding trumpets to cry alarm against you. O children of Israel, fight ye<small><small>[^39]</small></small> not against the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of your fathers; for ye shall not<small><small>[^40]</small></small> prosper. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But Jeroboam caused an ambushment<small><small>[^41]</small></small> to come about behind them: so they were before Judah, and the ambushment \[was\] behind them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when Judah looked priests<small><small>[^44]</small></small> sounded with the trumpets. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the men of Judah gave a shout: and as the men<small><small>[^45]</small></small> of Judah shouted, it came to pass, that God smote<small><small>[^46]</small></small> Jeroboam and all Israel before Abijah and Judah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the children of Israel fled before Judah: and God delivered<small><small>[^47]</small></small> them into their hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abijah and his people slew them with a great slaughter: so there fell down slain of Israel five hundred<small><small>[^48]</small></small> thousand chosen men. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus the children of Israel were brought under at that time, and the children of Judah prevailed, because they relied<small><small>[^49]</small></small> upon the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of their fathers. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abijah pursued after Jeroboam, and took cities<small><small>[^50]</small></small> from him, Beth–el with the towns thereof, and Jeshanah<small><small>[^51]</small></small> with the towns thereof, and Ephrain<small><small>[^52]</small></small> with the towns thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.13.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Neither did<small><small>[^53]</small></small> Jeroboam recover strength again in the days of Abijah: and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^54]</small></small></span> struck him, and he died<small><small>[^55]</small></small>.
