<big><big>[[2Chr.19.1 Jehoshaphat Reproved by Jehu|⥃]]</big></big>***Jehoshaphat Reproved by Jehu***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.19.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jehoshaphat the king of Judah returned to his house in peace<small><small>[^2]</small></small> to Jerusalem.<small><small>[^3]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.19.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jehu<small><small>[^4]</small></small> the son of Hanani<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the seer<small><small>[^6]</small></small> went out to meet him, and said to king Jehoshaphat, Shouldest<small><small>[^7]</small></small> thou help the ungodly, and love them that hate the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^8]</small></small></span>? therefore \[is\] wrath<small><small>[^9]</small></small> upon thee from before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.19.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nevertheless there are good things<small><small>[^10]</small></small> found in thee, in that thou hast taken away the groves out of the land, and hast prepared<small><small>[^11]</small></small> thine heart to seek God.

<big><big>[[2Chr.19.4 Jehoshaphat’s Reforms|⥃]]</big></big>***Jehoshaphat’s Reforms***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.19.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jehoshaphat dwelt at Jerusalem: and he went out again<small><small>[^12]</small></small> through the people from Beer–sheba<small><small>[^13]</small></small> to mount<small><small>[^14]</small></small> Ephraim, and brought<small><small>[^15]</small></small> them back unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of their fathers. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.19.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he set judges in the land throughout all the fenced cities of Judah, city by city,<small><small>[^16]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.19.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And said to the judges, Take<small><small>[^17]</small></small> heed what ye do: for ye judge<small><small>[^18]</small></small> not for man, but for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, who \[is\] with you in the judgment<small><small>[^19]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.19.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore now let the<small><small>[^20]</small></small> fear of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> be upon you; take heed and do \[it\]: for \[there is\] no iniquity<small><small>[^21]</small></small> with the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> our God, nor respect of persons<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, nor taking of gifts<small><small>[^23]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.19.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Moreover in Jerusalem did Jehoshaphat set of the Levites<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, and \[of\] the priests, and of the chief of the fathers of Israel, for the judgment<small><small>[^25]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and for controversies, when they returned to Jerusalem. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.19.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he charged them, saying, Thus shall ye do in the fear<small><small>[^26]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, faithfully, and with a perfect heart. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.19.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And what cause soever shall come to you of your brethren that dwell in their cities, between blood<small><small>[^27]</small></small> and blood, between law and commandment, statutes and judgments, ye shall even warn them<small><small>[^28]</small></small> that they trespass not against the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and \[so\] wrath come<small><small>[^29]</small></small> upon you, and upon your brethren: this do, and ye shall not trespass. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.19.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And, behold, Amariah<small><small>[^30]</small></small> the chief priest \[is\] over you in all matters<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and Zebadiah the son of Ishmael, the ruler of the house of Judah, for all the king’s matters: also the Levites \[shall be\] officers before you.
