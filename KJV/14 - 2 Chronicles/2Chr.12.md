<big><big>[[2Chr.12.1 Shishak Raids Jerusalem|⥃]]</big></big>***Shishak Raids Jerusalem***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Kgs.14|1 Kings 14:25–28]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, when Rehoboam<small><small>[^2]</small></small> had established the kingdom, and had strengthened himself, he forsook<small><small>[^3]</small></small> the law of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and all Israel<small><small>[^4]</small></small> with him.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, \[that\] in the fifth year of king Rehoboam Shishak<small><small>[^6]</small></small> king of Egypt came up against Jerusalem, because<small><small>[^7]</small></small> they had transgressed against the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>,<small><small>[^8]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> With twelve hundred<small><small>[^9]</small></small> chariots, and threescore thousand horsemen: and the people \[were\] without number<small><small>[^10]</small></small> that came with him out of Egypt; the Lubims<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, the Sukkiims<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, and the Ethiopians<small><small>[^13]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he took the fenced<small><small>[^14]</small></small> cities which \[pertained\] to Judah, and came<small><small>[^15]</small></small> to Jerusalem. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then came Shemaiah<small><small>[^16]</small></small> the prophet to Rehoboam, and \[to\] the princes of Judah, that were gathered together to Jerusalem because of Shishak, and said unto them, Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, Ye have forsaken me<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, and therefore have I also left you<small><small>[^18]</small></small> in the hand of Shishak. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whereupon the princes of Israel and the king humbled<small><small>[^19]</small></small> themselves; and they said, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^20]</small></small></span> \[is\] righteous. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^21]</small></small></span> saw that they humbled themselves, the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came to Shemaiah, saying, They have humbled themselves; \[therefore<small><small>[^22]</small></small>\] I will not destroy them, but I will grant them some<small><small>[^23]</small></small> deliverance; and my wrath<small><small>[^24]</small></small> shall not be poured out upon Jerusalem by the hand of Shishak. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nevertheless<small><small>[^25]</small></small> they shall be his servants; that they may<small><small>[^26]</small></small> know my service, and the service of the kingdoms of the countries. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So Shishak<small><small>[^27]</small></small> king of Egypt came up against Jerusalem, and took away<small><small>[^28]</small></small> the treasures of the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and the treasures of the king’s house; he took all: he carried away also the shields<small><small>[^29]</small></small> of gold which Solomon had made. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Instead of which king Rehoboam made shields of brass<small><small>[^30]</small></small>, and committed \[them\] to the hands of the chief<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of the guard, that kept the entrance of the king’s house. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when the king entered into the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, the guard came and fetched them, and brought them again into the guard chamber. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when<small><small>[^32]</small></small> he humbled himself, the wrath of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> turned from him, that he would not destroy \[him\] altogether: and also in Judah things went well<small><small>[^33]</small></small>.

<big><big>[[2Chr.12.13 Rehoboam’s Reign and Death|⥃]]</big></big>***Rehoboam’s Reign and Death***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Kgs.14|1 Kings 14:21–24]] ]</sup></small>

And his mother’s name \[was\] Naamah an Ammonitess<small><small>[^37]</small></small>.<small><small>[^38]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he did evil, because he prepared<small><small>[^39]</small></small> not his heart to seek<small><small>[^40]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now the acts of Rehoboam, first and last<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, \[are\] they not written in the book<small><small>[^42]</small></small> of Shemaiah<small><small>[^43]</small></small> the prophet, and of Iddo<small><small>[^44]</small></small> the seer concerning genealogies? And \[there were\] wars<small><small>[^45]</small></small> between Rehoboam and Jeroboam continually. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Chr.12.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Rehoboam slept<small><small>[^46]</small></small> with his fathers, and was buried in the city of David: and Abijah<small><small>[^47]</small></small> his son reigned in his stead. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[2Chr.11|<< 2 Chronicles 11]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[2Chr.13|2 Chronicles 13 >>]]</td></tr></table>
