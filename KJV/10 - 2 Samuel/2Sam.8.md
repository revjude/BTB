<big><big>[[2Sam.8.1 David’s Triumphs|⥃]]</big></big>***David’s Triumphs***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Chr.18|1 Chronicles 18:1–13]] <big>†</big> [[Ps.60|Psalms 60:1–12]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And after<small><small>[^2]</small></small> this it came to pass, that David smote the Philistines, and subdued them: and David took Metheg–ammah<small><small>[^3]</small></small> out of the hand of the Philistines.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he smote<small><small>[^5]</small></small> Moab, and measured<small><small>[^6]</small></small> them with a line, casting them down to the ground; even with two lines measured he to put to death, and with one full line to keep alive. And \[so<small><small>[^7]</small></small>\] the Moabites became David’s servants, \[and\] brought gifts<small><small>[^8]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]David smote also Hadadezer<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, the son of Rehob, king of Zobah<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, as he went to recover his border at the river<small><small>[^11]</small></small> Euphrates. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And David took from him<small><small>[^12]</small></small> a thousand \[chariots<small><small>[^13]</small></small>\], and seven hundred horsemen, and twenty thousand footmen: and David houghed all the chariot \[horses\], but reserved<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of them \[for\] an hundred chariots. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when<small><small>[^15]</small></small> the Syrians of Damascus came<small><small>[^16]</small></small> to succour Hadadezer king of Zobah<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, David slew of the Syrians two and twenty thousand men. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then David put garrisons<small><small>[^18]</small></small> in Syria of Damascus: and the Syrians became<small><small>[^19]</small></small> servants to David, \[and\] brought gifts. And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^20]</small></small></span> preserved David whithersoever he went. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And David took the shields<small><small>[^21]</small></small> of gold that were on the servants of Hadadezer, and brought them to Jerusalem. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And from Betah<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, and from Berothai<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, cities of Hadadezer, king David took exceeding<small><small>[^24]</small></small> much brass. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When Toi<small><small>[^25]</small></small> king of Hamath<small><small>[^26]</small></small> heard that David had smitten all the host of Hadadezer, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Toi sent Joram<small><small>[^27]</small></small> his son unto king David, to salute him<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, and to bless him<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, because he had fought against Hadadezer, and smitten him: for Hadadezer had wars<small><small>[^30]</small></small> with Toi. And \[Joram\] brought with him<small><small>[^31]</small></small> vessels of silver, and vessels of gold, and vessels of brass: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Which<small><small>[^32]</small></small> also king David did dedicate unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, with the silver and gold that he had dedicated of all nations which he subdued; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Of Syria<small><small>[^33]</small></small>, and of Moab, and of the children of Ammon, and of the Philistines, and of Amalek, and of the spoil of Hadadezer, son of Rehob, king of Zobah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And David gat \[him<small><small>[^34]</small></small>\] a name when he returned from smiting<small><small>[^35]</small></small> of the Syrians in the valley of salt<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, \[being<small><small>[^37]</small></small>\] eighteen thousand \[men\]. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.8.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And he put garrisons in Edom; throughout all Edom put he garrisons, and all they<small><small>[^38]</small></small> of Edom became David’s servants. And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^39]</small></small></span> preserved David whithersoever he went.
