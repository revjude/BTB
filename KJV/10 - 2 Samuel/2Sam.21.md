***David Avenges the Gibeonites*** ^bsb782

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then there was a famine<small><small>[^2]</small></small> in the days of David three years, year after year; and David enquired<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^4]</small></small></span>. And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> answered, \[It is<small><small>[^5]</small></small>\] for Saul<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, and for \[his\] bloody house, because he slew the Gibeonites.<small><small>[^7]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king called the Gibeonites, and said unto them; (now the<small><small>[^8]</small></small> Gibeonites \[were\] not of the children of Israel, but of the remnant of the Amorites<small><small>[^9]</small></small>; and the children of Israel had sworn unto them: and Saul sought to slay them in his zeal<small><small>[^10]</small></small> to the children of Israel and Judah.) <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore David said unto the Gibeonites, What shall I do for you? and wherewith<small><small>[^11]</small></small> shall I make the atonement, that ye may bless<small><small>[^12]</small></small> the inheritance of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the Gibeonites said unto him, We will<small><small>[^13]</small></small> have no silver nor gold of Saul, nor of his house; neither for us shalt thou kill any man in Israel. And he said, What ye shall say, \[that\] will I do for you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they answered the king, The man<small><small>[^14]</small></small> that consumed us, and that devised<small><small>[^15]</small></small> against us \[that\] we should be destroyed from remaining in any of the coasts of Israel, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let seven<small><small>[^16]</small></small> men of his sons be delivered unto us, and we will hang<small><small>[^17]</small></small> them up unto the <divin<small><small>[^18]</small></small>eName>Lord</divineName> in Gibeah of Saul, \[whom\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> did choose<small><small>[^19]</small></small>. And the king said, I will give \[them\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the king spared Mephibosheth<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, the son of Jonathan the son of Saul, because<small><small>[^21]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> oath that \[was\] between them, between David and Jonathan the son of Saul. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the king took the two sons of Rizpah<small><small>[^22]</small></small> the daughter of Aiah, whom she bare unto Saul, Armoni and Mephibosheth; and the five sons<small><small>[^23]</small></small> of Michal<small><small>[^24]</small></small> the daughter of Saul, whom she brought up for<small><small>[^25]</small></small> Adriel the son of Barzillai the Meholathite: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he delivered them into the hands of the Gibeonites, and they hanged them in the hill before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^26]</small></small></span>: and they fell \[all\] seven together, and were put to death in the days of harvest, in the first \[days\], in the beginning<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of barley harvest. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Rizpah<small><small>[^28]</small></small> the daughter of Aiah took sackcloth<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, and spread it for her upon the rock, from the<small><small>[^30]</small></small> beginning of harvest until water<small><small>[^31]</small></small> dropped upon them out of heaven, and suffered neither the birds<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of the air to rest on them by day, nor the beasts of the field by night. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it was told David<small><small>[^33]</small></small> what Rizpah the daughter of Aiah, the concubine of Saul, had done. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And David went and took the bones of Saul<small><small>[^34]</small></small> and the bones of Jonathan his son from the men of Jabesh–gilead, which had stolen them from the street of Beth–shan<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, where the Philistines had hanged them, when the Philistines had slain Saul in Gilboa<small><small>[^36]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he brought up from thence the bones of Saul and the bones of Jonathan his son; and they gathered the bones of them that were hanged. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Sam.21.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the bones of Saul and Jonathan his son buried<small><small>[^37]</small></small> they in the country of Benjamin in Zelah<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, in the sepulchre of Kish his father: and they performed all that the king commanded. And after that God<small><small>[^39]</small></small> was intreated for the land.
