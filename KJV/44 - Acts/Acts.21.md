<big><big>[[Acts.21.1 Paul’s Journey to Jerusalem|⥃]]</big></big>***Paul’s Journey to Jerusalem***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, that after we were<small><small>[^2]</small></small> gotten from them, and had<small><small>[^3]</small></small> launched, we came with a straight course unto Coos<small><small>[^4]</small></small>, and the \[day\] following unto Rhodes, and from thence unto Patara: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And finding<small><small>[^5]</small></small> a ship sailing over unto Phenicia<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, we went aboard, and set forth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now when we had discovered Cyprus<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, we left it on the left hand, and sailed into Syria<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, and landed at Tyre<small><small>[^9]</small></small>: for there the ship was to unlade her burden. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And finding<small><small>[^10]</small></small> disciples, we<small><small>[^11]</small></small> tarried there seven days: who said<small><small>[^12]</small></small> to Paul through the Spirit, that he should not go up to Jerusalem. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when we had accomplished those days, we departed and went our way; and they<small><small>[^13]</small></small> all brought us on our way, with<small><small>[^14]</small></small> wives and children, till \[we were\] out of the city: and we kneeled<small><small>[^15]</small></small> down on the shore, and prayed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when we had taken<small><small>[^16]</small></small> our leave one of another, we took ship; <big><big>[[Acts.21.8 Paul Visits Philip the Evangelist|⥃]]</big></big>***Paul Visits Philip the Evangelist***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the next \[day\] we that<small><small>[^20]</small></small> were of Paul’s company departed, and came unto Cesarea<small><small>[^21]</small></small>: and we entered into the house of Philip<small><small>[^22]</small></small> the evangelist<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, which was \[one\] of the seven; and abode with him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the same man had four daughters, virgins<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, which<small><small>[^25]</small></small> did prophesy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And as<small><small>[^26]</small></small> we tarried \[there\] many days, there came down from Judea a certain prophet, named Agabus<small><small>[^27]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when he was come unto us, he took<small><small>[^28]</small></small> Paul’s girdle, and bound his own hands and feet, and said, Thus<small><small>[^29]</small></small> saith the Holy Ghost, So shall<small><small>[^30]</small></small> the Jews at Jerusalem bind the man that owneth this girdle, and shall<small><small>[^31]</small></small> deliver \[him\] into the hands of the Gentiles. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when we heard these things, both we, and they of that place, besought<small><small>[^32]</small></small> him not to go up to Jerusalem. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Paul answered, What<small><small>[^33]</small></small> mean ye to weep<small><small>[^34]</small></small> and to break mine heart? <big><big>[[Acts.21.17 Paul’s Arrival at Jerusalem|⥃]]</big></big>***Paul’s Arrival at Jerusalem***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when we were come to Jerusalem, the brethren<small><small>[^41]</small></small> received us gladly. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the \[day\] following Paul went in with us unto<small><small>[^42]</small></small> James; and all<small><small>[^43]</small></small> the elders were present. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when he had saluted them, he declared<small><small>[^44]</small></small> particularly what things God had wrought among the Gentiles by<small><small>[^45]</small></small> his ministry. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when they heard \[it\], they glorified<small><small>[^46]</small></small> the Lord, and said unto him, Thou seest, brother, how<small><small>[^47]</small></small> many thousands<small><small>[^48]</small></small> of Jews there are which believe; and they<small><small>[^49]</small></small> are all zealous of the law: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they are informed of thee, that thou<small><small>[^50]</small></small> teachest all the Jews which are among the Gentiles to forsake Moses, saying that they ought not to circumcise \[their\] children, neither to walk after the customs. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What is it therefore? the multitude<small><small>[^51]</small></small> must needs come together: for they will hear that thou art come. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Do therefore this that we say to thee: We have<small><small>[^52]</small></small> four men which have a vow on them; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Them take, and purify<small><small>[^53]</small></small> thyself with them, and be at charges with them, that they<small><small>[^54]</small></small> may shave \[their\] heads: and all may know that those things, whereof they were informed concerning thee, are nothing; but<small><small>[^55]</small></small> \[that\] thou thyself also walkest orderly, and keepest the law. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As touching the Gentiles which believe, we have<small><small>[^56]</small></small> written \[and\] concluded that they observe no such thing, save only that they keep themselves from \[things\] offered to idols, and from blood, and from strangled, and from fornication. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.21.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then<small><small>[^57]</small></small> Paul took the men, and the next day purifying himself with them entered<small><small>[^58]</small></small> into the temple, to signify<small><small>[^59]</small></small> the accomplishment of the days of purification, until that an offering should be offered for every one of them.
