***Tertullus Prosecutes Paul*** ^bsb2676

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And after five<small><small>[^2]</small></small> days Ananias<small><small>[^3]</small></small> the high priest descended with the elders, and \[with\] a certain orator<small><small>[^4]</small></small> \[named\] Tertullus, who informed<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the governor against Paul. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when he was called forth, Tertullus began to accuse \[him\], saying, Seeing<small><small>[^6]</small></small> that by thee we enjoy great quietness, and that very worthy deeds are done unto this nation by thy providence, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> We accept \[it\] always, and in all places, most<small><small>[^7]</small></small> noble Felix, with all thankfulness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Notwithstanding, that<small><small>[^8]</small></small> I be not further tedious unto thee, I pray thee that thou wouldest hear us of thy clemency a few words. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For we have<small><small>[^9]</small></small> found this man \[a\] pestilent \[fellow\], and a mover<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of sedition among all the Jews throughout the world, and a ringleader of the sect<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of the Nazarenes<small><small>[^12]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who also hath gone<small><small>[^13]</small></small> about to profane the temple: whom<small><small>[^14]</small></small> we took, and<small><small>[^15]</small></small> would have judged according to our law. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the chief<small><small>[^16]</small></small> captain Lysias came \[upon us\], and with great<small><small>[^17]</small></small> violence took \[him\] away out of our hands, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Commanding<small><small>[^18]</small></small> his accusers to come unto thee: by<small><small>[^19]</small></small> examining of whom thyself mayest take knowledge of all these things, whereof we accuse him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the Jews also assented, saying that these things were so.<small><small>[^20]</small></small>

***Paul’s Defense to Felix*** ^bsb2677

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Paul, after that the governor had<small><small>[^21]</small></small> beckoned unto him to speak, answered, Forasmuch as I know that thou hast been of many<small><small>[^22]</small></small> years a judge<small><small>[^23]</small></small> unto this nation, I do<small><small>[^24]</small></small> the more cheerfully answer for myself: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because that thou mayest understand, that there are yet but<small><small>[^25]</small></small> twelve days since I went up to Jerusalem for to worship<small><small>[^26]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they neither found me in the temple disputing with any man, neither raising up the people, neither in the synagogues, nor in the city:<small><small>[^27]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Neither can they prove the things whereof they now accuse me.<small><small>[^28]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But this I confess<small><small>[^29]</small></small> unto thee, that after<small><small>[^30]</small></small> the way which they call heresy<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, so<small><small>[^32]</small></small> worship I the class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And have<small><small>[^36]</small></small> hope toward God, which they themselves also allow, that<small><small>[^37]</small></small> there shall be a resurrection of the dead, both of the just and unjust. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And herein do I exercise myself, to have always a conscience void of offence toward God, and \[toward\] men.<small><small>[^38]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now after many years I came to bring<small><small>[^39]</small></small> alms to my nation, and offerings<small><small>[^40]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whereupon certain Jews from Asia found me purified in the temple, neither with multitude, nor with tumult.<small><small>[^41]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who ought to have been here before thee, and object, if they had ought against me.<small><small>[^42]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Or else let these same \[here\] say, if they have found any evil doing in me, while I stood before the council, <span Touching<small><small>[^43]</small></small> the resurrection of the dead I am called in question by you this day.

***The Verdict Postponed*** ^bsb2678

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when Felix heard these things, having<small><small>[^44]</small></small> more perfect knowledge of \[that\] way, he deferred them, and said, When<small><small>[^45]</small></small> Lysias the chief captain shall come down, I will know the uttermost of your matter. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he commanded a centurion to keep Paul, and to<small><small>[^46]</small></small> let \[him\] have liberty, and that he should forbid none of his<small><small>[^47]</small></small> acquaintance to minister or come unto him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And after certain days, when Felix came with his wife Drusilla, which was a Jewess, he sent<small><small>[^48]</small></small> for Paul, and heard him concerning the faith<small><small>[^49]</small></small> in Christ. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And as he<small><small>[^50]</small></small> reasoned of righteousness<small><small>[^51]</small></small>, temperance<small><small>[^52]</small></small>, and judgment<small><small>[^53]</small></small> to come, Felix<small><small>[^54]</small></small> trembled, and answered, Go<small><small>[^55]</small></small> thy way for this time; when<small><small>[^56]</small></small> I have a convenient season, I will call for thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He hoped<small><small>[^57]</small></small> also that money should have been given him of Paul, that he might loose him: wherefore<small><small>[^58]</small></small> he sent for him the oftener, and communed with him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.24.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But after two<small><small>[^59]</small></small> years Porcius Festus<small><small>[^60]</small></small> came into Felix’ room: and Felix, willing<small><small>[^61]</small></small> to shew the Jews a pleasure, left Paul bound. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Acts.23|<< Acts 23]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Acts.25|Acts 25 >>]]</td></tr></table>
