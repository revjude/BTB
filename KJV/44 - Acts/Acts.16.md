 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then came he to Derbe<small><small>[^2]</small></small> and Lystra: and, behold, a certain disciple was there, named<small><small>[^3]</small></small> Timotheus, the son of a certain woman, which<small><small>[^4]</small></small> was a Jewess, and believed; but<small><small>[^5]</small></small> his father \[was\] a Greek: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Which was<small><small>[^6]</small></small> well reported of by the brethren that were at Lystra and Iconium<small><small>[^7]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Him would<small><small>[^8]</small></small> Paul have to go forth with him; and took<small><small>[^9]</small></small> and circumcised him because of the Jews which were in those quarters: for they knew all that his father was a Greek. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And as they went through the cities, they delivered<small><small>[^10]</small></small> them the decrees for to keep, that were ordained of the apostles and elders which were at Jerusalem. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And so<small><small>[^11]</small></small> were the churches established in the faith, and increased<small><small>[^12]</small></small> in number daily.

<big><big>[[Acts.16.6 Paul’s Vision of the Macedonian|⥃]]</big></big>***Paul’s Vision of the Macedonian***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now when they had gone throughout Phrygia<small><small>[^13]</small></small> and the region<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of Galatia, and were forbidden<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of the Holy Ghost to preach the word in Asia<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> After they were come to Mysia, they assayed to go into Bithynia<small><small>[^17]</small></small>: but the Spirit suffered them not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they passing by Mysia came down to Troas<small><small>[^18]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And a vision<small><small>[^19]</small></small> appeared to Paul in the night; There stood a man of Macedonia<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, and prayed him, saying, Come<small><small>[^21]</small></small> over into Macedonia, and help us. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And after he had seen the vision, immediately<small><small>[^22]</small></small> we endeavoured to go into Macedonia, assuredly gathering that the Lord had called us for to preach the gospel unto them.

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore loosing from Troas, we came with a straight course to Samothracia, and the next \[day\] to Neapolis; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And from thence to Philippi<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, which is the chief<small><small>[^24]</small></small> city of that part of Macedonia, \[and\] a colony<small><small>[^25]</small></small>: and we were in that city abiding certain days. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And on<small><small>[^26]</small></small> the sabbath<small><small>[^27]</small></small> we went out of the city by a river side, where<small><small>[^28]</small></small> prayer was wont to be made; and we<small><small>[^29]</small></small> sat down, and spake<small><small>[^30]</small></small> unto the women which resorted \[thither\].

<big><big>[[Acts.16.16 Paul and Silas Imprisoned|⥃]]</big></big>***Paul and Silas Imprisoned***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pas<small><small>[^39]</small></small>s, as we went to prayer, a certain damsel possessed<small><small>[^40]</small></small> with a spirit of divination<small><small>[^41]</small></small> met us, which<small><small>[^42]</small></small> brought her masters much gain by soothsaying: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The same followed Paul and us, and cried, saying, These<small><small>[^43]</small></small> men are the servants<small><small>[^44]</small></small> of the most<small><small>[^45]</small></small> high God, which shew unto us the way<small><small>[^46]</small></small> of salvation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And this did she many days. $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when her masters saw that the hope<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of their gains was gone, they<small><small>[^50]</small></small> caught Paul and Silas, and drew \[them\] into the marketplace<small><small>[^51]</small></small> unto the rulers, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And brought them to the magistrates, saying, These men, being<small><small>[^52]</small></small> Jews, do<small><small>[^53]</small></small> exceedingly trouble our city, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And teach customs, which are not lawful for us to receive, neither to observe, being Romans.<small><small>[^54]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the multitude<small><small>[^55]</small></small> rose up together against them: and the magistrates<small><small>[^56]</small></small> rent off their clothes, and commanded to beat \[them\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when they had laid many stripes upon them, they cast<small><small>[^57]</small></small> \[them\] into prison, charging the jailor to keep<small><small>[^58]</small></small> them safely: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.16.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who, having received such a charge, thrust them into the inner<small><small>[^59]</small></small> prison, and made<small><small>[^60]</small></small> their feet fast in the stocks.
