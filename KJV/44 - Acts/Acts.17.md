***The Uproar in Thessalonica*** ^bsb2650

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now when they had passed through Amphipolis and Apollonia, they came to Thessalonica<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, where<small><small>[^3]</small></small> was a synagogue of the Jews: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Paul, as<small><small>[^4]</small></small> his manner was, went<small><small>[^5]</small></small> in unto them, and three sabbath days reasoned<small><small>[^6]</small></small> with them out of the scriptures, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Opening<small><small>[^7]</small></small> and alleging, that Christ<small><small>[^8]</small></small> must needs have suffered, and risen again from the dead; and that this<small><small>[^9]</small></small> Jesus, whom I preach<small><small>[^10]</small></small> unto you, is Christ. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And some<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of them believed, and consorted<small><small>[^12]</small></small> with Paul and Silas<small><small>[^13]</small></small>; and of the devout<small><small>[^14]</small></small> Greeks a great multitude, and of the chief<small><small>[^15]</small></small> women not a few. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the Jews which believed not, moved<small><small>[^16]</small></small> with envy, took<small><small>[^17]</small></small> unto them certain lewd fellows of the baser sort, and gathered a company, and set<small><small>[^18]</small></small> all the city on an uproar, and assaulted the house of Jason<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, and sought to bring them out to the people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when they found them not, they drew<small><small>[^20]</small></small> Jason and certain brethren unto the rulers of the city, crying, These<small><small>[^21]</small></small> that have turned the world upside down are come hither also; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whom Jason hath received: and these<small><small>[^22]</small></small> all do contrary to the decrees of Cesar, saying that there is another king, \[one\] Jesus. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they troubled the people and the rulers of the city, when they heard these things.<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when they had taken security of Jason, and of the other, they let them go.

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the brethren<small><small>[^24]</small></small> immediately sent away Paul and Silas by night unto Berea<small><small>[^25]</small></small>: who coming \[thither\] went<small><small>[^26]</small></small> into the synagogue of the Jews. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> These were more<small><small>[^27]</small></small> noble than those in Thessalonica, in that they received<small><small>[^28]</small></small> the word with all readiness of mind, and searched<small><small>[^29]</small></small> the scriptures daily, whether those things were so. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore many<small><small>[^30]</small></small> of them believed; also of honourable<small><small>[^31]</small></small> women which were Greeks, and of men, not a few. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But when the Jews<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of Thessalonica had knowledge that the word of God was preached of Paul at Berea, they came thither also, and stirred<small><small>[^33]</small></small> up the people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And then<small><small>[^34]</small></small> immediately the brethren sent away Paul to go as it<small><small>[^35]</small></small> were to the sea: but<small><small>[^36]</small></small> Silas and Timotheus abode there still. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they that conducted Paul brought him unto Athens<small><small>[^37]</small></small>: and receiving<small><small>[^38]</small></small> a commandment unto Silas and Timotheus for to come to him with all speed, they departed.

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now while Paul waited for them at Athens, his spirit<small><small>[^39]</small></small> was stirred in him, when he saw the city wholly given to idolatry<small><small>[^40]</small></small>.<small><small>[^41]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore disputed<small><small>[^42]</small></small> he in the synagogue with the Jews, and with the devout<small><small>[^43]</small></small> persons, and in the market daily<small><small>[^44]</small></small> with them that met with him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Acts.17.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then certain philosophers<small><small>[^45]</small></small> of the Epicureans, and of the Stoicks, encountered<small><small>[^46]</small></small> him. \[whereof\] he hath given assurance<small><small>[^77]</small></small> unto all \[men\], in that<small><small>[^78]</small></small> he hath raised him from the dead.
