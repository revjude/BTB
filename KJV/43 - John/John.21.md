***Jesus Appears by the Sea of Tiberias*** ^bsb2578

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> After these<small><small>[^2]</small></small> things Jesus<small><small>[^3]</small></small> shewed himself again to the disciples at the sea<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of Tiberias; and on this wise shewed he \[himself\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There were together Simon Peter, and Thomas<small><small>[^5]</small></small> called Didymus, and Nathanael<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of Cana<small><small>[^7]</small></small> in Galilee, and the \[sons<small><small>[^8]</small></small>\] of Zebedee, and two other of his disciples. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Simon Peter saith unto them, I go<small><small>[^9]</small></small> a fishing. They say unto him, We also go with thee. They went forth, and entered into a ship immediately; and that<small><small>[^10]</small></small> night they caught nothing. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But<small><small>[^11]</small></small> when the morning was now come, Jesus stood on the shore: but the disciples knew not that it was Jesus. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Jesus saith unto them, <span style="color:red;">Children<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, have<small><small>[^13]</small></small> ye any meat?</span> They answered him, No. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said unto them, <span style="color:red;">Cast<small><small>[^14]</small></small> the net on the right side of the ship, and ye shall find.</span> They cast<small><small>[^15]</small></small> therefore, and now they were not able to draw it for the multitude<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of fishes. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore that disciple<small><small>[^17]</small></small> whom Jesus loved saith unto Peter, It is<small><small>[^18]</small></small> the Lord. Now when<small><small>[^19]</small></small> Simon Peter heard that it was the Lord, he girt \[his\] fisher’s coat<small><small>[^20]</small></small> \[unto him\], (for he was naked<small><small>[^21]</small></small>,) and did cast himself into the sea. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the other disciples came in a little ship; (for they were not far from land, but as it were two hundred cubits<small><small>[^22]</small></small>,) dragging the net with fishes. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As soon then as they were come to land, they saw<small><small>[^23]</small></small> a fire of coals there, and fish laid thereon, and bread. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Jesus saith unto them, <span style="color:red;">Bring of the fish which ye have now caught.</span> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Simon Peter went up, and drew the net to land full of great fishes, an hundred and fifty and three: and for<small><small>[^24]</small></small> all there were so many, yet was not the net broken. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Jesus saith unto them, <span style="color:red;">Come<small><small>[^25]</small></small> \[and\] dine<small><small>[^26]</small></small>.</span> And none of the disciples durst<small><small>[^27]</small></small> ask him, Who art thou? knowing that it was the Lord. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Jesus then cometh, and taketh bread, and giveth them, and fish likewise.<small><small>[^28]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> This is now the third time<small><small>[^29]</small></small> that Jesus shewed himself to his disciples, after that he was risen from the dead.

***Jesus and Peter*** ^bsb2579

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So when they had dined, Jesus saith to Simon Peter, <span style="color:red;">Simon, \[son<small><small>[^30]</small></small>\] of Jonas, lovest<small><small>[^31]</small></small> thou me more<small><small>[^32]</small></small> than these?</span> He saith unto him, Yea, Lord; thou knowest<small><small>[^33]</small></small> that I love thee. He saith unto him, <span style="color:red;">Feed<small><small>[^34]</small></small> my lambs<small><small>[^35]</small></small>.</span> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He saith to him again the second<small><small>[^36]</small></small> time, <span style="color:red;">Simon, \[son\] of Jonas, lovest thou me?</span> He saith unto him, Yea, Lord; thou knowest that I love thee. He saith unto him, <span style="color:red;">Feed my sheep<small><small>[^37]</small></small>.</span> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He saith unto him the third<small><small>[^38]</small></small> time, <span style="color:red;">Simon, \[son\] of Jonas, lovest thou me?</span> Peter was grieved<small><small>[^39]</small></small> because he said unto him the third time, Lovest thou me? And he said unto him, Lord<small><small>[^40]</small></small>, thou knowest all things; thou knowest that<small><small>[^41]</small></small> I love thee. Jesus saith unto him, <span style="color:red;">Feed<small><small>[^42]</small></small> my sheep.</span> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> <span style="color:red;">Verily, verily, I say unto thee, When thou wast young, thou girdedst thyself, and walkedst whither thou wouldest: but<small><small>[^43]</small></small> when thou shalt be old, thou shalt stretch forth thy hands, and another<small><small>[^44]</small></small> shall gird thee, and carry \[thee\] whither thou wouldest not<small><small>[^45]</small></small>.</span> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> This spake he, signifying by<small><small>[^46]</small></small> what death he should glorify God. And when he had spoken this, he saith unto him, <span style="color:red;">Follow<small><small>[^47]</small></small> me.</span>

***Jesus and the Beloved Disciple*** ^bsb2580

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Peter, turning about, seeth<small><small>[^48]</small></small> the disciple whom Jesus loved following; which<small><small>[^49]</small></small> also leaned on his breast at supper, and said, Lord, which is he that betrayeth thee? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Peter seeing him saith to Jesus, Lord<small><small>[^50]</small></small>, and what \[shall\] this man \[do\]? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Jesus saith unto him, <span style="color:red;">If<small><small>[^51]</small></small> I will that he tarry till I come, what \[is that\] to thee? follow<small><small>[^52]</small></small> thou me.</span> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then went this saying abroad among the brethren, that that disciple should not die: yet Jesus said not unto him, He shall not die; but, If I will that he tarry till I come, what<small><small>[^53]</small></small> \[is that\] to thee? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> This is the disciple which testifieth of these things, and wrote these things: and we know<small><small>[^54]</small></small> that his testimony is true. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[John.21.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there<small><small>[^55]</small></small> are also many other things which Jesus did, the which, if they should be written every one, I suppose that even<small><small>[^56]</small></small> the world itself could not contain the books that should be written.
