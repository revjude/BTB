***The Law of Liberty***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Matt.7|Matthew 7:1–6]] <big>†</big> [[Luke.6|Luke 6:37–42]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb2728

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Him that is weak<small><small>[^2]</small></small> in the faith receive<small><small>[^3]</small></small> ye, \[but\] not to doubtful disputations<small><small>[^4]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For one believeth that<small><small>[^5]</small></small> he may eat all things: another<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, who is weak, eateth<small><small>[^7]</small></small> herbs. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let not him that eateth despise<small><small>[^8]</small></small> him that eateth not; and let not him which eateth not judge<small><small>[^9]</small></small> him that eateth: for<small><small>[^10]</small></small> God hath received him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who<small><small>[^11]</small></small> art thou that judgest another man’s servant? to his own master he standeth or falleth. Yea, he shall<small><small>[^12]</small></small> be holden up: for<small><small>[^13]</small></small> God is able to make him stand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> One man esteemeth<small><small>[^14]</small></small> one day above another: another esteemeth every day \[alike\]. Let<small><small>[^15]</small></small> every man be fully persuaded<small><small>[^16]</small></small> in his own mind. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that regardeth<small><small>[^17]</small></small> the day, regardeth \[it<small><small>[^18]</small></small>\] unto the Lord; and he that regardeth not the day, to the Lord he doth not regard \[it\]. He that eateth, eateth to the Lord, for<small><small>[^19]</small></small> he giveth God thanks; and he that eateth not, to the Lord he eateth not, and giveth God thanks. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For none of us liveth to himself, and no man dieth to himself.<small><small>[^20]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For whether we live, we live unto the Lord; and whether we die, we die unto<small><small>[^21]</small></small> the Lord: whether we live therefore<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, or die, we are the Lord’s. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For to this end Christ<small><small>[^23]</small></small> both died, and rose, and revived, that he might be Lord<small><small>[^24]</small></small> both of the dead and living. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But why dost thou judge thy brother? or why dost thou set<small><small>[^25]</small></small> at nought thy brother? for<small><small>[^26]</small></small> we shall all stand before the judgment seat of Christ. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For it is written, \[As<small><small>[^27]</small></small>\] I live, saith the Lord, every knee<small><small>[^28]</small></small> shall bow to me, and every tongue shall confess<small><small>[^29]</small></small> to God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So then every one of us shall give account of himself to God.<small><small>[^30]</small></small>

***The Law of Love***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Ezek.14|Ezekiel 14:1–11]] <big>†</big> [[1Cor.8|1 Corinthians 8:1–13]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb2729

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let us not therefore judge one<small><small>[^31]</small></small> another any more: but<small><small>[^32]</small></small> judge this rather, that no man put<small><small>[^33]</small></small> a stumblingblock or<small><small>[^34]</small></small> an occasion to fall in \[his\] brother’s way. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I know, and am<small><small>[^35]</small></small> persuaded by the Lord Jesus, that \[there<small><small>[^36]</small></small> is\] nothing unclean<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of itself: but to him that esteemeth any thing to be unclean, to him \[it<small><small>[^38]</small></small> is\] unclean. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if thy brother<small><small>[^39]</small></small> be grieved with \[thy\] meat, now<small><small>[^40]</small></small> walkest thou not charitably<small><small>[^41]</small></small>. Destroy<small><small>[^42]</small></small> not him with thy meat, for whom Christ died. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let not then your good be evil spoken of:<small><small>[^43]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the kingdom<small><small>[^44]</small></small> of God is<small><small>[^45]</small></small> not meat and drink; but<small><small>[^46]</small></small> righteousness, and peace<small><small>[^47]</small></small>, and joy in the Holy Ghost. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he that in<small><small>[^48]</small></small> these things serveth Chris<small><small>[^49]</small></small>t \[is\] acceptable to God, and<small><small>[^50]</small></small> approved of men. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let us therefore follow<small><small>[^51]</small></small> after the things which make for peace, and<small><small>[^52]</small></small> things wherewith one may edify another. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^53]</small></small> meat destroy not the work<small><small>[^54]</small></small> of God. All<small><small>[^55]</small></small> things indeed \[are\] pure; but<small><small>[^56]</small></small> \[it is\] evil for that man who eateth with offence. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[It is\] good<small><small>[^57]</small></small> neither to eat flesh, nor to drink wine, nor \[any thing\] whereby<small><small>[^58]</small></small> thy brother stumbleth, or is offended, or is made weak. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.14.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hast thou<small><small>[^59]</small></small> faith? have \[it\] to thyself before God.
