<big><big>[[Rom.5.1 The Triumph of Faith|⥃]]</big></big>***The Triumph of Faith***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore being<small><small>[^2]</small></small> justified by faith, we have<small><small>[^3]</small></small> peace with God through<small><small>[^4]</small></small> our Lord Jesus Christ: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> By whom<small><small>[^5]</small></small> also we have access by faith into this grace wherein<small><small>[^6]</small></small> we stand, and rejoice<small><small>[^7]</small></small> in hope of the glory<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And not only \[so\], but we<small><small>[^9]</small></small> glory in tribulations also: knowing<small><small>[^10]</small></small> that tribulation worketh patience; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And patience<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, experience; and experience<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, hope: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And hope<small><small>[^13]</small></small> maketh not ashamed; because<small><small>[^14]</small></small> the love of God is shed<small><small>[^15]</small></small> abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us.

<big><big>[[Rom.5.6 Christ’s Sacrifice for the Ungodly|⥃]]</big></big>***Christ’s Sacrifice for the Ungodly***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[John.3|John 3:1–21]] ]</sup></small>

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^16]</small></small> when we were yet without<small><small>[^17]</small></small> strength, in due time<small><small>[^18]</small></small> Christ<small><small>[^19]</small></small> died for the ungodly<small><small>[^20]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For scarcely<small><small>[^21]</small></small> for a righteous man will one die: yet peradventure for a good<small><small>[^22]</small></small> man some<small><small>[^23]</small></small> would even dare to die. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But God commendeth<small><small>[^24]</small></small> his love toward us, in that<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Much more then, being<small><small>[^26]</small></small> now justified by his blood, we shall<small><small>[^27]</small></small> be saved from wrath through him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For if, when<small><small>[^28]</small></small> we were enemies, we were reconciled<small><small>[^29]</small></small> to God by the death of his Son, much more, being reconciled, we shall<small><small>[^30]</small></small> be saved by his life. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And not only \[so\], but we<small><small>[^31]</small></small> also joy in God through our Lord Jesus Christ, by whom<small><small>[^32]</small></small> we have now received the atonement<small><small>[^33]</small></small>.

<big><big>[[Rom.5.12 Death in Adam, Life in Christ|⥃]]</big></big>***Death in Adam, Life in Christ***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Gen.3|Genesis 3:1–7]] ]</sup></small>

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore, as by<small><small>[^34]</small></small> one man sin entered into the world, and death<small><small>[^35]</small></small> by sin; and so death passed upon all men, for that<small><small>[^36]</small></small> all<small><small>[^37]</small></small> have sinned: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> (For until<small><small>[^38]</small></small> the law sin was in the world: but sin<small><small>[^39]</small></small> is not imputed when there is no law. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nevertheless death<small><small>[^40]</small></small> reigned from Adam to Moses, even<small><small>[^41]</small></small> over them that had not sinned after the similitude of Adam’s transgression, who is the figure<small><small>[^42]</small></small> of him that was to come. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But not<small><small>[^43]</small></small> as the offence, so also \[is\] the free gift. For if through the offence of one many<small><small>[^44]</small></small> be dead, much<small><small>[^45]</small></small> more the grace of God, and the gift<small><small>[^46]</small></small> by grace, \[which is\] by one man, Jesus Christ, hath<small><small>[^47]</small></small> abounded unto many. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And not as \[it was\] by one that sinned, \[so is\] the gift: for the<small><small>[^48]</small></small> judgment \[was\] by one to condemnation, but the free<small><small>[^49]</small></small> gift \[is\] of many offences unto justification. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For if<small><small>[^50]</small></small> by one man’s offence<small><small>[^51]</small></small> death reigned by one; much more they which receive abundance<small><small>[^52]</small></small> of grace and of the gift<small><small>[^53]</small></small> of righteousness shall reign<small><small>[^54]</small></small> in life by one, Jesus Christ.) <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore as by the offence<small><small>[^55]</small></small> of one \[judgment came\] upon<small><small>[^56]</small></small> all men to condemnation; even so by the righteousness<small><small>[^57]</small></small> of one \[the free gift came\] upon all men<small><small>[^58]</small></small> unto justification of life. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For as by one<small><small>[^59]</small></small> man’s disobedience many were made sinners, so by<small><small>[^60]</small></small> the obedience of one shall many be made righteous. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover the law<small><small>[^61]</small></small> entered, that the offence might abound. But<small><small>[^62]</small></small> where sin abounded, grace did much more abound: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.5.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That<small><small>[^63]</small></small> as sin hath reigned unto death, even so might grace<small><small>[^64]</small></small> reign through<small><small>[^65]</small></small> righteousness unto<small><small>[^66]</small></small> eternal life by Jesus Christ our Lord. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Rom.4|<< Romans 4]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Rom.6|Romans 6 >>]]</td></tr></table>
