***Release from the Law***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Gal.3|Galatians 3:15–25]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb2706

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Know<small><small>[^2]</small></small> ye not, brethren<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, (for I speak to them that<small><small>[^4]</small></small> know the law<small><small>[^5]</small></small>,) how that the law hath dominion over a man<small><small>[^6]</small></small> as long as he liveth? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the woman<small><small>[^7]</small></small> which hath an husband is bound by the law to \[her\] husband so long as he liveth; but if the husband be dead, she is loosed from the law of \[her\] husband. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So then<small><small>[^8]</small></small> if, while \[her\] husband liveth, she be married to another man, she shall be called an adulteress: but if her husband be dead, she is free from that law; so that she is no adulteress, though<small><small>[^9]</small></small> she be married to another man. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore, my brethren, ye also<small><small>[^10]</small></small> are become dead to the law by the body<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of Christ; that ye<small><small>[^12]</small></small> should be married to another, \[even\] to him who is raised from the dead, that we<small><small>[^13]</small></small> should bring forth fruit unto God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For when we were in the flesh<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, the motions<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of sins, which<small><small>[^16]</small></small> were by the law, did work<small><small>[^17]</small></small> in our members<small><small>[^18]</small></small> to bring<small><small>[^19]</small></small> forth fruit unto death. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But<small><small>[^20]</small></small> now we are delivered from the law, that being dead<small><small>[^21]</small></small> wherein we were held; that we should serve<small><small>[^22]</small></small> in newness of spirit, and not \[in\] the oldness of the letter.

***God’s Law Is Holy*** ^bsb2707

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What<small><small>[^23]</small></small> shall we say then? \[Is\] the law sin? God forbid. Nay, I had<small><small>[^24]</small></small> not known sin, but by the law: for I had not known lust<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, except the law had said, Thou shalt<small><small>[^26]</small></small> not covet.<small><small>[^27]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But sin<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, taking occasion by the commandment, wrought<small><small>[^29]</small></small> in me all manner of concupiscence. For without<small><small>[^30]</small></small> the law sin \[was\] dead. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I<small><small>[^31]</small></small> was alive without<small><small>[^32]</small></small> the law once: but<small><small>[^33]</small></small> when the commandment came, sin<small><small>[^34]</small></small> revived, and I died<small><small>[^35]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the commandment, which \[was ordained\] to life, I found \[to be\] unto death.<small><small>[^36]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For sin<small><small>[^37]</small></small>, taking occasion by the commandment, deceived<small><small>[^38]</small></small> me, and by it slew \[me\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore the law<small><small>[^39]</small></small> \[is\] holy, and the commandment<small><small>[^40]</small></small> holy, and just, and good.

***Struggling with Sin*** ^bsb2708

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Was then<small><small>[^41]</small></small> that which is good made death unto me? God forbid. But sin<small><small>[^42]</small></small>, that it might appear sin, working death in me by that which is good; that sin by the commandment might become exceeding sinful. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For we know that the law<small><small>[^43]</small></small> is spiritual: but<small><small>[^44]</small></small> I am carnal<small><small>[^45]</small></small>, sold<small><small>[^46]</small></small> under sin. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For that<small><small>[^47]</small></small> which I do I allow<small><small>[^48]</small></small> not: for what<small><small>[^49]</small></small> I would, that do I not; but what I hate<small><small>[^50]</small></small>, that do I. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If then I do that which I would not, I consent<small><small>[^51]</small></small> unto the law that \[it is\] good. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now then it is no more<small><small>[^52]</small></small> I that do it, but sin<small><small>[^53]</small></small> that dwelleth in me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I know that in me<small><small>[^54]</small></small> (that is, in my<small><small>[^55]</small></small> flesh,) dwelleth no good thing: for to will<small><small>[^56]</small></small> is present with me; but \[how\] to perform that which is good I find not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the good that I would I do not: but the evil which I would not, that I do. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now if I do that I would not, it is no<small><small>[^57]</small></small> more I that do it, but sin that dwelleth in me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I find then a law<small><small>[^58]</small></small>, that, when I would do good, evil<small><small>[^59]</small></small> is present with me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I delight<small><small>[^60]</small></small> in the law of God after the inward<small><small>[^61]</small></small> man: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But I see another<small><small>[^62]</small></small> law in my members<small><small>[^63]</small></small>, warring against the law of my mind, and<small><small>[^64]</small></small> bringing me into captivity to the law of sin which is in my members. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O wretched<small><small>[^65]</small></small> man that I am! who<small><small>[^66]</small></small> shall deliver me from the body of this<small><small>[^67]</small></small> death? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.7.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I thank God<small><small>[^68]</small></small> through Jesus Christ our Lord.
