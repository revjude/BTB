<big><big>[[Rom.2.1 God’s Righteous Judgment|⥃]]</big></big>***God’s Righteous Judgment***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Ps.75|Psalms 75:1–10]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore<small><small>[^2]</small></small> thou art inexcusable, O man<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, whosoever<small><small>[^4]</small></small> thou art that judgest: for wherein thou judgest another, thou condemnest thyself; for thou that<small><small>[^5]</small></small> judgest doest the same things. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But we are sure that the judgment<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of God is according to truth against them which commit such things. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thinkest<small><small>[^7]</small></small> thou this, O man<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, that judgest them which do such things, and doest the same, that thou shalt<small><small>[^9]</small></small> escape the judgment of God? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Or despisest<small><small>[^10]</small></small> thou the riches<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of his goodness and forbearance<small><small>[^12]</small></small> and longsuffering; not knowing that the goodness<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of God leadeth thee to repentance? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But after<small><small>[^14]</small></small> thy hardness and impenitent heart treasurest<small><small>[^15]</small></small> up unto thyself wrath against the day<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of wrath and revelation<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of the righteous judgment of God; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who will render to every man according to his deeds:<small><small>[^18]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To them who by patient<small><small>[^19]</small></small> continuance in well doing seek for glory<small><small>[^20]</small></small> and honour and immortality<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, eternal<small><small>[^22]</small></small> life: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But unto them that are contentious<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, and do not<small><small>[^24]</small></small> obey the truth, but obey<small><small>[^25]</small></small> unrighteousness, indignation<small><small>[^26]</small></small> and wrath, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Tribulation<small><small>[^27]</small></small> and anguish, upon every soul<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of man that doeth evil, of the Jew<small><small>[^29]</small></small> first, and also of the Gentile<small><small>[^30]</small></small>; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But glory<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, honour, and peace<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, to every<small><small>[^33]</small></small> man that worketh good, to the Jew first, and also to the Gentile<small><small>[^34]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For there is no respect of persons with God.<small><small>[^35]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^36]</small></small> as many as have sinned without law shall also perish without law: and as many as have sinned in the law<small><small>[^37]</small></small> shall be judged by the law; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> (For not<small><small>[^38]</small></small> the hearers of the law \[are\] just before God, but the<small><small>[^39]</small></small> doers of the law shall be justified<small><small>[^40]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For when the Gentiles, which<small><small>[^41]</small></small> have not the law, do by<small><small>[^42]</small></small> nature the things contained in the law, these, having not the law, are a law<small><small>[^43]</small></small> unto themselves: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Which shew the work of the law written<small><small>[^44]</small></small> in their hearts, their conscience<small><small>[^45]</small></small> also bearing witness, and \[their\] thoughts the mean while<small><small>[^46]</small></small> accusing<small><small>[^47]</small></small> or else excusing one another;) <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the day when God<small><small>[^48]</small></small> shall judge the secrets of men by Jesus<small><small>[^49]</small></small> Christ according<small><small>[^50]</small></small> to my gospel.

<big><big>[[Rom.2.17 The Jews and the Law|⥃]]</big></big>***The Jews and the Law***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, thou art<small><small>[^51]</small></small> called a Jew, and restest<small><small>[^52]</small></small> in the law, and makest<small><small>[^53]</small></small> thy boast of God, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And knowest<small><small>[^54]</small></small> \[his\] will, and approvest the things that are more excellent<small><small>[^55]</small></small>, being instructed<small><small>[^56]</small></small> out of the law; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And art confident<small><small>[^57]</small></small> that thou thyself art a guide of the blind, a light<small><small>[^58]</small></small> of them which are in darkness, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> An instructor of the foolish, a teacher<small><small>[^59]</small></small> of babes, which hast the form<small><small>[^60]</small></small> of knowledge and of the truth in the law. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou therefore<small><small>[^61]</small></small> which teachest another, teachest thou not thyself? thou that preachest a man should not steal, dost thou steal<small><small>[^62]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou that sayest a man should not commit adultery<small><small>[^63]</small></small>, dost thou commit adultery? thou that abhorrest idols, dost thou commit sacrilege<small><small>[^64]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou that makest<small><small>[^65]</small></small> thy boast of the law, through breaking the law dishonourest thou God? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the name<small><small>[^66]</small></small> of God is blasphemed among the Gentiles through you, as it is written<small><small>[^67]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For circumcision<small><small>[^68]</small></small> verily profiteth, if thou keep the law: but if<small><small>[^69]</small></small> thou be a breaker of the law, thy circumcision is made uncircumcision. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore if the uncircumcision keep the righteousness of the law, shall not his uncircumcision be counted for circumcision?<small><small>[^70]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And shall not uncircumcision which is by nature, if it fulfil<small><small>[^71]</small></small> the law, judge<small><small>[^72]</small></small> thee, who by the<small><small>[^73]</small></small> letter and circumcision dost transgress the law? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he<small><small>[^74]</small></small> is not a Jew, which is one outwardly; neither<small><small>[^75]</small></small> \[is that\] circumcision, which is outward in the flesh: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.2.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But he \[is\] a Jew, which<small><small>[^76]</small></small> is one inwardly; and circumcision<small><small>[^77]</small></small> \[is that\] of the heart, in the spirit<small><small>[^78]</small></small>, \[and\] not in the letter; whose<small><small>[^79]</small></small> praise \[is\] not of men, but of God. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Rom.1|<< Romans 1]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Rom.3|Romans 3 >>]]</td></tr></table>
