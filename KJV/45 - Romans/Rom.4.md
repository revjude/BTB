<big><big>[[Rom.4.1 Abraham Justified by Faith|⥃]]</big></big>***Abraham Justified by Faith***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Gen.15|Genesis 15:1–7]] <big>†</big> [[Ps.32|Psalms 32:1–11]] <big>†</big> [[Heb.11|Hebrews 11:8–19]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What<small><small>[^2]</small></small> shall we say then that Abraham<small><small>[^3]</small></small> our father, as pertaining<small><small>[^4]</small></small> to the flesh, hath found? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For if Abraham<small><small>[^5]</small></small> were justified by works, he hath<small><small>[^6]</small></small> \[whereof\] to glory; but<small><small>[^7]</small></small> not before God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For what<small><small>[^8]</small></small> saith the scripture? Abraham<small><small>[^9]</small></small> believed God, and it was counted<small><small>[^10]</small></small> unto him for righteousness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now to him that worketh is the reward not reckoned of grace, but of debt.<small><small>[^11]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But to<small><small>[^12]</small></small> him that worketh not, but believeth<small><small>[^13]</small></small> on him that justifieth the ungodly<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, his faith<small><small>[^15]</small></small> is counted for righteousness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Even as David also describeth the blessedness<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of the man, unto whom God imputeth<small><small>[^17]</small></small> righteousness without<small><small>[^18]</small></small> works, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Saying\], Blessed \[are\] they whose iniquities are forgiven, and whose sins are covered.<small><small>[^19]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Blessed \[is\] the man to whom<small><small>[^20]</small></small> the Lord will not impute sin. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Cometh<small><small>[^21]</small></small>\] this blessedness then upon the circumcision \[only\], or upon the uncircumcision also? for we<small><small>[^22]</small></small> say that faith was reckoned to Abraham for righteousness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> How was it then reckoned? when he was in circumcision, or in uncircumcision? Not in circumcision<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, but in uncircumcision. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he received the sign<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of circumcision, a seal<small><small>[^25]</small></small> of the righteousness<small><small>[^26]</small></small> of the faith which \[he had yet\] being uncircumcised: that he might be the father<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of all them that believe, though they be not circumcised; that righteousness<small><small>[^28]</small></small> might be imputed unto them also: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the father of circumcision to them<small><small>[^29]</small></small> who are not of the circumcision only, but who also walk in the steps<small><small>[^30]</small></small> of that faith of our father Abraham, which \[he had\] being \[yet\] uncircumcised.

<big><big>[[Rom.4.13 Abraham Receives the Promise|⥃]]</big></big>***Abraham Receives the Promise***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Gen.15|Genesis 15:8–21]] ]</sup></small>

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the<small><small>[^31]</small></small> promise, that he should be the heir of the world, \[was\] not to Abraham, or to his seed, through the<small><small>[^32]</small></small> law, but through<small><small>[^33]</small></small> the righteousness of faith. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For if<small><small>[^34]</small></small> they which are of the law \[be\] heirs, faith is made<small><small>[^35]</small></small> void, and the promise made of none effect: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because<small><small>[^36]</small></small> the law worketh wrath: for where<small><small>[^37]</small></small> no law is, \[there is\] no transgression. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore \[it is\] of faith<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, that \[it might be\] by grace; to the end the promise<small><small>[^39]</small></small> might be sure to all the seed; not to that only which is of the law, but to<small><small>[^40]</small></small> that also which is of the faith of Abraham; who is the father<small><small>[^41]</small></small> of us all, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> (As it is written, I have<small><small>[^42]</small></small> made thee a father of many nations,) before him<small><small>[^43]</small></small> whom he believed, \[even\] God, who quickeneth<small><small>[^44]</small></small> the dead, and calleth<small><small>[^45]</small></small> those things which be not as though they were. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who against<small><small>[^46]</small></small> hope believed in hope, that he might become the father of many nations; according to that which was spoken, So shall<small><small>[^47]</small></small> thy seed be. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And being<small><small>[^48]</small></small> not weak in faith, he considered<small><small>[^49]</small></small> not his own body now dead, when he was about an hundred years old, neither yet the deadness of Sara’s womb: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He staggered<small><small>[^50]</small></small> not at the promise of God through unbelief; but was<small><small>[^51]</small></small> strong in faith, giving glory to God; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And being fully<small><small>[^52]</small></small> persuaded that, what he had promised, he was able<small><small>[^53]</small></small> also to perform. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And therefore it was imputed<small><small>[^54]</small></small> to him for righteousness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now it was not written for his sake alone, that it was imputed to him;<small><small>[^55]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But for us<small><small>[^56]</small></small> also, to whom it shall be imputed, if we<small><small>[^57]</small></small> believe on him that raised up Jesus our Lord from the dead; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.4.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who was<small><small>[^58]</small></small> delivered for our offences, and was raised<small><small>[^59]</small></small> again for our justification. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Rom.3|<< Romans 3]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Rom.5|Romans 5 >>]]</td></tr></table>
