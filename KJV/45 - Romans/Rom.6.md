<big><big>[[Rom.6.1 Dead to Sin, Alive to God|⥃]]</big></big>***Dead to Sin, Alive to God***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[2Cor.4|2 Corinthians 4:7–18]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What<small><small>[^2]</small></small> shall<small><small>[^3]</small></small> we say then? Shall we continue in sin, that grace may abound? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> God<small><small>[^4]</small></small> forbid. How<small><small>[^5]</small></small> shall we, that are dead<small><small>[^6]</small></small> to sin, live<small><small>[^7]</small></small> any longer therein? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Know<small><small>[^8]</small></small> ye not, that so many of us as were<small><small>[^9]</small></small><small><small>[^10]</small></small> baptized into Jesus Christ were baptized into his death? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore we are<small><small>[^11]</small></small> buried with him by baptism into death: that<small><small>[^12]</small></small> like as Christ was raised up from the dead by the<small><small>[^13]</small></small> glory of the Father, even<small><small>[^14]</small></small> so we also should walk in newness of life. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^15]</small></small> if we have been planted<small><small>[^16]</small></small> together in the likeness of his death, we shall be also \[in the likeness\] of \[his\] resurrection: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Knowing this, that our<small><small>[^17]</small></small> old man is crucified with \[him\], that the<small><small>[^18]</small></small> body of sin might be destroyed, that henceforth<small><small>[^19]</small></small> we should not serve sin. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he<small><small>[^20]</small></small> that is dead is freed<small><small>[^21]</small></small> from sin. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now<small><small>[^22]</small></small> if we be dead with Christ, we believe<small><small>[^23]</small></small> that we shall also live with him: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Knowing that Christ<small><small>[^24]</small></small> being raised from the dead dieth no more; death<small><small>[^25]</small></small> hath no more dominion over him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For in that he died, he died unto<small><small>[^26]</small></small> sin once: but in that he liveth, he liveth unto<small><small>[^27]</small></small> God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Likewise reckon<small><small>[^28]</small></small> ye also yourselves to be dead<small><small>[^29]</small></small> indeed unto sin, but<small><small>[^30]</small></small> alive unto God through<small><small>[^31]</small></small> Jesus Christ our Lord. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let not<small><small>[^32]</small></small> sin therefore reign in your mortal<small><small>[^33]</small></small> body, that ye should obey it in the lusts<small><small>[^34]</small></small> thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Neither<small><small>[^35]</small></small> yield ye your members \[as\] instruments<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of unrighteousness<small><small>[^37]</small></small> unto sin: but yield<small><small>[^38]</small></small> yourselves unto God, as those that are alive<small><small>[^39]</small></small> from the dead, and your<small><small>[^40]</small></small> members \[as\] instruments of righteousness unto God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For sin<small><small>[^41]</small></small> shall not have dominion over you: for ye<small><small>[^42]</small></small> are not under<small><small>[^43]</small></small> the law, but under grace.

<big><big>[[Rom.6.15 The Wages of Sin|⥃]]</big></big>***The Wages of Sin***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What<small><small>[^44]</small></small> then? shall we<small><small>[^45]</small></small> sin, because we are not under the law, but under grace? God forbid. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Know<small><small>[^46]</small></small> ye not, that to whom<small><small>[^47]</small></small> ye yield yourselves servants to obey, his servants ye are to whom ye obey; whether of sin<small><small>[^48]</small></small> unto death, or of obedience unto righteousness? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But<small><small>[^49]</small></small> God be thanked, that<small><small>[^50]</small></small> ye were the servants of sin, but ye<small><small>[^51]</small></small> have obeyed from the heart that form<small><small>[^52]</small></small> of doctrine which was delivered you<small><small>[^53]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Being then made<small><small>[^54]</small></small> free from sin, ye became the servants<small><small>[^55]</small></small> of righteousness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I speak<small><small>[^56]</small></small> after the manner of men because<small><small>[^57]</small></small> of the infirmity of your flesh: for as ye<small><small>[^58]</small></small> have yielded your members servants to uncleanness and to iniquity unto iniquity<small><small>[^59]</small></small>; even so now yield<small><small>[^60]</small></small> your members servants to righteousness unto holiness<small><small>[^61]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For when ye were the servants<small><small>[^62]</small></small> of sin, ye were free from<small><small>[^63]</small></small> righteousness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What<small><small>[^64]</small></small> fruit had ye then in those things whereof<small><small>[^65]</small></small> ye are now ashamed? for the<small><small>[^66]</small></small> end of those things \[is\] death. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But now<small><small>[^67]</small></small> being made free from sin, and become<small><small>[^68]</small></small> servants to God, ye have<small><small>[^69]</small></small> your fruit unto holiness, and the end<small><small>[^70]</small></small> everlasting life. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.6.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the wages<small><small>[^71]</small></small> of sin \[is\] death; but the<small><small>[^72]</small></small> gift of God \[is\] eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Rom.5|<< Romans 5]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Rom.7|Romans 7 >>]]</td></tr></table>
