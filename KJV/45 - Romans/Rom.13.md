<big><big>[[Rom.13.1 Submission to Authorities|⥃]]</big></big>***Submission to Authorities***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Pet.2|1 Peter 2:13–20]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let every<small><small>[^2]</small></small> soul be subject unto the higher powers. For there<small><small>[^3]</small></small> is no power but of God: the powers that be are ordained<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whosoever therefore resisteth the power<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, resisteth the ordinance<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of God: and they that resist shall receive<small><small>[^7]</small></small> to themselves damnation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For rulers<small><small>[^8]</small></small> are not a terror to good works, but to the evil. Wilt<small><small>[^9]</small></small> thou then not be afraid of the power? do that which is good, and thou shalt have praise of the same: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he is<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the minister of God to thee for good. But if thou do that which is evil, be<small><small>[^11]</small></small> afraid; for he beareth not the sword in vain: for he is the minister of God, a revenger<small><small>[^12]</small></small> to \[execute\] wrath upon him that doeth evil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore \[ye<small><small>[^13]</small></small>\] must needs be subject, not only for wrath, but also for conscience<small><small>[^14]</small></small> sake. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For for this cause pay<small><small>[^15]</small></small> ye tribute also: for they are God’s ministers, attending<small><small>[^16]</small></small> continually upon this very thing. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Render therefore<small><small>[^17]</small></small> to all their dues: tribute to whom tribute \[is due\]; custom to whom custom; fear to<small><small>[^18]</small></small> whom fear; honour to<small><small>[^19]</small></small> whom honour.

<big><big>[[Rom.13.8 Love Fulfills the Law|⥃]]</big></big>***Love Fulfills the Law***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Lev.19|Leviticus 19:9–18]] ]</sup></small>

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Owe<small><small>[^20]</small></small> no man any thing, but to love one another: for<small><small>[^21]</small></small> he that loveth another hath fulfilled the law. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For this<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, Thou shalt not commit adultery, Thou shalt not kill, Thou shalt not steal, Thou shalt not bear false witness, Thou shalt not covet<small><small>[^23]</small></small>; and if \[there be\] any other commandment, it is briefly comprehended in this saying, namely, Thou shalt love<small><small>[^24]</small></small> thy neighbour as thyself. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Love worketh<small><small>[^25]</small></small> no ill to his neighbour: therefore love \[is<small><small>[^26]</small></small>\] the fulfilling of the law.

<big><big>[[Rom.13.11 The Day Is Near|⥃]]</big></big>***The Day Is Near***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And that, knowing<small><small>[^27]</small></small> the time, that now \[it is<small><small>[^28]</small></small>\] high time to awake out of sleep: for now<small><small>[^29]</small></small> \[is\] our salvation nearer than when we believed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The night<small><small>[^30]</small></small> is far spent, the day is at hand: let us therefore cast<small><small>[^31]</small></small> off the works<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of darkness, and let us put<small><small>[^33]</small></small> on the armour of light. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let us<small><small>[^34]</small></small> walk honestly<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, as<small><small>[^36]</small></small> in the day; not in rioting<small><small>[^37]</small></small> and drunkenness, not in chambering<small><small>[^38]</small></small> and wantonness, not in strife<small><small>[^39]</small></small> and envying. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Rom.13.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But put<small><small>[^40]</small></small> ye on the Lord Jesus Christ, and<small><small>[^41]</small></small> make not provision for the flesh, to \[fulfil\] the lusts \[thereof\]. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Rom.12|<< Romans 12]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Rom.14|Romans 14 >>]]</td></tr></table>
