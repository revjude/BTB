<big><big>[[Heb.9.1 The Earthly Tabernacle|⥃]]</big></big>***The Earthly Tabernacle***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Exod.40|Exodus 40:1–33]] <big>†</big> [[Acts.7|Acts 7:44–47]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then verily the first<small><small>[^2]</small></small> \[covenant\] had<small><small>[^3]</small></small> also ordinances<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of divine service, and<small><small>[^5]</small></small> a worldly sanctuary. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For there was a tabernacle<small><small>[^6]</small></small> made; the first<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, wherein \[was\] the candlestick, and the table<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, and the shewbread<small><small>[^9]</small></small>; which is called the sanctuary<small><small>[^10]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And after the second<small><small>[^11]</small></small> veil, the tabernacle which is called the Holiest<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of all; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Which had the golden<small><small>[^13]</small></small> censer, and the ark<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of the covenant overlaid round about with gold, wherein \[was<small><small>[^15]</small></small>\] the golden pot that had manna, and Aaron’s<small><small>[^16]</small></small> rod that budded, and the<small><small>[^17]</small></small> tables of the covenant; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And over<small><small>[^18]</small></small> it the cherubims of glory shadowing the mercyseat<small><small>[^19]</small></small>; of which we cannot now speak particularly. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now when these things were thus ordained, the priests<small><small>[^20]</small></small> went always into the first tabernacle, accomplishing the service \[of God\].  <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But into<small><small>[^21]</small></small> the second \[went\] the high priest alone once every year, not<small><small>[^22]</small></small> without blood, which he offered for himself, and \[for\] the errors<small><small>[^23]</small></small> of the people: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The Holy Ghost<small><small>[^24]</small></small> this signifying, that the way<small><small>[^25]</small></small> into the holiest of all was not yet made manifest, while as the first tabernacle was yet standing: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Which \[was\] a figure<small><small>[^26]</small></small> for the time<small><small>[^27]</small></small> then present, in which were offered both gifts<small><small>[^28]</small></small> and sacrifices, that could<small><small>[^29]</small></small> not make him that did the service perfect, as pertaining<small><small>[^30]</small></small> to the conscience; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Which stood\] only in meats<small><small>[^31]</small></small> and drinks, and divers<small><small>[^32]</small></small> washings, and carnal<small><small>[^33]</small></small> ordinances<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, imposed \[on them\] until<small><small>[^35]</small></small> the time of reformation.

<big><big>[[Heb.9.11 Redemption through His Blood|⥃]]</big></big>***Redemption through His Blood***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But Christ<small><small>[^36]</small></small> being come an high priest<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of good<small><small>[^38]</small></small> things to come, by a greater<small><small>[^39]</small></small> and more perfect tabernacle, not made<small><small>[^40]</small></small> with hands, that is to say, not of this building; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Neither by the<small><small>[^41]</small></small> blood of goats and calves, but by his<small><small>[^42]</small></small> own blood he entered<small><small>[^43]</small></small> in once<small><small>[^44]</small></small> into the holy place, having<small><small>[^45]</small></small> obtained eternal redemption \[for us\].  <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For if<small><small>[^46]</small></small> the blood of bulls and<small><small>[^47]</small></small> of goats, and the ashes of an heifer sprinkling the unclean, sanctifieth to the purifying<small><small>[^48]</small></small> of the flesh: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> How<small><small>[^49]</small></small> much more shall the blood<small><small>[^50]</small></small> of Christ, who<small><small>[^51]</small></small> through the eternal<small><small>[^52]</small></small> Spirit offered<small><small>[^53]</small></small> himself without<small><small>[^54]</small></small> spot<small><small>[^55]</small></small> to God, God? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And for this cause he is the mediator<small><small>[^60]</small></small> of the new<small><small>[^61]</small></small> testament, that by means<small><small>[^62]</small></small> of death, for<small><small>[^63]</small></small> the redemption of the transgressions \[that were\] under the first<small><small>[^64]</small></small> testament, they which<small><small>[^65]</small></small> are called might receive the promise<small><small>[^66]</small></small> of eternal<small><small>[^67]</small></small> inheritance. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For where a testament \[is\], there must also of necessity be<small><small>[^68]</small></small> the death of the testator. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For a testament \[is\] of force after men are dead: otherwise it is of no strength at all while the testator liveth.<small><small>[^69]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.18|18]]**<span blood<small><small>[^72]</small></small> of calves and of goats, with water, and scarlet<small><small>[^73]</small></small> wool, and hyssop<small><small>[^74]</small></small>, and sprinkled<small><small>[^75]</small></small> both the book, and all the people, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Saying, class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover he sprinkled with blood both the tabernacle, and all the vessels of the ministry.<small><small>[^78]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And almost<small><small>[^79]</small></small> all things class="endversemarker">]</span> \[It was\] therefore necessary that the patterns<small><small>[^81]</small></small> of things in the heavens should be purified with these; but the heavenly<small><small>[^82]</small></small> things themselves with better sacrifices than these. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For Christ is not entered into the holy<small><small>[^83]</small></small> places made with hands, \[which are\] the figures<small><small>[^84]</small></small> of the true; but<small><small>[^85]</small></small> into heaven itself, now to appear<small><small>[^86]</small></small> in the presence of God for us: <span as<small><small>[^88]</small></small> the high priest entereth into the holy place every year with blood of others; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For then must he often have suffered since the foundation<small><small>[^89]</small></small> of the world: but now once in<small><small>[^90]</small></small> the end of the world hath he appeared<small><small>[^91]</small></small> to put away sin by the sacrifice<small><small>[^92]</small></small> of himself. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And as<small><small>[^93]</small></small> it is appointed unto men once to die, but<small><small>[^94]</small></small> after this the judgment: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.9.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So Christ was<small><small>[^95]</small></small> once offered to bear<small><small>[^96]</small></small> the sins of many; and unto them<small><small>[^97]</small></small> that look for him shall he
