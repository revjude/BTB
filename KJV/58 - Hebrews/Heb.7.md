<big><big>[[Heb.7.1 Melchizedek and Abraham|⥃]]</big></big>***Melchizedek and Abraham***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Gen.14|Genesis 14:17–24]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For this<small><small>[^2]</small></small> Melchisedec, king of Salem<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, priest of the most<small><small>[^4]</small></small> high God, who met Abraham returning from the slaughter<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of the kings, and blessed him; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To whom also Abraham gave a tenth<small><small>[^6]</small></small> part of all; first being by interpretation King of righteousness<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, and after that also King of Salem, which is, King of peace; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Without father<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, without mother, without descent<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, having neither beginning of days, nor end of life; but made like unto the Son of God; abideth a priest<small><small>[^10]</small></small> continually. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now consider how great this man \[was\], unto whom even the patriarch<small><small>[^11]</small></small> Abraham<small><small>[^12]</small></small> gave<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the tenth of the spoils. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And verily they that are of the sons of Levi, who<small><small>[^14]</small></small> receive the office of the priesthood, have a commandment to take<small><small>[^15]</small></small> tithes of the people according to the law, that is, of their brethren, though they come<small><small>[^16]</small></small> out of the loins of Abraham: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But he whose descent<small><small>[^17]</small></small> is not counted from them received<small><small>[^18]</small></small> tithes of Abraham, and blessed him that had<small><small>[^19]</small></small> the promises. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And without<small><small>[^20]</small></small> all contradiction the less<small><small>[^21]</small></small> is blessed of the better. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And here men<small><small>[^22]</small></small> that die receive tithes; but there he \[receiveth them\], of whom it is witnessed that he liveth<small><small>[^23]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And as I may so say, Levi also, who receiveth tithes, payed<small><small>[^24]</small></small> tithes in Abraham. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he was yet in the loins of his father, when Melchisedec met him.<small><small>[^25]</small></small>

<big><big>[[Heb.7.11 A Superior Priesthood|⥃]]</big></big>***A Superior Priesthood***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If therefore perfection<small><small>[^26]</small></small> were by the Levitical priesthood, (for under it the people received the law,) what<small><small>[^27]</small></small> further need \[was there\] that another<small><small>[^28]</small></small> priest should rise after the order of Melchisedec, and not be called after the order of Aaron? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the priesthood being changed, there is made of necessity a change<small><small>[^29]</small></small> also of the law. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he of whom these things are spoken pertaineth to another tribe, of which<small><small>[^30]</small></small> no man gave attendance at the altar. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For \[it is\] evident that our Lord<small><small>[^31]</small></small> sprang<small><small>[^32]</small></small> out of Juda; of which tribe Moses spake nothing concerning priesthood. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it is yet far more evident: for that after<small><small>[^33]</small></small> the similitude of Melchisedec there ariseth another priest, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who is made, not after the law<small><small>[^34]</small></small> of a carnal commandment, but after the power<small><small>[^35]</small></small> of an endless life. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he testifieth, Thou \[art\] a priest for ever after the order of Melchisedec.<small><small>[^36]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For there is verily a disannulling<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of the commandment going before for the weakness<small><small>[^38]</small></small> and unprofitableness thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the law<small><small>[^39]</small></small> made<small><small>[^40]</small></small> nothing perfect, but the bringing in<small><small>[^41]</small></small> of a better<small><small>[^42]</small></small> hope \[did\]; by the which we<small><small>[^43]</small></small> draw nigh unto God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And inasmuch as not without an oath \[he was made priest\]: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> (For those priests were made without an oath<small><small>[^44]</small></small>; but this with an oath by him that said unto him, The Lord<small><small>[^45]</small></small> sware<small><small>[^46]</small></small> and will not repent, Thou \[art\] a priest for ever after the order of Melchisedec:) <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> By so much was Jesus made a surety<small><small>[^47]</small></small> of a<small><small>[^48]</small></small> better testament<small><small>[^49]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they truly were<small><small>[^50]</small></small> many priests, because they were not suffered to continue by reason of death: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But<small><small>[^51]</small></small> this \[man\], because he continueth<small><small>[^52]</small></small> ever, hath<small><small>[^53]</small></small> an unchangeable priesthood<small><small>[^54]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore he is<small><small>[^55]</small></small> able also to save them to the uttermost<small><small>[^56]</small></small> that come<small><small>[^57]</small></small> unto God by him<small><small>[^58]</small></small>, seeing he ever<small><small>[^59]</small></small> liveth to make<small><small>[^60]</small></small> intercession for them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For such<small><small>[^61]</small></small> an high priest became<small><small>[^62]</small></small> us, \[who is\] holy<small><small>[^63]</small></small>, harmless, undefiled, separate from sinners, and made<small><small>[^64]</small></small> higher than the heavens; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who needeth not daily<small><small>[^65]</small></small>, as those high priests, to offer up sacrifice, first<small><small>[^66]</small></small> for his own sins, and then<small><small>[^67]</small></small> for the people’s: for this<small><small>[^68]</small></small> he did once, when he offered up himself. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.7.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the law maketh<small><small>[^69]</small></small> men high priests which have infirmity; but the word<small><small>[^70]</small></small> of the oath, which was since the law, \[maketh\] the<small><small>[^71]</small></small> Son, who<small><small>[^72]</small></small> is consecrated<small><small>[^73]</small></small> for evermore. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Heb.6|<< Hebrews 6]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Heb.8|Hebrews 8 >>]]</td></tr></table>
