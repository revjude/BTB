***Faith and Assurance***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Gen.1|Genesis 1:1–2]] <big>†</big> [[John.1|John 1:1–5]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb2948

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.11.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now faith<small><small>[^2]</small></small> is the<small><small>[^3]</small></small> substance<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of things hoped<small><small>[^5]</small></small> for, the evidence<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of things not seen. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.11.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For by it the elders<small><small>[^7]</small></small> obtained a good report. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.11.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Through faith<small><small>[^8]</small></small> we understand that the worlds were framed by the word of God, so that things which are seen were not made of things which do appear.

***The Faith of Abel, Enoch, Noah***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Gen.4—9|Genesis 4—9]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb2949

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.11.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> By faith<small><small>[^9]</small></small> Abel offered unto God a more<small><small>[^10]</small></small> excellent sacrifice than Cain, by which he obtained<small><small>[^11]</small></small> witness that he was righteous, God testifying of his gifts: and by<small><small>[^12]</small></small> it he being dead yet speaketh<small><small>[^13]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.11.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> By faith Enoch<small><small>[^14]</small></small> was translated<small><small>[^15]</small></small> that he should not see death; and was<small><small>[^16]</small></small> not found, because God had translated him: for before his translation he had this testimony<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, that he<small><small>[^18]</small></small> pleased God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.11.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But without<small><small>[^19]</small></small> faith \[it is\] impossible to please \[him\]: for he that<small><small>[^20]</small></small> cometh to God must<small><small>[^21]</small></small> believe that he is, and \[that\] he is a rewarder<small><small>[^22]</small></small> of them that diligently<small><small>[^23]</small></small> seek him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.11.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> By faith Noah<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, being warned<small><small>[^25]</small></small> of God of things<small><small>[^26]</small></small> not seen as yet, moved with fear<small><small>[^27]</small></small>, prepared<small><small>[^28]</small></small> an ark to the saving of his house; by the which he condemned<small><small>[^29]</small></small> the world, and became heir of the righteousness<small><small>[^30]</small></small> which is by faith.

the<small><small>[^64]</small></small> dead; from whence also he received him in a figure.

***The Faith of Isaac, Jacob, and Joseph***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Gen.27—50|Genesis 27—50]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb2951

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Heb.11.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> By faith<small><small>[^70]</small></small> Moses, when he was born, was hid three months of his parents, because they saw \[he was\] a proper child<small><small>[^71]</small></small>;
