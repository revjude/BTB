***The Razor of Judgment*** ^bsb1833

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou, son<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of man, take<small><small>[^3]</small></small> thee a sharp knife, take thee a barber’s razor, and cause \[it\] to pass upon thine head and upon thy beard: then<small><small>[^4]</small></small> take thee balances to weigh, and divide the \[hair\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt burn<small><small>[^5]</small></small> with fire a third part in the midst of the city<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, when the days of the siege are fulfilled: and thou shalt take a third part, \[and\] smite about it with a knife: and a third part thou shalt scatter in the wind; and I will draw<small><small>[^7]</small></small> out a sword after them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt also take thereof a few<small><small>[^8]</small></small> in number, and bind them in thy skirts<small><small>[^9]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then take<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of them again, and cast them into the midst of the fire, and burn them in the fire; \[for\] thereof shall a fire<small><small>[^11]</small></small> come forth into all the house of Israel. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; This<small><small>[^12]</small></small> \[is\] Jerusalem: I have<small><small>[^13]</small></small> set it in the midst of the nations and countries \[that are\] round about her. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And she hath<small><small>[^14]</small></small> changed my judgments into wickedness more than the nations, and my statutes more than the countries that \[are\] round about her: for they<small><small>[^15]</small></small> have refused my judgments and my statutes, they have not walked in them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Because ye multiplied more than the nations that \[are\] round about you, \[and\] have not walked in my statutes, neither have kept my judgments, neither have done<small><small>[^16]</small></small> according to the judgments of the nations that \[are\] round about you; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Behold, I, even I<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, \[am\] against thee, and will execute judgments in the<small><small>[^18]</small></small> midst of thee in the sight of the nations. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will do in thee that which<small><small>[^19]</small></small> I have not done, and whereunto I will not do any more the like, because of all thine abominations. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore the fathers<small><small>[^20]</small></small> shall eat the sons in the midst of thee, and the sons shall eat their fathers; and I will execute judgments in thee, and the whole<small><small>[^21]</small></small> remnant of thee will I scatter into all the winds.

***Famine, Sword, and Dispersion*** ^bsb1834

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore, \[as\] I live<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Surely, because thou hast<small><small>[^23]</small></small> defiled my sanctuary with all thy detestable<small><small>[^24]</small></small> things, and with all thine abominations, therefore will I<small><small>[^25]</small></small> also diminish \[thee\]; neither shall<small><small>[^26]</small></small> mine eye spare, neither will I have any pity. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]A third part of<small><small>[^27]</small></small> thee shall die with the pestilence, and with famine shall they be consumed in the midst of thee: and a third part shall fall by the sword round about thee; and I will scatter<small><small>[^28]</small></small> a third part into all the winds, and I will draw<small><small>[^29]</small></small> out a sword after them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus shall mine<small><small>[^30]</small></small> anger be accomplished, and I will cause<small><small>[^31]</small></small> my fury to rest upon them, and I will be<small><small>[^32]</small></small> comforted: and they shall know that I the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> have spoken<small><small>[^33]</small></small> \[it\] in my zeal, when I have accomplished my fury in them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover I will<small><small>[^34]</small></small> make thee waste, and a reproach among the nations<small><small>[^35]</small></small> that \[are\] round about thee, in the sight of all that pass by. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So it shall be a reproach and a taunt, an instruction<small><small>[^36]</small></small> and an astonishment unto the nations that \[are\] round about thee, when<small><small>[^37]</small></small> I shall execute judgments in thee in anger and in fury and in furious rebukes. I the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> have spoken \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When I shall send upon them the evil<small><small>[^38]</small></small> arrows of famine, which shall be for \[their\] destruction, \[and\] which I will send to destroy you: and I will increase the famine upon you, and will<small><small>[^39]</small></small> break your staff of bread: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.5.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So will I send upon you famine and evil<small><small>[^40]</small></small> beasts, and they shall bereave thee; and pestilence<small><small>[^41]</small></small> and blood shall pass through thee; and I<small><small>[^42]</small></small> will bring the sword upon thee. [^2]: In this expressive emblem, the prophet represents the Jewish nation; his hair, the people; the razor, the Chaldeans; the cutting of the hair, the calamities and disgrace coming upon them; the balances, the exact distribution of the Divine judgments; the third part of the hair burnt, those destroyed in the city; the third part smitten with a knife, those slain in attempting to escape; the third part scattered to the winds, those who escaped to other countries; the few hairs in his skirt, those left with Gedaliah; and the burning of these, their destruction in Egypt.
