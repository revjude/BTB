***The Desolation of Israel*** ^bsb1838

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.7.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They have<small><small>[^41]</small></small> blown the trumpet, even to make all ready; but none goeth to the battle: for<small><small>[^42]</small></small> my wrath \[is\] upon all the multitude thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.7.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The sword \[is\] without, and the pestilence and the famine within: he that \[is\] in the field shall die with the sword; and he that \[is\] in the city, famine and pestilence shall devour him.<small><small>[^43]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.7.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But they<small><small>[^44]</small></small> that escape of them shall escape, and shall be on the mountains like<small><small>[^45]</small></small> doves of the valleys, all of them mourning<small><small>[^46]</small></small>, every one for his iniquity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.7.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All hands<small><small>[^47]</small></small> shall be feeble, and all knees shall be weak \[as\] water<small><small>[^48]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.7.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They shall also<small><small>[^49]</small></small> gird \[themselves\] with sackcloth, and horror<small><small>[^50]</small></small> shall cover them; and shame \[shall be\] upon all faces, and baldness upon all their heads. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.7.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They shall cast<small><small>[^51]</small></small> their silver in the streets, and their gold shall be removed<small><small>[^52]</small></small>: their silver and their gold shall not be able to deliver them in the day of the wrath of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: they shall not<small><small>[^53]</small></small> satisfy their souls, neither fill their bowels: because it is the stumblingblock of their iniquity<small><small>[^54]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.7.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]As for the beauty<small><small>[^55]</small></small> of his ornament, he set it in majesty: but<small><small>[^56]</small></small> they made the images of their abominations \[and\] of their detestable things therein: therefore have I set it far from them<small><small>[^57]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.7.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will give it into the hands of the strangers for a prey, and to the wicked of the earth for a spoil; and they shall pollute it.<small><small>[^58]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.7.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My face<small><small>[^59]</small></small> will I turn also from them, and they shall pollute my secret \[place\]: for the robbers<small><small>[^60]</small></small> shall enter into it, and defile it. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.7.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Make a chain<small><small>[^61]</small></small>: for<small><small>[^62]</small></small> the land is full of bloody crimes, and the city is full of violence. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.7.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore I will bring<small><small>[^63]</small></small> the worst of the heathen, and they shall<small><small>[^64]</small></small> possess their houses: I will also<small><small>[^65]</small></small> make the pomp<small><small>[^66]</small></small> of the strong to cease;
