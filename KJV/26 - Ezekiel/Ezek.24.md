***The Parable of the Cooking Pot*** ^bsb1871

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Again in the ninth year<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, in the tenth month, in the tenth \[day\] of the month, the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,<small><small>[^3]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son of man, write<small><small>[^4]</small></small> thee the name of the day, \[even\] of this<small><small>[^5]</small></small> same day: the king of Babylon set himself against Jerusalem this same day. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And utter<small><small>[^6]</small></small> a parable unto the rebellious<small><small>[^7]</small></small> house, and say unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Set<small><small>[^8]</small></small> on a pot, set \[it\] on, and also pour water into it: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Gather the pieces thereof into it, \[even\] every good piece, the thigh, and the shoulder; fill \[it\] with the choice bones.<small><small>[^9]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Take the choice<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of the flock, and burn<small><small>[^11]</small></small> also the bones under it, \[and\] make it boil well, and let them seethe the bones of it therein. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Wherefore thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Woe<small><small>[^12]</small></small> to the bloody city, to the pot<small><small>[^13]</small></small> whose scum \[is\] therein, and whose scum is not gone out of it! bring<small><small>[^14]</small></small> it out piece by piece; let no<small><small>[^15]</small></small> lot fall upon it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For her blood<small><small>[^16]</small></small> is in the midst of her; she set it upon the top of a rock; she poured<small><small>[^17]</small></small> it not upon the ground, to cover it with dust; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That it might<small><small>[^18]</small></small> cause fury to come up to take vengeance; I have set<small><small>[^19]</small></small> her blood upon the top of a rock, that it should not be covered. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Woe<small><small>[^20]</small></small> to the bloody city! I will<small><small>[^21]</small></small> even make the pile for fire great. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Heap on wood, kindle the fire, consume the flesh, and spice<small><small>[^22]</small></small> it well, and let the bones be burned. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then set it<small><small>[^23]</small></small> empty upon the coals thereof, that the brass of it may be hot, and may burn, and \[that\] the filthiness<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of it may be molten in it, \[that\] the scum of it may be consumed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She hath wearied<small><small>[^25]</small></small> \[herself\] with lies, and her great<small><small>[^26]</small></small> scum went not forth out of her: her scum<small><small>[^27]</small></small> \[shall be\] in the fire. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In thy filthiness<small><small>[^28]</small></small> \[is\] lewdness: because<small><small>[^29]</small></small> I have purged thee, and thou wast not purged, thou shalt not be purged from thy filthiness any more, till I<small><small>[^30]</small></small> have caused my fury to rest upon thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> have spoken \[it\]: it shall come to pass, and I will do \[it\]; I will not go back, neither will I spare, neither will I repent; according to thy ways, and according to thy doings, shall they judge thee, saith the Lord<small><small>[^31]</small></small> <span class="smallcaps">God</span>.<small><small>[^32]</small></small><small><small>[^33]</small></small>

***Ezekiel’s Wife Dies*** ^bsb1872

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son of man, behold, I take away from thee the desire<small><small>[^34]</small></small> of thine eyes with a<small><small>[^35]</small></small> stroke: yet<small><small>[^36]</small></small> neither shalt thou mourn nor weep, neither shall thy tears<small><small>[^37]</small></small> run<small><small>[^38]</small></small> down. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Forbear to cry<small><small>[^39]</small></small>, make<small><small>[^40]</small></small> no mourning for the dead, bind<small><small>[^41]</small></small> the tire of thine head upon thee, and put<small><small>[^42]</small></small> on thy shoes upon thy feet, and cover<small><small>[^43]</small></small> not \[thy\] lips<small><small>[^44]</small></small>, and eat<small><small>[^45]</small></small> not the bread of men<small><small>[^46]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So I spake unto the people in the morning: and at<small><small>[^47]</small></small> even my wife died; and I did in the morning as I was commanded.

Behold, I will<small><small>[^49]</small></small> profane my sanctuary, the excellency<small><small>[^50]</small></small> of your strength, the desire<small><small>[^51]</small></small> of your eyes, and that which your soul pitieth<small><small>[^52]</small></small>; and your sons and your daughters whom ye have left shall fall by the sword. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And ye shall do as I have done: ye shall not cover \[your\] lips, nor eat the bread of men.<small><small>[^53]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And your tires \[shall be\] upon your heads, and your shoes upon your feet: ye shall not mourn nor weep; but<small><small>[^54]</small></small> ye shall pine away for your iniquities, and mourn<small><small>[^55]</small></small> one toward another. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus Ezekiel<small><small>[^56]</small></small> is unto you a sign: according to all that he hath done shall ye do: and when<small><small>[^57]</small></small> this cometh, ye shall<small><small>[^58]</small></small> know that I \[am\] the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also, thou son of man, \[shall it\] not \[be\] in the day when I take from them their strength<small><small>[^59]</small></small>, the joy of their glory, the desire of their eyes, and that whereupon they set their minds<small><small>[^60]</small></small>, their sons<small><small>[^61]</small></small> and their daughters, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[That\] he that escapeth in that day shall come unto thee, to cause \[thee\] to hear \[it\] with \[thine\] ears?<small><small>[^62]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.24.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In that day shall thy<small><small>[^63]</small></small> mouth be opened to him which is escaped, and thou shalt speak, and be no more dumb: and thou shalt be<small><small>[^64]</small></small> a sign unto them;
