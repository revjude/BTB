***Egypt Will Fall like Assyria*** ^bsb1888

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass in the eleventh<small><small>[^2]</small></small> year, in the third \[month\], in the first \[day\] of the month, \[that\] the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son of man, speak<small><small>[^3]</small></small> unto Pharaoh king of Egypt, and to his<small><small>[^4]</small></small> multitude; Whom<small><small>[^5]</small></small> art thou like in thy greatness? 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Behold, the Assyrian<small><small>[^6]</small></small> \[was\] a cedar<small><small>[^7]</small></small> in Lebanon with fair branches<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, and with a shadowing shroud, and of an high<small><small>[^9]</small></small> stature; and his top was among the thick boughs. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The waters<small><small>[^10]</small></small> made him great<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, the deep set<small><small>[^12]</small></small> him up on high with her rivers running round about his plants, and sent out her little rivers unto all the trees of the field. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore his height<small><small>[^13]</small></small> was exalted above all the trees of the field, and his boughs were multiplied, and his branches became long because of the multitude of waters, when he shot forth<small><small>[^14]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All the fowls of heaven made their nests in his boughs, and under his branches did all the beasts of the field bring forth their young, and under his shadow dwelt all great nations.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus was he fair in his greatness, in the length of his branches: for his root was by great waters. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The cedars<small><small>[^16]</small></small> in the garden of God could not hide him: the fir trees were not like his boughs, and the chestnut trees were not like his branches; nor any<small><small>[^17]</small></small> tree in the garden of God was like unto him in his beauty. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have made<small><small>[^18]</small></small> him fair by the multitude of his branches: so that all the trees<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of Eden, that \[were\] in the garden of God, envied<small><small>[^20]</small></small> him. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore<small><small>[^21]</small></small> thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Because<small><small>[^22]</small></small> thou hast lifted up thyself in height, and he hath shot up his top among the thick boughs, and his<small><small>[^23]</small></small> heart is lifted up in his height; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have therefore delivered<small><small>[^24]</small></small> him into the hand of the mighty<small><small>[^25]</small></small> one of the heathen; he shall surely deal with him<small><small>[^26]</small></small>: I have driven<small><small>[^27]</small></small> him out for his wickedness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And strangers<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, the terrible of the nations, have cut him off, and have left him: upon<small><small>[^29]</small></small> the mountains and in all the valleys his branches are fallen, and his boughs are broken by all the rivers of the land; and all the people of the earth are gone<small><small>[^30]</small></small> down from his shadow, and have left him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Upon his ruin shall all the fowls of the heaven remain, and all the beasts of the field shall be upon his branches:<small><small>[^31]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To the end<small><small>[^32]</small></small> that none of all the trees by the waters exalt themselves for their height, neither shoot up their top among the thick boughs, neither their trees stand up in their height<small><small>[^33]</small></small>, all that drink water: for they are all delivered<small><small>[^34]</small></small> unto death, to the nether<small><small>[^35]</small></small> parts of the earth, in the midst of the children of men, with them that go down to the pit. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; In the day when he went down to the grave I caused a<small><small>[^36]</small></small> mourn<small><small>[^37]</small></small>ing: I covered the deep for him, and I restrained the floods thereof, and the great waters were stayed: and I caused Lebanon to mourn for him, and all the trees of the field fainted for him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I made<small><small>[^38]</small></small> the nations to shake at the sound of his fall, when I<small><small>[^39]</small></small> cast him down to hell with them that descend into the pit: and all<small><small>[^40]</small></small> the trees of Eden, the choice and best of Lebanon, all that drink water, shall be comforted<small><small>[^41]</small></small> in the nether parts of the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They also went<small><small>[^42]</small></small> down into hell with him unto \[them that be\] slain with the sword; and \[they that were<small><small>[^43]</small></small>\] his arm, \[that\] dwelt<small><small>[^44]</small></small> under his shadow in the midst of the heathen. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.31.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]To whom<small><small>[^45]</small></small> art thou<small><small>[^46]</small></small> thus like in glory and in greatness among the trees of Eden? yet shalt thou be brought down with the<small><small>[^47]</small></small> trees of Eden unto the nether parts of the earth: thou shalt<small><small>[^48]</small></small> lie in the midst of the uncircumcised with \[them that be\] slain by the sword.
