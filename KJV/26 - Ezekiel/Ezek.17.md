***The Parable of Two Eagles and a Vine***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Matt.13|Matthew 13:24–30]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1855

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son of man, put forth a riddle, and speak a parable unto the house of Israel;<small><small>[^2]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; A great<small><small>[^3]</small></small> eagle with great wings<small><small>[^4]</small></small>, longwinged, full<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of feathers, which had divers colours<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, came<small><small>[^7]</small></small> unto Lebanon, and took the highest<small><small>[^8]</small></small> branch of the cedar<small><small>[^9]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He cropped off the top<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of his young twigs, and carried it into<small><small>[^11]</small></small> a land<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of traffick; he set it in a city<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of merchants. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He took also of the seed<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of the land, and planted it in a fruitful field<small><small>[^15]</small></small>; he placed<small><small>[^16]</small></small> \[it\] by great waters, \[and\] set it \[as\] a willow tree. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it grew<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, and became a spreading vine of low stature, whose<small><small>[^18]</small></small> branches turned toward him, and the roots thereof were under him: so it became a vine, and brought forth branches, and shot forth sprigs. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There was also another<small><small>[^19]</small></small> great eagle with great wings and many feathers: and, behold, this vine did bend<small><small>[^20]</small></small> her roots toward him, and shot forth her branches toward him, that he might water it by the furrows of her plantation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> It was planted in a good soil<small><small>[^21]</small></small> by great waters, that it might bring forth branches, and that<small><small>[^22]</small></small> it might bear fruit, that it might be a goodly vine. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Say thou, Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Shall it<small><small>[^23]</small></small> prosper? shall he<small><small>[^24]</small></small> not pull up the roots thereof, and cut off the fruit<small><small>[^25]</small></small> thereof, that it wither? it shall wither in all the leaves of her spring, even<small><small>[^26]</small></small> without great power or many people to pluck it up by the roots thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea, behold, \[being\] planted, shall it<small><small>[^27]</small></small> prosper? shall it not utterly wither, when the east wind toucheth it? it shall wither in the furrows where it grew.

***The Parable Explained*** ^bsb1856

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Moreover the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Say now to the<small><small>[^28]</small></small> rebellious house, Know<small><small>[^29]</small></small> ye not what these \[things mean\]? tell \[them\], Behold<small><small>[^30]</small></small>, the king of Babylon is come to Jerusalem, and hath taken the king thereof, and the princes thereof, and led<small><small>[^31]</small></small> them with him to Babylon; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And hath taken<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of the king’s seed, and made a covenant with him, and hath taken an oath of him<small><small>[^33]</small></small>: he hath also<small><small>[^34]</small></small> taken the mighty of the land: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That the kingdom<small><small>[^35]</small></small> might be base<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, that it might not lift itself up, \[but\] that by keeping of his covenant it might stand<small><small>[^37]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But he rebelled<small><small>[^38]</small></small> again<small><small>[^39]</small></small>st him in sending his ambassadors into Egypt, that they might give him horses and much people. Shall he prosper<small><small>[^40]</small></small>? shall he escape<small><small>[^41]</small></small> that doeth such \[things\]? or shall<small><small>[^42]</small></small> he break the covenant, and be delivered? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[As\] I live, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>, surely in the place \[where\] the king \[dwelleth\] that made him king, whose oath<small><small>[^43]</small></small> he despised, and whose covenant he brake, \[even<small><small>[^44]</small></small>\] with him in the midst of Babylon he shall die. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Neither shall<small><small>[^45]</small></small> Pharaoh with \[his\] mighty army and great company make for him in the war, by<small><small>[^46]</small></small> casting up mounts, and building forts, to cut off many persons: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Seeing<small><small>[^47]</small></small> he<small><small>[^48]</small></small> despised the oath by breaking the covenant, when, lo, he had given his hand, and hath done all these \[things\], he shall<small><small>[^49]</small></small> not escape. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; \[As\] I live, surely<small><small>[^50]</small></small> mine oath that he hath despised, and my covenant that he hath broken, even it will I recompense upon his own head. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will spread<small><small>[^51]</small></small> my net upon him, and he shall be taken in my snare, and I will bring him to Babylon, and will plead<small><small>[^52]</small></small> with him there for his trespass that he hath trespassed against me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all his fugitives<small><small>[^53]</small></small> with all his bands shall fall by the sword, and they that remain shall be scattered toward all winds: and ye shall know<small><small>[^54]</small></small> that I the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> have spoken \[it\]. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; I will also take of the highest<small><small>[^55]</small></small> branch of the high cedar, and will set \[it\]; I will crop off from the top of his young twigs a tender<small><small>[^56]</small></small> one, and will plant \[it\] upon<small><small>[^57]</small></small> an high mountain and eminent: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the mountain of the height of Israel will I plant it: and it<small><small>[^58]</small></small> shall bring forth boughs, and bear fruit, and be a goodly cedar: and under<small><small>[^59]</small></small> it shall dwell all fowl of every wing; in the shadow of the branches thereof shall they dwell. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.17.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all the trees<small><small>[^60]</small></small> of the field shall know that I the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> have brought<small><small>[^61]</small></small> down the high tree, have exalted the low tree, have dried up the green tree, and have made the dry tree to flourish: I the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> have spoken and have done \[it\]. <small><small>[^62]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ezek.16|<< Ezekiel 16]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ezek.18|Ezekiel 18 >>]]</td></tr></table>
