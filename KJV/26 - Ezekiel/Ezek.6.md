 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.6.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the word<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.6.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son of man, set<small><small>[^3]</small></small> thy face toward the mountains<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of Israel, and prophesy against them, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.6.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And say, Ye<small><small>[^5]</small></small> mountains of Israel, hear the word of the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span> to the mountains<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, and to the hills, to the rivers, and to the valleys; Behold, I, \[even\] I, will bring a sword upon you, and I will<small><small>[^7]</small></small> destroy your high places. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.6.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And your altars shall be desolate, and your images<small><small>[^8]</small></small> shall be broken: and I<small><small>[^9]</small></small> will cast down your slain \[men\] before your idols. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.6.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will lay<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the dead carcases of the children of Israel before their idols; that your altars<small><small>[^14]</small></small> may be laid waste and made desolate, and your idols may be broken and cease, and your images may be cut down, and your works<small><small>[^15]</small></small> may be abolished. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.6.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the slain<small><small>[^16]</small></small> shall fall in the midst of you, and ye<small><small>[^17]</small></small> shall know that I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.

***A Remnant to Be Blessed*** ^bsb1836

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.6.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet will I leave a remnant, that ye may have \[some\] that shall escape the sword among the nations, when ye shall be scattered through the countries.<small><small>[^18]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.6.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they that escape of you shall remember<small><small>[^19]</small></small> me among the nations whither they shall be carried captives, because I am<small><small>[^20]</small></small> broken with their whorish heart, which hath departed from me, and with their eyes<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, which go a whoring after their idols: and they shall<small><small>[^22]</small></small> lothe themselves for the evils which they have committed in all their abominations. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.6.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they shall know that I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[and that\] I have not said in vain that I would do this evil unto them.<small><small>[^23]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.6.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Smite<small><small>[^24]</small></small> with thine hand, and stamp with thy foot, and say, Alas<small><small>[^25]</small></small> for all the evil abominations of the house of Israel! for they shall fall<small><small>[^26]</small></small> by the sword, by the famine, and by the pestilence. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.6.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that is far off<small><small>[^27]</small></small> shall die of the pestilence; and he that is near shall fall by the sword; and he that remaineth and is besieged shall die by the famine: thus<small><small>[^28]</small></small> will I accomplish my fury upon them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.6.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shall ye know that I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, when<small><small>[^29]</small></small> their slain \[men\] shall be among their idols round about their altars, upon<small><small>[^30]</small></small> every high hill, in all the tops of the mountains, and under every green tree, and under every thick oak, the place where they did offer sweet savour to all their idols. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.6.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So will I<small><small>[^31]</small></small> stretch out my hand upon them, and make the land desolate, yea, more desolate than the wilderness<small><small>[^32]</small></small> toward Diblath<small><small>[^33]</small></small>, in all their habitations: and they shall know that I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ezek.5|<< Ezekiel 5]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ezek.7|Ezekiel 7 >>]]</td></tr></table>
