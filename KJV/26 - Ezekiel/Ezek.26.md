<big><big>[[Ezek.26.1 A Prophecy against Tyre|⥃]]</big></big>***A Prophecy against Tyre***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Isa.23|Isaiah 23:1–18]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass in the eleventh year, in the first \[day\] of the month, \[that\] the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,<small><small>[^2]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son of man, because that Tyrus<small><small>[^3]</small></small> hath said against Jerusalem, Aha<small><small>[^4]</small></small>, she is broken \[that was\] the gates<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of the people: she is<small><small>[^6]</small></small> turned unto me: I shall be replenished, \[now\] she is laid waste: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Behold<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, I \[am<small><small>[^8]</small></small>\] against thee, O Tyrus, and will cause many<small><small>[^9]</small></small> nations to come up against thee, as the sea<small><small>[^10]</small></small> causeth his waves to come up. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they shall destroy<small><small>[^11]</small></small> the walls of Tyrus, and break down her towers: I<small><small>[^12]</small></small> will also scrape her dust from her, and make<small><small>[^13]</small></small> her like the top of a rock. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> It shall be \[a place for\] the spreading<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of nets in the midst of the sea: for I have spoken \[it\], saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>: and it<small><small>[^15]</small></small> shall become a spoil to the nations. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And her daughters<small><small>[^16]</small></small> which \[are\] in the field shall be slain by the sword; and they<small><small>[^17]</small></small> shall know that I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]For thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Behold, I will<small><small>[^18]</small></small> bring upon Tyrus Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon, a king<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of kings, from the north, with mount<small><small>[^22]</small></small> against thee, and lift up the buckler against thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he shall set engines of war against thy walls, and with his axes he shall break down thy towers.<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> By reason of the abundance<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of his horses their dust shall cover thee: thy walls shall shake<small><small>[^25]</small></small> at the noise of the horsemen, and of the wheels, and of the chariots, when he shall enter<small><small>[^26]</small></small> into thy gates, as men enter into a city wherein is made a breach<small><small>[^27]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> With the hoofs<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of his horses shall he tread down all thy<small><small>[^29]</small></small> streets: he shall slay thy people by the sword, and thy strong garrisons shall go down to the ground. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they shall make a spoil<small><small>[^30]</small></small> of thy riches, and make a prey of thy merchandise<small><small>[^31]</small></small>: and they shall break down thy walls, and destroy thy pleasant houses<small><small>[^32]</small></small>: and they shall lay<small><small>[^33]</small></small> thy stones and thy timber and thy dust in the midst of the water. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will cause the noise of thy songs to cease; and the sound of thy harps shall be no more heard.<small><small>[^34]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will<small><small>[^35]</small></small> make thee like<small><small>[^36]</small></small> the top of a rock: thou shalt be \[a place\] to spread nets upon; thou shalt be built<small><small>[^37]</small></small> no more: for I<small><small>[^38]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span> to Tyrus; Shall not the isles shake<small><small>[^39]</small></small> at the sound of thy fall, when the wounded cry, when the slaughter is made in the midst of thee? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then all the princes<small><small>[^40]</small></small> of the sea shall come<small><small>[^41]</small></small> down from their thrones, and lay away their robes, and put off their broidered garments: they shall clothe<small><small>[^42]</small></small> themselves with trembling<small><small>[^43]</small></small>; they shall sit<small><small>[^44]</small></small> upon the ground, and shall tremble<small><small>[^45]</small></small> at \[every\] moment, and be astonished<small><small>[^46]</small></small> at thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they shall take<small><small>[^47]</small></small> up a lamentation for thee, and say to thee, How art<small><small>[^48]</small></small> thou destroyed, \[that wast\] inhabited of seafaring men<small><small>[^49]</small></small>, the renowned city, which wast strong<small><small>[^50]</small></small> in the sea, she and her inhabitants, which cause their terror \[to be\] on all that haunt it! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now shall the isles tremble<small><small>[^51]</small></small> in the day of thy fall; yea, the isles that \[are\] in the sea shall be troubled at thy<small><small>[^52]</small></small> departure. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; When I shall make thee a desolate city, like the cities that are not inhabited; when I shall bring<small><small>[^53]</small></small> up the deep upon thee, and great waters shall cover thee; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When I shall bring<small><small>[^54]</small></small> thee down with them that descend into the pit, with the people of old time, and shall set thee in the low parts of the earth, in places<small><small>[^55]</small></small> desolate of old, with them that go down to the pit, that thou be not inhabited; and I shall set<small><small>[^56]</small></small> glory in the land<small><small>[^57]</small></small> of the living; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.26.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will make thee a terror<small><small>[^58]</small></small>, and thou \[shalt be\] no \[more\]: though<small><small>[^59]</small></small> thou be sought for, yet shalt thou never be found again, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ezek.25|<< Ezekiel 25]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ezek.27|Ezekiel 27 >>]]</td></tr></table>

[^7]: [[Ezek.26|Ezek 26:3-6]] contain a summary prediction of what befell both the continental and insular Tyre, during a long succession of ages. The former was totally destroyed by Nebuchadnezzar, after a siege of thirteen years, bc 573; and the latter, which arose out of its ruins, after seventy years recovered its ancient wealth and splendour, as foretold by Isaiah ([[Isa.23|Isa 23:15-17]]). After it was taken and burnt by Alexander, bc 332, it speedily recovered its strength and dignity, and nineteen years afterwards withstood both the fleets and armies of Antigonus. Agreeably to the prophetic declarations ([[Ps.45|Ps 45:12]]; [[Ps.72|Ps 72:10]]. [[Isa.23|Isa 23:18]]. [[Zech.9|Zech 9:1-7]]), it was early converted to Christianity; and after being successively taken by the Saracens, Christians, Mamalukes, and Turks, in whose hands it still remains, it became "a place for the spreading of nets."
