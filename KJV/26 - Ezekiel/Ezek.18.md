<big><big>[[Ezek.18.1 The Soul Who Sins Will Die|⥃]]</big></big>***The Soul Who Sins Will Die***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me again, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What mean<small><small>[^2]</small></small> ye, that ye use this proverb concerning the land<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of Israel, saying, The fathers<small><small>[^4]</small></small> have eaten sour grapes, and the children’s teeth are set on edge? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[As\] I live, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>, ye shall not have \[occasion\] any more to use this proverb in Israel.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, all souls<small><small>[^6]</small></small> are mine; as the soul of the father, so also the soul of the son is mine: the soul that<small><small>[^7]</small></small> sinneth, it shall die. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]But if<small><small>[^8]</small></small> a man be just, and do that<small><small>[^9]</small></small> which is lawful and right, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[And\] hath not<small><small>[^10]</small></small> eaten upon the mountains, neither hath lifted<small><small>[^11]</small></small> up his eyes to the idols of the house of Israel, neither hath defiled<small><small>[^12]</small></small> his neighbour’s wife, neither hath come near to a menstruous woman, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And hath not<small><small>[^13]</small></small> oppressed any, \[but\] hath restored<small><small>[^14]</small></small> to the debtor his pledge, hath spoiled<small><small>[^15]</small></small> none by violence, hath given<small><small>[^16]</small></small> his bread to the hungry, and hath covered the naked with a garment; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He \[that\] hath not<small><small>[^17]</small></small> given forth upon usury, neither hath taken any increase, \[that\] hath withdrawn<small><small>[^18]</small></small> his hand from iniquity, hath executed<small><small>[^19]</small></small> true judgment between man and man, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hath walked<small><small>[^20]</small></small> in my statutes, and hath kept my judgments, to deal truly; he \[is\] just<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, he shall<small><small>[^22]</small></small> surely live, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If he beget a son \[that is<small><small>[^23]</small></small>\] a robber<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, a shedder<small><small>[^25]</small></small> of blood, and \[that\] doeth the like to \[any\] one of these \[things<small><small>[^26]</small></small>\], <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And that<small><small>[^27]</small></small> doeth not any of those \[duties\], but even hath eaten<small><small>[^28]</small></small> upon the mountains, and defiled his neighbour’s wife, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hath oppressed<small><small>[^29]</small></small> the poor and needy, hath spoiled by violence, hath not restored the pledge, and hath lifted up his eyes to the idols, hath committed<small><small>[^30]</small></small> abomination, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hath given<small><small>[^31]</small></small> forth upon usury, and hath taken increase: shall he<small><small>[^32]</small></small> then live? he shall not live: he hath done all these abominations; he shall surely die; his blood<small><small>[^33]</small></small> shall be upon him. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Now, lo, \[if\] he<small><small>[^34]</small></small> beget a son, that seeth<small><small>[^35]</small></small> all his father’s sins which he hath done, and considereth<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, and doeth not such like, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[That\] hath not eaten upon the mountains, neither hath lifted up his eyes to the idols of the house of Israel, hath not defiled his neighbour’s wife,<small><small>[^37]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Neither hath oppressed any, hath not withholden the pledge<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, neither hath spoiled by violence, \[but\] hath<small><small>[^39]</small></small> given his bread to the hungry, and hath covered the naked with a garment, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[That\] hath taken<small><small>[^40]</small></small> off his hand from the poor, \[that\] hath not<small><small>[^41]</small></small> received usury nor increase, hath executed my judgments, hath walked in my statutes; he shall not<small><small>[^42]</small></small> die for the iniquity of his father, he shall surely<small><small>[^43]</small></small> live. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[As for\] his father, because he cruelly oppressed, spoiled his brother by violence, and did \[that\] which \[is\] not good among his people, lo, even<small><small>[^44]</small></small> he shall die in his iniquity. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Yet say ye, Why<small><small>[^45]</small></small>? doth not the son bear the iniquity of the father? When<small><small>[^46]</small></small> the son hath done that which is lawful and right, \[and\] hath kept all my statutes, and hath done them, he shall surely live. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The soul that<small><small>[^47]</small></small> sinneth, it shall die. The son shall not bear<small><small>[^48]</small></small> the iniquity of the father, neither shall the father bear the iniquity of the son: the righteousness<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of the righteous shall be upon him, and the wickedness of the wicked shall be upon him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if the<small><small>[^50]</small></small> wicked will turn from all his sins that he hath committed, and keep<small><small>[^51]</small></small> all my statutes, and do<small><small>[^52]</small></small> that which is lawful and right, he shall surely<small><small>[^53]</small></small> live, he shall not die. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All his transgressions<small><small>[^54]</small></small> that he hath committed, they shall not be mentioned unto him: in his<small><small>[^55]</small></small> righteousness that he hath done he shall live. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Have I any<small><small>[^56]</small></small> pleasure at all that the wicked should die? saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>: \[and\] not that<small><small>[^57]</small></small> he should return from his ways, and live? 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]But when<small><small>[^58]</small></small> the righteous turneth away from his righteousness, and committeth iniquity, \[and\] doeth<small><small>[^59]</small></small> according to all the abominations that the wicked \[man\] doeth, shall he live? All his<small><small>[^60]</small></small> righteousness that he hath done shall not be mentioned: in his<small><small>[^61]</small></small> trespass that he hath trespassed, and in his sin that he hath sinned, in them shall he die. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Yet ye say, The way<small><small>[^62]</small></small> of the Lord is not equal. Hear now, O house of Israel; Is not my<small><small>[^63]</small></small> way equal? are<small><small>[^64]</small></small> not your ways unequal? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When a righteous \[man\] turneth away from his righteousness, and committeth iniquity, and dieth in them; for his iniquity that he hath done shall he die. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Again, when<small><small>[^65]</small></small> the wicked \[man\] turneth away from his wickedness that he hath committed, and doeth that which is lawful and right, he shall<small><small>[^66]</small></small> save his soul alive. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because he considereth<small><small>[^67]</small></small>, and turneth<small><small>[^68]</small></small> away from all his transgressions that he hath committed, he shall surely live, he shall not die. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet saith the house of Israel, The way of the Lord is not equal. O house of Israel, are not my ways equal? are not your ways unequal?<small><small>[^69]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore I will<small><small>[^70]</small></small> judge you, O house of Israel, every<small><small>[^71]</small></small> one according to his ways, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>. Repent<small><small>[^72]</small></small>, and turn \[yourselves<small><small>[^73]</small></small>\] from all your transgressions; so<small><small>[^74]</small></small> iniquity shall not be your ruin. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Cast<small><small>[^75]</small></small> away from you all your transgressions, whereby ye have transgressed; and make<small><small>[^76]</small></small> you a new heart and a new spirit: for why<small><small>[^77]</small></small> will ye die, O house of Israel? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.18.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I have<small><small>[^78]</small></small> no pleasure in the death of him that dieth, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>: wherefore turn \[yourselves<small><small>[^79]</small></small>\], and live ye. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ezek.17|<< Ezekiel 17]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ezek.19|Ezekiel 19 >>]]</td></tr></table>
