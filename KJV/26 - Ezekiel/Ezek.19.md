***A Lament for the Princes of Israel*** ^bsb1858

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover take<small><small>[^2]</small></small> thou up a lamentation for the princes<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of Israel, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And say, What \[is\] thy mother? A lioness<small><small>[^4]</small></small>: she lay<small><small>[^5]</small></small> down among lions, she nourished her whelps<small><small>[^6]</small></small> among young lions<small><small>[^7]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And she brought up one<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of her whelps: it became<small><small>[^9]</small></small> a young lion, and it learned to catch the prey; it devoured men. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The nations also heard of him; he was<small><small>[^10]</small></small> taken in their pit, and they brought him with chains unto the land of Egypt. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now when she saw that she had waited, \[and\] her hope was lost, then she took another<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of her whelps, \[and\] made him a young lion<small><small>[^12]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he went<small><small>[^13]</small></small> up and down among the lions, he became<small><small>[^14]</small></small> a young lion, and learned to catch the prey, \[and\] devoured men. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he knew their desolate palaces<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, and he laid waste their cities; and the land<small><small>[^16]</small></small> was desolate, and the fulness<small><small>[^17]</small></small> thereof, by the noise of his roaring. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the nations<small><small>[^18]</small></small> set against him on every side from the provinces, and spread<small><small>[^19]</small></small> their net over him: he was taken in their pit. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they put him in ward in chains<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, and brought<small><small>[^21]</small></small> him to the king of Babylon: they brought him into holds, that his<small><small>[^22]</small></small> voice should no more be heard upon the mountains of Israel. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy mother<small><small>[^23]</small></small> \[is\] like<small><small>[^24]</small></small> a vine in thy blood<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, planted by the waters: she was<small><small>[^26]</small></small> fruitful and full<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of branches by reason of many waters. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And she had<small><small>[^28]</small></small> strong rods for the sceptres of them that bare rule, and her stature<small><small>[^29]</small></small> was exalted among the thick branches, and she appeared in her height with the multitude of her branches. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But she was<small><small>[^30]</small></small> plucked up in fury, she was cast down to the ground, and the east<small><small>[^31]</small></small> wind dried up her fruit: her strong<small><small>[^32]</small></small> rods were broken and withered; the fire<small><small>[^33]</small></small> consumed them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And now she \[is<small><small>[^34]</small></small>\] planted in the wilderness<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, in a dry<small><small>[^36]</small></small> and thirsty ground. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.19.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And fire<small><small>[^37]</small></small> is gone out of a rod of her branches, \[which\] hath devoured her fruit, so that she hath<small><small>[^38]</small></small> no strong rod \[to be\] a sceptre to rule.
