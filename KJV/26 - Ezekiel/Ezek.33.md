***Ezekiel the Watchman for Israel*** ^bsb1891

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Again the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son of man, speak<small><small>[^2]</small></small> to the children of thy people, and say unto them, When I bring the sword upon a land<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, if the people of the land take a man of their coasts, and set<small><small>[^4]</small></small> him for their watchman: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If when he seeth the sword come upon the land, he blow<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the trumpet, and warn the people; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then whosoever heareth<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the sound of the trumpet, and taketh not warning; if the sword come, and take him away, his blood<small><small>[^7]</small></small> shall be upon his own head. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He heard<small><small>[^8]</small></small> the sound of the trumpet, and took not warning; his blood<small><small>[^9]</small></small> shall be upon him. But<small><small>[^10]</small></small> he that taketh warning shall deliver his soul. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if the watchman see the sword come, and blow<small><small>[^11]</small></small> not the trumpet, and the people be not warned; if the sword come, and take \[any\] person from among them, he is<small><small>[^12]</small></small> taken away in his iniquity; but his blood<small><small>[^13]</small></small> will I require at the watchman’s hand. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]So thou, O son of man, I have<small><small>[^14]</small></small> set thee a watchman unto the house of Israel; therefore thou shalt<small><small>[^15]</small></small> hear the word at my mouth, and warn them from me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When I say unto the wicked, O wicked<small><small>[^16]</small></small> \[man\], thou shalt surely die; if thou<small><small>[^17]</small></small> dost not speak to warn the wicked from his way, that wicked<small><small>[^18]</small></small> \[man\] shall die in his iniquity; but his blood will I require at thine hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nevertheless, if thou<small><small>[^19]</small></small> warn the wicked of his way to turn from it; if he<small><small>[^20]</small></small> do not turn from his way, he shall die in his iniquity; but thou hast<small><small>[^21]</small></small> delivered thy soul.

***The Message of the Watchman*** ^bsb1892

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore, O thou son of man, speak unto the house of Israel; Thus ye speak, saying, If our<small><small>[^22]</small></small> transgressions and our sins \[be\] upon us, and we pine away in them, how<small><small>[^23]</small></small> should we then live? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Say unto them, \[As\] I live<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>, I have<small><small>[^25]</small></small> no pleasure in the death of the wicked; but that the wicked turn from his way and live: turn ye<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, turn ye from your evil ways; for why will ye die, O house of Israel? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore, thou son of man, say<small><small>[^27]</small></small> unto the children of thy people, The righteousness<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of the righteous shall not deliver him in the day of his transgression: as for<small><small>[^29]</small></small> the wickedness of the wicked, he shall not fall thereby in the day that he turneth from his wickedness; neither<small><small>[^30]</small></small> shall the righteous be able to live for his \[righteousness\] in the day that he sinneth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When I shall say to the righteous, \[that\] he shall surely live; if he<small><small>[^31]</small></small> trust to his own righteousness, and commit iniquity, all his righteousnesses shall not be remembered; but for his iniquity that he hath committed, he shall die<small><small>[^32]</small></small> for it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Again, when I say unto the wicked, Thou shalt<small><small>[^33]</small></small> surely die; if he<small><small>[^34]</small></small> turn from his sin, and do that which is lawful and right<small><small>[^35]</small></small>; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[If\] the wicked restore<small><small>[^36]</small></small> the pledge, give<small><small>[^37]</small></small> again that he had robbed, walk<small><small>[^38]</small></small> in the statutes of life, without committing iniquity; he shall<small><small>[^39]</small></small> surely live, he shall not die. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> None of his sins that he hath committed shall be mentioned unto him: he hath done that which is lawful and right; he shall surely live.<small><small>[^40]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Yet the children of thy people say, The way of the Lord is not equal: but as for them, their way is not equal.<small><small>[^41]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When the righteous turneth from his righteousness, and committeth iniquity, he shall even die thereby.<small><small>[^42]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if the wicked turn from his wickedness, and do that which is lawful and right, he shall live thereby.<small><small>[^43]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Yet<small><small>[^44]</small></small> ye say, The way of the Lord is not equal. O ye house of Israel, I will<small><small>[^45]</small></small> judge you every one after his ways.

***Word of Jerusalem’s Fall*** ^bsb1893

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass in the twelfth<small><small>[^46]</small></small> year of our captivity, in the tenth \[month\], in the fifth \[day\] of the month, \[that\] one<small><small>[^47]</small></small> that had escaped out of Jerusalem came unto me, saying, The city<small><small>[^48]</small></small> is smitten. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now the hand<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> was upon me in the evening, afore he that was escaped came; and had opened my mouth, until he came to me in the morning; and my<small><small>[^50]</small></small> mouth was opened, and I was no more dumb. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son of man, they that<small><small>[^51]</small></small> inhabit those wastes<small><small>[^52]</small></small> of the land of Israel speak, saying, Abraham<small><small>[^53]</small></small> was one, and he inherited the land: but we<small><small>[^54]</small></small> \[are\] many; the land is given us for inheritance. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore say unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Ye eat<small><small>[^55]</small></small> with the blood, and lift up<small><small>[^56]</small></small> your eyes toward your idols, and shed<small><small>[^57]</small></small> blood: and shall ye<small><small>[^58]</small></small> possess the land? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ye stand<small><small>[^59]</small></small> upon your sword, ye work<small><small>[^60]</small></small> abomination, and ye<small><small>[^61]</small></small> defile every one his neighbour’s wife: and shall<small><small>[^62]</small></small> ye possess the land? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Say thou thus unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; \[As\] I live, surely<small><small>[^63]</small></small> they that \[are\] in the wastes shall fall by the sword, and him that \[is\] in the open field will I<small><small>[^64]</small></small> give to the beasts to be devoured<small><small>[^65]</small></small>, and they that \[be\] in the forts and in the caves<small><small>[^66]</small></small> shall die of the pestilence. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I will lay<small><small>[^67]</small></small> the land most desolate<small><small>[^68]</small></small>, and the pomp<small><small>[^69]</small></small> of her strength shall cease; and the mountains<small><small>[^70]</small></small> of Israel shall be desolate, that none shall pass through. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.33.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shall<small><small>[^71]</small></small> they know that I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, when I have laid the land most desolate because<small><small>[^72]</small></small> of all their abominations which they have committed.
