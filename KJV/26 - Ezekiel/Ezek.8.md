<big><big>[[Ezek.8.1 The Vision of Idolatry in the Temple|⥃]]</big></big>***The Vision of Idolatry in the Temple***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass in the sixth year<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, in the sixth \[month\], in the fifth \[day\] of the month, \[as\] I sat in mine house, and the<small><small>[^3]</small></small> elders of Judah sat before me, that the<small><small>[^4]</small></small> hand of the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span> fell there upon me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then I beheld<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, and lo a likeness as the appearance of fire: from the appearance of his loins even downward, fire; and from his loins even upward, as the appearance of brightness, as the colour of amber<small><small>[^6]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he put<small><small>[^7]</small></small> forth the form of an hand, and took me by a lock of mine head; and the spirit<small><small>[^8]</small></small> lifted me up between the earth and the heaven, and brought me in the visions of God to Jerusalem, to the door<small><small>[^9]</small></small> of the inner gate that looketh toward the north; where \[was\] the seat of the image<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of jealousy, which provoketh<small><small>[^11]</small></small> to jealousy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And, behold, the glory of the God of Israel \[was\] there, according to the vision that I saw in the plain.<small><small>[^12]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then said he unto me, Son of man, lift<small><small>[^13]</small></small> up thine eyes now the way toward the north. So I lifted up mine eyes the way toward the north, and behold northward at the<small><small>[^14]</small></small> gate of the altar this image of jealousy in the entry. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He said furthermore unto me, Son of man, seest<small><small>[^15]</small></small> thou what they do? \[even<small><small>[^16]</small></small>\] the great abominations that the house of Israel committeth here, that I<small><small>[^17]</small></small> should go far off from my sanctuary? but turn thee yet again, \[and\] thou shalt see greater<small><small>[^18]</small></small> abominations. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he brought me to the door of the court; and when I looked, behold a hole in the wall.<small><small>[^19]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said he unto me, Son of man, dig now in the wall: and when I had digged in the wall, behold a door.<small><small>[^20]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said unto me, Go in, and behold the wicked abominations that they<small><small>[^21]</small></small> do here. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So I went in and saw; and behold<small><small>[^22]</small></small> every<small><small>[^23]</small></small> form of creeping things, and abominable beasts, and all the idols of the house of Israel, pourtrayed upon the wall round about. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there stood before them seventy<small><small>[^24]</small></small> men of the ancients of the house of Israel, and in the midst of them stood Jaazaniah the son of Shaphan<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, with every<small><small>[^26]</small></small> man his censer in his hand; and a thick cloud of incense went up. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said he unto me, Son of man, hast<small><small>[^27]</small></small> thou seen what the ancients<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of the house of Israel do in the<small><small>[^29]</small></small> dark, every man in the chambers of his imagery? for they say, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> seeth<small><small>[^30]</small></small> us not; the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath forsaken the earth. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He said also unto me, Turn thee yet again, \[and\] thou shalt see greater<small><small>[^31]</small></small> abominations that they do. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then he brought me to the door of the gate of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> house which \[was\] toward<small><small>[^32]</small></small> the north; and, behold, there sat women weeping for Tammuz. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then said he unto me, Hast<small><small>[^33]</small></small> thou seen \[this\], O son of man? turn thee yet again, \[and\] thou shalt see greater<small><small>[^34]</small></small> abominations than these. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he brought me into the inner<small><small>[^35]</small></small> court of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> house, and, behold, at the door<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of the temple of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, between the porch and the altar, \[were\] about<small><small>[^37]</small></small> five and twenty men, with their<small><small>[^38]</small></small> backs toward the temple of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and their faces<small><small>[^39]</small></small> toward the east; and they<small><small>[^40]</small></small> worshipped the sun toward the east. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then he said unto me, Hast thou seen \[this\], O son of man? Is it a light<small><small>[^41]</small></small> thing to the house of Judah that they commit the abominations which they commit here? for<small><small>[^42]</small></small> they have filled the land with violence, and have returned to provoke me to anger: and, lo, they put<small><small>[^43]</small></small> the branch to their nose. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.8.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore will I also<small><small>[^44]</small></small> deal in fury: mine eye shall not spare, neither will I have pity: and though<small><small>[^45]</small></small> they cry in mine ears with a loud voice, \[yet\] will I not hear them. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ezek.7|<< Ezekiel 7]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ezek.9|Ezekiel 9 >>]]</td></tr></table>

[^6]: Amber is a hard, inflammable, bituminous substance, of a beautiful yellow colour, very transparent, and susceptible of an exquisite polish. When rubbed it is highly endowed with electricity; a name which the moderns have formed from its Greek name <span class="asgreek"><span class='asgreek'>ηλεκτρον</span></span>. But, as amber becomes dim as soon as it feels the fire, and is speedily consumed, it is probable that the original ***chashmal*** which Bochart derives from the Chaldee ***nechash*** copper, and ***melala*** gold, was a mixed metal, similar to that which the Greeks called <span class="asgreek"><span class='asgreek'>ηλεκτρον</span></span>, ***electrum***, as the LXX and Vulgate render, from its resemblance to amber in colour.
