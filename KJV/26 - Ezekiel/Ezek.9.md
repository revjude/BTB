***Execution of the Idolaters*** ^bsb1840

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.9.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He cried<small><small>[^2]</small></small> also in mine ears with a loud voice, saying, Cause<small><small>[^3]</small></small> them that have charge over the city to draw near, even every man \[with\] his destroying weapon in his hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.9.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And, behold, six<small><small>[^4]</small></small> men came from the way of the higher<small><small>[^5]</small></small> gate, which lieth<small><small>[^6]</small></small> toward the north, and every man a slaughter weapon<small><small>[^7]</small></small> in his hand; and one<small><small>[^8]</small></small> man among them \[was\] clothed with linen, with a writer’s inkhorn<small><small>[^9]</small></small> by his side<small><small>[^10]</small></small>: and they went in, and stood beside<small><small>[^11]</small></small> the brasen altar. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.9.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the glory of the God of Israel was gone up from the cherub, whereupon he was, to the threshold of the house. And he called to the man clothed with linen, which \[had\] the writer’s inkhorn by his side;<small><small>[^12]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.9.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto him, Go through the midst of the city, through the midst of Jerusalem, and set a mark<small><small>[^13]</small></small> upon the foreheads of the men that sigh<small><small>[^14]</small></small> and that cry for all the abominations that be done in the midst thereof. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.9.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And to the others he said in mine hearing<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, Go<small><small>[^16]</small></small> ye after him through the city, and smite: let not your eye spare, neither have ye pity: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.9.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Slay utterly<small><small>[^17]</small></small> old<small><small>[^18]</small></small> \[and\] young, both maids, and little children, and women: but<small><small>[^19]</small></small> come not near any man upon whom \[is\] the mark; and begin<small><small>[^20]</small></small> at my sanctuary. Then they began at the<small><small>[^21]</small></small> ancient men which \[were\] before the house. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.9.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said unto them, Defile the house, and fill the courts with the slain: go ye forth. And they went forth, and slew in the city.<small><small>[^22]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.9.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And it came to pass, while they were slaying them, and I was left, that I<small><small>[^23]</small></small> fell upon my face, and cried, and said, Ah<small><small>[^24]</small></small> Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>! wilt thou destroy all the residue of Israel in thy pouring out of thy fury upon Jerusalem? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.9.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said he unto me, The iniquity<small><small>[^25]</small></small> of the house of Israel and Judah \[is\] exceeding great, and the land<small><small>[^26]</small></small> is full of<small><small>[^27]</small></small> blood, and the city full of perverseness<small><small>[^28]</small></small>: for they say, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath forsaken the earth, and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> seeth not.<small><small>[^29]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.9.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And as for me also, mine<small><small>[^30]</small></small> eye shall not spare, neither will I have pity, \[but<small><small>[^31]</small></small>\] I will recompense their way upon their head. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.9.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And, behold, the man clothed with linen, which \[had\] the inkhorn by his side, reported the matter<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, saying, I have<small><small>[^33]</small></small> done as thou hast commanded me. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ezek.8|<< Ezekiel 8]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ezek.10|Ezekiel 10 >>]]</td></tr></table>
