 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son of man, prophesy against<small><small>[^2]</small></small> the prophets of Israel that prophesy, and say thou unto them that prophesy out of<small><small>[^3]</small></small> their own hear<small><small>[^4]</small></small>ts, Hear ye the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Woe<small><small>[^5]</small></small> unto the foolish<small><small>[^6]</small></small> prophets, that follow<small><small>[^7]</small></small> their own spirit, and have seen nothing<small><small>[^8]</small></small>! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O Israel, thy prophets<small><small>[^9]</small></small> are like<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the foxes in the deserts. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ye have not<small><small>[^11]</small></small> gone up into the gaps<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, neither made up the hedge<small><small>[^13]</small></small> for the house of Israel to stand<small><small>[^14]</small></small> in the battle in the day<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They have seen<small><small>[^16]</small></small> vanity and lying<small><small>[^17]</small></small> divination, saying<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> saith: and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath not sent them: and they have made<small><small>[^19]</small></small> \[others\] to hope that they would confirm the word. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Have ye not seen a vain vision, and have ye not spoken a lying divination, whereas ye say, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^20]</small></small></span> saith \[it\]; albeit I have not spoken? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Because ye have spoken vanity, and seen lies, therefore, behold<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, I \[am\] against you, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And mine<small><small>[^22]</small></small> hand shall be upon the prophets that see vanity, and that divine lies: they shall not be in the assembly<small><small>[^23]</small></small> of my people, neither shall they be<small><small>[^24]</small></small> written in the writing of the house of Israel, neither shall they enter<small><small>[^25]</small></small> into the land of Israel; $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because, even because they have seduced<small><small>[^27]</small></small> my people, saying, Peace<small><small>[^28]</small></small>; and \[there was\] no peace; and one<small><small>[^29]</small></small> built up a wall<small><small>[^30]</small></small>, and, lo, others<small><small>[^31]</small></small> daubed it with untempered \[morter\]: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Say unto them which daub \[it\] with untempered \[morter\], that it shall fall: there shall<small><small>[^32]</small></small> be an overflowing shower; and ye, O great hailstones, shall fall; and a stormy wind shall rend \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lo, when the wall is fallen, shall it not be said unto you, Where<small><small>[^33]</small></small> \[is\] the daubing wherewith ye have daubed \[it\]? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; I will even rend \[it\] with a stormy<small><small>[^34]</small></small> wind in my fury; and there shall be an overflowing shower in mine anger, and great<small><small>[^35]</small></small> hailstones in \[my\] fury to consume \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So will I break down the wall that ye have daubed with untempered \[morter\], and bring it down to the ground, so that the foundation<small><small>[^36]</small></small> thereof shall be discovered, and it shall fall, and ye shall be<small><small>[^37]</small></small> consumed in the midst thereof: and ye shall know<small><small>[^38]</small></small> that I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus will I accomplish my wrath upon the wall<small><small>[^39]</small></small>, and upon them that have daubed it with untempered \[morter\], and will say unto you, The wall \[is\] no \[more\], neither they that daubed it; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[To wit\], the prophets of Israel which prophesy concerning Jerusalem, and which see visions<small><small>[^40]</small></small> of peace for her, and \[there<small><small>[^41]</small></small> is\] no peace, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>.

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Likewise, thou son of man, set thy<small><small>[^42]</small></small> face against the daughters<small><small>[^43]</small></small> of thy people, which prophesy<small><small>[^44]</small></small> out of<small><small>[^45]</small></small> their own heart; and prophesy thou against them, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Woe<small><small>[^46]</small></small> to the \[women\] that sew<small><small>[^47]</small></small> pillows to all armholes<small><small>[^48]</small></small>, and make kerchiefs upon the head of every stature to hunt souls<small><small>[^49]</small></small>! Will ye hunt the souls of my people, and will ye save<small><small>[^50]</small></small> the souls alive \[that come\] unto you? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.13.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And will ye pollute<small><small>[^51]</small></small> me among my people for handfuls<small><small>[^52]</small></small> of barley and for pieces of bread, to slay<small><small>[^53]</small></small> the souls that should not die, and to save<small><small>[^54]</small></small> the souls alive that should not live, by your lying to my people that hear \[your\] lies?
