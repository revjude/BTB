***The Valley of Dry Bones*** ^bsb1900

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.37.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The hand<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> was upon me, and carried<small><small>[^3]</small></small> me out in the spirit of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and set me down in the midst of the valley which \[was\] full of bones, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.37.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And caused me to pass by them round about: and, behold, \[there were\] very many in the open valley<small><small>[^4]</small></small>; and, lo, \[they were<small><small>[^5]</small></small>\] very dry. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.37.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said unto me, Son of man, can<small><small>[^6]</small></small> these bones live? And I answered, O Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>, thou knowest.<small><small>[^7]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.37.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Again he said unto me, Prophesy<small><small>[^8]</small></small> upon these bones, and say unto them, O ye<small><small>[^9]</small></small> dry bones, hear the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.37.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span> unto these bones; Behold, I will<small><small>[^10]</small></small> cause breath to enter into you, and ye shall live: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.37.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will<small><small>[^11]</small></small> lay sinews upon you, and will bring up flesh upon you, and cover you with skin, and put breath in you, and ye shall<small><small>[^12]</small></small> live; and ye shall know that I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.37.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So I prophesied as<small><small>[^13]</small></small> I was commanded: and as I prophesied, there<small><small>[^14]</small></small> was a noise, and behold a shaking, and the bones came together, bone to his bone. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.37.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when I beheld, lo, the sinews and the flesh came up upon them, and the skin covered them above: but \[there was\] no breath in them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.37.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said he unto me, Prophesy unto the wind<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, prophesy, son of man, and say to the wind, Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Come<small><small>[^16]</small></small> from the four winds, O breath, and breathe upon these slain, that they may live. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.37.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So I prophesied as he commanded me, and the breath<small><small>[^17]</small></small> came into them, and they lived, and stood up upon their feet, an exceeding great army.

it shall be an everlasting covenant with them: and I will place them, and multiply<small><small>[^47]</small></small> them, and will set<small><small>[^48]</small></small> my sanctuary in the midst of them for evermore. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.37.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My tabernacle<small><small>[^49]</small></small> also shall be with them: yea, I will<small><small>[^50]</small></small> be their God, and they shall be my people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.37.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the heathen<small><small>[^51]</small></small> shall know that I the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> do sanctify<small><small>[^52]</small></small> Israel, when my sanctuary shall be in the midst of them for evermore. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ezek.36|<< Ezekiel 36]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ezek.38|Ezekiel 38 >>]]</td></tr></table>

[^2]: In this vision, the dry bones aptly represent the ruined and desperate state of both Israel and Judah; and the revivification of these bones signifies their restoration to their own land after their captivity, and also their recovery from their present long dispersion. [^21]: This is a pointed allusion to the resurrection; under which figure Isaiah ([[Isa.26|Isa 26:9]]) also describes the restoration of the house of Israel, when he says, "thy dead men shall live;" at which time their bones are said to flourish ([[Isa.66|Isa 66:14]]), or to be restored to their former strength and vigour; and, in like manner, St. Paul ([[Rom.11|Rom 11:15]]), expresses their conversion by "life from the dead." In the land of their captivity, they seemed as absolutely deprived of their country as persons committed to the grave are cut off from the land of the living; but when Cyrus issued his proclamation, Jehovah, as it were, opened their graves, and when he stirred up their spirits to embrace the proffered liberty, he put his Spirit within them, that they might live; and their re-establishment in their own land evinced the truth of God in the prediction, and his power in its accomplishment.
