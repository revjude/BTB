***Idolatrous Elders Condemned***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Rom.14|Romans 14:13–23]] <big>†</big> [[1Cor.8|1 Corinthians 8:1–13]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1849

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then came certain<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of the elders of Israel unto me, and sat<small><small>[^3]</small></small> before me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son of man, these men<small><small>[^5]</small></small> have set up their idols in their heart, and put<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the stumblingblock of their iniquity before their face: should<small><small>[^7]</small></small> I be enquired of at all by them? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore speak<small><small>[^8]</small></small> unto them, and say unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Every man of the house of Israel that setteth up his idols in his heart, and putteth the stumblingblock of his iniquity before his face, and cometh to the prophet; I the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^9]</small></small></span> will answer him that cometh according to the multitude of his idols; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That I may<small><small>[^10]</small></small> take the house of Israel in their own heart, because they are all estranged<small><small>[^11]</small></small> from me through their idols. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Therefore say unto the house of Israel, Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Repent<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, and turn \[yourselves<small><small>[^13]</small></small>\] from your idols; and turn<small><small>[^14]</small></small> away your faces from all your abominations. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For every one of the house of Israel, or of the stranger<small><small>[^15]</small></small> that sojourneth in Israel, which separateth<small><small>[^16]</small></small> himself from me, and setteth<small><small>[^17]</small></small> up his idols in his heart, and putteth the stumblingblock of his iniquity before his face, and cometh<small><small>[^18]</small></small> to a prophet to enquire of him concerning me; I the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> will answer him by<small><small>[^19]</small></small> myself: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will set<small><small>[^20]</small></small> my face against that man, and will make him a sign<small><small>[^21]</small></small> and a proverb, and I will cut<small><small>[^22]</small></small> him off from the midst of my people; and ye<small><small>[^23]</small></small> shall know that I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if the<small><small>[^24]</small></small> prophet be deceived when he hath spoken a thing, I the<small><small>[^25]</small></small> <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> have deceived that prophet, and I will<small><small>[^26]</small></small> stretch out my hand upon him, and will destroy him from the midst of my people Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they shall<small><small>[^27]</small></small> bear the punishment<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of their iniquity: the punishment of the prophet shall be even as the punishment of him that seeketh \[unto him\]; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That the house<small><small>[^29]</small></small> of Israel may go no more astray from me, neither<small><small>[^30]</small></small> be polluted any more with all their transgressions; but that they<small><small>[^31]</small></small> may be my people, and I may be their God, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>.

***Four Dire Judgments*** ^bsb1850

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came again to me, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son of man, when<small><small>[^32]</small></small> the land sinneth against me by trespassing grievously, then will I stretch out mine hand upon it, and will break<small><small>[^33]</small></small> the staff of the bread thereof, and will send famine upon it, and will cut<small><small>[^34]</small></small> off man and beast from it: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Though these<small><small>[^35]</small></small> three men, Noah<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, Daniel<small><small>[^37]</small></small>, and Job<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, were in it, they should deliver<small><small>[^39]</small></small> \[but\] their own souls by their righteousness, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]If I cause noisome<small><small>[^40]</small></small> beasts to pass through the land, and they spoil<small><small>[^41]</small></small> it, so that it be desolate, that no man may pass through because of the beasts: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Though\] these<small><small>[^42]</small></small> three men \[were\] in it<small><small>[^43]</small></small>, \[as\] I live<small><small>[^44]</small></small>, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>, they shall<small><small>[^45]</small></small> deliver neither sons nor daughters; they only shall be delivered, but the land shall be desolate. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Or \[if\] I bring<small><small>[^46]</small></small> a sword upon that land, and say, Sword, go through the land; so that<small><small>[^47]</small></small> I cut off man and beast from it: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Though these three men \[were\] in it, \[as\] I live, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>, they shall deliver neither sons nor daughters, but they only shall be delivered themselves.<small><small>[^48]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Or \[if\] I<small><small>[^49]</small></small> send a pestilence into that land, and pour<small><small>[^50]</small></small> out my fury upon it in blood, to cut off from it man and beast: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Though Noah<small><small>[^51]</small></small>, Daniel<small><small>[^52]</small></small>, and Job, \[were\] in it, \[as\] I live, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>, they shall deliver neither son nor daughter; they shall \[but\] deliver their own souls by<small><small>[^53]</small></small> their righteousness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; How much more when<small><small>[^54]</small></small> I send my four<small><small>[^55]</small></small> sore judgments upon Jerusalem, the sword, and the famine, and the noisome beast, and the pestilence, to cut off from it man and beast? 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Yet, behold, therein<small><small>[^56]</small></small> shall be left a remnant that shall be brought forth, \[both\] sons and daughters: behold, they shall come forth unto you, and ye shall see<small><small>[^57]</small></small> their way and their doings: and ye shall be<small><small>[^58]</small></small> comforted concerning the evil that I have brought upon Jerusalem, \[even\] concerning all that I have brought upon it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.14.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they shall comfort you, when ye see their ways and their doings: and ye shall know that I have not<small><small>[^59]</small></small> done without cause all that I have done in it, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ezek.13|<< Ezekiel 13]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ezek.15|Ezekiel 15 >>]]</td></tr></table>
