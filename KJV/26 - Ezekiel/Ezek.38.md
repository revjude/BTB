***A Prophecy against Gog*** ^bsb1902

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of man, set<small><small>[^3]</small></small> thy face against Gog<small><small>[^4]</small></small>, the land of Magog<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, the chief prince of<small><small>[^6]</small></small> Meshech<small><small>[^7]</small></small> and Tubal, and prophesy against him, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Behold, I \[am<small><small>[^8]</small></small>\] against thee, O Gog, the chief prince of Meshech and Tubal: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will turn<small><small>[^9]</small></small> thee back, and put hooks into thy jaws, and I will bring thee forth, and all thine army, horses<small><small>[^10]</small></small> and horsemen, all of them<small><small>[^11]</small></small> clothed with all sorts \[of armour\], \[even\] a great company \[with\] bucklers and shields, all of them handling<small><small>[^12]</small></small> swords: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Persia<small><small>[^13]</small></small>, Ethiopia, and Libya<small><small>[^14]</small></small> with them; all of them with shield and helmet: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Gomer<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, and all his bands; the house of Togarmah<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of the north quarters, and all his bands: \[and\] many people with thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be thou prepared, and prepare for thyself, thou, and all thy company that are assembled unto thee, and be thou a guard unto them.<small><small>[^17]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]After many days<small><small>[^18]</small></small> thou shalt be<small><small>[^19]</small></small> visited: in the latter years thou shalt come into the land<small><small>[^20]</small></small> \[that is\] brought back from the sword, \[and is\] gathered out of many people, against the mountains<small><small>[^21]</small></small> of Israel, which have been always waste: but it is<small><small>[^22]</small></small> brought forth out of the nations, and they shall<small><small>[^23]</small></small> dwell safely all of them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt ascend<small><small>[^24]</small></small> and come like<small><small>[^25]</small></small> a storm, thou shalt be like a cloud to cover the land, thou, and all thy<small><small>[^26]</small></small> bands, and many people with thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; It shall also come to pass, \[that\] at<small><small>[^27]</small></small> the same time shall things come into thy mind, and thou shalt think an evil thought<small><small>[^28]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt say, I will go up<small><small>[^29]</small></small> to the land of unwalled villages; I will go to<small><small>[^30]</small></small> them that are at rest, that dwell safely<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, all of them dwelling without walls, and having neither bars nor gates, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To take a spoil, and to take a prey<small><small>[^32]</small></small>; to turn<small><small>[^33]</small></small> thine hand upon the desolate<small><small>[^34]</small></small> places \[that are now\] inhabited, and upon<small><small>[^35]</small></small> the people \[that are\] gathered out of the nations, which have gotten cattle and goods, that dwell in the midst<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of the land. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Sheba<small><small>[^37]</small></small>, and Dedan, and the merchants of Tarshish, with<small><small>[^38]</small></small> all the young lions thereof, shall say unto thee, Art thou come to take a spoil? hast thou gathered thy company to take a prey? to carry away silver and gold, to take away cattle and goods, to take a great spoil? 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore, son of man, prophesy and say unto Gog, Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; In that day when my people of Israel dwelleth<small><small>[^39]</small></small> safely, shalt<small><small>[^40]</small></small> thou not know \[it\]?<small><small>[^41]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt come from thy place<small><small>[^42]</small></small> out of the north parts, thou, and many<small><small>[^43]</small></small> people with thee, all of them riding upon horses, a great company, and a mighty army: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt come up against my people of Israel, as a cloud<small><small>[^44]</small></small> to cover the land; \[Art\] thou he of whom<small><small>[^47]</small></small> I have spoken in old time by<small><small>[^48]</small></small> my servants the prophets of Israel, which prophesied in those days \[many\] years that I would bring thee against them? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it shall come to pass at the same time when Gog shall come against the land of Israel, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>, \[that<small><small>[^49]</small></small>\] my fury shall come up in my face. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For in my<small><small>[^50]</small></small> jealousy \[and\] in the fire of my wrath have I spoken, Surely<small><small>[^51]</small></small> in that day there shall be a great shaking in the land of Israel; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So that the fishes<small><small>[^52]</small></small> of the sea, and the fowls of the heaven, and the beasts of the field, and all creeping things that creep upon the earth, and all the men that \[are\] upon the face of the earth, shall shake at my presence, and the mountains shall be thrown down, and the steep places<small><small>[^53]</small></small> shall fall, and every wall shall fall to the ground. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will<small><small>[^54]</small></small> call for a sword against him throughout all my mountains, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>: every<small><small>[^55]</small></small> man’s sword shall be against his brother. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will plead<small><small>[^56]</small></small> against him with pestilence<small><small>[^57]</small></small> and with blood; and I will rain upon him, and upon his bands, and upon the many people that \[are\] with him, an overflowing<small><small>[^58]</small></small> rain, and great hailstones, fire, and brimstone. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.38.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus will I magnify<small><small>[^59]</small></small> myself, and sanctify myself; [^45]: Though it is not generally agreed what people or transactions are here predicted, yet it seems evident that the prophecy is not yet accomplished. Nothing occurred in the wars of Cambyses, or Antiochus Epiphanes with the Jews, that answers to it; and the expression here used, in the latter days, plainly implies that there should be a succession of many ages between the publication of the prediction and its accomplishment. It is therefore supposed, with much probability, that its fulfilment will be posterior to the conversion of the Jews and their restoration to their own land; and that the Turks, Tarters, or Scythians, from the northern parts of Asia, perhaps uniting with the inhabitants of some more southern regions, will make war upon the Jews and be cut off in a manner predicted here.
