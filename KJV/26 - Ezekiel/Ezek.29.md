<big><big>[[Ezek.29.1 A Prophecy against Pharaoh|⥃]]</big></big>***A Prophecy against Pharaoh***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the tenth year, in the tenth \[month\], in the twelfth \[day\] of the month, the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,<small><small>[^2]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son of man, set<small><small>[^3]</small></small> thy face against Pharaoh<small><small>[^4]</small></small> king of Egypt, and prophesy against him, and against all<small><small>[^5]</small></small> Egypt: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Speak, and say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Behold, I \[am<small><small>[^6]</small></small>\] against thee, Pharaoh king of Egypt, the great<small><small>[^7]</small></small> dragon that lieth in the midst of his rivers, which hath said, My river<small><small>[^8]</small></small> \[is\] mine own, and I have made \[it\] for myself. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But I will put<small><small>[^9]</small></small> hooks in thy jaws, and I will cause the fish<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of thy rivers to stick unto thy scales, and I will bring thee up out of the midst of thy rivers, and all the fish of thy rivers shall stick unto thy scales. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will leave<small><small>[^11]</small></small> thee \[thrown\] into the wilderness, thee and all the fish of thy rivers: thou shalt fall upon the open fields<small><small>[^12]</small></small>; thou shalt not be brought together, nor gathered: I have<small><small>[^13]</small></small> given thee for meat to the beasts of the field and to the fowls of the heaven. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all the inhabitants of Egypt shall know<small><small>[^14]</small></small> that I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, because they have been a staff<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of reed to the house of Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When they took<small><small>[^16]</small></small> hold of thee by thy hand, thou didst<small><small>[^17]</small></small> break, and rend all their shoulder: and when they leaned upon thee, thou brakest, and madest all their loins to be at a stand.

<big><big>[[Ezek.29.8 The Desolation of Egypt|⥃]]</big></big>***The Desolation of Egypt***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Behold, I will<small><small>[^18]</small></small> bring a sword upon thee, and cut<small><small>[^19]</small></small> off man and beast out of thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the land<small><small>[^20]</small></small> of Egypt shall be desolate and waste; and they shall know that I \[am\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: because<small><small>[^21]</small></small> he hath said, The river \[is\] mine, and I have made \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, therefore I \[am\] against thee, and against thy rivers, and I will<small><small>[^22]</small></small> make the land of Egypt utterly waste<small><small>[^23]</small></small> \[and\] desolate, from the tower of<small><small>[^24]</small></small> Syene<small><small>[^25]</small></small> even unto the border of Ethiopia. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> No foot of man<small><small>[^26]</small></small> shall pass through it, nor foot of beast shall pass through it, neither shall it be inhabited forty<small><small>[^27]</small></small> years. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will make the land of Egypt desolate in<small><small>[^28]</small></small> the midst of the countries \[that are\] desolate, and her cities among the cities \[that are\] laid waste shall be desolate forty years: and I will scatter<small><small>[^29]</small></small> the Egyptians among the nations, and will disperse them through the countries. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Yet thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; At the<small><small>[^30]</small></small> end of forty years will I gather the Egyptians from the people whither they were scattered: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will bring again the captivity of Egypt, and will cause them to return \[into\] the land of Pathros<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, into the land of their habitation<small><small>[^32]</small></small>; and they shall be there a base<small><small>[^33]</small></small> kingdom. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> It shall be the basest<small><small>[^34]</small></small> of the kingdoms; neither shall it exalt itself any more above the nations: for I will diminish them, that they shall no more rule<small><small>[^35]</small></small> over the nations. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it shall be no more the confidence<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of the house of Israel, which bringeth<small><small>[^37]</small></small> \[their\] iniquity to remembrance, when they shall look after them: but<small><small>[^38]</small></small> they shall know that I \[am\] the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>.

<big><big>[[Ezek.29.17 Egypt the Reward of Nebuchadnezzar|⥃]]</big></big>***Egypt the Reward of Nebuchadnezzar***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass in the seven and twentieth year, in the first \[month\], in the first \[day\] of the month, the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,<small><small>[^39]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Son of man, Nebuchadrezzar<small><small>[^40]</small></small> king of Babylon caused his army to serve a great<small><small>[^41]</small></small> service against Tyrus: every head \[was\] made bald, and every shoulder \[was\] peeled: yet<small><small>[^42]</small></small> had he no wages, nor his army, for Tyrus, for the service that he had served against it: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>; Behold, I will<small><small>[^43]</small></small> give the land of Egypt unto Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon; and he shall take her multitude, and take her spoil, and take her prey<small><small>[^44]</small></small>; and it shall be the wages for his army. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezek.29.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have given him the land of Egypt \[for\] his labour<small><small>[^45]</small></small> wherewith he served<small><small>[^46]</small></small> against it, because they wrought for me, saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>.
