<big><big>[[Exod.34.1 New Stone Tablets|⥃]]</big></big>***New Stone Tablets***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Deut.10|Deuteronomy 10:1–11]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Hew<small><small>[^2]</small></small> thee two tables of stone like unto the first: and I will<small><small>[^3]</small></small> write upon \[these\] tables the words<small><small>[^4]</small></small> that were in the first tables, which<small><small>[^5]</small></small> thou brakest. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And be ready in the morning, and come up in the morning unto mount Sinai, and present thyself there to me in the top<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of the mount. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And no man shall come up with thee, neither let any man be seen throughout all the mount; neither let the flocks nor herds feed before that mount.<small><small>[^7]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And he hewed two tables of stone like unto the first; and Moses rose up early in the morning, and went up unto mount Sinai, as the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had commanded him, and took in his hand the two tables of stone. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> descended<small><small>[^8]</small></small> in the cloud, and stood with him there, and proclaimed the name<small><small>[^9]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> passed<small><small>[^10]</small></small> by before him, and proclaimed<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^12]</small></small></span>, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God, merciful<small><small>[^13]</small></small> and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant<small><small>[^14]</small></small> in goodness and truth<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Keeping<small><small>[^16]</small></small> mercy for thousands, forgiving<small><small>[^17]</small></small> iniquity and transgression and sin, and that will by no means clear \[the guilty<small><small>[^18]</small></small>\]; visiting<small><small>[^19]</small></small> the iniquity of the fathers upon the children, and upon the children’s children, unto the third and to the fourth \[generation\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses made haste, and bowed his head toward the earth, and worshipped.<small><small>[^20]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, If now<small><small>[^21]</small></small> I have found grace in thy sight, O Lord, let my Lord<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, I pray thee, go among us; for it \[is\] a stiffnecked<small><small>[^23]</small></small> people; and pardon<small><small>[^24]</small></small> our iniquity and our sin, and take us<small><small>[^25]</small></small> for thine inheritance.

<big><big>[[Exod.34.10 The LORD Renews the Covenant|⥃]]</big></big>***The LORD Renews the Covenant***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[2Cor.3|2 Corinthians 3:7–18]] ]</sup></small>

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, Behold, I make<small><small>[^26]</small></small> a covenant: before all thy people I will do marvels<small><small>[^27]</small></small>, such as have not been done in all the earth, nor in any nation: and all the people among which thou \[art\] shall see the work of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: for it \[is\] a terrible<small><small>[^28]</small></small> thing that I will do with thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Observe<small><small>[^29]</small></small> thou that which I command thee this day: behold, I drive<small><small>[^30]</small></small> out before thee the Amorite, and the Canaanite, and the Hittite, and the Perizzite, and the Hivite, and the Jebusite. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Take heed<small><small>[^31]</small></small> to thyself, lest<small><small>[^32]</small></small> thou make a covenant with the inhabitants of the land whither thou goest, lest it be for a snare in the midst of thee: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But ye shall<small><small>[^33]</small></small> destroy their altars, break their images<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, and cut down their groves: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thou shalt worship<small><small>[^35]</small></small> no other god: for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, whose<small><small>[^36]</small></small> name \[is\] Jealous, \[is\] a jealous God<small><small>[^37]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lest thou make<small><small>[^38]</small></small> a covenant with the inhabitants of the land, and they go a whoring<small><small>[^39]</small></small> after their gods, and do sacrifice unto their gods, and \[one\] call thee<small><small>[^40]</small></small>, and thou eat<small><small>[^41]</small></small> of his sacrifice; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou take of their daughters unto thy sons, and their daughters go a whoring after their gods, and make thy sons go a whoring after their gods.<small><small>[^42]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt make thee no molten gods.<small><small>[^43]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The feast of unleavened bread shalt thou keep. Seven days thou shalt eat unleavened bread, as I commanded thee, in the time of the month Abib: for in the month Abib thou camest out from Egypt.<small><small>[^44]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All that openeth<small><small>[^45]</small></small> the matrix \[is\] mine; and every firstling among thy cattle, \[whether\] ox or sheep, \[that is male\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the firstling<small><small>[^46]</small></small> of an ass thou shalt redeem with a lamb<small><small>[^47]</small></small>: and if thou redeem \[him\] not, then shalt thou break his neck. All the<small><small>[^48]</small></small> firstborn of thy sons thou shalt redeem. And none<small><small>[^49]</small></small> shall appear before me empty. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Six<small><small>[^50]</small></small> days thou shalt work, but on the seventh day thou shalt rest: in earing<small><small>[^51]</small></small> time and in harvest thou shalt rest. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And thou shalt observe the feast of weeks<small><small>[^52]</small></small>, of the firstfruits of wheat harvest, and the feast of ingathering at the year’s end<small><small>[^53]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Thrice<small><small>[^54]</small></small> in the year shall all your men children appear before the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>, the God<small><small>[^55]</small></small> of Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I will<small><small>[^56]</small></small> cast out the nations before thee, and enlarge<small><small>[^57]</small></small> thy borders: neither shall any man desire<small><small>[^58]</small></small> thy land, when thou shalt go up to appear before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God thrice in the year. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt not offer the blood of my sacrifice with leaven<small><small>[^59]</small></small>; neither shall the sacrifice of the feast of the passover be left<small><small>[^60]</small></small> unto the morning. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The first<small><small>[^61]</small></small> of the firstfruits of thy land thou shalt bring unto the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God. Thou shalt not seethe<small><small>[^62]</small></small> a kid in his mother’s milk. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Write<small><small>[^63]</small></small> thou these words: for after the tenor of these words I have<small><small>[^64]</small></small> made a covenant with thee and with Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he was there with the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> forty days<small><small>[^65]</small></small> and forty nights; he did neither eat bread, nor drink water. And he wrote<small><small>[^66]</small></small> upon the tables the words of the covenant, the ten commandments<small><small>[^67]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And it came to pass, when Moses came down from mount Sinai with the two tables<small><small>[^68]</small></small> of testimony in Moses’ hand, when he came down from the mount, that Moses wist<small><small>[^69]</small></small> not that the skin<small><small>[^70]</small></small> of his face shone<small><small>[^71]</small></small> while he talked with him.<small><small>[^72]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when Aaron and all the children of Israel saw Moses, behold, the skin of his face shone; and they were afraid<small><small>[^73]</small></small> to come nigh him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses called<small><small>[^74]</small></small> unto them; and Aaron and all the rulers of the congregation returned unto him: and Moses talked<small><small>[^75]</small></small> with them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And afterward all the children of Israel came nigh: and he gave<small><small>[^76]</small></small> them in commandment all that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had spoken with him in mount Sinai. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And \[till\] Moses had done speaking with them, he put a vail<small><small>[^77]</small></small> on his face. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.34|34]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But when Moses went in before the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> to speak with him, he took<small><small>[^78]</small></small> the vail off, until he came out. And he came out, and spake unto the children of Israel \[that\] which he was commanded. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.34.35|35]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the children of Israel saw the face of Moses, that the skin of Moses’ face shone: and Moses put the vail upon his face again, until he went in to speak with him. <small><small>[^79]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Exod.33|<< Exodus 33]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Exod.35|Exodus 35 >>]]</td></tr></table>
