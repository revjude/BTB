<big><big>[[Exod.11.1 The Plague on the Firstborn Foretold|⥃]]</big></big>***The Plague on the Firstborn Foretold***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.11.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Yet will<small><small>[^2]</small></small> I bring one plague \[more\] upon Pharaoh, and upon Egypt; afterwards<small><small>[^3]</small></small> he will let you go hence: when he shall let \[you\] go, he shall surely thrust you<small><small>[^4]</small></small> out hence altogether. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.11.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Speak now in the ears of the people, and let every man borrow<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of his neighbour, and every woman of her neighbour, jewels<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of silver, and jewels of gold. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.11.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^7]</small></small></span> gave the people favour in the sight of the Egyptians. Moreover the man Moses<small><small>[^8]</small></small> \[was\] very great in the land of Egypt, in the sight of Pharaoh’s servants, and in the sight of the people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.11.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses said, Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, About<small><small>[^9]</small></small> midnight will I go<small><small>[^10]</small></small> out into the midst of Egypt: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.11.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all the firstborn<small><small>[^11]</small></small> in the land of Egypt shall die, from the firstborn of Pharaoh that sitteth upon his throne, even unto the firstborn of the maidservant that \[is\] behind<small><small>[^12]</small></small> the mill; and all the firstborn of beasts. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.11.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And there shall be a great cry throughout all the land of Egypt, such as there was none like it, nor shall be like it any more.<small><small>[^13]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.11.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But against any of the children of Israel shall not a dog<small><small>[^14]</small></small> move his tongue, against man or beast: that ye may know how that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> doth put a difference<small><small>[^15]</small></small> between the Egyptians and Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.11.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all<small><small>[^16]</small></small> these thy servants shall come down unto me, and bow down themselves unto me, saying, Get thee out, and all the people that follow thee<small><small>[^17]</small></small>: and after that I will go out. And he went out from Pharaoh in a great anger<small><small>[^18]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.11.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Pharaoh<small><small>[^19]</small></small> shall not hearken unto you; that my wonders<small><small>[^20]</small></small> may be multiplied in the land of Egypt. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.11.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses and Aaron did all these wonders before Pharaoh: and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^21]</small></small></span> hardened Pharaoh’s heart, so that he would not let the children of Israel go out of his land. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Exod.10|<< Exodus 10]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Exod.12|Exodus 12 >>]]</td></tr></table>
