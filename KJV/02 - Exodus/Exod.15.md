<big><big>[[Exod.15.1 The Song at the Sea|⥃]]</big></big>***The Song at the Sea***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Judg.5|Judges 5:1–31]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then<small><small>[^2]</small></small> sang Moses and the children of Israel this song unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and spake, saying, I will sing unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, for<small><small>[^3]</small></small> he hath triumphed gloriously: the horse and his rider hath he thrown into the sea. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] my strength<small><small>[^4]</small></small> and song<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, and he is become my salvation<small><small>[^6]</small></small>: he \[is\] my God<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, and I will prepare him an habitation<small><small>[^8]</small></small>; my father’s God<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, and I will exalt him<small><small>[^10]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] a man<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of war: the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] his name<small><small>[^12]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Pharaoh’s chariots and his host hath he cast into the sea: his chosen<small><small>[^13]</small></small> captains also are drowned in the Red sea.<small><small>[^14]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The depths<small><small>[^15]</small></small> have covered them: they<small><small>[^16]</small></small> sank into the bottom as a stone. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy right hand<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, is become glorious in power: thy right hand, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, hath dashed<small><small>[^18]</small></small> in pieces the enemy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And in the greatness<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of thine excellency thou hast overthrown them that<small><small>[^20]</small></small> rose up against thee: thou sentest forth thy wrath, \[which\] consumed<small><small>[^21]</small></small> them as stubble. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And with the blast<small><small>[^22]</small></small> of thy nostrils the waters were gathered together, the floods<small><small>[^23]</small></small> stood upright as an heap, \[and\] the depths were congealed in the heart of the sea. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The enemy said, I will pursue<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, I will overtake, I will divide the spoil; my lust shall be satisfied upon them; I will draw my sword, my hand shall destroy<small><small>[^25]</small></small> them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou didst blow<small><small>[^26]</small></small> with thy wind, the sea<small><small>[^27]</small></small> covered them: they sank<small><small>[^28]</small></small> as lead in the mighty waters. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who \[is\] like unto thee<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, among the gods<small><small>[^30]</small></small>? who \[is\] like thee, glorious<small><small>[^31]</small></small> in holiness, fearful<small><small>[^32]</small></small> \[in\] praises, doing wonders? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou stretchedst<small><small>[^33]</small></small> out thy right hand, the earth swallowed them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou<small><small>[^34]</small></small> in thy mercy hast led<small><small>[^35]</small></small> forth the people \[which\] thou hast redeemed: thou hast guided<small><small>[^36]</small></small> \[them\] in thy strength unto thy holy<small><small>[^37]</small></small> habitation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The people shall hear<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, \[and\] be afraid: sorrow shall take hold on the inhabitants of Palestina<small><small>[^39]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the dukes<small><small>[^40]</small></small> of Edom shall be amazed; the mighty men of Moab<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, trembling shall take hold upon them; all the<small><small>[^42]</small></small> inhabitants of Canaan shall melt<small><small>[^43]</small></small> away. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Fear and dread<small><small>[^44]</small></small> shall fall upon them; by the greatness of thine arm they shall be \[as\] still<small><small>[^45]</small></small> as a stone; till thy people pass over, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, till the people pass over, \[which\] thou<small><small>[^46]</small></small> hast purchased. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shalt bring them in, and plant<small><small>[^47]</small></small> them in the mountain<small><small>[^48]</small></small> of thine inheritance, \[in\] the place, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[which\] thou hast made for thee to dwell in, \[in\] the Sanctuary, O Lord, \[which\] thy hands have established. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall reign for ever and ever.<small><small>[^49]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the horse<small><small>[^50]</small></small> of Pharaoh went in with his chariots and with his horsemen into the sea, and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> brought<small><small>[^51]</small></small> again the waters of the sea upon them; but the children of Israel went on dry \[land\] in the midst of the sea. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And Miriam the prophetess<small><small>[^52]</small></small>, the sister<small><small>[^53]</small></small> of Aaron, took a timbrel<small><small>[^54]</small></small> in her hand; and all the<small><small>[^55]</small></small> women went out after her with timbrels and with dances. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Miriam answered<small><small>[^56]</small></small> them, Sing ye<small><small>[^57]</small></small> to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, for he hath triumphed gloriously; the horse and his rider hath he thrown into the sea.

<big><big>[[Exod.15.22 The Waters of Marah|⥃]]</big></big>***The Waters of Marah***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So Moses brought Israel from the Red sea, and they went out into the wilderness of Shur<small><small>[^58]</small></small>; and they went three days<small><small>[^59]</small></small> in the wilderness, and found no water. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And when they came to Marah<small><small>[^60]</small></small><small><small>[^61]</small></small>, they could not drink of the waters of Marah, for they \[were\] bitter: therefore the name of it was called Marah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the people murmured against Moses, saying, What<small><small>[^62]</small></small> shall we drink?<small><small>[^63]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he cried<small><small>[^64]</small></small> unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shewed him a tree<small><small>[^65]</small></small>, \[which\] when he had cast into the waters, the waters were made sweet: there he made for them a statute<small><small>[^66]</small></small> and an ordinance, and there he proved<small><small>[^67]</small></small> them, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And said, If thou<small><small>[^68]</small></small> wilt diligently hearken to the voice of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God, and wilt<small><small>[^69]</small></small> do that which is right in his sight, and wilt give ear to his commandments, and keep all his statutes, I will put none of these diseases<small><small>[^70]</small></small> upon thee, which I have brought upon the Egyptians: for I \[am<small><small>[^71]</small></small>\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> that healeth thee. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.15.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And they came to Elim<small><small>[^72]</small></small>, where \[were\] twelve wells of water, and threescore and ten palm trees: and they encamped there by the waters. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Exod.14|<< Exodus 14]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Exod.16|Exodus 16 >>]]</td></tr></table>
