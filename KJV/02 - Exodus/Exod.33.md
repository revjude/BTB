<big><big>[[Exod.33.1 The Command to Leave Sinai|⥃]]</big></big>***The Command to Leave Sinai***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Deut.1|Deuteronomy 1:1–8]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.33.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Depart<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, \[and\] go up hence, thou and the people which thou hast<small><small>[^3]</small></small> brought up out of the land<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of Egypt, unto<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the land which I sware unto Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob, saying, Unto thy seed will I give it: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.33.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will send an angel<small><small>[^6]</small></small> before thee; and I will drive out the Canaanite<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, the Amorite, and the Hittite, and the Perizzite, the Hivite, and the Jebusite: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.33.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Unto a land<small><small>[^8]</small></small> flowing with milk and honey: for I<small><small>[^9]</small></small> will not go up in the midst of thee; for thou \[art\] a stiffnecked<small><small>[^10]</small></small> people: lest I<small><small>[^11]</small></small> consume thee in the way. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.33.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And when the people heard these evil tidings, they mourned<small><small>[^12]</small></small>: and no<small><small>[^13]</small></small> man did put on him his ornaments. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.33.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had said unto Moses, Say unto the children of Israel, Ye \[are<small><small>[^14]</small></small>\] a stiffnecked people: I will come up into the midst of thee in a moment<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, and consume thee: therefore now put off<small><small>[^16]</small></small> thy ornaments from thee, that I may<small><small>[^17]</small></small> know what to do unto thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.33.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the children of Israel stripped themselves of their ornaments by the mount Horeb.<small><small>[^18]</small></small>

<big><big>[[Exod.33.7 The Tent of Meeting|⥃]]</big></big>***The Tent of Meeting***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.33.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses took the tabernacle<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, and pitched it without the camp, afar off<small><small>[^20]</small></small> from the camp, and called it the Tabernacle of<small><small>[^21]</small></small> the congregation. And it came to pass, \[that\] every one which sought<small><small>[^22]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> went out<small><small>[^23]</small></small> unto the tabernacle of the congregation, which \[was\] without the camp. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.33.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, when Moses went out unto the tabernacle, \[that\] all the people rose up, and stood<small><small>[^24]</small></small> every man \[at\] his tent door, and looked after Moses, until he was gone into the tabernacle. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.33.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, as Moses entered into the tabernacle, the cloudy<small><small>[^25]</small></small> pillar descended, and stood \[at\] the door of the tabernacle, and \[the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>\] talked<small><small>[^26]</small></small> with Moses. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.33.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all the people saw the cloudy pillar stand \[at\] the tabernacle door: and all the people rose up and worshipped<small><small>[^27]</small></small>, every man \[in\] his tent door. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.33.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> spake<small><small>[^28]</small></small> unto Moses face to face, as a man speaketh unto his friend<small><small>[^29]</small></small>. And he turned again into the camp: but his servant<small><small>[^30]</small></small> Joshua, the son of Nun, a young man, departed not out of the tabernacle.
