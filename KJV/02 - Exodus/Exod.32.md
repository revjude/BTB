<big><big>[[Exod.32.1 The Golden Calf|⥃]]</big></big>***The Golden Calf***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Deut.9|Deuteronomy 9:7–29]] <big>†</big> [[Acts.7|Acts 7:39–43]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when the people saw that Moses delayed<small><small>[^2]</small></small> to come down out of the mount, the people gathered themselves together unto Aaron, and said unto him, Up<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, make<small><small>[^4]</small></small> us gods, which shall<small><small>[^5]</small></small> go before us; and Aaron made proclamation<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, and said, To morrow \[is\] a feast<small><small>[^18]</small></small> to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they rose up early on the morrow, and offered<small><small>[^19]</small></small> burnt offerings, and brought peace offerings; and the people sat down<small><small>[^20]</small></small> to eat and to drink, and rose up to play. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Go<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, get thee down; for thy people<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, which thou broughtest out of the land of Egypt, have corrupted<small><small>[^23]</small></small> \[themselves\]: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They have turned<small><small>[^24]</small></small> aside quickly out of the way which I<small><small>[^25]</small></small> commanded them: they have made them a molten calf, and have worshipped it, and have sacrificed thereunto, and said, These \[be<small><small>[^26]</small></small>\] thy gods, O Israel, which have brought thee up out of the land of Egypt. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, I have seen<small><small>[^27]</small></small> this people, and, behold, it \[is\] a stiffnecked<small><small>[^28]</small></small> people: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now therefore let me alone<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, that my wrath<small><small>[^30]</small></small> may wax hot against them, and that I may consume them: and I will<small><small>[^31]</small></small> make of thee a great nation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses besought<small><small>[^32]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> his God<small><small>[^33]</small></small>, and said, <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, why doth<small><small>[^34]</small></small> thy wrath wax hot against thy people, which thou<small><small>[^35]</small></small> hast brought forth out of the land of Egypt with great power, and with a mighty hand? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore should<small><small>[^36]</small></small> the Egyptians speak, and say, For mischief did he bring them out, to slay them in the mountains, and to consume them from the face of the earth? Turn from<small><small>[^37]</small></small> thy fierce wrath, and repent<small><small>[^38]</small></small> of this evil against thy people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Remember<small><small>[^39]</small></small> Abraham, Isaac, and Israel, thy servants, to whom<small><small>[^40]</small></small> thou swarest by thine own self, and saidst unto them, I will multiply<small><small>[^41]</small></small> your seed as the stars of heaven, and all this land that I have spoken of will I give unto your seed, and they shall inherit \[it\] for ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> repented of the evil which he thought to do unto his people.<small><small>[^42]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses turned<small><small>[^43]</small></small>, and went down from the mount, and the two tables of the testimony<small><small>[^44]</small></small> \[were\] in his hand: the tables \[were\] written<small><small>[^45]</small></small> on both their sides; on the one side and on the other \[were\] they written. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the tables \[were\] the work of God, and the writing \[was\] the writing of God, graven upon the tables.<small><small>[^46]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when Joshua<small><small>[^47]</small></small> heard the noise of the people as they shouted<small><small>[^48]</small></small>, he said unto Moses, \[There is\] a noise<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of war in the camp.<small><small>[^50]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, \[It is\] not the voice of \[them that\] shout for mastery, neither \[is it\] the voice of \[them that\] cry for being overcome<small><small>[^51]</small></small>: \[but\] the<small><small>[^52]</small></small> noise of \[them that\] sing do I hear. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And it came to pass, as soon as he came nigh unto the camp, that he saw<small><small>[^53]</small></small> the calf, and the dancing<small><small>[^54]</small></small>: and Moses’ anger<small><small>[^55]</small></small> waxed hot, and he cast the tables out of his hands, and brake them<small><small>[^56]</small></small> beneath the mount. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he took the calf<small><small>[^57]</small></small> which they had made, and burnt \[it\] in the fire, and ground \[it\] to powder, and strawed \[it\] upon the water, and made the<small><small>[^58]</small></small> children of Israel drink \[of it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses said unto Aaron, What did this people unto thee, that thou hast brought so great a sin upon them?<small><small>[^59]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Aaron said, Let not the anger of my lord wax hot: thou knowest<small><small>[^60]</small></small> the people, that they \[are<small><small>[^61]</small></small> set\] on mischief. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For they said unto me, Make us gods, which shall go before us: for \[as for\] this Moses, the man that brought us up out of the land of Egypt, we wot not what is become of him.<small><small>[^62]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.32.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I said unto them, Whosoever hath any gold, let them break \[it\] off. [^57]: How truly contemptible must the object of their idolatry appear, when they were obliged to drink their god, reduced to powder, and strewed on the water! Some have asked, how gold, the most ductile and ponderous of all metals, could have been stamped into dust, and strewed on the water. In [[Deut.9|Deut 9:21]], this is fully explained. I took, says Moses, your sin, the calf which ye had made, and burnt it with fire; that is, melted it down, probably into ingots or gross plates, and stamped it, beat it into thin lamine, something like our gold leaf, and ground it very small, even until it was as small as dust, which might be very easily done by the action of the hands, when beat into thin plates or leaves as the original words ***ekkoth***, and ***dak***, imply.
