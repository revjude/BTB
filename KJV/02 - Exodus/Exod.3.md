<big><big>[[Exod.3.1 Moses at the Burning Bush|⥃]]</big></big>***Moses at the Burning Bush***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Acts.7|Acts 7:30–38]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now Moses kept<small><small>[^2]</small></small> the flock of Jethro his father<small><small>[^3]</small></small> in law, the priest of Midian: and he led the flock to the backside of the desert, and came to the mountain<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of God, \[even\] to Horeb<small><small>[^5]</small></small>.<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the angel<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> appeared unto him in a flame of fire out of the midst of a bush: and he looked, and, behold, the bush burned<small><small>[^8]</small></small> with fire, and the bush \[was\] not consumed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses said, I will now turn aside, and see this great sight, why the bush is not burnt.<small><small>[^9]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> saw that he turned aside to see, God called unto him<small><small>[^10]</small></small> out of the midst of the bush, and said, Moses<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, Moses. And he said, Here \[am\] I. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, Draw not<small><small>[^12]</small></small> nigh hither: put off<small><small>[^13]</small></small> thy shoes from off thy feet, for the place whereon thou standest \[is\] holy ground. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover he said, I \[am<small><small>[^14]</small></small>\] the God of thy father<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob. And Moses hid<small><small>[^16]</small></small> his face; for he was afraid to look upon God. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said, I have<small><small>[^17]</small></small> surely seen the affliction of my people which \[are\] in Egypt, and have heard their cry by reason<small><small>[^18]</small></small> of their taskmasters; for I know<small><small>[^19]</small></small> their sorrows; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I am<small><small>[^20]</small></small> come down to deliver<small><small>[^21]</small></small> them out of the hand of the Egyptians, and to bring them up out of that land unto a good<small><small>[^22]</small></small> land and a large, unto a land flowing with milk and honey; unto the place of the Canaanites<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, and the Hittites, and the Amorites, and the Perizzites, and the Hivites, and the Jebusites. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now therefore, behold, the cry<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of the children of Israel is come unto me: and I have<small><small>[^25]</small></small> also seen the oppression wherewith the Egyptians oppress them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Come now therefore, and I will send thee unto Pharaoh, that thou mayest bring forth my people the children of Israel out of Egypt.<small><small>[^26]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses said unto God, Who \[am\] I, that I should go unto Pharaoh, and that I should bring forth the children of Israel out of Egypt?<small><small>[^27]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, Certainly<small><small>[^28]</small></small> I will be with thee; and this \[shall be\] a token<small><small>[^29]</small></small> unto thee, that I have sent thee: When thou hast brought forth the people out of Egypt, ye shall<small><small>[^30]</small></small> serve God upon this mountain. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses said unto God, Behold, \[when\] I come unto the children of Israel, and shall say unto them, The God of your fathers hath sent me unto you; and they shall say to me, What \[is\] his name<small><small>[^31]</small></small>? what shall I say unto them? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And God said unto Moses, I AM THAT I AM: and he said, Thus shalt thou say unto the children of Israel, I AM hath<small><small>[^32]</small></small> sent me unto you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And God said moreover unto Moses, Thus shalt thou say unto the children of Israel, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^33]</small></small></span> God of your fathers, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob, hath sent me unto you: this \[is\] my name for ever<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, and this \[is\] my memorial<small><small>[^35]</small></small> unto all generations. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Go, and gather the elders<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of Israel together, and say unto them, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of your fathers, the God of Abraham, of Isaac, and of Jacob, appeared unto me, saying, I have surely visited<small><small>[^37]</small></small> you, and \[seen\] that which is done to you in Egypt: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I have said, I will bring<small><small>[^38]</small></small> you up out of the affliction of Egypt unto the land<small><small>[^39]</small></small> of the Canaanites, and the Hittites, and the Amorites, and the Perizzites, and the Hivites, and the Jebusites, unto a land flowing with milk and honey. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they<small><small>[^40]</small></small> shall hearken to thy voice: and thou<small><small>[^41]</small></small> shalt come, thou and the elders of Israel, unto the king of Egypt, and ye shall say unto him, The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^42]</small></small></span> God of the Hebrews hath met<small><small>[^43]</small></small> with us: and now let us go, we beseech thee, three days’<small><small>[^44]</small></small> journey into the wilderness, that we may<small><small>[^45]</small></small> sacrifice to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> our God. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And I am sure that the king of Egypt will not<small><small>[^46]</small></small> let you go, no, not by a mighty hand<small><small>[^47]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will stretch<small><small>[^48]</small></small> out my hand, and smite<small><small>[^49]</small></small> Egypt with all my wonders which I will do in the midst thereof: and after that<small><small>[^50]</small></small> he will let you go.<small><small>[^51]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will give this people favour in the sight of the Egyptians: and it shall come to pass, that, when ye go, ye shall not go empty:<small><small>[^52]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.3.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But<small><small>[^53]</small></small> every woman shall borrow<small><small>[^54]</small></small> of her neighbour, and of her that sojourneth in her house, jewels of silver, and jewels of gold, and raiment: and ye shall put \[them\] upon your sons, and upon your daughters; and ye shall spoil<small><small>[^55]</small></small> the Egyptians<small><small>[^56]</small></small>. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Exod.2|<< Exodus 2]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Exod.4|Exodus 4 >>]]</td></tr></table>
