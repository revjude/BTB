 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now these \[are\] the judgments<small><small>[^2]</small></small> which<small><small>[^3]</small></small> thou shalt set before them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If thou buy an Hebrew<small><small>[^4]</small></small> servant, six years he shall serve: and in the<small><small>[^5]</small></small> seventh he shall go out free for nothing. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If he came in by himself<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, he shall go out by himself: if he were married, then his wife shall go out with him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If his master have given him a wife, and she have born him sons or daughters; the wife and her children shall be her<small><small>[^7]</small></small> master’s, and he shall go out by himself. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if<small><small>[^8]</small></small> the servant shall plainly say<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, I love my master, my wife, and my children; I will not go out free: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then his master shall bring him unto the judges<small><small>[^10]</small></small>; he shall also bring him to the door, or unto the door post; and his master shall bore his ear<small><small>[^11]</small></small> through with an aul; and he shall serve him for ever<small><small>[^12]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And if a man sell<small><small>[^13]</small></small> his daughter to be a maidservant, she shall not go out<small><small>[^14]</small></small> as the menservants do. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If she please not<small><small>[^15]</small></small> her master, who hath<small><small>[^16]</small></small> betrothed her to himself, then shall he let her be redeemed: to sell her unto a strange nation he shall have no power, seeing<small><small>[^17]</small></small> he hath dealt deceitfully with her. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if he have betrothed her unto<small><small>[^18]</small></small> his son, he shall deal with her after the manner of daughters. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If he take him another \[wife\]; her food<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, her raiment, and her duty of marriage, shall he not diminish. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if he do not these three unto her, then shall she go out free without money.<small><small>[^20]</small></small>

***Personal Injury Laws*** ^bsb201

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that smiteth a man, so that he die, shall be surely put to death.<small><small>[^21]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if a man lie not<small><small>[^22]</small></small> in wait, but God<small><small>[^23]</small></small> deliver \[him\] into his hand; then I will appoint<small><small>[^24]</small></small> thee a place whither he shall flee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if a man come presumptuously<small><small>[^25]</small></small> upon his neighbour, to slay<small><small>[^26]</small></small> him with guile; thou shalt take him<small><small>[^27]</small></small> from mine altar, that he may die. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And he that smiteth his father, or his mother, shall be surely put to death.<small><small>[^28]</small></small><small><small>[^29]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And he that stealeth<small><small>[^30]</small></small> a man, and selleth him<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, or if he be found in<small><small>[^32]</small></small> his hand, he shall surely be put to death. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And he that curseth<small><small>[^33]</small></small> his father, or his mother, shall surely be put to death. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And if men<small><small>[^34]</small></small> strive together, and one smite another<small><small>[^35]</small></small> with a stone<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, or with \[his\] fist, and he die not, but keepeth \[his\] bed: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If he rise again, and walk abroad upon his staff<small><small>[^37]</small></small>, then shall he that smote \[him\] be quit: only he shall pay<small><small>[^38]</small></small> \[for\] the loss<small><small>[^39]</small></small> of his time, and shall cause \[him\] to be thoroughly healed. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And if a man smite<small><small>[^40]</small></small> his servant, or his maid, with a rod, and he die under his hand; he shall<small><small>[^41]</small></small> be surely punished<small><small>[^42]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Notwithstanding, if he continue a day or two, he shall not be punished: for he \[is\] his money.<small><small>[^43]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If men strive<small><small>[^44]</small></small>, and hurt a woman with child, so that her fruit depart \[from her\], and yet no mischief follow: he shall be surely punished, according as the woman’s husband will lay upon him; and he shall pay as the judges<small><small>[^45]</small></small> \[determine\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if \[any\] mischief follow, then thou shalt give life for life<small><small>[^46]</small></small>, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Eye for eye, tooth for tooth, hand for hand, foot for foot,<small><small>[^47]</small></small><small><small>[^48]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Burning for burning, wound for wound, stripe for stripe. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And if a man smite the eye of his servant, or the eye of his maid, that it perish; he shall let him go free for his eye’s sake.<small><small>[^49]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if he smite<small><small>[^50]</small></small> out his manservant’s tooth, or his maidservant’s tooth; he shall let him go free for his tooth’s sake. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]If an ox gore a man or a woman, that they die: then the ox<small><small>[^51]</small></small> shall be surely stoned, and his flesh shall not be eaten; but the owner of the ox \[shall be\] quit. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if the ox were wont to push with his horn in time past, and it hath been testified to his owner, and he hath not kept him in, but that he hath killed a man or a woman; the ox shall be stoned, and his owner also<small><small>[^52]</small></small> shall be put to death. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If there be laid on him a sum of money, then he shall give for the ransom<small><small>[^53]</small></small> of his life whatsoever is laid upon him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whether he have gored a son, or have gored a daughter, according to this judgment<small><small>[^54]</small></small> shall it be done unto him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If the ox shall push a manservant or a maidservant; he shall give unto their master thirty shekels of silver, and the ox<small><small>[^55]</small></small> shall be stoned.<small><small>[^56]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And if a man shall open a pit, or if a man shall dig a pit, and not cover it, and an ox or an ass fall therein;<small><small>[^57]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.34|34]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The owner of the pit shall make \[it\] good, \[and\] give money unto the owner of them; and the dead \[beast\] shall be his.<small><small>[^58]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.35|35]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And if one man’s ox hurt another’s, that he die; then they shall sell the live ox, and divide the money of it; and the dead \[ox\] also they shall divide. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.21.36|36]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Or if it be known that the ox hath used to push in time past, and his owner hath not kept him in; he shall surely pay ox for ox; and the dead shall be his own. <small><small>[^59]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Exod.20|<< Exodus 20]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Exod.22|Exodus 22 >>]]</td></tr></table>
