<big><big>[[Exod.18.1 The Visit of Jethro|⥃]]</big></big>***The Visit of Jethro***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When Jethro<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, the priest of Midian, Moses’ father in law, heard<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of all that God<small><small>[^4]</small></small> had done<small><small>[^5]</small></small> for Moses, and for Israel his people, \[and\] that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had brought Israel out of Egypt; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Jethro, Moses’ father in law, took Zipporah<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, Moses’ wife, after he had sent her back, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And her two sons<small><small>[^7]</small></small>; of which the name of the one \[was\] Gershom<small><small>[^8]</small></small>; for he said, I have been an alien in a strange land: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the name of the other \[was\] Eliezer<small><small>[^9]</small></small>; for the God of my father, \[said he\], \[was\] mine help, and delivered<small><small>[^10]</small></small> me from the sword of Pharaoh: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jethro, Moses’ father in law, came with his sons and his wife unto Moses into the wilderness, where he encamped at the mount of God:<small><small>[^11]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said<small><small>[^12]</small></small> unto Moses, I thy father in law Jethro am come unto thee, and thy wife, and her two sons with her. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses went<small><small>[^13]</small></small> out to meet his father in law, and did obeisance<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, and kissed<small><small>[^15]</small></small> him; and they asked each other of \[their\] welfare<small><small>[^16]</small></small>; and they came into the tent. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses told<small><small>[^17]</small></small> his father in law all that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had done unto Pharaoh and to the Egyptians for Israel’s sake, \[and\] all the<small><small>[^18]</small></small> travail that had come upon them<small><small>[^19]</small></small> by the way, and \[how\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^20]</small></small></span> delivered them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jethro rejoiced for all the goodness which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had done to Israel, whom he had delivered out of the hand of the Egyptians.<small><small>[^21]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jethro said, Blessed \[be\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, who hath delivered you out of the hand of the Egyptians, and out of the hand of Pharaoh, who hath delivered the people from under the hand of the Egyptians.<small><small>[^22]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now I<small><small>[^23]</small></small> know that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^24]</small></small></span> \[is\] greater than all gods: for in the thing<small><small>[^25]</small></small> wherein they dealt proudly<small><small>[^26]</small></small> \[he was\] above them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jethro, Moses’ father in law, took<small><small>[^27]</small></small> a burnt offering and sacrifices for God: and Aaron<small><small>[^28]</small></small> came, and all the elders of Israel, to eat bread<small><small>[^29]</small></small> with Moses’ father in law before God.

<big><big>[[Exod.18.13 Jethro Advises Moses|⥃]]</big></big>***Jethro Advises Moses***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Deut.1|Deuteronomy 1:9–18]] ]</sup></small>

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass on the morrow, that Moses sat to judge the people: and the people stood by Moses from the morning unto the evening.<small><small>[^30]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when Moses’ father in law saw all that he did to the people, he said, What \[is\] this thing that thou doest to the people? why sittest thou thyself alone, and all the people stand by thee from morning unto even? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses said unto his father in law, Because the people come unto me to enquire<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of God: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When they have a matter<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, they come unto me; and I judge between one and another<small><small>[^33]</small></small>, and I do make<small><small>[^34]</small></small> \[them\] know the statutes of God, and his laws. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses’ father in law said unto him, The thing that thou doest \[is\] not good<small><small>[^35]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou wilt surely wear away<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, both thou, and this people that \[is\] with thee: for this thing \[is\] too heavy for thee; thou art<small><small>[^37]</small></small> not able to perform it thyself alone. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hearken<small><small>[^38]</small></small> now unto my voice, I will give thee counsel, and God shall<small><small>[^39]</small></small> be with thee: Be thou<small><small>[^40]</small></small> for the people to God-ward, that thou mayest bring<small><small>[^41]</small></small> the causes unto God: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou shalt teach<small><small>[^42]</small></small> them ordinances and laws, and shalt shew them the way<small><small>[^43]</small></small> wherein they must walk, and the work<small><small>[^44]</small></small> that they must do. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover<small><small>[^45]</small></small> thou shalt provide out of all the people able men<small><small>[^46]</small></small>, such as<small><small>[^47]</small></small> fear God, men<small><small>[^48]</small></small> of truth, hating<small><small>[^49]</small></small> covetousness; and place \[such\] over them, \[to be\] rulers of thousands<small><small>[^50]</small></small>, \[and\] rulers of hundreds, rulers of fifties, and rulers of tens: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And let them judge the people at all seasons<small><small>[^51]</small></small>: and it shall be, \[that\] every great<small><small>[^52]</small></small> matter they shall<small><small>[^53]</small></small> bring unto thee, but every small matter they shall judge: so shall it be easier for thyself, and they shall bear \[the burden\] with thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If thou shalt do this thing, and God<small><small>[^54]</small></small> command thee \[so\], then thou shalt be able to endure, and all this<small><small>[^55]</small></small> people shall also go to their place in peace. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So Moses hearkened to the voice of his father in law, and did all that he had said.<small><small>[^56]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses chose able men out of all Israel, and made them heads over the people, rulers of thousands, rulers of hundreds, rulers of fifties, and rulers of tens.<small><small>[^57]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.18.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they judged the people at all<small><small>[^58]</small></small> seasons: the hard causes<small><small>[^59]</small></small> they brought unto Moses, but every small matter they judged themselves.
