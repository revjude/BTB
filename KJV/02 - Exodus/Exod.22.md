***Property Laws*** ^bsb202

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If a man shall steal an ox, or a sheep<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, and kill it, or sell it; he shall<small><small>[^3]</small></small> restore five oxen<small><small>[^4]</small></small> for an ox, and four sheep for a sheep. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]If a thief be found breaking<small><small>[^5]</small></small> up, and be smitten that he die, \[there shall\] no blood<small><small>[^6]</small></small> \[be shed\] for him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If the sun be risen upon him, \[there shall be\] blood \[shed\] for him; \[for\] he should make full restitution; if he have nothing, then he shall<small><small>[^7]</small></small> be sold for his theft. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If the theft be certainly found<small><small>[^8]</small></small> in his hand alive, whether it be ox, or ass, or sheep; he shall restore double<small><small>[^9]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]If a man shall cause a field or vineyard to be eaten, and shall put in his beast, and shall feed in another man’s field; of the best of his own field, and of the best of his own vineyard, shall he make restitution<small><small>[^10]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]If fire break out<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, and catch in thorns, so that the stacks of corn<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, or the standing corn, or the field, be consumed \[therewith\]; he that kindled the fire<small><small>[^13]</small></small> shall surely make restitution. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If a man shall deliver unto his neighbour money or stuff to keep, and it be stolen out of the man’s house; if the thief be found<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, let him pay double<small><small>[^15]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If the thief be not found, then the master of the house shall be brought unto the judges<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, \[to see\] whether he have put his hand unto his neighbour’s goods. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For all manner of trespass<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, \[whether it be\] for ox, for ass, for sheep, for raiment, \[or\] for any manner of lost thing, which \[another\] challengeth to be his, the cause of both parties<small><small>[^18]</small></small> shall come before the judges; \[and\] whom the judges shall condemn, he shall pay double unto his<small><small>[^19]</small></small> neighbour. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If a man deliver unto his neighbour an ass, or an ox, or a sheep, or any beast, to keep; and it die, or be hurt, or driven away, no man seeing \[it\]:<small><small>[^20]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Then\] shall an oath of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^21]</small></small></span> be between them both, that he hath not<small><small>[^22]</small></small> put his hand unto his neighbour’s goods; and the owner of it shall accept \[thereof\], and he shall not make \[it\] good. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if it be stolen from him<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, he shall make restitution unto the owner thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If it be torn in pieces<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, \[then\] let him bring it \[for\] witness<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, \[and\] he shall not make good that which was torn. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And if a man borrow<small><small>[^26]</small></small> \[ought\] of his neighbour, and it be hurt, or die, the owner thereof \[being\] not with it, he shall surely make \[it\] good<small><small>[^27]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[But\] if the owner thereof \[be\] with it, he shall not make \[it\] good: if it \[be\] an hired \[thing\], it came for his hire<small><small>[^28]</small></small>.

***Laws of Social Responsibility*** ^bsb203

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if a man entice<small><small>[^29]</small></small> a maid that is not betrothed, and lie with her, he shall surely endow her to be his wife. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If her father utterly<small><small>[^30]</small></small> refuse to give her unto him, he shall pay<small><small>[^31]</small></small> money according to the dowry of virgins<small><small>[^32]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Thou shalt not suffer a witch to live.<small><small>[^33]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Whosoever lieth with a beast shall surely be put to death.<small><small>[^34]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]He that sacrificeth<small><small>[^35]</small></small> unto \[any\] god, save unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> only, he shall be utterly<small><small>[^36]</small></small> destroyed. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Thou shalt neither vex a stranger<small><small>[^37]</small></small>, nor oppress him: for ye were strangers<small><small>[^38]</small></small> in the land of Egypt. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Ye shall not afflict any widow, or fatherless child.<small><small>[^39]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If thou afflict them in any wise, and they cry at all<small><small>[^40]</small></small> unto me, I will surely<small><small>[^41]</small></small> hear their cry; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And my wrath<small><small>[^42]</small></small> shall wax hot, and I will kill you with the sword; and your wives<small><small>[^43]</small></small> shall be widows, and your children fatherless. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If thou lend money to \[any of\] my people \[that is\] poor by thee, thou shalt not be to him as an usurer, neither shalt thou lay upon him usury.<small><small>[^44]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If thou at all take thy neighbour’s raiment to pledge<small><small>[^45]</small></small>, thou shalt deliver it unto him by that the sun goeth down: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For that \[is\] his covering only, it \[is\] his raiment for his skin: wherein shall he sleep? and it shall come to pass, when he crieth<small><small>[^46]</small></small> unto me, that I will hear; for I \[am\] gracious<small><small>[^47]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Thou shalt not revile the gods<small><small>[^48]</small></small>, nor curse<small><small>[^49]</small></small> the ruler of thy people. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Thou shalt not delay<small><small>[^50]</small></small> \[to offer\] the first of thy ripe fruits<small><small>[^51]</small></small>, and of thy liquors<small><small>[^52]</small></small>: the firstborn<small><small>[^53]</small></small> of thy sons shalt thou give unto me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Likewise<small><small>[^54]</small></small> shalt thou do with thine oxen, \[and\] with thy sheep: seven days<small><small>[^55]</small></small> it shall be with his dam; on the eighth day thou shalt give it me. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Exod.22.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And ye shall be holy<small><small>[^56]</small></small> men unto me: neither<small><small>[^57]</small></small> shall ye eat \[any\] flesh \[that is\] torn of beasts in the field; ye shall cast it to the dogs. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Exod.21|<< Exodus 21]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Exod.23|Exodus 23 >>]]</td></tr></table>
