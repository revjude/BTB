***Daniel Removed to Babylon*** ^bsb1938

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the third year of the reign of Jehoiakim king of Judah came Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon unto Jerusalem, and besieged it.<small><small>[^2]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the Lord<small><small>[^3]</small></small> gave Jehoiakim king of Judah into his hand, with part<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of the vessels of the house of God: which he carried into the land of Shinar<small><small>[^5]</small></small> to the house of his god; and he<small><small>[^6]</small></small> brought the vessels into the treasure house of his god.<small><small>[^7]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And the king spake unto Ashpenaz the master of his eunuchs, that he should bring \[certain\] of the children of Israel, and of the king’s seed, and of the princes;<small><small>[^8]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Children<small><small>[^9]</small></small> in whom<small><small>[^10]</small></small> \[was\] no blemish, but well favoured, and skilful<small><small>[^11]</small></small> in all wisdom, and cunning in knowledge, and understanding science, and such as \[had\] ability<small><small>[^12]</small></small> in them to stand in the king’s palace, and whom they might teach the learning and the tongue of the Chaldeans. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king appointed them a daily<small><small>[^13]</small></small> provision of the king’s meat, and of the wine which he drank<small><small>[^14]</small></small>: so nourishing them three years, that at the end thereof they might stand<small><small>[^15]</small></small> before the king. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now among these were of the children of Judah, Daniel<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Unto whom the prince<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of the eunuchs gave names<small><small>[^18]</small></small>: for he gave unto Daniel \[the name\] of Belteshazzar; and to Hananiah<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, of Shadrach; and to Mishael, of Meshach; and to Azariah, of Abed–nego.

***Daniel’s Faithfulness*** ^bsb1939

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But Daniel purposed<small><small>[^20]</small></small> in his heart that he would not defile<small><small>[^21]</small></small> himself with the portion of the king’s meat, nor with the wine which he drank: therefore he requested of the prince of the eunuchs that he might not defile himself. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now God had brought Daniel into favour and tender love with the prince of the eunuchs.<small><small>[^22]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the prince of the eunuchs said unto Daniel, I fear<small><small>[^23]</small></small> my lord the king, who hath appointed your meat and your drink: for why should he see your faces worse liking<small><small>[^24]</small></small> than the children which \[are\] of your sort<small><small>[^25]</small></small>? then shall ye make \[me\] endanger my head to the king. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said Daniel to Melzar<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, whom the prince of the eunuchs had set over Daniel, Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Prove thy servants, I beseech thee, ten days; and let them give us pulse to eat<small><small>[^27]</small></small>, and water to drink. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then let our countenances be looked upon before thee, and the countenance of the children that eat of the portion of the king’s meat: and as thou seest, deal with thy servants. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So he consented to them in this matter, and proved them ten days. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And at the end of ten days their<small><small>[^28]</small></small> countenances appeared fairer and fatter in flesh than all the children which did eat the portion of the king’s meat. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.1.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus Melzar took away the portion of their meat, and the wine that they should drink; and gave them pulse.<small><small>[^29]</small></small>

***Daniel’s Wisdom*** ^bsb1940
