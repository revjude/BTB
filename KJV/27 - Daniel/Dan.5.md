***Belshazzar’s Feast*** ^bsb1953

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.5.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Belshazzar the king made<small><small>[^2]</small></small> a great feast to a thousand of his lords, and drank wine before the thousand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.5.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Belshazzar<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, whiles he tasted the wine, commanded to bring the golden<small><small>[^4]</small></small> and silver vessels which his father<small><small>[^5]</small></small> Nebuchadnezzar had taken out<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of the temple which \[was\] in Jerusalem; that the king, and his princes, his wives, and his concubines, might<small><small>[^7]</small></small> drink therein. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.5.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then they brought the golden vessels that were taken out of the temple of the house of God which \[was\] at Jerusalem; and the king, and his princes, his wives, and his concubines, drank in them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.5.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They drank wine, and praised<small><small>[^8]</small></small> the gods of gold<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, and of silver, of brass, of iron, of wood, and of stone.

***Daniel Interprets the Handwriting*** ^bsb1955

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.5.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then was Daniel brought in before the king. \[And\] the king spake and said unto Daniel, \[Art\] thou<small><small>[^42]</small></small> that Daniel, which \[art\] of the children<small><small>[^43]</small></small> of the captivity of Judah, whom the king my father<small><small>[^44]</small></small> brought out of Jewry<small><small>[^45]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.5.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have even heard of thee, that the spirit of the gods \[is\] in thee, and \[that\] light and understanding and excellent wisdom is found in thee.<small><small>[^46]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.5.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And now the wise \[men\], the astrologers, have been brought in before me, that they should read this writing, and make known unto me the interpretation thereof: but they could not shew the interpretation of the thing:<small><small>[^47]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.5.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I have heard of thee, that thou canst make<small><small>[^48]</small></small> interpretations, and dissolve doubts: now if thou canst read the writing, and make known to me the interpretation thereof, thou shalt<small><small>[^49]</small></small> be clothed with scarlet, and \[have\] a chain of gold about thy neck, and shalt be the third ruler in the kingdom.
