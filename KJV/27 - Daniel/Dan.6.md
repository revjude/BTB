***The Plot against Daniel*** ^bsb1956

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> It pleased Darius<small><small>[^2]</small></small> to set over the kingdom an<small><small>[^3]</small></small> hundred and twenty princes, which should be over the whole kingdom; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And over these three presidents; of<small><small>[^4]</small></small> whom Daniel \[was\] first: that<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the princes might give accounts unto them, and the<small><small>[^6]</small></small> king should have no damage. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then this Daniel was preferred<small><small>[^7]</small></small> above the presidents an<small><small>[^8]</small></small>d princes, because an excellent spirit \[was\] in him; and the king thought<small><small>[^9]</small></small> to set him over the whole realm. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then the presidents and princes sought<small><small>[^10]</small></small> to find occasion against Daniel concerning the kingdom; but<small><small>[^11]</small></small> they could find none occasion nor fault; forasmuch as he \[was\] faithful, neither was there any error or fault found in him.<small><small>[^12]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said these men, We shall not find any occasion against this Daniel, except we find \[it\] against him concerning the law of his God.<small><small>[^13]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then these presidents and princes assembled together<small><small>[^14]</small></small> to the king, and said thus unto him, King<small><small>[^15]</small></small> Darius, live for ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All<small><small>[^16]</small></small> the presidents of the kingdom, the governors, and the princes, the counsellors, and the captains, have consulted<small><small>[^17]</small></small> together to establish a royal statute, and to make a firm decree<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, that whosoever shall ask a petition of any God or man for thirty days, save of thee, O king, he shall<small><small>[^19]</small></small> be cast into the den of lions. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now, O king, establish<small><small>[^20]</small></small> the decree, and sign the writing, that it be not changed, according<small><small>[^21]</small></small> to the law of the Medes and Persians, which altereth not<small><small>[^22]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore king Darius signed<small><small>[^23]</small></small> the writing and the decree.

***Daniel in the Lions’ Den*** ^bsb1957

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Now when<small><small>[^24]</small></small> Daniel knew that the writing was signed, he went into his house; and his windows<small><small>[^25]</small></small> being open in his chamber toward Jerusalem, he kneeled<small><small>[^26]</small></small> upon his knees three<small><small>[^27]</small></small> times a day, and prayed, and gave<small><small>[^28]</small></small> thanks before his God, as he<small><small>[^29]</small></small> did aforetime. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then these men assembled<small><small>[^30]</small></small>, and found Daniel praying and making supplication before his God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then they<small><small>[^31]</small></small> came near, and spake before the king concerning the king’s decree; Hast thou not signed a decree, that every man that shall ask \[a petition\] of any God or man within thirty days, save of thee, O king, shall be cast into the den<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of lions? The king answered and said, The thing<small><small>[^33]</small></small> \[is\] true, according to the law of the Medes and Persians, which altereth not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then answered they and said before the king, That Daniel<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, which \[is\] of the children of the captivity of Judah, regardeth<small><small>[^35]</small></small> not thee, O king, nor the decree that thou hast signed, but maketh his petition three times a day. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the king, when he heard \[these\] words, was sore<small><small>[^36]</small></small> displeased with himself, and set \[his\] heart on Daniel to deliver him: and he<small><small>[^37]</small></small> laboured till the going down of the sun to deliver him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then these men assembled unto the king, and said unto the king, Know<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, O king, that the law of the Medes and Persians \[is\], That no decree nor statute which the king establisheth may be changed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the king<small><small>[^39]</small></small> commanded, and they brought Daniel, and cast \[him\] into the den of lions. \[Now\] the king spake and said unto Daniel, Thy God<small><small>[^40]</small></small> whom thou servest continually, he will deliver thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And a stone<small><small>[^41]</small></small> was brought, and laid upon the mouth of the den; and the king sealed it with his own signet, and with the signet of his lords; that the purpose might not be changed concerning Daniel. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the king went to his palace, and passed<small><small>[^42]</small></small> the night fasting: neither were instruments<small><small>[^43]</small></small> of musick brought before him: and<small><small>[^44]</small></small> his sleep went from him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the king arose very early in the morning, and went in haste unto the den of lions.<small><small>[^45]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when he came to the den, he cried with a lamentable voice unto Daniel: \[and\] the king spake and said to Daniel, O Daniel, servant of the living God, is<small><small>[^46]</small></small> thy God, whom thou servest<small><small>[^47]</small></small> continually, able<small><small>[^48]</small></small> to deliver thee from the lions? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said Daniel unto the king, O king<small><small>[^49]</small></small>, live for ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My God<small><small>[^50]</small></small> hath sent<small><small>[^51]</small></small> his angel, and hath shut<small><small>[^52]</small></small> the lions’ mouths, that they have not hurt me: forasmuch<small><small>[^53]</small></small> as before him innocency was found in me; and also<small><small>[^54]</small></small> before thee, O king, have I done no hurt. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then was<small><small>[^55]</small></small> the king exceeding glad for him, and commanded that they should take Daniel up out of the den. So Daniel was taken up out of the den, and no manner of hurt was found upon him, because<small><small>[^56]</small></small> he believed in his God. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.6.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And the king commanded, and they brought<small><small>[^57]</small></small> those men which had accused Daniel, and they cast \[them<small><small>[^58]</small></small>\] into the den of lions, them, their children<small><small>[^59]</small></small>, and their wives; and the lions<small><small>[^60]</small></small> had the mastery of them, and brake all their bones in pieces or ever they came at the bottom of the den.
