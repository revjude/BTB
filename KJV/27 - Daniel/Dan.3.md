***Nebuchadnezzar’s Golden Statue*** ^bsb1945

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nebuchadnezzar the king made<small><small>[^2]</small></small> an image of gold, whose height \[was\] threescore cubits, \[and\] the breadth thereof six cubits: he set it up in the plain of Dura, in the province<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of Babylon.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Nebuchadnezzar the king sent<small><small>[^5]</small></small> to gather together the princes, the governors, and the captains, the judges, the treasurers, the counsellors, the sheriffs, and all the rulers of the provinces, to come to the dedication of the image which Nebuchadnezzar the king had set up. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then the princes<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, the governors<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, and captains<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, the judges<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, the treasurers<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, the counsellors<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, the sheriffs<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, and all the rulers of the provinces, were gathered together unto the dedication of the image that Nebuchadnezzar the king had set up; and they stood before the image that Nebuchadnezzar had set up. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then an herald cried aloud<small><small>[^13]</small></small>, To you it is commanded<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, O people<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, nations, and languages, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[That\] at what time ye hear the sound of the cornet<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, flute<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, harp, sackbut, psaltery, dulcimer<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, and all kinds of musick, ye fall down and worship the golden image that Nebuchadnezzar the king hath set up: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And whoso falleth<small><small>[^19]</small></small> not down and worshippeth shall the same<small><small>[^20]</small></small> hour be cast into the midst of a burning<small><small>[^21]</small></small> fiery furnace. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore at that time, when<small><small>[^22]</small></small> all the people<small><small>[^23]</small></small> heard the sound of the cornet, flute, harp, sackbut, psaltery, and all kinds of musick, all the people, the nations, and the languages, fell down \[and\] worshipped the golden image that Nebuchadnezzar the king had set up.

***Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego Accused*** ^bsb1946

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore at that time certain Chaldeans came near, and accused<small><small>[^24]</small></small> the Jews. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They spake and said to the king<small><small>[^25]</small></small> Nebuchadnezzar, O king<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, live for ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou, O king, hast made<small><small>[^27]</small></small> a decree, that every man that shall hear the sound of the cornet<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, flute, harp, sackbut, psaltery, and dulcimer, and all kinds of musick, shall fall down and worship the golden image: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And whoso falleth not down and worshippeth, \[that\] he should be cast into the midst of a burning fiery furnace. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There are certain<small><small>[^29]</small></small> Jews whom thou hast set over the affairs of the province of Babylon, Shadrach, Meshach, and Abed–nego; these men, O king, have not regarded thee<small><small>[^30]</small></small>: they serve not thy gods, nor worship the golden image which thou hast set up. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then Nebuchadnezzar in \[his<small><small>[^31]</small></small>\] rage and fury commanded to bring Shadrach, Meshach, and Abed–nego. Then<small><small>[^32]</small></small> they brought these men before the king. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nebuchadnezzar spake and said unto them, \[Is it\] true<small><small>[^33]</small></small>, O Shadrach, Meshach, and Abed–nego, do not ye serve my gods<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, nor worship the golden image which I have set up? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now if ye be ready that at what time ye hear<small><small>[^35]</small></small> the sound of the cornet, flute, harp<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, sackbut<small><small>[^37]</small></small>, psaltery<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, and dulcimer<small><small>[^39]</small></small>, and all kinds of musick, ye fall<small><small>[^40]</small></small> down and worship the image which I have made; \[well<small><small>[^41]</small></small>\]: but if ye worship not, ye shall be cast the same hour into the midst of a burning fiery furnace; and who<small><small>[^42]</small></small> \[is\] that God that shall deliver you out of my hands?<small><small>[^43]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Shadrach, Meshach, and Abed–nego, answered and said to the king, O Nebuchadnezzar, we \[are\] not careful to answer thee in this matter. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If it be \[so\], our God<small><small>[^44]</small></small> whom we serve is able to deliver us from the burning fiery furnace, and he will deliver \[us\] out of thine hand, O king. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.3.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if not, be it<small><small>[^45]</small></small> known unto thee, O king, that<small><small>[^46]</small></small> we will not serve thy gods, nor worship the golden image which thou hast set up.
