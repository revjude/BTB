$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I Nebuchadnezzar<small><small>[^10]</small></small> was<small><small>[^11]</small></small> at rest in mine house, and flourishing in my palace: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I saw a dream<small><small>[^12]</small></small> which made me afraid, and the thoughts<small><small>[^13]</small></small> upon my bed and the visions of my head troubled me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore made I a decree to bring<small><small>[^14]</small></small> in all the wise \[men\] of Babylon before me, that they might make known unto me the interpretation of the dream. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then came<small><small>[^15]</small></small> in the magicians, the astrologers, the Chaldeans, and the soothsayers: and I told the dream before them; but<small><small>[^16]</small></small> they did not make known unto me the interpretation thereof.

***Daniel Interprets the Second Dream*** ^bsb1950

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Daniel<small><small>[^46]</small></small>, whose name \[was\] Belteshazzar, was astonied<small><small>[^47]</small></small> for one hour, and his thoughts troubled him. The king spake, and said, Belteshazzar, let<small><small>[^48]</small></small> not the dream, or the interpretation thereof, trouble thee. Belteshazzar answered and said, My lord<small><small>[^49]</small></small>, the dream<small><small>[^50]</small></small> \[be\] to them that hate thee, and the interpretation thereof to thine enemies. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The tree that thou sawest, which grew, and was strong, whose height reached unto the heaven, and the sight thereof to all the earth;<small><small>[^51]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whose leaves \[were\] fair, and the fruit thereof much, and in it \[was\] meat for all; under which the beasts of the field dwelt, and upon whose branches the fowls of the heaven had their habitation: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> It \[is\] thou<small><small>[^52]</small></small>, O king, that art grown and become strong: for thy greatness<small><small>[^53]</small></small> is grown, and reacheth unto heaven, and thy dominion to the end of the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And whereas the king saw<small><small>[^54]</small></small> a watcher and an holy one coming down from heaven, and saying, Hew the tree down, and destroy it; yet leave the stump of the roots thereof in the earth, even with a band of iron and brass, in the tender grass of the field; and let it be wet with the dew of heaven, and \[let\] his<small><small>[^55]</small></small> portion \[be\] with the beasts of the field, till seven times pass over him; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> This \[is\] the interpretation, O king, and this \[is\] the decree<small><small>[^56]</small></small> of the most High, which is come<small><small>[^57]</small></small> upon my lord the king: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That they shall drive<small><small>[^58]</small></small> thee from men, and thy dwelling<small><small>[^59]</small></small> shall be with the beasts of the field, and they shall make thee to eat<small><small>[^60]</small></small> grass as oxen, and they shall wet thee with the dew of heaven, and seven times shall pass over thee, till<small><small>[^61]</small></small> thou know that the most High ruleth in the kingdom of men, and giveth it to whomsoever he will. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And whereas they commanded to leave<small><small>[^62]</small></small> the stump of the tree roots; thy kingdom shall be sure unto thee, after that thou shalt have known that the heavens<small><small>[^63]</small></small> do rule. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore, O king, let<small><small>[^64]</small></small> my counsel be acceptable unto thee, and break<small><small>[^65]</small></small> off thy sins by righteousness, and thine iniquities by shewing<small><small>[^66]</small></small> mercy to the poor; if it<small><small>[^67]</small></small> may be a lengthening of thy tranquillity<small><small>[^68]</small></small>.

***The Second Dream Fulfilled*** ^bsb1951

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All this came upon the king Nebuchadnezzar.<small><small>[^69]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> At the end<small><small>[^70]</small></small> of twelve months he walked in<small><small>[^71]</small></small> the palace of the kingdom of Babylon. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The king spake, and said, Is not<small><small>[^72]</small></small> this great<small><small>[^73]</small></small> Babylon, that<small><small>[^74]</small></small> I have built for the house of the kingdom by the might of my power, and for<small><small>[^75]</small></small> the honour of my majesty? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> While the word<small><small>[^76]</small></small> \[was\] in the king’s mouth, there fell<small><small>[^77]</small></small> a voice from heaven, \[saying\], O king Nebuchadnezzar, to thee it is spoken; The kingdom<small><small>[^78]</small></small> is departed from thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they shall drive<small><small>[^79]</small></small> thee from men, and thy dwelling \[shall be\] with the beasts of the field: they shall make thee to eat grass as oxen, and seven times shall pass over thee, until<small><small>[^80]</small></small> thou know that the most High ruleth in the kingdom of men, and giveth it to whomsoever he will. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.4.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The same<small><small>[^81]</small></small> hour was the thing fulfilled upon Nebuchadnezzar: and he was<small><small>[^82]</small></small> driven from men, and did eat grass as oxen, and his body was wet with the dew of heaven, till his hairs were grown like eagles’ \[feathers\], and his nails like birds’ \[claws\].
