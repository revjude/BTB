<big><big>[[Dan.8.1 Daniel’s Vision of the Ram and the Goat|⥃]]</big></big>***Daniel’s Vision of the Ram and the Goat***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the third<small><small>[^2]</small></small> year of the reign of king Belshazzar a vision appeared unto me, \[even unto\] me Daniel<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, after that which appeared unto me at the first.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I saw in<small><small>[^5]</small></small> a vision; and it came to pass, when I saw, that I \[was\] at Shushan<small><small>[^6]</small></small> \[in\] the palace, which \[is\] in the province<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of Elam; and I saw in a vision, and I was by the river of Ulai<small><small>[^8]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then I lifted<small><small>[^9]</small></small> up mine eyes, and saw, and, behold, there stood before the river a ram<small><small>[^10]</small></small> which had \[two\] horns: and the \[two\] horns \[were\] high; but one<small><small>[^11]</small></small> \[was\] higher than the other<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, and the higher came up last. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I saw the ram pushing<small><small>[^13]</small></small> westward, and northward, and southward; so that no beasts might stand before him, neither<small><small>[^14]</small></small> \[was there any\] that could deliver out of his hand; but<small><small>[^15]</small></small> he did according to his will, and became great. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And as I was considering, behold, an he goat<small><small>[^16]</small></small> came from the west on the face of the whole earth, and touched not the ground<small><small>[^17]</small></small>: and the goat \[had\] a notable horn between his eyes. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he came to the<small><small>[^18]</small></small> ram that had \[two\] horns, which I had seen standing before the river, and ran unto him in the fury of his power. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I saw him come close unto the ram, and he was moved<small><small>[^19]</small></small> with choler against him, and smote the ram, and brake his two horns: and there was no<small><small>[^20]</small></small> power in the ram to stand before him, but<small><small>[^21]</small></small> he cast him down to the ground, and stamped upon him: and there was none<small><small>[^22]</small></small> that could deliver the ram out of his hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore the he goat waxed<small><small>[^23]</small></small> very great: and when<small><small>[^24]</small></small> he was strong, the great<small><small>[^25]</small></small> horn was broken; and for it came up four notable ones toward<small><small>[^26]</small></small> the four winds of heaven. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And out of one of them came<small><small>[^27]</small></small> forth a little horn, which waxed exceeding great, toward the south, and toward the east, and toward the pleasant<small><small>[^28]</small></small> \[land\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it waxed great, \[even\] to the host<small><small>[^29]</small></small> of heaven; and it cast down \[some\] of the host and of the stars to the ground, and stamped<small><small>[^30]</small></small> upon them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea, he magnified<small><small>[^31]</small></small> \[himself\] even to<small><small>[^32]</small></small> the prince<small><small>[^33]</small></small> of the host, and by him<small><small>[^34]</small></small> the daily<small><small>[^35]</small></small> \[sacrifice\] was taken away, and the place<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of his sanctuary was cast down. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And an host was given \[him\] against the daily \[sacrifice<small><small>[^37]</small></small>\] by reason of transgression, and it cast<small><small>[^38]</small></small> down the truth to the ground; and it practised<small><small>[^39]</small></small>, and prospered. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then I heard one saint<small><small>[^40]</small></small> speaking, and another saint said unto that certain \[saint<small><small>[^41]</small></small>\] which spake, How<small><small>[^42]</small></small> long \[shall be\] the vision<small><small>[^43]</small></small> \[concerning\] the daily \[sacrifice\], and the<small><small>[^44]</small></small> transgression of desolation<small><small>[^45]</small></small>, to give both the sanctuary and the host to be<small><small>[^46]</small></small> trodden under foot? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Dan.8.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said unto<small><small>[^47]</small></small> me, Unto two thousand<small><small>[^48]</small></small> and three hundred days<small><small>[^49]</small></small>; then<small><small>[^50]</small></small> shall the sanctuary be cleansed<small><small>[^51]</small></small>.
