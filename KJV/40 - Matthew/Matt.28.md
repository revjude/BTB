<big><big>[[Matt.28.1 The Resurrection|⥃]]</big></big>***The Resurrection***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Mark.16|Mark 16:1–8]] <big>†</big> [[Luke.24|Luke 24:1–12]] <big>†</big> [[John.20|John 20:1–9]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Matt.28.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the end<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of the sabbath, as it began to dawn toward the first \[day\] of the week, came Mary Magdalene<small><small>[^3]</small></small> and the other Mary to see the sepulchre. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Matt.28.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And, behold, there<small><small>[^4]</small></small> was<small><small>[^5]</small></small> a great earthquake: for<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the angel of the Lord descended from heaven, and came and rolled back the stone from the door, and sat upon it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Matt.28.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> His countenance<small><small>[^7]</small></small> was like lightning, and his raiment<small><small>[^8]</small></small> white as snow: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Matt.28.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And for fear of him the<small><small>[^9]</small></small> keepers did shake<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, and became as dead \[men\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Matt.28.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the angel answered and said unto the women, Fear<small><small>[^11]</small></small> not ye: for I know that ye seek<small><small>[^12]</small></small> Jesus, which was crucified. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Matt.28.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He is not here: for he is risen, as<small><small>[^13]</small></small> he said. Come<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, see the place where the Lord lay. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Matt.28.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And go<small><small>[^15]</small></small> quickly, and tell his disciples that he is risen from the dead; and, behold, he goeth<small><small>[^16]</small></small> before you into Galilee; there shall ye see him: lo<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, I have told you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Matt.28.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they departed quickly from the sepulchre with<small><small>[^18]</small></small> fear and great joy; and did run to bring his disciples word. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Matt.28.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And as<small><small>[^19]</small></small> they went to tell his disciples, behold, Jesus met them, saying, <span style="color:red;">All hail<small><small>[^20]</small></small>.</span> And they came and held<small><small>[^21]</small></small> him by the feet, and worshipped<small><small>[^22]</small></small> him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Matt.28.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said Jesus unto them, <span style="color:red;">Be<small><small>[^23]</small></small> not afraid: go<small><small>[^24]</small></small> tell my<small><small>[^25]</small></small> brethren that they go into Galilee, and there shall they see me.</span>

[^1]:  [[Matt.28|Matt 28:1]], Christ’s resurrection is declared by an angel to the women; [[Matt.28|Matt 28:9]], He himself appears unto them; [[Matt.28|Matt 28:11]], The chief priests give the soldiers money to say that he was stolen out of his sepulchre; [[Matt.28|Matt 28:16]], Christ appears to his disciples, [[Matt.28|Matt 28:18]]. and sends them to baptize and teach all nations.

[^2]: The Hebrew word ***Schabbath*** from which our English word is derived, signifies rest, and is applied to all solemn festivals, equally with that one day of every week devoted to the worship of God; [[Ezek.20|Ezek 20:21]], "they polluted my sabbaths." Three evangelists say, the transaction recorded in this verse, occurred upon the first day of the week, early in the morning, about sunrising, and John says, while it was yet dark. <span class="asgreek"><span class='asgreek'>Οψε</span></span> <small><sup>[[G3796]]</sup></small>, <span class="asgreek"><span class='asgreek'>σαββατων</span></span> <small><sup>[[G4521]]</sup></small>, does not signify "in the evening of sabbath," but "sabbaths." Hence, the great feast having been concluded, the term "end of the sabbaths" denotes the time very clearly. Again, it may be observed that the Jews, speaking of their passover, sometimes speak according to their civil computation, wherein they measured their days from sun-rising to sun-rising. Sometimes according to their sacred computation, which was from sun-set to sun-set. This reconciles [[Num.28|Num 28:18]], which seems to make the fourteenth day of the first month, the first day of unleavened bread.
