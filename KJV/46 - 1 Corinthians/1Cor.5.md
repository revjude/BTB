<big><big>[[1Cor.5.1 Immorality Rebuked|⥃]]</big></big>***Immorality Rebuked***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Lev.20|Leviticus 20:10–21]] <big>†</big> [[Prov.5|Proverbs 5:1–23]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.5.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> It is reported<small><small>[^2]</small></small> commonly \[that there is\] fornication<small><small>[^3]</small></small> among you, and<small><small>[^4]</small></small> such fornication as is not so much as named among the Gentiles, that one<small><small>[^5]</small></small> should have his father’s wife. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.5.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And ye are<small><small>[^6]</small></small> puffed up, and have not rather mourned<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, that he that hath done this deed might<small><small>[^8]</small></small> be taken away from among you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.5.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I verily, as absent<small><small>[^9]</small></small> in body, but present in spirit, have judged<small><small>[^10]</small></small> already, as though I were present, \[concerning\] him that hath so done this deed, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.5.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the name<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of our Lord Jesus Christ, when<small><small>[^12]</small></small> ye are gathered together, and my spirit, with the power of our Lord Jesus Christ, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.5.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To deliver<small><small>[^13]</small></small> such an one unto Satan for the destruction of the flesh, that<small><small>[^14]</small></small> the spirit may be saved in the day<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of the Lord Jesus. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.5.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Your glorying<small><small>[^16]</small></small> \[is\] not good. Know ye not that a little<small><small>[^17]</small></small> leaven leaveneth the whole lump? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.5.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Purge<small><small>[^18]</small></small> out therefore the old leaven, that ye may<small><small>[^19]</small></small> be a new lump, as ye are unleavened. For even Christ<small><small>[^20]</small></small> our passover is sacrificed<small><small>[^21]</small></small> for us: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.5.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore let<small><small>[^22]</small></small> us keep the feast<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, not<small><small>[^24]</small></small> with old leaven, neither<small><small>[^25]</small></small> with the leaven of malice and wickedness; but<small><small>[^26]</small></small> with the unleavened \[bread\] of sincerity and truth.

<big><big>[[1Cor.5.9 Expel the Immoral Brother|⥃]]</big></big>***Expel the Immoral Brother***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.5.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I wrote unto you in an epistle not<small><small>[^27]</small></small> to company with fornicators: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.5.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet not altogether<small><small>[^28]</small></small> with the fornicators of this<small><small>[^29]</small></small> world, or with the covetous, or extortioners, or with idolaters; for<small><small>[^30]</small></small> then must ye needs go out of the world. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.5.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But now I have written unto you not to keep company, if any man that is called<small><small>[^31]</small></small> a brother be a fornicator<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, or covetous<small><small>[^33]</small></small>, or an idolater<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, or a railer<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, or a drunkard<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, or an extortioner<small><small>[^37]</small></small>; with<small><small>[^38]</small></small> such an one no not to eat. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.5.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For what<small><small>[^39]</small></small> have I to do to judge them<small><small>[^40]</small></small> also that are without? do not<small><small>[^41]</small></small> ye judge them that are within? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.5.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But them that are without God<small><small>[^42]</small></small> judgeth. Therefore<small><small>[^43]</small></small> put away from among yourselves that wicked person. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[1Cor.4|<< 1 Corinthians 4]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[1Cor.6|1 Corinthians 6 >>]]</td></tr></table>
