***Prophecy and Tongues*** ^bsb2771

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Follow<small><small>[^2]</small></small> after charity<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, and desire<small><small>[^4]</small></small> spiritual \[gifts\], but rather that ye may prophesy<small><small>[^5]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he that<small><small>[^6]</small></small> speaketh in an \[unknown\] tongue speaketh not unto men, but unto God: for no man understandeth<small><small>[^7]</small></small> \[him\]; howbeit<small><small>[^8]</small></small> in the spirit he speaketh mysteries. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But he that prophesieth speaketh unto men \[to\] edification<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, and exhortation<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, and comfort<small><small>[^11]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that speaketh in an \[unknown\] tongue edifieth himself<small><small>[^12]</small></small>; but he that prophesieth edifieth the<small><small>[^13]</small></small> church. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I would<small><small>[^14]</small></small> that ye all spake with tongues, but rather that ye prophesied: for<small><small>[^15]</small></small> greater \[is\] he that prophesieth than he that speaketh with tongues, except<small><small>[^16]</small></small> he interpret, that the church may receive edifying. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now, brethren, if I come unto you speaking with tongues, what shall I<small><small>[^17]</small></small> profit you, except I shall speak to you either by revelation<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, or by knowledge<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, or by prophesying<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, or by doctrine<small><small>[^21]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And even things<small><small>[^22]</small></small> without life giving sound, whether pipe or harp, except<small><small>[^23]</small></small> they give a distinction in the sounds<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, how shall it be known what is piped or harped? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For if the trumpet give an uncertain sound, who shall prepare himself to the battle?<small><small>[^25]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So likewise ye, except ye utter by the tongue words easy<small><small>[^26]</small></small> to be understood, how shall it be known what is spoken? for<small><small>[^27]</small></small> ye shall speak into the air. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There are, it may be, so many kinds of voices in the world, and none of them \[is\] without signification. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore if I know not the meaning of the voice, I shall<small><small>[^28]</small></small> be unto him that speaketh a barbarian, and he that speaketh \[shall be\] a barbarian unto me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Even so ye, forasmuch<small><small>[^29]</small></small> as ye are zealous of spiritual \[gifts<small><small>[^30]</small></small>\], seek<small><small>[^31]</small></small> that ye may excel to the edifying of the church. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore let him that speaketh in an \[unknown\] tongue pray<small><small>[^32]</small></small> that he may interpret. <span but<small><small>[^34]</small></small> my understanding is unfruitful. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What<small><small>[^35]</small></small> is it then? I will pray with the spirit<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, and I will pray with the understanding also: I will sing with the spirit, and I will sing<small><small>[^37]</small></small> with the understanding also. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Else when thou shalt bless<small><small>[^38]</small></small> with the spirit, how shall he that occupieth the room of the unlearned<small><small>[^39]</small></small> say Amen<small><small>[^40]</small></small> at<small><small>[^41]</small></small> thy giving of thanks, seeing he understandeth not what thou sayest? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For thou verily givest thanks well, but<small><small>[^42]</small></small> the other is not edified. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I thank my God, I speak with tongues more than ye all:<small><small>[^43]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet in the<small><small>[^44]</small></small> church I had rather speak five words with my understanding, that \[by my voice\] I might teach others also, than ten thousand words in an \[unknown\] tongue. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Brethren, be not<small><small>[^45]</small></small> children in understanding: howbeit in malice<small><small>[^46]</small></small> be ye children, but<small><small>[^47]</small></small> in understanding be men<small><small>[^48]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the law<small><small>[^49]</small></small> it is written, With<small><small>[^50]</small></small> \[men of\] other tongues and other lips will I speak unto this people; and yet for all that will they not hear me, saith the Lord. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore tongues are for a<small><small>[^51]</small></small> sign, not to<small><small>[^52]</small></small> them that believe, but to them that believe not: but prophesying \[serveth\] not for them that believe not, but for<small><small>[^53]</small></small> them which believe. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If therefore the whole<small><small>[^54]</small></small> church be come together into one place, and all speak with tongues, and there come in \[those that are\] unlearned, or unbelievers, will<small><small>[^55]</small></small> they not say that ye are mad? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if all prophesy, and there come in one that believeth not, or \[one\] unlearned, he is convinced<small><small>[^56]</small></small> of all, he is judged of all: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thus are the secrets of his heart made manifest; and so falling<small><small>[^57]</small></small> down on \[his\] face he will worship God, and report that God is<small><small>[^58]</small></small> in you of a truth.

***Orderly Worship*** ^bsb2772

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> How is it then, brethren? when ye come together, every<small><small>[^59]</small></small> one of you hath a psalm, hath a doctrine, hath a tongue, hath a revelation, hath an interpretation. Let<small><small>[^60]</small></small> all things be done unto edifying. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If any man speak in an \[unknown\] tongue, \[let it be\] by two, or at the most \[by\] three, and \[that\] by course; and let one interpret. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if there be no interpreter, let him keep silence in the church; and let him speak to himself, and to God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let the prophets<small><small>[^61]</small></small> speak two or three, and let the other<small><small>[^62]</small></small> judge. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If \[any thing\] be revealed<small><small>[^63]</small></small> to another that sitteth by, let<small><small>[^64]</small></small> the first hold his peace. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For ye may all prophesy one by one, that all may learn<small><small>[^65]</small></small>, and all may be<small><small>[^66]</small></small> comforted. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the spirits of the prophets are subject to the prophets.<small><small>[^67]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For God is not \[the author\] of confusion<small><small>[^68]</small></small>, but<small><small>[^69]</small></small> of peace, as in<small><small>[^70]</small></small> all churches of the saints. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.34|34]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let your women<small><small>[^71]</small></small> keep silence in the churches: for it is not permitted unto them to speak; but \[they are<small><small>[^72]</small></small> commanded\] to be under obedience, as<small><small>[^73]</small></small> also saith the law. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.35|35]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if they will learn any thing, let<small><small>[^74]</small></small> them ask their husbands at home: for it is a shame<small><small>[^75]</small></small> for women to speak in the church. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.36|36]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What? came<small><small>[^76]</small></small> the wor<small><small>[^77]</small></small>d of God out from you? or came it unto you only? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.37|37]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If any<small><small>[^78]</small></small> man think himself to be a prophet, or spiritual, let<small><small>[^79]</small></small> him acknowledge that the things that I write unto you are the commandments of the Lord. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.38|38]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if any man be ignorant, let him be ignorant.<small><small>[^80]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.39|39]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore, brethren, covet<small><small>[^81]</small></small> to prophesy, and forbid not to speak with tongues. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.14.40|40]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let all things be done decently and in order. <small><small>[^82]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[1Cor.13|<< 1 Corinthians 13]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[1Cor.15|1 Corinthians 15 >>]]</td></tr></table>
