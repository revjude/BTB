<big><big>[[1Cor.7.1 Principles of Marriage|⥃]]</big></big>***Principles of Marriage***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now concerning the things whereof ye wrote unto me: \[It is\] good<small><small>[^2]</small></small> for a man not to touch<small><small>[^3]</small></small> a woman. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nevertheless, \[to avoid<small><small>[^4]</small></small>\] fornication, let<small><small>[^5]</small></small> every man have his own wife, and let every woman have her own husband. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let the husband render unto the wife due benevolence: and likewise also the wife unto the husband.<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wife hath not power of her own body, but the husband: and likewise also the husband hath not power of his own body, but the wife.<small><small>[^7]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Defraud ye not one the other, except<small><small>[^8]</small></small> \[it be\] with consent for a time, that ye may give yourselves to fasting and prayer; and come together again, that Satan<small><small>[^9]</small></small> tempt you not for your incontinency. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But I speak this by<small><small>[^10]</small></small> permission, \[and\] not of commandment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I would<small><small>[^11]</small></small> that all men were even as I myself. But<small><small>[^12]</small></small> every man hath his proper gift of God, one after this manner, and another after that. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I say therefore to the unmarried and widows, It is good for them if they abide even as I.<small><small>[^13]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if they cannot contain, let<small><small>[^14]</small></small> them marry: for it is better to marry than to burn. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And unto the married I command, \[yet<small><small>[^15]</small></small>\] not I, but the Lord, Let<small><small>[^16]</small></small> not the wife depart from \[her\] husband: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But and if she depart, let her remain unmarried, or<small><small>[^17]</small></small> be reconciled to \[her\] husband: and let<small><small>[^18]</small></small> not the husband put away \[his\] wife. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But to the rest speak<small><small>[^19]</small></small> I, not the Lord: If<small><small>[^20]</small></small> any brother hath a wife that believeth not, and she be pleased to dwell with him, let him not put her away. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the woman which hath an husband that believeth not, and if he be pleased to dwell with her, let her not leave him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the unbelieving husband<small><small>[^21]</small></small> is sanctified by the wife, and the unbelieving wife is sanctified by the husband: else<small><small>[^22]</small></small> were your children unclean; but now are they holy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if the unbelieving depart, let him depart. A brother<small><small>[^23]</small></small> or a sister is not under bondage in such \[cases\]: but<small><small>[^24]</small></small> God hath called us to peace<small><small>[^25]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For what knowest thou, O wife<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, whether thou shalt save \[thy\] husband? or how<small><small>[^27]</small></small> knowest thou, O man, whether thou shalt save \[thy\] wife?

<big><big>[[1Cor.7.17 Live Your Calling|⥃]]</big></big>***Live Your Calling***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But as God<small><small>[^28]</small></small> hath distributed to every man, as the<small><small>[^29]</small></small> Lord hath called every one, so let him walk. And so ordain<small><small>[^30]</small></small> I in all churches. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Is any man called being<small><small>[^31]</small></small> circumcised? let him not become uncircumcised. Is any called in uncircumcision? let him not be circumcised. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Circumcision<small><small>[^32]</small></small> is nothing, and uncircumcision is nothing, but<small><small>[^33]</small></small> the keeping of the commandments of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let every man abide<small><small>[^34]</small></small> in the same calling wherein he was called. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Art thou called \[being<small><small>[^35]</small></small>\] a servant<small><small>[^36]</small></small>? care<small><small>[^37]</small></small> not for it: but if thou mayest be made free, use \[it\] rather. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he that is called in the Lord, \[being\] a servant, is the<small><small>[^38]</small></small> Lord’s freeman<small><small>[^39]</small></small>: likewise also he that is called, \[being\] free, is Christ’s<small><small>[^40]</small></small> servant. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ye are<small><small>[^41]</small></small> bought with a price; be<small><small>[^42]</small></small> not ye the servants of men. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Brethren, let<small><small>[^43]</small></small> every man, wherein he is called, therein abide<small><small>[^44]</small></small> with God.

<big><big>[[1Cor.7.25 The Unmarried and Widowed|⥃]]</big></big>***The Unmarried and Widowed***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now concerning<small><small>[^45]</small></small> virgins<small><small>[^46]</small></small> I have<small><small>[^47]</small></small> no commandment of the Lord: yet I give my judgment, as one that hath obtained<small><small>[^48]</small></small> mercy of the Lord to be faithful. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I suppose therefore that<small><small>[^49]</small></small> this is good for the present distress<small><small>[^50]</small></small>, \[I say\], that \[it is\] good for a man so to be. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Art thou bound<small><small>[^51]</small></small> unto a wife? seek not to be loosed. Art thou loosed from a wife? seek not a wife. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But and if thou marry, thou hast<small><small>[^52]</small></small> not sinned; and if a virgin marry, she hath not sinned. Nevertheless<small><small>[^53]</small></small> such shall have trouble in the flesh: but<small><small>[^54]</small></small> I spare you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But this I say, brethren, the time<small><small>[^55]</small></small> \[is\] short: it remaineth, that both<small><small>[^56]</small></small> they that have wives be as though they had none; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they that weep<small><small>[^57]</small></small>, as though they wept not; and they that rejoice, as though they rejoiced not; and they that buy, as though they possessed not; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they that use<small><small>[^58]</small></small> this world, as not abusing \[it\]: for<small><small>[^59]</small></small> the fashion<small><small>[^60]</small></small> of this world passeth away. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But I would<small><small>[^61]</small></small> have you without carefulness. He that<small><small>[^62]</small></small> is unmarried careth for the things that belong to the Lord<small><small>[^63]</small></small>, how he may please the Lord: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But he that is married careth<small><small>[^64]</small></small> for the things that are of the world, how<small><small>[^65]</small></small> he may please \[his\] wife. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.34|34]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There is difference \[also\] between a wife and a virgin. The unmarried woman careth<small><small>[^66]</small></small> for the things of the Lord, that she may be holy both<small><small>[^67]</small></small> in body and in spirit: but she that<small><small>[^68]</small></small> is married careth for the things of the world, how she may please \[her\] husband. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.35|35]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And this I speak for your own profit; not<small><small>[^69]</small></small> that I may cast a snare upon you, but for that which is comely<small><small>[^70]</small></small>, and that<small><small>[^71]</small></small> ye may attend upon the Lord without distraction. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.36|36]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if any man think that he behaveth himself uncomely toward his virgin<small><small>[^72]</small></small>, if she pass the flower<small><small>[^73]</small></small> of \[her\] age, and need<small><small>[^74]</small></small> so require, let him do what he will, he sinneth<small><small>[^75]</small></small> not: let them marry. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.37|37]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nevertheless he that standeth stedfast in his heart, having no necessity, but hath power over his own will, and hath so decreed in his heart that he will keep his virgin, doeth well. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.38|38]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So then<small><small>[^76]</small></small> he that giveth \[her\] in marriage doeth well<small><small>[^77]</small></small>; but he that giveth \[her\] not in marriage doeth better<small><small>[^78]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.39|39]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wife<small><small>[^79]</small></small> is bound by the law as long as her husband liveth; but if her husband be dead, she is at liberty to be married to whom she will; only<small><small>[^80]</small></small> in the Lord. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.7.40|40]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But she<small><small>[^81]</small></small> is happier if she so abide, after my judgment: and I think<small><small>[^82]</small></small> also that I have the Spirit of God. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[1Cor.6|<< 1 Corinthians 6]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[1Cor.8|1 Corinthians 8 >>]]</td></tr></table>
