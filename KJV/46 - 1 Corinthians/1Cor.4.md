***Servants of Christ*** ^bsb2748

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.4.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let a man so account<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of us, as of the ministers<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of Christ, and stewards<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of the mysteries<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.4.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover it is required in stewards, that<small><small>[^6]</small></small> a man be found faithful. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.4.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But with me it is<small><small>[^7]</small></small> a very small thing that I should be judged of you, or of man’s judgment<small><small>[^8]</small></small>: yea, I judge not mine own self. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.4.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^9]</small></small> I know<small><small>[^10]</small></small> nothing by myself; yet<small><small>[^11]</small></small> am I not hereby justified: but<small><small>[^12]</small></small> he that judgeth me is the Lord. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.4.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore judge<small><small>[^13]</small></small> nothing before the time, until<small><small>[^14]</small></small> the Lord come, who<small><small>[^15]</small></small> both will bring to light the hidden things of darkness, and will make manifest the counsels of the hearts: and then shall every man have praise<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.4.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And these<small><small>[^17]</small></small> things, brethren, I have in a figure transferred to myself and \[to\] Apollos for<small><small>[^18]</small></small> your sakes; that ye<small><small>[^19]</small></small> might learn in us not to think \[of men\] above that which is written, that no one of you be puffed<small><small>[^20]</small></small> up for one against another. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.4.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For who<small><small>[^21]</small></small> maketh thee to differ<small><small>[^22]</small></small> \[from another\]? and what<small><small>[^23]</small></small> hast thou that thou didst not receive? now if thou didst receive \[it\], why<small><small>[^24]</small></small> dost thou glory, as if thou hadst not received \[it\]? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.4.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now ye are full<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, now ye are rich, ye have reigned as kings without<small><small>[^26]</small></small> us: and I<small><small>[^27]</small></small> would to God ye did<small><small>[^28]</small></small> reign, that we also might reign with you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.4.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I<small><small>[^29]</small></small> think that God hath set forth us the apostles las<small><small>[^30]</small></small>t, as<small><small>[^31]</small></small> it were appointed to death: for we are<small><small>[^32]</small></small> made a spectacle<small><small>[^33]</small></small> unto the world, and to angels, and to men<small><small>[^34]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.4.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> We \[are\] fools<small><small>[^35]</small></small> for<small><small>[^36]</small></small> Christ’s sake, but ye \[are\] wise<small><small>[^37]</small></small> in Christ; we \[are\] weak<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, but ye<small><small>[^39]</small></small> \[are\] strong; ye \[are\] honourable, but we<small><small>[^40]</small></small> \[are\] despised. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.4.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Even unto<small><small>[^41]</small></small> this present hour we both hunger, and thirst, and are naked<small><small>[^42]</small></small>, and are buffeted<small><small>[^43]</small></small>, and have<small><small>[^44]</small></small> no certain dwellingplace; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.4.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And labour<small><small>[^45]</small></small>, working with our own hands: being reviled<small><small>[^46]</small></small>, we bless; being persecuted<small><small>[^47]</small></small>, we suffer it: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[1Cor.4.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Being defamed, we intreat: we are made as the filth of the world, \[and are\] the offscouring of all things unto this day.<small><small>[^48]</small></small>
