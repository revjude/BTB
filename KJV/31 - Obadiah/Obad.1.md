***The Destruction of Edom***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Jer.49|Jeremiah 49:7–22]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb2013

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The vision of Obadiah. Thus saith the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span> concerning<small><small>[^2]</small></small> Edom; We<small><small>[^3]</small></small> have heard a rumour from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and an<small><small>[^4]</small></small> ambassador is sent among the heathen, Arise<small><small>[^5]</small></small> ye, and let us rise up against her in battle. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, I have made thee small among the heathen: thou art greatly despised.<small><small>[^6]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]The pride<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of thine heart hath deceived thee, thou<small><small>[^8]</small></small> that dwellest in the clefts of the rock, whose habitation \[is\] high; that saith<small><small>[^9]</small></small> in his heart, Who shall bring me down to the ground? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Though thou exalt<small><small>[^10]</small></small> \[thyself\] as the eagle, and though thou set thy nest among<small><small>[^11]</small></small> the stars, thence will I bring thee down, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If thieves came to thee, if robbers<small><small>[^12]</small></small> by night, (how<small><small>[^13]</small></small> art thou cut off!) would they not have stolen till they had enough? if the<small><small>[^14]</small></small> grapegatherers came to thee, would they not leave \[some\] grapes<small><small>[^15]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> How are \[the<small><small>[^16]</small></small> things\] of Esau searched out! \[how\] are his<small><small>[^17]</small></small> hidden things sought up! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All the men of<small><small>[^18]</small></small> thy confederacy have brought thee \[even\] to the border: the men that were at peace with thee<small><small>[^19]</small></small> have deceived thee, \[and\] prevailed against thee; \[they that eat\] thy bread<small><small>[^20]</small></small> have laid a wound under thee: \[there is<small><small>[^21]</small></small>\] none understanding in him<small><small>[^22]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Shall I not in that day, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, even<small><small>[^23]</small></small> destroy the wise \[men\] out of Edom, and understanding out of the mount of Esau? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thy<small><small>[^24]</small></small> mighty \[men\], O<small><small>[^25]</small></small> Teman, shall be dismayed, to the end that every<small><small>[^26]</small></small> one of the mount<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of Esau may be cut off by slaughter. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For \[thy\] violence<small><small>[^28]</small></small> against thy brother Jacob shame<small><small>[^29]</small></small> shall cover thee, and<small><small>[^30]</small></small> thou shalt be cut off for ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the day that thou stoodest on the other side, in the day that the<small><small>[^31]</small></small> strangers carried away captive his forces<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, and foreigners entered into his gates, and cast<small><small>[^33]</small></small> lots upon Jerusalem, even<small><small>[^34]</small></small> thou \[wast\] as one of them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But thou<small><small>[^35]</small></small> shouldest not have looked<small><small>[^36]</small></small> on the day of thy brother in the day that he became a stranger; neither shouldest thou have rejoiced<small><small>[^37]</small></small> over the children of Judah in the day of their destruction; neither shouldest thou have<small><small>[^38]</small></small> spoken proudly<small><small>[^39]</small></small> in the day of distress. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou shouldest not have entered into the gate of my people in the day of their calamity; yea, thou shouldest not have looked<small><small>[^40]</small></small> on their affliction in the day of their calamity, nor have laid \[hands\] on their substance<small><small>[^41]</small></small> in the day of their calamity; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Neither shouldest thou have stood in the crossway, to cut off those of his that did escape; neither shouldest<small><small>[^42]</small></small> thou have delivered up<small><small>[^43]</small></small> those of his that did remain in the day<small><small>[^44]</small></small> of distress.

***The Deliverance of Israel*** ^bsb2014

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the day<small><small>[^45]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] near upon all the heathen: as<small><small>[^46]</small></small> thou hast done, it shall be done unto thee: thy reward shall return upon thine own head. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For as ye<small><small>[^47]</small></small> have drunk upon my holy mountain, \[so\] shall all the heathen drink continually, yea, they shall drink, and they shall swallow down<small><small>[^48]</small></small>, and they shall be<small><small>[^49]</small></small> as though they had not been. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But upon<small><small>[^50]</small></small> mount Zion shall be<small><small>[^51]</small></small> deliverance<small><small>[^52]</small></small>, and there shall be holiness<small><small>[^53]</small></small>; and the house of Jacob shall possess<small><small>[^54]</small></small> their possessions. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the house of Jacob shall be<small><small>[^55]</small></small> a fire, and the house of Joseph<small><small>[^56]</small></small> a flame, and the house of Esau for stubble<small><small>[^57]</small></small>, and they shall kindle in them, and devour them; and there<small><small>[^58]</small></small> shall not be \[any\] remaining of the house of Esau; for the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath spoken \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And \[they of\] the south<small><small>[^59]</small></small> shall possess the mount of Esau; and \[they of\] the plain<small><small>[^60]</small></small> the Philistines: and they shall possess the fields of Ephraim<small><small>[^61]</small></small>, and the fields of Samaria: and Benjamin<small><small>[^62]</small></small> \[shall possess\] Gilead. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the captivity of this<small><small>[^63]</small></small> host of the children of Israel \[shall possess\] that of the Canaanites, \[even\] unto Zarephath<small><small>[^64]</small></small>; and the captivity of Jerusalem, which \[is\] in Sepharad, shall possess<small><small>[^65]</small></small> the cities of the south. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Obad.1.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And saviours<small><small>[^66]</small></small> shall come up on mount Zion to judge<small><small>[^67]</small></small> the mount of Esau;
