<big><big>[[2Cor.12.1 Paul’s Revelation|⥃]]</big></big>***Paul’s Revelation***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> It is not expedient<small><small>[^2]</small></small> for me doubtless to glory<small><small>[^3]</small></small>. I will come<small><small>[^4]</small></small> to visions<small><small>[^5]</small></small> and revelations of the Lord. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I knew<small><small>[^6]</small></small> a man in Christ<small><small>[^7]</small></small> above<small><small>[^8]</small></small> fourteen years ago, (whether in the<small><small>[^9]</small></small> body, I cannot tell; or whether out of the body, I cannot tell: God<small><small>[^10]</small></small> knoweth;) such an one caught<small><small>[^11]</small></small> up to the third<small><small>[^12]</small></small> heaven. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I knew such a man, (whether in the body, or out of the body, I cannot tell: God knoweth;) <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> How that he was caught up into paradise<small><small>[^13]</small></small>, and heard unspeakable words, which it is not lawful<small><small>[^14]</small></small> for a man to utter.

<big><big>[[2Cor.12.5 Paul’s Thorn and God’s Grace|⥃]]</big></big>***Paul’s Thorn and God’s Grace***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Of such<small><small>[^15]</small></small> an one will I glory: yet<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of myself I will not glory, but in mine infirmities. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For though I would<small><small>[^17]</small></small> desire to glory, I shall not be a fool; for I will<small><small>[^18]</small></small> say the truth: but \[now\] I forbear, lest any man should think of me above that<small><small>[^19]</small></small> which he seeth me \[to be\], or \[that\] he heareth of me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And lest<small><small>[^20]</small></small> I should be exalted above measure through the abundance<small><small>[^21]</small></small> of the revelations, there was given to me a thorn<small><small>[^22]</small></small> in the flesh, the messenger<small><small>[^23]</small></small> of Satan to buffet<small><small>[^24]</small></small> me, lest I should be exalted above measure. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For this thing I besought<small><small>[^25]</small></small> the Lord thrice, that it might depart from me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said unto me, <span style="color:red;">My grace<small><small>[^26]</small></small> is sufficient for<small><small>[^27]</small></small> thee: for my strength is made perfect in weakness.</span> Most<small><small>[^28]</small></small> gladly therefore will I rather glory<small><small>[^29]</small></small> in my infirmities, that the power<small><small>[^30]</small></small> of Christ may rest upon me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore I take<small><small>[^31]</small></small> pleasure in infirmities<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, in reproaches, in necessities, in persecutions, in distresses for Christ’s<small><small>[^33]</small></small> sake: for when<small><small>[^34]</small></small> I am weak, then am I strong.

<big><big>[[2Cor.12.11 Paul’s Concern for the Corinthians|⥃]]</big></big>***Paul’s Concern for the Corinthians***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I am become<small><small>[^35]</small></small> a fool in glorying; ye have compelled me: for I ought to have been commended of you: for in<small><small>[^36]</small></small> nothing am I behind the very chiefest apostles, though<small><small>[^37]</small></small> I be nothing. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Truly the signs of an apostle were wrought among you in all patience, in signs, and wonders, and mighty deeds.<small><small>[^38]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For what is it wherein ye were inferior to other churches, except \[it be\] that I myself<small><small>[^39]</small></small> was not burdensome to you? forgive<small><small>[^40]</small></small> me this wrong. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, the third<small><small>[^41]</small></small> time I am ready to come to you; and I will not be burdensome to you: for I<small><small>[^42]</small></small> seek not yours, but you: for the<small><small>[^43]</small></small> children ought not to lay up for the parents, but the parents for the children. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will<small><small>[^44]</small></small> very gladly spend and be spent for you<small><small>[^45]</small></small>; though<small><small>[^46]</small></small> the more abundantly I love you, the less I be loved. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But be it so, I did not<small><small>[^47]</small></small> burden you: nevertheless, being<small><small>[^48]</small></small> crafty, I caught you with guile. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Did I make a gain of you by any of them whom I sent unto you?<small><small>[^49]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I desired Titus<small><small>[^50]</small></small>, and with<small><small>[^51]</small></small> \[him\] I sent a brother. Did Titus make a gain of you? walked we not in the same spirit<small><small>[^52]</small></small>? \[walked we\] not in the same steps<small><small>[^53]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Again, think<small><small>[^54]</small></small> ye that we excuse ourselves unto you? we speak<small><small>[^55]</small></small> before God in Christ: but<small><small>[^56]</small></small> \[we do\] all things, dearly<small><small>[^57]</small></small> beloved, for your edifying. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I fear, lest, when I come, I shall not<small><small>[^58]</small></small> find you such as I would, and \[that<small><small>[^59]</small></small>\] I shall be found unto you such as ye would not: lest \[there be\] debates<small><small>[^60]</small></small>, envyings, wraths, strifes, backbitings, whisperings<small><small>[^61]</small></small>, swellings<small><small>[^62]</small></small>, tumults: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Cor.12.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[And\] lest, when I come again, my God<small><small>[^63]</small></small> will humble me among you, and \[that\] I<small><small>[^64]</small></small> shall bewail many which have sinned<small><small>[^65]</small></small> already, and have not<small><small>[^66]</small></small> repented of the uncleanness<small><small>[^67]</small></small> and fornication and lasciviousness which they have committed. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[2Cor.11|<< 2 Corinthians 11]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[2Cor.13|2 Corinthians 13 >>]]</td></tr></table>
