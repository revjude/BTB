 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And again<small><small>[^2]</small></small> he entered into Capernaum after \[some\] days; and it<small><small>[^3]</small></small> was noised that he was in the house. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And straightway<small><small>[^4]</small></small> many were gathered together, insomuch that there was no room to receive \[them\], no, not so much as about the door: and he<small><small>[^5]</small></small> preached the word unto them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they come unto him, bringing<small><small>[^6]</small></small> one sick of the palsy, which was borne of four. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when they could not come nigh unto him for the press, they uncovered<small><small>[^7]</small></small> the roof where he was: and when they had broken \[it\] up, they let down the bed wherein the sick of the palsy lay. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When Jesus saw<small><small>[^8]</small></small> their faith, he said<small><small>[^9]</small></small> unto the sick of the palsy, <span style="color:red;">Son<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, thy sins<small><small>[^11]</small></small> be forgiven thee.</span> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But there were certain of the scribes sitting there, and reasoning<small><small>[^12]</small></small> in their hearts, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Why doth this \[man\] thus speak<small><small>[^13]</small></small> blasphemies? who<small><small>[^14]</small></small> can forgive sins but God only? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And immediately when<small><small>[^15]</small></small> Jesus perceived in his spirit that they so reasoned within themselves, he said unto them, <span style="color:red;">Why<small><small>[^16]</small></small> reason ye these things in your hearts?</span> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> <span style="color:red;">Whether is it<small><small>[^17]</small></small> easier to say to the sick of the palsy, \[Thy\] sins<small><small>[^18]</small></small> be forgiven thee; or to say, Arise, and take up thy bed, and walk?</span> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> <span style="color:red;">But that ye may know that the Son of man hath power on earth to forgive sins,</span> (he saith to the sick of the palsy,)<small><small>[^19]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> <span style="color:red;">I say unto thee, Arise, and take up thy bed, and go thy way into thine house.</span><small><small>[^20]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And immediately he arose, took up the bed, and went forth before them all; insomuch<small><small>[^21]</small></small> that they were all amazed, and glorified<small><small>[^22]</small></small> God, saying, We never<small><small>[^23]</small></small> saw it on this fashion.

***Jesus Calls Levi***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Matt.9|Matthew 9:9–13]] <big>†</big> [[Luke.5|Luke 5:27–32]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb2255

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he went forth again by<small><small>[^24]</small></small> the sea side; and all<small><small>[^25]</small></small> the multitude resorted unto him, and he taught them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And as he passed by, he saw<small><small>[^26]</small></small> Levi the \[son\] of Alpheus<small><small>[^27]</small></small> sitting at the receipt of custom<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, and said unto him, <span style="color:red;">Follow me<small><small>[^29]</small></small>.</span> And he arose and followed him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, that, as Jesus sat at meat in his house, many publicans and sinners sat also together with Jesus and his disciples: for there were many, and they followed him.<small><small>[^30]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And when the scribes and Pharisees saw him eat with publicans and sinners, they said unto his disciples, How<small><small>[^31]</small></small> is it that he eateth and drinketh with publicans<small><small>[^32]</small></small> and sinners? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Mark.2.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When Jesus heard \[it\], he saith unto them, <span style="color:red;">They that are whole<small><small>[^33]</small></small> have no need of the physician, but they that are sick: I came<small><small>[^34]</small></small> not to call the righteous, but sinners to repentance.</span>
