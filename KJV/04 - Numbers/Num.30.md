***Laws about Vows***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Matt.5|Matthew 5:33–37]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb443

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Moses spake unto the heads of the tribes concerning the children of Israel, saying, This \[is\] the thing which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath commanded.<small><small>[^2]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If a man<small><small>[^3]</small></small> vow a vow<small><small>[^4]</small></small> unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, or swear<small><small>[^5]</small></small> an oath to bind<small><small>[^6]</small></small> his soul with a bond; he shall not break<small><small>[^7]</small></small> his word, he shall do<small><small>[^8]</small></small> according to all that proceedeth out of his mouth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If a woman also vow a vow unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and bind \[herself\] by a bond, \[being\] in her father’s house in her youth; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And her father hear her vow, and her bond wherewith she hath bound her soul, and her father shall hold his peace at her: then all her vows shall stand, and every bond wherewith she hath bound her soul shall stand.<small><small>[^9]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if her father disallow her in the day that he heareth; not any of her vows, or of her bonds wherewith she hath bound her soul, shall stand: and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall forgive her, because her father disallowed her.<small><small>[^10]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if she had at all an husband, when she vowed<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, or uttered ought out of her lips, wherewith she bound her soul; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And her husband heard \[it\], and held his peace at her in the day that he heard \[it\]: then her vows shall stand, and her bonds wherewith she bound her soul shall stand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if her husband disallowed her on the day that he heard \[it\]; then he shall make her vow which she vowed, and that which she uttered with her lips, wherewith she bound her soul, of none effect: and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall forgive her.<small><small>[^12]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But every vow of a widow, and of her that is divorced, wherewith they have bound their souls, shall stand against her.<small><small>[^13]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if she vowed in her husband’s house, or bound her soul by a bond with an oath; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And her husband heard \[it\], and held his peace at her, \[and\] disallowed her not: then all her vows shall stand, and every bond wherewith she bound her soul shall stand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if her husband hath utterly made them void on the day he heard \[them\]; \[then\] whatsoever proceeded out of her lips concerning her vows, or concerning the bond of her soul, shall not stand: her husband hath made<small><small>[^14]</small></small> them void; and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall forgive her.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Every vow, and every<small><small>[^16]</small></small> binding oath to afflict<small><small>[^17]</small></small> the soul, her husband may establish it, or her husband may make it void. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if her husband altogether hold his peace at her from day to day; then he establisheth all her vows, or all her bonds, which \[are\] upon her: he confirmeth them, because he held his peace at her in the day that he heard \[them\].<small><small>[^18]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if he shall any ways make them void after that he hath heard \[them\]; then he shall bear<small><small>[^19]</small></small> her iniquity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Num.30.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> These \[are\] the statutes, which the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> commanded Moses, between a man and his wife, between the father and his daughter, \[being yet\] in her youth in her father’s house. <small><small>[^20]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Num.29|<< Numbers 29]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Num.31|Numbers 31 >>]]</td></tr></table>
