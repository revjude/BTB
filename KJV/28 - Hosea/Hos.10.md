<big><big>[[Hos.10.1 Retribution for Israel’s Sin|⥃]]</big></big>***Retribution for Israel’s Sin***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Israel \[is\] an empty vine<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, he bringeth forth fruit unto himself: according to the multitude<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of his fruit he hath increased the altars; according to the goodness of his land they have made goodly images<small><small>[^4]</small></small>.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Their heart is divided<small><small>[^6]</small></small>; now shall they be found faulty: he shall break down<small><small>[^7]</small></small> their altars, he shall spoil their images. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For now they shall say, We have<small><small>[^8]</small></small> no king, because we feared not the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; what then should a king do to us? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They have spoken words, swearing<small><small>[^9]</small></small> falsely in making a covenant: thus<small><small>[^10]</small></small> judgment springeth up as hemlock in the furrows of the field. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The inhabitants of Samaria shall fear because of the calves<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of Beth–aven<small><small>[^12]</small></small>: for the people<small><small>[^13]</small></small> thereof shall mourn over it, and the priests<small><small>[^14]</small></small> thereof \[that\] rejoiced on it, for the glory<small><small>[^15]</small></small> thereof, because it is departed from it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> It shall be also carried<small><small>[^16]</small></small> unto Assyria \[for\] a present<small><small>[^17]</small></small> to king Jareb: Ephraim shall receive<small><small>[^18]</small></small> shame, and Israel shall be ashamed<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of his own counsel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[As for\] Samaria<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, her king<small><small>[^21]</small></small> is cut off as the foam upon the water<small><small>[^22]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The high places<small><small>[^23]</small></small> also of Aven, the sin<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of Israel, shall be destroyed: the thorn<small><small>[^25]</small></small> and the thistle shall come up on their altars<small><small>[^26]</small></small>; and they shall<small><small>[^27]</small></small> say to the mountains, Cover us; and to the hills, Fall on us. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O Israel, thou hast sinned from<small><small>[^28]</small></small> the days of Gibeah: there they stood: the battle<small><small>[^29]</small></small> in Gibeah against the children of iniquity did<small><small>[^30]</small></small> not overtake them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[It is\] in my<small><small>[^31]</small></small> desire that I should chastise them; and the<small><small>[^32]</small></small> people shall be gathered against them, when they<small><small>[^33]</small></small> shall bind themselves in their two furrows. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Ephraim \[is as\] an heifer<small><small>[^34]</small></small> \[that is\] taught, \[and\] loveth<small><small>[^35]</small></small> to tread out \[the corn\]; but<small><small>[^36]</small></small> I passed over upon her fair neck<small><small>[^37]</small></small>: I will make Ephraim to ride; Judah<small><small>[^38]</small></small> shall plow, \[and\] Jacob shall break his clods. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Sow<small><small>[^39]</small></small> to yourselves in righteousness, reap in mercy; break<small><small>[^40]</small></small> up your fallow ground: for \[it is\] time<small><small>[^41]</small></small> to seek the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, till he come and rain<small><small>[^42]</small></small> righteousness upon you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ye have plowed<small><small>[^43]</small></small> wickedness, ye have reaped iniquity; ye have eaten<small><small>[^44]</small></small> the fruit of lies: because thou didst<small><small>[^45]</small></small> trust in thy way, in the<small><small>[^46]</small></small> multitude of thy mighty men. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore shall a<small><small>[^47]</small></small> tumult arise among thy people, and all<small><small>[^48]</small></small> thy fortresses shall be spoiled, as<small><small>[^49]</small></small> Shalman spoiled Beth–arbel in the day of battle: the mother<small><small>[^50]</small></small> was dashed in pieces upon \[her\] children. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.10.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So shall Beth–el<small><small>[^51]</small></small> do unto you because of your great wickedness<small><small>[^52]</small></small>: in<small><small>[^53]</small></small> a morning shall the king of Israel utterly be cut off. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Hos.9|<< Hosea 9]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Hos.11|Hosea 11 >>]]</td></tr></table>
