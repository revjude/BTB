<big><big>[[Hos.13.1 God’s Anger against Israel|⥃]]</big></big>***God’s Anger against Israel***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When Ephraim<small><small>[^2]</small></small> spake trembling, he exalted<small><small>[^3]</small></small> himself in Israel; but when he offended<small><small>[^4]</small></small> in Baal, he died<small><small>[^5]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And now<small><small>[^6]</small></small> they sin more and more<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, and have made<small><small>[^8]</small></small> them molten images of their silver, \[and\] idols according<small><small>[^9]</small></small> to their own understanding, all of it the work of the craftsmen: they say of them, Let the men that sacrifice<small><small>[^10]</small></small> kiss<small><small>[^11]</small></small> the calves. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore they shall be as the morning<small><small>[^12]</small></small> cloud, and as the early dew that passeth away, as the chaff<small><small>[^13]</small></small> \[that\] is driven with the whirlwind out of the floor, and as the smoke out of the chimney. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet I \[am<small><small>[^14]</small></small>\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> thy God from the land of Egypt, and thou shalt know no god but me: for<small><small>[^15]</small></small> \[there is\] no saviour beside me. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I did know<small><small>[^16]</small></small> thee in the wilderness<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, in the land of great drought<small><small>[^18]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> According to<small><small>[^19]</small></small> their pasture, so were they filled; they were filled, and their heart was exalted; therefore<small><small>[^20]</small></small> have they forgotten me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore I will be unto them as a lion: as a leopard by the way will I observe \[them\]:<small><small>[^21]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will meet them as a<small><small>[^22]</small></small> bear \[that is\] bereaved \[of her whelps\], and will rend the caul of their heart, and there will I devour them like a lion: the wild beast<small><small>[^23]</small></small> shall tear them.

<big><big>[[Hos.13.9 Death and Resurrection|⥃]]</big></big>***Death and Resurrection***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Cor.15|1 Corinthians 15:50–58]] ]</sup></small>

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O Israel, thou<small><small>[^24]</small></small> hast destroyed thyself; but<small><small>[^25]</small></small> in me \[is\] thine help<small><small>[^26]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will be thy king<small><small>[^27]</small></small>: where<small><small>[^28]</small></small> \[is any other\] that may save thee in all thy cities? and thy judges<small><small>[^29]</small></small> of whom thou saidst, Give me a king and princes? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I gave thee a king in mine anger, and took \[him\] away in my wrath.<small><small>[^30]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The iniquity of Ephraim \[is\] bound up; his sin \[is\] hid.<small><small>[^31]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The sorrows<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of a travailing woman<small><small>[^33]</small></small> shall come upon him: he \[is\] an unwise son; for he<small><small>[^34]</small></small> should not stay long<small><small>[^35]</small></small> in \[the place of\] the breaking forth of children. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will ransom<small><small>[^36]</small></small> them from the power<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of the grave; I will redeem them from death: O death<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, I will be thy plagues; O grave, I will be thy destruction: repentance<small><small>[^39]</small></small> shall be hid from mine eyes.

<big><big>[[Hos.13.15 Judgment on Samaria|⥃]]</big></big>***Judgment on Samaria***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Though he be<small><small>[^40]</small></small> fruitful among \[his\] brethren, an east<small><small>[^41]</small></small> wind shall come, the wind of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall come up from the wilderness, and his spring<small><small>[^42]</small></small> shall become dry, and his fountain shall be dried up: he shall spoil the treasure of all pleasant vessels<small><small>[^43]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.13.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Samaria<small><small>[^44]</small></small> shall become desolate; for she hath rebelled against her God: they shall fall by the sword: their infants<small><small>[^45]</small></small> shall be dashed in pieces, and their women with child shall be ripped up. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Hos.12|<< Hosea 12]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Hos.14|Hosea 14 >>]]</td></tr></table>
