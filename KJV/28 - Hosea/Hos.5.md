***Judgment on Israel and Judah*** ^bsb1976

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.5.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear ye this, O priests<small><small>[^2]</small></small>; and hearken, ye house of Israel; and give ye ear, O house<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of the king; for<small><small>[^4]</small></small> judgment \[is\] toward you, because ye have<small><small>[^5]</small></small> been a snare on Mizpah, and a net spread upon Tabor<small><small>[^6]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.5.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the revolters<small><small>[^7]</small></small> are profound<small><small>[^8]</small></small> to make slaughter, though<small><small>[^9]</small></small> I \[have been\] a Ephraim<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, and Israel is not hid from me: for now, O Ephraim, thou<small><small>[^13]</small></small> committest whoredom, \[and\] Israel is defiled. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.5.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They will not frame their doings<small><small>[^14]</small></small> to turn unto their God: for<small><small>[^15]</small></small> the spirit of whoredoms \[is\] in the midst of them, and<small><small>[^16]</small></small> they have not known the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.5.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the pride<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of Israel doth testify<small><small>[^18]</small></small> to his face: therefore shall Israel and Ephraim fall in<small><small>[^19]</small></small> their iniquity; Judah<small><small>[^20]</small></small> also shall fall with them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.5.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They shall go<small><small>[^21]</small></small> with their flocks and with their herds to seek the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; but the<small><small>[^22]</small></small>y<small><small>[^23]</small></small> shall not find \[him\]; he hath withdrawn himself from them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.5.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They have dealt<small><small>[^24]</small></small> treacherously against the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: for they have begotten<small><small>[^25]</small></small> strange children: now shall a month<small><small>[^26]</small></small> devour them with their portions. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.5.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Blow<small><small>[^27]</small></small> ye the cornet in Gibeah<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, \[and\] the trumpet in Ramah<small><small>[^29]</small></small>: cry aloud \[at\] Beth–aven<small><small>[^30]</small></small>, after<small><small>[^31]</small></small> thee, O Benjamin. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.5.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ephraim<small><small>[^32]</small></small> shall be desolate in the day of rebuke: among the tribes of Israel have<small><small>[^33]</small></small> I made known that which shall surely be. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.5.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The princes<small><small>[^34]</small></small> of Judah were like them that remove<small><small>[^35]</small></small> the bound: \[therefore\] I will pour out my wrath upon them like<small><small>[^36]</small></small> water. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.5.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ephraim \[is\] oppressed<small><small>[^37]</small></small> \[and\] broken in judgment, because he willingly<small><small>[^38]</small></small> walked after the commandment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.5.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore \[will\] I \[be\] unto Ephraim as<small><small>[^39]</small></small> a<small><small>[^40]</small></small> moth, and to the house of Judah as rottenness<small><small>[^41]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.5.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When Ephraim saw his sickness, and Judah \[saw\] his wound<small><small>[^42]</small></small>, then went<small><small>[^43]</small></small> Ephraim to the Assyrian, and sent to king Jareb<small><small>[^44]</small></small>: yet<small><small>[^45]</small></small> could he not heal you, nor cure you of your wound. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.5.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I \[will be\] unto Ephraim as a lion<small><small>[^46]</small></small>, and as a young lion to the house of Judah: I, \[even\] I, will tear<small><small>[^47]</small></small> and go away; I will take away, and none<small><small>[^48]</small></small> shall rescue \[him\].
