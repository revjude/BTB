<big><big>[[Hos.7.1 Ephraim’s Iniquity|⥃]]</big></big>***Ephraim’s Iniquity***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When I would<small><small>[^2]</small></small> have healed Israel, then the iniquity<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of Ephraim was discovered, and the wickedness<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of Samaria: for they commit<small><small>[^5]</small></small> falsehood; and the thief cometh in, \[and\] the troop<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of robbers spoileth<small><small>[^7]</small></small> without. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they consider not in<small><small>[^8]</small></small> their hearts \[that\] I remember<small><small>[^9]</small></small> all their wickedness: now their own<small><small>[^10]</small></small> doings have beset them about; they are before<small><small>[^11]</small></small> my face. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They make the king glad with their wickedness, and the princes with their lies.<small><small>[^12]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They \[are\] all<small><small>[^13]</small></small> adulterers, as<small><small>[^14]</small></small> an oven heated by the baker, \[who\] ceaseth<small><small>[^15]</small></small> from raising<small><small>[^16]</small></small> after he hath kneaded the dough, until it be leavened. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the day<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of our king the princes have made<small><small>[^18]</small></small> \[him\] sick with bottles of wine<small><small>[^19]</small></small>; he stretched<small><small>[^20]</small></small> out his hand with scorners<small><small>[^21]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For they<small><small>[^22]</small></small> have made ready<small><small>[^23]</small></small> their heart like an oven, whiles they lie in wait: their baker sleepeth all the night; in the morning it burneth as a flaming fire. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They are all hot as an oven, and have devoured<small><small>[^24]</small></small> their judges; all their kings are fallen: \[there<small><small>[^25]</small></small> is\] none among them that calleth unto me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ephraim, he hath<small><small>[^26]</small></small> mixed himself among the people; Ephraim is a cake<small><small>[^27]</small></small> not turned. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Strangers have devoured<small><small>[^28]</small></small> his strength, and he knoweth \[it\] not: yea, gray hairs are here and there<small><small>[^29]</small></small> upon him, yet he knoweth not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the pride<small><small>[^30]</small></small> of Israel testifieth to his face: and they<small><small>[^31]</small></small> do not return to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> their God, nor<small><small>[^32]</small></small> seek him for all this. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Ephraim also is like a silly<small><small>[^33]</small></small> dove without<small><small>[^34]</small></small> heart: they call<small><small>[^35]</small></small> to Egypt, they go to Assyria. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When they shall go, I will spread<small><small>[^36]</small></small> my net upon them; I will bring<small><small>[^37]</small></small> them down as the fowls of the heaven; I will chastise them, as their<small><small>[^38]</small></small> congregation hath heard. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Woe<small><small>[^39]</small></small> unto them! for they have fled<small><small>[^40]</small></small> from me: destruction<small><small>[^41]</small></small> unto them! because they have transgressed against me: though<small><small>[^42]</small></small> I have redeemed them, yet they have spoken<small><small>[^43]</small></small> lies against me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they have not<small><small>[^44]</small></small> cried unto me with their heart, when<small><small>[^45]</small></small> they howled upon their beds: they assemble<small><small>[^46]</small></small> themselves for corn and wine, \[and\] they rebel against me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Though I have<small><small>[^47]</small></small> bound<small><small>[^48]</small></small> \[and\] strengthened their arms, yet do they imagine<small><small>[^49]</small></small> mischief against me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hos.7.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They return<small><small>[^50]</small></small>, \[but\] not to the most High: they are like<small><small>[^51]</small></small> a deceitful bow: their princes shall fall by the sword for the rage<small><small>[^52]</small></small> of their tongue: this<small><small>[^53]</small></small> \[shall be\] their derision in the land of Egypt. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Hos.6|<< Hosea 6]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Hos.8|Hosea 8 >>]]</td></tr></table>
