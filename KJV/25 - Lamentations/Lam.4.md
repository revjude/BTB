<big><big>[[Lam.4.1 The Distress of Zion|⥃]]</big></big>***The Distress of Zion***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> How is the gold<small><small>[^2]</small></small> become dim! \[how\] is the most fine gold changed! the stones<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of the sanctuary are poured out in the top of every street. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The precious sons<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of Zion, comparable to fine gold, how<small><small>[^5]</small></small> are they esteemed as earthen pitchers, the work of the hands of the potter! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Even the sea monsters<small><small>[^6]</small></small> draw out the breast, they give suck to their young ones: the daughter<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of my people \[is become\] cruel, like<small><small>[^8]</small></small> the ostriches in the wilderness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The tongue<small><small>[^9]</small></small> of the sucking child cleaveth to the roof of his mouth for thirst: the young<small><small>[^10]</small></small> children ask bread, \[and\] no man breaketh \[it\] unto them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They that did<small><small>[^11]</small></small> feed delicately are desolate in the streets: they that were brought<small><small>[^12]</small></small> up in scarlet embrace<small><small>[^13]</small></small> dunghills. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the punishment of the iniquity of the daughter<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of my people is greater than the punishment<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of the sin of Sodom, that was overthrown as in a moment, and no hands stayed on her. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Her Nazarites<small><small>[^16]</small></small> were purer<small><small>[^17]</small></small> than snow, they were whiter than milk, they were more ruddy in body than rubies, their polishing<small><small>[^18]</small></small> \[was\] of sapphire: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Their visage<small><small>[^19]</small></small> is blacker than a coal<small><small>[^20]</small></small>; they<small><small>[^21]</small></small> are not known in the streets: their skin<small><small>[^22]</small></small> cleaveth to their bones; it is withered, it is become like a stick. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[They that be\] slain with the sword are better than \[they that be\] slain with hunger: for<small><small>[^23]</small></small> these pine away<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, stricken through for \[want of\] the fruits of the field. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The hands<small><small>[^25]</small></small> of the pitiful<small><small>[^26]</small></small> women have sodden their own children: they were their meat in<small><small>[^27]</small></small> the destruction of the daughter of my people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^28]</small></small></span> hath accomplished his fury; he hath poured out his fierce anger, and hath kindled<small><small>[^29]</small></small> a fire in Zion, and it hath devoured the foundations thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The kings of the earth, and all the inhabitants of the world, would not have believed that the adversary and the enemy should have entered into the gates of Jerusalem.<small><small>[^30]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the sins<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of her prophets, \[and\] the iniquities of her priests, that<small><small>[^32]</small></small> have shed the blood of the just in the midst of her, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They have wandered<small><small>[^33]</small></small> \[as\] blind \[men\] in the streets, they have polluted<small><small>[^34]</small></small> themselves with blood, so that men could not touch<small><small>[^35]</small></small> their garments. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They cried unto them, Depart ye; \[it is\] unclean; depart, depart, touch not: when they fled away and wandered, they said among the heathen, They shall no more sojourn \[there\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The anger<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath<small><small>[^37]</small></small> divided them; he will<small><small>[^38]</small></small> no more regard them: they respected<small><small>[^39]</small></small> not the persons of the priests, they favoured not the elders. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As for us, our eyes<small><small>[^40]</small></small> as yet failed for our vain help: in our watching we have watched for a<small><small>[^41]</small></small> nation \[that\] could not save \[us\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They hunt<small><small>[^42]</small></small> our steps, that we cannot go in our streets: our end is near<small><small>[^43]</small></small>, our days are fulfilled; for our end is come. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Our persecutors<small><small>[^44]</small></small> are swifter than the eagles<small><small>[^45]</small></small> of the heaven: they pursued<small><small>[^46]</small></small> us upon the mountains, they laid wait for us in the wilderness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The breath<small><small>[^47]</small></small> of our nostrils, the anointed<small><small>[^48]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, was taken<small><small>[^49]</small></small> in their pits, of whom we said, Under his shadow we shall live among the heathen. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.4.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Rejoice and be glad<small><small>[^50]</small></small>, O daughter of Edom, that dwellest in the land<small><small>[^51]</small></small> of Uz; the cup<small><small>[^52]</small></small> also shall pass through unto thee: thou shalt be drunken, and shalt<small><small>[^53]</small></small> make thyself naked.
