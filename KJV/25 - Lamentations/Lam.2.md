 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> How<small><small>[^2]</small></small> hath the Lord covered<small><small>[^3]</small></small> the daughter of Zion with a cloud in his anger, \[and\] cast<small><small>[^4]</small></small> down from heaven unto the earth the beauty<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of Israel, and remembered not his footstool<small><small>[^6]</small></small> in the day of his anger! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The Lord hath swallowed<small><small>[^7]</small></small> up all the habitations of Jacob, and hath not pitied: he hath thrown<small><small>[^8]</small></small> down in his wrath the strong holds of the daughter of Judah; he hath brought \[them\] down to<small><small>[^9]</small></small> the ground: he hath polluted<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the kingdom and the princes thereof. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He hath cut off in \[his\] fierce anger all the horn<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of Israel: he hath<small><small>[^12]</small></small> drawn back his right hand from before the enemy, and he burned<small><small>[^13]</small></small> against Jacob like a flaming fire, \[which\] devoureth round about. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He hath bent<small><small>[^14]</small></small> his bow like an enemy: he stood with his right hand as an adversary, and slew all \[that were\] pleasant to the eye<small><small>[^15]</small></small> in the tabernacle of the daughter of Zion: he poured<small><small>[^16]</small></small> out his fury like fire. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The Lord was<small><small>[^17]</small></small> as an enemy: he hath swallowed up Israel<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, he hath swallowed up all her palaces: he hath destroyed his strong holds, and hath increased in the daughter of Judah mourning<small><small>[^19]</small></small> and lamentation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he hath violently<small><small>[^20]</small></small> taken away his tabernacle<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, as \[if<small><small>[^22]</small></small> it were of\] a garden: he hath destroyed his places of the assembly: the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath caused<small><small>[^23]</small></small> the solemn feasts and sabbaths to be forgotten in Zion, and hath despised in the indignation of his anger the king<small><small>[^24]</small></small> and the priest. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The Lord hath cast off<small><small>[^25]</small></small> his altar, he hath abhorred his sanctuary, he hath given up<small><small>[^26]</small></small> into the hand of the enemy the walls of her palaces; they have<small><small>[^27]</small></small> made a noise in the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, as in the day of a solemn feast. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath purposed<small><small>[^28]</small></small> to destroy the wall of the daughter of Zion: he hath stretched<small><small>[^29]</small></small> out a line, he hath not<small><small>[^30]</small></small> withdrawn his hand from destroying<small><small>[^31]</small></small>: therefore he made<small><small>[^32]</small></small> the rampart and the wall to lament; they languished together. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Her gates<small><small>[^33]</small></small> are sunk into the ground; he hath destroyed and broken her bars: her king<small><small>[^34]</small></small> and her princes \[are\] among the Gentiles: the law<small><small>[^35]</small></small> \[is\] no \[more\]; her prophets<small><small>[^36]</small></small> also find no vision from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The elders<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of the daughter of Zion sit<small><small>[^38]</small></small> upon the ground, \[and\] keep<small><small>[^39]</small></small> silence: they have cast up<small><small>[^40]</small></small> dust upon their heads; they have girded<small><small>[^41]</small></small> themselves with sackcloth: the virgins<small><small>[^42]</small></small> of Jerusalem hang down their heads to the ground. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Mine eyes<small><small>[^43]</small></small> do fail with tears, my bowels<small><small>[^44]</small></small> are troubled, my liver<small><small>[^45]</small></small> is poured upon the earth, for<small><small>[^46]</small></small> the destruction of the daughter of my people; because<small><small>[^47]</small></small> the children and the sucklings swoon<small><small>[^48]</small></small> in the streets of the city. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They say to their mothers, Where \[is\] corn and wine? when they swooned as the<small><small>[^49]</small></small> wounded in the streets of the city, when their soul<small><small>[^50]</small></small> was poured out into their mothers’ bosom. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What thing shall I take<small><small>[^51]</small></small> to witness for<small><small>[^52]</small></small> thee? what thing shall I liken to thee, O daughter of Jerusalem? what shall I equal to thee, that I may comfort thee, O virgin daughter of Zion? for thy breach \[is\] great like the sea: who can<small><small>[^53]</small></small> heal thee? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy prophets<small><small>[^54]</small></small> have seen vain and foolish things for thee: and they have<small><small>[^55]</small></small> not discovered thine iniquity, to turn away thy captivity; but have seen for thee false<small><small>[^56]</small></small> burdens and causes of banishment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All that pass<small><small>[^57]</small></small> by<small><small>[^58]</small></small> clap<small><small>[^59]</small></small> \[their\] hands at thee; they<small><small>[^60]</small></small> hiss and wag<small><small>[^61]</small></small> their head at the daughter of Jerusalem, \[saying\], \[Is\] this<small><small>[^62]</small></small> the city that \[men\] call The perfection of beauty, The joy of the whole earth?<small><small>[^63]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All thine<small><small>[^64]</small></small> enemies have opened their mouth against thee: they hiss and gnash<small><small>[^65]</small></small> the teeth: they say, We have swallowed<small><small>[^66]</small></small> \[her\] up: certainly this \[is\] the day that we looked for; we have found, we have seen<small><small>[^67]</small></small> \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath done<small><small>[^68]</small></small> \[that\] which he had devised; he hath fulfilled his word that he had commanded in the days of old: he hath thrown<small><small>[^69]</small></small> down, and hath not pitied: and he hath caused<small><small>[^70]</small></small> \[thine\] enemy to rejoice over thee, he hath set up the horn of thine adversaries. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Their heart<small><small>[^71]</small></small> cried unto the Lord, O wall<small><small>[^72]</small></small> of the daughter of Zion, let tears<small><small>[^73]</small></small> run down like a river day and night: give thyself no rest; let not the apple<small><small>[^74]</small></small> of thine eye cease. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Lam.2.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Arise, cry out<small><small>[^75]</small></small> in the night: in the beginning of the watches<small><small>[^76]</small></small> pour<small><small>[^77]</small></small> out thine heart like water before the face of the Lord: lift up<small><small>[^78]</small></small> thy hands toward him for the life of thy young children, that faint<small><small>[^79]</small></small> for hunger in the top of every street.
