***To Everything There Is a Season*** ^bsb1515

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To every \[thing<small><small>[^2]</small></small> there is\] a season, and a time to every purpose under<small><small>[^3]</small></small> the heaven: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A time to be born<small><small>[^4]</small></small>, and a time<small><small>[^5]</small></small> to die; a time to plant<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, and a time to pluck up \[that which is\] planted; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A time to kill<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, and a time to heal<small><small>[^8]</small></small>; a time to break<small><small>[^9]</small></small> down, and a time to build up; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A time to weep<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, and a time to laugh<small><small>[^11]</small></small>; a time to mourn, and a time to dance<small><small>[^12]</small></small>; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A time to cast<small><small>[^13]</small></small> away stones, and a time to gather stones together; a time to embrace<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, and a time to refrain from<small><small>[^15]</small></small> embracing; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A time to get<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, and a time to lose; a time to keep, and a time to cast<small><small>[^17]</small></small> away; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A time to rend<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, and a time to sew; a time to keep<small><small>[^19]</small></small> silence, and a time to speak<small><small>[^20]</small></small>; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A time to love<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, and a time to hate<small><small>[^22]</small></small>; a time of war<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, and a time of peace.

***God’s Works Remain Forever*** ^bsb1516

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What profit hath he that worketh in that wherein he laboureth?<small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have seen the travail, which God hath given to the sons of men to be exercised in it.<small><small>[^25]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He hath made<small><small>[^26]</small></small> every \[thing\] beautiful in his time: also<small><small>[^27]</small></small> he hath set the world in their heart, so<small><small>[^28]</small></small> that no man can find out the work that God maketh from the beginning to the end. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I know that \[there is\] no good in them, but<small><small>[^29]</small></small> for \[a man\] to rejoice, and to do good in his life. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And also that every man should eat and drink, and enjoy the good of all his labour, it \[is\] the gift of God.<small><small>[^30]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I know that, whatsoever<small><small>[^31]</small></small> God doeth, it shall be for ever: nothing<small><small>[^32]</small></small> can be put to it, nor any thing taken from it: and God doeth \[it<small><small>[^33]</small></small>\], that \[men\] should fear before him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That which hath<small><small>[^34]</small></small> been is now; and that which is to be hath already been; and God requireth that which is past<small><small>[^35]</small></small>.

***From Dust to Dust*** ^bsb1517

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And moreover I saw under the sun the place of judgment, \[that\] wickedness \[was\] there; and the place of righteousness, \[that\] iniquity \[was\] there.<small><small>[^36]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I said<small><small>[^37]</small></small> in mine heart, God<small><small>[^38]</small></small> shall judge the righteous and the wicked: for<small><small>[^39]</small></small> \[there is\] a time there for every purpose and for every work. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I said in mine heart concerning<small><small>[^40]</small></small> the estate of the sons of men, that God<small><small>[^41]</small></small> might manifest them, and that<small><small>[^42]</small></small> they might see that they themselves are beasts. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For that which<small><small>[^43]</small></small> befalleth the sons of men befalleth beasts; even one thing befalleth them: as the<small><small>[^44]</small></small> one dieth, so dieth the other; yea, they have all one breath; so that a man hath no preeminence above a beast: for<small><small>[^45]</small></small> all \[is\] vanity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All go<small><small>[^46]</small></small> unto one place; all are<small><small>[^47]</small></small> of the dust, and all turn to dust again. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who knoweth<small><small>[^48]</small></small> the spirit of man that goeth upward<small><small>[^49]</small></small>, and the spirit of the beast that goeth downward to the earth? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.3.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore I perceive that \[there is\] nothing<small><small>[^50]</small></small> better, than that a man should rejoice in his own works; for that \[is\] his portion: for who<small><small>[^51]</small></small> shall bring him to see what shall be after him?
