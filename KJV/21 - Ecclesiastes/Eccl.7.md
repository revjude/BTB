***The Value of Wisdom*** ^bsb1523

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A good name<small><small>[^2]</small></small> \[is\] better than precious<small><small>[^3]</small></small> ointment; and the day<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of death than the day of one’s birth. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]\[It is\] better<small><small>[^5]</small></small> to go to the house of mourning, than to go to the house of feasting: for that<small><small>[^6]</small></small> \[is\] the end of all men; and the living<small><small>[^7]</small></small> will lay \[it\] to his heart. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Sorrow<small><small>[^8]</small></small> \[is\] better<small><small>[^9]</small></small> than laughter: for by<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the sadness of the countenance the heart is made better. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The heart<small><small>[^11]</small></small> of the wise \[is\] in the house of mourning; but the heart<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of fools \[is\] in the house of mirth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[It is\] better<small><small>[^13]</small></small> to hear the rebuke of the wise, than for a man to hear the song<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of fools. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For as<small><small>[^15]</small></small> the crackling<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of thorns under a pot, so \[is\] the laughter<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of the fool: this also \[is\] vanity. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Surely oppression<small><small>[^18]</small></small> maketh a wise man mad; and a gift<small><small>[^19]</small></small> destroyeth the heart. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Better<small><small>[^20]</small></small> \[is\] the end of a thing than the beginning thereof: \[and\] the patient<small><small>[^21]</small></small> in spirit \[is\] better than the proud in spirit. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be not hasty<small><small>[^22]</small></small> in thy spirit to be angry: for anger<small><small>[^23]</small></small> resteth in the bosom of fools. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Say not thou, What<small><small>[^24]</small></small> is \[the cause\] that the former days were better than these? for thou dost not enquire wisely<small><small>[^25]</small></small> concerning this. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wisdom \[is\] good with an inheritance<small><small>[^26]</small></small>: and \[by it there is\] profit to them<small><small>[^27]</small></small> that see the sun. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For wisdom<small><small>[^28]</small></small> \[is\] a defence<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, \[and\] money \[is\] a defence: but the excellency<small><small>[^30]</small></small> of knowledge \[is\], \[that\] wisdom giveth life to them that have it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Consider<small><small>[^31]</small></small> the work of God: for who<small><small>[^32]</small></small> can make \[that\] straight, which he hath made crooked? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.7.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the day<small><small>[^33]</small></small> of prosperity be joyful, but<small><small>[^34]</small></small> in the day of adversity consider: God also hath set<small><small>[^35]</small></small> the one over against the other, to the end that man should find nothing after him.
