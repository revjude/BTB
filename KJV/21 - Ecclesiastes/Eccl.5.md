***Approaching God with Awe*** ^bsb1520

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Keep thy foot<small><small>[^2]</small></small> when thou goest to the house of God, and be more ready<small><small>[^3]</small></small> to hear, than to give<small><small>[^4]</small></small> the sacrifice of fools: for they consider not that they do evil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be not rash<small><small>[^5]</small></small> with thy mouth, and let not thine heart be hasty to utter \[any\] thing<small><small>[^6]</small></small> befor<small><small>[^7]</small></small>e God: for God \[is\] in heaven, and thou upon earth: therefore let thy words be few. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For a dream cometh through the multitude of business; and a fool’s<small><small>[^8]</small></small> voice \[is known\] by multitude of words. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When thou vowest<small><small>[^9]</small></small> a vow unto God, defer not to pay it; for<small><small>[^10]</small></small> \[he hath\] no pleasure in fools: pay<small><small>[^11]</small></small> that which thou hast vowed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Better \[is it\] that thou shouldest not vow, than that thou shouldest vow and not pay.<small><small>[^12]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Suffer not thy mouth<small><small>[^13]</small></small> to cause thy flesh to sin; neither say thou before<small><small>[^14]</small></small> the angel, that it \[was<small><small>[^15]</small></small>\] an error: wherefore should God be angry at thy voice, and destroy<small><small>[^16]</small></small> the work of thine hands? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For in the<small><small>[^17]</small></small> multitude of dreams and many words \[there are\] also \[divers\] vanities: but<small><small>[^18]</small></small> fear thou God.

***The Futility of Wealth***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Ps.49|Psalms 49:1–20]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1521

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]If thou seest<small><small>[^19]</small></small> the oppression of the poor, and violent perverting of judgment and justice in a province, marvel<small><small>[^20]</small></small> not at the matter<small><small>[^21]</small></small>: for<small><small>[^22]</small></small> \[he that is\] higher than the highest regardeth<small><small>[^23]</small></small>; and \[there be\] higher than they<small><small>[^24]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Moreover the profit<small><small>[^25]</small></small> of the earth is for all: the king<small><small>[^26]</small></small> \[himself\] is served by the field. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^27]</small></small> loveth silver shall not be satisfied with silver; nor he that loveth abundance with increase: this<small><small>[^28]</small></small> \[is\] also vanity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When goods increase, they<small><small>[^29]</small></small> are increased that eat them: and what<small><small>[^30]</small></small> good \[is there\] to the owners thereof, saving the beholding \[of them\] with their eyes? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The sleep of a labouring man \[is\] sweet, whether he eat little or much: but the abundance of the rich will not suffer him to sleep.<small><small>[^31]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There is a sore<small><small>[^32]</small></small> evil \[which\] I have seen under the sun, \[namely\], riches<small><small>[^33]</small></small> kept for the owners thereof to their hurt. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But those<small><small>[^34]</small></small> riches perish by evil travail: and he<small><small>[^35]</small></small> begetteth a son, and \[there is\] nothing in his hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As he came forth of his mother’s womb, naked shall he return to go as he came, and shall take nothing of his labour, which he may carry away in his hand.<small><small>[^36]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And this also \[is\] a sore<small><small>[^37]</small></small> evil, \[that\] in all points as he came, so shall he go: and what<small><small>[^38]</small></small> profit hath he that hath laboured for<small><small>[^39]</small></small> the wind? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All his days also he eateth<small><small>[^40]</small></small> in darkness, and \[he hath\] much<small><small>[^41]</small></small> sorrow and wrath with his sickness. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold \[that\] which I have seen: \[it is\] good and comely<small><small>[^42]</small></small> \[for one\] to eat and to drink, and to enjoy the good of all his labour that he taketh under the sun all the days<small><small>[^43]</small></small> of his life, which God giveth him: for it \[is\] his<small><small>[^44]</small></small> portion. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Every man also to whom<small><small>[^45]</small></small> God hath given riches and wealth, and hath given him power to eat thereof, and to take his portion, and to rejoice in his labour; this \[is<small><small>[^46]</small></small>\] the gift of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.5.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he shall not much remember<small><small>[^47]</small></small> the days of his life; because<small><small>[^48]</small></small> God answereth \[him\] in the joy of his heart. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Eccl.4|<< Ecclesiastes 4]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Eccl.6|Ecclesiastes 6 >>]]</td></tr></table>
