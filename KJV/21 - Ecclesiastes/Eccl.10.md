***Wisdom and Folly*** ^bsb1531

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Dead flies<small><small>[^2]</small></small> cause the ointment<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of the apothecary to send forth a stinking savour: \[so doth\] a little<small><small>[^4]</small></small> folly him that is in reputation for wisdom \[and\] honour. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A wise<small><small>[^5]</small></small> man’s heart \[is\] at his right hand; but<small><small>[^6]</small></small> a fool’s heart at his left. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea also, when he that is a fool walketh by the way, his wisdom<small><small>[^7]</small></small> faileth \[him\], and he<small><small>[^8]</small></small> saith to every one \[that\] he \[is\] a fool. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If the spirit of the ruler rise up against thee, leave<small><small>[^9]</small></small> not thy place; for<small><small>[^10]</small></small> yielding pacifieth great offences. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There is an evil<small><small>[^11]</small></small> \[which\] I have seen under the sun, as an<small><small>[^12]</small></small> error \[which\] proceedeth from<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the ruler: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Folly<small><small>[^14]</small></small> is set in great dignity<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, and the rich<small><small>[^16]</small></small> sit in low place. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have seen servants upon horses, and princes walking as servants upon the earth.<small><small>[^17]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that<small><small>[^18]</small></small> diggeth a pit shall fall into it; and whoso breaketh an hedge, a serpent<small><small>[^19]</small></small> shall bite him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso removeth stones shall be hurt therewith; \[and\] he that cleaveth wood shall be endangered thereby. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If the iron be blunt, and he do not whet the edge, then must he put to more strength: but wisdom<small><small>[^20]</small></small> \[is\] profitable to direct. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Surely the serpent<small><small>[^21]</small></small> will bite without enchantment; and a babbler<small><small>[^22]</small></small> is no better. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The words<small><small>[^23]</small></small> of a wise man’s mouth \[are\] gracious<small><small>[^24]</small></small>; but<small><small>[^25]</small></small> the lips of a fool will swallow up himself. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The beginning<small><small>[^26]</small></small> of the words of his mouth \[is\] foolishness: and the end of his talk<small><small>[^27]</small></small> \[is\] mischievous madness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A fool<small><small>[^28]</small></small> also is full of words<small><small>[^29]</small></small>: a man<small><small>[^30]</small></small> cannot tell what shall be; and what shall be after him, who can tell him? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The labour<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of the foolish wearieth every one of them, because<small><small>[^32]</small></small> he knoweth not how to go to the city. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Woe to thee, O land, when<small><small>[^33]</small></small> thy king \[is\] a child, and<small><small>[^34]</small></small> thy princes eat in the<small><small>[^35]</small></small> morning! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Blessed \[art\] thou, O land, when<small><small>[^36]</small></small> thy king \[is\] the son of nobles, and thy<small><small>[^37]</small></small> princes eat in due season, for strength, and not for drunkenness! 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]By much slothfulness the building decayeth; and through idleness of the hands the house droppeth through.<small><small>[^38]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]A feast<small><small>[^39]</small></small> is made for laughter, and wine<small><small>[^40]</small></small> maketh merry<small><small>[^41]</small></small>: but<small><small>[^42]</small></small> money answereth all \[things\]. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.10.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Curse<small><small>[^43]</small></small> not the king, no not in thy thought<small><small>[^44]</small></small>; and curse not the rich in thy bedchamber<small><small>[^45]</small></small>: for a bird of the air shall carry the voice, and that which hath wings shall tell the matter. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Eccl.9|<< Ecclesiastes 9]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Eccl.11|Ecclesiastes 11 >>]]</td></tr></table>
