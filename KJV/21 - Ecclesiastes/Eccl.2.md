<big><big>[[Eccl.2.1 The Futility of Pleasure|⥃]]</big></big>***The Futility of Pleasure***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I said<small><small>[^2]</small></small> in mine heart, Go to<small><small>[^3]</small></small> now, I will<small><small>[^4]</small></small> prove thee with mirth, therefore enjoy pleasure: and, behold, this also \[is\] vanity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I said<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of laughter, \[It is<small><small>[^6]</small></small>\] mad: and of mirth, What doeth it? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I sought<small><small>[^7]</small></small> in mine heart to give myself unto wine<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, yet<small><small>[^9]</small></small> acquainting mine heart with wisdom; and to lay<small><small>[^10]</small></small> hold on folly, till<small><small>[^11]</small></small> I might see what \[was\] that good for the sons of men, which they should do under the heaven all<small><small>[^12]</small></small> the days of their life. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I made<small><small>[^13]</small></small> me great works; I builded<small><small>[^14]</small></small> me houses; I planted<small><small>[^15]</small></small> me vineyards: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I made me<small><small>[^16]</small></small> gardens and orchards, and I planted<small><small>[^17]</small></small> trees in them of all \[kind of\] fruits: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I made me pools<small><small>[^18]</small></small> of water, to water<small><small>[^19]</small></small> therewith the wood that bringeth forth trees: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I got \[me\] servants<small><small>[^20]</small></small> and maidens, and had<small><small>[^21]</small></small> servants born in my house<small><small>[^22]</small></small>; also<small><small>[^23]</small></small> I had great possessions of great and small cattle above all that were in Jerusalem before me: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I gathered me also silver<small><small>[^24]</small></small> and gold, and the peculiar treasure of kings and of the provinces: I gat me men singers<small><small>[^25]</small></small> and women singers, and the delights of the sons of men, \[as\] musical instruments<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, and that of all sorts. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So I was great, and increased more than all that were before me in Jerusalem: also my wisdom remained with me.<small><small>[^27]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And whatsoever<small><small>[^28]</small></small> mine eyes desired I kept not from them, I withheld not my heart from any joy; for my heart rejoiced<small><small>[^29]</small></small> in all my labour: and this was my portion of all my labour. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then I looked<small><small>[^30]</small></small> on all the works that my hands had wrought, and on the labour that I had laboured to do: and, behold<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, all \[was\] vanity and vexation of spirit, and \[there was\] no profit under the sun.

<big><big>[[Eccl.2.12 The Wise and the Foolish|⥃]]</big></big>***The Wise and the Foolish***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I turned<small><small>[^32]</small></small> myself to behold wisdom, and madness, and folly: for what \[can\] the man \[do\] that cometh after the king? \[even\] that which hath been already done. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then I saw<small><small>[^33]</small></small> that wisdom excelleth folly<small><small>[^34]</small></small>, as far as light<small><small>[^35]</small></small> excelleth darkness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wise<small><small>[^36]</small></small> man’s eyes \[are\] in his head; but the fool walketh in darkness: and I myself perceived also that one<small><small>[^37]</small></small> event happeneth to them all. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said I in my heart, As it happeneth to the fool, so it happeneth even to me<small><small>[^38]</small></small><small><small>[^39]</small></small>; and why was I then more wise? Then I<small><small>[^40]</small></small> said in my heart, that this also \[is\] vanity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For \[there is<small><small>[^41]</small></small>\] no remembrance of the wise more than of the fool for ever; seeing that which now \[is\] in the days to come shall all be forgotten. And how<small><small>[^42]</small></small> dieth the wise \[man\]? as the fool. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore I hated<small><small>[^43]</small></small> life; because the work<small><small>[^44]</small></small> that is wrought under the sun \[is\] grievous unto me: for<small><small>[^45]</small></small> all \[is\] vanity and vexation of spirit.

<big><big>[[Eccl.2.18 The Futility of Work|⥃]]</big></big>***The Futility of Work***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Yea, I hated<small><small>[^46]</small></small> all my labour which I had taken<small><small>[^47]</small></small> under the sun: because I should<small><small>[^48]</small></small> leave it unto the man that shall be after me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And who knoweth<small><small>[^49]</small></small> whether he shall be a wise \[man\] or a fool? yet shall he have rule over all my labour wherein I have laboured, and wherein I have shewed myself wise under<small><small>[^50]</small></small> the sun. This \[is\] also vanity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore I went about to cause my heart to despair of all the labour which I took under the sun.<small><small>[^51]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For there is a man whose<small><small>[^52]</small></small> labour \[is\] in wisdom, and in knowledge, and in equity; yet to a man that hath not laboured therein shall he leave<small><small>[^53]</small></small> it \[for\] his portion. This also \[is\] vanity and a great evil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For what hath man<small><small>[^54]</small></small> of all his labour, and of the<small><small>[^55]</small></small> vexation of his heart, wherein he hath laboured under the sun? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For all<small><small>[^56]</small></small> his days \[are\] sorrows, and his travail grief; yea, his heart<small><small>[^57]</small></small> taketh not rest in the night. This is also vanity. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]\[There is\] nothing<small><small>[^58]</small></small> better for a man, \[than\] that he should eat and drink, and \[that\] he should make his soul enjoy good<small><small>[^59]</small></small> in his labour. This also I saw, that it<small><small>[^60]</small></small> \[was\] from the hand of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For who can<small><small>[^61]</small></small> eat, or who else can hasten \[hereunto\], more than I? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.2.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For \[God\] giveth to a man that \[is\] good in his sight<small><small>[^62]</small></small> wisdom<small><small>[^63]</small></small>, and knowledge, and joy: but to the sinner<small><small>[^64]</small></small> he giveth travail, to gather and to heap up, that he may give to \[him that is\] good before God. This also \[is\] vanity and vexation of spirit. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Eccl.1|<< Ecclesiastes 1]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Eccl.3|Ecclesiastes 3 >>]]</td></tr></table>
