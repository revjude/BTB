***Obey the King*** ^bsb1525

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who \[is\] as the<small><small>[^2]</small></small> wise \[man\]? and who knoweth<small><small>[^3]</small></small> the interpretation of a thing? a man’s<small><small>[^4]</small></small> wisdom maketh his face to shine, and the<small><small>[^5]</small></small> boldness<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of his face shall be changed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I \[counsel<small><small>[^7]</small></small> thee\] to keep the king’s commandment, and \[that\] in regard<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of the oath of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be not hasty<small><small>[^9]</small></small> to go out of his sight: stand<small><small>[^10]</small></small> not in an evil thing; for<small><small>[^11]</small></small> he doeth whatsoever pleaseth him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Where the word<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of a king \[is\], \[there is\] power: and who may say unto him, What<small><small>[^13]</small></small> doest thou? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whoso keepeth<small><small>[^14]</small></small> the commandment shall feel<small><small>[^15]</small></small> no evil thing: and a wise<small><small>[^16]</small></small> man’s heart discerneth both time and judgment. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because to every<small><small>[^17]</small></small> purpose there is time and judgment, therefore<small><small>[^18]</small></small> the misery of man \[is\] great upon him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he knoweth<small><small>[^19]</small></small> not that which shall be: for who can tell him when<small><small>[^20]</small></small> it shall be? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] no<small><small>[^21]</small></small> man that hath power<small><small>[^22]</small></small> over the spirit to retain the spirit; neither \[hath he\] power in the day of death: and \[there is\] no discharge<small><small>[^23]</small></small> in \[that\] war; neither<small><small>[^24]</small></small> shall wickedness deliver those that are given to it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All this<small><small>[^25]</small></small> have I seen, and applied my heart unto every work that is done under the sun: \[there is<small><small>[^26]</small></small>\] a time wherein one man ruleth over another to his own hurt.

***Fear God***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Isa.8|Isaiah 8:11–15]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb1526

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And so<small><small>[^27]</small></small> I saw the wicked buried, who had come and gone from the place<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of the holy, and they were<small><small>[^29]</small></small> forgotten in the city where they had so done: this \[is\] also vanity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because sentence<small><small>[^30]</small></small> against an evil work is not executed speedily, therefore the heart of the sons of men is fully<small><small>[^31]</small></small> set in them to do evil. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Though a sinner<small><small>[^32]</small></small> do evil an hundred times, and his \[days\] be prolonged, yet surely<small><small>[^33]</small></small> I know that it shall be well with them that fear God, which fear before<small><small>[^34]</small></small> him: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But it shall<small><small>[^35]</small></small> not be well with the wicked, neither<small><small>[^36]</small></small> shall he prolong \[his\] days, \[which are\] as a<small><small>[^37]</small></small> shadow; because he feareth not before God.

***God’s Ways Are Mysterious*** ^bsb1527

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There is a vanity<small><small>[^38]</small></small> which is done upon the earth; that there be just<small><small>[^39]</small></small> \[men\], unto whom it happeneth according to the work of the wicked; again, there be wicked \[men\], to whom it happeneth according to the work of the righteous: I said that this also \[is\] vanity. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then I<small><small>[^40]</small></small> commended mirth, because a man hath no better thing under the sun, than to eat, and to drink, and to be merry: for that shall abide with him of his labour the days of his life, which God giveth him under the sun. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]When I<small><small>[^41]</small></small> applied mine heart to know wisdom, and to see the business that is done upon the earth: (for also \[there is that<small><small>[^42]</small></small>\] neither day nor night seeth sleep with his eyes:) <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.8.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then I beheld all the work of God, that a man<small><small>[^43]</small></small> cannot find out the work that is done under the sun: because though a man labour to seek \[it\] out, yet he shall not find \[it\]; yea further; though a wise \[man\] think to know \[it\], yet shall he not be able to find \[it\]. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Eccl.7|<< Ecclesiastes 7]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Eccl.9|Ecclesiastes 9 >>]]</td></tr></table>
