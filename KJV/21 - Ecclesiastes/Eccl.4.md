<big><big>[[Eccl.4.1 The Evil of Oppression|⥃]]</big></big>***The Evil of Oppression***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So I returned<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, and considered<small><small>[^3]</small></small> all the oppressions that are done under the sun: and behold the tears<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of \[such as were\] oppressed, and they had<small><small>[^5]</small></small> no comforter; and on the side<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of their oppressors \[there was\] power; but they had no comforter. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wherefore I praised the dead which are already dead more than the living which are yet alive.<small><small>[^7]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea, better<small><small>[^8]</small></small> \[is he\] than both they, which hath not yet been, who<small><small>[^9]</small></small> hath not seen the evil work that is done under the sun. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Again, I considered all travail, and every<small><small>[^10]</small></small> right work, that for this a man is envied of his neighbour. This \[is<small><small>[^11]</small></small>\] also vanity and vexation of spirit. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The fool<small><small>[^12]</small></small> foldeth his hands together, and eateth<small><small>[^13]</small></small> his own flesh. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Better \[is\] an handful \[with\] quietness, than both the hands full \[with\] travail and vexation of spirit.<small><small>[^14]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then I returned, and I saw vanity under the sun.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There is one<small><small>[^16]</small></small> \[alone\], and \[there is\] not a second; yea, he hath<small><small>[^17]</small></small> neither child no<small><small>[^18]</small></small>r brother: yet \[is there\] no end of all his labour; neither is his<small><small>[^19]</small></small> eye satisfied with riches; neither \[saith he\], For<small><small>[^20]</small></small> whom do I labour, and bereave my soul of good? This \[is\] also vanity, yea, it \[is<small><small>[^21]</small></small>\] a sore travail. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Two \[are<small><small>[^22]</small></small>\] better than one; because they have a good<small><small>[^23]</small></small> reward for their labour. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For if<small><small>[^24]</small></small> they fall, the one will lift up his fellow: but<small><small>[^25]</small></small> woe to him \[that is\] alone when he falleth; for \[he hath\] not another to help him up. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Again, if two lie together, then they have heat: but how can one be warm \[alone\]?<small><small>[^26]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And if<small><small>[^27]</small></small> one prevail against him, two shall withstand him; and a<small><small>[^28]</small></small> threefold cord is not quickly broken.

<big><big>[[Eccl.4.13 The Futility of Power|⥃]]</big></big>***The Futility of Power***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Better \[is\] a poor<small><small>[^29]</small></small> and a wise child than an old and foolish king, who will no more be<small><small>[^30]</small></small> admonished. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For out<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of prison he cometh to reign; whereas also<small><small>[^32]</small></small> \[he that is\] born in his kingdom becometh poor. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I considered all the living which walk under the sun, with the second child<small><small>[^33]</small></small> that shall stand up in his stead. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.4.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] no end<small><small>[^34]</small></small> of all the people, \[even\] of all that have been before them: they also<small><small>[^35]</small></small> that come after shall not rejoice in him.
