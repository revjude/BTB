<big><big>[[Eccl.11.1 Cast Your Bread upon the Waters|⥃]]</big></big>***Cast Your Bread upon the Waters***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.11.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Cast<small><small>[^2]</small></small> thy bread<small><small>[^3]</small></small> upon the waters<small><small>[^4]</small></small>: for<small><small>[^5]</small></small> thou shalt find it after many days.<small><small>[^6]</small></small><small><small>[^7]</small></small><small><small>[^8]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.11.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Give a portion<small><small>[^9]</small></small> to seven<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, and also to eight; for<small><small>[^11]</small></small> thou knowest not what evil shall be upon the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.11.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If the clouds<small><small>[^12]</small></small> be full of rain, they empty \[themselves\] upon the earth: and if the tree<small><small>[^13]</small></small> fall toward the south, or toward the north, in the place where the tree falleth, there it shall be. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.11.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He that observeth the wind shall not sow; and he that regardeth the clouds shall not reap.<small><small>[^14]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.11.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As thou knowest not what<small><small>[^15]</small></small> \[is\] the way of the spirit, \[nor<small><small>[^16]</small></small>\] how the bones \[do grow\] in the womb of her that is with child: even<small><small>[^17]</small></small> so thou knowest not the works of God who maketh all. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.11.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the morning sow<small><small>[^18]</small></small> thy seed, and in the evening withhold not thine hand: for thou knowest<small><small>[^19]</small></small> not whether shall prosper<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, either this or that, or whether they both \[shall be\] alike good.

<big><big>[[Eccl.11.7 Enjoy Your Years|⥃]]</big></big>***Enjoy Your Years***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.11.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Truly the light<small><small>[^21]</small></small> \[is\] sweet, and a pleasant<small><small>[^22]</small></small> \[thing it is\] for the eyes to behold the sun: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.11.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But if a man live many years, \[and\] rejoice<small><small>[^23]</small></small> in them all; yet<small><small>[^24]</small></small> let him remember the days of darkness; for they shall be many. All that<small><small>[^25]</small></small> cometh \[is\] vanity.<small><small>[^26]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.11.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Rejoice<small><small>[^27]</small></small>, O young man, in thy youth<small><small>[^28]</small></small>; and let thy heart cheer thee in the days of thy youth, and walk<small><small>[^29]</small></small> in the ways of thine heart, and in the sight<small><small>[^30]</small></small> of thine eyes: but know<small><small>[^31]</small></small> thou, that for all these \[things\] God will bring thee into judgment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.11.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore remove<small><small>[^32]</small></small> sorrow<small><small>[^33]</small></small> from thy heart, and put<small><small>[^34]</small></small> away evil from thy flesh: for<small><small>[^35]</small></small> childhood and youth \[are\] vanity. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Eccl.10|<< Ecclesiastes 10]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Eccl.12|Ecclesiastes 12 >>]]</td></tr></table>
