***Everything Is Futile*** ^bsb1510

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The words of the Preacher<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, the son of David, king<small><small>[^3]</small></small> in Jerusalem. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Vanity of vanities, saith the Preacher, vanity of vanities; all \[is\] vanity.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> What profit<small><small>[^5]</small></small> hath a man of all his labour which he taketh under<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the sun? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[One\] generation<small><small>[^7]</small></small> passeth away, and \[another\] generation cometh: but<small><small>[^8]</small></small> the earth abideth for ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The sun<small><small>[^9]</small></small> also ariseth, and the sun goeth down, and hasteth<small><small>[^10]</small></small> to his place where he arose. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wind<small><small>[^11]</small></small> goeth toward the south, and turneth about unto the north; it whirleth about continually, and the wind returneth again according to his circuits. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All the rivers run<small><small>[^12]</small></small> into the sea; yet the sea \[is\] not full; unto the place from whence the rivers come, thither they return again<small><small>[^13]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All things \[are\] full<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of labour; man<small><small>[^15]</small></small> cannot utter \[it\]: the eye<small><small>[^16]</small></small> is not satisfied with seeing, nor the ear filled with hearing. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The thing that hath<small><small>[^17]</small></small> been, it \[is that\] which shall be; and that which is done \[is\] that which shall be done: and \[there<small><small>[^18]</small></small> is\] no new \[thing\] under the sun. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Is there \[any\] thing whereof it may be said, See, this \[is\] new? it hath<small><small>[^19]</small></small> been already of old time, which was before us. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is<small><small>[^20]</small></small>\] no remembrance of former \[things\]; neither shall there be \[any\] remembrance of \[things\] that are to come with \[those\] that shall come after.

***With Wisdom Comes Sorrow*** ^bsb1511

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I the Preacher was king over Israel in Jerusalem.<small><small>[^21]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I gave<small><small>[^22]</small></small> my heart to seek and search out by wisdom concerning all \[things\] that are done under heaven: this sore<small><small>[^23]</small></small> travail hath God given to the sons of man to be exercised<small><small>[^24]</small></small> therewith. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.1.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have seen all the works that are done under the sun;
