***Remember Your Creator*** ^bsb1534

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.12.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Remember<small><small>[^2]</small></small> now thy Creator in the days of thy youth, while<small><small>[^3]</small></small> the evil days come not, nor the years draw nigh, when<small><small>[^4]</small></small> thou shalt say, I have no pleasure in them; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.12.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> While the sun<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, or the light, or the moon, or the stars, be not darkened, nor<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the clouds return after the rain: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.12.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the day when the keepers of the house shall tremble, and the strong<small><small>[^7]</small></small> men shall bow themselves, and the grinders cease because they are few, and those<small><small>[^8]</small></small> that look out of the windows be darkened, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.12.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the doors shall<small><small>[^9]</small></small> be shut in the streets, when the sound of the grinding is low, and he shall rise up at the voice of the bird, and all the daughters of musick shall be brought low; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.12.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also \[when\] they shall be afraid of \[that which is\] high, and fears \[shall be\] in the way, and the almond<small><small>[^10]</small></small> tree shall flourish, and the grasshopper shall be a burden, and desire shall fail: because<small><small>[^11]</small></small> man goeth to his long home, and the mourners<small><small>[^12]</small></small> go about the streets: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.12.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Or ever the silver cord be loosed, or the golden bowl be broken, or the pitcher be broken at the fountain, or the wheel broken at the cistern. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.12.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shall the dust<small><small>[^13]</small></small> return to the earth as it was: and the spirit<small><small>[^14]</small></small> shall return unto God<small><small>[^15]</small></small> who gave it. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.12.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Vanity of vanities, saith the preacher; all \[is\] vanity.<small><small>[^16]</small></small>

***The Whole Duty of Man*** ^bsb1535

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.12.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And moreover, because the preacher was wise<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, he still<small><small>[^18]</small></small> taught the people knowledge; yea, he gave<small><small>[^19]</small></small> good heed, and sought out, \[and\] set in order many proverbs. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.12.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The preacher<small><small>[^20]</small></small> sought to find out acceptable words<small><small>[^21]</small></small>: and \[that which was\] written<small><small>[^22]</small></small> \[was\] upright, \[even\] words of truth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.12.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The words of the wise \[are\] as goads<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, and as nails fastened \[by\] the masters<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of assemblies, \[which\] are given<small><small>[^25]</small></small> from one shepherd. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Eccl.12.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And further, by these<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, my son, be admonished: of making many books \[there is\] no end; and much study<small><small>[^27]</small></small> \[is\] a weariness<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of the flesh.
