***Judgment on Nineveh*** ^bsb2038

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Woe to<small><small>[^2]</small></small> the bloody city<small><small>[^3]</small></small>! it \[is\] all full<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of lies \[and\] robbery; the prey departeth not; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The noise<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of a whip, and the noise of the rattling of the wheels, and of the pransing horses, and of the jumping chariots. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The horseman lifteth up both the bright sword and the glittering spear<small><small>[^6]</small></small>: and \[there<small><small>[^7]</small></small> is\] a multitude of slain, and a great number of carcases; and \[there is\] none end of \[their\] corpses; they stumble upon their corpses: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because of the multitude of the whoredoms of the wellfavoured harlot, the mistress<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of witchcrafts, that selleth nations through her whoredoms, and families through her witchcrafts. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, I \[am<small><small>[^9]</small></small>\] against thee, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts; and I will discover<small><small>[^10]</small></small> thy skirts upon thy face, and I will shew the nations thy nakedness, and the kingdoms thy shame. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I will cast<small><small>[^11]</small></small> abominable filth upon thee, and make<small><small>[^12]</small></small> thee vile, and will set<small><small>[^13]</small></small> thee as a gazingstock. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it shall come to pass, \[that\] all<small><small>[^14]</small></small> they that look upon thee shall flee from thee, and say, Nineveh<small><small>[^15]</small></small> is laid waste: who<small><small>[^16]</small></small> will bemoan her? whence shall I seek comforters for thee? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Art thou<small><small>[^17]</small></small> better than populous No<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, that was situate among the rivers, \[that had<small><small>[^19]</small></small>\] the waters round about it, whose rampart \[was\] the sea, \[and\] her wall \[was\] from the sea? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ethiopia<small><small>[^20]</small></small> and Egypt \[were\] her strength, and \[it was\] infinite; Put<small><small>[^21]</small></small> and Lubim were thy helpers<small><small>[^22]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet \[was\] she carried<small><small>[^23]</small></small> away, she went into captivity: her young<small><small>[^24]</small></small> children also were dashed in pieces at<small><small>[^25]</small></small> the top of all the streets: and they cast<small><small>[^26]</small></small> lots for her honourable men, and all her great men were bound in chains. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou also<small><small>[^27]</small></small> shalt be drunken<small><small>[^28]</small></small>: thou shalt be hid<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, thou also<small><small>[^30]</small></small> shalt seek strength because of the enemy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> All thy strong holds \[shall be like\] fig trees with the firstripe figs: if they be shaken, they shall even fall into the mouth of the eater.<small><small>[^31]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, thy people<small><small>[^32]</small></small> in the midst of thee \[are\] women: the gates<small><small>[^33]</small></small> of thy land shall be set wide open unto thine enemies: the fire shall devour thy bars<small><small>[^34]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Draw<small><small>[^35]</small></small> thee waters for the siege, fortify<small><small>[^36]</small></small> thy strong holds: go into clay, and tread the morter, make strong the brickkiln. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There shall the<small><small>[^37]</small></small> fire devour thee; the sword shall cut thee off, it<small><small>[^38]</small></small> shall eat thee up like the cankerworm: make thyself many as the cankerworm, make thyself many as the locusts<small><small>[^39]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou hast multiplied thy merchants above<small><small>[^40]</small></small> the stars of heaven: the cankerworm spoileth<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, and flieth away. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy crowned \[are\] as the locusts, and thy captains as the great grasshoppers, which camp in the hedges in the cold day, \[but\] when the sun ariseth they flee away, and their place is not known where they \[are\].<small><small>[^42]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy shepherds<small><small>[^43]</small></small> slumber, O king of Assyria: thy nobles<small><small>[^44]</small></small> shall dwell \[in the dust\]: thy people<small><small>[^45]</small></small> is scattered upon the mountains, and no man gathereth \[them\].<small><small>[^46]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Nah.3.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] no<small><small>[^47]</small></small> healing<small><small>[^48]</small></small> of thy bruise; thy wound is grievous: all that hear the bruit<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of thee shall<small><small>[^50]</small></small> clap the hands over thee: for upon<small><small>[^51]</small></small> whom hath not thy wickedness passed continually?  <div canonical="true" eID="gen962" osisID="Nah" type="book"/><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Nah.2|<< Nahum 2]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Hab.1|Habakkuk 1 >>]]</td></tr></table>
