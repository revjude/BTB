***Ezra Arrives in Jerusalem*** ^bsb1183

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now after these things, in the reign of Artaxerxes<small><small>[^2]</small></small> king of Persia, Ezra<small><small>[^3]</small></small> the son of Seraiah<small><small>[^4]</small></small>, the son of Azariah, the son of Hilkiah<small><small>[^5]</small></small>,<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The son of Shallum, the son of Zadok<small><small>[^7]</small></small>, the son of Ahitub, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The son of Amariah, the son of Azariah, the son of Meraioth, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The son of Zerahiah, the son of Uzzi, the son of Bukki, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The son of Abishua, the son of Phinehas<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, the son of Eleazar<small><small>[^9]</small></small>, the son of Aaron the chief priest<small><small>[^10]</small></small>: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> This Ezra went up from Babylon; ***Artaxerxes’ Letter for Ezra*** ^bsb1184

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now this \[is\] the copy<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of the letter that the king offering willingly for the house of their God which \[is\] in Jerusalem: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That thou mayest buy speedily<small><small>[^41]</small></small> with this money bullocks, rams, lambs, with their meat offerings<small><small>[^42]</small></small> and their drink offerings, and offer them<small><small>[^43]</small></small> upon the altar of the house of your God which \[is\] in Jerusalem. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.18|18]]**<span do<small><small>[^45]</small></small> after the will<small><small>[^46]</small></small> of your God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And whatsoever more shall be needful for the house of thy God, which thou shalt have occasion to bestow, bestow \[it<small><small>[^49]</small></small>\] out of the king’s treasure house. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I, \[even\] I Artaxerxes<small><small>[^50]</small></small> the king, do make a decree to all the treasurers which \[are\] beyond the river<small><small>[^51]</small></small>, that whatsoever Ezra the <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Also we certify you, that touching any<small><small>[^59]</small></small> of the priests and Levites, singers, porters, Nethinims, or ministers of this house of God, it shall not be lawful to impose toll, tribute, or custom, upon them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And thou, Ezra, after the wisdom<small><small>[^60]</small></small> of thy God, that \[is\] in thine hand, set magistrates<small><small>[^61]</small></small> and judges, which may judge all the people that \[are\] beyond the river<small><small>[^62]</small></small>, all such as know the laws of thy God; and teach ye<small><small>[^63]</small></small> them that know \[them\] not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ezra.7.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And whosoever<small><small>[^64]</small></small> will not do the law of thy God<small><small>[^65]</small></small>, and the law of the king, let judgment be executed speedily upon him, whether \[it be<small><small>[^66]</small></small>\] unto death, or to banishment<small><small>[^67]</small></small>, or to confiscation of goods, or to imprisonment.
