<big><big>[[Ps.32.1 The Joy of Forgiveness|⥃]]</big></big>***The Joy of Forgiveness***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Rom.4|Romans 4:1–12]] ]</sup></small>



\[A Psalm\] of David, Maschil.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.32.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Blessed<small><small>[^2]</small></small> \[is he whose\] transgression<small><small>[^3]</small></small> \[is\] forgiven, \[whose\] sin \[is\] covered<small><small>[^4]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.32.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Blessed \[is\] the man unto whom the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^5]</small></small></span> imputeth not iniquity, and in whose<small><small>[^6]</small></small> spirit \[there is\] no guile. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.32.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When<small><small>[^7]</small></small> I kept silence, my bones<small><small>[^8]</small></small> waxed old through my roaring<small><small>[^9]</small></small> all the day long. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.32.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For day and night thy hand<small><small>[^10]</small></small> was heavy upon me: my moisture<small><small>[^11]</small></small> is turned into the drought of summer. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.32.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I acknowledged<small><small>[^12]</small></small> my sin unto thee, and mine iniquity have<small><small>[^13]</small></small> I not hid. I said<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, I will confess my transgressions unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and thou forgavest<small><small>[^15]</small></small> the iniquity<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of my sin. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.32.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For this<small><small>[^17]</small></small> shall every one that is godly<small><small>[^18]</small></small> pray<small><small>[^19]</small></small> unto thee in a time<small><small>[^20]</small></small> when thou mayest be found: surely in the floods<small><small>[^21]</small></small> of great waters they shall not come nigh unto him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.32.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou \[art\] my<small><small>[^22]</small></small> hiding place; thou shalt preserve me from trouble; thou shalt compass<small><small>[^23]</small></small> me about with songs<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of deliverance. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.32.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will instruct<small><small>[^25]</small></small> thee and teach thee in the way which thou shalt go: I will guide<small><small>[^26]</small></small> thee with mine eye. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.32.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be ye<small><small>[^27]</small></small> no<small><small>[^28]</small></small>t as the horse, \[or\] as the mule, \[which\] have no understanding: whose mouth must be held in with bit and bridle, lest they come near unto thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.32.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Many<small><small>[^29]</small></small> sorrows \[shall be\] to the wicked: but<small><small>[^30]</small></small> he that trusteth in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, mercy shall compass him about. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.32.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be glad<small><small>[^31]</small></small> in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and rejoice, ye righteous: and shout<small><small>[^32]</small></small> for joy, all \[ye that are\] upright<small><small>[^33]</small></small> in heart. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ps.31|<< Psalm 31]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ps.33|Psalm 33 >>]]</td></tr></table>
