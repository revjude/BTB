***The LORD Is My Salvation*** ^bsb1320



\[A Psalm\] of David.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] my light<small><small>[^2]</small></small> and my salvation<small><small>[^3]</small></small>; whom shall I fear? the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] the strength<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of my life; of whom<small><small>[^5]</small></small> shall I be afraid? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When the wicked<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, \[even\] mine enemies and my foes, came upon<small><small>[^7]</small></small> me to<small><small>[^8]</small></small> eat up my flesh, they<small><small>[^9]</small></small> stumbled and fell. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Though an host<small><small>[^10]</small></small> should encamp against me, my heart shall not fear: though war<small><small>[^11]</small></small> should rise again<small><small>[^12]</small></small>st me, in this \[will\] I \[be\] confident. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> One<small><small>[^13]</small></small> \[thing\] have I desired of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, that will I seek<small><small>[^14]</small></small> after; that I may dwell<small><small>[^15]</small></small> in the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> all the days of my life, to behold<small><small>[^16]</small></small> the beauty<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and to enquire<small><small>[^18]</small></small> in his temple. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For in<small><small>[^19]</small></small> the time of trouble he shall hide<small><small>[^20]</small></small> me in his pavilion: in the secret<small><small>[^21]</small></small> of his tabernacle shall he hide me; he shall set me<small><small>[^22]</small></small> up upon a rock. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And<small><small>[^23]</small></small> now shall mine head be lifted up above<small><small>[^24]</small></small> mine enemies round about me: therefore<small><small>[^25]</small></small> will I offer in his tabernacle sacrifices of joy<small><small>[^26]</small></small>; I will<small><small>[^27]</small></small> sing, yea, I will sing praises unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[when\] I cry with my voice: have mercy also upon me, and answer me.<small><small>[^28]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[When<small><small>[^29]</small></small> thou saidst\], Seek<small><small>[^30]</small></small> ye my face; my heart said unto thee, Thy<small><small>[^31]</small></small> face, <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, will I seek. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hide<small><small>[^32]</small></small> not thy face \[far\] from me; put<small><small>[^33]</small></small> not thy servant away in anger: thou<small><small>[^34]</small></small> hast been my help; leave<small><small>[^35]</small></small> me not, neither forsake me, O God<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of my salvation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When<small><small>[^37]</small></small> my father and my mother forsake me, then the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^38]</small></small></span> will take me up<small><small>[^39]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Teach<small><small>[^40]</small></small> me thy way, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and lead me in a plain path<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, because of mine enemies<small><small>[^42]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Deliver me not over unto the will<small><small>[^43]</small></small> of mine enemies: for false<small><small>[^44]</small></small> witnesses are risen up against me, and such as breathe<small><small>[^45]</small></small> out cruelty. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[I had fainted<small><small>[^46]</small></small>\], unless I had believed to see the goodness of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> in the<small><small>[^47]</small></small> land of the living. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.27.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wait<small><small>[^48]</small></small> on the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: be<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of good courage, and<small><small>[^50]</small></small> he shall strengthen thine heart: wait, I say, on the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ps.26|<< Psalm
