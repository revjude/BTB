To the chief Musician, A Psalm of David.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.64.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear<small><small>[^2]</small></small> my voice, O God, in my prayer: preserve<small><small>[^3]</small></small> my life from fear of the enemy.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.64.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hide<small><small>[^5]</small></small> me from the secret<small><small>[^6]</small></small> counsel of the wicked; from the insurrection<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of the workers of iniquity: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.64.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who whet<small><small>[^8]</small></small> their tongue like a sword, \[and\] bend<small><small>[^9]</small></small> \[their bows to shoot\] their arrows, \[even\] bitter words: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.64.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That they may shoot<small><small>[^10]</small></small> in secret at the perfect<small><small>[^11]</small></small>: suddenly<small><small>[^12]</small></small> do they shoot at him, and fear not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.64.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They encourage<small><small>[^13]</small></small> themselves \[in\] an evil matter<small><small>[^14]</small></small>: they commune<small><small>[^15]</small></small> of laying snares<small><small>[^16]</small></small> privily; they say, Who<small><small>[^17]</small></small> shall see them? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.64.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They search<small><small>[^18]</small></small> out iniquities; they accomplish<small><small>[^19]</small></small> a diligent search: both the inward \[thought\] of every one \[of them\], and the heart, \[is\] deep. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.64.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But God<small><small>[^20]</small></small> shall shoot at them \[with\] an arrow; suddenly<small><small>[^21]</small></small> shall they be wounded<small><small>[^22]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.64.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So they shall make their own tongue<small><small>[^23]</small></small> to fall upon themselves: all that<small><small>[^24]</small></small> see them shall flee away. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.64.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And all men shall fear<small><small>[^25]</small></small>, and shall declare the work of God; for they<small><small>[^26]</small></small> shall wisely consider of his doing. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.64.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The righteous<small><small>[^27]</small></small> shall be glad in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and shall trust in him; and all the upright<small><small>[^28]</small></small> in heart shall glory. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ps.63|<< Psalm 63]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ps.65|Psalm 65 >>]]</td></tr></table>
