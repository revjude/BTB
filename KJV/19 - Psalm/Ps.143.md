<big><big>[[Ps.143.1 I Stretch Out My Hands to You|⥃]]</big></big>***I Stretch Out My Hands to You***



A Psalm of David.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.143.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear my prayer, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, give ear to my supplications: in thy faithfulness<small><small>[^2]</small></small> answer me, \[and\] in thy righteousness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.143.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And enter not<small><small>[^3]</small></small> into judgment with thy servant: for in thy sight<small><small>[^4]</small></small> shall no man living be justified. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.143.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the enemy<small><small>[^5]</small></small> hath persecuted my soul; he hath smitten<small><small>[^6]</small></small> my life down to the ground; he hath made me<small><small>[^7]</small></small> to dwell in darkness, as those that have been long dead. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.143.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore is my spirit<small><small>[^8]</small></small> overwhelmed within me; my heart<small><small>[^9]</small></small> within me is desolate. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.143.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I remember<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the days of old; I meditate on all thy works; I muse on the work of thy hands. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.143.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I stretch forth<small><small>[^11]</small></small> my hands unto thee: my soul<small><small>[^12]</small></small> \[thirsteth\] after thee, as a thirsty land. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.143.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear me<small><small>[^13]</small></small> speedily, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: my spirit<small><small>[^14]</small></small> faileth: hide not<small><small>[^15]</small></small> thy face from me, lest I be like<small><small>[^16]</small></small> unto them<small><small>[^17]</small></small> that go down into the pit. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.143.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Cause me to hear<small><small>[^18]</small></small> thy lovingkindness in the morning; for in thee do I trust: cause me<small><small>[^19]</small></small> to know the way wherein I should walk; for I lift<small><small>[^20]</small></small> up my soul unto thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.143.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Deliver me, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, from mine enemies: I flee unto thee<small><small>[^21]</small></small> to hide me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.143.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Teach<small><small>[^22]</small></small> me to do thy will; for thou \[art<small><small>[^23]</small></small>\] my God: thy spirit<small><small>[^24]</small></small> \[is\] good; lead me into the land<small><small>[^25]</small></small> of uprightness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.143.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Quicken<small><small>[^26]</small></small> me, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, for thy name’s sake: for thy righteousness’<small><small>[^27]</small></small> sake bring<small><small>[^28]</small></small> my soul out of trouble. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.143.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And of thy mercy<small><small>[^29]</small></small> cut off mine enemies, and destroy all them that afflict my soul: for I \[am\] thy<small><small>[^30]</small></small> servant. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ps.142|<< Psalm 142]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ps.144|Psalm 144 >>]]</td></tr></table>


[^1]:  [[Ps.143|Ps 143:1]], David prays for favour in judgment; [[Ps.143|Ps 143:3]], He complains of his griefs; [[Ps.143|Ps 143:5]], He strengthens his faith by meditation and prayer; [[Ps.143|Ps 143:7]], He prays for grace; [[Ps.143|Ps 143:9]], for deliverance; [[Ps.143|Ps 143:10]], for sanctification; [[Ps.143|Ps 143:12]], for destruction of his enemies;  The LXX, Vulgate, Ethiopic, and Arabic state that this Psalm was composed by David on the rebellion of his son Absalom; and there are several passages in it which agree remarkably well with that period; for then he had most reason to fear lest God should deal with him according to his sins; which he deprecates with such a deep sense of his unworthiness, that it has hence been numbered among the penitential Psalms, of which it is the last. In it he prays to God for pardon, [[Ps.143|Ps 143:1]]; acknowledges the impossibility of being saved but by grace, [[Ps.143|Ps 143:2]]; deplores the lamentable effects of sin, [[Ps.143|Ps 143:3-4]]; comforts himself with a retrospect of Godôòùs mercies of old, [[Ps.143|Ps 143:5]]; and prays, in a variety of expressions, for remission of sin, sanctification, and redemption, [[Ps.143|Ps 143:6-12]].
