$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Deliver<small><small>[^2]</small></small> me from mine enemies, O my God: defend me<small><small>[^3]</small></small> from them that rise up against me.<small><small>[^4]</small></small><small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Deliver me from the workers of iniquity, and save<small><small>[^6]</small></small> me from bloody men. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For, lo, they<small><small>[^7]</small></small> lie in wait for my soul: the mighty<small><small>[^8]</small></small> are gathered against me; not<small><small>[^9]</small></small> \[for\] my transgression, nor \[for\] my sin, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They run<small><small>[^10]</small></small> and prepare themselves without \[my\] fault: awake<small><small>[^11]</small></small> to help me<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, and behold. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou therefore, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of hosts, the God<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of Israel, awake to visit<small><small>[^14]</small></small> all the heathen<small><small>[^15]</small></small>: be not<small><small>[^16]</small></small> merciful to any wicked transgressors. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They return at evening: they make a noise like a dog, and go round about the city.<small><small>[^17]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, they belch<small><small>[^18]</small></small> out with their mouth: swords<small><small>[^19]</small></small> \[are\] in their lips: for who<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, \[say they\], doth hear? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But thou<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, shalt laugh at them; thou shalt have all the heathen<small><small>[^22]</small></small> in derision. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Because of\] his strength<small><small>[^23]</small></small> will I wait upon thee: for God<small><small>[^24]</small></small> \[is\] my defence<small><small>[^25]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The God<small><small>[^26]</small></small> of my mercy shall prevent<small><small>[^27]</small></small> me: God shall let<small><small>[^28]</small></small> me see \[my desire\] upon mine enemies<small><small>[^29]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Slay<small><small>[^30]</small></small> them not, lest my people forget: scatter<small><small>[^31]</small></small> them by thy power; and bring<small><small>[^32]</small></small> them down, O Lord our shield<small><small>[^33]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[For\] the<small><small>[^34]</small></small> sin of their mouth \[and\] the words of their lips let them even be taken<small><small>[^35]</small></small> in their pride: and for cursing<small><small>[^36]</small></small> and lying \[which\] they speak. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Consume<small><small>[^37]</small></small> \[them\] in wrath, consume \[them\], that they \[may\] not \[be\]: and let<small><small>[^38]</small></small> them know that God ruleth in Jacob unto the ends of the earth. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And at evening<small><small>[^39]</small></small> let them return; \[and\] let them make a noise like a dog, and go round about the city. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let them wander<small><small>[^40]</small></small> up and down for meat<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, and grudge<small><small>[^42]</small></small> if<small><small>[^43]</small></small> they be not satisfied. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But<small><small>[^44]</small></small> I will sing of thy power; yea, I will sing aloud<small><small>[^45]</small></small> of thy mercy in the morning<small><small>[^46]</small></small>: for thou<small><small>[^47]</small></small> hast been my defence and refuge in the day<small><small>[^48]</small></small> of my trouble. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.59.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Unto thee, O my<small><small>[^49]</small></small> strength, will I sing: for<small><small>[^50]</small></small> God \[is\] my defence, \[and\] the God of my mercy. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ps.58|<< Psalm 58]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ps.60|Psalm 60 >>]]</td></tr></table>
