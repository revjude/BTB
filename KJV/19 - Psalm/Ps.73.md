***Surely God Is Good to Israel*** ^bsb1366



A Psalm of Asaph.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Truly<small><small>[^2]</small></small> God<small><small>[^3]</small></small> \[is\] good to Israel, \[even\] to such<small><small>[^4]</small></small> as are of a clean heart<small><small>[^5]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But<small><small>[^6]</small></small> as for me, my feet<small><small>[^7]</small></small> were almost gone; my steps<small><small>[^8]</small></small> had well nigh slipped. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I was<small><small>[^9]</small></small> envious at the foolish, \[when\] I saw the prosperity of the wicked. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For \[there are\] no<small><small>[^10]</small></small> bands in their death: but their strength \[is\] firm<small><small>[^11]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They \[are<small><small>[^12]</small></small>\] not in trouble \[as other<small><small>[^13]</small></small>\] men; neither are they plagued like<small><small>[^14]</small></small> \[other\] men. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore<small><small>[^15]</small></small> pride compasseth them about as a chain<small><small>[^16]</small></small>; violence<small><small>[^17]</small></small> covereth<small><small>[^18]</small></small> them \[as\] a garment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Their eyes<small><small>[^19]</small></small> stand out with fatness: they have<small><small>[^20]</small></small> more than heart could wish. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They are corrupt<small><small>[^21]</small></small>, and speak wickedly<small><small>[^22]</small></small> \[concerning\] oppression: they speak loftily<small><small>[^23]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They set<small><small>[^24]</small></small> their mouth against the heavens, and their tongue<small><small>[^25]</small></small> walketh through the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore his people return hither: and waters<small><small>[^26]</small></small> of a full \[cup\] are wrung out to them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they say, How<small><small>[^27]</small></small> doth God know? and is there<small><small>[^28]</small></small> knowledge in the most High? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, these<small><small>[^29]</small></small> \[are\] the ungodly, who prosper<small><small>[^30]</small></small> in the world; they<small><small>[^31]</small></small> increase \[in\] riches. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Verily<small><small>[^32]</small></small> I have cleansed my heart \[in\] vain, and washed<small><small>[^33]</small></small> my hands in innocency. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For all<small><small>[^34]</small></small> the day long have I been plagued, and chastened<small><small>[^35]</small></small> every morning. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If I say, I will speak thus; behold, I should offend<small><small>[^36]</small></small> \[against\] the generation<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of thy children. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When<small><small>[^38]</small></small> I thought to know this, it \[was\] too painful for me<small><small>[^39]</small></small>; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Until<small><small>[^40]</small></small> I went into the sanctuary of God; \[then<small><small>[^41]</small></small>\] understood I their end. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Surely<small><small>[^42]</small></small> thou didst set them in slippery places: thou castedst<small><small>[^43]</small></small> them down into destruction. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> How<small><small>[^44]</small></small> are they \[brought\] into desolation, as in a moment! they are<small><small>[^45]</small></small> utterly consumed with terrors. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As a<small><small>[^46]</small></small> dream when<small><small>[^47]</small></small> \[one\] awaketh; \[so\], O Lord, when thou awakest, thou shalt despise their<small><small>[^48]</small></small> image. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thus my heart<small><small>[^49]</small></small> was grieved, and I was pricked in my<small><small>[^50]</small></small> reins. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So<small><small>[^51]</small></small> foolish \[was\] I, and ignorant<small><small>[^52]</small></small>: I was \[a<small><small>[^53]</small></small>s\] a beast before thee<small><small>[^54]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nevertheless<small><small>[^55]</small></small> I \[am\] continually with thee: thou hast<small><small>[^56]</small></small> holden \[me\] by my right hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou<small><small>[^57]</small></small> shalt guide me with thy counsel, and afterward receive<small><small>[^58]</small></small> me \[to\] glory. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whom<small><small>[^59]</small></small> have I in heaven \[but thee\]? and \[there is\] none upon<small><small>[^60]</small></small> earth \[that\] I desire beside thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My flesh<small><small>[^61]</small></small> and my heart faileth: \[but<small><small>[^62]</small></small>\] God \[is\] the strength<small><small>[^63]</small></small> of my heart, and my portion<small><small>[^64]</small></small> for ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For, lo<small><small>[^65]</small></small>, they that are far from thee shall perish: thou hast destroyed all them that go<small><small>[^66]</small></small> a whoring from thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.73.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But<small><small>[^67]</small></small> \[it is\] good for me to draw near to God: I have put my trust in the Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span>, that I
