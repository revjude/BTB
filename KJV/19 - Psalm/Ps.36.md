***The Transgression of the Wicked*** ^bsb1329



To the chief Musician, \[A Psalm\] of David the servant<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.36.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The transgression<small><small>[^3]</small></small> of the wicked saith within my heart, \[that there is\] no<small><small>[^4]</small></small> fear of God before his eyes. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.36.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he<small><small>[^5]</small></small> flattereth himself in his own eyes, until<small><small>[^6]</small></small> his iniquity be found to be hateful. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.36.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The words<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of his mouth \[are\] iniquity and deceit: he hath<small><small>[^8]</small></small> left off to be wise, \[and\] to do good. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.36.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He deviseth<small><small>[^9]</small></small> mischief<small><small>[^10]</small></small> upon his bed; he setteth<small><small>[^11]</small></small> himself in a way \[that is\] not good; he abhorreth<small><small>[^12]</small></small> not evil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.36.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy mercy<small><small>[^13]</small></small>, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[is\] in the heavens; \[and\] thy faithfulness<small><small>[^14]</small></small> \[reacheth\] unto the clouds. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.36.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy righteousness<small><small>[^15]</small></small> \[is\] like the great mountains<small><small>[^16]</small></small>; thy judgments<small><small>[^17]</small></small> \[are\] a great deep: O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, thou<small><small>[^18]</small></small> preservest man and beast. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.36.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> How<small><small>[^19]</small></small> excellent<small><small>[^20]</small></small> \[is\] thy lovingkindness, O God! therefore the children of men put their<small><small>[^21]</small></small> trust under the shadow of thy wings. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.36.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They shall be abundantly<small><small>[^22]</small></small> satisfied<small><small>[^23]</small></small> with the fatness of thy house; and thou<small><small>[^24]</small></small> shalt make them drink of the river of thy pleasures<small><small>[^25]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.36.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^26]</small></small> with thee \[is\] the fountain of life: in thy<small><small>[^27]</small></small> light shall we see light. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.36.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O continue<small><small>[^28]</small></small> thy lovingkindness unto them that<small><small>[^29]</small></small> know thee; and thy<small><small>[^30]</small></small> righteousness to the upright in heart. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.36.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let not the foot<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of pride come against me, and let not the hand<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of the wicked remove me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.36.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There<small><small>[^33]</small></small> are the workers of iniquity fallen: they are cast down, and shall<small><small>[^34]</small></small> not be able to rise. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ps.35|<< Psalm 35]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ps.37|Psalm 37 >>]]</td></tr></table>
