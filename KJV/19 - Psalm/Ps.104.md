***How Many Are Your Works, O LORD!*** ^bsb1397

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.104.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Bless<small><small>[^2]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, O my soul. and majesty.<small><small>[^6]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.104.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who coverest \[thyself\] with light<small><small>[^7]</small></small> as \[with\] a garment: who stretchest<small><small>[^8]</small></small> out the heavens like a curtain: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.104.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who layeth<small><small>[^9]</small></small> the beams of his chambers in the waters: who maketh<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the clouds his chariot: who walketh<small><small>[^11]</small></small> upon the wings of the wind: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.104.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who maketh<small><small>[^12]</small></small> his angels spirits; his ministers<small><small>[^13]</small></small> a flaming fire: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.104.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Who\] laid the foundations of the earth<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, \[that\] it<small><small>[^15]</small></small> should not be removed for ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.104.6|6]]**<span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.104.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> At thy<small><small>[^17]</small></small> rebuke they fled<small><small>[^18]</small></small>; at the voice of thy thunder they hasted away. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.104.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They go up<small><small>[^19]</small></small> by the mountains; they go wild<small><small>[^24]</small></small> asses quench<small><small>[^25]</small></small> their thirst. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.104.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> wine<small><small>[^34]</small></small> \[that\] maketh glad the heart of man, \[and\] oil to make \[his<small><small>[^35]</small></small>\] face to shine, and bread<small><small>[^36]</small></small> \[which\] strengtheneth man’s heart. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.104.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The trees of the <span <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.104.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The young<small><small>[^46]</small></small> lions roar after their prey, and [^40]: The stork is a species of the ***ardea*** or heron genus, about the size of a goose in its body, but when erect, about three or four feet high; its general colour is white; extremity of the wings, and small part of the head, black; legs, very long, red, and naked a great way up; the toes four, long and connected, with flat nails like those of a man; beak long, jagged, red, and somewhat compressed; the upper and under chaps both of a length, with a furrow from the nostrils. It feeds on serpents, frogs, and insects, on which account it might be deemed unclean; lays four eggs, and sits thirty days; migrates about August, and returns in spring; and is remarkable for its love to its parents, whom it never forsakes, but feeds and cherishes when old; whence it had the name ***chaseedah***, which denotes kindness or piety, and stork, from the Greek <span class="asgreek"><span class='asgreek'>στοργη</span></span>, natural affection.
