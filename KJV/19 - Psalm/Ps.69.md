***The Waters Are up to My Neck*** ^bsb1362



<w lemma="strong:H5329" morph="strongMorph:TH8764">To the chief Musician</w> <w lemma="strong:H7799">upon Shoshannim</w>, \[A Psalm<small><small>[^2]</small></small>\] <w lemma="strong:H1732">of David</w>.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Save me, O God; for the waters<small><small>[^3]</small></small> are come in unto \[my\] soul. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I sink<small><small>[^4]</small></small> in deep mire<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, where \[there is\] no standing: I am come into deep waters<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, where the floods<small><small>[^7]</small></small> overflow me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I am<small><small>[^8]</small></small> weary of my crying: my throat<small><small>[^9]</small></small> is dried: mine<small><small>[^10]</small></small> eyes fail while I wait<small><small>[^11]</small></small> for my God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They that hate<small><small>[^12]</small></small> me without a cause are more than<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the hairs of mine head: they that would destroy me, \[being<small><small>[^14]</small></small>\] mine enemies wrongfully, are mighty: then I<small><small>[^15]</small></small> restored \[that\] which I took not away. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O God, thou knowest my foolishness; and my sins<small><small>[^16]</small></small> are not hid<small><small>[^17]</small></small> from thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let not<small><small>[^18]</small></small> them that wait on thee, O Lord <span class="smallcaps">God</span> of hosts, be ashamed for my sake: let not those that seek thee be confounded for my sake, O God of Israel<small><small>[^19]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because<small><small>[^20]</small></small> for thy sake I have borne reproach; shame<small><small>[^21]</small></small> hath covered my face. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I am become<small><small>[^22]</small></small> a stranger unto my brethren, and an alien<small><small>[^23]</small></small> unto my mother’s children. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the zeal<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of thine house hath eaten me up; and the<small><small>[^25]</small></small> reproaches of them that reproached thee are fallen upon me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When I wept, \[and chastened\] my soul with fasting, that was to my reproach.<small><small>[^26]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I made<small><small>[^27]</small></small> sackcloth also my garment; and I became<small><small>[^28]</small></small> a proverb to them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They<small><small>[^29]</small></small> that sit in the gate speak against me; and I \[was<small><small>[^30]</small></small>\] the song of the drunkards<small><small>[^31]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But as for me, my prayer<small><small>[^32]</small></small> \[is\] unto thee, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[in\] an<small><small>[^33]</small></small> acceptable time: O God, in the<small><small>[^34]</small></small> multitude of thy mercy hear me, in the truth of thy salvation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Deliver<small><small>[^35]</small></small> me out of the mire, and let me<small><small>[^36]</small></small> not sink: let me be delivered from them that hate me, and out of<small><small>[^37]</small></small> the deep waters. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let not the waterflood<small><small>[^38]</small></small> overflow me, neither let the deep swallow me up, and let not the pit<small><small>[^39]</small></small> shut her mouth upon me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear me, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; for thy<small><small>[^40]</small></small> lovingkindness \[is\] good: turn<small><small>[^41]</small></small> unto me according<small><small>[^42]</small></small> to the multitude of thy tender mercies. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And hide<small><small>[^43]</small></small> not thy face from thy servant; for I am<small><small>[^44]</small></small> in trouble: hear me speedily<small><small>[^45]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Draw<small><small>[^46]</small></small> nigh unto my soul, \[and\] redeem<small><small>[^47]</small></small> it: deliver me because<small><small>[^48]</small></small> of mine enemies. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou hast known my reproach<small><small>[^49]</small></small>, and my shame, and my dishonour<small><small>[^50]</small></small>: mine<small><small>[^51]</small></small> adversaries \[are\] all before thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Reproach<small><small>[^52]</small></small> hath broken my heart; and I am<small><small>[^53]</small></small> full of heaviness: and I looked<small><small>[^54]</small></small> \[for some\] to take pity<small><small>[^55]</small></small>, but \[there<small><small>[^56]</small></small> was\] none; and for comforters<small><small>[^57]</small></small>, but I found none. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They gave me also gall for my meat<small><small>[^58]</small></small>; and in my thirst they gave me vinegar<small><small>[^59]</small></small> to drink. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let their table<small><small>[^60]</small></small> become a snare before them: and \[that which should have been\] for \[their\] welfare, \[let it become\] a trap<small><small>[^61]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let their eyes<small><small>[^62]</small></small> be darkened, that they see not; and make their<small><small>[^63]</small></small> loins continually to shake. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Pour<small><small>[^64]</small></small> out thine indignation upon them, and let thy wrathful anger take<small><small>[^65]</small></small> hold of them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let their<small><small>[^66]</small></small> habitation<small><small>[^67]</small></small> be desolate; \[and\] let none dwell<small><small>[^68]</small></small> in their tents. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^69]</small></small> they persecute \[him\] whom<small><small>[^70]</small></small> thou hast smitten; and they talk<small><small>[^71]</small></small> to the grief of those<small><small>[^72]</small></small> whom thou hast wounded. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Add<small><small>[^73]</small></small> iniquity<small><small>[^74]</small></small> unto their iniquity: and let them<small><small>[^75]</small></small> not come into thy righteousness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let them be blotted<small><small>[^76]</small></small> out of the book of the living, and not be written<small><small>[^77]</small></small> with the righteous. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But I \[am\] poor<small><small>[^78]</small></small> and sorrowful: let thy<small><small>[^79]</small></small> salvation, O God, set me up on high. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will<small><small>[^80]</small></small> praise the name of God with a song, and will magnify<small><small>[^81]</small></small> him with thanksgiving. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[This\] also shall<small><small>[^82]</small></small> please the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> better than an ox \[or\] bullock that hath horns and hoofs. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The humble<small><small>[^83]</small></small> shall see \[this\], \[and\] be glad: and your heart<small><small>[^84]</small></small> shall live that seek God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^85]</small></small></span> heareth the poor, and despiseth not his prisoners<small><small>[^86]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.34|34]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let<small><small>[^87]</small></small> the heaven and earth praise him, the seas, and every thing that moveth<small><small>[^88]</small></small> therein. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.35|35]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For God<small><small>[^89]</small></small> will save Zion, and will build<small><small>[^90]</small></small> the cities of Judah: that they may dwell there, and have it in possession. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.69.36|36]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The seed<small><small>[^91]</small></small> also of his servants shall inherit it: and they<small><small>[^92]</small></small> that love his name shall dwell therein.
