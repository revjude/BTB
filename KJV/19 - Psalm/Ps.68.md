***God’s Enemies Are Scattered*** <small><small>[^2]</small></small>  ^bsb1361



To the chief Musician, A Psalm \[or\] Song of David.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let God arise<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, let his enemies be scattered<small><small>[^4]</small></small>: let them also that hate<small><small>[^5]</small></small> him flee before him<small><small>[^6]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As smoke<small><small>[^7]</small></small> is driven away, \[so\] drive \[them\] away: as wax<small><small>[^8]</small></small> melteth before the fire, \[so\] let the wicked perish at the presence of God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But<small><small>[^9]</small></small> let the righteous be glad; let them rejoice<small><small>[^10]</small></small> before God: yea, let them exceedingly rejoice<small><small>[^11]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Sing unto God<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, sing praises to his name: extol him that rideth<small><small>[^13]</small></small> upon the heavens by his name<small><small>[^14]</small></small> <span class="smallcaps">Jah<small><small>[^15]</small></small></span>, and rejoice before him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A father<small><small>[^16]</small></small> of the fatherless, and a judge<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of the widows, \[is\] God in his<small><small>[^18]</small></small> holy habitation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> God<small><small>[^19]</small></small> setteth the solitary in families<small><small>[^20]</small></small>: he bringeth<small><small>[^21]</small></small> out those which are bound with chains: but the rebellious<small><small>[^22]</small></small> dwell in a dry \[land\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O God<small><small>[^23]</small></small>, when thou wentest forth before thy people, when thou didst<small><small>[^24]</small></small> march through the wilderness; Selah: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The earth<small><small>[^25]</small></small> shook, the heavens<small><small>[^26]</small></small> also dropped at the presence of God: \[even\] Sinai<small><small>[^27]</small></small> itself \[was moved\] at the presence of God, the God<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou, O God, didst<small><small>[^29]</small></small> send<small><small>[^30]</small></small> a plentiful rain, whereby thou didst confirm thine inheritance<small><small>[^31]</small></small>, when it was weary. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy congregation<small><small>[^32]</small></small> hath dwelt therein: thou<small><small>[^33]</small></small>, O God, hast prepared of thy goodness for the poor. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The Lord<small><small>[^34]</small></small> gave the word: great \[was\] the company<small><small>[^35]</small></small> of those that published \[it\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Kings<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of armies did flee<small><small>[^37]</small></small> apace<small><small>[^38]</small></small>: and she that tarried at home divided the spoil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Though<small><small>[^39]</small></small> ye have<small><small>[^40]</small></small> lien among the pots, \[yet shall ye be as\] the wings<small><small>[^41]</small></small> of a dove covered with silver, and her feathers with yellow gold. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When<small><small>[^42]</small></small> the Almighty scattered kings in it, it was<small><small>[^43]</small></small> \[white\] as snow<small><small>[^44]</small></small> in Salmon. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The hill of God<small><small>[^45]</small></small> \[is as\] the hill of Bashan<small><small>[^46]</small></small>; an high hill \[as\] the hill of Bashan. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Why<small><small>[^47]</small></small> leap ye, ye high hills? \[this is\] the hill<small><small>[^48]</small></small> \[which\] God desireth to dwell in; yea, the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> will dwell \[in it\] for ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The chariots<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of God \[are\] twenty thousand<small><small>[^50]</small></small>, \[even\] thousands of angels: the Lord \[is\] among them, \[as in\] Sinai<small><small>[^51]</small></small>, in the holy \[place\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou hast ascended<small><small>[^52]</small></small> on high, thou hast led<small><small>[^53]</small></small> captivity captive: thou hast received<small><small>[^54]</small></small> gifts for men<small><small>[^55]</small></small>; yea, \[for\] the rebellious<small><small>[^56]</small></small> also, that<small><small>[^57]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God might dwell \[among them\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Blessed<small><small>[^58]</small></small> \[be\] the Lord, \[who\] daily<small><small>[^59]</small></small> loadeth us \[with benefits\], \[even\] the God of our salvation. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[He that is\] our God<small><small>[^60]</small></small> \[is\] the God of salvation; and unto<small><small>[^61]</small></small> <span class="smallcaps">God</span> the Lord \[belong\] the issues<small><small>[^62]</small></small> from death. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But God<small><small>[^63]</small></small> shall wound the head of his enemies, \[and\] the hairy<small><small>[^64]</small></small> scalp of such<small><small>[^65]</small></small> an one as goeth on still in his trespasses. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The Lord said, I will bring again from Bashan<small><small>[^66]</small></small>, I will bring \[my people\] again from the depths<small><small>[^67]</small></small> of the sea: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That<small><small>[^68]</small></small> thy foot may be dipped<small><small>[^69]</small></small> in the blood of \[thine\] enemies, \[and\] the tongue<small><small>[^70]</small></small> of thy dogs in the same. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They have seen thy goings, O God; \[even<small><small>[^71]</small></small>\] the goings of my God, my King, in the sanctuary. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The singers went before, the players<small><small>[^72]</small></small> on instruments \[followed\] after; among<small><small>[^73]</small></small> \[them were\] the damsels playing with timbrels. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Bless<small><small>[^74]</small></small> ye God in the congregations, \[even\] the Lord, from the fountain<small><small>[^75]</small></small> of Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There \[is\] little<small><small>[^76]</small></small> Benjamin \[with\] their ruler, the princes<small><small>[^77]</small></small> of Judah \[and\] their council<small><small>[^78]</small></small>, the princes of Zebulun, \[and\] the princes of Naphtali. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy God hath commanded<small><small>[^79]</small></small> thy strength: strengthen<small><small>[^80]</small></small>, O God, that which thou hast wrought for us. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because<small><small>[^81]</small></small> of thy temple at Jerusalem shall<small><small>[^82]</small></small> kings bring presents unto thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Rebuke<small><small>[^83]</small></small> the company of spearmen<small><small>[^84]</small></small>, the multitude<small><small>[^85]</small></small> of the bulls, with the calves of the people, \[till every<small><small>[^86]</small></small> one\] submit himself with pieces of silver: scatter thou<small><small>[^87]</small></small> the people \[that\] delight<small><small>[^88]</small></small> in war. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Princes<small><small>[^89]</small></small> shall come out of Egypt; Ethiopia<small><small>[^90]</small></small> shall soon stretch<small><small>[^91]</small></small> out her hands unto God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.32|32]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Sing unto God, ye kingdoms<small><small>[^92]</small></small> of the earth; O sing praises unto the Lord; Selah: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.33|33]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To him that rideth<small><small>[^93]</small></small> upon the heavens of heavens, \[which were\] of old<small><small>[^94]</small></small>; lo, he doth send out<small><small>[^95]</small></small> his voice<small><small>[^96]</small></small>, \[and that\] a mighty voice. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.34|34]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Ascribe<small><small>[^97]</small></small> ye strength unto God: his excellency<small><small>[^98]</small></small> \[is\] over Israel, and his strength<small><small>[^99]</small></small> \[is\] in the clouds<small><small>[^100]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.68.35|35]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O God, \[thou art\] terrible<small><small>[^101]</small></small> out of thy holy places: the God of Israel \[is\] he that giveth<small><small>[^102]</small></small> strength and power unto \[his\] people.
