<big><big>[[Ps.77.1 In the Day of Trouble I Sought the Lord|⥃]]</big></big>***In the Day of Trouble I Sought the Lord***



To the chief Musician, to Jeduthun, A Psalm<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of Asaph<small><small>[^3]</small></small>.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I cried<small><small>[^4]</small></small> unto God with my voice, \[even\] unto God with my voice; and he gave<small><small>[^5]</small></small> ear unto me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the<small><small>[^6]</small></small> day of my<small><small>[^7]</small></small> trouble I sought the Lord: my sore<small><small>[^8]</small></small> ran in the night, and ceased not: my soul<small><small>[^9]</small></small> refused to be comforted. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I remembered<small><small>[^10]</small></small> God, and was troubled: I complained<small><small>[^11]</small></small>, and my spirit<small><small>[^12]</small></small> was overwhelmed. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou holdest<small><small>[^13]</small></small> mine eyes waking: I am<small><small>[^14]</small></small> so troubled that I cannot speak. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I have considered the days of old, the years of ancient times.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I call to remembrance my song<small><small>[^16]</small></small> in the night: I commune<small><small>[^17]</small></small> with mine own heart: and<small><small>[^18]</small></small> my spirit made diligent search. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Will the Lord<small><small>[^19]</small></small> cast off for ever? and will<small><small>[^20]</small></small> he be favourable no more? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Is his<small><small>[^21]</small></small> mercy clean gone for ever? doth<small><small>[^22]</small></small> \[his\] promise fail for evermore<small><small>[^23]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hath God<small><small>[^24]</small></small> forgotten to be gracious? hath he in anger shut up<small><small>[^25]</small></small> his tender mercies? Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I said, This \[is<small><small>[^26]</small></small>\] my infirmity: \[but I will remember\] the years<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of the right hand of the most High.<small><small>[^28]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will remember the works of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: surely I will remember thy wonders of old.<small><small>[^29]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will meditate<small><small>[^30]</small></small> also of all thy work, and talk<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of thy doings. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy way<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, O God, \[is\] in the sanctuary: who<small><small>[^33]</small></small> \[is so\] great a God as \[our\] God? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou \[art\] the God<small><small>[^34]</small></small> that doest wonders: thou hast<small><small>[^35]</small></small> declared thy strength among the people. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou hast with<small><small>[^36]</small></small> \[thine\] arm redeemed thy people, the sons<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of Jacob and Joseph. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The waters saw thee, O God, the waters saw thee; they were afraid: the depths also were troubled.<small><small>[^38]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The clouds poured out water: the skies sent out a sound: thine<small><small>[^39]</small></small> arrows also went abroad.<small><small>[^40]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The voice<small><small>[^41]</small></small> of thy thunder \[was\] in the heaven: the lightnings<small><small>[^42]</small></small> lightened the world: the earth<small><small>[^43]</small></small> trembled and shook. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy way<small><small>[^44]</small></small> \[is\] in the sea, and thy path in the great waters, and thy footsteps<small><small>[^45]</small></small> are not known. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.77.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou leddest thy people like a flock by the hand of Moses and Aaron. <small><small>[^46]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ps.76|<< Psalm 76]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ps.78|Psalm 78 >>]]</td></tr></table>
