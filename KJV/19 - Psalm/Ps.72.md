<big><big>[[Ps.72.1 Endow the King with Your Justice|⥃]]</big></big>***Endow the King with Your Justice***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[1Kgs.3|1 Kings 3:1–15]] <big>†</big> [[2Chr.1|2 Chronicles 1:1–13]] <big>†</big> [[Ps.45|Psalms 45:1–17]] ]</sup></small>



\[A Psalm\] for<small><small>[^2]</small></small> Solomon.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Give<small><small>[^3]</small></small> the king thy judgments, O God, and thy righteousness unto the king’s<small><small>[^4]</small></small> son. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He shall<small><small>[^5]</small></small> judge thy people with righteousness, and thy poor<small><small>[^6]</small></small> with judgment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The mountains<small><small>[^7]</small></small> shall bring peace to the people, and the little<small><small>[^8]</small></small> hills, by righteousness<small><small>[^9]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He shall judge<small><small>[^10]</small></small> the poor of the people, he shall save the children of the needy, and shall break<small><small>[^11]</small></small> in pieces the oppressor<small><small>[^12]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They shall<small><small>[^13]</small></small> fear thee as long<small><small>[^14]</small></small> as the sun and moon endure, throughout all generations. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He shall come down like<small><small>[^15]</small></small> rain upon the mown grass: as showers \[that\] water the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In his days<small><small>[^16]</small></small> shall the righteous flourish; and abundance<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of peace so long as the moon endureth<small><small>[^18]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He shall<small><small>[^19]</small></small> have dominion also from sea to sea, and from the river unto the ends<small><small>[^20]</small></small> of the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They that<small><small>[^21]</small></small> dwell in the wilderness shall bow before him; and his enemies<small><small>[^22]</small></small> shall lick<small><small>[^23]</small></small> the dust. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The kings of Tarshish and of the isles shall bring presents: the kings of Sheba and Seba shall offer gifts.<small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea, all kings<small><small>[^25]</small></small> shall fall down before him: all nations<small><small>[^26]</small></small> shall serve him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^27]</small></small> he shall deliver the needy when he crieth; the poor also, and \[him<small><small>[^28]</small></small>\] that hath no helper. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He shall spare the poor and needy, and shall save<small><small>[^29]</small></small> the souls of the needy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He shall<small><small>[^30]</small></small> redeem their soul from deceit and violence: and precious<small><small>[^31]</small></small> shall their blood be in his sight. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he<small><small>[^32]</small></small> shall live, and to him<small><small>[^33]</small></small> shall be given<small><small>[^34]</small></small> of the gold of Sheba: prayer<small><small>[^35]</small></small> also shall be made for him continually; \[and\] daily<small><small>[^36]</small></small> shall he be praised. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> There<small><small>[^37]</small></small> shall be an handful of corn in the earth upon<small><small>[^38]</small></small> the top of the mountains; the fruit<small><small>[^39]</small></small> thereof shall shake like Lebanon: and \[they\] of the city<small><small>[^40]</small></small> shall flourish like grass of the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> His name<small><small>[^41]</small></small> shall endure<small><small>[^42]</small></small> for ever: his name<small><small>[^43]</small></small> shall be continued as long as the sun: and \[men<small><small>[^44]</small></small>\] shall be blessed in him: all nations<small><small>[^45]</small></small> shall call him blessed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Blessed<small><small>[^46]</small></small> \[be\] the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God, the God of Israel, who only<small><small>[^47]</small></small> doeth wondrous things. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And blessed<small><small>[^48]</small></small> \[be\] his glorious name for ever: and let<small><small>[^49]</small></small> the whole earth be filled \[with\] his glory; Amen<small><small>[^50]</small></small>, and Amen. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.72.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The prayers<small><small>[^51]</small></small> of David the son of Jesse are ended. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ps.71|<< Psalm 71]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ps.73|Psalm 73 >>]]</td></tr></table>


[^1]:  [[Ps.72|Ps 72:1]], David, praying for Solomon, shews the goodness and glory of his kingdom, and in type of Christ’s kingdom [[Ps.72|Ps 72:18]], He blesses God.  This Psalm seems to have been composed by David in his last days, when he had set his beloved son on the throne. “Then,” says Calmet, “transported with joy and gratitude, he addressed this Psalm to God, in which he prays Him to pour out His blessings on the young king, and upon the people. He then, wrapped up in a divine enthusiasm, ascends to a higher subject; and sings the glory of the Messiah, and the magnificence of his reign.”
