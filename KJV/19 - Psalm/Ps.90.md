<big><big>[[Ps.90.1 From Everlasting to Everlasting|⥃]]</big></big>***From Everlasting to Everlasting***



A Prayer of Moses the man<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of God.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lord<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, thou hast been our dwelling place in all generations<small><small>[^4]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Before<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the mountains were brought forth, or ever<small><small>[^6]</small></small> thou hadst formed the earth and the world, even from<small><small>[^7]</small></small> everlasting to everlasting, thou<small><small>[^8]</small></small> \[art\] God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou<small><small>[^9]</small></small> turnest man to destruction; and sayest, Return, ye children of men<small><small>[^10]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^11]</small></small> a thousand years in thy sight \[are but\] as yesterday when it is past<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, and \[as<small><small>[^13]</small></small>\] a watch in the night. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou<small><small>[^14]</small></small> carriest them away as with a flood; they are \[as\] a sleep<small><small>[^15]</small></small>: in the morning<small><small>[^16]</small></small> \[they are\] like grass \[which\] groweth up<small><small>[^17]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the morning it flourisheth, and groweth up; in the evening it is cut down, and withereth.<small><small>[^18]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For we<small><small>[^19]</small></small> are consumed by thine anger, and by thy wrath are we<small><small>[^20]</small></small> troubled. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou<small><small>[^21]</small></small> hast set our<small><small>[^22]</small></small> iniquities before thee, our secret \[sins\] in the<small><small>[^23]</small></small> light of thy countenance. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^24]</small></small> all our days are passed<small><small>[^25]</small></small> away in thy wrath: we spend<small><small>[^26]</small></small> our years as a tale<small><small>[^27]</small></small> \[that is told\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The days<small><small>[^28]</small></small> of our years \[are\] threescore years and ten; and if by reason of strength \[they be\] fourscore years, yet<small><small>[^29]</small></small> \[is\] their strength labour and sorrow; for<small><small>[^30]</small></small> it is soon cut off, and we fly away. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Who knoweth the power of thine anger? even according to thy fear, \[so is\] thy wrath.<small><small>[^31]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So<small><small>[^32]</small></small> teach \[us\] to number our days, that<small><small>[^33]</small></small> we may apply \[our\] hearts<small><small>[^34]</small></small> unto wisdom. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Return<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, how<small><small>[^36]</small></small> long? and let it<small><small>[^37]</small></small> repent thee concerning thy servants. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O satisfy<small><small>[^38]</small></small> us early with thy mercy; that we<small><small>[^39]</small></small> may rejoice and be glad all our days. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Make<small><small>[^40]</small></small> us glad according to the days \[wherein\] thou hast afflicted us, \[and\] the years<small><small>[^41]</small></small> \[wherein\] we have seen evil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let<small><small>[^42]</small></small> thy work appear unto thy servants, and<small><small>[^43]</small></small> thy glory unto their children. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.90.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And let<small><small>[^44]</small></small> the beauty of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> our God be upon us: and establish<small><small>[^45]</small></small> thou the work of our hands upon us; [^26]: The Vulgate has, ***Anni nostri sicut aranea mediatabuntur***, "Our years pass away like those of the spider." Our plans and operations are like the spider's web. Life is as frail, and the thread of it as brittle, as one of those which constitute the well-wrought and curious, but fragile habitation of that insect. All the versions have the word spider, but it is not found in any Hebrew manuscripts, or edition yet collated. The Hebrew might be rendered, "We consume our lives with a groan," ***kemo hegeh***.
