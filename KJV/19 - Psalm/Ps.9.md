***I Will Give Thanks to the LORD*** ^bsb1302



To the chief Musician upon Muth–labben, A Psalm of David.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will praise<small><small>[^2]</small></small> \[thee\], O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, with my<small><small>[^3]</small></small> whole heart; I will shew<small><small>[^4]</small></small> forth all thy marvellous works. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will be<small><small>[^5]</small></small> glad and rejoice in thee: I will sing praise to thy name, O thou<small><small>[^6]</small></small> most High. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When mine enemies are turned back, they shall<small><small>[^7]</small></small> fall and perish at thy presence. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^8]</small></small> thou hast maintained<small><small>[^9]</small></small> my right<small><small>[^10]</small></small> and my cause; thou satest in the throne judging right. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou hast rebuked<small><small>[^11]</small></small> the heathen, thou hast destroyed<small><small>[^12]</small></small> the wicked, thou hast put out<small><small>[^13]</small></small> their name for ever and ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O thou<small><small>[^14]</small></small> enemy, destructions<small><small>[^15]</small></small> are come to a perpetual end: and thou hast<small><small>[^16]</small></small> destroyed cities; their memorial<small><small>[^17]</small></small> is perished with them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But<small><small>[^18]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall endure for ever: he hath<small><small>[^19]</small></small> prepared his throne for judgment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he shall judge the world in righteousness, he shall minister judgment to the people in uprightness.<small><small>[^20]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^21]</small></small></span> also will be a refuge<small><small>[^22]</small></small> for the oppressed, a refuge in times<small><small>[^23]</small></small> of trouble. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they that know<small><small>[^24]</small></small> thy name will put<small><small>[^25]</small></small> their trust in thee: for thou, <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, hast<small><small>[^26]</small></small> not forsaken them that seek thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Sing<small><small>[^27]</small></small> praises to the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, which<small><small>[^28]</small></small> dwelleth in Zion: declare<small><small>[^29]</small></small> among the people his doings. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When<small><small>[^30]</small></small> he maketh inquisition for blood, he remembereth them: he forgetteth<small><small>[^31]</small></small> not the cry of the humble<small><small>[^32]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Have<small><small>[^33]</small></small> mercy upon me, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; consider<small><small>[^34]</small></small> my trouble \[which I suffer\] of them that hate me, thou<small><small>[^35]</small></small> that liftest me up from the gates of death: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That<small><small>[^36]</small></small> I may shew forth all thy praise in the gates<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of the daughter<small><small>[^38]</small></small> of Zion: I will<small><small>[^39]</small></small> rejoice in thy salvation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The heathen are sunk down in the pit \[that\] they made: in the net which they hid is their own foot taken.<small><small>[^40]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> is known<small><small>[^41]</small></small> \[by\] the judgment \[which\] he executeth: the wicked<small><small>[^42]</small></small> is snared in the work of his own hands. Higgaion<small><small>[^43]</small></small>. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The wicked<small><small>[^44]</small></small> shall be turned into hell, \[and\] all the nations that forget<small><small>[^45]</small></small> God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the<small><small>[^46]</small></small> needy shall not alway be forgotten: the expectation<small><small>[^47]</small></small> of the poor shall \[not\] perish for ever. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Arise<small><small>[^48]</small></small>, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; let not<small><small>[^49]</small></small> man prevail: let the<small><small>[^50]</small></small> heathen be judged in thy sight. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.9.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Put<small><small>[^51]</small></small> them in fear, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>: \[that\] the nations may<small><small>[^52]</small></small> know themselves \[to be but\] men. [^1]:  [[Ps.9|Ps 9:1]], David praises God for executing judgment; [[Ps.9|Ps 9:11]], He incites others to praise him; [[Ps.9|Ps 9:13]], He prays that he may have cause to praise him.  Probably, “the death of the champion:” so the Chaldee has, “A Psalm of David, to be sung concerning the death of the man who went out between (***mibbeyney*** ) the camps;” evidently considering ***labben*** , of the same import as ***bainayim*** , “a middle-man or champion,” as Goliath is termed, [[1Sam.17|1Sam 17:4]], concerning whose defeat this psalm is generally supposed to have been composed.
