$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Give<small><small>[^2]</small></small> ear to my prayer, O God; and hide<small><small>[^3]</small></small> not thyself from my supplication. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Attend unto me, and hear me: I mourn<small><small>[^4]</small></small> in my complaint, and make a noise; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because of the voice of the enemy, because of the oppression<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of the wicked: for they<small><small>[^6]</small></small> cast iniquity upon me, and in wrath they hate me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My<small><small>[^7]</small></small> heart is sore pained within me: and the terrors<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of death are fallen upon me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Fearfulness<small><small>[^9]</small></small> and trembling are come upon me, and horror<small><small>[^10]</small></small> hath overwhelmed<small><small>[^11]</small></small> me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I said, Oh that I had wings like a dove! \[for then\] would I fly away, and be at rest.<small><small>[^12]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Lo, \[then\] would I wander far off, \[and\] remain in the wilderness. Selah.<small><small>[^13]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I would hasten my escape from the windy storm<small><small>[^14]</small></small> \[and\] tempest. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Destroy, O Lord, \[and\] divide<small><small>[^15]</small></small> their tongues: for I have<small><small>[^16]</small></small> seen violence and strife in the city. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Day<small><small>[^17]</small></small> and night they go about it upon the walls thereof: mischief<small><small>[^18]</small></small> also and sorrow \[are\] in the midst of it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wickedness<small><small>[^19]</small></small> \[is\] in the midst thereof: deceit<small><small>[^20]</small></small> and guile depart not from her streets. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^21]</small></small> \[it was\] not an enemy \[that\] reproached me; and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall save me.<small><small>[^31]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Evening<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, and morning, and at noon, will I pray, and cry<small><small>[^33]</small></small> aloud: and he shall hear my voice. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He hath<small><small>[^34]</small></small> delivered my soul in peace from the battle \[that was\] against me: for there<small><small>[^35]</small></small> were many with me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> God shall hear<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, and afflict them, even<small><small>[^37]</small></small> he that abideth of old. Selah. Because<small><small>[^38]</small></small> they have no changes<small><small>[^39]</small></small>, therefore they fear not God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He hath put<small><small>[^40]</small></small> forth his hands against such as be at peace<small><small>[^41]</small></small> with him: he hath broken<small><small>[^42]</small></small> his covenant. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[The words<small><small>[^43]</small></small>\] of his mouth were smoother than butter, but war<small><small>[^44]</small></small> \[was\] in his heart: his words were softer than oil, yet \[were\] they drawn swords. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Cast<small><small>[^45]</small></small> thy burden<small><small>[^46]</small></small> upon the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and he shall sustain thee: he shall never suffer<small><small>[^47]</small></small> the righteous to be moved. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.55.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But thou, O God<small><small>[^48]</small></small>, shalt bring them down into the pit<small><small>[^49]</small></small> of destruction: bloody and deceitful men shall not live out half their days<small><small>[^50]</small></small>; but I will trust in thee. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ps.54|<< Psalm 54]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ps.56|Psalm 56 >>]]</td></tr></table>
