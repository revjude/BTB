$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.46.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> God \[is\] our refuge<small><small>[^2]</small></small> and strength, a very<small><small>[^3]</small></small> present help in trouble.<small><small>[^4]</small></small><small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.46.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore will<small><small>[^6]</small></small> not we fear, though<small><small>[^7]</small></small> the earth be removed, and though the mountains<small><small>[^8]</small></small> be carried into the midst of the sea<small><small>[^9]</small></small>; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.46.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Though\] the waters<small><small>[^10]</small></small> thereof roar \[and\] be troubled, \[though\] the mountains<small><small>[^11]</small></small> shake with the swelling thereof. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.46.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[There is\] a river<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, the streams whereof shall make glad the city<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of God, the holy<small><small>[^14]</small></small> \[place\] of the tabernacles of the most<small><small>[^15]</small></small> High. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.46.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> God \[is<small><small>[^16]</small></small>\] in the midst of her; she<small><small>[^17]</small></small> shall not be moved: God shall help her, \[and that<small><small>[^18]</small></small>\] right early. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.46.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The heathen<small><small>[^19]</small></small> raged, the kingdoms<small><small>[^20]</small></small> were moved: he uttered his voice, the earth<small><small>[^21]</small></small> melted. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.46.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^22]</small></small></span> of hosts \[is\] with us; the God of Jacob \[is\] our refuge<small><small>[^23]</small></small>. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.46.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Come<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, behold the works of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, what desolations<small><small>[^25]</small></small> he hath made in the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.46.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He maketh<small><small>[^26]</small></small> wars to cease unto the end of the earth; he breaketh<small><small>[^27]</small></small> the bow, and cutteth the spear in sunder; he burneth<small><small>[^28]</small></small> the chariot in the fire. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.46.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Be still<small><small>[^29]</small></small>, and know<small><small>[^30]</small></small> that I \[am\] God: I will be<small><small>[^31]</small></small> exalted among the heathen, I will be exalted in the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.46.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts \[is\] with us; the God<small><small>[^32]</small></small> of Jacob \[is\] our refuge.
