<big><big>[[Ps.80.1 Hear Us, O Shepherd of Israel|⥃]]</big></big>***Hear Us, O Shepherd of Israel***



To the chief Musician upon Shoshannim–Eduth, A Psalm<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of Asaph<small><small>[^3]</small></small>.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Give ear<small><small>[^4]</small></small>, O Shepherd<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of Israel, thou that leadest<small><small>[^6]</small></small> Joseph like a flock; thou that dwellest<small><small>[^7]</small></small> \[between\] the cherubims, shine<small><small>[^8]</small></small> forth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Before<small><small>[^9]</small></small> Ephraim and Benjamin and Manasseh stir up<small><small>[^10]</small></small> thy strength, and come \[and\] save us<small><small>[^11]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Turn us<small><small>[^12]</small></small> again, O God, and cause<small><small>[^13]</small></small> thy face to shine; and we shall be saved. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of hosts, how long<small><small>[^14]</small></small> wilt thou be angry<small><small>[^15]</small></small> against the prayer of thy people? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou feedest them with the bread of tears; and givest them tears to drink in great measure.<small><small>[^16]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou<small><small>[^17]</small></small> makest us a strife unto our neighbours: and our enemies<small><small>[^18]</small></small> laugh among themselves. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Turn<small><small>[^19]</small></small> us again, O God of hosts, and cause thy face to shine; and we shall<small><small>[^20]</small></small> be saved. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou hast brought a vine<small><small>[^21]</small></small> out of Egypt: thou hast cast<small><small>[^22]</small></small> out the heathen, and planted it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou preparedst<small><small>[^23]</small></small> \[room\] before it, and didst cause it to take<small><small>[^24]</small></small> deep root, and it<small><small>[^25]</small></small> filled the land. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The hills were covered with the shadow of it, and the boughs thereof \[were like\] the goodly cedars<small><small>[^26]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> She sent out her boughs unto the sea, and her branches unto the river.<small><small>[^27]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Why hast thou \[then\] broken<small><small>[^28]</small></small> down her hedges, so that all they which pass by the way do pluck her? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The boar<small><small>[^29]</small></small> out of the wood doth waste it, and the wild beast of the field doth devour it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Return<small><small>[^30]</small></small>, we beseech thee, O God of hosts: look down<small><small>[^31]</small></small> from heaven, and behold, and visit this vine; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the vineyard<small><small>[^32]</small></small> which thy right hand hath planted, and the branch<small><small>[^33]</small></small> \[that\] thou madest strong for thyself. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[It is\] burned<small><small>[^34]</small></small> with fire, \[it is\] cut down: they perish<small><small>[^35]</small></small> at the rebuke of thy countenance. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let thy hand be upon the man of thy right hand, upon the son of man \[whom\] thou madest strong for thyself.<small><small>[^36]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So will<small><small>[^37]</small></small> not we go back from thee: quicken<small><small>[^38]</small></small> us, and we will call upon thy name. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.80.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Turn us<small><small>[^39]</small></small> again, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God of hosts, cause<small><small>[^40]</small></small> thy face to shine;
