<big><big>[[Ps.109.1 The Song of the Slandered|⥃]]</big></big>***The Song of the Slandered*** <small><small>[^2]</small></small> 



To the chief Musician, A Psalm of David.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hold<small><small>[^3]</small></small> not thy peace, O God<small><small>[^4]</small></small> of my praise; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the mouth<small><small>[^5]</small></small> of the wicked and the mouth of the deceitful<small><small>[^6]</small></small> are opened<small><small>[^7]</small></small> against me: they have spoken against me with<small><small>[^8]</small></small> a lying tongue. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They compassed<small><small>[^9]</small></small> me about also with words of hatred; and fought<small><small>[^10]</small></small> against me without a cause. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For my<small><small>[^11]</small></small> love they are my adversaries: but I<small><small>[^12]</small></small> \[give myself unto\] prayer. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they<small><small>[^13]</small></small> have rewarded me evil for good, and hatred<small><small>[^14]</small></small> for my love. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Set thou<small><small>[^15]</small></small> a wicked man over him: and let<small><small>[^16]</small></small> Satan<small><small>[^17]</small></small> stand at his right hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When he shall be judged, let him be condemned<small><small>[^18]</small></small>: and let<small><small>[^19]</small></small> his prayer become sin. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let his days<small><small>[^20]</small></small> be few; \[and\] let another<small><small>[^21]</small></small> take his office<small><small>[^22]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let his children be fatherless, and his wife a widow.<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let his children be continually vagabonds, and beg: let them seek \[their bread\] also out of their desolate places.<small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let the extortioner<small><small>[^25]</small></small> catch all that he hath; and let the strangers<small><small>[^26]</small></small> spoil his labour. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let there be none<small><small>[^27]</small></small> to extend mercy unto him: neither let there be any to favour<small><small>[^28]</small></small> his fatherless children. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let his<small><small>[^29]</small></small> posterity be cut off; \[and\] in the generation following let their name<small><small>[^30]</small></small> be blotted out. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let the<small><small>[^31]</small></small> iniquity of his fathers be remembered with the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; and let not<small><small>[^32]</small></small> the sin of his mother be blotted<small><small>[^33]</small></small> out. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let them be before<small><small>[^34]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> continually, that he may cut off<small><small>[^35]</small></small> the memory of them from the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because that he remembered<small><small>[^36]</small></small> not to shew mercy, but persecuted<small><small>[^37]</small></small> the poor and needy man, that he might even slay<small><small>[^38]</small></small> the broken in heart. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As he loved cursing, so let it come unto him: as he delighted not in blessing, so let it be far from him.<small><small>[^39]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> As he<small><small>[^40]</small></small> clothed himself with cursing like as with his garment, so let<small><small>[^41]</small></small> it come into his bowels<small><small>[^42]</small></small> like water, and like oil into his bones. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let it be unto him as the garment<small><small>[^43]</small></small> \[which\] covereth him, and for a girdle wherewith he is girded continually. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Let\] this<small><small>[^44]</small></small> \[be\] the reward of mine adversaries from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and of them<small><small>[^45]</small></small> that speak evil against my soul. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But do<small><small>[^46]</small></small> thou for me, O <span class="smallcaps">God</span> the Lord, for thy name’s sake: because thy mercy<small><small>[^47]</small></small> \[is\] good, deliver thou me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For I<small><small>[^48]</small></small> \[am\] poor and needy, and my<small><small>[^49]</small></small> heart is wounded within me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I am gone<small><small>[^50]</small></small> like the shadow when it declineth: I am tossed<small><small>[^51]</small></small> up and down as the locust. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My knees<small><small>[^52]</small></small> are weak through fasting; and my flesh<small><small>[^53]</small></small> faileth of fatness. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I became also a reproach<small><small>[^54]</small></small> unto them: \[when\] they<small><small>[^55]</small></small> looked upon me they shaked their heads. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Help<small><small>[^56]</small></small> me, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> my God: O save me<small><small>[^57]</small></small> according to thy mercy: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That they may know that this \[is\] thy hand; \[that\] thou, <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, hast done it.<small><small>[^58]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.28|28]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let them<small><small>[^59]</small></small> curse, but bless thou: when they arise, let them be ashamed; but let<small><small>[^60]</small></small> thy servant rejoice. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.29|29]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Let mine adversaries be clothed<small><small>[^61]</small></small> with shame, and let them cover themselves with their own confusion, as with a mantle. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.30|30]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will greatly<small><small>[^62]</small></small> praise the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> with my mouth; yea, I will praise<small><small>[^63]</small></small> him among the multitude. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.109.31|31]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For he<small><small>[^64]</small></small> shall stand at the right hand of the poor<small><small>[^65]</small></small>, to save<small><small>[^66]</small></small> \[him\] from those that condemn<small><small>[^67]</small></small> his soul. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td
