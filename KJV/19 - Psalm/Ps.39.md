***I Will Watch My Ways*** ^bsb1332



To the chief Musician, \[even\] to Jeduthun, A Psalm of David.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.39.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I said<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, I will take heed to my ways, that I<small><small>[^3]</small></small> sin not with my tongue: I will keep my mouth<small><small>[^4]</small></small> with a bridle, while<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the wicked is before me. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.39.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I was<small><small>[^6]</small></small> dumb with silence, I held my peace, \[even<small><small>[^7]</small></small>\] from good; and my sorrow<small><small>[^8]</small></small> was stirred<small><small>[^9]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.39.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My heart was hot within me, while I was musing the fire burned: \[then\] spake I with my tongue,<small><small>[^10]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.39.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, make<small><small>[^11]</small></small> me to know mine end, and the measure of my days, what it \[is\]; \[that\] I may know how frail I \[am<small><small>[^12]</small></small>\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.39.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold<small><small>[^13]</small></small>, thou hast made my days \[as\] an handbreadth; and mine age \[is\] as nothing<small><small>[^14]</small></small> before thee: verily<small><small>[^15]</small></small> every man at his best state<small><small>[^16]</small></small> \[is\] altogether vanity. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.39.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Surely every man walketh in a vain shew<small><small>[^17]</small></small>: surely<small><small>[^18]</small></small> they are disquieted in vain: he heapeth<small><small>[^19]</small></small> up \[riches\], and knoweth not who shall gather them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.39.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And now, Lord, what wait<small><small>[^20]</small></small> I for? my hope<small><small>[^21]</small></small> \[is\] in thee. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.39.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Deliver<small><small>[^22]</small></small> me from all my transgressions: make<small><small>[^23]</small></small> me not the reproach of the foolish. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.39.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I was dumb, I opened not my mouth; because thou didst \[it\].<small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.39.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Remove<small><small>[^25]</small></small> thy stroke away from me: I am consumed<small><small>[^26]</small></small> by the blow<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of thine hand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.39.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When<small><small>[^28]</small></small> thou with rebukes dost correct man for iniquity, thou makest his beauty<small><small>[^29]</small></small> to consume away like a moth: surely<small><small>[^30]</small></small> every man \[is\] vanity. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.39.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear my prayer, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and give ear unto my cry; hold<small><small>[^31]</small></small> not thy peace at my tears: for I \[am<small><small>[^32]</small></small>\] a stranger with thee, \[and\] a sojourner, as all<small><small>[^33]</small></small> my fathers \[were\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.39.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O spare<small><small>[^34]</small></small> me, that I may recover strength, before I go hence, and be no<small><small>[^35]</small></small> more. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ps.38|<< Psalm 38]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ps.40|Psalm 40 >>]]</td></tr></table>

Jeduthun, probably the same as Ethan, [[1Chr.6|1Chr 6:44]], was one of the sons of Merari, and is supposed to have been one the four masters of music, or leaders of bands, belonging to the temple service. It is therefore probable that David, having composed this Psalm, gave it to Jeduthun and his company to sing; and it is very likely, that it was written on the same occasion as the preceding.
