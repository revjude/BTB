<big><big>[[Ps.85.1 You Showed Favor to Your Land|⥃]]</big></big>***You Showed Favor to Your Land***



To the chief Musician, A Psalm for the sons of Korah.


$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.85.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^2]</small></small></span>, thou hast been favourable unto<small><small>[^3]</small></small> thy land: thou hast<small><small>[^4]</small></small> brought back the captivity of Jacob. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.85.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou hast forgiven<small><small>[^5]</small></small> the iniquity of thy people, thou hast covered all their sin. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.85.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou hast taken<small><small>[^6]</small></small> away all thy wrath: thou hast turned<small><small>[^7]</small></small> \[thyself\] from the fierceness of thine anger. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.85.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Turn us<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, O God<small><small>[^9]</small></small> of our salvation, and cause<small><small>[^10]</small></small> thine anger toward us to cease. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.85.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wilt thou be angry<small><small>[^11]</small></small> with us for ever? wilt thou draw<small><small>[^12]</small></small> out thine anger to all generations? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.85.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Wilt thou not revive<small><small>[^13]</small></small> us again: that thy people<small><small>[^14]</small></small> may rejoice in thee? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.85.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Shew us thy mercy, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and grant us thy salvation.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.85.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will hear<small><small>[^16]</small></small> what God the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> will speak: for he<small><small>[^17]</small></small> will speak peace unto his<small><small>[^18]</small></small> people, and to his saints: but<small><small>[^19]</small></small> let them not turn again to folly<small><small>[^20]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.85.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Surely<small><small>[^21]</small></small> his salvation \[is\] nigh them that fear him; that glory<small><small>[^22]</small></small> may dwell in our land. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.85.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Mercy<small><small>[^23]</small></small> and truth are met together; righteousness<small><small>[^24]</small></small> and peace have kissed \[each other\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.85.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Truth<small><small>[^25]</small></small> shall spring out of the earth; and righteousness<small><small>[^26]</small></small> shall look down from heaven. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.85.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea, the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^27]</small></small></span> shall give \[that which is\] good; and our land<small><small>[^28]</small></small> shall yield her increase. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.85.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Righteousness<small><small>[^29]</small></small> shall go before him; and shall set<small><small>[^30]</small></small> \[us\] in the way of his steps. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ps.84|<< Psalm 84]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ps.86|Psalm 86 >>]]</td></tr></table>
