$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.92.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[It is a\] good<small><small>[^3]</small></small> \[thing\] to give thanks unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and to sing praises unto thy name, O most<small><small>[^4]</small></small> High: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.92.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To shew<small><small>[^5]</small></small> forth thy lovingkindness in the morning, and thy faithfulness every night<small><small>[^6]</small></small>, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.92.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Upon an instrument<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of ten strings, and upon the psaltery; neither doth a fool<small><small>[^15]</small></small> understand this. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.92.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When the wicked<small><small>[^16]</small></small> spring as the grass, and when all the workers<small><small>[^17]</small></small> of iniquity do flourish; \[it is\] that<small><small>[^18]</small></small> they shall be destroyed for ever: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.92.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But thou, <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, \[art most<small><small>[^19]</small></small>\] high for evermore. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.92.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, lo, thine enemies, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, for, lo, thine enemies shall perish; all the workers of iniquity shall be scattered<small><small>[^21]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.92.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But<small><small>[^22]</small></small> my horn shalt thou exalt like \[the horn of\] an unicorn<small><small>[^23]</small></small>: I shall<small><small>[^24]</small></small> be anointed with fresh oil. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.92.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Mine eye also shall see \[my desire\] on mine enemies, \[and\] mine ears shall hear \[my desire\] of the wicked that rise up against me.<small><small>[^25]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.92.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The righteous<small><small>[^26]</small></small> shall flourish like the palm tree: he shall grow like a cedar<small><small>[^27]</small></small> in Lebanon. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.92.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Those<small><small>[^28]</small></small> that be planted in the house of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall flourish<small><small>[^29]</small></small> in the<small><small>[^30]</small></small> courts of our God. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.92.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They<small><small>[^31]</small></small> shall still bring forth fruit in old age<small><small>[^32]</small></small>; they shall be fat and flourishing<small><small>[^33]</small></small>; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Ps.92.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> To shew<small><small>[^34]</small></small> that the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> \[is\] upright: \[he is\] my rock<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, and<small><small>[^36]</small></small> \[there is\] no unrighteousness in him. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Ps.91|<< Psalm 91]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Ps.93|Psalm 93 >>]]</td></tr></table>
