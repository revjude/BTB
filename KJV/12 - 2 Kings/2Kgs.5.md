<big><big>[[2Kgs.5.1 Naaman Cured of Leprosy|⥃]]</big></big>***Naaman Cured of Leprosy***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Luke.17|Luke 17:11–19]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now Naaman<small><small>[^2]</small></small>, captain of the host of the king of Syria, was a great<small><small>[^3]</small></small> man with<small><small>[^4]</small></small> his master, and honourable<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, because by him<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had given deliverance<small><small>[^7]</small></small> unto Syria: he was also a mighty man in valour, \[but he was\] a leper<small><small>[^8]</small></small>.<small><small>[^9]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the Syrians had gone out by companies<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, and had brought away captive out of the land of Israel a little maid; and she waited on<small><small>[^11]</small></small> Naaman’s wife. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And she said unto her mistress, Would God<small><small>[^12]</small></small> my lord \[were\] with<small><small>[^13]</small></small> the prophet that \[is\] in Samaria! for he would<small><small>[^14]</small></small> recover him of<small><small>[^15]</small></small> his leprosy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And \[one\] went in, and told his lord<small><small>[^16]</small></small>, saying, Thus and thus said the maid that \[is\] of the land of Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the king of Syria said, Go<small><small>[^17]</small></small> to, go, and I will send a letter unto the king of Israel. And he departed, and took<small><small>[^18]</small></small> with him<small><small>[^19]</small></small> ten talents of silver<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, and six thousand<small><small>[^21]</small></small> \[pieces\] of gold, and ten changes<small><small>[^22]</small></small> of raiment. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he brought the letter to the king of Israel, saying, Now when this letter is come unto thee, behold, I have \[therewith\] sent Naaman my servant to thee, that thou mayest recover him of his leprosy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, when the king of Israel had read the letter, that he rent<small><small>[^23]</small></small> his clothes, and said, \[Am\] I God<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, to kill and to make alive, that this man doth send unto me to recover a man of his leprosy? wherefore consider, I pray you, and see how<small><small>[^25]</small></small> he seeketh a quarrel against me. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And it was \[so\], when Elisha the man of God had heard that the king of Israel had rent his clothes<small><small>[^26]</small></small>, that he sent to the king, saying, Wherefore hast thou rent thy clothes? let him come<small><small>[^27]</small></small> now to me, and he shall<small><small>[^28]</small></small> know that there is a prophet in Israel. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So Naaman came with his horses and with his chariot, and stood at the door of the house of Elisha.<small><small>[^29]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Elisha sent a messenger<small><small>[^30]</small></small> unto him, saying, Go and wash<small><small>[^31]</small></small> in Jordan seven times<small><small>[^32]</small></small>, and thy flesh<small><small>[^33]</small></small> shall come again to thee, and thou shalt be clean. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But Naaman<small><small>[^34]</small></small> was wroth, and went away<small><small>[^35]</small></small>, and said, Behold<small><small>[^36]</small></small>, I thought<small><small>[^37]</small></small>, He will surely come out to me, and stand, and call on the name of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> his God, and strike his hand over the place, and recover the leper. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Are\] not Abana and Pharpar<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, rivers of Damascus, better<small><small>[^39]</small></small> than all the waters of Israel? may I not wash in them, and be clean? And he said, Thy servant<small><small>[^79]</small></small> went no whither<small><small>[^80]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.26|26]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said<small><small>[^81]</small></small> unto him, Went<small><small>[^82]</small></small> not mine heart \[with thee\], when the man turned again from his chariot to meet thee? \[Is it\] a time<small><small>[^83]</small></small> to receive money, and to receive garments, and oliveyards, and vineyards, and sheep, and oxen, and menservants, and maidservants? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.5.27|27]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The leprosy<small><small>[^84]</small></small> therefore of Naaman shall cleave unto thee, and unto thy seed<small><small>[^85]</small></small> for ever.
