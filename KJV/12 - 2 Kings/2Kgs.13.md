<big><big>[[2Kgs.13.1 Jehoahaz Reigns in Israel|⥃]]</big></big>***Jehoahaz Reigns in Israel***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the three and twentieth year<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of Joash the son of Ahaziah king of Judah Jehoahaz<small><small>[^3]</small></small> the son of Jehu began to reign over Israel in Samaria, \[and reigned\] seventeen years. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he did \[that which was\] evil in the sight of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and followed<small><small>[^4]</small></small> the sins of Jeroboam the son of Nebat, which made Israel to sin; he departed not therefrom.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And the anger of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> was kindled against Israel, and he delivered<small><small>[^6]</small></small> them into the hand of Hazael<small><small>[^7]</small></small> king of Syria, and into the hand of Ben–hadad<small><small>[^8]</small></small> the son of Hazael, all \[their\] days<small><small>[^9]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jehoahaz<small><small>[^10]</small></small> besought the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^11]</small></small></span>, and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hearkened unto him: for he saw<small><small>[^12]</small></small> the oppression of Israel, because the king<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of Syria oppressed them. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> (And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> gave Israel a saviour<small><small>[^14]</small></small>, so that they went out from under the hand of the Syrians: and the children of Israel dwelt in their tents, as beforetime<small><small>[^15]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nevertheless they departed<small><small>[^16]</small></small> not from the sins of the house of Jeroboam, who made Israel sin, \[but\] walked<small><small>[^17]</small></small> therein: and there remained<small><small>[^18]</small></small> the grove also in Samaria.) <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Neither did he leave of the people to Jehoahaz but fifty horsemen<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, and ten chariots, and ten thousand footmen; for the king<small><small>[^20]</small></small> of Syria had destroyed them, and had made them like the dust<small><small>[^21]</small></small> by threshing. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Now the rest<small><small>[^22]</small></small> of the acts of Jehoahaz, and all that he did, and his might, \[are\] they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel?<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jehoahaz slept with his fathers; and they buried him<small><small>[^24]</small></small> in Samaria: and Joash<small><small>[^25]</small></small> his son reigned in his stead<small><small>[^26]</small></small>.<small><small>[^27]</small></small>

<big><big>[[2Kgs.13.10 Jehoash Reigns in Israel|⥃]]</big></big>***Jehoash Reigns in Israel***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> In the thirty<small><small>[^28]</small></small> and seventh year of Joash king of Judah began Jehoash<small><small>[^29]</small></small> the son of Jehoahaz to reign over Israel in Samaria, \[and reigned\] sixteen years. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he did \[that which was\] evil in the sight of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; he departed<small><small>[^30]</small></small> not from all the sins of Jeroboam the son of Nebat, who made Israel sin: \[but\] he walked therein. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the rest<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of the acts of Joash, and all that he did, and his might<small><small>[^32]</small></small> wherewith he fought against Amaziah king of Judah, \[are\] they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel?<small><small>[^33]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Joash slept<small><small>[^34]</small></small> with his fathers; and Jeroboam<small><small>[^35]</small></small> sat upon his throne: and Joash was buried<small><small>[^36]</small></small> in Samaria with the kings of Israel.

<big><big>[[2Kgs.13.14 Elisha’s Final Prophecy|⥃]]</big></big>***Elisha’s Final Prophecy***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Now Elisha was fallen sick<small><small>[^37]</small></small> of his sickness whereof he died<small><small>[^38]</small></small>. And Joash the king of Israel came down unto him, and wept over his face, and said, O my father<small><small>[^39]</small></small>, my father, the chariot of Israel, and the horsemen thereof.<small><small>[^40]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Elisha said unto him, Take bow and arrows. And he took unto him bow and arrows. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said to the king of Israel, Put thine hand<small><small>[^41]</small></small> upon the bow. And he put his hand \[upon it\]: and Elisha<small><small>[^42]</small></small> put his hands upon the king’s hands. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, Open<small><small>[^43]</small></small> the window eastward. And he opened \[it\]. Then Elisha said, Shoot. And he shot. And he said, The arrow<small><small>[^44]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> deliverance, and the arrow of deliverance from Syria: for thou shalt smite the Syrians in Aphek<small><small>[^45]</small></small>, till thou have consumed \[them\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, Take the arrows. And he took \[them\]. And he said unto the king of Israel, Smite<small><small>[^46]</small></small> upon the ground. And he smote thrice<small><small>[^47]</small></small>, and stayed. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the man of God<small><small>[^48]</small></small> was wroth<small><small>[^49]</small></small> with him, and said, Thou shouldest have smitten five or six times; then hadst thou smitten Syria till thou hadst consumed \[it\]: whereas now thou shalt<small><small>[^50]</small></small> smite Syria \[but\] thrice. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Elisha died, and they buried him<small><small>[^51]</small></small>. And the bands<small><small>[^52]</small></small> of the Moabites<small><small>[^53]</small></small> invaded the land at the coming in of the year.<small><small>[^54]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, as they were burying a man, that, behold, they spied a band \[of men\]; and they cast the man into the sepulchre of Elisha: and when the man was let down<small><small>[^55]</small></small>, and touched<small><small>[^56]</small></small> the bones of Elisha, he revived, and stood up on his feet. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]But Hazael<small><small>[^57]</small></small> king of Syria oppressed Israel all the days of Jehoahaz.<small><small>[^58]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^59]</small></small></span> was gracious unto them, and had compassion on them, and had respect<small><small>[^60]</small></small> unto them, because of his covenant<small><small>[^61]</small></small> with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, and would not destroy them, neither cast he<small><small>[^62]</small></small> them from his presence<small><small>[^63]</small></small> as yet. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So Hazael<small><small>[^64]</small></small> king of Syria died; and Ben–hadad his son reigned in his stead. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Kgs.13.25|25]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Jehoash the son of Jehoahaz took again<small><small>[^65]</small></small> out of the hand of Ben–hadad the son of Hazael the cities, which he had taken out of the hand of Jehoahaz his father by war. Three times did Joash beat him, and recovered the cities of Israel. <small><small>[^66]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[2Kgs.12|<< 2 Kings 12]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[2Kgs.14|2 Kings 14 >>]]</td></tr></table>
