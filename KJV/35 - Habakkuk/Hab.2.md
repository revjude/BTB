<big><big>[[Hab.2.1 The LORD Answers Again|⥃]]</big></big>***The LORD Answers Again***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I will stand<small><small>[^2]</small></small> upon my watch, and set me upon the tower<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, and will<small><small>[^4]</small></small> watch to see what he will say unto me<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, and what I shall answer when I am reproved<small><small>[^6]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> answered me, and said, Write<small><small>[^7]</small></small> the vision, and make<small><small>[^8]</small></small> \[it\] plain upon tables, that he may run that readeth it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the vision<small><small>[^9]</small></small> \[is\] yet for an appointed time, but<small><small>[^10]</small></small> at the end it shall speak, and not lie: though it tarry, wait<small><small>[^11]</small></small> for it; because it will surely<small><small>[^12]</small></small> come, it will not tarry. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, his<small><small>[^13]</small></small> soul \[which\] is lifted up is not upright in him: but<small><small>[^14]</small></small> the just shall live by his faith. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yea also<small><small>[^15]</small></small>, because he transgresseth<small><small>[^16]</small></small> by wine, \[he is\] a proud man<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, neither keepeth<small><small>[^18]</small></small> at home, who<small><small>[^19]</small></small> enlargeth his desire as hell<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, and \[is\] as death, and cannot be satisfied, but gathereth<small><small>[^21]</small></small> unto him all nations, and heapeth unto him all people:

<big><big>[[Hab.2.6 Woe to the Chaldeans|⥃]]</big></big>***Woe to the Chaldeans***

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Shall not all these take<small><small>[^22]</small></small> up a parable against him, and a taunting proverb against him, and say, Woe to him<small><small>[^23]</small></small> that increaseth<small><small>[^24]</small></small> \[that which is\] not his! how<small><small>[^25]</small></small> long? and to him that ladeth<small><small>[^26]</small></small> himself with thick clay! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Shall they<small><small>[^27]</small></small> not rise up suddenly that shall bite<small><small>[^28]</small></small> thee, and awake that shall vex thee, and thou shalt be for booties unto them? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Because thou<small><small>[^29]</small></small> hast spoiled many nations, all the remnant of the people shall spoil thee; because of men’s blood<small><small>[^30]</small></small>, and \[for\] the violence<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of the land, of the city, and of all that dwell therein. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Woe to him that coveteth an evil covetousness<small><small>[^32]</small></small> to his house, that he may set<small><small>[^33]</small></small> his nest on high, that he may be delivered from the power of evil<small><small>[^34]</small></small>! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou hast consulted<small><small>[^35]</small></small> shame to thy house by cutting off many people, and hast sinned<small><small>[^36]</small></small> \[against\] thy soul. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the stone<small><small>[^37]</small></small> shall cry out of the wall, and the beam<small><small>[^38]</small></small> out of the timber shall answer it<small><small>[^39]</small></small>. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Woe to him<small><small>[^40]</small></small> that buildeth a town with blood<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, and stablisheth a city by iniquity! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, \[is it<small><small>[^42]</small></small>\] not of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts that the people shall labour in the very fire, and the people shall weary themselves for very vanity<small><small>[^43]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the earth<small><small>[^44]</small></small> shall be filled with the knowledge of the glory<small><small>[^45]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, as the waters cover the sea. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Woe unto<small><small>[^46]</small></small> him that giveth his neighbour drink, that puttest<small><small>[^47]</small></small> thy bottle to \[him\], and makest \[him\] drunken also, that thou<small><small>[^48]</small></small> mayest look on their nakedness! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou art filled with shame for glory<small><small>[^49]</small></small>: drink<small><small>[^50]</small></small> thou also, and let<small><small>[^51]</small></small> thy foreskin be uncovered: the cup<small><small>[^52]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord’s</span> right hand shall be turned unto thee, and shameful<small><small>[^53]</small></small> spewing \[shall be\] on thy glory. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the violence<small><small>[^54]</small></small> of Lebanon shall cover thee, and the spoil of beasts, \[which\] made them afraid, because<small><small>[^55]</small></small> of men’s blood, and for the violence of the land, of the city<small><small>[^56]</small></small>, and of all that dwell therein. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]What profiteth<small><small>[^57]</small></small> the graven image that the maker thereof hath graven it; the molten image, and a teacher<small><small>[^58]</small></small> of lies, that the<small><small>[^59]</small></small> maker of his work<small><small>[^60]</small></small> trusteth therein, to make dumb<small><small>[^61]</small></small> idols? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Woe unto him that<small><small>[^62]</small></small> saith to the wood, Awake; to the dumb stone, Arise, it shall teach! Behold, it \[is<small><small>[^63]</small></small>\] laid over with gold and silver, and \[there<small><small>[^64]</small></small> is\] no breath at all in the midst of it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.2.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^65]</small></small></span> \[is\] in his holy temple: let all the earth keep silence before him<small><small>[^66]</small></small>. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Hab.1|<< Habakkuk 1]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Hab.3|Habakkuk 3 >>]]</td></tr></table>
