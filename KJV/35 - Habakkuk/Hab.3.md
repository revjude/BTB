<big><big>[[Hab.3.1 Habakkuk’s Prayer|⥃]]</big></big>***Habakkuk’s Prayer***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> A prayer<small><small>[^2]</small></small> of Habakkuk the prophet upon Shigionoth<small><small>[^3]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, I have<small><small>[^4]</small></small> heard thy speech<small><small>[^5]</small></small>, \[and\] was afraid: O <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^6]</small></small></span>, revive<small><small>[^7]</small></small> thy work in the<small><small>[^8]</small></small> midst of the years, in the midst of the years make known; in wrath<small><small>[^9]</small></small> remember mercy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> God came<small><small>[^10]</small></small> from<small><small>[^11]</small></small> Teman<small><small>[^12]</small></small>, and the Holy One from mount Paran<small><small>[^13]</small></small>. Selah<small><small>[^14]</small></small>. His glory<small><small>[^15]</small></small> covered the heavens, and the earth<small><small>[^16]</small></small> was full of his praise. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And \[his\] brightness<small><small>[^17]</small></small> was as the light; he had horns \[coming\] out of his hand<small><small>[^18]</small></small>: and there \[was\] the hiding<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of his power. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Before him went<small><small>[^20]</small></small> the pestilence, and<small><small>[^21]</small></small> burning coals<small><small>[^22]</small></small> went forth at his feet. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> He stood, and measured<small><small>[^23]</small></small> the earth: he beheld, and drove<small><small>[^24]</small></small> asunder the nations; and the everlasting<small><small>[^25]</small></small> mountains were scattered, the perpetual hills did bow: his<small><small>[^26]</small></small> ways \[are\] everlasting. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I saw the<small><small>[^27]</small></small> tents of Cushan<small><small>[^28]</small></small> in affliction<small><small>[^29]</small></small>: \[and\] the curtains of the land of Midian<small><small>[^30]</small></small> did tremble. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Was the <span class="smallcaps">Lord<small><small>[^31]</small></small></span> displeased against the rivers? \[was\] thine anger against the rivers? \[was\] thy wrath against the sea, that thou didst ride<small><small>[^32]</small></small> upon thine horses \[and\] thy chariots of salvation<small><small>[^33]</small></small>? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thy bow<small><small>[^34]</small></small> was made quite naked, \[according<small><small>[^35]</small></small>\] to the oaths of the tribes, \[even thy\] word. Selah<small><small>[^36]</small></small>. Thou<small><small>[^37]</small></small> didst cleave the earth with rivers<small><small>[^38]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The mountains<small><small>[^39]</small></small> saw thee, \[and\] they trembled: the overflowing<small><small>[^40]</small></small> of the water passed by: the deep<small><small>[^41]</small></small> uttered his voice, \[and\] lifted up his hands on high. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The sun<small><small>[^42]</small></small> \[and\] moon stood still in their habitation<small><small>[^43]</small></small>: at the light of thine arrows they went<small><small>[^44]</small></small>, \[and\] at the shining of thy glittering spear. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou didst march<small><small>[^45]</small></small> through the land in indignation, thou didst thresh<small><small>[^46]</small></small> the heathen in anger. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou wentest<small><small>[^47]</small></small> forth for the salvation of thy people, \[even\] for salvation with<small><small>[^48]</small></small> thine anointed; thou woundedst<small><small>[^49]</small></small> the head out of the house of the wicked, by discovering<small><small>[^50]</small></small> the foundation unto the neck. Selah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou didst strike through with his staves the head<small><small>[^51]</small></small> of his villages: they<small><small>[^52]</small></small> came out<small><small>[^53]</small></small> as a whirlwind to scatter me: their<small><small>[^54]</small></small> rejoicing \[was\] as to devour the poor secretly. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Thou didst walk<small><small>[^55]</small></small> through the sea with thine horses, \[through\] the heap<small><small>[^56]</small></small> of great waters. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When I heard<small><small>[^57]</small></small>, my belly<small><small>[^58]</small></small> trembled; my lips quivered at the voice: rottenness entered into my bones, and I trembled in myself, that I<small><small>[^59]</small></small> might rest in the day of trouble: when he cometh up unto the people, he will<small><small>[^60]</small></small> invade them<small><small>[^61]</small></small> with his troops.

<big><big>[[Hab.3.17 Habakkuk Rejoices|⥃]]</big></big>***Habakkuk Rejoices***

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Although the fig tree<small><small>[^62]</small></small> shall not blossom, neither \[shall\] fruit \[be\] in the vines; the labour of the olive shall fail<small><small>[^63]</small></small>, and the fields shall yield no meat; the flock shall be cut off from the fold, and \[there shall be\] no herd in the stalls: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Yet I will rejoice<small><small>[^64]</small></small> in the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, I will joy in the God<small><small>[^65]</small></small> of my salvation. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.3.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> God \[is\] my strength<small><small>[^66]</small></small>, and he will make my feet like<small><small>[^67]</small></small> hinds’ \[feet\], and he will make me to walk<small><small>[^68]</small></small> upon mine high places.
