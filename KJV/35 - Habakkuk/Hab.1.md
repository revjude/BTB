***Habakkuk’s First Complaint*** ^bsb2039

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The burden which Habakkuk the prophet did see.<small><small>[^2]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, how<small><small>[^3]</small></small> long shall I cry, and thou wilt not hear! \[even\] cry out unto thee \[of\] violence, and thou wilt not save<small><small>[^4]</small></small>! <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Why dost thou shew me iniquity, and cause \[me\] to behold grievance? for spoiling and violence \[are\] before me: and there are \[that\] raise up strife and contention.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore the law<small><small>[^6]</small></small> is slacked, and judgment doth never go for<small><small>[^7]</small></small>th: for the wicked doth compass about the righteous; therefore wrong<small><small>[^8]</small></small> judgment proceedeth.

***The LORD’s Answer*** ^bsb2040

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold ye among<small><small>[^9]</small></small> the heathen, and regard<small><small>[^10]</small></small>, and wonder marvellously: for<small><small>[^11]</small></small> \[I\] will work a work in your days, \[which\] ye will not believe, though it be told \[you\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For, lo, I raise<small><small>[^12]</small></small> up the Chaldeans, \[that\] bitter and hasty nation, which shall march through the breadth<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of the land, to possess the dwellingplaces \[that are\] not theirs. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They \[are\] terrible and dreadful: their judgment<small><small>[^14]</small></small> and their dignity shall proceed of themselves. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Their horses<small><small>[^15]</small></small> also are swifter than the leopards, and are more fierce<small><small>[^16]</small></small> than the evening<small><small>[^17]</small></small> wolves: and their horsemen shall spread themselves, and their horsemen shall come from far; they<small><small>[^18]</small></small> shall fly as the eagle \[that\] hasteth to eat. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They shall come all for<small><small>[^19]</small></small> violence: their faces shall sup up \[as\] the east<small><small>[^20]</small></small> wind, and they shall gather<small><small>[^21]</small></small> the captivity as the sand. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they shall scoff<small><small>[^22]</small></small> at the kings, and the princes shall be a scorn unto them: they shall deride<small><small>[^23]</small></small> every strong hold; for they shall heap dust, and take it. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then shall \[his<small><small>[^24]</small></small>\] mind change, and he shall pass over, and offend, \[imputing<small><small>[^25]</small></small>\] this his power unto his god.

***Habakkuk’s Second Complaint***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Ps.11|Psalms 11:1–7]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb2041

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Art\] thou not<small><small>[^26]</small></small> from everlasting, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> my God, mine<small><small>[^27]</small></small> Holy One? we<small><small>[^28]</small></small> shall not die. O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, thou hast ordained<small><small>[^29]</small></small> them for judgment; and, O mighty God<small><small>[^30]</small></small>, thou hast established<small><small>[^31]</small></small> them for<small><small>[^32]</small></small> correction. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> \[Thou art\] of<small><small>[^33]</small></small> purer eyes than to behold evil, and canst not look on iniquity<small><small>[^34]</small></small>: wherefore<small><small>[^35]</small></small> lookest thou upon them that deal<small><small>[^36]</small></small> treacherously, \[and\] holdest<small><small>[^37]</small></small> thy tongue when the wicked<small><small>[^38]</small></small> devoureth \[the man that is\] more righteous than he? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And makest men as the fishes of the sea, as the creeping<small><small>[^39]</small></small> things, \[that have\] no<small><small>[^40]</small></small> ruler over them? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> They take<small><small>[^41]</small></small> up all of them with the angle, they catch<small><small>[^42]</small></small> them in their net, and gather them in their drag<small><small>[^43]</small></small>: therefore<small><small>[^44]</small></small> they rejoice and are glad. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore they<small><small>[^45]</small></small> sacrifice unto their net, and burn incense unto their drag; because by them their portion \[is\] fat, and their meat plenteous<small><small>[^46]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Hab.1.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Shall they therefore empty their net, and<small><small>[^47]</small></small> not spare continually to slay the nations?
