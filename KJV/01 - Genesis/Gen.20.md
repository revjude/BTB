<big><big>[[Gen.20.1 Abraham, Sarah, and Abimelech|⥃]]</big></big>***Abraham, Sarah, and Abimelech***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abraham journeyed from<small><small>[^2]</small></small> thence toward the south country, and dwelled between Kadesh<small><small>[^3]</small></small> and Shur, and sojourned in Gerar<small><small>[^4]</small></small>.<small><small>[^5]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abraham said<small><small>[^6]</small></small> of Sarah his wife, She \[is\] my sister: and Abimelech<small><small>[^7]</small></small> king of Gerar sent, and took Sarah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But God came to Abimelech in a dream<small><small>[^8]</small></small> by night, and said to him, Behold, thou \[art but\] a dead<small><small>[^9]</small></small> man, for the woman which thou hast taken; for she \[is\] a man’s wife<small><small>[^10]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But Abimelech had<small><small>[^11]</small></small> not come near her: and he said, Lord, wilt<small><small>[^12]</small></small> thou slay also a righteous nation? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Said he not unto me, She \[is\] my sister? and she, even she herself said, He \[is\] my brother: in the integrity<small><small>[^13]</small></small> of my heart and innocency<small><small>[^14]</small></small> of my hands have I done this. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And God said unto him in a dream, Yea, I know that thou didst this in the integrity of thy heart; for I also withheld<small><small>[^15]</small></small> thee from sinning<small><small>[^16]</small></small> against me: therefore suffered I thee not to touch<small><small>[^17]</small></small> her. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Now therefore restore the man \[his\] wife; for he \[is\] a prophet<small><small>[^18]</small></small>, and he shall pray<small><small>[^19]</small></small> for thee, and thou shalt live: and if thou restore \[her\] not, know thou that thou shalt surely<small><small>[^20]</small></small> die, thou, and all<small><small>[^21]</small></small> that \[are\] thine. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore Abimelech rose early in the morning, and called all his servants, and told all these things in their ears: and the men were sore afraid. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then Abimelech called Abraham, and said unto him, What hast<small><small>[^22]</small></small> thou done unto us? and what have I offended thee, that thou hast brought on me and on my kingdom a great<small><small>[^23]</small></small> sin? thou hast done deeds unto me that ought<small><small>[^24]</small></small> not to be done. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abimelech said unto Abraham, What sawest thou, that thou hast done this thing? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abraham said, Because I thought, Surely<small><small>[^25]</small></small> the fear of God \[is\] not in this place; and they will slay<small><small>[^26]</small></small> me for my wife’s sake. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And yet<small><small>[^27]</small></small> indeed \[she is\] my sister; she \[is\] the<small><small>[^28]</small></small> daughter of my father, but not the daughter of my mother; and she became my wife. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And it came to pass, when God<small><small>[^29]</small></small> caused me to wander from my father’s house, that I said unto her, This<small><small>[^30]</small></small> \[is\] thy kindness which thou shalt shew unto me; at every place whither we shall come, say<small><small>[^31]</small></small> of me, He \[is\] my brother. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abimelech took<small><small>[^32]</small></small> sheep, and oxen, and menservants, and womenservants, and gave \[them\] unto Abraham, and restored<small><small>[^33]</small></small> him Sarah his wife. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abimelech said, Behold, my land<small><small>[^34]</small></small> \[is\] before thee: dwell where it pleaseth thee<small><small>[^35]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And unto Sarah he said, Behold, I have given thy<small><small>[^36]</small></small> brother a thousand<small><small>[^37]</small></small> \[pieces\] of silver: behold<small><small>[^38]</small></small>, he \[is\] to thee a covering<small><small>[^39]</small></small> of the eyes, unto all that \[are\] with thee, and with all \[other\]: thus<small><small>[^40]</small></small> she was reproved. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]So Abraham prayed unto God: and God healed Abimelech, and his wife, and his maidservants; and they bare \[children\].<small><small>[^41]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.20.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had fast closed up all the wombs of the house of Abimelech, because of Sarah Abraham’s wife. <small><small>[^42]</small></small><table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Gen.19|<< Genesis 19]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Gen.21|Genesis 21 >>]]</td></tr></table>

[^4]: Gerar was a city of Arabia Petrea, under a king of the Philistines, 25 miles from Eleutheropolis beyond Daroma, in the south of Judah. From [[Gen.10|Gen 10:19]], it appears to have been situated in the angle where the south and west sides of Canaan met, and to have been not far from Gaza. Jerome, in his Hebrew Traditions on Genesis, says, from Gerar to Jerusalem was three days' journey. There was a wood near Gerar, spoken of by Theodoret; and a brook ([[Gen.26|Gen 26:26]]), on which was a monastery, noticed by Sozomen.
