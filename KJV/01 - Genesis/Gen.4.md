***Cain and Abel***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Heb.11|Hebrews 11:4]] ]</sup></small> ^bsb15

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Adam knew<small><small>[^2]</small></small> Eve his wife; and she conceived, and bare Cain<small><small>[^3]</small></small>, and said, I have<small><small>[^4]</small></small> gotten a man from the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And she again bare his brother Abel<small><small>[^5]</small></small>. And Abel<small><small>[^6]</small></small> was a keeper<small><small>[^7]</small></small> of sheep, but Cain was a tiller<small><small>[^8]</small></small> of the ground. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And in process of time<small><small>[^9]</small></small> it came to pass, that Cain brought of the fruit<small><small>[^10]</small></small> of the ground an offering unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>.<small><small>[^11]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abel, he also brought of the firstlings<small><small>[^12]</small></small> of his flock<small><small>[^13]</small></small> and of the fat<small><small>[^14]</small></small> thereof. And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> had<small><small>[^15]</small></small> respect unto Abel and to his offering: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But<small><small>[^16]</small></small> unto Cain and to his offering he had not respect. And Cain was very wroth<small><small>[^17]</small></small>, and his countenance fell. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto Cain, Why art thou wroth? and why is thy countenance fallen?<small><small>[^18]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If thou doest well<small><small>[^19]</small></small>, shalt thou not be accepted<small><small>[^20]</small></small>? and if thou doest not well, sin<small><small>[^21]</small></small> lieth at the door. And unto thee<small><small>[^22]</small></small> \[shall be\] his desire, and thou shalt rule over him. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Cain talked<small><small>[^23]</small></small> with Abel his brother: and it came to pass, when they were in the field, that Cain rose<small><small>[^24]</small></small> up against Abel his brother, and slew him. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto Cain, Where \[is<small><small>[^25]</small></small>\] Abel thy brother? And he said, I know<small><small>[^26]</small></small> not: \[Am\] I my brother’s keeper? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he said, What<small><small>[^27]</small></small> hast thou done? the voice of thy brother’s blood<small><small>[^28]</small></small> crieth<small><small>[^29]</small></small> unto me from the ground. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And now \[art\] thou cursed from the earth, which hath opened<small><small>[^30]</small></small> her mouth to receive thy brother’s blood from thy hand;<small><small>[^31]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> When thou tillest the ground, it<small><small>[^32]</small></small> shall not henceforth yield unto thee her strength; a fugitive<small><small>[^33]</small></small> and a vagabond shalt thou be in the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Cain said unto the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, My punishment \[is\] greater than I can bear.<small><small>[^34]</small></small><small><small>[^35]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Behold, thou hast driven<small><small>[^36]</small></small> me out this day from the face of the earth; and from thy<small><small>[^37]</small></small> face shall I be hid; and I shall be a fugitive<small><small>[^38]</small></small> and a vagabond in the earth; and it shall come to pass, \[that<small><small>[^39]</small></small>\] every one that findeth me shall slay me.<small><small>[^40]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> said unto him, Therefore<small><small>[^41]</small></small> whosoever slayeth Cain, vengeance shall be taken on him sevenfold<small><small>[^42]</small></small>. And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> set a mark<small><small>[^43]</small></small> upon Cain, lest any finding him should kill him. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Cain went<small><small>[^44]</small></small> out from the presence of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>, and dwelt in the land of Nod<small><small>[^45]</small></small>, on the east of Eden.

***The Descendants of Cain*** ^bsb16

 <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Cain knew his wife; and she conceived, and bare Enoch<small><small>[^46]</small></small>: and he<small><small>[^47]</small></small> builded a city, and called the name<small><small>[^48]</small></small> of the city, after the name of his son, Enoch. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And unto Enoch was born Irad: and Irad begat Mehujael: and Mehujael begat Methusael: and Methusael begat Lamech<small><small>[^49]</small></small>.<small><small>[^50]</small></small> 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Lamech took unto him two wives<small><small>[^51]</small></small>: the name of the one \[was\] Adah, and the name of the other Zillah. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Adah bare Jabal: he was the<small><small>[^52]</small></small> father<small><small>[^53]</small></small> of such as dwell<small><small>[^54]</small></small> in tents, and \[of such as have\] cattle. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.21|21]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And his brother’s name \[was\] Jubal: he was the father<small><small>[^55]</small></small> of all such as handle the harp<small><small>[^56]</small></small> and organ.<small><small>[^57]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.22|22]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Zillah, she also bare Tubal–cain, an instructer of every artificer in brass<small><small>[^58]</small></small> and iron: and the sister of Tubal–cain \[was\] Naamah.<small><small>[^59]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.23|23]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Lamech said unto his wives, Adah and Zillah, Hear my voice; ye wives of Lamech, hear<small><small>[^60]</small></small>ken unto my speech: for I have slain a man to my wounding<small><small>[^61]</small></small>, and a young man to my hurt<small><small>[^62]</small></small>. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.4.24|24]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> If Cain shall be avenged sevenfold, truly Lamech seventy<small><small>[^63]</small></small> and sevenfold.<small><small>[^64]</small></small>
