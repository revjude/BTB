<big><big>[[Gen.23.1 The Death and Burial of Sarah|⥃]]</big></big>***The Death and Burial of Sarah***

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Sarah<small><small>[^2]</small></small> was an<small><small>[^3]</small></small> hundred and seven and twenty years old: \[these were\] the years of the life of Sarah.<small><small>[^4]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Sarah died in Kirjath–arba<small><small>[^5]</small></small>; the same \[is\] Hebron in the land of Canaan: and Abraham came<small><small>[^6]</small></small> to mourn<small><small>[^7]</small></small> for Sarah, and to weep for her. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abraham stood up from before his dead, and spake unto the sons of Heth<small><small>[^8]</small></small>, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I \[am\] a stranger<small><small>[^9]</small></small> and a sojourner with you: give me a possession of a bury<small><small>[^10]</small></small>ing place<small><small>[^11]</small></small> with you, that I may bury my dead out of my sight. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the children of Heth answered Abraham, saying unto him, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Hear us, my lord<small><small>[^12]</small></small>: thou \[art\] a mighty prince<small><small>[^13]</small></small><small><small>[^14]</small></small> among us: in the choice of our sepulchres bury thy dead; none of us shall withhold from thee his sepulchre, but that thou mayest bury thy dead. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abraham stood up, and bowed himself to the people of the land, \[even\] to the children of Heth.<small><small>[^15]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he communed with them, saying, If it be your mind that I should bury my dead out of my sight; hear me, and intreat<small><small>[^16]</small></small> for me to Ephron the son of Zohar, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> That he may give me the cave of Machpelah, which he hath, which \[is\] in the end of his field; for as much money<small><small>[^17]</small></small> as it is worth he shall give it me for a possession of a burying place amongst you. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Ephron dwelt<small><small>[^18]</small></small> among the children of Heth: and Ephron the Hittite answered Abraham in the audience<small><small>[^19]</small></small> of the children of Heth, \[even\] of all that<small><small>[^20]</small></small> went in at the gate of his<small><small>[^21]</small></small> city, saying, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Nay, my lord<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, hear me: the field give I thee, and the cave that \[is\] therein, I give it thee; in the<small><small>[^23]</small></small> presence of the sons of my people give I it thee: bury thy dead. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abraham bowed down himself before the people of the land.<small><small>[^24]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And he spake unto Ephron in the audience of the people of the land, saying, But if thou \[wilt give it\], I pray thee, hear me: I will<small><small>[^25]</small></small> give thee money for the field; take \[it\] of me, and I will bury my dead there. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Ephron answered Abraham, saying unto him, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> My lord, hearken unto me: the land \[is worth<small><small>[^26]</small></small>\] four hundred shekels<small><small>[^27]</small></small> of silver; what \[is\] that betwixt me and thee? bury therefore thy dead. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And Abraham hearkened unto Ephron; and Abraham weighed<small><small>[^28]</small></small> to Ephron the silver, which he had named in the audience of the sons of Heth, four<small><small>[^29]</small></small> hundred shekels of silver, current \[money\] with the merchant. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]And the field<small><small>[^30]</small></small> of Ephron, which \[was\] in Machpelah, which \[was\] before Mamre, the field, and the cave which \[was\] therein, and all the trees that \[were\] in the field, that \[were\] in all the borders round about, were made sure<small><small>[^31]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Unto Abraham for a possession in the presence of the children of Heth, before all<small><small>[^32]</small></small> that went in at the gate of his city. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And after this, Abraham buried Sarah his wife in the cave of the field of Machpelah before Mamre: the same \[is\] Hebron in the land of Canaan.<small><small>[^33]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Gen.23.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the field, and the cave that \[is\] therein, were<small><small>[^34]</small></small> made sure unto Abraham for a<small><small>[^35]</small></small> possession of a burying place by the sons of Heth. <table width="100%" border="0"><tr><td style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;" width="1%" align="left">[[Gen.22|<< Genesis 22]]</td><td align="center" style="border:none;">&nbsp;</td><td width="1%" align="right" style="border:none;white-space: nowrap;">[[Gen.24|Genesis 24 >>]]</td></tr></table>

[^26]: Though the words "is worth" are not in the Text, yet they are clearly implied, to adapt the Hebrew to the English idiom. A shekel, according to the general opinion, was equal in value to about 2s. 6d. of our money, but according to Dr. Prideaux, 3s. English. In those early times, money was given in weight; for it is said ([[Gen.23|Gen 23:16]]) that "Abraham weighed," ***wayishkal***, the silver; and hence, we find that it was a certain weight which afterwards passed as a current coin; for the word shekel is not only used to denote a piece of silver, but also to weigh.
