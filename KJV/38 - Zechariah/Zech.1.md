and they returned<small><small>[^17]</small></small> and said, Like<small><small>[^18]</small></small> as the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts thought<small><small>[^19]</small></small> to do unto us, according to our ways<small><small>[^20]</small></small>, and according to our doings, so hath he dealt with us.

***The Vision of the Horses*** ^bsb2062

 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Zech.1.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Upon the four and twentieth day of the eleventh<small><small>[^21]</small></small> month, which \[is\] the month Sebat<small><small>[^22]</small></small>, in the second year of Darius, came the word of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> unto Zechariah, the son of Berechiah, the son of Iddo the prophet, saying,<small><small>[^23]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Zech.1.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> I saw by night<small><small>[^24]</small></small>, and behold<small><small>[^25]</small></small> a man riding<small><small>[^26]</small></small> upon a red horse, and he stood among<small><small>[^27]</small></small> the myrtle trees that \[were\] in the bottom; and behind him \[were there\] red horses, speckled<small><small>[^28]</small></small>, and white. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Zech.1.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Then said I, O my lord, what<small><small>[^29]</small></small> \[are\] these? And the angel<small><small>[^30]</small></small> that talked with me said unto me, I will shew thee what these \[be\]. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Zech.1.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the man<small><small>[^31]</small></small> that stood among the myrtle trees answered and said, These<small><small>[^32]</small></small> \[are they\] whom the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> hath sent to walk to and fro through the earth. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Zech.1.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And they answe<small><small>[^33]</small></small>red<small><small>[^34]</small></small> the angel of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> that stood among the myrtle trees, and said, We have walked to and fro through the earth, and, behold, all the earth sitteth still, and is<small><small>[^35]</small></small> at rest. 

$\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Zech.1.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> [¶]Then the angel<small><small>[^36]</small></small> of the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> answered and said, O <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, how<small><small>[^37]</small></small> long wilt thou not have mercy on Jerusalem and on the cities of Judah, against which thou hast<small><small>[^38]</small></small> had indignation these threescore and ten years? <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Zech.1.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> answered the angel that talked with me \[with\] good<small><small>[^39]</small></small> words \[and\] comfortable words. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Zech.1.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> So the angel<small><small>[^40]</small></small> that communed with me said unto me, Cry<small><small>[^41]</small></small> thou, saying, Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts; I am<small><small>[^42]</small></small> jealous for Jerusalem and for Zion with a great jealousy. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Zech.1.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And I am very sore displeased with the heathen \[that are\] at ease: for<small><small>[^43]</small></small> I was but a little displeased, and<small><small>[^44]</small></small> they helped forward the affliction.<small><small>[^45]</small></small> <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Zech.1.16|16]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Therefore thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span>; I am<small><small>[^46]</small></small> returned to Jerusalem with mercies: my house<small><small>[^47]</small></small> shall be built in it, saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts, and<small><small>[^48]</small></small> a line shall be stretched forth upon Jerusalem. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[Zech.1.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Cry yet, saying, Thus saith the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> of hosts; My cities<small><small>[^49]</small></small> through prosperity<small><small>[^50]</small></small> shall yet be spread abroad; and the <span class="smallcaps">Lord</span> shall<small><small>[^51]</small></small> yet comfort Zion, and shall yet choose<small><small>[^52]</small></small> Jerusalem.
