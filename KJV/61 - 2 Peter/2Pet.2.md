<big><big>[[2Pet.2.1 Deliverance from False Prophets|⥃]]</big></big>***Deliverance from False Prophets***&emsp;<small><sup>\[ [[Jude.1|Jude 1:3–16]] ]</sup></small>

 $\quad$ <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.1|1]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But there were<small><small>[^2]</small></small> false prophets also among the people, even<small><small>[^3]</small></small> as there shall be false teachers among you, who privily<small><small>[^4]</small></small> shall bring in damnable<small><small>[^5]</small></small> heresies, even denying<small><small>[^6]</small></small> the Lord that bought<small><small>[^7]</small></small> them, and bring<small><small>[^8]</small></small> upon themselves swift destruction. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.2|2]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And many<small><small>[^9]</small></small> shall follow their pernicious ways<small><small>[^10]</small></small><small><small>[^11]</small></small>; by reason of whom the way of truth shall be evil<small><small>[^12]</small></small> spoken of. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.3|3]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And through<small><small>[^13]</small></small> covetousness shall they with<small><small>[^14]</small></small> feigned words make<small><small>[^15]</small></small> merchandise of you: whose<small><small>[^16]</small></small> judgment now of a long time lingereth not, and their damnation slumbereth not. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.4|4]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For if God spared<small><small>[^17]</small></small> not the angels<small><small>[^18]</small></small> that sinned, but<small><small>[^19]</small></small> cast \[them\] down to hell, and delivered \[them\] into<small><small>[^20]</small></small> chains of darkness, to be<small><small>[^21]</small></small> reserved unto judgment; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.5|5]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And spared<small><small>[^22]</small></small> not the old world, but saved Noah the eighth<small><small>[^23]</small></small> \[person\], a preacher<small><small>[^24]</small></small> of righteousness, bringing<small><small>[^25]</small></small> in the flood upon the world of the ungodly; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.6|6]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And turning<small><small>[^26]</small></small> the cities of Sodom and Gomorrha into ashes condemned \[them\] with an overthrow, making<small><small>[^27]</small></small> \[them\] an ensample unto those that after should live ungodly; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.7|7]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And delivered<small><small>[^28]</small></small> just Lot, vexed<small><small>[^29]</small></small> with the filthy conversation of the wicked: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.8|8]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> (For that<small><small>[^30]</small></small> righteous man dwelling among them, in seeing<small><small>[^31]</small></small> and hearing, vexed \[his\] righteous soul from day to day with \[their\] unlawful deeds;) <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.9|9]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> The Lord knoweth<small><small>[^32]</small></small> how to deliver the godly<small><small>[^33]</small></small> out of temptations, and<small><small>[^34]</small></small> to reserve the unjust unto<small><small>[^35]</small></small> the day of judgment to be punished: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.10|10]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But chiefly them that<small><small>[^36]</small></small> walk after the flesh in the<small><small>[^37]</small></small> lust of uncleanness, and despise<small><small>[^38]</small></small> government<small><small>[^39]</small></small>. Presumptuous<small><small>[^40]</small></small> \[are they\], selfwilled<small><small>[^41]</small></small>, they are not afraid to speak<small><small>[^42]</small></small> evil of dignities. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.11|11]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Whereas angels<small><small>[^43]</small></small>, which are greater in power and might, bring not railing accusation against them<small><small>[^44]</small></small> before the Lord. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.12|12]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> But these, as natural<small><small>[^45]</small></small> brute beasts, made to be taken and destroyed, speak evil of the things that they understand not; and shall utterly perish<small><small>[^46]</small></small> in their own corruption; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.13|13]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> And shall receive the reward<small><small>[^47]</small></small> of unrighteousness, \[as\] they that count it pleasure to riot<small><small>[^48]</small></small> in the day time. Spots<small><small>[^49]</small></small> \[they are\] and blemishes, sporting themselves with their own deceivings while<small><small>[^50]</small></small> they feast with you; <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.14|14]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Having eyes<small><small>[^51]</small></small> full of adultery<small><small>[^52]</small></small>, and that cannot<small><small>[^53]</small></small> cease from sin; beguiling<small><small>[^54]</small></small> unstable souls: an heart<small><small>[^55]</small></small> they have exercised with covetous practices; cursed<small><small>[^56]</small></small> children: <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.15|15]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> Which have prophet. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.17|17]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> These are wells<small><small>[^63]</small></small> without water, <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.18|18]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For when they speak<small><small>[^67]</small></small> great swelling<small><small>[^68]</small></small> \[words\] of vanity, they allure through the lusts of the flesh, \[through much\] wantonness<small><small>[^69]</small></small>, those that were<small><small>[^70]</small></small> clean<small><small>[^71]</small></small> escaped from them who live in error. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.19|19]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> While they promise<small><small>[^72]</small></small> them liberty, they themselves<small><small>[^73]</small></small> are the servants of corruption: for of whom a man is overcome<small><small>[^74]</small></small>, of the same is he brought in bondage. <span class="startversemarker">[</span>**[[2Pet.2.20|20]]**<span class="endversemarker">]</span> For if
