<span class="asform"><span class="asgreek"><span class="asbold">Βάαλ</span></span> (Rec. <span class="asgreek">βαάλ</span>), <span class="asgreek">ὁ, ἡ</span>, indecl. (Heb. <span class="ashebrew">בַּעַל</span> <small><sup>[[H1167]]</sup></small>, <span class="asitalic">lord</span>),</span>
<span class="asitalic">Baal</span>: [[Rom.11.4|Ro 11:4]]<span class="assuperscript">(LXX)</span>. The fem. art. here agrees with the usage of LXX, where, following a similar Hebrew practice (<span class="ashebrew">בֹּשֶׁת</span> <small><sup>[[H1322]]</sup></small> for <span class="ashebrew">בַּעַל</span> <small><sup>[[H1167]]</sup></small>), <span class="asgreek">αἰσχύνη</span> appears to have been substituted in reading for the written <span class="asgreek">Βάαλ</span> (cf. [[LXX.1Kgs.18.19|III Ki 18:19]]), and to account for the freq. use of the fem. art. bef. <span class="asgreek">Β</span>. The usage, however, is not general, and in the passage cited in Ro ([[LXX.1Kgs.19.18|III Ki 19:18]]), LXX reads <span class="asgreek">τῷ Β</span>.†
