<span class="asform"><span class="asgreek"><span class="asbold">ἀγαθός</span></span>, <span class="asgreek">-ή, -όν</span>,</span> <span class="asetym">  <span class="asseptuagint">\[in LXX chiefly for **<big>[[H2896|טוֹב]]</big>**;\]</span> </span>
in general, <span class="asitalic">good</span>, in physical and in moral sense, used of persons, things, acts, conditions, etc., applied to that which is regarded as "perfect in its kind, so as to produce pleasure and satisfaction, . . . that which, in itself good, is also at once for the good and the advantage of him who comes in contact with it" (Cremer, 3): <span class="asgreek">γῆ</span>, [[Luke.8.8|Lk 8:8]]; <span class="asgreek">δένδρον</span>, [[Matt.7.18|Mt 7:18]]; <span class="asgreek">καρδία</span>, [[Luke.8.15|Lk 8:15]]; <span class="asgreek">δόσις</span>, [[Jas.1.17|Ja 1:17]]; <span class="asgreek">μέρις</span>, [[Luke.10.42|Lk 10:42]]; <span class="asgreek">ἔργον</span> (freq. in Pl.), [[Phil.1.6|Phl 1:6]]; <span class="asgreek">ἐλπίς</span>, [[2Thess.2.16|II Th 2:16]]; <span class="asgreek">θησαυρός</span>, [[Matt.12.35|Mt 12:35]]; <span class="asgreek">μνεία</span>, [[1Thess.3.6|I Th 3:6]] (cf. [[2Macc.7.20|II Mac 7:20]]); as subst., <span class="asgreek">τὸ ἀ.</span>, that which is morally good, beneficial, acceptable to God, [[Rom.12.2|Ro 12:2]]; <span class="asgreek">ἐργάζεσθαι τὸ ἀ</span>, [[Rom.2.10|Ro 2:10]], [[Eph.4.28|Eph 4:28]]; <span class="asgreek">πράσσειν</span>, [[Rom.9.11|Ro 9:11]], [[2Cor.5.10|II Co 5:10]]; <span class="asgreek">διώκειν</span>, [[1Thess.5.15|I Th 5:15]]; <span class="asgreek">μιμεῖσθαι</span>, [[3John.1.11|III Jo 11]]; <span class="asgreek">κολλᾶσθαι τῷ ἀ.</span>, [[Rom.12.9|Ro 12:9]]; <span class="asgreek">ερωτᾶν περὶ τοῦ ἀ.</span>, [[Matt.19.17|Mt 19:17]]; <span class="asgreek">διάκονος εἰς τὸ ἀ.</span>, [[Rom.13.4|Ro 13:4]]; <span class="asgreek">τὸ ἀ. σου</span>, <span class="asitalic">thy favour, benefit</span>, [[Phlm.1.14|Phm 14]]; pl., <span class="asgreek">τὰ ἀ.</span>, of goods, possessions, [[Luke.12.18|Lk 12:18]]; of spiritual benefits, [[Rom.10.15|Ro 10:15]], [[Heb.9.11|He 9:11]], [[Heb.10.1|10:1]]. <span class="asgreek">ἀ.</span> is opp. to <span class="asgreek">πονηρός</span>, [[Matt.5.45|Mt 5:45]], [[Matt.20.15|20:5]]; <span class="asgreek">κακός</span>, [[Rom.7.19|Ro 7:19]]; <span class="asgreek">φαῦλος</span>, [[Rom.9.11|Ro 9:11]], [[2Cor.5.10|II Co 5:10]] (cf. MM, <span class="asitalic">VGT</span>, s.v.).
<span class="asre"><span class="asitalic">SYN.</span>: <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">καλός</span></span>, <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">δίκαιος</span></span>. <span class="asgreek">κ.</span> properly refers to <span class="asitalic">goodliness</span> as manifested in form: <span class="asgreek">ἀ.</span> to inner excellence (cf. the cl. <span class="asgreek">καλὸς κἀγαθός</span> and <span class="asgreek">ἐν καρδίᾳ κ. καὶ ἀ.</span>, [[Luke.8.15|Lk 8:15]]). In [[Rom.5.7|Ro 5:7]], where it is contrasted with <span class="asgreek">δ.</span>, <span class="asgreek">ἀ.</span> implies a kindliness and attractiveness not necessarily possessed by the <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">δίκαιος</span></span>, who merely measures up to a high standard of rectitude (cf. <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">ἀγαθωσύνη</span></span>). </span>
