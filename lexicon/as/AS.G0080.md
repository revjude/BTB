<span class="asform"><span class="asgreek"><span class="asbold">ἀδελφός</span></span>, <span class="asgreek">-οῦ, ὁ</span> </span> <span class="asetym">  <span class="asderivation">(&lt; <span class="asgreek">ἀ-</span> copul., <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">δελφύς</span></span>, <span class="asitalic">womb</span>),</span> </span>
in cl., <span class="asitalic">a brother</span>, born of the same parent or parents. \[In LXX (Hort, <span class="asitalic">Ja.</span>, 102 f.), for <span class="ashebrew">אָח</span> <small><sup>[[H0251]]</sup></small>;\]
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">1.</span> lit. of a brother ([[Gen.4.2|Ge 4:2]], al.).</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">2.</span> Of a neighbour ([[Lev.19.17|Le 19:17]]).</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">3.</span> Of a member of the same nation ([[Exod.2.14|Ex 2:14]], [[Deut.15.3|De 15:3]]). In NT in each of these senses (1. [[Matt.1.2|Mt 1:2]], al.; 2. [[Matt.7.3|Mt 7:3]]; 3. [[Rom.9.3|Ro 9:3]]) and also,</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">4.</span> of a fellow-Christian: [[1Cor.1.1|I Co 1:1]], [[Acts.9.30|Ac 9:30]]. This usage finds illustration in <span class="asgreek">π.</span>, where <span class="asgreek">ἀ.</span> is used of members of a pagan religious community (M, <span class="asitalic">Th.</span>, I, 1:4; MM, <span class="asitalic">VGT</span>, s.v.). The <span class="asgreek">ἀδελφοὶ τ. Κυρίου</span> ([[Matt.12.46|Mt 12:46-49]] [[Matt.13.55|13:55]] [[Matt.28.10|28:10]], [[Mark.3.31|Mk 3:31-34]], [[Luke.8.19|Lk 8:19-21]], [[John.2.12|Jo 2:12]]  [[John.7.3|7:3]],  [[John.7.5|5]] [[John.7.10|10]] [[John.20.17|20:17]], [[Acts.1.14|Ac 1:14]], [[1Cor.9.5|I Co 9:5]]) may have been sons of Joseph and Mary (Mayor, <span class="asitalic">Ja.</span>, Intr. viff.; <span class="asitalic">DB</span>, i, 320 ff.) or of Joseph by a former marriage (Lft., <span class="asitalic">Gal.</span>, 252ff.; <span class="asitalic">DCG</span>, i, 232 ff.), but the view of Jerome, which makes <span class="asgreek">ἀ.</span> equivalent to <span class="asgreek">ἀνεψιός</span>, is inconsistent with Greek usage. (Cremer, 66.)</span>
