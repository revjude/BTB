<span class="asform"><span class="asgreek"><span class="asbold">ἄν</span></span>, </span>  <span class="asgramGrp"><span class="aspos">conditional particle</span>, </span>
which cannot usually be separately translated in English, its force depending on the constructions which contain it (see further, LS, s.v.; WM, §xlii; M, <span class="asitalic">Pr.</span>, 165 ff.; MM, <span class="asitalic">VGT</span>, s.v.).
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">1.</span> In apodosis,</span>
$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(i)</span> c. indic, impf. or aor., expressing what would be or would have been if (<span class="asgreek">εἰ</span> c. impf., aor. or plpf.) some condition were or had been fulfilled: [[Luke.7.39|Lk 7:39]]  [[Luke.17.6|17:6]], [[John.5.46|Jo 5:46]], [[Gal.1.10|Ga 1:10]], [[Matt.12.7|Mt 12:7]]  [[Matt.24.43|24:43]], [[1Cor.2.8|I Co 2:8]], [[Acts.18.14|Ac 18:14]], [[1John.2.19|I Jo 2:19]], al. The protasis is sometimes understood (as also in cl.): [[Matt.25.27|Mt 25:27]], [[Luke.19.23|Lk 19:23]]. In hypothetical sentences, expressing unreality, <span class="asgreek">ἄν</span> (as often in late writers, more rarely in cl.) is omitted: [[John.8.39|Jo 8:39]]  [[John.15.24|15:24]] 19:11, [[Rom.7.7|Ro 7:7]], [[Gal.4.15|Ga 4:15]];</span>
$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(ii)</span> c. opt., inf., ptcp. (cl.; v. LS, s.v.; M, <span class="asitalic">Int.</span>, §275; M, <span class="asitalic">Pr.</span>, 167:4).</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">2.</span> In combination with conditional, relative, temporal, and final words;</span>
$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(i)</span> as in cl., c. subj.,</span>
$\quad$$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(a)</span> in protasis with <span class="asgreek">εἰ</span>, in Attic contr. <span class="asgreek">ἐάν</span>, q.v.;</span>
$\quad$$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(b)</span> in conditional, relative, and temporal clauses (coalescing with <span class="asgreek">ὅτε, ἐπεί</span>, etc.; v.s. <span class="asgreek">ὅταν, ἐπάν</span>, etc.), <span class="asitalic">ever, soever</span>;</span>
$\quad$$\quad$$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(α)</span> c. pres., <span class="asgreek">ἡνίκα</span>, [[2Cor.3.15|II Co 3:15]]; <span class="asgreek">ὃς ἄν</span>, [[Rom.9.15|Ro 9:15]]<span class="assuperscript">(LXX)</span> [[Rom.16.2|16:2]], al.; <span class="asgreek">ὅσοι ἄν</span>, [[Luke.9.5|Lk 9:5]]; <span class="asgreek">ὡς ἄν</span>, [[Rom.15.24|Ro 15:24]] (M, <span class="asitalic">Pr.</span>, 167);</span>
$\quad$$\quad$$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(β)</span> c. aor., <span class="asgreek">ὃς ἄν</span>, [[Matt.5.21|Mt 5:21]],  [[Matt.5.22|22]], [[Matt.5.31|31]]; <span class="asgreek">ἕως ἄν</span>, <span class="asitalic">until</span>, [[Matt.2.13|Mt 2:13]], [[Mark.6.10|Mk 6:10]], al.; <span class="asgreek">ὡς ἄν</span>, <span class="asitalic">as soon as</span> (M, <span class="asitalic">Pr.</span>, 167), [[1Cor.11.34|I Co 11:34]], [[Phil.2.23|Phl 2:23]]. $\quad$$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(ii)</span> in late Gk., when some actual fact is spoken of, c. indic.: <span class="asgreek">ὅταν</span> (q.v.); <span class="asgreek">ὅπου ἄν</span>, [[Mark.6.56|Mk 6:56]] (M, <span class="asitalic">Pr.</span>, 168); <span class="asgreek">καθότι ἄν</span>, [[Acts.2.45|Ac 2:45]]  [[Acts.4.35|4:35]]; <span class="asgreek">ὡς ἄν</span>, [[1Cor.12.2|I Co 12:2]].</span>
$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">3.</span> in iterative construction, c. impf. and aor. indic. (M, <span class="asitalic">Pr.</span>, 167): [[Acts.2.45|Ac 2:45]]  [[Acts.4.35|4:35]], [[1Cor.12.2|I Co 12:2]].</span>
