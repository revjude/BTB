<span class="asform"><span class="asgreek"><span class="asbold">οἰκία</span></span>, <span class="asgreek">-ας, ἡ</span></span> <span class="asetym">  <span class="asderivation">(&lt; <span class="asgreek">οἶκος</span>),</span>  <span class="asseptuagint">\[in LXX chiefly (very freq.) for <span class="ashebrew">בַּיִת</span> <small><sup>[[H1004]]</sup></small>;\]</span> </span>
<span class="asitalic">a house</span>, <span class="asitalic">dwelling</span>: [[Matt.2.11|Mt 2:11]] [[Matt.7.24|7:24-27]], [[Mark.1.29|Mk 1:29]], al.; <span class="asgreek">ἐν οἰκίᾳ</span> (= cl. <span class="asgreek">κατ ̓ οἰκίαν</span>), <span class="asitalic">at home</span>, [[Luke.8.27|Lk 8:27]]; <span class="asgreek">εἰς οἰ.</span>, [[2John.1.10|II Jo 10]]; <span class="asgreek">οἰ</span>, c. gen. pers., usually has the art. ([[Matt.8.14|Mt 8:14]], al., but cf. <span class="asgreek">οἶκος</span> and v. Bl., § 46, 9); <span class="asgreek">ἡ οἰ. τ. πατρός μου</span>, [[John.14.2|Jo 14:2]]. Metaph.,
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(a)</span> of the body as the dwelling of the soul: [[2Cor.5.1|II Co 5:1]];</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(b)</span> of property (as <span class="ashebrew">בַּיִת</span> <small><sup>[[H1004]]</sup></small>, [[Gen.45.18|Ge 45:18]], LXX, <span class="asgreek">τ. ὑπάρχοντα</span>; [[1Kgs.13.8|III Ki 13:8]], LXX, <span class="asgreek">οἶκος</span>) = <span class="asgreek">οἶκος</span> (q.v.): [[Mark.12.40|Mk 12:40]], [[Luke.20.47|Lk 20:47]];</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(c)</span> of the inmates of the dwelling, <span class="asitalic">the household</span>: [[Matt.12.25|Mt 12:25]]; c. gen. pers., [[John.4.53|Jo 4:53]], [[1Cor.16.15|I Co 16:15]].</span>
<span class="asre"><span class="asitalic">SYN.</span>: <span class="asgreek">οἶκος</span> <small><sup>[[G3624]]</sup></small>, which in Attic law denoted the whole <span class="asitalic">estate</span>, <span class="asgreek">οἰκία</span> <small><sup>[[G3614]]</sup></small>, the <span class="asitalic">dwelling</span> only. In cl. poets <span class="asgreek">οἶκος</span> has also the latter sense, but not in prose, except in metaph. usage, where it signifies both <span class="asitalic">property</span> and <span class="asitalic">household</span>. The foregoing distinction is not, however, consistently maintained in late Greek; cf. MM, ii, xvii, and v. Thayer, s.v. <span class="asgreek">οἰκία</span>. </span>
