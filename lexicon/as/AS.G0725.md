<span class="asform">\*† <span class="asgreek"><span class="asbold">ἁρπαγμός</span></span>, <span class="asgreek">οῦ, ὁ</span></span> <span class="asetym">  <span class="asderivation">(&lt; <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">ἁρπάζω</span></span>);</span> </span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">1.</span> prop., acc., to the rule of its formation (Bl., § 27, 2), actively, <span class="asitalic">the act of seizing</span>, <span class="asitalic">robbery</span> (Plut., <span class="asitalic">de Puer. Educ.</span>, p. 12A), [[Phil.2.6|Phl 2:6]], AV (Waterland, <span class="asitalic">Works</span>, II, 108; Cremer, 649 f.; Meyer, in l.; cf. also <span class="asitalic">JThS</span>, July, 1909, April, 1911; MM, s.v.).</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">2.</span> Passively = <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">ἅρπαγμα</span></span> ([[Ezek.22.25|Ez 22:25]], of a lion's prey, <span class="ashebrew">טֶרֶף</span> <small><sup>[[H2964]]</sup></small>), <span class="asitalic">a thing seized</span>, hence, <span class="asitalic">a prize</span>: [[Phil.2.6|Phl 2:6]], RV (Lft., Ellic., <span class="asitalic">ICC</span>, in l.; Donaldson, <span class="asitalic">NCrat.</span>, 450 ff.; and esp. Gifford, <span class="asitalic">The Incarnation</span>, 59-71, and reff. in <span class="asitalic">DB</span>, ii, 835 B). The lexical data favour the active meaning, but as they also admit the possibility of the alternative, most modern expositors have accepted the latter as seeming to suit the logic of the passage better. The lexical difficulty, however, remains (MM, s.v., esp. the last ref.). As to the usage of St. Paul, he seems inclined to adopt the <span class="asgreek">-μα</span> form where it is appropriate (e.g. [[Rom.11.9|Ro 11:9]], where cf. LXX; [[1Cor.13.9|I Co 13:9]], [[2Cor.1.9|II Co 1:9]]), and there is certainly a presumption in favour of the active meaning here from the fact that he does not use the LXX <span class="asgreek">ἅρπαγμα</span>. Suggestions looking to a fresh exegesis are given in <span class="asitalic">JThS</span>, July, 1909, April, 1911.†</span>
