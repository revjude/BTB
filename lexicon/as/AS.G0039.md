<span class="asform"><span class="asgreek"><span class="asbold">ἅγιος</span></span>, <span class="asgreek">-α, ον</span>  </span> <span class="asetym">  <span class="asderivation">(&lt; <ref target="ἅγός|G0040"><span class="asgreek">τό ἅγός</span></span>, <span class="asitalic">religious awe</span>; <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">ἅζω</span></span>, <span class="asitalic">to venerate</span>), </span>  <span class="asseptuagint">\[in LXX chiefly for **<big>[[H6944|קֹ֫דֶשׁ]]</big>**;\]</span> </span>
primarily, <span class="asitalic">dedicated to the gods</span>, <span class="asitalic">sacred</span> (Hdt.; rare in Att., never in Hom., Hes. and Trag., who use <span class="asgreek">ἁγνός</span>), hence, <span class="asitalic">holy</span>, characteristic of God, separated to God, worthy of veneration.
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">1.</span>  Its highest application is to God himself, in his purity, majesty, and glory: [[Luke.1.49|Lk 1:49]], [[John.17.11|Jo 17:11]], [[Rev.4.8|Re 4:8]]. Hence</span>
$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(a)</span> of things and places which have a claim to reverence as sacred to God, e.g. the Temple: [[Matt.24.15|Mt 24:15]], [[Heb.9.1|He 9:1]];</span>
$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(b)</span> of persons employed by him, as angels: [[1Thess.3.13|I Th 3:13]]; prophets, [[Luke.1.70|Lk 1:70]]; apostles, [[Eph.3.5|Eph 3:5]].</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">2.</span> Applied to persons as separated to God's service:</span>
$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(a)</span> of Christ, [[Mark.1.24|Mk 1:24]], [[John.6.69|Jo 6:69]], [[Acts.4.30|Ac 4:30]];</span>
$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(b)</span> of Christians, [[Acts.9.13|Ac 9:13]], [[Rom.1.7|Ro 1:7]], [[Heb.6.10|He 6:10]], [[Rev.5.8|Re 5:8]].</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">3.</span> In the moral sense of sharing God's purity: [[Mark.6.20|Mk 6:20]], [[John.17.11|Jo 17:11]], [[Acts.3.14|Ac 3:14]], [[Rev.3.7|Re 3:7]].</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">4.</span> Of pure, clean sacrifices and offerings: [[1Cor.7.14|I Co 7:14]], [[Eph.1.4|Eph 1:4]].</span>
<span class="asre"><span class="asitalic">SYN.</span>: <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">ἁγνός</span></span>, <span class="asitalic">pure</span>, both in ceremonial and spiritual sense; <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">ἱερός</span></span> (<span class="aslatin">sacer</span>), <span class="asitalic">sacred</span>, that which is inviolable because of its (external) relation to God; <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">ὅσιος</span></span> (<span class="aslatin">sanctus</span> as opp. to <span class="aslatin">nefas</span>), that which is based on everlasting ordinances of right. (Cf. Tr., <span class="asitalic">Syn.</span>, §lxxxviii; <span class="asitalic">DB</span>, ii, 399 f.; Cremer, 34, 594-601; MM, <span class="asitalic">VGT</span>, s.v.) </span>
