<span class="asform"><span class="asgreek"><span class="asbold">λειτουργέω</span></span>, <span class="asgreek">-ῶ</span></span> <span class="asetym">  <span class="asderivation">(&lt; <span class="asgreek">λειτουργός</span>),</span>  <span class="asseptuagint">\[in LXX chiefly for **<big>[[H8334|שׁרת]]</big>** pi., also for **<big>[[H5647|עבד]]</big>**, **<big>[[H6633|צבא]]</big>**, etc.;\]</span> </span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">1.</span> in cl., at Athens, <span class="asitalic">to supply public offices at one's own cost</span>, <span class="asitalic">render public service to the State</span>, hence, generally,</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">2.</span> <span class="asitalic">to serve the State</span>, <span class="asitalic">do a service</span>, <span class="asitalic">serve</span> (of service to the Gods, Diod., i, 21): of the official service of priests and Levites ([[LXX.Exod.29.30|Ex 29:30]], [[LXX.Num.16.9|Nu 16:9]], [[Sir.4.14|Si 4:14]], [[1Macc.10.42|I Mac 10:42]], al.; cf. Deiss., <span class="asitalic">BS</span>, 140 f.), [[Heb.10.11|He 10:11]]; of Christians: c. dat. pers. seq. <span class="asgreek">ἐν</span>, [[Rom.15.27|Ro 15:27]] (cf. [[Sir.10.25|Si 10:25]]); <span class="asgreek">τ. κυρίῳ</span>, [[Acts.13.2|Ac 13:2]].†</span>
<span class="asre"><span class="asitalic">SYN.</span>: **<big>[[G3000|λατρεύω]]</big>** (q.v.), prop., <span class="asitalic">to serve for hire</span>, LXX (as sometimes in cl.), always of service to the deity on the part of both priests and people ([[LXX.Exod.4.3|Ex 4:3]], [[LXX.Deut.10.12|De 10:12]], and similarly in NT). **<big>[[G3008|λειτουργέω]]</big>** "is the fulfilment of an office: it has a definite representative character, and corresponds with a function to be discharged". It is therefore used of serving in an office or ministry: in LXX always of priests and Levites, in NT, with its cognates ([[Rom.13.6|Ro 13:6]] [[Rom.15.27|15:27]], are not really exceptions), of services rendered either to God or man by apostles, prophets, teachers, and other officers of the church (cf. Tr., <span class="asitalic">Syn.</span>, § xxxv; <span class="asitalic">ICC</span> on [[Rom.1.9|Ro 1:9]]; Westc., <span class="asitalic">He.</span>, 232 ff.). </span>
