<span class="asitalic">thou, you</span>, gen., <span class="asgreek">σοῦ</span>, dat., <span class="asgreek">σοί</span>, acc., <span class="asgreek">σέ</span>, pl., <span class="asgreek">ὑμεῖς, -ῶν, -ῖν, -ᾶς</span> (enclitic in oblique cases sing., except after prep. (BL, §48, 3), though <span class="asgreek">πρὸς σέ</span> occurs in [[Matt.25.39|Mt 25:39]]). Nom. for emphasis or contrast: [[John.1.30|Jo 1:30]], [[John.1.42|42]] [[John.4.10|4:10]] [[John.5.33|5:33]], [[John.5.39|39]], [[John.5.44|44]], [[Acts.4.7|Ac 4:7]], [[Eph.5.32|Eph 5:32]]; so also perhaps <span class="asgreek">σὺ εἶπας</span>, [[Matt.26.64|Mt 26:64]], al. (M, <span class="asitalic">Pr.</span>, 86); before voc., [[Matt.2.6|Mt 2:6]], [[Luke.1.76|Lk 1:76]], [[John.17.5|Jo 17:5]], al.; sometimes without emphasis (M, <span class="asitalic">Pr.</span>, 85f.), as also in cl., but esp. as rendering of Heb. phrase, e.g. <span class="asgreek">υἱός μου εἶ σύ</span> (**<big>[[H1121|בְּנִי]]</big>** **<big>[[H0859|אַתָּה]]</big>**, [[LXX.Ps.2.7|Ps 2:7]]), [[Acts.13.33|Ac 13:33]]. The gen. (<span class="asgreek">σοῦ, ὑμῶν</span>) is sometimes placed bef. the noun: [[Luke.7.48|Lk 7:48]], [[Luke.12.30|12:30]], al.; so also the enclitic <span class="asgreek">σοῦ</span>, [[Matt.9.6|Mt 9:6]]; on <span class="asgreek">τί ἐμοὶ κ. σοί</span>, v.s. <span class="asgreek">ἐγώ</span>.
