<span class="asform">† <span class="asgreek"><span class="asbold">ἀγάπη</span></span>, <span class="asgreek">-ης, ἡ</span> </span> <span class="asetym">  <span class="asseptuagint">\[in LXX for <span class="ashebrew">אַהֲבָה</span> <small><sup>[[H0160]]</sup></small>, which is also rendered by <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">ἀγάπησις</span></span> and <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">φιλία</span></span>;\]</span> </span>
<span class="asitalic">love</span>, <span class="asitalic">goodwill</span>, <span class="asitalic">esteem</span>. Outside of bibl. and eccl. books, there is no clear instance (with Deiss., <span class="asitalic">LAE</span>, 18:4, 70:2, cf. the same writer in <span class="asitalic">Constr. Quar.</span>, ii, 4; and with MM, <span class="asitalic">VGT</span>, s.v., cf. Dr. Moulton in <span class="asitalic">Exp. Times</span>, xxvi, 3, 139). In NT, like <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">ἀγαπάω</span></span>, <span class="asgreek">-ῶ</span>,
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">1.</span> Of men's love:</span>
$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(a)</span> to one another, [[John.13.35|Jo 13:35]];</span>
$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(b)</span> to God, [[1John.2.5|I Jo 2:5]].</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">2.</span> Of divine love;</span>
$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(a)</span> God's love: to men, [[Rom.5.8|Ro 5:8]]; to Christ, [[John.17.26|Jo 17:26]];</span>
$\quad$ <span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(b)</span> Christ's love to men: [[Rom.8.35|Ro 8:35]].</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">3.</span> In pl., <span class="asitalic">love feasts</span>: [[Jude.1.12|Ju 12]] (<span class="asitalic">DB</span>, iii, 157).</span>
<span class="asre"><span class="asitalic">SYN.</span>: <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">φιλία</span></span>. <span class="asgreek">ἀ.</span>, signifying properly (v.s. <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">ἀγαπάω</span></span>) love which chooses its object, is taken over from LXX, where its connotation is more general, into NT, and there used exclusively to express that spiritual bond of love between God and man and between man and man, in Christ, which is characteristic of Christianity. It is thus distinct from <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">φιλία</span></span>, <span class="asitalic">friendship</span> ([[Jas.4.4|Ja 4:4]] only), <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">στοργή</span></span>, <span class="asitalic">natural affection</span> (in NT only in compounds, v.s. <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">ἄστοργος</span></span>) and <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">ἔρως</span></span>, <span class="asitalic">sexual love</span>, which is not used in NT, its place being taken by <span class="asref"><span class="asgreek">ἐπιθυμία</span></span>.
