<span class="asform">† <span class="asgreek"><span class="asbold">γέεννα</span></span> (<span class="asgreek">γέενα</span>, [[Mark.9.45|Mk 9:45]], Rec.), <span class="asgreek">-ης, ἡ</span> (perh. through Aram. <span class="asaramic">גֵּיהִנָּם</span>, from Heb. <span class="ashebrew">גֵּי הִנֹּם</span> <small><sup>[[H2011]]</sup></small>, [[LXX.Neh.11.30|Ne 11:30]]; <span class="ashebrew">גֵּי בֶּן־הִנֹּם</span> <small><sup>[[H1121]]</sup></small><small><sup>[[H2011]]</sup></small>, [[John.18.16|Jo 18:16]]; <span class="ashebrew">גֵּי בּנֵי־הִנֹּם</span> <small><sup>[[H1121]]</sup></small><small><sup>[[H2011]]</sup></small>, [[LXX.2Kgs.23.10|IV Ki 23:10]]; <span class="asitalic">valley of (the son, sons of) lamentation</span>); </span> <span class="asetym">  <span class="asseptuagint">\[in LXX the nearest approach to <span class="asgreek">γ.</span> is <span class="asgreek">γαίεννα</span>, [[LXX.Josh.18.16|Jos 18:16]] (<span class="asgreek">Γαὶ Ὀννόμ</span>, A), elsewhere <span class="asgreek">φάραγξ Ὀνόμ</span> ([[LXX.Josh.15.8|Jos 15:8]], al.), V. Swete on [[Mark.9.43|Mk 9:43]] ;\]</span> </span>
<span class="asitalic">Gehenna</span>, a valley W. and S. of Jerusalem, which as the site of fire-worship from the time of Ahaz, was desecrated by Josiah and became a dumping-place for the offal of the city. Later, the name was used as a symbol of the place of future punishment, as in NT: [[Matt.5.29|Mt 5:29]], [[Matt.5.30|30]] [[Matt.10.28|10:28]], [[Mark.9.43|Mk 9:43]],  [[Mark.9.45|45]], [[Mark.9.47|47]], [[Luke.12.5|Lk 12:5]], [[Jas.3.6|Ja 3:6]]; <span class="asgreek">γ. τ. πυρός</span>, [[Matt.5.22|Mt 5:22]]  [[Matt.18.19|18:19]], prob. with ref. to fires of Moloch (<span class="asitalic">DB</span>, ii, 119b); <span class="asgreek">υἱὸς γεέννης</span>, [[Matt.23.15|Mt 23:15]]; <span class="asgreek">κρίσις γεέννης</span>, [[Matt.23.33|23:33]].†
