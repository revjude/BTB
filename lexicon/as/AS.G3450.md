<span class="asform"><span class="asgreek"><span class="asbold">ἐγώ</span></span>, gen, etc., <span class="asgreek">ἐμοῦ, ἐμοί, ἐμέ</span> (enclitic <span class="asgreek">μου, μοι, με</span>), pl. <span class="asgreek">ἡμεῖς, -ῶν, -ῖν, -ᾶς</span>, </span>  <span class="asgramGrp"><span class="aspos">pers. pron.</span> </span>
<span class="asitalic">I</span>.
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(a)</span> The nom. is usually emphatic, when expressed as subjc, as in [[Matt.3.11|Mt 3:11]], [[Mark.1.8|Mk 1:8]], [[Luke.3.16|Lk 3:16]], al. But often there is no apparent emphasis, as [[Matt.10.16|Mt 10:16]], [[John.10.17|Jo 10:17]]; <span class="asgreek">ἰδοὺ ἐ.</span> (= Heb. **<big>[[H2005|הִנֵּנִי]]</big>**, cf. [[LXX.1Sam.3.8|I Ki 3:8]]), [[Acts.9.10|Ac 9:10]]; <span class="asgreek">ἐ.</span> (like Heb. **<big>[[H0589|אֲנִי]]</big>**), <span class="asitalic">I am</span>, [[John.1.23|Jo 1:23 (LXX)]], [[Acts.7.32|Ac 7:32 (LXX)]].</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(b)</span> The enclitic forms (v. supr.) are used with nouns, adjectives, verbs, adverbs, where there is no emphasis: <span class="asgreek">ἐν τ. πατρί μου</span>, [[John.14.20|Jo 14:20]]; <span class="asgreek">μου τ. λόγους</span>, [[Matt.7.24|Mt 7:24]]; <span class="asgreek">ὀπίσω μου</span>, [[Matt.3.11|Mt 3:11]]; <span class="asgreek">ἰσχυρότερός μου</span>, [[Matt.3.11|ib.]]; <span class="asgreek">λέγει μοι</span>, [[Rev.5.5|Re 5:5]]; also with the prep. <span class="asgreek">πρός</span>, as [[Mark.9.19|Mk 9:19]], al. The full forms (<span class="asgreek">ἐμοῦ</span>, etc.) are used with the other prepositions, as <span class="asgreek">δι' ἐμοῦ, ἐν ἐμοί, εἰς ἐμέ</span>, etc., also for emphasis, as [[Luke.10.16|Lk 10:16]], [[John.7.23|Jo 7:23]], [[Mark.14.7|Mk 14:7]], al.</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(c)</span> The gen. <span class="asgreek">μου</span> and <span class="asgreek">ἡμῶν</span> are often used for the poss. pronouns <span class="asgreek">ἐμός, ἡμέτερος: τ. λαόν μου</span>, [[Matt.2.6|Mt 2:6]]; <span class="asgreek">μου τῂ ἀπιστιᾳ</span>, [[Mark.9.24|Mk 9:24]].</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(d)</span> <span class="asgreek">τί ἐμοὶ καὶ σοί</span> ( = Heb. **<big>[[H4100|וָלָךְ מַה־לִי]]</big>**, [[LXX.Judg.11.12|Jg 11:12]], al.), i.e. <span class="asitalic">what have we in common</span>: [[Matt.8.29|Mt 8:29]], [[Mark.1.24|Mk 1:24]] [[Mark.5.7|5:7]], [[Luke.8.28|Lk 8:28]], [[John.2.4|Jo 2:4]]; <span class="asgreek">τί γάρ μοι</span>, [[1Cor.5.2|I Co 5:2]].</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(e)</span> The interchange of <span class="asgreek">ἐγώ</span> and <span class="asgreek">ἡμεῖς</span>, common in <span class="asgreek">π.</span>, appears in Pauline Epp. (v. M, <span class="asitalic">Pr.</span>, 86f., M, <span class="asitalic">Th.</span>, 131f.).</span>
<span class="asbullet"><span class="asbold">(f)</span> <span class="asgreek">κἀγώ</span> (= <span class="asgreek">καὶ ἐγώ</span>, <span class="asitalic">and I, even I, I also</span>: [[Matt.2.8|Mt 2:8]], [[Luke.2.48|Lk 2:48]], [[John.6.56|Jo 6:56]], [[Rom.3.7|Ro 3:7]], [[1Cor.7.40|I Co 7:40]], al.; <span class="asgreek">κἀγώ . . . καί</span>, <span class="asitalic">both . . . and</span>, [[John.7.28|Jo 7:28]].</span>
