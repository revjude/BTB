<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Lycia</span>, a region on the southwest coast of Asia Minor, passed by Paul on his journey to Rome \(Acts 27:5\).
- A location only mentioned at [[Judg.1.26|Act.27.5]]; 
- only referred to as Lycia **<big>[[G3073|Λυκία]]</big>**.

Lycia was a region located on the southwest coast of Asia Minor \(modern-day Turkey\). In Acts 27:5, it is mentioned that Paul's ship, while on its way to Rome, sailed along the coast of Cilicia and Pamphylia and arrived at Myra in Lycia.
