Nethanel son of Zuar was the leader of the Israelite tribe of Issachar during the wilderness wanderings under Moses \([[Num.1.8|Numbers 1:8]]\). He was appointed to help take the census \([[Num.1.8|Numbers 1:8]]\) and made offerings at the dedication of the tabernacle \([[Num.7.18|Numbers 7:18]],23\). Nethanel and the men of Issachar marched under their tribal standard as part of the camp of Judah, on the east side of the tabernacle \([[Num.2.5|Numbers 2:5]]\). His name means "given of God."
