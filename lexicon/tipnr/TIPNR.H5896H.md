<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Ira</span> the Tekoite was one of King David's Thirty mighty warriors \([[2Sam.23.26|2Sa.23.26]], [[1Chr.11.28|1Ch.11.28]]\).
- A man living at the time of United Monarchy, first mentioned at [[2Sam.23.26|2Sa.23.26]]; 
- only referred to as Ira<[[H5896H]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>יר</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>\); 
- a son of Ikkesh<[[H6142]]>.

Ira the Tekoite is named as one of King David's Thirty mighty warriors in [[2Sam.23.26|2 Samuel 23:26]] and [[1Chr.11.28|1 Chronicles 11:28]]. The Thirty were an elite group of soldiers who fought valiantly for David. Ira came from the town of Tekoa in Judah, about 10 miles south of Jerusalem. [[1Chr.27.9|1 Chronicles 27:9]] mentions that Ira was in charge of a division of 24,000 men in David's army, overseeing them during the sixth month of the year. He is called the son of Ikkesh the Tekoite.
