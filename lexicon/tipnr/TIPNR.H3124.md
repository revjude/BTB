Jonah was a prophet from Gath-hepher during the reign of Jeroboam II, king of Israel \([[2Kgs.14.25|2 Kings 14:25]]\). God called Jonah to preach against the wickedness of Nineveh, the capital of Assyria \([[Jonah.1.1|Jonah 1:1]]-2\). Jonah fled by ship in the opposite direction to Tarshish \([[Jonah.1.3|Jonah 1:3]]\). A storm arose and the sailors, discovering it was Jonah's fault, threw him overboard \(Jonah 1:4-15\). God provided a great fish to swallow Jonah, and he was in its belly for three days and nights \([[Jonah.1.1|Jonah 1:1]]7\). Jonah prayed, and the fish vomited him onto dry land \([[Jonah.2.1|Jonah 2:1]]-10\). God again told Jonah to preach to Nineveh \([[Jonah.3.1|Jonah 3:1]]-2\). Jonah obeyed, and the people repented \([[Jonah.3.3|Jonah 3:3]]-9\). God relented of the disaster he had threatened \([[Jonah.3.1|Jonah 3:1]]0\). Jonah was angry that God spared the city \([[Jonah.4.1|Jonah 4:1]]-3\). God caused a plant to shade Jonah, then made it wither \([[Jonah.4.5|Jonah 4:5]]-8\). God used this to rebuke Jonah's lack of pity for Nineveh \([[Jonah.4.9|Jonah 4:9]]-11\). Jesus compared his future burial and resurrection to Jonah's time in the fish \([[Matt.12.39|Matthew 12:39]]-41, 16:4; [[Luke.11.29|Luke 11:29]]-32\).
