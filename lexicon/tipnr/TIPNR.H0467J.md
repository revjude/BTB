Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Eliphelet</span> was a descendant of King Saul from the tribe of Benjamin \([[1Chr.8.39|1Ch.8.39]]\).
- A man from the tribe of Benjamin living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.8.39|1Ch.8.39]]; 
- only referred to as Eliphelet **<big>[[H0467|אֱלִיפֶלֶט]]</big>**; 
-  son of Eleasah **<big>[[H0501|אֶלְעָשָׂה]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Ulam **<big>[[H0198|אוּלָם]]</big>** and Jeush **<big>[[H3266|יְעוּשׁ]]</big>**.

Eliphelet is mentioned in the genealogy of King Saul's descendants from the tribe of Benjamin \([[1Chr.8.39|1Ch.8.39]]\). He is listed as one of the sons of Eshek, who was a descendant of Saul's son Jonathan. This genealogy demonstrates the continuation of Saul's lineage after his death and the establishment of David's kingdom. The Bible does not provide any further information about Eliphelet's life, role, or significance within the tribe of Benjamin or the kingdom of Israel.
