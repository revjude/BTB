Meshelemiah, also known as Shelemiah, was a Levite gatekeeper who served during the reign of King David. He was the son of Kore, from the clan of Korah \([[1Chr.9.21|1Ch.9.21]]; 26.1,14\). Meshelemiah had seven sons: Zechariah, Jediael, Zebadiah, Jathniel, Elam, Jehohanan, and Eliehoenai \([[1Chr.26.2|1Ch.26.2]]\). He and his sons were assigned to guard the gates of the tabernacle \([[1Chr.26.1|1Ch.26.1]]\). Meshelemiah was specifically in charge of the East Gate \([[1Chr.26.1|1Ch.26.1]]4\). His sons and relatives, who were capable men, totaled 62 individuals serving as gatekeepers \([[1Chr.26.9|1Ch.26.9]]\).
