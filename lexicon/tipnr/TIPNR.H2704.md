Hazar-enan, meaning "village of springs," was a town situated on the northern border of the Promised Land, as described in [[Num.34.9|Numbers 34:9]]-10. It served as a boundary marker between the territories of Damascus and Hamath. The prophet Ezekiel, in his vision of the restored land of Israel, mentioned Hazar-enan as part of the northern border of the land allocated to the tribe of Dan \(Ezek 48:1\). Hazar-enan was also listed as a point on the eastern frontier of the land \(Ezek 47:17\). Its exact location is uncertain, but it was likely in the region of modern-day Lebanon or Syria.
