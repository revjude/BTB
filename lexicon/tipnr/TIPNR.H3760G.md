<small>*Carmel • Town in Judah; home of Nabal and Abigail*</small>

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Carmel</span> was a town in Judah, known as the home of Nabal and his wife Abigail, who later married David.
- A location first mentioned at [[Josh.12.22|Jos.12.22]]; 
- referred to as Carmel<[[H3760G]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>כ</span>ַּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ֶ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>\), or Carmelite<[[H3761]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>כ</span>ַּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>\), or Carmelitess<[[H3762]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>כ</span>ַּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ית</span>\), or Racal \(KJV= Rachal, NIV= Rakal\) <[[H7403]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>כ</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>\).

Carmel, also known as Racal, was a town located in the hill country of Judah \([[Josh.15.55|Joshua 15:55]]\). It is mentioned several times in the Bible, particularly in relation to David's life. Carmel was the home of Nabal, a wealthy but foolish man who refused to assist David and his men \(1 Samuel 25:2-11\). Nabal's wife, Abigail, intervened and provided for David's needs, demonstrating her wisdom and resourcefulness \(1 Samuel 25:14-35\). 

After Nabal's death, David married Abigail, and she became one of his wives \([[1Sam.25.40|1 Samuel 25:40]]-42\). Later, when David was fleeing from Saul, he sent his two wives, Ahinoam and Abigail, to stay in Carmel \([[1Sam.27.3|1 Samuel 27:3]]\). The town is also mentioned in the list of places that received spoils from David's victory over the Amalekites \([[1Sam.30.29|1 Samuel 30:29]]\). 

In the Song of Solomon, the beauty of the bride's head is compared to Mount Carmel \(Song of Solomon 7:5\), likely referring to the lush vegetation and majesty of the mountain. The prophets Isaiah, Jeremiah, Amos, and Nahum use Carmel as a symbol of fertility, beauty, and God's judgment \([[Isa.33.9|Isaiah 33:9]], 35:2; [[Jer.46.18|Jeremiah 46:18]], 50:19; Amos 1:2, 9:3; Nahum 1:4\).
