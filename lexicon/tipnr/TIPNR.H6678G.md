Zobah, also referred to as Zobah-Hamath or Hamath-zobah, was an Aramean kingdom located northeast of Israel, in the region of modern-day Syria. During the reigns of Saul and David, the kings of Zobah were enemies of Israel \([[1Sam.14.47|1Sa.14.47]]; [[2Sam.8.3|2Sa.8.3]]\). David defeated Hadadezer, king of Zobah, and his Aramean allies \([[2Sam.8.5|2Sa.8.5]],12; 10:6,8; [[1Chr.18.5|1Ch.18.5]],9; 19:6\). One of David's mighty men, Igal, was from Zobah \([[2Sam.23.36|2Sa.23.36]]\). During Solomon's reign, Rezon of Zobah established himself as king in Damascus and became an adversary to Israel \([[1Kgs.11.23|1Ki.11.23]]\). Solomon later conquered Hamath-zobah \([[2Chr.8.3|2Ch.8.3]]\). The exact extent and location of Zobah are uncertain, but it was likely situated in the region between Hamath and Damascus, possibly centered around the modern-day city of Homs in Syria.
