Joshaphat the Mithnite is mentioned in [[1Chr.11.43|1 Chronicles 11:43]] as one of David's mighty warriors. The context is a list of David's elite soldiers known for their military prowess and loyalty \(1 Chronicles 11:10-47\). These warriors played a significant role in establishing and expanding David's kingdom during his reign as king of Israel, which lasted from approximately 1010 to 970 BC. The descriptor "Mithnite" likely refers to Joshaphat's place of origin or clan affiliation, but no further details are provided about its location or significance.
