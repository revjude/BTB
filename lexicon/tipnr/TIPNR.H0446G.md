Man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Eliab</span>, son of Helon, was the leader of the tribe of Zebulun during the wilderness journey.
- A man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, first mentioned at [[Num.1.9|Num.1.9]]; 
- only referred to as Eliab **<big>[[H0446|אֱלִיאָב]]</big>**; 
- a son of Helon **<big>[[H2497|חֵלֹן]]</big>**.

Eliab, the son of Helon, is mentioned in [[Num.1.9|Numbers 1:9]]; 2:7; 7:24,29; and 10:16 as the leader of the tribe of Zebulun during the Israelites' wilderness journey. He was appointed as the representative of his tribe during the census conducted by Moses and Aaron \([[Num.1.9|Numbers 1:9]]\). Eliab's tribe, along with the tribes of Judah and Issachar, camped on the east side of the tabernacle \([[Num.2.7|Numbers 2:7]]\). As the leader, Eliab presented the offering of the tribe of Zebulun during the dedication of the tabernacle \([[Num.7.24|Numbers 7:24]],29\). He also led his tribe in the order of march when the Israelites set out from Mount Sinai \([[Num.10.16|Numbers 10:16]]\).
