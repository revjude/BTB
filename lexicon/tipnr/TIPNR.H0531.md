<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Amoz</span>, the father of the prophet Isaiah.
- Isaiah's father living at the time of Divided Monarchy, first mentioned at [[2Kgs.19.2|2Ki.19.2]]; 
- only referred to as Amoz<[[H0531]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מו</span>ֹ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ץ</span>\); 
-  father of Isaiah<[[H3470A]]>.

Amoz was the father of the prophet Isaiah \([[Isa.1.1|Isaiah 1:1]]; 2:1\). He lived during the reigns of Uzziah, Jotham, Ahaz, and Hezekiah, kings of Judah \([[Isa.1.1|Isaiah 1:1]]\). Isaiah often mentions his father's name when introducing himself or his prophecies \([[Isa.2.1|Isaiah 2:1]]; 13:1; 20:2\). Amoz was also mentioned in connection with the historical records of Uzziah's reign \([[2Chr.26.22|2 Chronicles 26:22]]\) and Hezekiah's reign \([[2Chr.32.32|2 Chronicles 32:32]]\). His name appears in the narrative of Hezekiah's illness and recovery, as Isaiah, the son of Amoz, was sent to deliver messages from God to the king \([[2Kgs.19.2|2 Kings 19:2]],20; 20:1; [[Isa.37.2|Isaiah 37:2]],21; 38:1\).
