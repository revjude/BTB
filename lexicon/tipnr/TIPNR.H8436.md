Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Tilon</span> was a son of Shimon, a descendant of Judah.
- A man from the tribe of Judah\(?\) living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.4.20|1Ch.4.20]]; 
- only referred to as Tilon **<big>[[H8436|תּוּלוֹן]]</big>**; 
-  son of Shimon **<big>[[H7889|שִׁימוֹן]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Amnon **<big>[[H0550|אַמְנוֹן]]</big>**, Rinnah **<big>[[H7441|רִנָּה]]</big>**, Ben-hanan **<big>[[H1135|בֶּן־חָנָן]]</big>**.

Tilon is mentioned briefly in [[1Chr.4.20|1 Chronicles 4:20]] as one of the sons of Shimon, along with his brothers Amnon, Rinnah, and Ben-hanan. Shimon was a descendant of Judah through Perez and Hezron. No other details are provided about Tilon's life or role in the tribe of Judah. His mention serves to trace the genealogy of Judah and demonstrate the continuity of the tribe from the time of the patriarchs to the period of the divided monarchy.
