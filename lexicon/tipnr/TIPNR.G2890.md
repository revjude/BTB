Man living at the time of the New Testament

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Quartus</span>, a Christian brother in Corinth, sent greetings to the believers in Rome through Paul's letter.
- A man living at the time of the New Testament, only mentioned at [[Rom.16.23|Rom.16.23]]; 
- only referred to as Quartus<[[G2890]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangGreek'>Κούαρτος</span>\).

Quartus is mentioned briefly in Paul's letter to the Romans as a Christian brother who sent greetings to the believers in Rome \([[Rom.16.23|Romans 16:23]]\). He was likely a member of the church in Corinth, where Paul was writing from at the time. Paul's inclusion of Quartus' greeting suggests that he was a well-known and respected member of the Corinthian church. His mention in Paul's letter indicates that he was an active participant in the early Christian community and had a connection to the believers in Rome.
