Arabian living at the time of the Patriarchs

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Tema</span> was a son of Ishmael and is associated with the ancient Arabian city of Tema.
- A man of the Arabs living at the time of the Patriarchs, first mentioned at [[Gen.25.15|Gen.25.15]]; 
- only referred to as Tema **<big>[[H8485|תֵּימָא]]</big>**; 
-  son of Ishmael **<big>[[H3458|יִשְׁמָעֵאל]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Nebaioth **<big>[[H5032|נְבָיוֹת]]</big>**, Kedar **<big>[[H6938|קֵדָר]]</big>**, Adbeel **<big>[[H0110|אַדְבְּאֵל]]</big>**, Mibsam **<big>[[H4017|מִבְשָׂם]]</big>**, Mishma **<big>[[H4927|מִשְׁמָע]]</big>**, Dumah **<big>[[H1746|דּוּמָה]]</big>**, Massa **<big>[[H4854|מַשָּׂא]]</big>**, Hadad **<big>[[H2301|חֲדַד]]</big>**, Jetur **<big>[[H3195|יְטוּר]]</big>**, Naphish **<big>[[H5305|נָפִישׁ]]</big>**, Kedemah **<big>[[H6929|קֵדְמָה]]</big>** and Mahalath **<big>[[H4258|מַחֲלַת]]</big>**.

Tema is mentioned in [[Gen.25.15|Genesis 25:15]] and [[1Chr.1.30|1 Chronicles 1:30]] as one of the twelve sons of Ishmael, Abraham's son by Hagar. He is listed among the "princes according to their nations" that descended from Ishmael. Tema is associated with the ancient city of Tema, an important oasis and caravan stop in northwestern Arabia. His descendants likely settled in or around this city.
