Ornan, also known as Araunah, was a Jebusite who owned a threshing floor in Jerusalem during the reign of King David \([[2Sam.24.16|2 Samuel 24:16]]; [[1Chr.21.15|1 Chronicles 21:15]]\). After David sinned by taking a census of Israel, God sent a plague that killed 70,000 people \(2 Samuel 24:15; 1 Chronicles 21:14\). When the angel of the Lord was about to destroy Jerusalem, God commanded the angel to stop at Ornan's threshing floor \([[2Sam.24.16|2 Samuel 24:16]]; [[1Chr.21.15|1 Chronicles 21:15]]\). David was instructed by the prophet Gad to build an altar to the Lord on Ornan's threshing floor \([[2Sam.24.18|2 Samuel 24:18]]; [[1Chr.21.18|1 Chronicles 21:18]]\). Ornan offered to give David the threshing floor and the oxen for the sacrifice, but David insisted on paying the full price \([[2Sam.24.21|2 Samuel 24:21]]-24; [[1Chr.21.22|1 Chronicles 21:22]]-25\). David purchased the threshing floor and the oxen for 50 shekels of silver \([[2Sam.24.24|2 Samuel 24:24]]\) or 600 shekels of gold \([[1Chr.21.25|1 Chronicles 21:25]]\). The threshing floor became the site where David built an altar and offered sacrifices to the Lord \(2 Samuel 24:25; 1 Chronicles 21:26\). This location later became the site of Solomon's temple \([[2Chr.3.1|2 Chronicles 3:1]]\).
