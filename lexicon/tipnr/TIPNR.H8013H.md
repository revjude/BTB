<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Shelomoth</span>, a Levite treasurer during King David's reign.
- A woman from the tribe of Levi living at the time of Divided Monarchy, first mentioned at [[1Chr.26.25|1Ch.26.25]]; 
- referred to as Shelomoth or Shelomoth \(KJV= Shelomith\)  **<big>[[H8013|שְׁלֹמוֹת]]</big>**, or Shelomoth \(KJV= Shelomith\)  **<big>[[H8019|שְׁלֹמִית]]</big>**; 
- a daughter of Zichri **<big>[[H2147|זִכְרִי]]</big>**.

Shelomoth was a Levite who served as a treasurer during the reign of King David. In [[1Chr.26.25|1 Chronicles 26:25]]-26, he is mentioned as a son of Zichri and a descendant of Eliezer, son of Moses. Shelomoth and his brothers were in charge of the treasuries for the dedicated gifts brought to the temple. These included the spoils of war dedicated by King David, the chiefs of the fathers' houses, and the commanders of the army \([[1Chr.26.26|1 Chronicles 26:26]]-28\). Shelomoth's role was to oversee and manage these treasuries.
