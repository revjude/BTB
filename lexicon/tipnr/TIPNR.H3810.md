<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Lo-debar</span> \(Debir\), a town in Gilead where Mephibosheth, Jonathan's son, lived \([[2Sam.9.4|2 Samuel 9:4]]-5\).

Lo-debar, also referred to as Debir, was a town located in Gilead, east of the Jordan River \([[Josh.13.26|Joshua 13:26]]\). It is mentioned in the context of King David's kindness to Mephibosheth, the son of Jonathan. After the deaths of Saul and Jonathan, Mephibosheth was living in Lo-debar under the care of Machir, son of Ammiel \([[2Sam.9.4|2 Samuel 9:4]]-5\). David summoned Mephibosheth from Lo-debar and showed him kindness for the sake of his father Jonathan \([[2Sam.9.5|2 Samuel 9:5]]\). Lo-debar is also mentioned in a sarcastic statement by King David, referring to it as a place of little significance \(Amos 6:13\).
