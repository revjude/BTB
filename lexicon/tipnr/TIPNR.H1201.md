<small>*Male • King of N. Israel • Third king of Israel who ruled for 24 years.*</small>

King living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Baasha</span>, son of Ahijah, became king of Israel and reigned for 24 years.
- A king of Northern Israel, living at the time of Divided Monarchy, first mentioned at [[1Kgs.15.16|1Ki.15.16]]; 
- only referred to as Baasha **<big>[[H1201|בַּעְשָׁא]]</big>**; 
- a son of Ahijah **<big>[[H0281|אֲחִיָּה]]</big>**; 
-  father of Elah **<big>[[H0425|אֵלָה]]</big>**.

Baasha was the third king of the northern kingdom of Israel, ruling for 24 years \([[1Kgs.15.33|1 Kings 15:33]]\). He came to power by assassinating Nadab, the son of Jeroboam, and then proceeded to kill off all of Jeroboam's family \(1 Kings 15:27-29\). Despite this, Baasha continued in the sinful ways of Jeroboam \(1 Kings 15:34\). He engaged in warfare with Asa, king of Judah, who bribed Ben-hadad of Syria to attack Israel \([[1Kgs.15.16|1 Kings 15:16]]-22; [[2Chr.16.1|2 Chronicles 16:1]]-6\). The prophet Jehu pronounced judgment on Baasha's dynasty, predicting its downfall \([[1Kgs.16.1|1 Kings 16:1]]-4\). After Baasha's death, his son Elah became king \([[1Kgs.16.6|1 Kings 16:6]]\). Years later, Baasha's sins were remembered alongside those of other wicked kings \([[1Kgs.21.22|1 Kings 21:22]]; [[2Kgs.9.9|2 Kings 9:9]]\). [[Jer.41.9|Jeremiah 41:9]] mentions a pit made by Baasha during his war with Asa, which was later used to dispose of bodies.
