Gershon was the firstborn son of Levi, the third son of Israel \(or 'Jacob'\) \([[Gen.46.11|Genesis 46:11]]; [[Exod.6.16|Exodus 6:16]]\). He had two sons, Libni and Shimei, who became the heads of the Gershonite Levite clans \([[Exod.6.17|Exodus 6:17]]; [[Num.3.18|Numbers 3:18]], 21; [[1Chr.6.1|1 Chronicles 6:1]]7, 20\). The Gershonites were one of the three main divisions of the Levites, along with the Kohathites and Merarites \([[Num.3.17|Numbers 3:17]]; [[1Chr.6.1|1 Chronicles 6:1]], 16\). 

During the wilderness wanderings, the Gershonites were responsible for the tabernacle curtains, coverings, screens, and related duties \([[Num.3.25|Numbers 3:25]]-26; 4:24-28\). They were counted in the census of the Levites \(Numbers 3:22; 4:38-41\) and were given wagons and oxen to transport their assigned parts of the tabernacle \([[Num.7.7|Numbers 7:7]]\). 

In the division of the Promised Land, the Gershonites were allocated thirteen cities across the territories of Issachar, Asher, Naphtali, and eastern Manasseh \([[Josh.21.6|Joshua 21:6]], 27-33; [[1Chr.6.62|1 Chronicles 6:62]], 71-76\). Notable Gershonites include Asaph, a prominent musician and psalmist in King David's court \(1 Chronicles 6:39-43\), and Jahath, a Levite leader during the reign of King Hezekiah \([[2Chr.29.12|2 Chronicles 29:12]]\). 

The Gershonites played a significant role in the transportation, setup, and maintenance of the tabernacle, and later in the music and worship of the temple. Their dedicated service, along with that of the other Levite clans, was essential to the proper functioning of Israel's religious life.
