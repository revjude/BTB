Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Joseph</span>, the son of Jonam, is listed as an ancestor of Jesus Christ in Luke's genealogy.
- A man from the tribe of Judah living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[Luke.3.30|Luk.3.30]]; 
- only referred to as Joseph<[[G2501L]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangGreek'>Ἰωσήφ</span>\); 
- a son of Jonam<[[G2494]]>; 
-  father of Judah<[[G2455J]]>.

Joseph, the son of Jonam, is mentioned in [[Luke.3.30|Luke 3:30]] as part of the genealogy of Jesus Christ. This genealogy traces Jesus' lineage through his mother Mary's family line. Joseph is listed as the son of Jonam and the father of Judah. The genealogy spans several centuries, from Jesus back to Adam, demonstrating Jesus' connection to the ancestral line of David and the fulfillment of messianic prophecies. However, no additional information is provided about Joseph's life or the specific time period in which he lived. His inclusion in the genealogy helps establish Jesus' lineage and ancestral heritage.
