Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Iphdeiah</span> was a descendant of Benjamin through Shashak \([[1Chr.8.25|1Ch.8.25]]\).
- A man from the tribe of Benjamin  living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.8.25|1Ch.8.25]]; 
- only referred to as Iphdeiah<[[H3301]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>פ</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ד</span>ְּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ה</span>\); 
-  son of Shashak<[[H8349]]>; 
- a brother of Ishpan<[[H3473]]>, Eber<[[H5677J]]>, Eliel<[[H0447I]]>, Abdon<[[H5658I]]>, Zichri<[[H2147I]]>, Hanan<[[H2605G]]>, Hananiah<[[H2608O]]>, Elam<[[H5867G]]>, Anthothijah<[[H6070]]> and Penuel<[[H6439I]]>.

Iphdeiah is mentioned in [[1Chr.8.25|1 Chronicles 8:25]] in a genealogy of the descendants of Benjamin. He was the son of Shashak. The passage lists him among Shashak's other sons: Iphedeiah, Penuel and Shuppim. No other details are provided about Iphdeiah in the biblical text. His name likely means "Yahweh redeems" in Hebrew.
