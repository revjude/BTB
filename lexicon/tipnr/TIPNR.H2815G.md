Levite living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Hasshub</span> was a Levite who lived in Jerusalem after the Babylonian exile. \([[1Chr.9.14|1Ch.9.14]]; [[Neh.11.15|Neh.11.15]]\)
- A man from the tribe of Levi  living at the time of Exile and Return, first mentioned at [[1Chr.9.14|1Ch.9.14]]; 
- only referred to as Hasshub **<big>[[H2815|חַשּׁוּב]]</big>**; 
- a son of Azrikam **<big>[[H5840|עַזְרִיקָם]]</big>**; 
-  father of Shemaiah **<big>[[H8098|שְׁמַעְיָה]]</big>**.

Hasshub was a Levite who lived in Jerusalem after the return from Babylonian exile. He is mentioned in the lists of inhabitants of Jerusalem recorded in 1 Chronicles and Nehemiah. In both passages, Hasshub is identified as a Levite, indicating his role in the religious life of the community. The Levites were responsible for various duties in the temple and in the worship services. No other information is provided about Hasshub in the Bible. \([[1Chr.9.14|1Ch.9.14]]; [[Neh.11.15|Neh.11.15]]\).
