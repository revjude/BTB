<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Ziphah</span>, a descendant of Judah, was one of the sons of Jehallelel \([[1Chr.4.16|1Ch.4.16]]\).
- A man from the tribe of Judah  living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.4.16|1Ch.4.16]]; 
- only referred to as Ziphah **<big>[[H2129|זִיפָה]]</big>**; 
-  son of Jehallelel **<big>[[H3094|יְהַלֶּלְאֵל]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Ziph **<big>[[H2128|זִיף]]</big>**, Tiria **<big>[[H8493|תִּירְיָא]]</big>** and Asarel **<big>[[H0840|אֲשַׂרְאֵל]]</big>**.

Ziphah is mentioned in [[1Chr.4.16|1 Chronicles 4:16]] as one of the sons of Jehallelel, along with his brothers Ziph, Tiria, and Asarel. They are listed in the genealogy of Judah, which traces the lineage of the various clans and families within the tribe. The inclusion of Ziphah and his brothers in this record highlights the importance of family lineage and the preservation of ancestral heritage among the Israelites. However, the text does not provide any additional information about Ziphah's life, descendants, or role within the clan.
