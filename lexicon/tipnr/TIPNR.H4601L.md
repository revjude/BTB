Woman living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Maacah</span>, Caleb's concubine, was the mother of Sheber and Tirhanah \([[1Chr.2.48|1Ch.2.48]]\).
- A woman from the tribe of Judah  living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.2.48|1Ch.2.48]]; 
- only referred to as Maacah **<big>[[H4601|מַעֲכָה]]</big>**; 
- wife of Caleb **<big>[[H3612|כָּלֵב]]</big>**; 
-  mother of Sheber **<big>[[H7669|שֶׁבֶר]]</big>**, Tirhanah **<big>[[H8647|תִּרְחֲנָה]]</big>**, Shaaph **<big>[[H8174|שַׁעַף]]</big>**, Sheva **<big>[[H7724|שְׁוָא]]</big>**.

Maacah was a concubine of Caleb, the son of Hezron \([[1Chr.2.48|1Ch.2.48]]\). She is mentioned in the genealogy of Judah, specifically in the line of Caleb. Maacah gave birth to two sons, Sheber and Tirhanah. No further information is provided about Maacah or her sons in the biblical record. Her inclusion in the genealogy highlights the complex family structures of the period and the importance of documenting lineage in ancient Israel.
