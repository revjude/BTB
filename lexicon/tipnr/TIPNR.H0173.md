Oholibamah was one of the wives of Esau, the son of Isaac and Rebekah \([[Gen.36.2|Genesis 36:2]]\). She was the daughter of Anah and the granddaughter of Zibeon, both descendants of Seir the Horite \([[Gen.36.2|Genesis 36:2]], 14\). Oholibamah bore three sons to Esau: Jeush, Jalam, and Korah \([[Gen.36.5|Genesis 36:5]], 14, 18\). She is mentioned in the genealogies of Esau, which list his wives, descendants, and the chiefs of Edom \(Genesis 36:18, 41; [[1Chr.1.52|1 Chronicles 1:52]]\). As one of Esau's wives, Oholibamah played a role in the establishment of the Edomite nation, which descended from Esau.
