<small>*Female • Believer in Rome who sent greetings to Timothy*</small>

Woman living at the time of the New Testament

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Claudia</span>, along with others, sent greetings to Timothy in Paul's letter. \([[2Tim.4.21|2Ti.4.21]]\)
- A woman living at the time of the New Testament, only mentioned at [[2Tim.4.21|2Ti.4.21]]; 
- only referred to as Claudia **<big>[[G2803|Κλαυδία]]</big>**.

Claudia is mentioned in [[2Tim.4.21|2 Timothy 4:21]] as one of the believers in Rome who sent greetings to Timothy through Paul's letter. She is listed alongside Eubulus, Pudens, Linus, and "all the brethren." The inclusion of her name suggests that she was a prominent member of the Roman church and known to both Paul and Timothy. As Paul was writing from prison in Rome, Claudia's greeting indicates her support and concern for both Paul and Timothy.
