Iye-abarim was a place where the Israelites camped during their wilderness journey, as recorded in the book of Numbers. It was located on the border of Moab, east of the Jordan River \([[Num.21.11|Numbers 21:11]]; 33:44\). The name "Iye-abarim" means "ruins of Abarim" or "ruins of the crossings." It was one of the last campsites before the Israelites entered the Promised Land.
