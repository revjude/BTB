Elishua, also referred to as Elishama, is mentioned in the lists of King David's sons born to him in Jerusalem \([[2Sam.5.15|2Sa.5.15]]; [[1Chr.14.5|1Ch.14.5]]; 3.6\). He was born after David had established himself as king over Israel and had conquered the city of Jerusalem. The name Elishua appears in the list of David's sons in 2 Samuel 5 and 1 Chronicles 14, while the name Elishama is used in the genealogy of David's descendants in 1 Chronicles 3. This suggests that the two names refer to the same individual.
