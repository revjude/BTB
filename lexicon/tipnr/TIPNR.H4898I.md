Man living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Meshezabel</span>, from the descendants of Judah, was the people's representative to the Persian king \([[Neh.11.24|Neh.11.24]]\).
- A man living at the time of Exile and Return, only mentioned at [[Neh.11.24|Neh.11.24]]; 
- only referred to as Meshezabel **<big>[[H4898|מְשֵׁיזַבְאֵל]]</big>**; 
-  father of Pethahiah **<big>[[H6611|פְּתַחְיָה]]</big>**.

Meshezabel, a descendant of Judah, is mentioned in the book of Nehemiah as the people's representative to the Persian king \([[Neh.11.24|Neh.11.24]]\). This information is provided in the context of a list of people who resettled in Jerusalem after the Babylonian exile. Pethahiah, the son of Meshezabel, was appointed to represent the people in all matters concerning the king.
