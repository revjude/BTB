Levite living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Jekameam</span> was a Levite, the son of Hebron, mentioned in the genealogies of the Levites in 1 Chronicles.
- A man from the tribe of Levi living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, first mentioned at [[1Chr.23.19|1Ch.23.19]]; 
- only referred to as Jekameam **<big>[[H3360|יְקַמְעָם]]</big>**; 
-  son of Hebron **<big>[[H2275|חֶבְרוֹן]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Jeriah **<big>[[H3404|יְרִיָּה]]</big>**, Amariah **<big>[[H0568|אֲמַרְיָה]]</big>** and Jahaziel **<big>[[H3166|יַחֲזִיאֵל]]</big>**.

Jekameam is mentioned in the genealogies of the Levites in 1 Chronicles. He was the son of Hebron, who was a descendant of Kohath, one of the sons of Levi. Jekameam is listed among the Levites who were appointed to various duties in the service of the tabernacle during the time of David. The genealogical record in 1 Chronicles emphasizes the importance of the Levitical lineage and their roles in the worship and service of God.
