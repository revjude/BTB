Leah was the elder daughter of Laban, the brother of Rebekah, Jacob's mother \([[Gen.29.16|Gen.29.16]]\). Israel \(or 'Jacob'\) agreed to work for Laban for seven years to marry Rachel, Leah's younger sister \(Gen.29.18\). However, Laban deceived Jacob and gave him Leah instead, requiring him to work another seven years for Rachel \([[Gen.29.23|Gen.29.23]]-28\). Leah bore Jacob six sons: Reuben, Simeon, Levi, Judah, Issachar, and Zebulun, as well as a daughter, Dinah \([[Gen.29.32|Gen.29.32]]-35; 30.17-21\). Despite being the less favored wife, Leah hoped to gain Jacob's affection through childbearing \([[Gen.29.32|Gen.29.32]]\). Leah accompanied Jacob when he left Laban's household and reconciled with his brother Esau \([[Gen.31.4|Gen.31.4]],14; 33.1-2,7\). She is last mentioned as being buried with Jacob in the cave of Machpelah \([[Gen.49.31|Gen.49.31]]\). Leah's sons became the ancestors of six of the twelve tribes of Israel \([[Gen.35.23|Gen.35.23]]; 46.15; 49.1-28\).
