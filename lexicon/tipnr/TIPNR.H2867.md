Man living before Israel's Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Hathath</span> was a son of Othniel, the son of Kenaz, from the tribe of Judah. \([[1Chr.4.13|1Ch.4.13]]\)
- A man from the tribe of Judah  living before Israel's Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.4.13|1Ch.4.13]]; 
- only referred to as Hathath<[[H2867]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>ֲ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ת</span>ַ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ת</span>\); 
-  son of Othniel<[[H6274]]> whose wife was Achsah<[[H5915G]]>; 
- a brother of Meonothai<[[H4587]]>\(?\).

Hathath is mentioned in the genealogy of the tribe of Judah in the book of 1 Chronicles. He was a son of Othniel, who was the son of Kenaz. Othniel was a notable figure in the tribe of Judah, known for his military prowess and for being the first judge of Israel after the death of Joshua. No further information is provided about Hathath in the Bible. \([[1Chr.4.13|1Ch.4.13]]\).
