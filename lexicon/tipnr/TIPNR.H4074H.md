Media was an ancient kingdom located in western Iran, which played a significant role in biblical history. The Medes, along with the Babylonians, conquered the Assyrian Empire in 612 BC \([[Isa.13.17|Isaiah 13:17]]; [[Jer.51.11|Jeremiah 51:11]], 28\). Later, the Medes joined forces with the Persians to form the powerful Medo-Persian Empire. This empire, under the rule of Cyrus the Great, conquered Babylon in 539 BC \([[Dan.5.28|Daniel 5:28]], 31\). The Medes are mentioned in the book of Esther as one of the peoples under the rule of King Ahasuerus \(Xerxes\) \([[Esth.1.3|Esther 1:3]], 14, 18; 10:2\). The Medes were also involved in the deportation of the Israelites from the northern kingdom \([[2Kgs.17.6|2 Kings 17:6]]; 18:11\). The prophet Daniel served in the Medo-Persian court \([[Dan.6.8|Daniel 6:8]], 12, 15; 9:1; 11:1\) and interpreted the handwriting on the wall, which announced the fall of Babylon to the Medes and Persians \([[Dan.5.28|Daniel 5:28]]\). Medes were among the peoples present in Jerusalem on the day of Pentecost \(Acts 2:9\).
