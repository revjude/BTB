Man living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Meremoth</span>, a priest who returned from the Babylonian exile and was involved in the rebuilding of Jerusalem \([[Ezra.8.33|Ezr.8.33]]; [[Neh.3.4|Neh.3.4]],21\).
- A priest living at the time of Exile and Return, first mentioned at [[Ezra.8.33|Ezr.8.33]]; 
- referred to as Meremoth **<big>[[H4822|מְרֵמוֹת]]</big>**, or Meraioth **<big>[[H4812|מְרָיוֹת]]</big>**; 
- a son of Uriah **<big>[[H0223|אוּרִיָּה]]</big>**.

Meremoth was a priest who returned from the Babylonian exile. He was among those who received the silver, gold, and vessels brought back to Jerusalem by Ezra \([[Ezra.8.33|Ezr.8.33]]\). During the rebuilding of the walls of Jerusalem under Nehemiah, Meremoth, the son of Uriah, repaired two sections of the wall \([[Neh.3.4|Neh.3.4]],21\). He is also listed among the priests who sealed the covenant with Nehemiah to follow the Law of God \([[Neh.10.5|Neh.10.5]]\). In the lists of priests and Levites who returned with Zerubbabel, Meremoth is mentioned as the head of a priestly family \([[Neh.12.3|Neh.12.3]],15\).
