Arabian living at the time of the Patriarchs

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Mibsam</span> was a son of Ishmael and grandson of Abraham \([[Gen.25.13|Gen.25.13]]; [[1Chr.1.29|1Ch.1.29]]\).
- A man of the Arabs living at the time of the Patriarchs, first mentioned at [[Gen.25.13|Gen.25.13]]; 
- only referred to as Mibsam<[[H4017G]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ב</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ש</span>ָׂ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ם</span>\); 
-  son of Ishmael<[[H3458G]]>; 
- a brother of Nebaioth<[[H5032]]>, Kedar<[[H6938]]>, Adbeel<[[H0110]]>, Mishma<[[H4927G]]>, Dumah<[[H1746G]]>, Massa<[[H4854]]>, Hadad<[[H2301]]>, Tema<[[H8485G]]>, Jetur<[[H3195G]]>, Naphish<[[H5305]]>, Kedemah<[[H6929]]> and Mahalath<[[H4258G]]>.

Mibsam is mentioned in the genealogies of Ishmael, the son of Abraham, in the books of Genesis and 1 Chronicles \([[Gen.25.13|Gen.25.13]]; [[1Chr.1.29|1Ch.1.29]]\). He was one of the twelve sons of Ishmael, who were described as "princes according to their nations" \(Gen.25.16\). The listing of Ishmael's sons demonstrates the fulfillment of God's promise to Abraham that He would make Ishmael fruitful and multiply him exceedingly \(Gen.17.20\).
