<small>*Male • Son of Israel • Son of Jacob; progenitor of the Levites*</small>

Jacob's son living at the time of the Patriarchs

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Levi</span>, son of Jacob and Leah, was the progenitor of the Levites, the priestly tribe of Israel \([[Gen.29.34|Gen.29.34]]; 49.5-7; [[Exod.6.16|Exo.6.16]]-25\).
- Jacob's son who founded the tribe of Levi  living at the time of the Patriarchs, first mentioned at [[Gen.29.34|Gen.29.34]]; 
- referred to as Levi or Levite<[[H3878]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ֵ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ו</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>\), or Levite<[[H3879]]> \(Aramiac <span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ֵ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ו</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>\), or Levi or Levite or Levitical<[[H3881]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ֵ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ו</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>\), or Levi<[[G3017J]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangGreek'>Λευΐ</span>\), or Levite<[[G3019]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangGreek'>Λευΐτης</span>\), or Levitical<[[G3020]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangGreek'>Λευ</span>ϊ<span class='tipnrLangGreek'>τικός</span>\); 
-  son of Israel<[[H3478]]> and Leah<[[H3812]]>; 
- a brother of Reuben<[[H7205]]>, Simeon<[[H8095G]]>, Judah<[[H3063G]]>, Issachar<[[H3485G]]>, Zebulun<[[H2074]]> and Dinah<[[H1783]]>; 
-  half-brother of: Dan<[[H1835H]]>, Naphtali<[[H5321G]]>, Gad<[[H1410G]]>, Asher<[[H0836]]>, Joseph<[[H3130G]]> and Benjamin<[[H1144G]]>; 
-  father of Gershon<[[H1648]]>, Kohath<[[H6955]]>, Merari<[[H4847]]> and Jochebed<[[H3115]]>.

Levi was the third son of Israel \(or 'Jacob'\) and Leah \([[Gen.29.34|Gen.29.34]]\). Along with his brother Simeon, he took revenge on the Shechemites for the rape of their sister Dinah \([[Gen.34.25|Gen.34.25]]-31\). Jacob rebuked them for this act on his deathbed \([[Gen.49.5|Gen.49.5]]-7\). Levi had three sons: Gershon, Kohath, and Merari \([[Gen.46.11|Gen.46.11]]; [[Exod.6.16|Exo.6.16]]\). His descendants, the Levites, were set apart as the priestly tribe \([[Exod.32.26|Exo.32.26]]-29; Num.3.5-13\). They were responsible for the tabernacle and its services \([[Num.1.47|Num.1.47]]-54; 3.14-39; 4.1-49; 8.5-26\). The Levites were given cities throughout Israel, but no territorial inheritance \(Num.35.1-8; [[Josh.21.1|Jos.21.1]]-42\). Notable Levites include Moses, Aaron, and Samuel. The Levites are frequently mentioned in the historical and prophetic books of the Old Testament, as well as in the genealogies of 1 Chronicles. In the New Testament, Levites are mentioned in connection with the temple and its services \(Lk.10.32; Jn.1.19; Act.4.36\). The Levitical priesthood is contrasted with the superior priesthood of Christ \(Heb.7.1-28\).
