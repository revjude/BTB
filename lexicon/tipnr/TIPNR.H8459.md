Levite living at the time of United Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Tohu</span>, also known as Nahath or Toah, was an ancestor of the prophet Samuel, mentioned in his genealogy.
- A man from the tribe of Levi living at the time of United Monarchy, first mentioned at [[1Sam.1.1|1Sa.1.1]]; 
- referred to as Tohu **<big>[[H8459|תֹּחוּ]]</big>**, or Nahath **<big>[[H5184|נַחַת]]</big>**, or Toah **<big>[[H8430|תּוֹחַ]]</big>**; 
- a son of Zuph **<big>[[H6689|צוּף]]</big>**; 
-  father of Elihu **<big>[[H0453|אֱלִיהוּ]]</big>**.

Tohu, also referred to as Nahath or Toah, is mentioned in the genealogies of the prophet Samuel \([[1Sam.1.1|1 Samuel 1:1]]; [[1Chr.6.26|1 Chronicles 6:26]], 34\). He was the son of Zuph, an Ephraimite, and an ancestor of Samuel. The genealogy in [[1Sam.1.1|1 Samuel 1:1]] traces Samuel's lineage back to Tohu, emphasizing his Ephraimite heritage. In [[1Chr.6.26|1 Chronicles 6:26]] and 6:34, Tohu is mentioned in the genealogy of the Levites, specifically the descendants of Kohath. This suggests that Samuel's family had both Ephraimite and Levitical connections.
