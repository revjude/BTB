Levite living at the time of United Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Joah</span>, a son of Obed-edom, was one of the gatekeepers assigned to guard the temple \([[1Chr.26.4|1Ch.26.4]]\).
- A man from the tribe of Levi living at the time of United Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.26.4|1Ch.26.4]]; 
- only referred to as Joah<[[H3098I]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>יו</span>ֹ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>\); 
-  son of Obed-edom<[[H5654]]>; 
- a brother of Shemaiah<[[H8098I]]>, Jehozabad<[[H3075H]]>, Sachar<[[H7940G]]>, Nethanel<[[H5417K]]>, Ammiel<[[H5988I]]>, Issachar<[[H3485H]]> and Peullethai<[[H6469]]>.

Joah is briefly mentioned in [[1Chr.26.4|1 Chronicles 26:4]] as one of the sons of Obed-edom, a Levite who served as a gatekeeper for the tabernacle during King David's reign. Obed-edom and his family were entrusted with the responsibility of guarding the temple gates. Joah is listed among Obed-edom's eight sons, who are described as capable men with the strength to do the work.
