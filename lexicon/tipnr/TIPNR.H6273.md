Othni was one of the sons of Shemaiah, a Levite from the family of Obed-Edom \([[1Chr.26.7|1 Chronicles 26:7]]\). He is mentioned in the context of the organization of the gatekeepers who were responsible for guarding the entrances to the temple and the storerooms containing the sacred items. Othni and his brothers were described as capable men with the strength to do their work \([[1Chr.26.7|1 Chronicles 26:7]]\). They were assigned to serve as gatekeepers for the ark of the covenant, ensuring its security and proper handling. The mention of Othni in this passage highlights the importance of the Levites' roles in maintaining the sacred spaces and objects in the temple.
