Man living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Meshullam</span>, a Benjamite who lived in Jerusalem after the Babylonian exile \([[Neh.11.7|Neh.11.7]]\).
- A man living at the time of Exile and Return, only mentioned at [[Neh.11.7|Neh.11.7]]; 
- only referred to as Meshullam **<big>[[H4918|מְשֻׁלָּם]]</big>**; 
- a son of Joed **<big>[[H3133|יוֹעֵד]]</big>**; 
-  father of Sallu **<big>[[H5543|סַלּוּ]]</big>**.

Meshullam is mentioned in the book of Nehemiah as one of the Benjamites who lived in Jerusalem after the Babylonian exile \([[Neh.11.7|Neh.11.7]]\). He is listed among the descendants of Sallu, along with Gabbai and Sallai, who settled in Jerusalem. This information is provided in the context of a list of people who resettled in Jerusalem and other cities of Judah after the exile. The mention of Meshullam in this list indicates that he was part of the group that returned to Jerusalem and contributed to the repopulation and restoration of the city.
