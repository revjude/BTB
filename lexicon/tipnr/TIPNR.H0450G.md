David's son living at the time of United Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Eliada</span>, also known as Beeliada, was a son of David born in Jerusalem.
- David's son from the tribe of Judah living at the time of United Monarchy, first mentioned at [[2Sam.5.16|2Sa.5.16]]; 
- referred to as Eliada<[[H0450G]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ֶ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ד</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>\), or Beeliada<[[H1182]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ב</span>ְּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ֶ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ד</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>\);

Eliada, also referred to as Beeliada, is mentioned in [[2Sam.5.16|2 Samuel 5:16]], [[1Chr.3.8|1 Chronicles 3:8]], and [[1Chr.14.7|1 Chronicles 14:7]] as one of the sons of David born to him in Jerusalem. He was born after David had established his reign and conquered the city of Jerusalem. The name Eliada means "God knows," while Beeliada means "the Lord knows." The reason for the variation in his name is not explicitly stated in the biblical text.
