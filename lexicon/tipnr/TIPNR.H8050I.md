Shemuel was a son of Tola and a leader of the tribe of Issachar, as mentioned in [[1Chr.7.2|1 Chronicles 7:2]]. He is listed among the descendants of Issachar who were described as valiant men of might and heads of their fathers' houses. The passage states that during the reign of King David, the number of these mighty men from the tribe of Issachar was 22,600. Shemuel's inclusion in this list suggests that he was a prominent figure within his tribe and a respected warrior during David's time.
