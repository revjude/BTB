Emperor living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Sennacherib</span> and Sargon were Assyrian kings who led military campaigns against Judah during the reigns of King Hezekiah and King Ahaz, respectively.
- Emperor living at the time of Divided Monarchy, first mentioned at [[2Kgs.18.13|2Ki.18.13]]; 
- referred to as Sennacherib **<big>[[H5576|סַנְחֵרִיב]]</big>**, or Sargon **<big>[[H5623|סַרְגּוֹן]]</big>**; 
-  father of Adrammelech **<big>[[H0152|אֲדְרַמֶּלֶךְ]]</big>**, Sharezer **<big>[[H8272|שַׁרְאֶצֶר]]</big>** and Esarhaddon **<big>[[H0634|אֵסַר־חַדּוֹן]]</big>**.

Sennacherib and Sargon were powerful Assyrian kings who significantly impacted the kingdom of Judah. Sargon II, mentioned in [[Isa.20.1|Isaiah 20:1]], was the king of Assyria who conquered Ashdod, a Philistine city. This event occurred during the reign of King Ahaz of Judah and served as a warning of the impending Assyrian threat. Sennacherib, Sargon's son and successor, is more prominently featured in the biblical narrative \(2 Kings 18-19; 2 Chronicles 32; Isaiah 36-37\). He invaded Judah during the reign of King Hezekiah and besieged Jerusalem. Sennacherib demanded tribute from Hezekiah and threatened to destroy the city. Hezekiah prayed to God for deliverance, and the prophet Isaiah prophesied that God would defend Jerusalem. As a result, the Assyrian army was miraculously defeated, and Sennacherib returned to Nineveh, where he was later assassinated by his own sons. These accounts demonstrate God's power to protect His people and the fulfillment of prophetic words.
