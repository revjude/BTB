Man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Bela</span> was a son of Benjamin and the head of the Belaite clan within the tribe of Benjamin.
- A man from the tribe of Benjamin living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, first mentioned at [[Gen.46.21|Gen.46.21]]; 
- referred to as Bela **<big>[[H1106|בֶּלַע]]</big>**, or Belaite **<big>[[H1108|בַּלְעִי]]</big>**; 
-  son of Benjamin **<big>[[H1144|בִּנְיָמִין]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Becher **<big>[[H1071|בֶכֶר]]</big>**, Ashbel **<big>[[H0788|אַשְׁבֵּל]]</big>**, Gera **<big>[[H1617|גֵּרָא]]</big>**, Naaman **<big>[[H5283|נַעֲמָן]]</big>**, Ahiram **<big>[[H0297|אֲחִירָם]]</big>**, Rosh **<big>[[H7220|רֹאשׁ]]</big>**, Muppim **<big>[[H4649|מֻפִּים]]</big>**, Huppim **<big>[[H2650|חֻפִּים]]</big>**, Ard **<big>[[H0714|אַרְדְּ]]</big>**, Ahiram **<big>[[H0297|אֲחִירָם]]</big>** and Jediael **<big>[[H3043|יְדִיעֲאֵל]]</big>**; 
-  father of Ard **<big>[[H0714|אַרְדְּ]]</big>**, Naaman **<big>[[H5283|נַעֲמָן]]</big>**, Ezbon **<big>[[H0675|אֶצְבּוֹן]]</big>**, Uzzi **<big>[[H5813|עֻזִּי]]</big>**, Uzziel **<big>[[H5816|עֻזִּיאֵל]]</big>**, Jerimoth **<big>[[H3406|יְרִימוֹת]]</big>**, Iri **<big>[[H5901|עִירִי]]</big>**, Addar **<big>[[H0146|אַדָּר]]</big>**, Gera **<big>[[H1617|גֵּרָא]]</big>**, Abihud **<big>[[H0031|אֲבִיהוּד]]</big>**, Abishua **<big>[[H0050|אֲבִישׁוּעַ]]</big>** and Ahoah **<big>[[H0265|אֲחוֹחַ]]</big>**.

Bela is mentioned in several passages \([[Gen.46.21|Genesis 46:21]]; [[Num.26.38|Numbers 26:38]], 40; [[1Chr.7.6|1 Chronicles 7:6]], 7; 8:1, 3\) as one of the sons of Benjamin, the youngest son of Israel \(or 'Jacob'\). He is consistently listed as the first among Benjamin's sons, suggesting his prominence within the family. In the census of the Israelites in the wilderness \(Numbers 26\), Bela is named as the head of the Belaite clan, one of the clans within the tribe of Benjamin. The descendants of Bela are also mentioned in the genealogies of 1 Chronicles, highlighting the continuity of his lineage. The repeated mention of Bela in these various contexts emphasizes his importance as a progenitor of the Benjamite tribe and the role of his clan in the history of Israel.
