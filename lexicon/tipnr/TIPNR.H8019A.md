Levite living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Shelomith</span> \(or Shelomoth\), a Levite of the family of Izhar.
- A man from the tribe of Levi living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, first mentioned at [[1Chr.23.18|1Ch.23.18]]; 
- referred to as Shelomith **<big>[[H8019|שְׁלֹמִית]]</big>**, or Shelomoth **<big>[[H8013|שְׁלֹמוֹת]]</big>**; 
-  son of Izhar **<big>[[H3324|יִצְהָר]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Korah **<big>[[H7141|קֹרַח]]</big>**, Nepheg **<big>[[H5298|נֶפֶג]]</big>** and Zichri **<big>[[H2147|זִכְרִי]]</big>**; 
-  father of Jahath **<big>[[H3189|יַחַת]]</big>**.

Shelomith \(or Shelomoth\) was a Levite who belonged to the family of Izhar, son of Kohath. In [[1Chr.23.18|1 Chronicles 23:18]], he is listed as the first of the sons of Izhar. Later, in 1 Chronicles 24:22, Shelomoth is mentioned twice as the head of the father's house of Izhar. This suggests that he was a prominent figure within the Izharite clan of the Levites during the time of King David's organization of the Levitical duties.
