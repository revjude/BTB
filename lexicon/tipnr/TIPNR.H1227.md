Man living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Bakbuk</span> was the ancestor of a family of temple servants who returned from exile.
- A man living at the time of Exile and Return, first mentioned at [[Ezra.2.51|Ezr.2.51]]; 
- only referred to as Bakbuk<[[H1227]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ב</span>ַּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ק</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ב</span>ּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ו</span>ּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ק</span>\).

Bakbuk is mentioned in [[Ezra.2.51|Ezra 2:51]] and [[Neh.7.53|Nehemiah 7:53]] as the ancestor of a family of temple servants \(Nethinim\) who returned to Jerusalem from Babylonian captivity. The temple servants were a group of people assigned to assist the Levites in the maintenance and service of the temple. The lists in Ezra and Nehemiah record the names of the families who returned to Judah to resettle and rebuild the temple.
