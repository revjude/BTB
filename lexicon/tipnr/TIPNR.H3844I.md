The Valley of Lebanon, a valley in the Promised Land conquered by Joshua \([[Josh.11.17|Joshua 11:17]]; 12:7\).
- A location first mentioned at [[Josh.11.17|Jos.11.17]]; 
- only referred to as Lebanon\_Valley<[[H3844I]]><[[H1237I]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ב</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>נו</span>ֹ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ן</span> <span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ב</span>ִּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ק</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ה</span>\).

The Valley of Lebanon is mentioned in [[Josh.11.17|Joshua 11:17]] and 12:7 as part of the territory conquered by Joshua during the Israelite conquest of the Promised Land. In [[Josh.11.17|Joshua 11:17]], it is described as extending from Mount Halak to Baal-gad in the Valley of Lebanon below Mount Hermon. [[Josh.12.7|Joshua 12:7]] lists it among the regions on the west side of the Jordan River that were allotted to the Israelite tribes.
