<small>*Male • Father of Neriah • Grandfather of Baruch, Jeremiah's scribe.*</small>

Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Mahseiah</span> was the grandfather of Baruch, Jeremiah's scribe \([[Jer.32.12|Jer.32.12]]; 51.59\).
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, first mentioned at [[Jer.32.12|Jer.32.12]]; 
- only referred to as Mahseiah **<big>[[H4271|מַחְסֵיָה]]</big>**; 
-  father of Neriah **<big>[[H5374|נֵרִיָּה]]</big>**.

Mahseiah is mentioned as the grandfather of Baruch, who served as the scribe and assistant to the prophet Jeremiah. In one instance, Baruch was tasked with writing down Jeremiah's words on a scroll and reading them in the temple \([[Jer.32.12|Jeremiah 32:12]]\). Later, Baruch accompanied Seraiah, the quartermaster, on a journey to Babylon, carrying a message from Jeremiah \([[Jer.51.59|Jeremiah 51:59]]\).
