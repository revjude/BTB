Man living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Hasshub</span>, son of Pahath-moab, repaired a section of Jerusalem's wall. \([[Neh.3.11|Neh.3.11]]\)
- A man living at the time of Exile and Return, only mentioned at [[Neh.3.11|Neh.3.11]]; 
- only referred to as Hasshub<[[H2815H]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>ַ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ש</span>ּׁ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ו</span>ּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ב</span>\); 
- a son of Pahath-moab<[[H6355H]]>.

Hasshub, the son of Pahath-moab, is mentioned in the book of Nehemiah as one of the individuals who helped repair Jerusalem's wall during the time of Nehemiah. He, along with Malchijah, repaired another section of the wall and the Tower of the Ovens. Pahath-moab was likely a family or clan name, indicating that Hasshub belonged to this group. His participation in the rebuilding of the wall demonstrates the involvement of various families and individuals in the restoration of Jerusalem. \([[Neh.3.11|Neh.3.11]]\).
