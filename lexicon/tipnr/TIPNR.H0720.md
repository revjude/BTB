<small>*Male • Son of Gad • Son of Gad and founder of the Arodite clan*</small>

Man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Arod</span>, son of Gad, was the founder of the Arodite clan \([[Gen.46.16|Gen.46.16]]; [[Num.26.17|Num.26.17]]\).
- A man from the tribe of Gad living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, first mentioned at [[Gen.46.16|Gen.46.16]]; 
- referred to as Arod<[[H0720]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ֲ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>רו</span>ֹ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ד</span>\), or Arodi or Arodite<[[H0722]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ֲ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>רו</span>ֹ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ד</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>\); 
-  son of Gad<[[H1410G]]>; 
- a brother of Ziphion<[[H6837]]>, Haggi<[[H2291]]>, Shuni<[[H7764]]>, Ezbon<[[H0675G]]>, Eri<[[H6179]]> and Areli<[[H0692]]>.

Arod was a son of Gad and grandson of Israel \(or 'Jacob'\), listed among the 70 members of Jacob's family who moved to Egypt during the famine. He was the founder of the Arodite clan within the tribe of Gad. In the census taken in the wilderness, the Arodite clan numbered 40,500 men age 20 and up, showing God's fulfillment of his promise to make Abraham's descendants as numerous as the stars.
