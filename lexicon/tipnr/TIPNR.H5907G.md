King living at the time of the Patriarchs

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Achbor</span> was an Edomite king, the son of Baal-hanan, who reigned before the establishment of the Israelite monarchy.
- An Edomite king living at the time of the Patriarchs, first mentioned at [[Gen.36.38|Gen.36.38]]; 
- only referred to as Achbor **<big>[[H5907|עַכְבּוֹר]]</big>**; 
-  father of Baal-hanan **<big>[[H1177|בַּעַל חָנָן]]</big>**.

Achbor was an Edomite king mentioned in the genealogies of Esau, the son of Isaac and the brother of Israel \(or 'Jacob'\) \([[Gen.36.38|Genesis 36:38]],39; [[1Chr.1.49|1 Chronicles 1:49]]\). He was the son of Baal-hanan and reigned as king of Edom. The genealogies in Genesis and 1 Chronicles list the kings who ruled in Edom before any king reigned over the Israelites. 

No further information is provided about Achbor's reign or his accomplishments as king. His mention in the genealogies serves to document the succession of Edomite kings and to provide historical context for the relationship between the Edomites and the Israelites.
