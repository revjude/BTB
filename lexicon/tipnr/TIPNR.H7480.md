Man living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Reelaiah</span> \(or Raamiah\) was one of the leaders who returned to Jerusalem with Zerubbabel after the Babylonian exile.
- A man living at the time of Exile and Return, first mentioned at [[Ezra.2.2|Ezr.2.2]]; 
- referred to as Reelaiah<[[H7480]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ֵ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ה</span>\), or Raamiah<[[H7485]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ַ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ַ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ה</span>\).

Reelaiah, also known as Raamiah, is mentioned in the lists of leaders who returned to Jerusalem with Zerubbabel after the Babylonian exile \([[Ezra.2.2|Ezra 2:2]]; [[Neh.7.7|Nehemiah 7:7]]\). Following the decree of King Cyrus of Persia, which allowed the Jews to return to their homeland and rebuild the temple \(Ezra 1:1-4\), a group of exiles led by Zerubbabel made the journey back to Jerusalem. Reelaiah \(Raamiah\) is listed among the leaders of this group, along with Zerubbabel, Jeshua, Nehemiah, and others. The returning exiles faced the challenge of rebuilding the temple and reestablishing Jewish worship in Jerusalem. The mention of Reelaiah \(Raamiah\) in these lists emphasizes the significant role played by various leaders in the restoration of Jewish life and faith after the exile.
