<small>*Male • Son of Ishmael • A son of Ishmael, Abraham's son.*</small>

Arabian living at the time of the Patriarchs

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Hadad</span> was one of the twelve sons of Ishmael, Abraham's son.
- A man of the Arabs living at the time of the Patriarchs, first mentioned at [[Gen.25.15|Gen.25.15]]; 
- referred to as Hadad **<big>[[H2301|חֲדַד]]</big>**, or Hadad \(KJV= Hadar, in Ben Chaim\)  **<big>[[H2316|חֲדַר]]</big>**; 
-  son of Ishmael **<big>[[H3458|יִשְׁמָעֵאל]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Nebaioth **<big>[[H5032|נְבָיוֹת]]</big>**, Kedar **<big>[[H6938|קֵדָר]]</big>**, Adbeel **<big>[[H0110|אַדְבְּאֵל]]</big>**, Mibsam **<big>[[H4017|מִבְשָׂם]]</big>**, Mishma **<big>[[H4927|מִשְׁמָע]]</big>**, Dumah **<big>[[H1746|דּוּמָה]]</big>**, Massa **<big>[[H4854|מַשָּׂא]]</big>**, Tema **<big>[[H8485|תֵּימָא]]</big>**, Jetur **<big>[[H3195|יְטוּר]]</big>**, Naphish **<big>[[H5305|נָפִישׁ]]</big>**, Kedemah **<big>[[H6929|קֵדְמָה]]</big>** and Mahalath **<big>[[H4258|מַחֲלַת]]</big>**.

Hadad is mentioned in [[Gen.25.15|Genesis 25:15]] and [[1Chr.1.30|1 Chronicles 1:30]] as one of the twelve sons of Ishmael, who was the son of Abraham and Hagar. Ishmael's sons, including Hadad, are described as tribal rulers, suggesting that they became leaders of their own clans or tribes. The listing of Ishmael's sons in these passages is part of the genealogical record of Abraham's descendants.
