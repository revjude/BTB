Man living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Bani</span> was a Levite who led prayers of confession and sealed the covenant during Nehemiah's time.
- A man living at the time of Exile and Return, first mentioned at [[Neh.9.4b|Neh.9.4b]]; 
- only referred to as Bani **<big>[[H1137|בָּנִי]]</big>**.

Bani is mentioned in Nehemiah 9:4 and 10:13 as a Levite who played a significant role in the spiritual renewal of Israel during the time of Nehemiah. In Nehemiah 9:4, Bani is one of the Levites who stood on the stairs and led the people in a prayer of confession. This prayer acknowledged the nation's sins, God's faithfulness, and the people's commitment to obey God's law. Later, in [[Neh.10.13|Nehemiah 10:13]], Bani is listed among the Levites who sealed the covenant, publicly declaring their dedication to follow God's commandments. These references highlight Bani's involvement in leading the people in prayer and covenant renewal, demonstrating his spiritual leadership within the community.
