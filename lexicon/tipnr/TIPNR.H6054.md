<small>*Male • Son of Elioenai • Descendant of King David*</small>

Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Anani</span> was a descendant of King David, mentioned in the genealogy recorded in 1 Chronicles.
- A man from the tribe of Judah living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.3.24|1Ch.3.24]]; 
- only referred to as Anani **<big>[[H6054|עֲנָנִי]]</big>**; 
-  son of Elioenai **<big>[[H0454|אֶלְיְהוֹעֵינַי]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Hodaviah **<big>[[H1938|הוֹדַוְיָה]]</big>**, Eliashib **<big>[[H0475|אֶלְיָשִׁיב]]</big>**, Pelaiah **<big>[[H6411|פְּלָיָה]]</big>**, Akkub **<big>[[H6126|עַקּוּב]]</big>**, Johanan **<big>[[H3110|יוֹחָנָן]]</big>** and Delaiah **<big>[[H1806|דְּלָיָה]]</big>**.

Anani is listed in the genealogy of King David found in 1 Chronicles 3. He is mentioned as one of the descendants of David, specifically from the line of Solomon. The genealogy traces David's lineage after the Babylonian exile, indicating that Anani lived during the post-exilic period. Apart from his mention in this genealogical record, no other information is provided about Anani in the Bible.
