Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Shelemiah</span>, the grandfather of Jehudi, who read Jeremiah's scroll to the officials.
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[Jer.36.14|Jer.36.14]]; 
- only referred to as Shelemiah **<big>[[H8018|שֶׁלֶמְיָה]]</big>**; 
- a son of Cushi **<big>[[H3570|כּוּשִׁי]]</big>**; 
-  father of Nethaniah **<big>[[H5418|נְתַנְיָה]]</big>**.

Shelemiah was the father of Nethaniah who was father of  Jehudi, a man mentioned in [[Jer.36.14|Jeremiah 36:14]]. When Jeremiah's scribe Baruch read the prophet's scroll in the temple, the officials sent Jehudi to retrieve the scroll. Jehudi then read the scroll to the officials in the chamber of Elishama the scribe. Shelemiah's role is limited to being identified as Jehudi's father, and no other details are given about his life.
