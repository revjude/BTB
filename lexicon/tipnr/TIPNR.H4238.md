- only referred to as Mahazioth<[[H4238]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ַ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>ֲ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ז</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>יא</span>ֹ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ת</span>\); 
-  son of Heman<[[H1968I]]>; 
- a brother of Bukkiah<[[H1232]]>, Mattaniah<[[H4983H]]>, Uzziel<[[H5816J]]>, Shebuel<[[H7619H]]>, Jerimoth<[[H3406L]]>, Hananiah<[[H2608P]]>, Hanani<[[H2607H]]>, Eliathah<[[H0448]]>, Giddalti<[[H1437]]>, Romamti-ezer<[[H7320]]>, Joshbekashah<[[H3436]]>, Mallothi<[[H4413]]>, Hothir<[[H1956]]>.

Mahazioth was one of the fourteen sons of Heman, a prominent musician and seer in King David's court. He was appointed by David to serve in the Temple as a musician, along with his brothers. Mahazioth and his brothers were skilled in music and were responsible for providing musical accompaniment during religious services. They were divided into twenty-four groups, each responsible for their duties in rotation \([[1Chr.25.4|1 Chronicles 25:4]], 30\).
