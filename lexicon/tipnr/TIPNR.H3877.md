<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Lotan</span>, a son of Seir the Horite and a chief of Edom \([[Gen.36.20|Gen.36.20]],22,29; [[1Chr.1.38|1Ch.1.38]],39\).
- A man living at the time of the Patriarchs, first mentioned at [[Gen.36.20|Gen.36.20]]; 
- only referred to as Lotan<[[H3877]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>לו</span>ֹ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ט</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ן</span>\); 
-  son of Seir<[[H8165B]]>; 
- a brother of Shobal<[[H7732G]]>, Zibeon<[[H6649]]>, Dishon<[[H1787G]]>, Ezer<[[H0687]]>, Dishan<[[H1789G]]> and Timna<[[H8555]]>; 
-  father of Hori<[[H2753H]]>, Hemam<[[H1950]]>.

Lotan was a son of Seir the Horite and a chief of the land of Edom \([[Gen.36.20|Gen.36.20]],29; [[1Chr.1.38|1Ch.1.38]]\). He had a sister named Timna \(Gen.36.22; [[1Chr.1.39|1Ch.1.39]]\). Lotan is listed among the descendants of Esau who settled in the region of Seir \([[Gen.36.20|Gen.36.20]]-30; [[1Chr.1.38|1Ch.1.38]]-42\). The Horites were the original inhabitants of Seir before Esau's descendants displaced them \(Deut.2.12\). As a chief of Edom, Lotan likely held a position of authority and influence among his people.
