Man living at the time of United Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Gareb</span> the Ithrite was one of David's mighty warriors, known for his military prowess.
- A man living at the time of United Monarchy, first mentioned at [[2Sam.23.38|2Sa.23.38]]; 
- only referred to as Gareb<[[H1619G]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ג</span>ָּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ֵ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ב</span>\).

Gareb the Ithrite is mentioned in [[2Sam.23.38|2 Samuel 23:38]] and [[1Chr.11.40|1 Chronicles 11:40]] as one of David's mighty warriors. The mighty warriors were a group of thirty-seven men who were known for their exceptional military skills and bravery. They played a significant role in establishing and defending David's kingdom. Gareb is specifically identified as an Ithrite, which suggests he belonged to the clan of Jether, a descendant of Kiriath-jearim \(1 Chronicles 2:53\). No further details are provided about Gareb's specific exploits or his role in David's military campaigns. However, his inclusion in the list of mighty warriors indicates that he was a highly skilled and respected soldier who demonstrated unwavering loyalty to King David.
