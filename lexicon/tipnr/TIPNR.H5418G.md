Nethaniah was the father of Ishmael, a man of royal blood who assassinated Gedaliah, the governor appointed by the Babylonians over Judah after the fall of Jerusalem \([[2Kgs.25.23|2 Kings 25:23]],25; [[Jer.40.8|Jeremiah 40:8]],14,15; 41:1-18\). Ishmael's mother was the daughter of Elnathan, an official in Jerusalem \(2 Kings 24:8\). After the murder of Gedaliah, Ishmael fled to the Ammonites. Nethaniah is only mentioned in reference to his son Ishmael.
