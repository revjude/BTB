<small>*Male • Son of Reuben • Son of Reuben; father of the Carmites*</small>

Man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Carmi</span>, the son of Reuben, was the father of the Carmite clan.
- A man from the tribe of Reuben living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, first mentioned at [[Gen.46.9|Gen.46.9]]; 
- referred to as Carmi **<big>[[H3756|כַּרְמִי]]</big>**, or Carmite \(NIV= Karmite\)  **<big>[[H3757|כַּרְמִי]]</big>**; 
-  son of Reuben **<big>[[H7205|רְאוּבֵן]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Pallu **<big>[[H6396|פַּלּוּא]]</big>**, Hezron **<big>[[H2696|חֶצְרוֹן]]</big>** and Hanoch **<big>[[H2585|חֲנוֹךְ]]</big>**.

Carmi was the son of Reuben, the firstborn of Israel \(or 'Jacob'\) \([[Gen.46.9|Genesis 46:9]]; [[Exod.6.14|Exodus 6:14]]; [[1Chr.5.3|1 Chronicles 5:3]]\). He was the progenitor of the Carmite clan within the tribe of Reuben \([[Num.26.6|Numbers 26:6]]\). The Carmites were one of the families descended from Reuben, as recorded in the census taken in the wilderness of Sinai and the plains of Moab. Carmi's inclusion in the genealogies of Reuben highlights the importance of family lineage and the organization of the Israelite tribes according to their ancestral heritage.
