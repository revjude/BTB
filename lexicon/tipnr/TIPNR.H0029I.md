Abijah, also known as Abijam \([[1Kgs.14.31|1 Kings 14:31]]\), was the second king of Judah after the division of the united monarchy. He was the son of Rehoboam and Maacah, the daughter of Absalom \([[2Chr.11.20|2 Chronicles 11:20]]\). Abijah reigned for three years in Jerusalem \([[2Chr.13.1|2 Chronicles 13:1]]-2; [[1Kgs.15.1|1 Kings 15:1]]-2\). 

During his reign, there was war between Abijah and Jeroboam, the king of Israel \([[2Chr.13.3|2 Chronicles 13:3]]\). In a battle at Mount Zemaraim, Abijah addressed the Israelites, emphasizing the legitimacy of the Davidic dynasty and the importance of the Levitical priesthood \([[2Chr.13.4|2 Chronicles 13:4]]-12\). Despite being outnumbered, Judah prevailed against Israel because they relied on the Lord \([[2Chr.13.1|2 Chronicles 13:1]]3-18\). 

Abijah expanded his power, taking cities from Jeroboam and growing stronger \([[2Chr.13.1|2 Chronicles 13:1]]9-21\). However, like his father Rehoboam, Abijah did not fully follow the Lord and allowed idolatry to persist in Judah \(1 Kings 15:3\). He walked in all the sins of his father and did not have a fully devoted heart like his ancestor David \(1 Kings 15:3\). 

Abijah had fourteen wives and fathered twenty-two sons and sixteen daughters \([[2Chr.13.2|2 Chronicles 13:2]]1\). After his death, his son Asa succeeded him as king \([[1Kgs.15.8|1 Kings 15:8]]; [[2Chr.14.1|2 Chronicles 14:1]]\). Abijah is also mentioned in the genealogy of Jesus Christ in Matthew 1:7.
