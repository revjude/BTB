Obil was an Ishmaelite who served as the overseer of King David's camels \([[1Chr.27.30|1 Chronicles 27:30]]\). He was one of the officials appointed by David to manage various aspects of his royal holdings. As an Ishmaelite, Obil likely had extensive knowledge of camels, which were important for transportation and trade in the region. His role demonstrates the diversity of David's administration and the king's willingness to employ skilled individuals from various backgrounds.
