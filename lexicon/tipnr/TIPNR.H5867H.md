<small>*Male • Son of Meshelemiah • A temple gatekeeper during David's reign*</small>

Levite living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Elam</span> was a temple gatekeeper during the reign of King David.
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.26.3|1Ch.26.3]]; 
- only referred to as Elam **<big>[[H5867|עֵילָם]]</big>**; 
-  son of Meshelemiah **<big>[[H4920|מְשֶׁלֶמְיָה]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Zechariah **<big>[[H2148|זְכַרְיָה]]</big>**, Jediael **<big>[[H3043|יְדִיעֲאֵל]]</big>**, Zebadiah **<big>[[H2069|זְבַדְיָה]]</big>**, Jathniel **<big>[[H3496|יַתְנִיאֵל]]</big>**, Jehohanan **<big>[[H3076|יְהוֹחָנָן]]</big>** and Eliehoenai **<big>[[H0454|אֶלְיְהוֹעֵינַי]]</big>**.

Elam is briefly mentioned in [[1Chr.26.3|1 Chronicles 26:3]] as one of the gatekeepers assigned to guard the thresholds of the tent of meeting during the reign of King David. The gatekeepers were responsible for protecting the entrances to the sacred areas and maintaining order.
