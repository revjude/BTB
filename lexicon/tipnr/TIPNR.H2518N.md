The <span class='tipnrhighlight'>father of Hilkiah</span>, who was the cousin of the prophet Jeremiah.
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, inferred at [[Jer.32.7|Jer.32.7]]; 
- only referred to as father\_of\_Hilkiah<[[H2518N]]> \(.\); 
-  unnamed father of: Hilkiah<[[H2518M]]> and Shallum<[[H7967R]]>.

The father of Hilkiah is mentioned in [[Jer.32.7|Jeremiah 32:7]] as the father of the prophet Jeremiah's cousin. Hilkiah approached Jeremiah with an offer to buy a field in Anathoth, their ancestral town, as a matter of right of redemption. This incident occurred during the Babylonian siege of Jerusalem, and the purchase of the field served as a symbolic act of hope and trust in God's promise of future restoration for the people of Judah.
