<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Mica</span> \(or Micaiah\), a Levite leader, served in Jerusalem after the Babylonian exile \([[Neh.11.22|Neh.11.22]]; 12.35\).
- A man living at the time of Exile and Return, first mentioned at [[Neh.11.22|Neh.11.22]]; 
- referred to as Mica<[[H4316I]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>יכ</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>\), or Micaiah \(KJV= Michaiah, NIV= Micaiah\) <[[H4320I]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>יכ</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ה</span>\);

Mica, also known as Micaiah, is mentioned in the book of Nehemiah as a Levite leader who served in Jerusalem after the Babylonian exile \([[Neh.11.22|Neh.11.22]]; 12.35\). He was a descendant of Asaph, one of the principal musicians appointed by David. In the post-exilic period, Mica is described as the leader of the Levites in Jerusalem and was responsible for the outside work of the house of God \([[Neh.11.22|Neh.11.22]]\). He is also listed among the priests and Levites who participated in the dedication of the wall of Jerusalem, playing musical instruments \([[Neh.12.35|Neh.12.35]]\). These details highlight Mica's significant role in the spiritual and practical affairs of the Jewish community in Jerusalem during the time of Nehemiah.
