<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Hashabiah</span> was the leader of the Levites during King David's reign. \([[1Chr.27.17|1Ch.27.17]]\)
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.27.17|1Ch.27.17]]; 
- only referred to as Hashabiah<[[H2811K]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>ֲ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ש</span>ַׁ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ב</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ָ֫<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>הו</span>ּ\); 
- a son of Kemuel<[[H7055I]]>.

Hashabiah was the leader of the Levites during the reign of King David. He is mentioned in the list of leaders of the tribes of Israel who assisted David in organizing the kingdom. As the head of the Levites, Hashabiah would have been responsible for overseeing the various duties of the Levites in the religious life of the nation, including their service in the tabernacle and later in the temple. No other information is provided about this Hashabiah in the Bible. \([[1Chr.27.17|1Ch.27.17]]\).
