<small>*Bamoth • Israelite encampment in Moab during the Exodus.*</small>

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Bamoth</span> was an Israelite encampment in Moab during the Exodus. \([[Num.21.19|Numbers 21:19]]-20\)
- A location near Pisgah **<big>[[H6449|פִּסְגָּה]]</big>** first mentioned at [[Num.21.19|Num.21.19]]; 
- only referred to as Bamoth **<big>[[H1120|בָּמוֹת]]</big>**.

Bamoth is mentioned in [[Num.21.19|Numbers 21:19]]-20 as one of the places where the Israelites camped during their journey through the wilderness after the Exodus from Egypt. It was located in the territory of Moab, near the Arnon River. The name "Bamoth" means "high places" in Hebrew, suggesting that it may have been situated on an elevated location.
