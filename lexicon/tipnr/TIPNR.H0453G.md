Levite living at the time of United Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Elihu</span>, also known as Eliel, was an ancestor of the prophet Samuel \([[1Sam.1.1|1Sa.1.1]]; [[1Chr.6.27|1Ch.6.27]],34\).
- A man from the tribe of Levi living at the time of United Monarchy, first mentioned at [[1Sam.1.1|1Sa.1.1]]; 
- referred to as Elihu<[[H0453G]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ֱ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>יהו</span>ּ\), or Eliab<[[H0446L]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ֱ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>יא</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ב</span>\), or Eliel<[[H0447O]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ֱ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>יא</span>ֵ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>\); 
- a son of Tohu<[[H8459]]>; 
-  father of Jeroham<[[H3395G]]>.

Elihu, also referred to as Eliel, is mentioned in the genealogies of the Kohathite Levites as an ancestor of the prophet Samuel \([[1Sam.1.1|1Sa.1.1]]; [[1Chr.6.27|1Ch.6.27]],34\). He was the son of Tohu \([[1Sam.1.1|1Sa.1.1]]\) or Toah \([[1Chr.6.34|1Ch.6.34]]\) and the father of Jeroham, who was the father of Elkanah, Samuel's father. The variations in the names \(Elihu, Eliel, and Eliab\) may be due to scribal differences or alternative names for the same individual. The Bible does not provide any further information about Elihu's life or role as a Levite.
