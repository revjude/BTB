<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Jetur</span>, a descendant of Ishmael, also a region \(Ituraea\) mentioned in the New Testament.
- A location first mentioned at [[1Chr.5.19|1Ch.5.19]];

Jetur was one of the twelve sons of Ishmael, the son of Abraham \(1 Chronicles 1:31\). His descendants likely inhabited the region known as Ituraea in the New Testament \([[Luke.3.1|Luke 3:1]]\). During the time of King David, the Hagrites, possibly descendants of Jetur, were defeated by the Reubenites, the Gadites, and the half-tribe of Manasseh \([[1Chr.5.19|1 Chronicles 5:19]]\). Ituraea was a region located in the northeastern part of modern-day Israel and southern Syria. In the New Testament, Philip, one of the sons of Herod the Great, is mentioned as the tetrarch of Ituraea and Trachonitis \([[Luke.3.1|Luke 3:1]]\).
