Man living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Paseah</span>, whose descendants were among the temple servants that returned from Babylonian exile.
- A man living at the time of Exile and Return, first mentioned at [[Ezra.2.49|Ezr.2.49]]; 
- only referred to as Paseah<[[H6454H]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>פ</span>ָּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ס</span>ֵ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>ַ\).

Paseah was the ancestor of a family of temple servants \(Nethinim\) that returned to Judah from Babylonian exile, according to [[Ezra.2.49|Ezra 2:49]] and [[Neh.7.51|Nehemiah 7:51]]. The temple servants were a group of people assigned to assist the Levites in the work of the temple. Paseah's descendants were among those who returned to Jerusalem and Judah to rebuild the temple and restore worship there after the Babylonian captivity ended in 539 BC.
