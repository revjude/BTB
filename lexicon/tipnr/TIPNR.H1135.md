Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Ben-hanan</span> was a descendant of Judah through Shimon.
- A man from the tribe of Judah\(?\) living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.4.20|1Ch.4.20]]; 
- only referred to as Ben-hanan<[[H1135]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ב</span>ֶּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ן</span>־<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>נ</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ן</span>\); 
-  son of Shimon<[[H7889]]>; 
- a brother of Amnon<[[H0550H]]>, Rinnah<[[H7441]]> and Tilon<[[H8436]]>.

Ben-hanan is mentioned in the genealogy of Judah as a son of Shimon \(1 Ch. 4:20\). He was part of the clan of Judah that descended from Perez, one of Judah's sons. No further details about Ben-hanan's life or role are provided in the biblical text. His inclusion in the genealogy highlights the importance of family lineage and the preservation of ancestral records among the people of Israel.
