Levite living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Merari</span>, the third son of Levi, was the founder of the Merarite clan of Levites \([[Gen.46.11|Gen.46.11]]; [[Exod.6.16|Exo.6.16]],19\).
- A man from the tribe of Levi living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, first mentioned at [[Gen.46.11|Gen.46.11]]; 
- referred to as Merari or Merarite<[[H4847]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>\), or Merari or Merarite<[[H4848]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>\);

Merari was the third son of Levi and the founder of the Merarite clan, one of the three main divisions of the Levites \([[Gen.46.11|Gen.46.11]]; [[Exod.6.16|Exo.6.16]],19\). The Merarites were responsible for the transportation and maintenance of the tabernacle's framework, pillars, bases, and related equipment during the Israelites' journey through the wilderness \([[Num.3.36|Num.3.36]]; 4.29,33,42,45\). They were also given specific duties in the tabernacle service \([[Num.7.8|Num.7.8]]; 10.17\). In the time of David, the Merarites were assigned various roles in the temple service, including gatekeepers, singers, and assistants to the priests \([[1Chr.6.44|1Ch.6.44]],47; 9.14; 15.6,17; 23.6,21; 24.26,27; 26.10,19\). The Merarites were allotted cities in the territories of Reuben, Gad, and Zebulun \([[Josh.21.7|Jos.21.7]],34,40; [[1Chr.6.63|1Ch.6.63]],77\). Members of the Merarite clan, such as Asaiah and Shemaiah, were involved in the reforms and restoration of temple worship during the reigns of Hezekiah and Josiah \([[2Chr.29.12|2Ch.29.12]]; 34.12\).
