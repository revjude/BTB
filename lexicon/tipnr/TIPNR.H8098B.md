<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Shemaiah</span>, a descendant of King Solomon, mentioned twice in the genealogy.
- A man from the tribe of Judah living at the time of Divided Monarchy, first mentioned at 1Ch.3.22; 
- only referred to as Shemaiah<[[H8098B]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ש</span>ְׁ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ַ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ָ֫<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>הו</span>ּ\);

-  father of Hattush<[[H2407G]]>, Igal<[[H3008I]]>, Bariah<[[H1282]]>, Neariah<[[H5294G]]> and Shaphat<[[H8202I]]>.

Shemaiah is mentioned twice in 1 Chronicles 3:22 as a descendant of King Solomon. He is listed in the genealogy of the royal line of Judah, but no additional information is provided about his life or role within the family. The repetition of his name in the verse may indicate that he was a significant figure, but the lack of details leaves his identity and contributions unknown.
