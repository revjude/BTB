<small>*Gilead • In  Manasseh • Mountainous region east of Jordan; tribal territory and personal name.*</small>

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Gilead</span> refers to a mountainous region east of the Jordan River, as well as being a personal name for several individuals in the Old Testament.
- A location in Manasseh Tribe first mentioned at [[Gen.31.21|Gen.31.21]]; 
- referred to as Gilead or Gileadite<[[H1568G]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ג</span>ִּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ד</span>\), or Gileadite or Gilead<[[H1569]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ג</span>ִּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ד</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>\).

Gilead is a mountainous region east of the Jordan River, extending from the Sea of Galilee in the north to the Dead Sea in the south. It is characterized by rugged terrain, forests, and fertile valleys. 

In the Old Testament, Gilead is often used to refer to the entire region east of the Jordan, which was allotted to the tribes of Reuben, Gad, and half of Manasseh \([[Num.32.1|Numbers 32:1]]-42; Joshua 13:24-31\). The region was known for its balm, a medicinal resin \([[Jer.8.22|Jeremiah 8:22]], 46:11\).
