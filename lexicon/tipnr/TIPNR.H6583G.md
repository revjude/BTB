Levite living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Pashhur</span>, the ancestor of a priestly family that returned from Babylonian exile.
- A priest from the tribe of Levi  living at the time of Exile and Return, first mentioned at [[1Chr.9.12|1Ch.9.12]]; 
- only referred to as Pashhur **<big>[[H6583|פַּשְׁחוּר]]</big>**; 
- a son of Malchijah **<big>[[H4441|מַלְכִּיָּה]]</big>**; 
-  father of Zechariah **<big>[[H2148|זְכַרְיָה]]</big>**.

Pashhur was the ancestor of a priestly family that returned to Judah from Babylonian exile. In [[Ezra.2.38|Ezra 2:38]] and [[Neh.7.41|Nehemiah 7:41]], the descendants of Pashhur are listed among the priests who returned to Jerusalem. [[1Chr.9.12|1 Chronicles 9:12]] mentions Adaiah son of Jeroham, the son of Pashhur, as one of the priests who settled in Jerusalem. [[Neh.11.12|Nehemiah 11:12]] also lists Adaiah the son of Jeroham, son of Pashhur, among the priests living in Jerusalem. In [[Ezra.10.22|Ezra 10:22]], descendants of Pashhur are named among those who had married foreign women, which was a violation of the Law. The Pashhur family was one of the important priestly clans in the post-exilic community in Judah.
