Jerusalem is one of the most important cities in the Bible, serving as the capital of Israel and a holy city for Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. It is mentioned hundreds of times throughout the Old and New Testaments. The city was originally known as Salem \([[Gen.14.18|Genesis 14:18]]; Psalm 76:2\) and was later called Jebus, after the Jebusites who inhabited it \([[Judg.19.10|Judges 19:10]]; [[1Chr.11.4|1 Chronicles 11:4]]\). David conquered the city and made it his capital \([[2Sam.5.6|2 Samuel 5:6]]-9; [[1Chr.11.4|1 Chronicles 11:4]]-9\). Solomon built the first temple in Jerusalem \(1 Kings 6-8; 2 Chronicles 3-7\), which became the center of Jewish worship. The city was destroyed by the Babylonians in 586 BC \([[2Kgs.25.1|2 Kings 25:1]]-10; [[2Chr.36.1|2 Chronicles 36:1]]7-19\) but was later rebuilt after the exile \(Ezra 1:1-4; [[Neh.2.11|Nehemiah 2:11]]-20\). In the New Testament, Jerusalem is the site of many events in the life of Jesus, including his triumphal entry \([[Matt.21.1|Matthew 21:1]]-11\), cleansing of the temple \([[Matt.21.1|Matthew 21:1]]2-17\), and crucifixion \(Matthew 27:32-56\). The early church also began in Jerusalem \(Acts 1-7\). Throughout the Bible, Jerusalem is referred to by various names, each highlighting a different aspect of its significance: Ariel \(Isaiah 29:1\), My Delight Is in Her \([[Isa.62.4|Isaiah 62:4]]\), Forsaken and Desolate \([[Isa.62.4|Isaiah 62:4]]\), Married \([[Isa.62.4|Isaiah 62:4]]\), the City of the Lord \([[Isa.60.14|Isaiah 60:14]]\), Zion \(Psalm 87:2\), and Mount Zion \(Psalm 48:1\). The name "Jerusalem" itself may mean "foundation of peace" or "possession of peace."
