Jezreel was a town located in the hill country of Judah \([[Josh.15.56|Joshua 15:56]]\). It is primarily mentioned in connection with Ahinoam, one of David's wives, who was from Jezreel \([[1Sam.25.43|1 Samuel 25:43]]\). Ahinoam was with David during his time in Ziklag, where he sought refuge from King Saul \([[1Sam.27.3|1 Samuel 27:3]]\). She was also one of the two wives taken captive by the Amalekites when they raided Ziklag, but David pursued the raiders and rescued her \([[1Sam.30.5|1 Samuel 30:5]]\). After Saul's death, Ahinoam went with David to Hebron, where he was anointed king over Judah \([[2Sam.2.2|2 Samuel 2:2]]\). She bore David's firstborn son, Amnon, in Hebron \([[2Sam.3.2|2 Samuel 3:2]]\). This Jezreel should not be confused with the more prominent city of Jezreel in the Jezreel Valley, associated with the kings of Israel.
