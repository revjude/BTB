Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Hakkoz</span> was a priest who served during the reign of King David, as mentioned in the priestly divisions.
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.24.10|1Ch.24.10]]; 
- only referred to as Hakkoz<[[H6976H]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ה</span>ַ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>קו</span>ֹ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ץ</span>\).

Hakkoz is mentioned in [[1Chr.24.10|1 Chronicles 24:10]] as one of the priests who served during the reign of King David. He was the head of the seventh priestly division, as organized by David. The priests were divided into 24 divisions, each responsible for serving in the temple for a week at a time, twice a year. This system ensured that all the priestly families had an opportunity to serve and that the temple services were conducted in an orderly manner.
