<small>*Male • Son of Amram • Prophet who led Israel out of Egypt*</small>

Levite living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Moses</span> was a prophet whom God used to lead Israel out of slavery in Egypt and give them the law.
- A man from the tribe of Levi living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, first mentioned at [[Exod.2.10|Exo.2.10]]; 
- referred to as Moses or "his" \(KJV= Moses\)  **<big>[[H4872|מֹשֶׁה]]</big>**, or Moses **<big>[[H4873|מֹשֶׁה]]</big>**, or Moses \(=LXX, KJV= Manasseh\)  **<big>[[H4519|מְנַשֶּׁה]]</big>**, or Moses **<big>[[G3475|Μωσεύς]]</big>**; 
-  son of Amram **<big>[[H6019|עַמְרָם]]</big>** and Jochebed **<big>[[H3115|יוֹכֶבֶד]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Aaron **<big>[[H0175|אַהֲרוֹן]]</big>** and Miriam **<big>[[H4813|מִרְיָם]]</big>**; 
- husband of Zipporah **<big>[[H6855|צִפֹּרָה]]</big>**; 
-  father of Gershom **<big>[[H1647|גֵּרְשֹׁם]]</big>** and Eliezer **<big>[[H0461|אֱלִיעֶזֶר]]</big>**.

Moses was born to Amram and Jochebed, Levites in Egypt, at a time when Pharaoh had commanded that all Hebrew male infants be killed \(Exodus 2:1-2\). His mother hid him for three months, then placed him in a basket on the Nile where Pharaoh's daughter found and adopted him \(Exodus 2:3-10\). As an adult, Moses killed an Egyptian who was beating a Hebrew and fled to Midian \([[Exod.2.11|Exodus 2:11]]-15\). There he married Zipporah and worked as a shepherd for his father-in-law Jethro \(Exodus 2:16-22\). 

God appeared to Moses in a burning bush and called him to lead Israel out of Egypt \(Exodus 3-4\). Moses, along with his brother Aaron, went to Pharaoh demanding that he let Israel go \([[Exod.5.1|Exodus 5:1]]\). Through a series of ten plagues, God compelled Pharaoh to release the Israelites \(Exodus 7-12\). Moses led them out of Egypt, through the Red Sea, and to Mount Sinai \(Exodus 13-19\). There God gave Moses the law and instructions for the tabernacle \(Exodus 20-31\). 

While Moses was on the mountain, Israel made and worshiped a golden calf \(Exodus 32\). Moses interceded for the people and God relented from destroying them \(Exodus 32-34\). Moses oversaw the construction of the tabernacle \(Exodus 35-40\). He led Israel for forty years in the wilderness, mediating between them and God \(Numbers, Deuteronomy\). God prohibited Moses from entering Canaan because of his disobedience at Meribah \(Numbers 20:1-13\). Before his death, Moses viewed the promised land from Mount Nebo \(Deuteronomy 34:1-4\). He died at 120 years old and was buried by God \(Deuteronomy 34:5-8\). 

The New Testament refers to Moses as the giver of the law \([[John.1.17|John 1:17]]\), a type of Christ \([[John.3.14|John 3:14]]\), and the greatest prophet of the old covenant \(Acts 3:22-23\).
