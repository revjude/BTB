Prophet living at the time of the New Testament

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>John</span> the Baptist was a prophet who preached repentance and baptized many, including Jesus, preparing the way for the Messiah \([[Matt.3.1|Mat.3.1]],4,13,14; [[Mark.1.4|Mrk.1.4]],6,9,14; [[Luke.1.13|Luk.1.13]],60,63; 3.2,15,16,20; Jhn.1\).

John the Baptist was a significant figure in the New Testament who played a crucial role in preparing the way for Jesus Christ. He was the son of Zechariah and Elizabeth, born miraculously in their old age \([[Luke.1.13|Luk.1.13]],60,63\). John lived an ascetic life in the wilderness, wearing camel's hair and eating locusts and wild honey \([[Matt.3.4|Mat.3.4]]; [[Mark.1.6|Mrk.1.6]]\). 

As a prophet, John preached a message of repentance and baptized people in the Jordan River \([[Matt.3.1|Mat.3.1]]; [[Mark.1.4|Mrk.1.4]]; [[Luke.3.2|Luk.3.2]]\). He emphasized the importance of bearing fruit worthy of repentance \(Mat.3.8\) and warned of the coming judgment \([[Matt.3.1|Mat.3.1]]0\). Many people, including tax collectors and soldiers, came to be baptized by him \(Luk.3.12,14\). 

John's ministry reached its climax when Jesus came to be baptized by him \([[Matt.3.1|Mat.3.1]]3-14; [[Mark.1.9|Mrk.1.9]]\). John initially hesitated, recognizing Jesus' superiority, but Jesus insisted, and John baptized Him. This event marked the beginning of Jesus' public ministry. 

Later, John was imprisoned by Herod Antipas for criticizing Herod's marriage to his brother's wife \([[Matt.4.12|Mat.4.12]]; 14.3-4; [[Mark.6.17|Mrk.6.17]]-18; [[Luke.3.2|Luk.3.2]]0\). While in prison, John sent his disciples to ask Jesus if He was the Messiah \([[Matt.11.2|Mat.11.2]]-3; Luk.7.18-19\). Jesus responded by pointing to His miracles and teachings, affirming His identity \([[Matt.11.4|Mat.11.4]]-6; [[Luke.7.22|Luk.7.22]]-23\). 

Tragically, John was beheaded at the request of Herodias' daughter \([[Matt.14.8|Mat.14.8]]-10; [[Mark.6.24|Mrk.6.24]]-28\). Jesus praised John as a great prophet and more than a prophet, the one who prepared the way for the Messiah \([[Matt.11.7|Mat.11.7]]-14; Luk.7.24-28\). 

John's disciples continued to follow some of his practices even after his death, such as fasting \([[Matt.9.14|Mat.9.14]]; Mrk.2.18; [[Luke.5.33|Luk.5.33]]\). Jesus spoke of John's greatness and his role in salvation history \([[Matt.11.11|Mat.11.11]]-13; [[Luke.16.16|Luk.16.16]]\). The early Church recognized John as the forerunner of Christ, fulfilling the prophecies of Isaiah and Malachi \(Mat.3.3; 11.10; Mrk.1.2; Luk.3.4-6; 7.27\).
