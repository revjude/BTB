Edomite living at the time of the Patriarchs

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Hemam</span> was a descendant of Seir the Horite, mentioned in the genealogies of Esau. \([[1Chr.1.39|1Ch.1.39]]; [[Gen.36.22|Gen.36.22]]\)
- A man living at the time of the Patriarchs, first mentioned at [[Gen.36.22|Gen.36.22]]; 
- referred to as Hemam **<big>[[H1950|הוֹמָם]]</big>**, or Hemam **<big>[[H1967|הֵימָם]]</big>**; 
-  son of Lotan **<big>[[H3877|לוֹטָן]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Hori **<big>[[H2753|חֹרִי]]</big>**.

Hemam is mentioned in the genealogies of Esau, the son of Isaac and brother of Israel \(or 'Jacob'\), in Genesis and 1 Chronicles. He was a descendant of Seir the Horite, the original inhabitants of the land of Seir \(Edom\) before Esau's descendants settled there \([[Gen.36.22|Gen.36.22]]; [[1Chr.1.39|1Ch.1.39]]\). The Horites were a people group that lived in the region of Seir prior to the Edomites. Hemam is listed among the sons of Lotan, one of the sons of Seir. No further details about Hemam's life or role are provided in the Bible. \([[1Chr.1.39|1Ch.1.39]]; [[Gen.36.22|Gen.36.22]]\).
