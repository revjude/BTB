Man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Mareshah</span>, a son of Mesha, was the father and re-founder of Hebron \([[1Chr.2.42|1Ch.2.42]],42\).
- A man from the tribe of Judah living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, only mentioned at [[1Chr.2.42|1Ch.2.42]]; 
- only referred to as Mareshah **<big>[[H4762|מַרְאֵשָׁה]]</big>**; 
-  son of Mesha **<big>[[H4337|מֵישָׁע]]</big>**; 
-  father and re-founder of Hebron **<big>[[H2275|חֶבְרוֹן]]</big>**.

Mareshah was a son of Mesha, son of Caleb, the son of Jephunneh, from the tribe of Judah. He restored and re-founded Hebron which had been given to his father \(Josh.14.3\) who removed the Anakim who inhabited it \(Jdg.1.20\). Mareshah is mentioned in the genealogy of Caleb's descendants \([[1Chr.2.42|1 Chronicles 2:42]]\).
