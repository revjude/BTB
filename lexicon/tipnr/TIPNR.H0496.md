<small>*Male • Son of Ephraim • Ephraimite killed by the men of Gath*</small>

Man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Elead</span> was an Ephraimite who was killed by the men of Gath.
- A man from the tribe of Ephraim living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, only mentioned at [[1Chr.7.21|1Ch.7.21]]; 
- only referred to as Elead<[[H0496]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ֶ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ד</span>\); 
-  son of Ephraim<[[H0669G]]>; 
- a brother of Shuthelah<[[H7803G]]>, Becher<[[H1071H]]>, Tahan<[[H8465G]]>, Ezer<[[H5829H]]> and Beriah<[[H1283H]]>.

Elead is briefly mentioned in [[1Chr.7.21|1 Chronicles 7:21]] as one of the sons of Ephraim who was killed by the men of Gath. The context suggests that Elead and his brother Ezer were killed when they attempted to raid the livestock of the Gathites. This incident is mentioned in the genealogy of the tribe of Ephraim, highlighting the challenges and conflicts they faced.
