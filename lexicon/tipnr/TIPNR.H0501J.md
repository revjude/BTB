<small>*Male • Son of Shaphan • Messenger who carried Jeremiah's letter to the exiles*</small>

Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Elasah</span> was a messenger who carried Jeremiah's letter to the exiles in Babylon.
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[Jer.29.3|Jer.29.3]]; 
- only referred to as Elasah<[[H0501J]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ֶ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ש</span>ָׂ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ה</span>\); 
-  son of Shaphan<[[H8227B]]>; 
- a brother of Ahikam<[[H0296]]> and Gemariah<[[H1587H]]>.

Elasah is mentioned in [[Jer.29.3|Jeremiah 29:3]] as one of the two messengers \(along with Gemariah\) who carried a letter from the prophet Jeremiah to the exiles in Babylon. The letter contained Jeremiah's message encouraging the exiles to settle down, build houses, and pray for the peace of the city where they were living. Elasah's role as a messenger indicates he was trusted to deliver this important communication.
