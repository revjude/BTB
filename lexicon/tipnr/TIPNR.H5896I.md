<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Ira</span> the Ithrite was one of King David's Thirty mighty warriors \([[2Sam.23.38|2Sa.23.38]], [[1Chr.11.40|1Ch.11.40]]\).
- A man living at the time of United Monarchy, first mentioned at [[2Sam.23.38|2Sa.23.38]]; 
- only referred to as Ira<[[H5896I]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>יר</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>\).

Ira the Ithrite is listed as one of King David's Thirty mighty warriors in [[2Sam.23.38|2 Samuel 23:38]] and [[1Chr.11.40|1 Chronicles 11:40]]. The Thirty were an elite group of soldiers who fought valiantly for David. Ira is identified as an Ithrite, indicating he likely came from the clan of Jether, who was a descendant of Esau's son Eliphaz \(Gen.36.26\).
