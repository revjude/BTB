<small>*Sidon • Phoenician city; home of Jezebel*</small>

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Sidon</span>, also called Great Sidon, was a prominent Phoenician city known for its trade and culture, and was the hometown of Jezebel, wife of King Ahab \(1 Ki. 16:31\). It is mentioned in various contexts throughout the Bible, including the Old Testament \(Gen. 10:19; Josh. 11:8; Judg. 10:6; Isa. 23:2-4; Jer. 27:3; Ezek. 28:21-22\) and the New Testament \(Matt. 11:21-22; [[Luke.4.26|Luke 4:26]]; Acts 27:3\).

Sidon was an ancient Phoenician city located on the Mediterranean coast. First mentioned in Genesis as a border marker of Canaan \([[Gen.10.19|Genesis 10:19]]\), it was named among the territories that Israel failed to conquer \([[Judg.1.31|Judges 1:31]]\). 

 The city was known for its maritime expertise. Sidonians were skilled sailors and craftsmen, particularly noted for their lumber trade. They supplied cedar for both Solomon's temple and the post-exile reconstruction \([[1Kgs.5.6|1 Kings 5:6]]; [[Ezra.3.7|Ezra 3:7]]\). Ezekiel described Sidon's mariners as skilled oarsmen \([[Ezek.27.8|Ezekiel 27:8]]\). 

 Sidon frequently appears in connection with idolatry. The city's deities, particularly Ashtoreth, influenced Israelite worship during periods of apostasy \([[Judg.10.6|Judges 10:6]]\). Solomon's Sidonian wives led him into idol worship \([[1Kgs.11.1|1 Kings 11:1]],5\), and Jezebel, a Sidonian princess, promoted Baal worship in Israel \([[1Kgs.16.31|1 Kings 16:31]]\). 

 Several prophets pronounced judgment against Sidon. Isaiah depicted it as a mourning merchant city \([[Isa.23.2|Isaiah 23:2]],4,12\). Jeremiah included it among nations to drink God's cup of wrath \([[Jer.25.22|Jeremiah 25:22]]\). Ezekiel prophesied against it, declaring God would be glorified through its judgment \([[Ezek.28.21|Ezekiel 28:21]]-22\). 

 In the New Testament, Jesus mentioned Sidon in comparing it favorably to unrepentant Jewish cities \([[Matt.11.21|Matthew 11:21]]-22\). He visited the region of Tyre and Sidon \([[Mark.7.24|Mark 7:24]],31\), and people from these areas came to hear him \([[Luke.6.17|Luke 6:17]]\). The apostle Paul visited Sidon during his journey to Rome \(Acts 27:3\). 

 The city maintained commercial relations with Herod's territories, as evidenced by the reference to Tyre and Sidon's dependence on Herod's country for food \(Acts 12:20\). 

 Probably corresponds to modern Saida in Lebanon, which has maintained continuous habitation since ancient times.
