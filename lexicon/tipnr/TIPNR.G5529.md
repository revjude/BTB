<small>*Male • Herod's steward, whose wife Joanna supported Jesus*</small>

Man living at the time of the New Testament

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Chuza</span>, Herod's steward, was the husband of Joanna, who supported Jesus' ministry. \([[Luke.8.3|Luk.8.3]]\)
- A man living at the time of the New Testament, only mentioned at [[Luke.8.3|Luk.8.3]]; 
- only referred to as Chuza **<big>[[G5529|Χουζᾶς]]</big>**; 
- husband of Joanna **<big>[[G2489|Ἰωάννα]]</big>**.

Chuza is mentioned in [[Luke.8.3|Luke 8:3]] as the steward of Herod Antipas, the ruler of Galilee. Chuza's wife, Joanna, is named among the women who supported Jesus and His disciples out of their own means. The fact that Chuza held a prominent position in Herod's court suggests that he and Joanna were wealthy and influential. Joanna's devotion to Jesus, despite her husband's role in Herod's service, indicates her deep faith and commitment.
