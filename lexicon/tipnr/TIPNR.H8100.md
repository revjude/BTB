Woman living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Shimeath</span> was an Ammonitess, the mother of Jozacar \(or 'Jozachar'\), one of the assassins of King Joash of Judah.
- A woman of the Ammonites living at the time of Divided Monarchy, first mentioned at [[2Kgs.12.21|2Ki.12.21]]; 
- only referred to as Shimeath<[[H8100]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ש</span>ִׁ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ת</span>\); 
-  mother of Jozacar<[[H3108]]>.

Shimeath was an Ammonite woman mentioned in [[2Kgs.12.21|2 Kings 12:21]] and [[2Chr.24.26|2 Chronicles 24:26]] as the mother of Jozacar \(or 'Jozachar'\) \(or Zabad\), one of the two servants who conspired against and assassinated King Joash of Judah. The biblical text identifies her as an Ammonitess, indicating that she was a foreigner from the nation of Ammon. Apart from her son's infamous act, no other details about Shimeath's life are provided in the Bible.
