Ramoth, also known as Ramoth-gilead, Galeed, Ramah, Ramath-mizpeh, Mizpah, and Jegar-sahadutha, was an important city east of the Jordan River. It was designated as a city of refuge \(Deu. 4:43; Jos. 20:8\) and a Levitical city in the territory of Gad \(Jos. 21:38; 1 Ch. 6:80\). Ramoth-gilead was the site of several battles between Israel and Aram \(1 Ki. 22; 2 Ki. 8-9; 2 Ch. 18; 22\). It is also mentioned in the story of Israel \(or 'Jacob'\) and Laban, who made a covenant there and named the place Galeed and Mizpah \(Gen. 31:47-49\). The exact location is uncertain, but it was likely an important fortress city in Gilead guarding a key trade route.
