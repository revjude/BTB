<small>*Holy Place\_2 • The inner sanctuary of the Tabernacle and Temple*</small>

The <span class='tipnrhighlight'>Holy Place</span> was the inner sanctuary of the Tabernacle and Temple.
- A location in Jerusalem<[[H3389]]> first mentioned at [[Exod.26.33|Exo.26.33]]; 
- referred to as Holy\_Place or Holy Place<[[H6944J]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ק</span>ֹ֫<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ד</span>ֶ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ש</span>ׁ\), or Holy Place<[[G0039G]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangGreek'>ἅγιον</span>\).

The Holy Place refers to the inner sanctuary of the Tabernacle and later the Temple, where the priests performed their duties. It was separated from the outer court by a veil \([[Exod.26.33|Exodus 26:33]]\). The Holy Place contained the lampstand, the table of showbread, and the altar of incense \(Exodus 31:8; 35:19; 39:1\). Only consecrated priests could enter the Holy Place \([[Exod.28.29|Exodus 28:29]],43; 29:30\). Rituals performed there included burning incense and presenting offerings \([[Lev.6.30|Leviticus 6:30]]; [[Num.28.7|Numbers 28:7]]\). The Holy Place represented God's presence among His people, though access was restricted. In the New Testament, Christ's sacrifice opened the way for believers to enter God's presence \([[Heb.9.1|Hebrews 9:1]]2; 10:19\).
