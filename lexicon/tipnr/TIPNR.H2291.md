<small>*Male • Son of Gad • Son of Gad, grandson of Jacob.*</small>

Man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Haggi</span> was a son of Gad and a grandson of Jacob, mentioned in the genealogies of Genesis and Numbers.
- A man from the tribe of Gad living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, first mentioned at [[Gen.46.16|Gen.46.16]]; 
- referred to as Haggi or Haggite **<big>[[H2291|חַגִּי]]</big>**; 
-  son of Gad **<big>[[H1410|גָּד]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Ziphion **<big>[[H6837|צִפְיוֹן]]</big>**, Shuni **<big>[[H7764|שׁוּנִי]]</big>**, Ezbon **<big>[[H0675|אֶצְבּוֹן]]</big>**, Eri **<big>[[H6179|עֵרִי]]</big>**, Arod **<big>[[H0720|אֲרוֹד]]</big>** and Areli **<big>[[H0692|אַרְאֵלִי]]</big>**.

Haggi is mentioned in [[Gen.46.16|Genesis 46:16]] and [[Num.26.15|Numbers 26:15]] as one of the sons of Gad, who was one of the twelve sons of Israel \(or 'Jacob'\). In the genealogy of Jacob's descendants who went to Egypt during the famine, Haggi is listed among the sons of Gad. The descendants of Haggi are referred to as the Haggites in [[Num.26.15|Numbers 26:15]], where they are counted as part of the tribe of Gad in the census taken in the wilderness.
