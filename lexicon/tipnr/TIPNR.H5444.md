<small>*Male • David's warrior • One of David's mighty men, a Hushathite.*</small>

Man living at the time of United Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Sibbecai</span> \(or Mebunnai\) was one of David's mighty men, a Hushathite who fought valiantly in David's battles.
- A man living at the time of United Monarchy, first mentioned at [[2Sam.21.18|2Sa.21.18]]; 
- referred to as Sibbecai or Sibbecai \(KJV= Sibbecai, NIV= Sibbekai\)  **<big>[[H5444|סִבְּכַי]]</big>**, or Mebunnai \(NIV= Sibbekai\)  **<big>[[H4012|מְבֻנַּי]]</big>**.

Sibbecai \(or Mebunnai\) is mentioned in [[2Sam.21.18|2 Samuel 21:18]], 23:27, and [[1Chr.11.29|1 Chronicles 11:29]], 20:4, 27:11 as one of David's mighty men, a group of skilled warriors who fought alongside David and played a significant role in his military campaigns. Sibbecai is described as a Hushathite, indicating his place of origin or clan affiliation. He is known for his bravery in battle, particularly in the fight against the Philistines at Gob, where he killed Saph \(or Sippai\), one of the descendants of the giants \([[2Sam.21.18|2 Samuel 21:18]]; [[1Chr.20.4|1 Chronicles 20:4]]\). In [[1Chr.27.11|1 Chronicles 27:11]], Sibbecai is listed as the commander of the eighth division of David's army, which served during the eighth month of the year. His inclusion among David's mighty men and his leadership role in the army demonstrate his valor and military prowess.
