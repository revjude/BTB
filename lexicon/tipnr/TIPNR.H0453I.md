<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Elihu</span> was one of the sons of Shemaiah, a gatekeeper of the temple \([[1Chr.26.7|1Ch.26.7]]\).
- A man from the tribe of Levi living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.26.7|1Ch.26.7]]; 
- only referred to as Elihu **<big>[[H0453|אֱלִיהוּ]]</big>**; 
-  son of Shemaiah **<big>[[H8098|שְׁמַעְיָה]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Othni **<big>[[H6273|עׇתְנִי]]</big>**, Rephael **<big>[[H7501|רְפָאֵל]]</big>**, Obed **<big>[[H5744|עוֹבֵד]]</big>**, Elzabad **<big>[[H0443|אֶלְזָבָד]]</big>** and Semachiah **<big>[[H5565|סְמַכְיָהוּ]]</big>**.

Elihu is mentioned as one of the sons of Shemaiah, who was a gatekeeper of the temple during the reign of King David \([[1Chr.26.7|1Ch.26.7]]\). Elihu and his brothers were described as capable men with the strength to do the work. They served in the role of gatekeepers, which involved guarding the entrances to the temple and ensuring that only authorized individuals were allowed to enter. The Bible does not provide any further details about Elihu's life or his specific duties as a gatekeeper.
