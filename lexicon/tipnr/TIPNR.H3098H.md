Levite living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Joah</span> \(or Ethan\), a Gershonite Levite, was the son of Zimmah in the genealogy of Levi \([[1Chr.6.21|1Ch.6.21]],42\).
- A man from the tribe of Levi living at the time of Divided Monarchy, first mentioned at [[1Chr.6.21|1Ch.6.21]]; 
- referred to as Joah<[[H3098H]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>יו</span>ֹ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>\), or Ethan<[[H0387I]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ֵ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ית</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ן</span>\); 
- a son of Zimmah<[[H2155G]]>; 
-  father of Iddo<[[H5714H]]>.

Joah \(or Ethan\) is mentioned in the genealogy of the Levites, specifically the Gershonite clan, in [[1Chr.6.21|1 Chronicles 6:21]] and 6:42. He is identified as the son of Zimmah and is listed among the ancestors of Asaph, a prominent Levitical musician in King David's time. The name Joah is used in verse 21, while Ethan is used in verse 42, suggesting that these two names refer to the same individual.
