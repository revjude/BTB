<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Likhi</span>, a son of Shemida and descendant of Manasseh \([[1Chr.7.19|1Ch.7.19]]\).
- A man from the tribe of Manasseh living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, only mentioned at [[1Chr.7.19|1Ch.7.19]]; 
- only referred to as Likhi<[[H3949]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ק</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>\); 
-  son of Shemida<[[H8061]]>; 
- a brother of Ahian<[[H0291]]>, Shechem<[[H7928H]]> and Aniam<[[H0593]]>.

Likhi was a son of Shemida, who was a descendant of Manasseh, the son of Joseph \([[1Chr.7.19|1Ch.7.19]]\). He is mentioned in the genealogy of the half-tribe of Manasseh, but no additional information is provided about his life or role in the biblical narrative. Likhi likely lived during the period of the judges or the early monarchy in Israel.
