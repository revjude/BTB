Man living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Azariah</span> was a priest who participated in the dedication of Jerusalem's wall during Nehemiah's time \([[Neh.7.7|Neh.7.7]]; 10:2; 12:33\).
- A priest living at the time of Exile and Return, first mentioned at [[Neh.7.7|Neh.7.7]]; 
- only referred to as Azariah<[[H5838X]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ֲ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ז</span>ַ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ה</span>\).

Azariah is mentioned in [[Neh.7.7|Nehemiah 7:7]], 10:2, and 12:33 as a priest who was involved in the events surrounding the rebuilding of Jerusalem's wall and the restoration of the Jewish community during the time of Nehemiah. In [[Neh.7.7|Nehemiah 7:7]], Azariah is listed among the priests who returned to Jerusalem with Zerubbabel, indicating that he was part of the first group of exiles to return from Babylon. In [[Neh.10.2|Nehemiah 10:2]], Azariah is one of the priests who signed the covenant, pledging to follow the Law of God and support the temple and its services. Finally, in [[Neh.12.33|Nehemiah 12:33]], Azariah is mentioned as one of the priests who participated in the dedication of the wall of Jerusalem, a significant event marking the completion of the rebuilding efforts and the restoration of the city's defenses. Azariah's involvement in these key events highlights his role as a prominent priest during this period of Jewish history.
