Man living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Shemaiah</span>, a Levite who led the singing during the dedication of Jerusalem's wall.
- A man living at the time of Exile and Return, only mentioned at [[Neh.12.42|Neh.12.42]]; 
- only referred to as Shemaiah **<big>[[H8098|שְׁמַעְיָה]]</big>**.

Shemaiah was a Levite who played a leadership role in the musical aspects of the dedication of Jerusalem's wall during the time of Nehemiah. In [[Neh.12.42|Nehemiah 12:42]], Shemaiah is listed among the Levites who led the singing, along with Maaseiah, Shemaiah, Eleazar, Uzzi, Jehohanan, Malchijah, Elam, and Ezer. These Levites sang loudly under the direction of Jezrahiah. Shemaiah's involvement in leading the singing highlights the importance of music and praise in the celebration and dedication of the restored wall of Jerusalem.
