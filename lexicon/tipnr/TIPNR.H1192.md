Baalath-beer, also known as Bealoth, Baal, or Ramoth, was a town located in the Negev region of Canaan. It was initially part of the territory allotted to the tribe of Judah \([[Josh.15.24|Joshua 15:24]]\) but was later given to the tribe of Simeon as part of their inheritance within Judah's territory \([[Josh.19.8|Joshua 19:8]]; [[1Chr.4.33|1 Chronicles 4:33]]\). The town was governed by Ben-hesed during Solomon's reign \([[1Kgs.4.16|1 Kings 4:16]]\). David sent gifts to the elders of Baalath-beer \(Ramoth\) after defeating the Amalekites \([[1Sam.30.27|1 Samuel 30:27]]\), suggesting that the town was friendly to him. The exact location of Baalath-beer is uncertain, but it was likely situated in the northern Negev, south of Judah.
