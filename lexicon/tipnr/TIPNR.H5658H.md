Judge living before Israel's Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Abdon</span>, the son of Hillel the Pirathonite, was a minor Judge of Israel who led for eight years.
- A Judge living before Israel's Monarchy, first mentioned at [[Judg.12.13|Jdg.12.13]]; 
- only referred to as Abdon **<big>[[H5658|עַבְדוֹן]]</big>**; 
- a son of Hillel **<big>[[H1985|הִלֵּל]]</big>**.

Abdon, the son of Hillel the Pirathonite, is mentioned in [[Judg.12.13|Judges 12:13]]-15 as a minor judge of Israel. He was from the tribe of Ephraim and succeeded Elon as a judge. Abdon judged Israel for eight years. He had forty sons and thirty grandsons, who rode on seventy donkeys, which was a sign of wealth and status in ancient Israel. Abdon died and was buried in Pirathon, located in the hill country of the Amalekites within the territory of Ephraim.
