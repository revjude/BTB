Man living at the time of the Patriarchs

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Elam</span> was a son of Shem and grandson of Noah.
- A man living at the time of the Patriarchs, first mentioned at [[Gen.10.22|Gen.10.22]]; 
- only referred to as Elam<[[H5867C]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ֵ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>יל</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ם</span>\); 
-  son of Shem<[[H8035]]>; 
- a brother of Asshur<[[H0804H]]>, Arpachshad<[[H0775]]>, Lud<[[H3865]]> and Aram<[[H0758G]]>.

Elam is mentioned in [[Gen.10.22|Genesis 10:22]] and [[1Chr.1.17|1 Chronicles 1:17]] as one of the sons of Shem, the son of Noah. Elam is listed among the descendants of Shem, who spread out into various lands after the flood, giving rise to different nations. Elam is significant as the ancestor of the Elamites, an ancient people who inhabited the region east of Mesopotamia, in present-day Iran. The Elamites developed a powerful kingdom that interacted with the Mesopotamian civilizations.
