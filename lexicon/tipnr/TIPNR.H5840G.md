<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Azrikam</span> was a descendant of David through Jehoiachin.
- A man from the tribe of Judah living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.3.23|1Ch.3.23]]; 
- only referred to as Azrikam **<big>[[H5840|עַזְרִיקָם]]</big>**; 
-  son of Neariah **<big>[[H5294|נְעַרְיָה]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Elioenai **<big>[[H0454|אֶלְיְהוֹעֵינַי]]</big>** and Hizkiah **<big>[[H2396|חִזְקִיָּה]]</big>**.

Azrikam was a descendant of King David through the line of Jehoiachin, who was taken captive to Babylon. He is mentioned in the genealogy found in [[1Chr.3.23|1 Chronicles 3:23]] as one of the sons or descendants of Neariah, who was himself a descendant of David through Solomon and the kings of Judah.
