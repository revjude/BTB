<small>*Male • Levite leader of singing and administration under David*</small>

Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Chenaniah</span> was a Levite leader in charge of singing and administration during David's reign. \([[1Chr.15.22|1Ch.15.22]],27; 26.29\)
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, first mentioned at [[1Chr.15.22|1Ch.15.22]]; 
- only referred to as Chenaniah **<big>[[H3663|כְּנַנְיָה]]</big>**.

Chenaniah was a Levite who served during the reign of King David. He had a leadership role in directing the singing when the ark of the covenant was brought to Jerusalem \([[1Chr.15.22|1 Chronicles 15:22]],27\). Chenaniah was skilled in this task and instructed others in singing to the Lord. Later, he was also appointed as an administrator over Israel's external business \([[1Chr.26.29|1 Chronicles 26:29]]\). These passages highlight Chenaniah's musical abilities and his trusted position in David's administration.
