<small>*Male • Son of Isshiah • A Levite during the reign of King David*</small>

Levite living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Joram</span> was a Levite who served during the reign of King David.
- A man from the tribe of Levi living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.26.25|1Ch.26.25]]; 
- only referred to as Joram **<big>[[H3141|יוֹרָם]]</big>**; 
- a son of Isshiah **<big>[[H3449|יִשִּׁיָּה]]</big>**; 
-  father of Zichri **<big>[[H2147|זִכְרִי]]</big>**.

Joram is mentioned in [[1Chr.26.25|1 Chronicles 26:25]] as a member of the Levitical family of Shelomoth during the reign of King David. The Levites were responsible for various duties related to the tabernacle and later the temple. In this context, the text is describing the organization of the Levites for their responsibilities, including oversight of the treasuries. Joram is listed as one of the relatives of Shelomoth who served in this capacity.
