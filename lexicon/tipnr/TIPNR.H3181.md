Man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Jahmai</span>, a son of Tola, was a descendant of Issachar and a clan head.
- A man from the tribe of Issachar living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, only mentioned at [[1Chr.7.2|1Ch.7.2]]; 
- only referred to as Jahmai **<big>[[H3181|יַחְמַי]]</big>**; 
-  son of Tola **<big>[[H8439|תּוֹלָע]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Uzzi **<big>[[H5813|עֻזִּי]]</big>**, Rephaiah **<big>[[H7509|רְפָיָה]]</big>**, Jeriel **<big>[[H3400|יְרִיאֵל]]</big>**, Ibsam **<big>[[H3005|יִבְשָׂם]]</big>** and Shemuel **<big>[[H8050|שְׁמוּאֵל]]</big>**.

Jahmai is mentioned in [[1Chr.7.2|1 Chronicles 7:2]] as one of the sons of Tola, who was a descendant of Issachar. He is listed among the clan heads of Issachar, indicating that he held a leadership position within the tribe. The biblical text describes Jahmai and his brothers as valiant warriors, suggesting that they were known for their military prowess.
