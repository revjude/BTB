Seraiah, mentioned in [[2Sam.8.17|2 Samuel 8:17]] and [[1Chr.18.16|1 Chronicles 18:16]], was a scribe who served during the reign of King David. He is also referred to as Shavsha \([[1Chr.18.16|1 Chronicles 18:16]]\), Sheva \([[2Sam.20.25|2 Samuel 20:25]]\), Sheya \([[2Sam.20.25|2 Samuel 20:25]] in some translations\), or Shisha \([[1Kgs.4.3|1 Kings 4:3]]\). As a scribe, Seraiah's primary responsibility would have been to record important documents, decrees, and correspondence for the king. He held a position of trust and significance within David's administration. The variations in his name across different passages may be due to differences in spelling or pronunciation over time.
