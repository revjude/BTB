Giant living at the time of United Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Rapha</span> was the ancestor of several Philistine giants who fought against David and his mighty warriors.
- A gigantic man living at the time of United Monarchy, first mentioned at [[2Sam.21.16|2Sa.21.16]]; 
- referred to as Rapha<[[H7497A]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>פ</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ה</span>\), or Raphaite \(KJV, ESV= "giant"\) <[[H7497H]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>פ</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ים</span>\); 
-  father of Goliath<[[H1555]]>, Ishbi-benob<[[H3430]]>, Saph<[[H5593]]>, Lahmi<[[H3902]]>, huge\_man<[[H4060G]]>.

Rapha is mentioned in 2 Samuel 21 and 1 Chronicles 20 as the ancestor of several Philistine giants who fought against King David and his mighty warriors. The name "Rapha" is used to describe these giants as descendants of the Rephaim, a people known for their great stature and strength \(Deuteronomy 2:11, 20-21\). In [[2Sam.21.16|2 Samuel 21:16]], Ishbi-benob, one of the descendants of Rapha, wielded a bronze spear and threatened to kill David, but Abishai came to David's aid and killed the giant. Another descendant of Rapha, Saph \(also called Sippai in [[1Chr.20.4|1 Chronicles 20:4]]\), was killed by Sibbecai the Hushathite \([[2Sam.21.18|2 Samuel 21:18]]\). In [[2Sam.21.20|2 Samuel 21:20]] and [[1Chr.20.6|1 Chronicles 20:6]], another descendant of Rapha is described as a huge man with six fingers on each hand and six toes on each foot. This giant was killed by Jonathan, the son of Shimea, David's brother \(2 Samuel 21:21; 1 Chronicles 20:7\). These battles against the descendants of Rapha are summarized in [[2Sam.21.22|2 Samuel 21:22]] and [[1Chr.20.8|1 Chronicles 20:8]], emphasizing that they were defeated by David and his servants. The mention of Rapha and his descendants highlights the ongoing conflict between the Israelites and the Philistines, as well as the bravery and skill of David and his warriors in facing and defeating these formidable enemies. The victory over the giants also demonstrates God's faithfulness in fulfilling His promise to give the Israelites victory over their enemies and to establish David's kingdom.
