Man living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Jonathan</span>, of the descendants of Adaiah, was among those who returned to Jerusalem from Babylonian exile.
- A man living at the time of Exile and Return, only mentioned at [[Ezra.8.6|Ezr.8.6]]; 
- only referred to as Jonathan<[[H3129H]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>יו</span>ֹ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>נ</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ת</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ן</span>\); 
-  father of Ebed<[[H5651H]]>.

Jonathan was one of the descendants of Adaiah who returned to Jerusalem and Judah from Babylonian exile, as recorded in [[Ezra.8.6|Ezra 8:6]]. He was part of the group that traveled with Ezra the scribe back to Jerusalem during the reign of King Artaxerxes of Persia, probably around 458 BC. The returning exiles sought to re-establish Jewish life and worship in the homeland after the Babylonian captivity.
