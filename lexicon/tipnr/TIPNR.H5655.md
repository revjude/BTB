<small>*Male • Father of Shelemiah • Father of Shelemiah, an official under King Jehoiakim*</small>

Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Abdeel</span> was the father of Shelemiah, an official sent by King Jehoiakim to arrest Baruch and Jeremiah.
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[Jer.36.26|Jer.36.26]]; 
- only referred to as Abdeel<[[H5655]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ַ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ב</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ד</span>ְּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ֵ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>\); 
-  father of Shelemiah<[[H8018L]]>.

Abdeel is mentioned in [[Jer.36.26|Jeremiah 36:26]] as the father of Shelemiah, an official during the reign of King Jehoiakim of Judah. When Jeremiah's scribe, Baruch, read the prophet's warnings in the temple, King Jehoiakim ordered Jerahmeel, Seraiah, and Shelemiah to arrest both Baruch and Jeremiah. However, the Lord hid them from the officials.
