Man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Eliasaph</span>, the son of Deuel, was the leader of the tribe of Gad during the wilderness journey.
- A man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, first mentioned at [[Num.1.14|Num.1.14]]; 
- only referred to as Eliasaph<[[H0460G]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ֶ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ס</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ף</span>\); 
-  son of Deuel<[[H1845]]>.

Eliasaph, the son of Deuel \(or Reuel\), is mentioned in [[Num.1.14|Numbers 1:14]]; 2:14; 7:42,47; and 10:20 as the leader of the tribe of Gad during the Israelites' wilderness journey. He was appointed as the representative of his tribe during the census conducted by Moses and Aaron \([[Num.1.14|Numbers 1:14]]\). Eliasaph's tribe, along with the tribes of Reuben and Simeon, camped on the south side of the tabernacle \([[Num.2.14|Numbers 2:14]]\). As the leader, Eliasaph presented the offering of the tribe of Gad during the dedication of the tabernacle \([[Num.7.42|Numbers 7:42]],47\). He also led his tribe in the order of march when the Israelites set out from Mount Sinai \([[Num.10.20|Numbers 10:20]]\).
