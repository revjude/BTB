Ahiezer, the son of Ammishaddai, was a prominent leader from the tribe of Dan during the Israelites' journey through the wilderness. He was appointed by God as the head of his tribe and played a significant role in the census and organization of the Israelite camp. \([[Num.1.12|Num.1.12]]; 2.25\) 

Ahiezer's tribe was positioned on the north side of the tabernacle, along with the tribes of Asher and Naphtali. \([[Num.2.25|Num.2.25]]\) He presented his tribe's offering at the dedication of the tabernacle, which included a silver plate, a silver basin, and various animals for sacrifice. \([[Num.7.66|Num.7.66]],71\) 

As a tribal leader, Ahiezer was responsible for leading the Danites in their journey and ensuring their obedience to God's commands. \([[Num.10.25|Num.10.25]]\)
