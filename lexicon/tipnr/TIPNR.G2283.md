<small>*Female • Judah's daughter-in-law who bore Perez and Zerah*</small>

Woman living at the time of the Patriarchs

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Tamar</span> was Judah's daughter-in-law who, after being wronged by him, bore twin sons Perez and Zerah.
- A woman living at the time of the Patriarchs, first mentioned at [[Gen.38.6|Gen.38.6]]; 
- referred to as Tamar **<big>[[H8559|תָּמָר]]</big>**, or Tamar \(KJV= Thamar\)  **<big>[[G2283|Θάμαρ]]</big>**; 
- wife of Judah **<big>[[H3063|יְהוּדָה]]</big>** and Er **<big>[[H6147|עֵר]]</big>**; 
-  mother of Perez **<big>[[H6557|פֶּרֶץ]]</big>** and Zerah **<big>[[H2226|זֶרַח]]</big>**.

Tamar was the daughter-in-law of Judah, married first to his son Er and then to Onan \([[Gen.38.6|Genesis 38:6]]-11\). After being wronged by Judah, Tamar disguised herself as a prostitute and conceived twins by him \([[Gen.38.13|Genesis 38:13]]-24\). She gave birth to Perez and Zerah, who became ancestors of the tribe of Judah \(Genesis 38:29-30; Ruth 4:12; [[1Chr.2.4|1 Chronicles 2:4]]\). Tamar is praised for her righteousness in contrast to Judah \(Genesis 38:26\). She is mentioned in the genealogy of Jesus \([[Matt.1.3|Matthew 1:3]]\).
