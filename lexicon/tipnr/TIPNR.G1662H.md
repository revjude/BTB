<small>*Male • Son of Melea • Ancestor of Jesus in Luke's genealogy*</small>

Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Eliakim</span> was an ancestor of Jesus mentioned in Luke's genealogy.
- A man from the tribe of Judah living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[Luke.3.30|Luk.3.30]]; 
- only referred to as Eliakim **<big>[[G1662|Ἐλιακείμ]]</big>**; 
- a son of Melea **<big>[[G3190|Μελεᾶς]]</big>**; 
-  father of Jonam **<big>[[G2494|Ἰωνάν]]</big>**.

Eliakim is mentioned in [[Luke.3.30|Luke 3:30]] in the genealogy of Jesus Christ. He was the son of Melea and the father of Jonam. Eliakim is listed in the genealogy that traces Jesus' lineage through his mother, Mary, all the way back to Adam. This genealogy emphasizes Jesus' descent from Adam, the first man, and his relationship to all humanity.
