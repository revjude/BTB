Levite living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Jehdeiah</span> was a Levite descendant of Amram who served during the reign of King David.
- A man from the tribe of Levi living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.24.20|1Ch.24.20]]; 
- only referred to as Jehdeiah<[[H3165G]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ֶ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ד</span>ְּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ָ֫<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>הו</span>ּ\); 
- a son of Shebuel<[[H7619G]]>.

Jehdeiah is mentioned in [[1Chr.24.20|1 Chronicles 24:20]] as a descendant of Amram, who was a Levite. This chapter describes the divisions of the Levites and their duties during the reign of King David. Jehdeiah is listed among the Levites who were appointed to serve in the temple. The text does not provide any further details about Jehdeiah's specific role or responsibilities. However, his inclusion in this list suggests that he was a member of the Levitical family and participated in the various tasks assigned to the Levites in the temple service.
