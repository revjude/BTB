Man living at the time of United Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Ahiam</span>, son of Sharar the Hararite, was one of David's mighty men. \([[2Sam.23.33|2Sa.23.33]]; [[1Chr.11.35|1Ch.11.35]]\)
- A man living at the time of United Monarchy, first mentioned at [[2Sam.23.33|2Sa.23.33]]; 
- only referred to as Ahiam **<big>[[H0279|אֲחִיאָם]]</big>**; 
- a son of Sharar **<big>[[H8325|שָׁרַר]]</big>**.

Ahiam was one of the mighty men who served under King David. He was the son of Sharar the Hararite. The mighty men were a group of skilled warriors known for their bravery and military prowess. They played a significant role in David's military campaigns and helped establish and defend his kingdom. Ahiam is mentioned in the lists of David's mighty men found in both 2 Samuel and 1 Chronicles, but no specific details about his individual exploits are recorded in the Bible. \([[2Sam.23.33|2Sa.23.33]]; [[1Chr.11.35|1Ch.11.35]]\).
