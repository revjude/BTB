<small>*Male • Son of Hoshaiah • A leader of the Judean forces*</small>

Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Jezaniah</span>, also known as Azariah, was a leader of the Judean forces after the fall of Jerusalem \([[Jer.40.8|Jer.40.8]]; 42.1; 43.2\).
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, first mentioned at [[2Kgs.25.23|2Ki.25.23]]; 
- referred to as Jezaniah **<big>[[H3153|יְזַנְיָה]]</big>**, or Jezaniah or Jaazaniah **<big>[[H2970|יַאֲזַנְיָה]]</big>**, or Azariah **<big>[[H5838|עֲזַרְיָה]]</big>**; 
- a son of Hoshaiah **<big>[[H1955|הוֹשַׁעְיָה]]</big>**.

Jezaniah, also referred to as Azariah, was one of the leaders of the Judean forces who survived the Babylonian destruction of Jerusalem. In [[Jer.40.8|Jeremiah 40:8]], he is mentioned among the commanders who came to Gedaliah, the appointed governor of Judah, at Mizpah. Later, in [[Jer.42.1|Jeremiah 42:1]], Jezaniah and the other leaders approached Jeremiah to seek guidance from the Lord regarding their plan to flee to Egypt. However, when Jeremiah delivered God's message, warning them not to go to Egypt, Jezaniah \(called Azariah in this passage\) and another leader, Johanan, accused Jeremiah of falsely prophesying and led the people to Egypt despite the divine warning \([[Jer.43.2|Jer.43.2]]\).
