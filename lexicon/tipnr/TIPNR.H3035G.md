Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Iddo</span>, the father of Zechariah, was a leader of the half-tribe of Manasseh during King David's reign.
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.27.21|1Ch.27.21]]; 
- only referred to as Iddo **<big>[[H3035|יִדּוֹ]]</big>**; 
- a son of Zechariah **<big>[[H2148|זְכַרְיָה]]</big>**.

Iddo is mentioned in [[1Chr.27.21|1 Chronicles 27:21]] as the father of Zechariah, who was a leader of the half-tribe of Manasseh during King David's reign. Zechariah was appointed as the representative of the half-tribe of Manasseh in David's administration, which was organized according to the tribes of Israel \(1 Chronicles 27:16-22\). Iddo's mention in this context suggests that he was a prominent figure in the tribe of Manasseh.
