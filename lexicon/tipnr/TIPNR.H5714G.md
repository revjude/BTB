Man living at the time of United Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Iddo</span>, the son of Abinadab, was an official in Mahanaim during King Solomon's reign.
- A man living at the time of United Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Kgs.4.14|1Ki.4.14]]; 
- only referred to as Iddo **<big>[[H5714|עִדּוֹ]]</big>**; 
-  father of Ahinadab **<big>[[H0292|אֲחִינָדָב]]</big>**.

Iddo, the son of Abinadab, is mentioned in [[1Kgs.4.14|1 Kings 4:14]] as one of the twelve officials appointed by King Solomon to oversee the provision of food for the king and his household. Iddo was responsible for the region of Mahanaim, which was located east of the Jordan River. Each official was assigned a specific month to provide the necessary supplies for the royal court \(1 Kings 4:7\).
