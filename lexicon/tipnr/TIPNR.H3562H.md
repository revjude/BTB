The father of Conaniah is mentioned in [[2Chr.31.12|2 Chronicles 31:12]] as the father of a Levite named Conaniah, who served during the reign of King Hezekiah. Conaniah and his brother Shimei were appointed to oversee the contributions, tithes, and dedicated gifts brought to the temple. They were entrusted with the responsibility of ensuring the proper distribution of these offerings among the priests and Levites. Although the name of Conaniah's father is not stated, his son's position indicates that he likely belonged to a family of Levites dedicated to temple service.
