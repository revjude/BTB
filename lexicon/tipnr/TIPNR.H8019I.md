Woman living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Shelomith</span>, a son of Zerubbabel, the governor of Judah after the exile.
- A woman from the tribe of Judah living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.3.19|1Ch.3.19]]; 
- only referred to as Shelomith **<big>[[H8019|שְׁלֹמִית]]</big>**; 
-  daughter of Zerubbabel **<big>[[H2216|זְרֻבָּבֶל]]</big>**; 
- a sister of Meshullam **<big>[[H4918|מְשֻׁלָּם]]</big>**, Hananiah **<big>[[H2608|חֲנַנְיָה]]</big>**, Hashubah **<big>[[H2807|חֲשֻׁבָה]]</big>**, Ohel **<big>[[H0169|אֹהֶל]]</big>**, Berechiah **<big>[[H1296|בֶּרֶכְיָה]]</big>**, Hasadiah **<big>[[H2619|חֲסַדְיָה]]</big>**, Jushab-hesed **<big>[[H3142|יוּשַׁב חֶסֶד]]</big>**, Abiud **<big>[[G0010|Ἀβιούδ]]</big>**.

Shelomith is listed in [[1Chr.3.19|1 Chronicles 3:19]] as one of the sons of Zerubbabel. Zerubbabel was the grandson of King Jehoiachin of Judah and served as the governor of Judah during the return from Babylonian exile. He played a key role in rebuilding the temple in Jerusalem. Shelomith is mentioned along with his brothers Meshullam, Hananiah, Hashubah, Ohel, Berechiah, Hasadiah, and Jushab-Hesed.
