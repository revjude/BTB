Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Neriah</span> was the father of Baruch, Jeremiah's scribe.
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, first mentioned at [[Jer.32.12|Jer.32.12]]; 
- only referred to as Neriah **<big>[[H5374|נֵרִיָּה]]</big>**; 
- a son of Mahseiah **<big>[[H4271|מַחְסֵיָה]]</big>**; 
-  father of Baruch **<big>[[H1263|בָּרוּךְ]]</big>**, Seraiah **<big>[[H8304|שְׂרָיָה]]</big>**.

Neriah was the father of Baruch, who served as the scribe and assistant to the prophet Jeremiah \([[Jer.32.12|Jeremiah 32:12]]\). Baruch wrote down the prophecies that Jeremiah dictated to him \([[Jer.36.4|Jeremiah 36:4]]\). When King Jehoiakim burned the scroll containing these prophecies, Neriah's son Baruch rewrote them as Jeremiah dictated again \([[Jer.36.32|Jeremiah 36:32]]\). Neriah's grandson Seraiah also served Jeremiah \([[Jer.51.59|Jeremiah 51:59]]\). The name Neriah means "lamp of Yahweh" in Hebrew.
