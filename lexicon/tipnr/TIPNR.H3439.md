<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Jeshohaiah</span> was a leader from the tribe of Simeon during the time of King Hezekiah.
- A man from the tribe of Simeon\(?\) living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.4.36|1Ch.4.36]]; 
- only referred to as Jeshohaiah<[[H3439]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ש</span>ׁ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ו</span>ֹ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ה</span>\).

Jeshohaiah is mentioned in the genealogy of the tribe of Simeon in 1 Chronicles. He is listed among the leaders of the Simeonites during the reign of King Hezekiah. The Simeonites were one of the twelve tribes of Israel, and they were known for their warriors and their role in the conquest of Canaan. The mention of Jeshohaiah in this context highlights the importance of tribal leadership and the participation of the Simeonites in the events of Hezekiah's reign.
