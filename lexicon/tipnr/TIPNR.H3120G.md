Man living at the time of the Patriarchs

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Javan</span>, the ancestor of the Greek people, was a son of Japheth and grandson of Noah.
- A man living at the time of the Patriarchs, first mentioned at [[Gen.10.2|Gen.10.2]]; 
- referred to as Javan **<big>[[H3120|יָוָן]]</big>**, or Greek **<big>[[H3125|יְוָנִי]]</big>**, Gentile or Greek \(NIV= Gentile\)  **<big>[[G1672|Ἕλλην]]</big>**, or Gentile \(KJV, NIV= Greek\)  **<big>[[G1674|Ἑλληνίς]]</big>**; 
-  son of Japheth **<big>[[H3315|יֶפֶת]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Gomer **<big>[[H1586|גֹּמֶר]]</big>**, Magog **<big>[[H4031|מָגוֹג]]</big>**, Madai **<big>[[H4074|מָדַי]]</big>**, Tubal **<big>[[H8422|תּוּבַל]]</big>**, Meshech **<big>[[H4902|מֶשֶׁךְ]]</big>** and Tiras **<big>[[H8494|תִּירָס]]</big>**; 
-  father of Elishah **<big>[[H0473|אֱלִישָׁה]]</big>**, Tarshish **<big>[[H8659|תַּרְשִׁישׁ]]</big>**, Kittim **<big>[[H3794|כִּתִּי]]</big>** and Dodanim **<big>[[H1721|דֹּדָנִים]]</big>**.

Javan is mentioned in the Table of Nations \([[Gen.10.2|Genesis 10:2]],4; [[1Chr.1.5|1 Chronicles 1:5]],7\) as one of the sons of Japheth and grandsons of Noah. He is considered the ancestor of the Greek people, and his name is often used in the Bible as a reference to Greece or the Greek people. In the Old Testament, Javan is associated with the trading of slaves \(Joel 3:6\), suggesting that the Greeks were involved in the slave trade. In the New Testament, the term "Greeks" \(Hellenes\) is used to refer to non-Jewish people, particularly those influenced by Greek culture. The Greeks are mentioned in various contexts, such as seeking Jesus \([[John.12.20|John 12:20]]\), attending synagogues \(Acts 14:1; 17:4; 18:4\), and being among the first Gentile converts to Christianity \(Acts 16:1,3\). The inclusion of Greeks in the early church underscores the universal nature of the gospel message \([[Rom.1.16|Romans 1:16]]; 10:12; [[Gal.3.28|Galatians 3:28]]\). The Greeks are also depicted as seekers of wisdom \([[1Cor.1.22|1 Corinthians 1:22]]\) and are contrasted with the Jews in terms of their religious and cultural background \([[Rom.1.14|Romans 1:14]]; 2:9-10; [[1Cor.10.32|1 Corinthians 10:32]]; 12:13\). In [[Mark.7.26|Mark 7:26]], a Syrophoenician woman is described as a Greek, highlighting the widespread influence of Greek culture in the region.
