Gad was one of the twelve tribes of Israel, named after Gad, the seventh son of Israel \(or 'Jacob'\) and Zilpah, Leah's maidservant \([[Gen.30.11|Genesis 30:11]]; 35:26\). The tribe of Gad played a significant role throughout Israel's history. During the Exodus, the tribe of Gad was counted among the Israelites \([[Num.1.14|Numbers 1:14]], 24-25\) and was positioned on the south side of the tabernacle, along with the tribes of Reuben and Simeon \(Numbers 2:14\). The tribe of Gad, along with Reuben and half of Manasseh, settled in the region east of the Jordan River \(Numbers 32; Joshua 13:24-28\). They were known for their military prowess and bravery \([[1Chr.12.8|1 Chronicles 12:8]], 14\). The Gadites were among the first to help David when he was hiding from Saul \([[1Chr.12.8|1 Chronicles 12:8]]-15\). They also supported David during the census and the purchase of the threshing floor for the temple \([[2Sam.24.5|2 Samuel 24:5]]; 1 Chronicles 21:2\). The Gadites were blessed by Moses for their faithfulness and strength \(Deuteronomy 33:20-21\). The tribe of Gad was allotted cities and pasturelands in the region of Gilead \(Joshua 13:24-28; [[1Chr.6.63|1 Chronicles 6:63]], 80\). They, along with the other tribes east of the Jordan, built an altar as a witness to their unity with the rest of Israel \(Joshua 22\). The Gadites were among the first tribes to be taken into captivity by the Assyrians \([[1Chr.5.26|1 Chronicles 5:26]]\). In Ezekiel's vision of the restored Israel, the tribe of Gad is allotted a portion of land in the far north \([[Ezek.48.27|Ezekiel 48:27]]-28, 34\). In the book of Revelation, Gad is mentioned as one of the tribes with 12,000 sealed servants of God \([[Rev.7.5|Revelation 7:5]]\).
