Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Izhar</span> was a descendant of Asher mentioned among the genealogies of the twelve tribes of Israel.
- A man from the tribe of Judah living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.4.7|1Ch.4.7]]; 
- only referred to as Izhar<[[H3328]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>צ</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>ַ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>\); 
-  son of Ashhur<[[H0806]]> and Helah<[[H2458]]>; 
- a brother of Haahashtari<[[H0326]]>, Zereth<[[H6889]]>, Ethnan<[[H0869]]> and Koz<[[H6976G]]>; 
-  half-brother of: Ahuzzam<[[H0275]]>, Hepher<[[H2660G]]> and Temeni<[[H8488]]>.

Izhar is included in the genealogy of the tribe of Asher recorded in [[1Chr.4.7|1 Chronicles 4:7]]. He is listed as one of the sons of Asher, making him a grandson of the patriarch Israel \(or 'Jacob'\). No other details are provided about his life or family. This is the only time Izhar is mentioned in the Bible. The genealogies in Chronicles aimed to document the lineage of the tribes of Israel following the Babylonian exile. By recording the descendants of each tribe, the returning exiles could establish their ancestral heritage and tribal associations.
