<small>*Male • Son of Malluch • A Levite, ancestor of Ethan the musician*</small>

Levite living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Abdi</span>, a Levite, was an ancestor of Ethan, one of the musicians appointed by David to serve in the temple.
- A man from the tribe of Levi living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.6.44|1Ch.6.44]]; 
- only referred to as Abdi<[[H5660G]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ע</span>ַ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ב</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ד</span>ִּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>\); 
- a son of Malluch<[[H4409G]]>; 
-  father of Kishi<[[H7029]]>.

Abdi is mentioned in [[1Chr.6.44|1 Chronicles 6:44]] as a Levite and an ancestor of Ethan, one of the musicians appointed by King David to serve in the temple. Ethan, along with Heman and Asaph, was a leading musician and singer during David's reign \(1 Chronicles 15:19\). Abdi is listed in the genealogy of the Levites, specifically within the lineage of Merari, one of the three main Levitical clans.
