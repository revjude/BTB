Ibleam, also known as Bileam or Gath-rimmon, was a Levitical city located in the territory of Issachar \([[Josh.17.11|Joshua 17:11]]; 21:25; [[1Chr.6.70|1 Chronicles 6:70]]\). It was one of the cities that were not fully conquered by the Israelites during the time of Joshua, and the Canaanite inhabitants continued to live there \([[Judg.1.27|Judges 1:27]]\). King Ahaziah of Judah was wounded by Jehu's soldiers near Ibleam as he fled from Jezreel \([[2Kgs.9.27|2 Kings 9:27]]\). The city was also the location where Zechariah, king of Israel, was assassinated by Shallum \([[2Kgs.15.10|2 Kings 15:10]]\). Its exact location is uncertain, but it was likely situated in the Jezreel Valley.
