<small>*Male • Son of Eshton • Descendant of Judah through Eshton*</small>

Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Beth-rapha</span> was a descendant of Judah through Eshton.
- A man from the tribe of Judah  living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.4.12|1Ch.4.12]]; 
- only referred to as Beth-rapha **<big>[[H1051|בֵּית רָפָא]]</big>**; 
-  son of Eshton **<big>[[H0850|אֶשְׁתּוֹן]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Paseah **<big>[[H6454|פָּסֵחַ]]</big>** and Tehinnah **<big>[[H8468|תְּחִנָּה]]</big>**.

Beth-rapha is mentioned in the genealogy of the tribe of Judah as a descendant of Eshton \(1 Ch. 4:12\). Eshton was a descendant of Chelub, who was from the lineage of Judah. No further details about Beth-rapha or the significance of this name are provided in the biblical text. The inclusion of Beth-rapha in the genealogy highlights the importance of family lineage and the preservation of ancestral records among the Israelites, particularly within the tribe of Judah.
