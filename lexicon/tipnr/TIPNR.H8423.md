<small>*Male • Son of Lamech • Descendant of Cain, first metalworker*</small>

Man living at the time of the Patriarchs

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Tubal-cain</span>, a descendant of Cain, was the first metalworker mentioned in the Bible, forging tools out of bronze and iron.
- A man living at the time of the Patriarchs, first mentioned at Gen.4.22; 
- only referred to as Tubal-cain **<big>[[H8423|תּוּבַל קַיִן]]</big>**; 
-  son of Lamech **<big>[[H3929|לֶמֶךְ]]</big>** and Zillah **<big>[[H6741|צִלָּה]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Jabal **<big>[[H2989|יָבָל]]</big>**, Jubal **<big>[[H3106|יוּבַל]]</big>** and Naamah **<big>[[H5279|נַעֲמָה]]</big>**.

Tubal-cain is mentioned in Genesis 4:22 as a descendant of Cain, the son of Lamech and Zillah. He is described as a forger of all instruments of bronze and iron, making him the first metalworker mentioned in the Bible. The name Tubal-cain consists of two elements meaning "producer" and "smith," emphasizing his role as a craftsman. No other details are provided about his life or specific works. Tubal-cain's skills in metalworking represent the development of civilization and technology among the descendants of Cain before the flood. His story illustrates God's grace in allowing cultural advancements even among those who had turned away from Him.
