Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Ginath</span> was the father of Tibni, who contended with Omri for the throne of Israel.
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, first mentioned at [[1Kgs.16.21|1Ki.16.21]]; 
- only referred to as Ginath<[[H1527]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ג</span>ִּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ינ</span>ַ֑<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ת</span>\); 
-  father of Tibni<[[H8402]]>.

Ginath is mentioned in [[1Kgs.16.21|1 Kings 16:21]]-22 as the father of Tibni. After the death of King Elah of Israel, the people were divided into two factions. Half of the people followed Tibni, son of Ginath, to make him king, while the other half followed Omri. The supporters of Omri proved stronger than those of Tibni, and Tibni died, allowing Omri to become king.
