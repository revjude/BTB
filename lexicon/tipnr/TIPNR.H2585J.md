<small>*Male • Son of Reuben • A son of Reuben, grandson of Jacob.*</small>

Man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Hanoch</span> was one of the sons of Reuben, Jacob's firstborn, and a grandson of Jacob.
- A man from the tribe of Reuben living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, first mentioned at [[Gen.46.9|Gen.46.9]]; 
- referred to as Hanoch<[[H2585J]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>ֲ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>נו</span>ֹ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ך</span>ְ\), or Hanochite  \(NIV= Hanokite\) <[[H2599]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ח</span>ֲ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>נ</span>ֹ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>כ</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>\); 
-  son of Reuben<[[H7205]]>; 
- a brother of Pallu<[[H6396]]>, Hezron<[[H2696G]]> and Carmi<[[H3756G]]>.

Hanoch is mentioned in [[Gen.46.9|Genesis 46:9]], [[Exod.6.14|Exodus 6:14]], [[Num.26.5|Numbers 26:5]], and [[1Chr.5.3|1 Chronicles 5:3]] as one of the sons of Reuben, the firstborn son of Israel \(or 'Jacob'\). He is listed among the descendants of Reuben who went down to Egypt with Jacob during the time of Joseph \(Genesis 46:8-9\). Hanoch's descendants formed the Hanochite clan within the tribe of Reuben \([[Num.26.5|Numbers 26:5]]\). The tribe of Reuben, along with the tribes of Gad and half of the tribe of Manasseh, settled in the region east of the Jordan River after the Israelites conquered the land of Canaan \(Numbers 32:1-5, 33-42\). The mention of Hanoch in the genealogies of Reuben emphasizes the importance of family lineage and the organization of the Israelite tribes according to their ancestors.
