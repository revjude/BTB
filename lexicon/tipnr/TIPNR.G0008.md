High Priest living at the time of United Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Abiathar</span>, son of Ahimelech, was a high priest who served during the reigns of David and Solomon.
- A High Priest living at the time of United Monarchy, first mentioned at [[1Sam.22.20|1Sa.22.20]]; 
- referred to as Abiathar **<big>[[H0054|אֶבְיָתָר]]</big>**, or Abiathar **<big>[[G0008|Ἀβιαθάρ]]</big>**; 
- a son of Ahimelech **<big>[[H0288|אֲחִימֶלֶךְ]]</big>**; 
-  father of Ahimelech **<big>[[H0288|אֲחִימֶלֶךְ]]</big>**, Jonathan **<big>[[H3083|יְהוֹנָתָן]]</big>**.

Abiathar was the son of Ahimelech, a priest at Nob. When King Saul ordered the execution of the priests at Nob, Abiathar was the only one who escaped and fled to David \([[1Sam.22.20|1 Samuel 22:20]]-23\). He brought with him the ephod, a sacred priestly garment used for seeking God's will \([[1Sam.23.6|1 Samuel 23:6]],9\). Abiathar remained loyal to David throughout his reign, serving as one of his chief priests \([[2Sam.8.17|2 Samuel 8:17]]; 15:24-36; 19:11; 20:25\). However, near the end of David's life, Abiathar supported Adonijah's attempt to become king instead of Solomon \([[1Kgs.1.7|1 Kings 1:7]],19,25\). When Solomon became king, he removed Abiathar from the priesthood, fulfilling the prophecy against the house of Eli \([[1Kgs.2.26|1 Kings 2:26]]-27,35\). Abiathar is also mentioned in the New Testament by Jesus, who referred to an incident during Abiathar's time as high priest \([[Mark.2.26|Mark 2:26]]\).
