Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Hezion</span> was the grandfather of Ben-Hadad, the king of Aram during the reign of Asa, king of Judah.
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Kgs.15.18|1Ki.15.18]]; 
- only referred to as Hezion **<big>[[H2383|חֶזְיוֹן]]</big>**; 
-  father of Tabrimmon **<big>[[H2886|טַבְרִמּוֹן]]</big>**.

Hezion is mentioned in [[1Kgs.15.18|1 Kings 15:18]] as the grandfather of Ben-Hadad, the king of Aram \(Syria\) during the reign of King Asa of Judah. The verse states that there was a treaty between Asa's father and Hezion, which Asa sought to renew with Ben-Hadad. His mention in this context suggests that he was a significant figure in the history of Aram, likely a former king or ruler, who had established a treaty with the kingdom of Judah.
