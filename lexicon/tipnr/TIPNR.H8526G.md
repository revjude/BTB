<small>*Male • Son of Anak • Anakim king of Hebron, defeated by Judah*</small>

Man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Talmai</span> was one of the Anakim kings of Hebron defeated by Caleb and the men of Judah.
- A man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, first mentioned at [[Num.13.22|Num.13.22]]; 
- only referred to as Talmai<[[H8526G]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ת</span>ַּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ַ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>י</span>\); 
-  son of Anak<[[H6061]]>; 
- a brother of Ahiman<[[H0289G]]> and Sheshai<[[H8344]]>.

Talmai is mentioned in [[Num.13.22|Numbers 13:22]], [[Josh.15.14|Joshua 15:14]], and [[Judg.1.10|Judges 1:10]] as one of the Anakim who were the pre-Israelite inhabitants of Hebron. The Anakim were giants, descendants of Anak, and were feared by the Israelite spies. Talmai, along with Sheshai and Ahiman, was a king of Hebron. After the conquest of Canaan, Caleb drove out these three Anakim kings when the city was allotted to him and the tribe of Judah.
