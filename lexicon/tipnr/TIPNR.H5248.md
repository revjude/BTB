Man living at the time before the Flood

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Nimrod</span> was a mighty hunter and ruler who built several notable cities, including Babel and Nineveh.
- A man of the Cushites living at the time before the Flood, first mentioned at [[Gen.10.8|Gen.10.8]]; 
- only referred to as Nimrod **<big>[[H5248|נִמְרוֹד]]</big>**; 
-  son of Cush **<big>[[H3568|כּוּשׁ]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Seba **<big>[[H5434|סְבָא]]</big>**, Havilah **<big>[[H2341|חֲוִילָה]]</big>**, Sabtah **<big>[[H5454|סַבְתָּא]]</big>**, Raamah **<big>[[H7484|רַעְמָה]]</big>** and Sabteca **<big>[[H5455|סַבְתְּכָא]]</big>**.

Nimrod was a descendant of Cush, the son of Ham \([[Gen.10.8|Genesis 10:8]]\). He was described as a mighty hunter before the Lord, indicating his prowess and skill \([[Gen.10.9|Genesis 10:9]]\). Nimrod became a powerful ruler, establishing a kingdom that included the cities of Babel, Erech, Accad, and Calneh in the land of Shinar \(Genesis 10:10\). He also expanded his kingdom to Assyria, where he built the cities of Nineveh, Rehoboth Ir, Calah, and Resen \(Genesis 10:11-12\). The land of Assyria is referred to as the land of Nimrod \([[Mic.5.6|Micah 5:6]]\), further emphasizing his influence and power in the region.
