Ochran was an Israelite from the tribe of Asher. He was the father of Pagiel, who was chosen as the leader of the tribe during the Exodus \([[Num.1.13|Numbers 1:13]]; 2:27\). Pagiel represented Asher in the census \([[Num.1.13|Numbers 1:13]]\), in the arrangement of the tribal camps \([[Num.2.27|Numbers 2:27]]\), and in the offerings presented at the dedication of the tabernacle \([[Num.7.72|Numbers 7:72]], 77\). He also led the tribe in the order of march \([[Num.10.26|Numbers 10:26]]\). Ochran's name is mentioned in connection with his son's leadership role, highlighting the importance of family lineage in the tribal structure of Israel.
