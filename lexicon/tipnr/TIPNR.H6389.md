<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Peleg</span>, a descendant of Shem and ancestor of Abraham, during whose time the earth was divided.
- A man living at the time of the Patriarchs, first mentioned at [[Gen.10.25|Gen.10.25]]; 
- referred to as Peleg<[[H6389]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>פ</span>ֶּ֫<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ֶ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ג</span>\), or Peleg \(KJV= Phalec\) <[[G5317]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangGreek'>Φάλεκ</span>\); 
-  son of Eber<[[H5677G]]>; 
- a brother of Joktan<[[H3355]]>; 
-  father of Reu<[[H7466]]>.

Peleg was a descendant of Shem, the son of Noah, and an ancestor of Abraham, according to the genealogies in [[Gen.10.25|Genesis 10:25]], 11:16-19, and [[1Chr.1.19|1 Chronicles 1:19]],25. He was the son of Eber and the brother of Joktan. Peleg lived 239 years and became the father of Reu at the age of 30. The name Peleg means "division," and [[Gen.10.25|Genesis 10:25]] states that in his days the earth was divided. This event is not described in detail, but it may refer to the division of languages and peoples described in the Tower of Babel story \(Genesis 11:1-9\). Peleg is also mentioned in the genealogy of Jesus in [[Luke.3.35|Luke 3:35]], emphasizing his role in the ancestral line of the Messiah.
