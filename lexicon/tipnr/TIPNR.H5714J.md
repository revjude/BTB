Man living at the time of Exile and Return

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Iddo</span>, the grandfather of the prophet Zechariah, was a priest and prophet in post-exilic Judah.
- A priest living at the time of Exile and Return, first mentioned at [[Ezra.5.1|Ezr.5.1]]; 
- only referred to as Iddo **<big>[[H5714|עִדּוֹ]]</big>**; 
-  father of Berechiah **<big>[[H1296|בֶּרֶכְיָה]]</big>**.

Iddo was the grandfather of the prophet Zechariah, who prophesied in Judah after the Babylonian exile \([[Zech.1.1|Zechariah 1:1]],7\). Iddo is described as a priest and prophet who, along with Haggai, encouraged the people to resume the rebuilding of the temple in Jerusalem \([[Ezra.5.1|Ezra 5:1]]; 6:14\). His prophetic ministry played a significant role in motivating the Jewish community to complete the temple reconstruction during the reign of the Persian king Darius.
