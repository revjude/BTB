Esau, also known as Edom, was the firstborn son of Isaac and Rebekah and the twin brother of Israel \(or 'Jacob'\) \([[Gen.25.25|Genesis 25:25]]-26\). He was a skillful hunter and his father's favorite son \([[Gen.25.27|Genesis 25:27]]-28\). Esau sold his birthright to Jacob for a bowl of stew when he was famished \([[Gen.25.29|Genesis 25:29]]-34\). He married two Hittite women, causing grief to his parents \([[Gen.26.34|Genesis 26:34]]-35\). Esau lost his father's blessing to Jacob through deception \(Genesis 27\). He held a grudge against Jacob but later reconciled with him \(Genesis 32-33\). Esau settled in the hill country of Seir, which became known as Edom \([[Gen.36.8|Genesis 36:8]]-9\). He had five sons and became the father of the Edomites \([[Gen.36.1|Genesis 36:1]]-43\). The Edomites were later considered enemies of Israel \(Numbers 20:14-21; 1 Samuel 14:47; 2 Samuel 8:13-14\). God's love for Jacob and His rejection of Esau is used as an example of His sovereignty \([[Mal.1.2|Malachi 1:2]]-3; [[Rom.9.13|Romans 9:13]]\).
