Rehoboam was the son of King Solomon and Naamah, an Ammonite woman \(1 Kings 14:21, 31\). He became king of Judah after Solomon's death \([[1Kgs.11.43|1 Kings 11:43]]\). When Rehoboam went to Shechem to be crowned, the people, led by Jeroboam, asked him to lighten the heavy yoke that Solomon had placed on them \([[1Kgs.12.1|1 Kings 12:1]]-4\). Rehoboam consulted with the elders who had served his father, but he rejected their advice to be a servant to the people \([[1Kgs.12.6|1 Kings 12:6]]-8\). Instead, he followed the counsel of the young men who had grown up with him and threatened to make the people's yoke even heavier \(1 Kings 12:9-14\). As a result, the ten northern tribes rebelled against Rehoboam and made Jeroboam their king, leaving only the tribes of Judah and Benjamin loyal to Rehoboam \([[1Kgs.12.1|1 Kings 12:1]]6-20\). Rehoboam intended to fight against the northern tribes, but God sent a prophet to stop him \(1 Kings 12:21-24\). During Rehoboam's reign, King Shishak of Egypt attacked Jerusalem and took away the treasures of the temple and the royal palace \([[1Kgs.14.25|1 Kings 14:25]]-28; [[2Chr.12.1|2 Chronicles 12:1]]-12\). Rehoboam did evil in the eyes of the Lord and allowed idol worship to spread in Judah \(1 Kings 14:22-24\). He reigned for 17 years and was succeeded by his son Abijah \(1 Kings 14:21, 31; [[2Chr.12.1|2 Chronicles 12:1]]6\). Rehoboam's story is a cautionary tale about the consequences of foolish decisions and disobedience to God.
