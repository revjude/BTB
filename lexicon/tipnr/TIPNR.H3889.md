Man living at the time of United Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Laish</span>, the father of Palti \(or 'Paltiel'\), to whom Saul gave his daughter Michal in marriage \([[1Sam.25.44|1Sa.25.44]]; [[2Sam.3.15|2Sa.3.15]]\).
- A man living at the time of United Monarchy, first mentioned at [[1Sam.25.44|1Sa.25.44]]; 
- referred to as Laish **<big>[[H3889|לוּשׁ]]</big>**, or Laish **<big>[[H3919|לַיִשׁ]]</big>**; 
-  father of Palti **<big>[[H6406|פַּלְטִי]]</big>**.

Laish was the father of Palti \(or 'Paltiel'\), a man from Gallim \([[1Sam.25.44|1Sa.25.44]]\). King Saul gave his daughter Michal, who was previously married to David, to Palti as a wife \([[1Sam.25.44|1Sa.25.44]]\). After Saul's death, David demanded that Michal be returned to him as part of a political agreement with Abner, the commander of Saul's army \(2Sa.3.12-16\). Paltiel followed Michal, weeping, as she was taken back to David, until Abner ordered him to return home \([[2Sam.3.15|2Sa.3.15]]-16\).
