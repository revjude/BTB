Kehelathah was one of the places where the Israelites camped during their wilderness journey after leaving Mount Sinai \([[Num.33.22|Numbers 33:22]]-23\). It is mentioned in the list of the stages of Israel's journey, which was recorded to commemorate God's guidance and provision throughout their travels. The Israelites arrived at Kehelathah after departing from Rissah and before moving on to Mount Shepher. Its location in the Sinai Peninsula is unknown.
