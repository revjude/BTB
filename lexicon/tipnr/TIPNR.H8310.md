<small>*Male • Babylonian official at Jerusalem's fall*</small>

Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Nebu-sar-sekim</span> was a high official in Nebuchadnezzar's army when Jerusalem fell.
- A man living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[Jer.39.3|Jer.39.3]]; 
- only referred to as Nebu-sar-sekim<[[H8310]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ש</span>ַׂ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ר</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ס</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>כ</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ים</span>\).

Nebu-sar-sekim is mentioned in a list of Babylonian officials present at the capture of Jerusalem in 587 BC \([[Jer.39.3|Jeremiah 39:3]]\). His title is given as Rab-saris, which means "chief eunuch" or "chief officer". This suggests he was a high-ranking official in Nebuchadnezzar's army or administration. The name Nebu-sar-sekim means "Nabu, protect the king!" referring to the Babylonian god Nabu.
