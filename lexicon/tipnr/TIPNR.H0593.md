Man living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Aniam</span> was a son of Shemida and a descendant of Manasseh, mentioned in the genealogy of the tribe of Manasseh.
- A man from the tribe of Manasseh living at the time of Egypt and Wilderness, only mentioned at [[1Chr.7.19|1Ch.7.19]]; 
- only referred to as Aniam<[[H0593]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>א</span>ֲ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>נ</span>ִ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>יע</span>ָ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ם</span>\); 
-  son of Shemida<[[H8061]]>; 
- a brother of Ahian<[[H0291]]>, Shechem<[[H7928H]]> and Likhi<[[H3949]]>.

Aniam is mentioned in the genealogy of the tribe of Manasseh, found in 1 Chronicles 7. He is listed as one of the sons of Shemida, who was a descendant of Manasseh, the son of Joseph. The genealogy provides a record of the families and clans within the tribe of Manasseh, one of the twelve tribes of Israel. Apart from this mention in the genealogical list, no further information is given about Aniam in the biblical text.
