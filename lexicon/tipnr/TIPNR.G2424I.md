Joshua, the son of Nun, was a prominent figure in the Old Testament who served as Moses' assistant and later succeeded him as the leader of the Israelites. He was from the tribe of Ephraim \([[Num.13.8|Numbers 13:8]]\) and was originally named Hoshea, but Moses changed his name to Joshua \([[Num.13.16|Numbers 13:16]]\). Joshua was one of the twelve spies sent to explore the land of Canaan \([[Num.13.8|Numbers 13:8]]\), and he and Caleb were the only ones who brought back a positive report and encouraged the people to trust God \([[Num.14.6|Numbers 14:6]]-9\). As a result, Joshua and Caleb were the only adults from their generation permitted to enter the Promised Land \([[Num.14.30|Numbers 14:30]], 38\). 

Before Moses' death, God appointed Joshua to succeed him as the leader of Israel \([[Num.27.18|Numbers 27:18]]-23; Deuteronomy 31:7-8, 14, 23\). After Moses' death, Joshua led the Israelites across the Jordan River and into the land of Canaan \(Joshua 1-4\). He oversaw the conquest of Jericho \(Joshua 6\), Ai \(Joshua 8\), and numerous other cities and territories \(Joshua 10-12\). Joshua also led the Israelites in renewing their covenant with God \([[Josh.8.30|Joshua 8:30]]-35; 24:1-28\). 

Under Joshua's leadership, the land of Canaan was divided among the tribes of Israel as their inheritance \(Joshua 13-21\). In his old age, Joshua gave a farewell address to the Israelites, encouraging them to remain faithful to God \(Joshua 23-24\). He died at the age of 110 and was buried in his inheritance at Timnath-serah \([[Josh.24.2|Joshua 24:2]]9-30; [[Judg.2.8|Judges 2:8]]-9\). 

Joshua is remembered as a courageous leader, a faithful servant of God, and a key figure in Israel's history who led the people into the Promised Land and helped establish them as a nation.
