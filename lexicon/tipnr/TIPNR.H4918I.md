<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Meshullam</span>, a Gadite leader during the reign of Jotham, king of Judah \([[1Chr.5.13|1Ch.5.13]]\).
- A man from the tribe of Gad  living at the time of Divided Monarchy, only mentioned at [[1Chr.5.13|1Ch.5.13]]; 
- only referred to as Meshullam<[[H4918I]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>מ</span>ְ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ש</span>ֻׁ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ל</span>ָּ<span class='tipnrLangHebrew'>ם</span>\); 
-  son of Abihail<[[H0032I]]>; 
- a brother of Michael<[[H4317H]]>, Sheba<[[H7652G]]>, Jorai<[[H3140]]>, Jacan<[[H3275]]>, Zia<[[H2127]]> and Eber<[[H5677H]]>.

Meshullam is mentioned in the genealogy of the tribe of Gad in the book of Chronicles \([[1Chr.5.13|1Ch.5.13]]\). He is listed among the leaders of the Gadites during the reign of Jotham, king of Judah. These leaders, including Michael, Meshullam, Sheba, Jorai, Jachan, Zia, and Eber, were described as mighty warriors, famous men, and heads of their fathers' houses.
