Man living before Israel's Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Elah</span> was a son of Caleb and the father of Kenaz.
- A man from the tribe of Judah  living before Israel's Monarchy, first mentioned at 1Ch.4.15; 
- only referred to as Elah **<big>[[H0425|אֵלָה]]</big>**; 
-  son of Caleb **<big>[[H3612|כָּלֵב]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Achsah **<big>[[H5915|עַכְסָה]]</big>**, Iru **<big>[[H5900|עִירוּ]]</big>** and Naam **<big>[[H5277|נַעַם]]</big>**; 
-  father of Kenaz **<big>[[H7073|קְנַז]]</big>** and Jehallelel **<big>[[H3094|יְהַלֶּלְאֵל]]</big>**.

Elah is mentioned in 1 Chronicles 4:15 as one of the sons of Caleb, along with Iru, Naam, and Kenaz. Elah is specifically noted as the father of Kenaz. Caleb was a prominent figure in the Bible, one of the twelve spies sent by Moses to explore Canaan, and one of only two spies \(along with Joshua\) who brought back a positive report.
