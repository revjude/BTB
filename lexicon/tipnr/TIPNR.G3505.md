Emperor living at the time of the New Testament

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Nero</span> was likely the Roman emperor during Paul's final imprisonment.
- A Roman Emperor living at the time of the New Testament, first mentioned at [[Phil.4.22|Php.4.22]]; 
- referred to as Nero<[[G3505]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangGreek'>Νέρων</span>\), or Caesar<[[G2541I]]> \(<span class='tipnrLangGreek'>Καῖσαρ</span>\).

Nero was the Roman emperor who reigned from 54-68 AD. Although not mentioned by name, he was likely the "Caesar" referred to in Paul's letters written during his imprisonment in Rome \([[Phil.4.22|Philippians 4:22]], [[2Tim.4.22|2 Timothy 4:22]]\). Nero was known for his brutal persecution of Christians. According to church tradition, both Paul and Peter were martyred during Nero's reign.
