Man living at the time of Divided Monarchy

<span class='tipnrhighlight'>Eleasah</span>, also known as Eshek, was a descendant of Saul and Jonathan, the son of Rapha.
- A man from the tribe of Benjamin living at the time of Divided Monarchy, first mentioned at [[1Chr.8.37|1Ch.8.37]]; 
- referred to as Eleasah **<big>[[H0501|אֶלְעָשָׂה]]</big>**, or Eshek **<big>[[H6232|עֵשֶׁק]]</big>**; 
-  son of Binea **<big>[[H1150|בִּנְעָא]]</big>**; 
- a brother of Raphah **<big>[[H7498|רָפָא]]</big>** and Azel **<big>[[H0682|אָצֵל]]</big>**; 
-  father of Ulam **<big>[[H0198|אוּלָם]]</big>**, Jeush **<big>[[H3266|יְעוּשׁ]]</big>** and Eliphelet **<big>[[H0467|אֱלִיפֶלֶט]]</big>**.

Eleasah, also known as Eshek, is mentioned in [[1Chr.8.37|1 Chronicles 8:37]], 9:43, and 8:39 as a descendant of Saul and Jonathan. He was the son of Rapha and is listed in the genealogy of the tribe of Benjamin. The name Eleasah is used in [[1Chr.8.37|1 Chronicles 8:37]], while Eshek is used in [[1Chr.9.43|1 Chronicles 9:43]] and 8:39, referring to the same person.
