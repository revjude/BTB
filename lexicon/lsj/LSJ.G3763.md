in <i>Attic dialect</i>, <span class='asgreek'>==οὐδέποτε==</span> is commonly found with <i>present</i> or <i>future</i> (or its equivalent, as in <span class='asgreek'>==οὐδέποτε==</span> <span class='asgreek'>μὴ</span> <span class='asgreek'>λειφθῇ</span> [<a href="javascript:void(0)" title=" “SIG” 800.29 (Lycosura, 1st c.AD)), οὐδεπώποτε">Refs 1st c.AD+</a>] with past tenses, so <span class='asgreek'>==οὐδέποτε==</span> <span class='asgreek'>ἐπὶ</span> <span class='asgreek'>μέλλοντος</span>.., <span class='asgreek'>ἐπὶ</span> <span class='asgreek'>δὲ</span> <span class='asgreek'>παρῳχημένου</span> <span class='asgreek'>τὸ</span> <span class='asgreek'>οὐδεπώποτε</span> [<a href="javascript:void(0)" title=" 2nd c.AD: Phrynichus Atticista “Praeparatio Sophistica” p.91 5th c.BC: Bacchylides Lyricus: but ==οὐδέποτε==">Refs 5th c.BC+</a>] occurs with past tenses in [<a href="javascript:void(0)" title=" “Comica Adespota” 23 (cited by 2nd c.AD: Phrynichus Atticista [same place]), 5th-6th c.BC: Xenophon Historicus “Anabasis” 2.6.13, “Ages.” 11.7, “Oec.” 20.22, 4th c.BC: Aeschines Orator 3.151, 4th-3rd c.BC: Menander Comicus 653 ">Refs 5th c.BC+</a>];
