
<b>==Ἑρμῆς== </b>, the Lat. <i>Mercurius</i>, son of Maia and Zeus; messenger of the gods  <span class='asgreek'>διάκτορος</span>);  giver of good luck  <span class='asgreek'>ἐριούνιος</span>, <span class='asgreek'>ἀκάκητα</span>);  god of all secret dealings, cunning, and stratagem  <span class='asgreek'>δόλιος</span>);  bearing a golden rod  <span class='asgreek'>χρυσόρραπις</span>);  conductor of defunct spirits  <span class='asgreek'>ψυχοπομπός</span>, <span class='asgreek'>πομπαῖος</span>);  tutelary god of all arts, of traffic, markets, roads  <span class='asgreek'>ἀγοραῖος</span>, <span class='asgreek'>ἐμπολαῖος</span>, <span class='asgreek'>ὅδιος</span>, <span class='asgreek'>ἐνόδιος</span>); and of heralds. His bust, mounted on a four-cornered pillar, was used to mark boundaries. —;Proverb., <span class='asgreek'>κοινὸς</span> <span class='asgreek'>Ἑρμῆς</span> <b>shares in your luck! </b> [<a href="javascript:void(0)" title="Theophrastus Philosophus">4th/3rd c.BC</a>]: compare <span class='asgreek'>ἕρμαιον</span>.
