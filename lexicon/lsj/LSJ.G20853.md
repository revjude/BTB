<b> <span class='asgreek'>==οὐθείς==</span></b>, <span class='asgreek'>οὐθέν</span>, 


 later form for <span class='asgreek'>οὐδείς</span>, <span class='asgreek'>οὐδέν</span>, found in <i>Attic dialect</i> Inscrr. from 4th c.BC onwards along with <span class='asgreek'>οὐδείς</span>, which it supersedes entirely from about 4th c.BC to 2nd c.BC (forty examples of <span class='asgreek'>θ</span>, none of <span class='asgreek'>δ</span>); <span class='asgreek'>==οὐθείς==</span> is in a majority in Ptolemaic papyri up to about 2nd c.BC, after which <span class='asgreek'>οὐδείς</span> begins to be common, but does not prevail until 1st c.AD; the evidence of non-<i>Attic dialect</i> Inscrr. is in general agreement with the {<span class='asgreek'>οὐθατόεις</span>}; codices of [<a href="javascript:void(0)" title=" 5th c.BC: Thucydides Historicus, 5th c.BC: Antipho Orator, 5th-6th c.BC: Andocides Orator, 5th c.BC: Lysias Orator, and 5th c.BC: Herodotus Historicus never have ==οὐθείς==">Refs 5th c.BC+</a>], but the <span class='asgreek'>θ</span> forms are frequently in those of [<a href="javascript:void(0)" title=" 5th-6th c.BC: Plato Philosophus, 5th-6th c.BC: Xenophon Historicus, 5th-6th c.BC: Isocrates Orator, 4th c.BC: Demosthenes Orator, 4th c.BC: Hyperides Orator, 4th c.BC: Aristoteles Philosophus, and 4th-3rd c.BC: Theophrastus Philosophus ">Refs 5th c.BC+</a>], frequently as variants for the <span class='asgreek'>δ</span> forms; also in Hellenistic writers, [<a href="javascript:void(0)" title=" 3rd c.BC: Teles Philosophus, 2nd c.BC: Polybius Historicus, etc.">Refs 3rd c.BC+</a>]; the frequency of <span class='asgreek'>θ</span> forms in the uncials of [<a href="javascript:void(0)" title=" LXX varies roughly according to the date (known or probable)">Refs</a>] of the translation of the book in question (though the <span class='asgreek'>δ</span> forms are in a large majority in the [<a href="javascript:void(0)" title=" LXX as a whole)">Refs</a>]; the <span class='asgreek'>θ</span> forms are rare in codices of [<a href="javascript:void(0)" title=" 1st c.BC-1st c.AD: Strabo Geographus ">Refs 1st c.BC+</a>] and later writers.
