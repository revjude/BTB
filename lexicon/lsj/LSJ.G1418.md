form of: <small>**<big>[[G1419|δυσβάστακτος]]</big>**</small> 


<b> <span class='asgreek'>==δυσ-==</span>-</b>, 


 insepar. Prefix, opposed to <span class='asgreek'>εὖ</span>, <b>un-, mis</b>-, with notion of <b>hard, bad, unlucky</b>, etc., as <span class='asgreek'>δυσήλιος</span>, <span class='asgreek'>δύσαγνος</span>; <b>destroying the good sense</b> of a word, or <b>increasing its bad sense</b>: hence, joined even to words expressing negation, as <span class='asgreek'>δυσάμμορος</span>, <span class='asgreek'>δυσανάσχετο</span>; <i>poetry</i> in strong contrasts, as <span class='asgreek'>Πάρις</span> <span class='asgreek'>Δύσπαρις</span>, <span class='asgreek'>γάμος</span> <span class='asgreek'>δύσγαμος</span>. Before <span class='asgreek'>στ</span>, <span class='asgreek'>σθ</span>, <span class='asgreek'>σπ</span>, <span class='asgreek'>σφ</span>, <span class='asgreek'>σχ</span>, the final <span class='asgreek'>ς</span> was omitted, see <span class='asgreek'>δυστ</span>-. (Cf. Sanskrit <i>du[snull]-, dur</i>-, e.g. <b>durmanās</b>, ={<span class='asgreek'>δυσμενής</span>}; ONorse <b>tor</b>-, e.g. <b>torsóttligr</b> (<span class='asgreek'>δύσμαχος</span>); OIr. <b>du-, do</b>-, e.g. <b>dochruth</b> 'misshapen'.)
