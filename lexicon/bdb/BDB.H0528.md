 I. <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אָמוֺן‎</span> **proper name** ==Amon==, an Egyptian god [[Nah.3.8|Nah 3:8]]; [[Jer.46.25|Jer 46:25]], compare by Greeks with Zeus (Herod.<sup>ii. 42</sup>; [[BDB#Diod|Diod]]<sup>i.13</sup>), <span class="bdbLangGreek">Ἀμμῶν</span>. He was originally the local deity of Thebes (= <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎נאֹ‎</span>, called <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎נאֹ אָמוֺן‎</span> [[Nah.3.8|Nah 3:8]], compare <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִנּאֹ‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎א‎</span> [[Jer.46.25|Jer 46:25]] **\*\***Spiegelb<sup>Randglossen, 43 ff.</sup> reads <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎נֹא אָמוֺן‎</span> (as [[Nah.3.8|Nah 3:8]]) for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אָמוֺן נֹא‎</span>, and finds in both a Thebes in the Delta.), but subsequently became the supreme god of the Egyptian Pantheon, the successor of the sun-god ***Ra*** and so-called ***Amon Ra.*** He was the secret god, who hid himself and was difficult to find (Amon = concealment, hidden); see Rawl.<sup>Hist, Anc. Egypt, i. 322</sup> [[BDB#Eb|Ebers]] [[BDB#Ri|Ri]]<sup>HWB.</sup> (II. III. <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אָמוֺן‎</span>.)
