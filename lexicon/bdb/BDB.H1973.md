H]] and other editions) The reading here became early a subject of dispute, and [[BDB#MSS|MSS]]. and editions vary accordingly. Some follow the school of Nehardea, and read <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַל יְהוָֺה‎</span>; others (so [[BDB#Norzi|Norzi]]) follow the school of Sora, and read <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַ לְיהוָֺה‎</span>; others (so Hahn) have <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַלְיהוָֺה‎</span>. Were <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַל‎</span> original, it would be an interrogative particle = Arabic <img class="bdbSeriacGlyph" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhGwAcAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAAAAAAALAAAAAAbABwAAAJMjI+py+0M4pvA1NmkxbleFYWPCGrciaaHqSJsa31wPLty/cL3mndLuIPsNL0fKFa03YCbEVDCDMh6H6g0SkskpaVuirXtPIOYce1cAAA7">, for which elsewhere <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲ‎</span> is always found. The other punctuations would also each be highly irregular. The true reading is undoubtedly <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎(הַלְיַהְוֶה) הֲלַיהוָֺה‎</span>. compare [[BDB#De|De]] Rossi<sup>Var. Lect.</sup> ***on the passage***; and on the possible origin of the anomaly, [[BDB#Gei|Gei]]<sup>Jüd. Ztschr. 1864-65, p. 89 f</sup>.
