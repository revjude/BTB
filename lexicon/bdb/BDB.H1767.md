 <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דַּי‎</span> **substantive** ==sufficiency, enough== (Late Hebrew, but not known in other cognate languages) — construct <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דֵּי‎</span>, with suffix (see below) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דַּיֶּ֑ךָּ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דַּיָּם‎</span>: — 

**1.** absolute thrice only [[Mal.3.10|Mal 3:10]] I will pour you out a blessing <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עַדבְּֿלִידָֽֿי‎</span> until there is not ***sufficiency***, i.e. until my abundance can be exhausted, or, as this can never be, for ever (compare [[Ps.72.7|Ps 72:7]]), [[Esth.1.18|Esth 1:18]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וּכְדַי בִּזָּיוֺן וָקָ֑צֶף‎</span> and (there will be) ***as enough*** (i.e. in plenty) contempt and wrath, [[2Chr.30.3|2Chr 30:3]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְמַדַּי‎</span> = <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְמַהדַּֿי‎</span> for what was ***sufficient.*** With a genitive of the person or thing ***for*** which anything suffices: [[Exod.36.5|Exod 36:5]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִדֵּי הָעֲבֹדָה‎</span> (more) than ***enough for*** the work, [[Lev.5.7|Lev 5:7]] and if his hand do not reach (if he do not command) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דֵּי שֶׂה‎</span> ***enough for*** (i.e. to buy) a lamb, [[Lev.12.8|Lev 12:8]]; [[Lev.25.28|Lev 25:28]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דֵּי הָשִׁיב לוֺ‎</span> ***enough for*** recovering it, [[Deut.15.8|Deut 15:8]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דֵּי מַחְסֹרוֺ‎</span> ***enough for*** his need, [[Isa.40.16|Isa 40:16]] (twice in verse). With suffix [[Prov.25.16|Prov 25:16]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֱכֹל דַּיֶּ֑ךָּ‎</span> eat ***that which is sufficient for*** thee; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דַּיָּם‎</span> [[Exod.36.7|Exod 36:7]]; [[Jer.49.9|Jer 49:9]]; [[Obad.1.5|Obad 1:5]]. Once with genitive of the thing which is sufficient, [[Prov.27.27|Prov 27:27]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דֵּי חֲלֵב עִזִּים‎</span> ***enough of*** goats' milk.


**2.** Combined with <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּ‎</span>, and especially <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִן‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דַּי‎</span> (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דֵּי‎</span>) has a tendency to form compound prepositions, used idiomatically in certain applications: — 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּדֵי‎</span> (<span class="bdbLangGreek">α</span>) ***for*** (the <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּ‎</span> ***pretii***) ***what suffices for:*** [[Nah.2.13|Nah 2:13]] the lion tare in pieces <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּדֵי גֹרוֺתָיו‎</span> ***for the need of*** his whelps (\|\| <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְלִבְאֹתָיו‎</span>; but ? <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּדֵי‎</span>, see **b**); iron. [[Hab.2.13|Hab 2:13]]; [[Jer.51.58|Jer 51:58]] the peoples labour <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּדֵיאֵֿשׁ‎</span> ***for*** fire (only to satisfy the fire), and the nations weary themselves <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּדֵירִֿיק‎</span> ***for*** what is empty. (<span class="bdbLangGreek">β</span>) ***in the abundance of***, i.e. ***as often*** as [[Job.39.25|Job 39:25]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּדֵי שׁוֺפָר‎</span> ***in the abundance of*** the trumpet, i.e. as often as the trumpet sounds (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִדֵּי‎</span> elsewhere in this sense). 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּדֵי‎</span> ***according to the sufficiency***, or ***abundance, of*** [[Lev.25.26|Lev 25:26]] and find <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּדֵי גְאֻלָּתוֺ‎</span> ***according to the sufficiency of*** his redemption, i.e. ***as much as*** it demands, [[Deut.25.2|Deut 25:2]]; [[Neh.5.8|Neh 5:8]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּדֵי בָנוּ‎</span> (***stative construct*** before <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּ‎</span>: [[BDB#Ges|Ges]]<sup>«GKC:130»§ 130</sup>, 1) 'quantum in nobis erat,' after our ability, [[Judg.6.5|Judg 6:5]] they came <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּדֵי אַרְבֶּה לָרֹב‎</span> ***according to the abundance of*** the locust in multitude (for which <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כָּאַרְבֶּה לָרֹב‎</span> would ordinarily be said: compare [[Judg.7.12|Judg 7:12]]). 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **c.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִדֵּי‎</span> ***out of the abundance of***, hence ***as often as***; — (<span class="bdbLangGreek">α</span>) followed by infinitive [[1Sam.1.7|1Sam 1:7]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִדֵּי עֲלֹתָהּ‎</span> = ***as often as*** she went up, [[1Sam.18.30|1Sam 18:30]]; [[1Kgs.14.28|1Kgs 14:28]] (= [[2Chr.12.11|2Chr 12:11]]) [[2Kgs.4.8|2Kgs 4:8]]; [[Isa.28.19|Isa 28:19]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִדֵּי עָבְרוֺ‎</span> ***as often as*** it passeth over, [[Jer.31.20|Jer 31:20]] (<span class="bdbLangGreek">β</span>) followed by substantive, [[Jer.48.27|Jer 48:27]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִדֵּי דְבָרֶיךָ בּוֺ‎</span> ***as often as*** thy words (are) of him; and in the idiomatic phrases <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִדֵּי שָׁנָה בְּשָׁנָה‎</span> = ***yearly*** (a combination of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִדֵּי שָׁנָה‎</span> and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שָׁנָה בְּשָׁנָה‎</span>: see below <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שָׁנָה‎</span>) [[1Sam.7.16|1Sam 7:16]] (see [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]) [[Zech.14.16|Zech 14:16]]; [[2Chr.24.5|2Chr 24:5]] and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִדֵּי חֹדֶשׁ‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּחָדְשׁוֺ‎</span> [[Isa.66.23|Isa 66:23]] ***as often as*** month (comes) in its month (i.e. in its own time: <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֹדֶשׁ‎</span> made more precise by the add. of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּחָדְשׁוֺ‎</span>; compare the phrase <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דְּבַר יוֺם בְּיוֺמוֺ‎</span>): so <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִדֵּי שַׁבָּת בְּשַׁבַּתּוֺ‎</span> ***ib.***; (<span class="bdbLangGreek">γ</span>) as conjunction, with the finite verb (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁר‎</span> being understood: compare <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בַּעֲבוּר‎</span> etc.), [[Jer.20.8|Jer 20:8]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִדֵּי אֲדַבֵּר‎</span> ***as often as*** I speak.
