In the Pentateuch, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הוא‎</span> is of common gender, the feminine form <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הִיא‎</span> occurring only 11 times, namely [[Gen.14.2|Gen 14:2]]; [[Gen.20.5|Gen 20:5]]; [[Gen.38.25|Gen 38:25]] (see Masora here), [[Lev.11.39|Lev 11:39]]; [[Lev.13.10|Lev 13:10]]; [[Lev.13.21|Lev 13:21]]; [[Lev.16.31|Lev 16:31]]; [[Lev.20.17|Lev 20:17]]; [[Lev.21.9|Lev 21:9]]; [[Num.5.13|Num 5:13]]; [[Num.5.14|Num 5:14]]. The punctuators, however, sought to assimilate the usage of the Pentateuch to that of the rest of the OT, and accordingly wherever <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הוא‎</span> was construed as a feminine pointed it <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הִוא‎</span> (as a ***[[BDB#Qr|Qr]] perpetuum***). Outside the Pentateuch the same [[BDB#Qr|Qr]] occurs [[1Kgs.17.15|1Kgs 17:15]]; [[Isa.30.33|Isa 30:33]]; [[Job.31.11|Job 31:11]]a — probably for the sake of removing gramm. anomalies: five instances of the converse change, namely of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎היא‎</span> to be read as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הוּא‎</span>, occur for a similar reason, [[1Kgs.17.15|1Kgs 17:15]] (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וַתּאֹכַל הוּאוָֿהִיא‎</span> to be read as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וַתּאֹכַל הִיאוָֿהוּא‎</span>, on account of the feminine verb) [[Ps.73.16|Ps 73:16]]; [[Job.31.11|Job 31:11]]b (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כי הוא זמה והיא עָוֺן‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎פלילים‎</span> to be read as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כי היא זמה והוא עון פלילים‎</span>), [[Eccl.5.8|Eccl 5:8]]; [[1Chr.29.16|1Chr 29:16]]. The origin of the peculiarity in the Pentateuch is uncertain. It can hardly be a real archaism : for the fact that arab., Aramaic, &amp; Ethiopic have distinct forms for masculine &amp; feminine shews that both must have formed part of the original Semitic stock, and consequently of Hebrew as well, from its earliest existence as an independent language. Nor is the peculiarity confined to the Pentateuch: in the Manuscript of the Later Prophets, of A.D. , now at St. Petersburg, published in facsimile by Strack (1876), the feminine occurs written <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הוא‎</span> (see the passages cited in the ***Adnotationes Criticae***, p. 026). In Phoenician both masculine and feminine are alike written <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הא‎</span> ([[BDB#CIS|CIS]] i. [[1Chr.1.9|1Chr 1:9]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מלך צדק הא‎</span>, [[1Chr.1.13|1Chr 1:13]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מלאכת הא‎</span>, [[1Chr.3.10|1Chr 3:10]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אדם הא‎</span>, [[1Chr.1.11|1Chr 1:11]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ממלכת הא‎</span>, [[1Chr.93.2|1Chr 93:2]]; [[1Chr.94.2|1Chr 94:2]]), though naturally this would be ***read as*** <abbr title="transliteration"><u>***hu°***</u></abbr> or <abbr title="transliteration"><u>***hi°***</u></abbr> as occasion required. Hence, as [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] shews that in the older Hebrew [[BDB#MSS|MSS]]. the ***scriptio plena*** was not yet generally introduced, it is probably that originally <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הא‎</span> was written for both genders in Hebrew likewise, and that the epicene <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הוא‎</span> in the Pentateuch originated at a comparatively late epoch in the transmission of the text — perhaps in connection with the assumption, which is partly borne out by facts (compare [[BDB#De|De]]<sup>ZKWL 1880, pp. 393-399</sup>), that in the older language feminine forms were more sparingly used than subsequently.)In usage <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הוּא‎</span> (feminine <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הִיא‎</span>; plural <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֵ֫מָּה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֵם‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׃ הֵ֫נָּה‎</span> see  <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֵ֫מָּה‎</span>) is

**3.** Where, however, the pron. ***follows*** the predicate, its position gives it the minimum of emphasis, and it expresses (or resumes) the subject as unobtrusively as possible: thus 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** [[Gen.12.18|Gen 12:18]] why didst thou not tell me <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כי אשׁתך‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הוא‎</span> that she was thy wife ? $\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** resuming the subject, [[Gen.31.16|Gen 31:16]] all the wealth which God hath taken etc., <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לנו הוא ולבנינו‎</span> ***it is*** ours and our children's, v[[Gen.31.43|Gen 31:43]] and all that thou seest, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לי הוא‎</span> ***it is*** mine (or, omitting the pronoun, as not required in our idiom, simply) ***is*** mine, [[Gen.41.26|Gen 41:26]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חלום פרעה אחד‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הוא‎</span> the dream of Pharaoh ***is*** one, [[Gen.48.5|Gen 48:5]] (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לי הם‎</span>), [[Exod.3.5|Exod 3:5]] for the place where on thou standest, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַדְמַת קֹדֶשׁ הוּא‎</span> ***it*** is holy ground, [[Num.13.32|Num 13:32]]; [[Num.21.26|Num 21:26]]; [[Deut.1.17|Deut 1:17]]; [[Josh.5.15|Josh 5:15]]; [[Josh.6.19|Josh 6:19]]; [[Job.3.19|Job 3:19]] + often; [[Gen.23.15|Gen 23:15]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ארץ ֗֗֗ מַההִֿוא‎</span>, so [[Ps.39.5|Ps 39:5]]; [[Isa.41.22|Isa 41:22]] (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֵנָּה‎</span>); <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֵמָּה‎</span> .... <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַתֶּם‎</span> (unusual) [[Zeph.2.12|Zeph 2:12]]. **4.** It ***anticipates*** (as it seems) the subject namely

(with change of person <span class="bdbLangGreek">κατά</span> <span class="bdbLangGreek">σύνεσιν</span>, compare [[Judg.13.11|Judg 13:11]]; [[1Chr.21.17|1Chr 21:17]]; [[Ezek.38.17|Ezek 38:17]].) So [[BDB#Ew|Ew]]<sup>§ 297 b</sup> [[BDB#Müll|Müll]]<sup>§ 499. **5.** As an emphatic predicate, of God, 'I am He,' i.e. I am He Who ***is*** (opposed to unreal gods, named in context, or to transitory world), the Unseen, yet Omni-present, and Self-consistent, Ruler of the world, [[Deut.32.39|Deut 32:39]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲנִי אֲנִי הוּא‎</span> I, I am ***he***, and beside me there is no God, [[Isa.41.4|Isa 41:4]] (see [[BDB#Che|Che]]) [[Isa.43.10|Isa 43:10]]; [[Isa.43.13|Isa 43:13]] even from to-day I am ***he***, [[Isa.46.4|Isa 46:4]]; [[Isa.48.12|Isa 48:12]]; [[Ps.102.28|Ps 102:28]] (see [[BDB#Che|Che]]) thou art ***he***, and thy years have no end ([[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] usually <span class="bdbLangGreek">ἐγώ εἰμι</span> : in Psalms <span class="bdbLangGreek">σὺ δὲ ὁ αὐτὸς εἶ</span>).
