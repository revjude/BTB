**1.** As mark of the accusative prefixed to substantives defined either by the article (or <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כֹּל‎</span>), or by a genitive or pronominal affix, or in virtue of being proper names: 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** with transitive verbs, [[Gen.1.1|Gen 1:1]]; [[Gen.1.16|Gen 1:16]]; [[Gen.1.29|Gen 1:29]]; [[Gen.1.30|Gen 1:30]]; [[Gen.2.11|Gen 2:11]]; [[Gen.4.1|Gen 4:1]]; [[Gen.4.2|Gen 4:2]]; [[Gen.9.3|Gen 9:3]] (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶתֿכֹּל׃‎</span>) etc. Similarly <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶתמִֿי‎</span> ***whom*** (in particular), [[Josh.24.15|Josh 24:15]]; [[1Sam.12.3|1Sam 12:3]]; [[1Sam.28.11|1Sam 28:11]]; [[Isa.6.8|Isa 6:8]] and elsewhere (but never <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶתמָֿה‎</span>); also with <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎זֶה‎</span> [[Gen.29.33|Gen 29:33]]; [[Gen.44.29|Gen 44:29]]; [[1Sam.21.16|1Sam 21:16]]; [[1Kgs.22.27|1Kgs 22:27]] +, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎זֹאת‎</span> [[Gen.29.27|Gen 29:27]]; [[2Sam.13.27|2Sam 13:27]] +, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵלֶּה‎</span> [[Gen.46.18|Gen 46:18]]; [[Lev.11.18|Lev 11:18]]; [[Isa.49.21|Isa 49:21]] +. So pretty uniformly in prose; but in poetry <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎את‎</span> is commonly dispensed with. By the use of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎את‎</span> with the pronominal affix, a pronoun can at once, if required, be placed in a position of emphasis; let the order of words from this point of view be carefully noticed in the following passages: [[Gen.7.1|Gen 7:1]]; [[Gen.24.14|Gen 24:14]]; [[Gen.37.4|Gen 37:4]]; [[Lev.10.17|Lev 10:17]]; [[Lev.11.33|Lev 11:33]]; [[Num.22.32|Num 22:32]] ***thee*** I had slain, and ***her*** I had kept alive, [[Deut.4.14|Deut 4:14]]; [[Deut.6.13|Deut 6:13]]; [[Deut.6.23|Deut 6:23]]; [[Deut.13.5|Deut 13:5]]; [[Judg.14.3|Judg 14:3]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אוֺתָהּ קַח לִי‎</span> take for me ***her***, [[1Sam.14.35|1Sam 14:35]]; [[1Sam.15.1|1Sam 15:1]]; [[1Sam.18.17|1Sam 18:17]]; [[1Sam.21.10|1Sam 21:10]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אִםאֹֿתָהּ תִּקַּחלְֿךָ קָ֔ח‎</span> if thou wilt take ***that***, take it, [[1Kgs.1.35|1Kgs 1:35]]; [[1Kgs.14.9|1Kgs 14:9]]; [[Isa.43.22|Isa 43:22]]; [[Isa.57.11|Isa 57:11]]; [[Jer.9.2|Jer 9:2]]. So <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַאוֺתִי‎</span> [[Jer.5.22|Jer 5:22]]; [[Jer.7.19|Jer 7:19]]. It also sometimes enables the reflexive sense to be expressed (elsewhere <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎נַפְשָׁם‎</span>) [[Jer.7.19|Jer 7:19]]; [[Ezek.34.2|Ezek 34:2]]. Rarely with a substantive which is undefined ([[BDB#Ew|Ew]]<sup>§ 277 d 2</sup> [[BDB#Ges|Ges]]<sup>«GKC:117»§ 117</sup>, 1, R. 2), as [[Exod.21.28|Exod 21:28]]; [[Num.21.9|Num 21:9]]; [[Lev.20.14|Lev 20:14]]; [[1Sam.24.6|1Sam 24:6]] (but see [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]) [[2Sam.4.11|2Sam 4:11]]; [[2Sam.18.18|2Sam 18:18]]; [[2Sam.23.21|2Sam 23:21]] or which, though definite, is without the article, [[Gen.21.30|Gen 21:30]]; [[2Sam.15.16|2Sam 15:16]]; [[Lev.26.5|Lev 26:5]]; [[1Sam.9.3|1Sam 9:3]] (so [[Num.16.15|Num 16:15]]) [[Isa.33.19|Isa 33:19]]; [[Isa.41.7|Isa 41:7]]; [[Ezek.43.10|Ezek 43:10]] (for further examples see [[BDB#Ew|Ew]] ***1.c.***) 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** with a ***passive*** verb ([[BDB#Ges|Ges]]<sup>«GKC:121»§ 121</sup>. 1 [[BDB#Ew|Ew]]<sup>§ 295 b</sup>) conceived as expressing neutrally the action in question, and construed accordingly with an ***accusative*** of that which is its real object: examples occur with tolerable frequency from [[Gen.4.18|Gen 4:18]] ([[BDB#J|J]]) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וַיִּוָּלֵד לַחֲנוֺךְ‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶתעֿירָד‎</span>, [[Gen.17.5|Gen 17:5]] ([[BDB#P|P]]), <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לֹא יִקָּרֵא עוֺד אֶתשִֿׁמְךָ אַברָם‎</span> ***there shall not be called*** (=one shall not call) ***thy name Abram***, [[Gen.21.5|Gen 21:5]] ([[BDB#E|E]]), [[Gen.27.42|Gen 27:42]]; [[2Sam.21.11|2Sam 21:11]]; [[1Kgs.18.13|1Kgs 18:13]]; [[Hos.10.6|Hos 10:6]] etc., to [[Jer.35.18|Jer 35:18]]; [[Jer.38.4|Jer 38:4]]; [[Jer.50.20|Jer 50:20]]; [[Ezek.16.4|Ezek 16:4]]; [[Ezek.16.5|Ezek 16:5]]; [[Esth.2.13|Esth 2:13]] (compare [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]<sup>JPh xi. 227 f.</sup>): also with passive verbs of filling ([[BDB#Ew|Ew]]<sup>§ 281 b</sup>), as [[Exod.1.7|Exod 1:7]] +. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **c.** with ***neuter*** verbs or expressions, especially such as involve the idea of ***regarding***, or ***treating***, appy. by a construction <span class="bdbLangGreek">κατὰ σύνεσιν</span> (rare), [[Josh.22.17|Josh 22:17]]; [[2Sam.11.25|2Sam 11:25]]; [[Neh.9.32|Neh 9:32]] (compare [[1Sam.20.13|1Sam 20:13]] [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]). Once after <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵין‎</span>, [[Hag.2.17|Hag 2:17]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵין אֶתְכֶם אֵלַיָֽ‎</span>. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **d.** poet. (si vera lectio), after an abstract noun used with a verbal force, [[Hab.3.13|Hab 3:13]] ([[Amos.4.11|Amos 4:11]]; [[Isa.13.19|Isa 13:19]]; [[Jer.50.40|Jer 50:40]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מַהְפֵּכָה‎</span> exerts a verbal force, like the Arabic ***nom. verbi*** [see [[BDB#W|W]]<sup>AG i. § 196, 43</sup>]; and [[Num.10.2|Num 10:2]]; [[Ezek.17.9|Ezek 17:9]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְמַסַּע‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְמַשְׂאוֺת‎</span> are Aramaizing infinitives: compare [[BDB#Ew|Ew]]<sup>§ 239 a</sup>).


**2.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎את‎</span> marks an accusative in other relations than that of direct object to a verb: 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** with verbs of motion (very rare) [[Num.13.17|Num 13:17]]; [[Deut.1.19|Deut 1:19]]; [[Deut.2.7|Deut 2:7]] (to 'walk the wilderness'); denoting the goal [[Judg.19.18|Judg 19:18]]; [[Ezek.21.25|Ezek 21:25]] ([[BDB#Ew|Ew]]<sup>§ 281 d, n., 282 a 1</sup>). 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** denoting time (duration), also very rare: [[Exod.13.7|Exod 13:7]]; [[Lev.25.22|Lev 25:22]]; [[Deut.9.25|Deut 9:25]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **c.** expressing the accus. of limitation (rare): [[Gen.17.11|Gen 17:11]]; [[Gen.17.14|Gen 17:14]]; [[1Kgs.15.23|1Kgs 15:23]].


**3.** Chiefly in an inferior or later style, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵת‎</span> (or <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְאֵת‎</span>) is used irregularly, partly (<span class="bdbLangGreek">α</span>), as it would seem, to give greater definiteness (so especially <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְאֵת‎</span>) at the mention of a new subject (when it may sometimes be rendered ***as regards***), or through the influence of a neighbouring verb (a construct <span class="bdbLangGreek">κατὰ σύνεσιν</span>), or by an anacoluthon, partly (<span class="bdbLangGreek">β</span>) as resuming loosely some other preposition Thus (<span class="bdbLangGreek">α</span>) [[Exod.1.14|Exod 1:14]]; [[Num.3.26|Num 3:26]]; [[Num.3.46|Num 3:46]]; [[Num.5.10|Num 5:10]] (with <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָיָה‎</span>: so [[Ezek.35.10|Ezek 35:10]]) [[Num.18.21|Num 18:21]]b [[Deut.11.2|Deut 11:2]] (anacoluthon), [[Deut.14.13|Deut 14:13]]; [[Josh.17.11|Josh 17:11]]; [[Judg.20.44|Judg 20:44]]; [[Judg.20.46|Judg 20:46]] (contr. v[[Judg.20.25|Judg 20:25]] v[[Judg.20.35|Judg 20:35]]) [[1Sam.17.34|1Sam 17:34]] (see [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]) [[1Sam.26.16|1Sam 26:16]]; [[2Sam.21.22|2Sam 21:22]]; [[2Kgs.6.5|2Kgs 6:5]]; [[Isa.53.8|Isa 53:8]] (probably), [[Isa.57.12|Isa 57:12]]; [[Jer.23.33|Jer 23:33]] (but read rather with [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] [[BDB#Vg|ᵑ9]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַתֶּם הַמַּשָּׂא‎</span>) [[Jer.27.8|Jer 27:8]]; [[Jer.36.22|Jer 36:22]]; [[Jer.38.16|Jer 38:16]] [[BDB#Kt|Kt]], [[Jer.45.4|Jer 45:4]] b [[Ezek.16.22|Ezek 16:22]]; [[Ezek.17.21|Ezek 17:21]]; [[Ezek.20.16|Ezek 20:16]]; [[Ezek.29.4|Ezek 29:4]] b [[Ezek.43.7|Ezek 43:7]] ([[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] [[BDB#Co|Co]] prefix <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲרָאִיתָ‎</span>) [[Ezek.44.3|Ezek 44:3]]; [[Zech.8.17|Zech 8:17]]; [[Eccl.4.3|Eccl 4:3]]; [[Dan.9.13|Dan 9:13]]; [[Neh.9.19|Neh 9:19]]; [[Neh.9.34|Neh 9:34]]; [[1Chr.2.9|1Chr 2:9]]; [[2Chr.31.17|2Chr 31:17]]. In [[1Sam.30.23|1Sam 30:23]]; [[Hag.2.5|Hag 2:5]] probably some such word as ***remember*** is to be understood. (<span class="bdbLangGreek">β</span>) [[Jer.38.9|Jer 38:9]]; [[Ezek.14.22|Ezek 14:22]] b [[Ezek.37.19|Ezek 37:19]] b [[Zech.12.10|Zech 12:10]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎סָבִיב אֵת‎</span> [[1Kgs.6.5|1Kgs 6:5]]; [[Ezek.43.17|Ezek 43:17]] strangely (in 1 Kings [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] omits the clause: so [[BDB#Sta|Sta]]<sup>ZAW 1883, 135</sup>). — In [[1Kgs.11.1|1Kgs 11:1]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְ‎</span> is merely ***and also, and especially*** (see  <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְ‎</span>); v[[1Kgs.11.25|1Kgs 11:25]] is corrupt (read with [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎זֹאת הָרָעָה אֲשֶׁר עָשָׂה הֲדָד‎</span>); [[Ezek.47.17|Ezek 47:17]]; [[Ezek.47.18|Ezek 47:18]]; [[Ezek.47.19|Ezek 47:19]] read similarly for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ואת‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎זֹאת‎</span>: see v[[Ezek.47.20|Ezek 47:20]]. — For some particulars as to the use of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎את‎</span>, see A.
