 [<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֹבֶן‎</span>] **noun [masculine]** ==wheel, disc== — [[BDB#Du|Du]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָאָבְנָ֑יִם‎</span> — 

**1.** potter's ***wheel*** [[Jer.18.3|Jer 18:3]] (two discs revolving one above the other; name from likeness to mill-stones; see [[BDB#AW|AW]]<sup>18</sup>). 

**2.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עַלהָֿא‎</span> [[Exod.1.16|Exod 1:16]] probably = ***sella parturientis***, = <span class="bdbLangGreek">δίφροι λοχειαῖοι</span> ***bearing-stool, midwife's stool*** (from likeness to potter's wheel; on custom of labor upon stool see Ploss<sup>Das Weib, 2nd ed. ii, 35, 179 etc. &amp; Cesnola collective</sup> from Cyprus, Metrop. Mus., N. York, No. 614, terra cotta figures from  4th or 5th cent. B.C.; Descriptive Atlas of Cesn. Collective<sup>l, plural lxvi. No. 435</sup>; compare W. H. W[ard]<sup>PESoc. 2nd Statement 1873, p. 76</sup>). Spiegelberg<sup>ZA xiv {1900}, 269 ff.; Randglossen 19 ff.</sup> explained as ***the two stones*** [read <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַבְנַיִם‎</span>], i.e. bearing-stool of 'stones' = bricks, tiles.
