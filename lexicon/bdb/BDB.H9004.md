**3.** before an infinitive: 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** ***like*** (literally ***with*** or ***in the likeness of . . .***), [[Judg.14.6|Judg 14:6]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּשַׁסַּע הַגְּדִי‎</span> ***like*** the rending of a kid, i.e. ***as when*** a kid is rent (by a lion), [[2Sam.3.34|2Sam 3:34]]; [[Isa.5.24|Isa 5:24]]; [[Ps.66.10|Ps 66:10]]; [[Ps.68.3|Ps 68:3]]; [[Job.2.10|Job 2:10]], etc.; = ***as if***, [[Isa.10.15|Isa 10:15]] or shall the saw magnify itself against him that wieldeth it <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כהניף שׁבט אתמֿרימיו‎</span> ***with the like of*** a staff's shaking (= ***as if*** a staff were to shake) him that lifted it ! 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** of time, ***about, at***, whether of the past or of the future: [[Gen.19.17|Gen 19:17]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כהוציאם‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אותם‎</span> ***at*** their bringing them forth, i.e. ***as*** or ***when*** they brought them forth [[Gen.24.30|Gen 24:30]]; [[Gen.29.13|Gen 29:13]]; [[Gen.39.10|Gen 39:10]]; [[Gen.39.13|Gen 39:13]] + very often; of the future, [[Gen.44.30|Gen 44:30]]; [[Gen.44.31|Gen 44:31]]; [[Deut.20.2|Deut 20:2]]; [[Deut.20.9|Deut 20:9]]; [[2Sam.13.28|2Sam 13:28]] +; [[Isa.28.20|Isa 28:20]]b (different from v[[Isa.28.29|Isa 28:29]] a) and the covering is (too) narrow ***when one gathers oneself*** in. **Note.** — <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּ‎</span> is rarely prefixed to prepositions, the only examples which occur being the idiomatic <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּבָרִאשֹׁנָה‎</span>, [[Judg.20.32|Judg 20:32]]; [[1Kgs.13.6|1Kgs 13:6]]; [[Isa.1.26|Isa 1:26]]; [[Jer.33.7|Jer 33:7]]; [[Jer.33.11|Jer 33:11]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּבַתְּחִלָּה‎</span> in the \|\| clause Isaiah l.c.; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּבַחֲצִי‎</span> [[1Sam.14.14|1Sam 14:14]] (apparently corrupt); <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּמִשְׁלשׁ חֳדָשִׁים‎</span> [[Gen.38.24|Gen 38:24]] (and probably <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְמֵחֹדֶשׁ‎</span> [[1Sam.10.27|1Sam 10:27]] [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]]); <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּמִפְּנֵי‎</span> [[Lev.26.37|Lev 26:37]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּעַל‎</span> (late): 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** pleon. for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּ‎</span> (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עַל‎</span> = ***according to***), [[Isa.63.7|Isa 63:7]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּעַל כָּלאֲֿשֶׁר עָשָׂה‎</span>, [[Isa.59.18|Isa 59:18]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּעַל גְּמֻלוֺת כְּעַל יְשַׁלֵּם‎</span> ***the like of*** their deeds is ***the like of*** (that which) he will repay (ellipse of rel., as [[Job.34.32|Job 34:32]], etc.); 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** = ***as over*** [[Ps.119.14|Ps 119:14]], = ***as concerning*** [[2Chr.32.19|2Chr 32:19]]. Where logically a preposition would seem to be required after <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כ‎</span>, it is in Hebrew regularly omitted ([[BDB#Ges|Ges]]<sup>«GKC:118»§ 118</sup>. 3 R), the nature of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּ‎</span>, as explained above, not in fact admitting it; thus [[Isa.1.25|Isa 1:25]] I will purge away thy dross <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּבֹּר‎</span> [not, as in English, ***like as with*** lye, but] (with) ***the like of*** lye (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כ‎</span> being an accusative defining the mode in which <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אצרף‎</span> takes place); [[Isa.5.17|Isa 5:17]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּדָבְרָם‎</span> in English as ***in*** their pasture, [[Judg.20.39|Judg 20:39]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּיוֺם‎</span> as ***in*** day of, [[Isa.9.3|Isa 9:3]]; [[Ps.95.8|Ps 95:8]] +, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כִּימֵי‎</span> [[Job.29.3|Job 29:3]] who will set me as ***in*** the days of old ! [[Isa.51.9|Isa 51:9]] +, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּנֹקֶף זַיִת‎</span> as ***at***, etc., [[Isa.17.6|Isa 17:6]]: so with <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְ‎</span> [[Ps.83.10|Ps 83:10]]; [[Job.29.23|Job 29:23]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎על‎</span> [[Job.30.5|Job 30:5]]. <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּ‎</span> is used also pregnantly with substantives, [[Ps.18.34|Ps 18:34]] who meketh my feet ***like hinds*** (for ***like the feet of hinds***), [[Isa.29.4|Isa 29:4]]; [[Isa.63.2|Isa 63:2]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בגדיך כדורך בגת‎</span>, [[Jer.50.9|Jer 50:9]]; [[Job.40.9|Job 40:9]] and elsewhere compare [[BDB#Ges|Ges]]<sup>§§ 118. 6; 141. 2 R. ***n.***</sup>
