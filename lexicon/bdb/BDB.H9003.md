Preposition denoting properly ***in***, Greek <span class="bdbLangGreek">ἐν</span>, but applied in many derived and figurative significations. The senses expressed by <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּ‎</span> are grouped by the Rabbis in three classes, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בֵּית הַכְּלִי‎</span> ***Beth vasculi***, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בֵּית הַדִּיבִּוּק וְהַנְּגִיעָה‎</span> ***Beth coniunctionis et viciniae***, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בֵּית הָעֵזֶר‎</span> ***Beth auxilii***; and the same arrangement may be followed here, though the limits between the three classes are not clearly defined, and they sometimes overlap one another.**I.** ***In:*** 

**1.** strictly, of position ***in*** a place (which often is expressed more precisely by <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּקֶרֶב‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּתוֺךְ‎</span>), as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בַּבַּיִת‎</span> ***in*** the house, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּעִיר‎</span> ***in*** the city, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בַּדּוּד‎</span> ***in*** the pot, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּאָרֶץ‎</span> ***in*** the land, etc. constantly. Hebrew idiom also says <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּהָר‎</span> ***in*** the mountain [[Exod.24.18|Exod 24:18]] etc., even in cases where we could hardly avoid saying ***on***, as [[1Kgs.11.7|1Kgs 11:7]]; [[1Kgs.19.11|1Kgs 19:11]]: so <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּחֹרֵב‎</span> [[Deut.1.16|Deut 1:16]] and elsewhere; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּראֹשׁ הָהָר‎</span> [[Exod.24.17|Exod 24:17]]; [[Ps.72.16|Ps 72:16]]. Preceded by a verb of motion (especially <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עָבַר‎</span>) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּ‎</span> = ***through***, as [[Gen.12.6|Gen 12:6]] and Abram passed through <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּאָרֶץ‎</span> ***in*** the land = passed through it, [[Gen.13.17|Gen 13:17]]; [[2Sam.24.2|2Sam 24:2]] +; ***in*** (= ***through***) a gate, [[Isa.62.10|Isa 62:10]]; [[Jer.17.19|Jer 17:19]]; [[Mic.2.13|Mic 2:13]]. Figurative to speak <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּאָזְנֵי‎</span> ***in*** the ears of ...; to be good (***or*** evil, etc.) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּעֵינֵי‎</span> ***in*** the eyes of ...

**IV.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּ‎</span> is used also with certain classes of verbs, though the explanation of its use may be sometimes doubtful: namely 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** with verbs of ***taking refuge, trusting, relying***, as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֶאֱמִין‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּטַח‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חָסָה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎נִשְׁעַן‎</span>. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** with verbs of ***ruling, governing, restraining***, as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מָלַךְ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מָשַׁל‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎נָגַשׂ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עָצַר‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎רָדָה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שָׁלַט‎</span>. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **c.** with verbs of ***rejoicing, feeling pleasure*** or ***satisfaction***, etc., as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎גִּיל‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עָלַץ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שׂוּשׂ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שָׂמֵחַ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חָפֵץ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎רָצָה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שָׂבַע‎</span> (but with this verb <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִן‎</span> is more common). [Probably a case of

$\quad$ $\quad$ **d.** with verbs expressive of sensible perception, to denote the pleasurable or attentive exercise of the faculty concerned, as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שָׁמַע בְּ‎</span> to listen ***to***, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הִבִּיט בְּ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎רָאָה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חָזָה‎</span>, to look ***upon***, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֵרִיחַ בְּ‎</span> to smell ***at*** (see these words). 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **e.** occasionally also with verbs of ***speaking, thinking, mentioning, knowing***, to denote the object of the action, as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דִּבֶּר בְּ‎</span> to speak ***about*** [[Deut.6.7|Deut 6:7]]; [[Ps.87.3|Ps 87:3]] and elsewhere (see below <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דִּבֶּר‎</span>); <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הִלֵּל בְּ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָגָה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שִׂיחַ‎</span> [[Ps.44.9|Ps 44:9]]; [[Ps.63.7|Ps 63:7]]; [[Ps.69.13|Ps 69:13]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יָדַע‎</span> [[Jer.38.24|Jer 38:24]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎זָכַר‎</span> once [[Jer.3.16|Jer 3:16]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הִזְכִּיר‎</span> often; [[Ps.71.6|Ps 71:6]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּךָ תְהִלָּתִי‎</span> ***of*** thee is my praise.

**V.** Followed by an infinitive with, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּ‎</span> forms a periphrasis for the gerund, though in English it is commonly to be rendered by a verb and conjunction, namely: — 

**1.** as a ***temporal*** conjunction, as [[Gen.2.4|Gen 2:4]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּהִבָּֽרְאָם‎</span> ***in*** their ***being created*** = ***when*** they ***were*** created, [[Gen.4.8|Gen 4:8]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בִּהְיוֺתָם‎</span> ***in*** their ***being*** (= ***when*** they ***were***) in the field; and constantly. Sometimes it has in appearance the force of ***after that***, as [[Gen.33.18|Gen 33:18]]; [[Exod.3.12|Exod 3:12]]; [[Exod.13.17|Exod 13:17]] but as a rule this is really due to the action denoted by the infinitive being treated as extending over a period ***within*** which the action of the principal verb takes place: so especially in the phrase <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּצֵאת יִשְׂרָאֵל‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִמִּצְרַיִם‎</span>, even of events at the close of the 40 years, [[Deut.4.45|Deut 4:45]]; [[Deut.23.5|Deut 23:5]]; [[Josh.5.4|Josh 5:4]], the whole period being treated as that in which Egypt was left (compare [[2Kgs.2.1|2Kgs 2:1]], where the time included is future). Cases, however, occur in which this explanation will hardly apply, as [[Deut.27.4|Deut 27:4]]; [[Deut.24.12|Deut 24:12]]. 

**2.** as a ***causal*** conjunction (compare above **III. 5**), as [[Gen.19.16|Gen 19:16]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּחֶמְלַת י‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עָלָיו‎</span> ***through*** J.'s having compassion upon him, [[Exod.16.7|Exod 16:7]]; [[Exod.33.16|Exod 33:16]]; [[Exod.34.29|Exod 34:29]]; [[Deut.1.27|Deut 1:27]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֹתָנוּ‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּשִׂנְאַת י‎</span> ***through*** J.'s hating us, etc. ([[Deut.9.28|Deut 9:28]] similarly <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִן‎</span>), [[1Kgs.18.18|1Kgs 18:18]] (= ***in that*** ye have ...) [[Ezek.9.8|Ezek 9:8]]; [[Ezek.43.8|Ezek 43:8]]; [[Ezek.44.7|Ezek 44:7]]; [[2Chr.28.6|2Chr 28:6]]. 

**3.** as a ***concessive*** conjunction, ***when*** = ***though:*** [[Isa.1.15|Isa 1:15]]; [[Ps.46.3|Ps 46:3]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּהָמִיר אָ֑רֶץ‎</span> ***though*** the earth do change.


**Note.** — [[Exod.10.12|Exod 10:12]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּאַרְבֶּה‎</span> can only be rendered '***with*** the locusts,' the locusts being conceived as implicit in Moses' uplifted hand: but probably <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לָאַרְבֶּה‎</span> should be read. Thrice in late Hebrew <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּ‎</span> is used peculiarly: [[1Chr.7.23|1Chr 7:23]] for ***with*** misfortune was it in his house (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּרָעָה‎</span> chosen for the purpose of explaining <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּרִיעָה‎</span>); [[1Chr.9.33|1Chr 9:33]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עֲלֵיהֶם בַּמְּלָאכָה‎</span> it devolved upon them ***with*** the work; [[Ezra.3.3|Ezra 3:3]] for ***with*** terror (was it) upon them from the peoples of the countries (the sentence without a verb as often in Chronicles: [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]<sup>Intr. 504 f.</sup>): compare [[Ezra.8.34|Ezra 8:34]]. compare [[BDB#Ew|Ew]]<sup>§ 295 f</sup>.
