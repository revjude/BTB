**8.** Expressing presence at a spot, ***against; at, by***, not merely after verbs expressing or implying motion (compare **1**, [[Gen.24.11|Gen 24:11]]), as [[Josh.11.5|Josh 11:5]] and they came and encamped together <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלמֵֿי מֵרוֺם‎</span> ***at*** the waters of Merom, [[1Sam.5.4|1Sam 5:4]] cut off (and fallen) ***on to*** the threshold, [[2Sam.2.23|2Sam 2:23]] and elsewhere and smote him <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלהַֿחֹמֶשׁ‎</span> ***in*** or ***on*** the belly, [[Deut.33.28|Deut 33:28]]; [[Exod.29.12|Exod 29:12]]; [[Lev.4.7|Lev 4:7]] but also in other cases, as [[Josh.5.3|Josh 5:3]] and he circumcised the Israelites <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶל‎</span> ***against, at*** the hill of the foreskins, [[Josh.22.11|Josh 22:11]] have built an altar <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶל גְּלִילוֺת הַיַּרְדֵּן‎</span> ***by*** the districts of Jordan, [[Judg.12.6|Judg 12:6]] &amp; [[2Sam.18.4|2Sam 18:4]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶליַֿד‎</span> ***at*** the side of (elsewhere <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְיַד‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עַל יַד‎</span>), [[1Kgs.13.20|1Kgs 13:20]] as they were sitting <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלהַֿשֻּׁלְחָן‎</span> ***at*** the table, [[2Kgs.11.14|2Kgs 11:14]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלהַֿמֶּלֶךְ‎</span> ***by*** the king, [[Jer.41.12|Jer 41:12]] and found him ***by*** the great waters, etc., [[Jer.46.10|Jer 46:10]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלנְֿהַר פְּרָת‎</span> ***by*** the Euphrates, [[Ezek.3.15|Ezek 3:15]]; [[Ezek.11.11|Ezek 11:11]]; [[Ezek.17.8|Ezek 17:8]]; [[Ezek.31.7|Ezek 31:7]]; [[Ezek.40.18|Ezek 40:18]]; [[Ezek.43.3|Ezek 43:3]]; [[Ezek.47.7|Ezek 47:7]]; [[Ezek.48.12|Ezek 48:12]].


**9.** Prefixed to other preps. it combines with them the idea of ***motion*** or ***direction to:*** thus <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלאַֿחֲרֵי‎</span> [[2Sam.5.23|2Sam 5:23]]; [[2Kgs.9.18|2Kgs 9:18]]; [[2Kgs.9.19|2Kgs 9:19]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎סֹב אֶלאַֿחֲרַי‎</span> turn ***to*** behind me, [[Zech.6.5|Zech 6:5]] (where <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶל‎</span> is pleonastic, probably due to clerical error; note <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יצא אל‎</span> before &amp; after); <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלבֵּֿין‎</span> ***in between*** [[Ezek.31.10|Ezek 31:10]]; [[Ezek.31.14|Ezek 31:14]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵלבֵּֿינוֺת‎</span> similarly [[Ezek.10.2|Ezek 10:2]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלמִֿבֵּית לְ‎</span> ***to*** (the part) ***within*** (see below <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בַּיִת‎</span>), ***in within*** [[Lev.16.15|Lev 16:15]]; [[2Kgs.11.15|2Kgs 11:15]] \|\| have her forth ***in within*** the ranks; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלמֿוּל‎</span> see  <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מוּל‎</span>; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלמִֿנֶּגֶב לְ‎</span> ***unto*** the south of [[Josh.15.3|Josh 15:3]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלמִֿחוּץ לְ‎</span> ***to*** the outside of [[Lev.4.12|Lev 4:12]]; [[Lev.4.21|Lev 4:21]] +; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלנֹֿכַח‎</span> ***to*** the front of [[Num.19.4|Num 19:4]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלתַּֿחַת‎</span> [[Judg.6.19|Judg 6:19]]; [[1Kgs.8.6|1Kgs 8:6]] and elsewhere (see below <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎תַּחַת‎</span>).


**Note** I. — In [[Gen.20.13|Gen 20:13]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלכָּֿלהַֿמָּקוֺם אֲשֶׁר נָבוֺא‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שָׁמָּה אִמְרִילִֿי וג‎</span>; [[Num.33.54|Num 33:54]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלאֲֿשֶׁר וג‎</span>; [[Prov.17.8|Prov 17:8]] (compare [[Deut.16.6|Deut 16:6]]); — <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶל‎</span> appears to be used by a species of attraction; the idea of motion involved in the relative clause influencing illogically the beginning of the sentence and causing <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶל‎</span> to be used instead of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּ‎</span>. In [[Deut.31.14|Deut 31:14]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵלֵיהֶם‎</span>, as pointed, can only be from <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַיִל‎</span> III or IV (q. v.); if the word be taken as the Pronoun with suffix ([[BDB#Hi|Hi]] [[BDB#Ke|Ke]]), <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲלֵיהֶם‎</span> must be read.


**Note** 2. — There is a tendency in Hebrew, especially manifest in Samuel Kings Jeremiah Ezekiel, to use <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶל‎</span> in the sense of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עַל‎</span>; sometimes <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶל‎</span> being used exceptionally in a phrase or construction which regularly, and in accusative with analogy, has <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עַל‎</span>; sometimes, the two prepositions interchanging, apparently without discrimination, in the same or parallel sentences. Thus
