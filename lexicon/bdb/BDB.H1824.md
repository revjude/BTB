 <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דֳּמִי‎</span> **noun [masculine]** ==cessation, pause, quiet, rest==; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ד‎</span> [[Isa.62.6|Isa 62:6]] + 2 t.; construct <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דְּמִי‎</span> [[Isa.38.10|Isa 38:10]] (but see below); — 

**1.** ***quiet***, in phrase <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לָכֶם‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַלדֿ‎</span> [[Isa.62.6|Isa 62:6]] ***keep not quiet*** (let there be ***no quiet to you***); in prayer to God <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לָךְ‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַלדֿ‎</span> [[Ps.83.2|Ps 83:2]] ***keep not quiet*** (inactive; \|\| <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חרשׂ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שׁקט‎</span>); compare <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לוֺ‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְאַלתִּֿתְּנוּ ד‎</span> [[Isa.62.7|Isa 62:7]] ***and give no rest to him*** (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎י‎</span>). 

**2.** in phrase <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בִּדְמִי יָמַי‎</span>, apparently ***quiet, peacefulness***, even tenour, of my days (so [[BDB#De|De]] Or [[BDB#SS|SS]]); others, as [[BDB#Hi|Hi]] [[BDB#Ew|Ew]] [[BDB#Che|Che]] [[BDB#Di|Di]], ***pause, resting-time***, i.e. ***noon-day*** (compare [[BDB#Syr|ᵑ6]] [[BDB#Vg|ᵑ9]], &amp; ***height*** [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]]), figurative of middle life, but usage dubious; [[BDB#Klo|Klo]]<sup>SK 1884, 157</sup> ***cessation, pause***, of natural end of life, Hezekiah's natural expectation, in, contrast with the speedy death implied in v<sup>b</sup>; but parallelism of v[[Isa.62.11|Isa 62:11]]a is ag. this; [[BDB#Brd|Brd]] emends <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּרֻם‎</span> after [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]]; [[BDB#Klo|Klo]]<sup>l.c. suggests</sup> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּתֹם‎</span> or <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּתֹם‎</span> as possible, though not necessary; — but view stated first is on the whole best.
