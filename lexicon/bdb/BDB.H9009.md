<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎<big>ה</big>‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ה‎</span>, ***He***, fifth letter; = numeral 5 in post-Biblical Hebrew; no evidence of this usage in OT times. 

<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַּ ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֶ‎</span> (on the use of these different forms, see the Grammars: e.g. [[BDB#Ges|Ges]]<sup>«GKC:35»§ 35</sup>), **definite article**, ==the== (so Moabite, Phoenician ([[BDB#Schröd|Schröd]]<sup>§ 62-4</sup>), <abbr title="transliteration"><u>Liµyan</u></abbr> (Northwest Arabia) <abbr title="transliteration"><u>***ha***</u></abbr> ([[BDB#DHM|DHM]]<sup>Epigr. Denkm. 4. 13. 58 ff.</sup>); not Assyrian Aramaic or Ethiopic: Arabic <img class="bdbSeriacGlyph" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhFAAeAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAAAAAAALAAAAAAUAB4AAAJEjI+pyx0AnYJxAmvqo3eezjVcJyFkiabng2qa477MKy90e9bYt85mb/u1EsDdZmgi9m5JI0tIlMh0ylHxN0ptrtqupAAAOw==">, of which, before dentals, sibilants, and liquids, the <abbr title="transliteration"><u>***l***</u></abbr> is written but not pronounced, thus <img class="bdbSeriacGlyph" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhPwAdAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAAAAAAALAAAAAA/AB0AAAKWjI+py+0Po5y02gdylpuDi2ia1zniBwYitWIHmrQpM0IdHL70XGJ1eFr0WEMX7YMDooqzpuKWdEp1BubUc6ReLVZcdOtTfUtfcGPDJJu3sLJpqa1aJ+2oW4zPv345W/35diI4iDYohnS4FzNH2Oj42FN0F6kVhFQoV6WnGPMWwWfWdbcW+DRHGtiIeuG4miLoGis7S1oAADs="> pronounced <abbr title="transliteration"><u>***'ash-shamsu***</u></abbr> = Hebrew <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַשֶּׁמֶשׁ‎</span>) — in general the use of the article in Hebrew is analogous to its use in Greek or German: but naturally there are applications peculiar to Hebrew (compare with what follows [[BDB#Ges|Ges]]<sup>«GKC:126»§ 126</sup> [[BDB#Ew|Ew]]<sup>§ 277</sup>): — 

**1.** joined with ***substantives:*** 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** to mark a definite concrete object, as [[Gen.1.1|Gen 1:1]] ***the*** heavens and ***the*** earth, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַמֶּלֶךְ‎</span> the king, etc. Never, however (as in Greek e.g. <span class="bdbLangGreek">ὁ Πλάτων</span>), before true proper names, though it is used with certain terms, chiefly geographical, of which the original appellative sense has not been lost, as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַבַּעַל‎</span> 'Baal,' literally 'the lord,' in plural <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַבְּעָלִים‎</span> i.e. the various local Baals, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַשָּׂטָן‎</span> ***the*** Adversary [[Job.1.6|Job 1:6]]ff. (as a proper name <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שָׂטָן‎</span>, 'Satan,' only [[1Chr.21.1|1Chr 21:1]]); <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַלְּבָנוֺן‎</span> (but not <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַחֶרְמוֺן‎</span>), <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַיַּרְדֵּן‎</span> (but not <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָאַרְנֹן‎</span>), <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַכַּרְמֶל‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַשָּׁרוֺן‎</span> the Sharon, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַשְּׁפֵלָה‎</span> the (Judaean) lowland, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַכִּכָּר‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַמִּישׁוֺר‎</span> the (Moabite) table-land, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָעֲרָבָה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָעַי‎</span> '\`Ai,' <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַגִּבְעָה‎</span> 'Gibeah,' [[Judg.20.5|Judg 20:5]]ff., <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַגָּלִיל‎</span> 'Galilee,' <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַגִּלְגָּל‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַגִּלְבֹּעַ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַבָּשָׁן‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַגִּלְעָד‎</span> (often), <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַשִּׁטִּים‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַמִּצְפָּה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָרָמָה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַיְשִׁימוֺן‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָעֹפֶל‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַפִּסְגָּה‎</span>. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** with an adjective to denote one who exhibits a quality <span class="bdbLangGreek">κατ’ ἐξοχήν</span>, i.e. to express the comparative or superlative degree: so often, as [[Gen.1.16|Gen 1:16]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎המאור הגדל‎</span> the ***greater*** light, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎המאור‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הקטן‎</span> the ***lesser*** light, [[Gen.27.1|Gen 27:1]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בנו הגדול‎</span> = his ***elder*** son, [[Gen.48.14|Gen 48:14]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הצעיר‎</span> the ***younger***, [[Gen.42.13|Gen 42:13]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַקָּטֹן‎</span> the little one, i.e. the ***youngest***, (of Joseph's brethren), [[Lev.21.10|Lev 21:10]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַגָּדוֺל מֵאֶחָיו‎</span> the ***chiefest*** of his brethren, [[Num.35.25|Num 35:25]] + <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הכהן הגדול‎</span> the ***chief*** priest, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הצעיר ב‎</span> the ***least*** among [[Judg.6.15|Judg 6:15]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הצעירה מן‎</span> the ***least*** of [[1Sam.9.21|1Sam 9:21]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַיָּפָה ב‎</span> the ***fairest*** among [[Song.1.8|Song 1:8]]; [[2Kgs.10.3|2Kgs 10:3]]; [[Prov.30.30|Prov 30:30]], compare [[Job.14.15|Job 14:15]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **c.** with nouns which are not definite in themselves, but ***acquire their definition from the context, or from the manner in which they are introduced:*** thus (<span class="bdbLangGreek">α</span>) in the standing phrases <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַיּוֺם‎</span> ***to-day***, [[Gen.4.14|Gen 4:14]]; [[Gen.21.26|Gen 21:26]] + often; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַלַּיְלָה‎</span> ***to-night***, [[Gen.19.5|Gen 19:5]]; [[Gen.30.15|Gen 30:15]] +, once [[1Sam.15.16|1Sam 15:16]] ***last night***; so <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַשָּׁנָה‎</span> ***this*** year, [[2Kgs.19.29|2Kgs 19:29]]; [[Jer.28.16|Jer 28:16]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַפַּעַם‎</span> ***this*** time, [[Gen.18.32|Gen 18:32]] +. (<span class="bdbLangGreek">β</span>) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַנָּהָר‎</span> ***the*** river (<span class="bdbLangGreek">κατ’ ἐξοχήν</span>), i.e. the Euphrates; [[Exod.2.15|Exod 2:15]] ***the well***, the well namely of the district, [[Josh.8.11|Josh 8:11]] ***the*** valley, [[1Sam.17.3|1Sam 17:3]]; [[1Sam.19.10|1Sam 19:10]] ***the*** wall, v[[1Sam.19.13|1Sam 19:13]] ***the*** bed, [[1Sam.20.21|1Sam 20:21]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַנַּעַר‎</span> ***the*** lad (whom Jonathan would naturally take with him), v[[1Sam.20.34|1Sam 20:34]] ***the*** table. Hence occas. where a suffix would define the noun more precisely, as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַחֲמוֺר‎</span> [[2Sam.19.27|2Sam 19:27]] + = ***my*** ass, [[Judg.3.20|Judg 3:20]]; [[1Sam.1.9|1Sam 1:9]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַכִּסֵּא‎</span> = ***his*** seat, [[Judg.4.15|Judg 4:15]]; [[1Kgs.22.35|1Kgs 22:35]]; [[2Kgs.10.15|2Kgs 10:15]] + <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַמֶּרְכָּבָה‎</span>, [[1Sam.18.10|1Sam 18:10]]; [[1Sam.20.33|1Sam 20:33]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַחֲנִית‎</span>. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **d.** it is a peculiarity of Hebrew thought to conceive an object as defined by its being ***taken for a particular purpose***, and thus by a kind of prolepsis to prefix the article to the noun denoting it: [[1Sam.10.1|1Sam 10:1]] and Samuel took <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶתפַּֿךְ הַשֶּׁמֶן‎</span> literally ***the*** cruse of oil, not, however, a cruse which had been defined previously, but one rendered definiteby ***being now taken***; in English idiom '***a*** cruse of oil,' v[[1Sam.10.25|1Sam 10:25]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בַּסֵּפֶר‎</span> literally in ***the*** scroll or book, the one, namely taken for the purpose, i.e. in ***a*** scroll (so [[Exod.17.14|Exod 17:14]]; [[Num.5.23|Num 5:23]]; [[Job.19.23|Job 19:23]]), [[Job.21.10|Job 21:10]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בַּשִּׂמְלָה‎</span>, [[Judg.4.18|Judg 4:18]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וַתְּכַסֵּהוּ בַּשְּׂמִיכָה‎</span>, v[[Judg.4.19|Judg 4:19]]; [[Judg.7.13|Judg 7:13]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָאֹהֶל‎</span> ***a*** tent, [[Judg.8.25|Judg 8:25]]; [[Judg.9.48|Judg 9:48]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶתהַֿקַּרְדֻּמֹּת‎</span> ***hatchets***, [[Judg.20.16|Judg 20:16]] every one able to sling <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּאֶבֶן אֶלהַֿשַּׂעֲרָה‎</span> with ***a*** stone at ***a*** hair, [[1Sam.6.8|1Sam 6:8]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּאַרְגָּז‎</span> (unless indeed the <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַרְגָּז‎</span> was an understood appendage in every cart), [[Num.11.27|Num 11:27]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַנַּעַר‎</span>, ***a*** young man, [[Num.13.23|Num 13:23]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בַּמּוֺט‎</span> on ***a*** pole, [[Josh.2.15|Josh 2:15]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בַּחֶבֶל‎</span> with ***a*** cord, [[2Sam.17.17|2Sam 17:17]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎השׁפחה‎</span> ***a*** girl (compare [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]<sup>[[1Sam.1.4|1Sam 1:4]]; [[1Sam.19.13|1Sam 19:13]]</sup>). Sometimes it is uncertain whether an article is to be referred to **c** or **d**: e.g. [[1Sam.2.13|1Sam 2:13]] ***his*** prong or ***a*** prong, [[2Sam.18.9|2Sam 18:9]] ***his*** mule or ***a*** mule, etc. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **e.** with nouns that denote ***objects or classes of objects that are known to all***, as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַצּאֹן‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַזָּהָב‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַמַּיִם‎</span>; [[Gen.13.2|Gen 13:2]] Abram was very rich <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בַּמִּקְנֶה בַּכֶּסֶף וּבַזָּהָב‎</span>, [[Exod.31.4|Exod 31:4]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לַעֲשׂוֺת בַּזָּהָב וּבַכֶּסֶף‎</span>, [[Deut.14.26|Deut 14:26]] and thou shalt lay out the money <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בַּבָּקָר‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וּבַצּאֹן וּבַיַּיִן וּבַשֵּׁכָר‎</span>, [[2Kgs.9.30|2Kgs 9:30]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וַתָּשֶׂם בַּפּוּךְ עֵינֶיהָ‎</span>, in French 'elle mit ***du*** fard à ses yeux.' It is, however, remarkable that this usage depends mostly on the punctuation, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַכֶּסֶף‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַיַּיִן‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַלֶּחֶם‎</span> etc. (except as applied to denote ***definite*** quantities of gold, wine, etc., as [[Josh.6.24|Josh 6:24]]) being far less common than <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כֶּסֶף‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יַיִן‎</span> etc., but <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בַּכֶּסֶף‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לַכֶּסֶף‎</span> etc. being much more frequently than <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּכֶסֶף‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְכֶסֶף‎</span> etc.: for instances in which the article forms part of the consonantal text, see [[Gen.6.20|Gen 6:20]]; [[Gen.7.8|Gen 7:8]] + <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎העוף‎</span> and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הבהמה‎</span>, [[Deut.8.3|Deut 8:3]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַלָּחֶם‎</span>, [[1Kgs.5.8|1Kgs 5:8]]; [[1Kgs.5.13|1Kgs 5:13]]; [[Isa.28.7|Isa 28:7]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַיַּיִן‎</span> and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַשֵּׁכָר‎</span>, [[Isa.60.17|Isa 60:17]]; [[Ezek.15.4|Ezek 15:4]]; [[Ezek.15.7|Ezek 15:7]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָאֵשׁ‎</span>, [[Hab.2.5|Hab 2:5]]; [[Prov.20.1|Prov 20:1]]; [[Ps.65.14|Ps 65:14]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַצּאֹן‎</span>, [[Song.1.11|Song 1:11]]; [[Eccl.7.12|Eccl 7:12]]. compare below, **h. feminine** in ***comparisons***, the object compared being, as a rule, not an individual as such, but one exhibiting the characteristics of a class: [[Isa.1.18|Isa 1:18]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּשָּׁנִים‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּתּוֺלָע‎</span> like scarlet, like crimson (both meant generally), [[Isa.5.24|Isa 5:24]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּמַּק יִהְיֶה‎</span>, v[[Isa.5.28|Isa 5:28]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּצַּר‎</span> and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּסּוּפָה‎</span>, [[Isa.10.14|Isa 10:14]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּקֵּן‎</span>, [[Isa.13.8|Isa 13:8]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּיּוֺלֵדָה‎</span> (as always with this word, e.g. [[Isa.42.14|Isa 42:14]]; [[Ps.48.7|Ps 48:7]]), [[Ps.11.7|Ps 11:7]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּבָּקָר‎</span>; + often (The usage is not, however, quite uniform, at least according to the punctuation: there occurs e.g. <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּקַשׁ‎</span> [[Job.41.21|Job 41:21]]; [[Isa.47.14|Isa 47:14]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּאַרְיֵה‎</span> [[Ps.7.3|Ps 7:3]] and elsewhere: and we find both <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּלָּבִיא‎</span>) [[Isa.5.29|Isa 5:29]] and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּלָבִיא‎</span> [[Hos.13.8|Hos 13:8]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כָּאֲרִי‎</span> [[Isa.38.13|Isa 38:13]] and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּאֲרִי‎</span> [[Num.24.9|Num 24:9]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּגִּבּוֺר‎</span> [[Isa.42.13|Isa 42:13]] and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּגבור‎</span> [[Job.16.14|Job 16:14]] etc.) Similarly [[Gen.19.28|Gen 19:28]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּקִיטֹר הַכִּבְשָׁן‎</span> as the smoke of ***a*** furnace, [[Num.11.8|Num 11:8]]; [[Num.11.12|Num 11:12]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּאֲשֶׁר יִשָּׂא‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָאֹמֵן אֶתהַֿיֹּנֵק‎</span>, [[Deut.28.29|Deut 28:29]] (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָעִוֵּר‎</span>), [[Judg.8.18|Judg 8:18]]b <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּתֹאַר בְּנֵי‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַמֶּלֶךְ‎</span>, [[Judg.14.6|Judg 14:6]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּשַׁסַּע הַגְּדִי‎</span> like (a lion's) rending ***a*** kid, [[Judg.16.9|Judg 16:9]]; [[1Kgs.14.15|1Kgs 14:15]]; [[Zech.12.10|Zech 12:10]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּ מִסְפֵּד עַל הַיָּחִיד‎</span> as mourning over ***an*** only child, [[Zech.13.9|Zech 13:9]] +. Where, however, the standard of comparison is not the class in general, but only a particular part of it, defined by a special epithet (whether adjective or verb), the article is naturally omitted: thus <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּמֹּץ‎</span> like chaff (in general) [[Isa.41.15|Isa 41:15]], but <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּמֹץ עֹבֵר‎</span> like chaff passing away [[Isa.29.5|Isa 29:5]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּצֵּל‎</span> [[Job.14.2|Job 14:2]], but <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּצֵל נָטוּי‎</span> [[Ps.102.12|Ps 102:12]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּקַּשׁ‎</span> [[Isa.40.24|Isa 40:24]], but <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּקַשׁ נִדָּף‎</span> [[Isa.41.2|Isa 41:2]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כָּאַיָּל‎</span> [[Isa.35.6|Isa 35:6]], but, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּאַיָּל תַּעֲרֹג על וג‎</span> like ***a hind (that) longeth for streams of water*** [[Ps.42.2|Ps 42:2]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כָּאֵשׁ‎</span> [[Isa.9.17|Isa 9:17]], but <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּאֵשׁ תבער יער‎</span> like ***fire*** (***that***) ***kindleth a wood*** [[Ps.83.15|Ps 83:15]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כֶעָשָׁן‎</span> [[Isa.51.6|Isa 51:6]], but <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּעָשָׁן מֵאֲרֻבָּה‎</span> like ***smoke from a chimney*** [[Hos.13.3|Hos 13:3]]: so [[Isa.62.1|Isa 62:1]]b [[Job.9.26|Job 9:26]]; [[Job.11.16|Job 11:16]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כְּמַיִם עָֽבְרוּ‎</span> like ***waters (that) have passed by***, etc.; [[Deut.32.2|Deut 32:2]] a <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּמָּטָר‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּטַּל‎</span>, but v[[Deut.32.2|Deut 32:2]] b <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כִּשְׂעִירִים עֲלֵידֶֿשֶׁא‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כִּרְבִיבִים‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עֲלֵיעֵֿשֶׂב‎</span>. (:6 b the iniquity of my aggressors surroundeth me), <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַבֹּטְחִים‎</span> ***who trust*** in their riches, etc., [[Gen.49.21|Gen 49:21]]; [[Isa.46.6|Isa 46:6]]; [[Isa.51.20|Isa 51:20]]; [[Job.6.16|Job 6:16]] + (see [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]<sup>§ 135. 7</sup>). 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** (rare) when the substantive qualified by the adjective or participle was felt to be sufficiently definite for its own article to be dispensed with, as sometimes with the word ***day*** [[Gen.1.31|Gen 1:31]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יום השׁשׁי‎</span>, [[Gen.2.3|Gen 2:3]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יום השׁביעי‎</span>, [[Exod.12.15|Exod 12:15]]; [[Exod.12.18|Exod 12:18]]; [[Exod.20.10|Exod 20:10]] (= [[Deut.5.14|Deut 5:14]]) [[Lev.19.6|Lev 19:6]]; [[Lev.22.27|Lev 22:27]]: so with <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מָנוֺא‎</span> ***entrance*** [[Jer.38.14|Jer 38:14]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שַׁעַר‎</span> [[Zech.14.10|Zech 14:10]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חָצֵר‎</span> ***court*** [[1Kgs.7.8|1Kgs 7:8]]; [[1Kgs.7.12|1Kgs 7:12]]; [[Ezek.40.28|Ezek 40:28]] and elsewhere; also in certain phrases (peculiar to [[BDB#P|P]]) where the substantive is defined by <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כל‎</span>, as [[Gen.1.21|Gen 1:21]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כָּלנֶֿפֶשׁ‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַחַיָּה‎</span> ***all*** living souls, v[[Gen.1.28|Gen 1:28]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כָּלחַֿיָּה הָרֹמֶשֶׂת‎</span> all living things ***that creep***, etc., [[Gen.7.21|Gen 7:21]]; [[Gen.9.10|Gen 9:10]]; [[Lev.11.10|Lev 11:10]]; [[Lev.11.46|Lev 11:46]] further in isolated cases, hardly reducible to rule, [[Lev.24.10|Lev 24:10]]; [[1Sam.12.23|1Sam 12:23]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דֶּרֶךְ הַטּוֺבָה‎</span>, [[1Sam.16.23|1Sam 16:23]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎רוּחַ הָרָעָה‎</span>, [[2Sam.12.4|2Sam 12:4]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְאישׁ העשׁיר‎</span> [[Jer.6.20|Jer 6:20]]; [[Jer.17.2|Jer 17:2]]; [[Zech.4.7|Zech 4:7]]; [[Ps.104.18|Ps 104:18]]; [[Ezra.10.9|Ezra 10:9]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֹדֶשׁ הַתְּשִׁיעִי‎</span> (quite exceptional in OT). (With pronouns this use is so rare that, where it occurs, it is dubious if the text be sound: [[1Sam.17.12|1Sam 17:12]]; [[1Sam.17.17|1Sam 17:17]]; [[Jer.40.3|Jer 40:3]] [[BDB#Kt|Kt]] [[Mic.7.11|Mic 7:11]]). And with the participle: [[1Sam.25.10|1Sam 25:10]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎רַבִּים עֲבָדִים הַמִּתְפָּֽרְצִים‎</span> many are the slaves who break away etc., [[Isa.7.20|Isa 7:20]]; [[Jer.27.3|Jer 27:3]]; [[Jer.46.16|Jer 46:16]] = [[Jer.50.16|Jer 50:16]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֶרֶב‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַיּוֺנָה‎</span> the oppressing sword, [[Ezek.2.3|Ezek 2:3]] (but [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] [[BDB#Co|Co]] omit <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הגוים‎</span>), [[Ezek.14.22|Ezek 14:22]]; [[Ezek.32.22|Ezek 32:22]]; [[Ezek.32.24|Ezek 32:24]]; [[Ps.62.4|Ps 62:4]]; [[Prov.26.18|Prov 26:18]]; [[Judg.21.19|Judg 21:19]] (very anomalous, read probably <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לַמְּסִלָּה‎</span>). This usage is somewhat more frequently in the later parts of OT; and in Post-Biblical Hebrew it is very general (e.g. <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יֵצֶד הָרַע‎</span> the evil inclination): see further [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]<sup>§ 209.</sup> 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **c.** with the participle, where the participle with the article forms really the ***subject:*** [[Gen.2.11|Gen 2:11]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הוּא‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַסּוֺבֵב‎</span> not 'it was encompassing,' but 'it is ***that which encompassed***,' [[Gen.45.12|Gen 45:12]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎פִּי הַמְדַבֵּר‎</span> my mouth is ***that which*** speaketh, [[Deut.3.21|Deut 3:21]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עֵינֶיךָ הָראֹוֺת‎</span> thine eyes were ***those which saw***, [[Deut.4.3|Deut 4:3]]; [[Deut.8.18|Deut 8:18]] <span class="bdbLangGreek">ὅτι οὗτος ἐστιν</span> <span class="bdbLangGreek">ὁ διδοὺς σοί</span>, [[Isa.14.27|Isa 14:27]]; [[Isa.66.9|Isa 66:9]] (see [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]<sup>§ 135. 7</sup>).


**3.** The article is prefixed exceptionally — mostly in the latest Hebrew — with the force of a relative to the ***verb:*** [[Josh.10.24|Josh 10:24]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֶהָֽלְכוּא אִתּוֺ‎</span> ***that*** went with him, [[Ezek.26.17|Ezek 26:17]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָעִיר הַהֻ֫לָּלָה‎</span>, [[1Chr.26.28|1Chr 26:28]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְכֹל הַהִקְדִּישׁ שְׁמוּאֵל‎</span> and all ***that*** Samuel had dedicated, [[1Chr.29.8|1Chr 29:8]]; [[1Chr.29.17|1Chr 29:17]]; [[2Chr.1.4|2Chr 1:4]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ַבּהֵכִין‎</span> in (the place) ***that*** he had prepared, [[2Chr.29.36|2Chr 29:36]]; [[Ezra.8.25|Ezra 8:25]]; [[Ezra.10.14|Ezra 10:14]]; [[Ezra.10.17|Ezra 10:17]]. According to the punctuation, it occurs similarly elsewhere, as [[Gen.18.21|Gen 18:21]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַשָבּאָה‎</span> (so [[Gen.46.27|Gen 46:27]]; [[Job.2.11|Job 2:11]]), [[Job.21.3|Job 21:3]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַנּוֺלַדלֿוֺ‎</span>, [[1Kgs.11.9|1Kgs 11:9]] &amp; [[Dan.8.1|Dan 8:1]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַנִּרְאָה‎</span>, [[Isa.51.10|Isa 51:10]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַשָּׂ֨מָה֨‎</span>, [[Isa.56.3|Isa 56:3]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַנִּלְוָה‎</span>, [[Ruth.1.22|Ruth 1:22]]; [[Ruth.2.6|Ruth 2:6]]; [[Ruth.4.3|Ruth 4:3]] (all <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַשָּׁ֫בָה‎</span>): but in all these passages, the change of a point, or even sometimes of an accent, would restore the normal participial construction (as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַבָּשָאה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַנּוֺלָד לוֺ‎</span> compare [[Gen.48.5|Gen 48:5]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַנִּרְאֶה‎</span>), which is, no doubt, what was intended by the original writers, and is recognised elsewhere by the Masora, e.g. [[Gen.12.7|Gen 12:7]]; [[Gen.35.1|Gen 35:1]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַנִּרְאֶה‎</span>, [[Gen.46.26|Gen 46:26]] &amp; [[Ruth.4.11|Ruth 4:11]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַבָּאָ֣ה‎</span> (compare [[BDB#Ew|Ew]]<sup>§ 331 b</sup> [[BDB#Ges|Ges]]<sup>138, 3 b</sup>). Once, still more anomalously, before a preposition [[1Sam.9.24|1Sam 9:24]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְהֶעָלֶיהָ‎</span> (as though <span class="bdbLangGreek">καὶ τὸ ἐπ’</span> <span class="bdbLangGreek">αὐτῆς</span>): but read probably <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְהָאַלְיָה‎</span> ***and the fat tail***, see [[BDB#Dr|Dr]].
