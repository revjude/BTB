**1.** ***skilful*** in technical work; artificers [[Isa.3.3|Isa 3:3]] sailors [[Ezek.27.8|Ezek 27:8]], so (probably) v[[Ezek.27.9|Ezek 27:9]] mourning women [[Jer.9.16|Jer 9:16]] artisans of tabernacle and temple and their furniture [[Exod.28.3|Exod 28:3]]; [[Exod.31.6|Exod 31:6]]; [[Exod.35.10|Exod 35:10]]; [[Exod.36.1|Exod 36:1]]; [[Exod.36.2|Exod 36:2]]; [[Exod.36.4|Exod 36:4]]; [[Exod.36.8|Exod 36:8]] ([[BDB#P|P]]), [[1Chr.22.15|1Chr 22:15]]; [[2Chr.2.6|2Chr 2:6]]; [[2Chr.2.12|2Chr 2:12]]; [[2Chr.2.13|2Chr 2:13]] (twice in verse); women in spinning [[Exod.35.25|Exod 35:25]] ([[BDB#P|P]]); goldsmiths and other artisans [[Jer.10.9|Jer 10:9]] makers of idol-images [[Isa.40.20|Isa 40:20]]. 

**2.** ***wise in the administration of affairs:*** Joseph in Egypt [[Gen.41.33|Gen 41:33]]; [[Gen.41.39|Gen 41:39]] ([[BDB#E|E]]); heads of tribes [[Deut.1.13|Deut 1:13]]; [[Deut.1.15|Deut 1:15]]; [[Judg.16.19|Judg 16:19]] David [[2Sam.14.20|2Sam 14:20]], of Solomon [[1Kgs.2.9|1Kgs 2:9]]; [[1Kgs.3.12|1Kgs 3:12]]; [[1Kgs.5.21|1Kgs 5:21]] = [[2Chr.2.11|2Chr 2:11]] the prince of Tyre, in satire [[Ezek.28.3|Ezek 28:3]] of kings in general [[Prov.20.26|Prov 20:26]] class of political advisers of Judah [[Isa.29.14|Isa 29:14]]; [[Jer.18.18|Jer 18:18]], apparently also [[Isa.5.21|Isa 5:21]] of Egypt [[Isa.19.11|Isa 19:11]] (twice in verse); [[Isa.19.12|Isa 19:12]], Edom [[Obad.1.8|Obad 1:8]], the nations [[Jer.10.7|Jer 10:7]] so of God [[Isa.31.2|Isa 31:2]]. 

**3.** ***shrewd, crafty, cunning:*** Jonadab [[2Sam.13.3|2Sam 13:3]] the princesses [[Judg.5.29|Judg 5:29]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אשׁה חכמה‎</span> ***wise woman*** [[2Sam.14.2|2Sam 14:2]]; [[2Sam.20.16|2Sam 20:16]] generally, [[Jer.9.22|Jer 9:22]] intelligent animals [[Prov.30.24|Prov 30:24]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בערמם‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לכד ח‎</span> ***taking the cunning in their craft*** [[Job.5.13|Job 5:13]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חכם בעיני‎</span> ***wise in one's own eyes*** [[Prov.3.7|Prov 3:7]]; [[Prov.26.5|Prov 26:5]]; [[Prov.26.12|Prov 26:12]]; [[Prov.26.16|Prov 26:16]]; [[Prov.28.11|Prov 28:11]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חכמי לב‎</span> ***wise of mind*** (in their own mind) [[Job.37.24|Job 37:24]]. 

**4.** plural class of ***learned*** and ***shrewd*** men, including astrologers, magicians and the like, of Egypt [[Gen.41.8|Gen 41:8]] ([[BDB#E|E]]), [[Exod.7.11|Exod 7:11]] ([[BDB#P|P]]; compare [[Isa.19.11|Isa 19:11]] (twice in verse); [[Isa.19.12|Isa 19:12]]); Babylon [[Isa.44.25|Isa 44:25]]; [[Jer.50.35|Jer 50:35]]; [[Jer.51.57|Jer 51:57]] Persia [[Esth.1.13|Esth 1:13]]; [[Esth.6.13|Esth 6:13]].

**6.** ***wise***, ethically and religiously,. in Wisdom Literature: 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** as adjective, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֲכַם לֵב‎</span> ***wise of mind*** [[Prov.10.8|Prov 10:8]]; [[Prov.16.21|Prov 16:21]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לֵב חָכָם‎</span> [[Eccl.8.5|Eccl 8:5]] of the son [[Prov.10.1|Prov 10:1]] = [[Prov.15.20|Prov 15:20]]; [[Prov.13.1|Prov 13:1]]; [[Prov.23.24|Prov 23:24]]; [[Eccl.2.19|Eccl 2:19]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ילד ח‎</span> [[Eccl.4.13|Eccl 4:13]] ***wise boy***; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎גבר ח‎</span> [[Job.34.34|Job 34:34]] ***wise man***; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מוכיח ח‎</span> ***wise reprover*** [[Prov.25.12|Prov 25:12]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** = substantive: (1) as a ***wise learner*** in school of wisdom, he fears God and departs from evil [[Prov.14.16|Prov 14:16]] is silent [[Prov.17.28|Prov 17:28]] hearkens to counsel [[Prov.12.15|Prov 12:15]] hears and increases in learning [[Prov.1.5|Prov 1:5]] his ear seeketh knowledge [[Prov.18.15|Prov 18:15]] he receives it [[Prov.21.11|Prov 21:11]] and stores it up [[Prov.10.14|Prov 10:14]] his ears hearken to the reproof which giveth life [[Prov.15.31|Prov 15:31]] and he becomes wiser through it [[Prov.9.8|Prov 9:8]]; [[Prov.9.9|Prov 9:9]]. (2) as a ***wise teacher, a sage***, he does not answer with windy knowledge [[Job.15.2|Job 15:2]] he tells the experience of the past [[Job.15.48|Job 15:48]] has knowledge [[Job.34.2|Job 34:2]] teaches it [[Eccl.12.9|Eccl 12:9]] disperses it [[Prov.15.7|Prov 15:7]] his tongue is health [[Prov.12.18|Prov 12:18]] it utters knowledge aright [[Prov.15.2|Prov 15:2]] his mind instructs his mouth and adds learning to his lips [[Prov.16.23|Prov 16:23]] his words are gracious [[Eccl.10.12|Eccl 10:12]] it is good to hear his rebuke [[Eccl.7.5|Eccl 7:5]] his instruction is a fountain of life [[Prov.13.14|Prov 13:14]] one walking with him becomes wiser [[Prov.13.20|Prov 13:20]]. The <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חכמים‎</span> recall the Greek <span class="bdbLangGreek">σόφοι</span>, having their schools, pupils (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בֵן‎</span>), discipline (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מוּסָר‎</span>), principles and collections of wisdom, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דברי‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חכמים‎</span> [[Prov.1.6|Prov 1:6]]; [[Prov.22.17|Prov 22:17]] (compare [[Prov.24.23|Prov 24:23]]), [[Eccl.9.17|Eccl 9:17]]; [[Eccl.12.11|Eccl 12:11]] God himself is <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חכם לבב‎</span> [[Job.9.4|Job 9:4]]. (3) ***the wise*** are prosperous: true, in sceptical view of Ecclesiastes, they have no advantage over the fool [[Eccl.6.8|Eccl 6:8]] but die as the fool [[Eccl.2.16|Eccl 2:16]] (twice in verse); [[Ps.49.11|Ps 49:11]] they will not be able to find the works of God [[Eccl.8.17|Eccl 8:17]] like others are in the hands of God [[Eccl.9.1|Eccl 9:1]] they do not secure bread [[Eccl.9.11|Eccl 9:11]] but elsewhere reverse is true: precious treasure is in his dwelling [[Prov.21.20|Prov 21:20]] his lips preserve him [[Prov.14.3|Prov 14:3]] they inherit glory [[Prov.3.35|Prov 3:35]] wealth is their crown [[Prov.14.24|Prov 14:24]] the wise man is strong [[Prov.24.5|Prov 24:5]]; [[Eccl.7.19|Eccl 7:19]] his eyes are in his head [[Eccl.2.14|Eccl 2:14]] his mind is at his right hand [[Eccl.10.2|Eccl 10:2]] he interprets things [[Eccl.8.1|Eccl 8:1]] (4) the ***wise*** man is a blessing to others: he wins men [[Prov.11.30|Prov 11:30]] scales the city of the mighty [[Prov.21.22|Prov 21:22]] though poor he delivers the city [[Eccl.9.15|Eccl 9:15]] is a reproof to scorners [[Prov.15.12|Prov 15:12]] his mind is in the house of mourning [[Eccl.7.4|Eccl 7:4]] injustice makes him foolish [[Eccl.7.7|Eccl 7:7]] Job finds no wise men among his cruel friends [[Job.17.10|Job 17:10]].
