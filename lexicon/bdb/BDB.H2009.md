$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** pointing to persons or things, [[Gen.12.19|Gen 12:19]] and now <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הִנֵּה אִשְׁתְּךָ‎</span> ***behold*** thy wife ! $\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** introducing clauses involving predication: 
$\quad$ $\quad$ $\quad$ (***a***) with reference to the past or present, it points generally to some truth either newly asserted, or newly recognized, [[Gen.1.29|Gen 1:29]] ***behold !*** I have given to you all herbs etc. [[Gen.17.20|Gen 17:20]]; [[Gen.27.6|Gen 27:6]]; [[1Sam.14.33|1Sam 14:33]] etc.; often one upon which some proposal or suggestion is to be founded, [[Exod.1.9|Exod 1:9]] (compare <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הן‎</span> [[Gen.11.6|Gen 11:6]]) [[1Sam.20.2|1Sam 20:2]]; [[1Sam.20.5|1Sam 20:5]]; [[2Kgs.5.20|2Kgs 5:20]]. Here it serves to introduce a solemn or important declaration [[Exod.32.34|Exod 32:34]]; [[Exod.34.10|Exod 34:10]]; [[Isa.7.14|Isa 7:14]] and is used especially with the participle (the ***futuram instans*** [immanent future], [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]<sup>§ 135. 3</sup>) in predictions or threats, [[Gen.20.3|Gen 20:3]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הִנְּךָ מֵת‎</span> (literally) ***behold*** thee (accusative) about to die, thou art about to die, [[Exod.4.23|Exod 4:23]]; [[Exod.7.17|Exod 7:17]]; [[Exod.9.3|Exod 9:3]]; [[Deut.31.17|Deut 31:17]]; [[1Sam.3.11|1Sam 3:11]]; [[1Kgs.20.36|1Kgs 20:36]]; [[1Kgs.22.25|1Kgs 22:25]]; [[Isa.3.1|Isa 3:1]]; [[Isa.10.33|Isa 10:33]]; [[Isa.17.1|Isa 17:1]]; [[Isa.19.1|Isa 19:1]]; [[Isa.22.17|Isa 22:17]]; [[Isa.24.1|Isa 24:1]] + often; in the phrase <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הנה‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ימים באים‎</span> [[1Sam.2.31|1Sam 2:31]]; [[2Kgs.20.17|2Kgs 20:17]] = [[Isa.39.6|Isa 39:6]]; [[Amos.4.2|Amos 4:2]]; [[Amos.8.11|Amos 8:11]]; [[Amos.9.13|Amos 9:13]] + [[Jer.15|Jer 15]] t.; very often with the suffix of 1 person singular, as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הִנְנִי מֵבִיא‎</span> ***Behold***, I bring (literally ***behold me bringing***, or ***about to bring***) . . . $\quad$ $\quad$ **c.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְהִנֵּה ֗֗֗ ‎</span> very frequently in historical style, especially (but not exclusively) after verbs of ***seeing*** or ***discovering***, making the narrative graphic and vivid, and enabling the reader to enter into the surprise or satisfaction of the speaker or actor concerned: [[Gen.1.31|Gen 1:31]] and ***behold***, it was very good, [[Gen.6.12|Gen 6:12]]; [[Gen.8.13|Gen 8:13]]; [[Gen.15.12|Gen 15:12]]; [[Gen.18.2|Gen 18:2]]; [[Gen.37.29|Gen 37:29]]; [[Exod.2.6|Exod 2:6]]; [[Deut.9.13|Deut 9:13]] etc.: in the description of a dream [[Gen.37.7|Gen 37:7]]; [[Gen.37.9|Gen 37:9]]; [[Gen.40.9|Gen 40:9]]; [[Gen.40.16|Gen 40:16]]; [[Gen.41.1|Gen 41:1]]; [[Gen.41.2|Gen 41:2]]; [[Gen.41.3|Gen 41:3]]; [[Isa.29.8|Isa 29:8]], or of a vision [[Amos.7.1|Amos 7:1]]; [[Amos.7.4|Amos 7:4]]; [[Amos.8.1|Amos 8:1]] etc. With a participle (the context fixing the sense to the past), [[Gen.24.30|Gen 24:30]]; [[Gen.37.15|Gen 37:15]] (both without suffix); [[Judg.9.43|Judg 9:43]]; [[Judg.11.34|Judg 11:34]]; [[1Kgs.19.5|1Kgs 19:5]]; [[1Kgs.19.11|1Kgs 19:11]] +.
