**2.** ***law***, permanently valid and applicable, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְיִכָּתֵב בְּדָתֵי פָּרַסוּֿמָדַי וְלֹא יַעֲבוֺר‎</span> [[Esth.1.19|Esth 1:19]] compare v[[Esth.1.15|Esth 1:15]]; [[Esth.4.11|Esth 4:11]]; [[Esth.4.16|Esth 4:16]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יֹדְעֵי דָּת וָדִין‎</span> [[Esth.1.13|Esth 1:13]] ***those that understood law and judgment***; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דָּתֵי הַמֶּלֶךְ‎</span> [[Esth.3.8|Esth 3:8]] of laws of the Jews in Persia [[Esth.3.8|Esth 3:8]] even of rules for drinking at a feast [[Esth.1.8|Esth 1:8]] for purifying of women [[Esth.2.12|Esth 2:12]]. — The distinction between **1** &amp; **2** ==is not absolute==. The king's will was law, and the royal edict (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דְּבַר מַלְכוּת‎</span>) concerning Vashti was reckoned among the laws (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דָּתֵי‎</span>) of the Persians &amp; Medes ([[Esth.1.19|Esth 1:19]]). — In [[Deut.33.2|Deut 33:2]] [[BDB#MT|ᵑ0]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵשׁ דָּת לָמוֺ‎</span> ***fire was a law for them*** is corrupt; many emendations proposed, compare [[BDB#Di|Di]]; [[BDB#Di|Di]] suggests <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דֹת‎</span>[<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לַפִּ‎</span>] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵשׁ‎</span> ***fire of flames***, flaming fire, compare <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַלַּפִּידִם‎</span> [[Exod.20.18|Exod 20:18]] (but <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לַפִּדֹת‎</span> plural in <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎תֹ֯‎</span> not in OT unless as proper name), or (better), <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דֶת‎</span>[<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יֹקֶ‎</span>] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵשׁ‎</span> ***a burning fire***, compare [[Isa.65.5|Isa 65:5]] (different sense).
