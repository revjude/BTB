 <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כִּי עַל כֵּן‎</span> ***forasmuch as***, a peculiar phrase found [[Gen.18.5|Gen 18:5]]; [[Gen.19.8|Gen 19:8]]; [[Gen.33.10|Gen 33:10]]; [[Gen.38.26|Gen 38:26]]; [[Num.10.31|Num 10:31]]; [[Num.14.43|Num 14:43]]; [[Judg.6.22|Judg 6:22]]; [[2Sam.18.20|2Sam 18:20]] [[BDB#Qr|Qr]] (rightly), [[Jer.29.28|Jer 29:28]]; [[Jer.38.4|Jer 38:4]] — literally ***for therefore***, emphasizing the ground pleonastically ([[BDB#Ew|Ew]]<sup>§ 353 a</sup> ). The original force of the phrase is traceable in some of the passages in which it occurs, as [[Gen.18.5|Gen 18:5]] let me fetch a morsel of bread, and comfort your heart; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כִּיעַֿלכֵּֿן עֲבַרְתֶּם‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עַל עַבְדְּכֶם‎</span> ***for therefore*** (that is, to partake of such hospitality) are ye come to your servant, [[Num.14.43|Num 14:43]] the Amalekite and the Canaanite are there, and ye will fall by the sword, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כִּיעַֿלכֵּֿן שַׁבְתֶּם‎</span> ***for therefore*** (to encounter such a fate) have ye turned back from <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎י‎</span> etc.: but in process of time the distinct sense of its component parts was no doubt gradually obscured, and it thus came to be used conventionally, as a ***mere*** particle of causation, even where there was no preceding statement to which <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עַל כֵּן‎</span> ***therefore*** could be explicitly referred. <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁר עַל כֵּן‎</span> appears to be used similarly (compare <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁר‎</span> **8 c**) [[Job.34.27|Job 34:27]].
