**1. interrogative adverb** ==where?== 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** so with suffix <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַיֶּ֫כָּה‎</span> [a verbal form, see [[BDB#Sta|Sta]]<sup>§ 355 b. 3</sup>] [[Gen.3.9|Gen 3:9]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַיּוֺ‎</span> [[Exod.2.20|Exod 2:20]], poetry ***where*** is he? = he is nowhere [[Job.14.10|Job 14:10]]; [[Job.20.7|Job 20:7]] ([[Jer.37.19|Jer 37:19]] read [[BDB#Qr|Qr]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַיֵּה‎</span>); <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַיָּם‎</span> [[Nah.3.17|Nah 3:17]] (in indirect question) and the place thereof is not known, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַיָּם‎</span> ***where*** they are. Idiomatically, with the suffix anticipating the noun to which it refers ([[BDB#Ew|Ew]]<sup>§ 309 c</sup>, compare [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]<sup>Sm. i. 21, 14</sup>) [[2Kgs.19.13|2Kgs 19:13]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַיּוֺ מֶלֶחחֲֿמָת‎</span> ***where is he***, the king of Hamath? (\|\| [[Isa.37.13|Isa 37:13]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַיֵּה‎</span>) [[Isa.19.12|Isa 19:12]]; [[Mic.7.10|Mic 7:10]]. When used alone, or with other adverbs (see below), it is contracted to <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵי‎</span> [[Gen.4.9|Gen 4:9]]; [[Deut.32.37|Deut 32:37]]; [[1Sam.26.16|1Sam 26:16]]; [[Prov.31.4|Prov 31:4]] [[BDB#Qr|Qr]]. (The more usual form is <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַיֵּה‎</span>.) 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** strengthened by the enclitic <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎זֶה‎</span> (see  <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎זֶה‎</span>, **4** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵיזֶֿה‎</span><sub>15</sub> ***where, then ?*** (never of a ***person***, except [[Esth.7.5|Esth 7:5]] (late), &amp; only once [[1Kgs.22.24|1Kgs 22:24]] [but see [[2Chr.18.23|2Chr 18:23]]] with a ***verb***) [[Isa.50.1|Isa 50:1]]; [[Isa.66.1|Isa 66:1]] (twice in verse); [[Jer.6.16|Jer 6:16]], in indirect question [[1Sam.9.18|1Sam 9:18]] in the phrase <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵיזֶֿה הַדֶּרֶח ֗֗֗ ‎</span> ***where*** is the way (that) . . . ? [[1Kgs.13.12|1Kgs 13:12]]; [[2Kgs.3.8|2Kgs 3:8]]; [[2Chr.18.23|2Chr 18:23]]; [[Job.38.19|Job 38:19]] a. 24. 

**2.** prefixed to other adverbs or pron., <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵי‎</span> imparts to them an interrogative force: thus 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵיזֶֿה‎</span> ***which*** (of two or more)? only [[Eccl.2.3|Eccl 2:3]]; [[Eccl.11.6|Eccl 11:6]] (late), in indirect question 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵימִֿזֶּה‎</span> ***whence ?*** (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִזֶּה‎</span> = ***hence***; see below <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎זֶה‎</span>) [[Gen.16.8|Gen 16:8]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵימִֿזֶּה‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָאתְ‎</span> [[1Sam.30.13|1Sam 30:13]]; [[2Sam.1.3|2Sam 1:3]]; [[2Sam.1.13|2Sam 1:13]]; [[Job.2.2|Job 2:2]] in indirect question [[Judg.13.6|Judg 13:6]]; [[1Sam.25.11|1Sam 25:11]] with substantive annexed [[2Sam.15.2|2Sam 15:2]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵימִֿזֶּה‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עִיר אַתָּה‎</span> literally ***whence***, as regards city, art thou ? [[Jonah.1.8|Jonah 1:8]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **c.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵילָֿזֹאת‎</span> [[Jer.5.7|Jer 5:7]] ***upon what ground ?*** ([[BDB#Vg|ᵑ9]] super quo? ***how***? With other adverbs, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵי‎</span> coalesces into one word, see  <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵיכָה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵיכָכָה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵיפֹה‎</span>.
