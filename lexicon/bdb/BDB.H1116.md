 <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּמָה‎</span><sub>104</sub> **noun feminine** ==high place== (√ apparently <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בום‎</span> on account of firm <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ָ֯‎</span>; compare Assyrian <abbr title="transliteration"><u>***bâmâtê***</u></abbr> [[BDB#Zim|Zim]]<sup>BP 48, Moab.</sup> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎במת‎</span> [[BDB#MI|MI]]<sup>3, 27</sup>) — [[Jer.48.35|Jer 48:35]] + 18 t.; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַבָּמָ֫תָה‎</span> [[1Sam.9.13|1Sam 9:13]] plural <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּמוֺת‎</span> [[Num.21.19|Num 21:19]] + 62 t.; construct <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּֽמֳתֵי‎</span> [[Job.9.8|Job 9:8]]; [[Isa.14.14|Isa 14:14]]; [[Amos.4.13|Amos 4:13]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּֽמֳו֯תֵי‎</span> [[Deut.32.13|Deut 32:13]]; [[Isa.58.14|Isa 58:14]]; [[Mic.1.3|Mic 1:3]] ([[BDB#Ew|Ew]]<sup>§ 211 d</sup> [[BDB#Ges|Ges]]<sup>«GKC:87»§ 87</sup>, 5 archaic feminine construct with retracted accent before monosyl. in poetry, <abbr title="transliteration"><u>***bâm©-thê***</u></abbr> not <abbr title="transliteration"><u>***b©m***</u></abbr>); suffix <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּמֹתַי‎</span> [[2Sam.22.34|2Sam 22:34]] + 3 t.; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּמוֺתַי‎</span> [[Ps.18.34|Ps 18:34]] + 10 t.; — 

**1.** ***high place, mountain:*** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בָּמוֺת יָעַר‎</span> ***forest mountains*** [[Mic.3.12|Mic 3:12]] = [[Jer.26.18|Jer 26:18]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎במות עולם‎</span> ***ancient mountains*** [[Ezek.36.2|Ezek 36:2]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎במות ארנן‎</span> [[Num.21.28|Num 21:28]] ([[BDB#E|E]] poetry) 

**2.** ***high places, battle-fields***, the chief places of the land giving possession, victory, dominion: <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎על במותיך‎</span> ***on thy high places*** (Gilboa, the battle-field) [[2Sam.1.19|2Sam 1:19]]; [[2Sam.1.25|2Sam 1:25]] (in v[[2Sam.1.19|2Sam 1:19]] [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] has a doublet <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מֵתֶיךָ‎</span> ***thy dead***, see [[BDB#We|We]] [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]). 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** ***of Israel:*** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎רכב על במתי‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ארץ‎</span> ***ride upon the high places of the land*** [[Deut.32.13|Deut 32:13]] &amp; [[Isa.58.14|Isa 58:14]] compare [[Deut.33.29|Deut 33:29]]; [[Ps.18.34|Ps 18:34]] = [[2Sam.22.34|2Sam 22:34]]; [[Hab.3.19|Hab 3:19]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** ***of God:*** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דרך על במתי ארץ‎</span> ***tread upon the high places of the earth*** [[Amos.4.13|Amos 4:13]] compare [[Mic.1.3|Mic 1:3]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎במתי ים‎</span> [[Job.9.8|Job 9:8]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עלה על במתי עב‎</span> (aspiration of the king of Babylon) [[Isa.14.14|Isa 14:14]]. 

**3.** ***high places***, as places of worship, at first on hills and mountains, later on artificial mounds or platforms, under green trees, and in cities; still later for the chapels erected thereon, and once apparently for a portable sanctuary (decked with diverse colours) [[Ezek.16.16|Ezek 16:16]]. The ancient worship of Israel was conducted on these high places. In the times of Samuel and David they ascended to them, descended from them, and offered sacrifices on them, <ref ref="1Sam 9:12-25" b="9" cBegin="9" vBegin="12" cEnd="9" vEnd="25" onclick="bcv(9,9,12)">1Sam 9:12-25</ref>; [[1Sam.10.5|1Sam 10:5]]; [[1Sam.10.13|1Sam 10:13]] (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הבמה‎</span> for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הביתה‎</span> [[BDB#We|We]] [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]). The custom continued in the reign of Solomon, but Gibeon was <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הבמה‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הגדולה‎</span> <ref ref="1Kgs 3:2-4" b="11" cBegin="3" vBegin="2" cEnd="3" vEnd="4" onclick="bcv(11,3,2)">1Kgs 3:2-4</ref> compare [[1Chr.16.39|1Chr 16:39]]; [[1Chr.21.29|1Chr 21:29]]; [[2Chr.1.3|2Chr 1:3]]; [[2Chr.1.13|2Chr 1:13]]. High places of Baal were also used [[Num.22.41|Num 22:41]] ([[BDB#E|E]]) [[Jer.19.5|Jer 19:5]]; [[Jer.32.35|Jer 32:35]] of Moab [[Isa.15.2|Isa 15:2]]; [[Isa.16.12|Isa 16:12]]; [[Jer.48.35|Jer 48:35]] (compare [[BDB#MI|MI]]<sup>27</sup>); these must be demolished [[Num.33.52|Num 33:52]] ([[BDB#J|J]]). Solomon built <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎במות‎</span> (platforms or chapels) to Chemosh and Milkom on the Mt. of Evil Counsel opposite Jerusalem [[1Kgs.11.7|1Kgs 11:7]]: Jeroboam made temples on the ancient high places of Dan and Bethel [[1Kgs.12.31|1Kgs 12:31]]; [[1Kgs.12.32|1Kgs 12:32]]; [[2Chr.11.15|2Chr 11:15]] they are called <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎במות און‎</span> [[Hos.10.8|Hos 10:8]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎במות ישׂחק‎</span> [[Amos.7.9|Amos 7:9]]: the kings of Israel built <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎במות‎</span> and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בתי הבמות‎</span> in all their cities [[2Kgs.17.9|2Kgs 17:9]], and the people worshipped there [[2Kgs.17.11|2Kgs 17:11]] these were also used by the mixed population after the exile of Israel [[2Kgs.17.29|2Kgs 17:29]]; [[2Kgs.17.32|2Kgs 17:32]] (twice in verse): these various idolatrous high places were first destroyed by Josiah [[1Kgs.13.2|1Kgs 13:2]]; [[1Kgs.13.32|1Kgs 13:32]]; [[1Kgs.13.38|1Kgs 13:38]]; <ref ref="2Kgs 23:5-20" b="12" cBegin="23" vBegin="5" cEnd="23" vEnd="20" onclick="bcv(12,23,5)">2Kgs 23:5-20</ref>; [[2Chr.34.3|2Chr 34:3]]. The worship of Yahweh on high places continued in Judah until the exile [[1Kgs.22.44|1Kgs 22:44]]; [[2Kgs.15.35|2Kgs 15:35]] the sanctity code predicts that Yahweh will destroy them [[Lev.26.30|Lev 26:30]] they were regarded as the reason for the rejection of Shiloh [[Ps.78.58|Ps 78:58]]. The compiler of Kings, writing from the point of view of the Deuteronomic code, complains <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎רַק הַבָּמוֺת‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לֹא סָרוּ‎</span> [[2Kgs.12.4|2Kgs 12:4]]; [[2Kgs.14.4|2Kgs 14:4]]; [[2Kgs.15.4|2Kgs 15:4]]; [[2Kgs.15.35|2Kgs 15:35]] compare [[2Chr.15.17|2Chr 15:17]]; [[2Chr.20.33|2Chr 20:33]], and praises the few pious kings who destroyed them. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** Rehoboam built <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎במות‎</span> with <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מצבות‎</span> &amp; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אשׁרים‎</span> on every high hill and under every green tree [[1Kgs.14.23|1Kgs 14:23]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** Asa did not remove the high places [[1Kgs.15.14|1Kgs 15:14]] ([[2Chr.14.2|2Chr 14:2]]; [[2Chr.14.4|2Chr 14:4]] is incorrect unless <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎במות בעל‎</span>). 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **c.** Jehoshaphat in his reform on the basis of the covenant code did not remove them [[1Kgs.22.44|1Kgs 22:44]] (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עוֺד הֵסִיר אֶתהַֿבָּמוֺת‎</span> [[2Chr.17.16|2Chr 17:16]] is doubtless incorrect, possibly read <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מצבות‎</span>); Jehoram, his son, made high places in the cities of Judah [[2Chr.21.11|2Chr 21:11]] ([[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] [[BDB#Vg|ᵑ9]]; not ***mountains*** HCT); and Ahaz sacrificed on high places on the hills and under every green tree and in every city of Judah [[2Kgs.16.4|2Kgs 16:4]]; [[2Chr.28.4|2Chr 28:4]]; [[2Chr.28.25|2Chr 28:25]] compare [[Mic.1.5|Mic 1:5]] (read <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חטאת‎</span>? so [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] [[BDB#Syr|ᵑ6]] [[BDB#Tg|ᵑ7]] [[BDB#Che|Che]] and others; yet compare [[BDB#JBL|JBL]]<sup>1890, 73 f.</sup>) 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **d.** Hezekiah removed them [[2Kgs.18.4|2Kgs 18:4]]; [[2Kgs.18.22|2Kgs 18:22]]; [[2Chr.31.1|2Chr 31:1]]; [[2Chr.32.12|2Chr 32:12]]; [[Isa.36.7|Isa 36:7]] but Manasseh rebuilt them [[2Kgs.21.3|2Kgs 21:3]]; [[2Chr.33.3|2Chr 33:3]]; [[2Chr.33.19|2Chr 33:19]], and the people continued to sacrifice thereon to Yahweh [[2Chr.33.17|2Chr 33:17]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **e.** Josiah, in his reform, based on the Deuteronomic code, defiled them and brake them down from Geba to Beersheba [[2Kgs.23.5|2Kgs 23:5]]; [[2Kgs.23.8|2Kgs 23:8]]; [[2Kgs.23.9|2Kgs 23:9]] but subsequently there were <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎במות‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎התפת‎</span> in the valley of Ben Hinnom [[Jer.7.31|Jer 7:31]], and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎במות‎</span> throughout Judah [[Jer.17.3|Jer 17:3]] compare [[Ezek.6.3|Ezek 6:3]]; [[Ezek.6.6|Ezek 6:6]]; [[Ezek.20.29|Ezek 20:29]] (questioned by [[BDB#Ew|Ew]] &amp; [[BDB#Co|Co]]).
