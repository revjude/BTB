 <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎גִּיחוֺן‎</span> **proper name, of a river** (***a bursting forth***) — 

**1.** one of the rivers of Eden [[Gen.2.13|Gen 2:13]] (on theories of identity see Commentaries; also [[BDB#Smith|Smith]]<sup>Dict. Bible</sup> Schaff-Herzog<sup>Art. ***Eden***</sup> Spurrell<sup>Text of Gen.</sup> ***on the passage*** [[BDB#Ri|Ri]]<sup>HWB</sup>). 

**2.** spring of water near Jerusalem: <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎גִּחוֺן‎</span> [[1Kgs.1.33|1Kgs 1:33]]; [[1Kgs.1.38|1Kgs 1:38]]; [[1Kgs.1.45|1Kgs 1:45]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מֵימֵי‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎גִיחוֺן הָעֶלְיוֺן‎</span> [[2Chr.32.30|2Chr 32:30]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְגִיחוֺן בַּנַּחַל‎</span> [[2Chr.33.14|2Chr 33:14]] — there are two main theories as to locality: 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** west of Jerusalem, connected with ***Birket Mamilla***, and aqueduct into city [[BDB#Rob|Rob]]<sup>BR i. 239, 345 ff.</sup> [[BDB#Survey|Survey]]<sup>Jerusalem</sup> and others; 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** east of Jerusalem = Fountain of the Virgin, Furrer<sup>Schenkel BL ii. 483</sup> [[BDB#Bäd|Bd]]<sup>Pal 101</sup>, or Siloah water-system [[BDB#Gu|Guthe]]<sup>ZPV, v., 1882, 359 ff</sup>.
