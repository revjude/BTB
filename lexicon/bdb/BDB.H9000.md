[[Prov.25.3|Prov 25:3]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שֹׁ֖ד וָשֶשׁבֶר גָּדוֺל׃‎</span> [[Jer.48.3|Jer 48:3]], compare [[Jer.32.29|Jer 32:29]]). — <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְ‎</span> is used very freely and widely in Hebrew, but also with much delicacy, to express relations and shades of meaning which Western languages would usually indicate by distinct particles. But in Hebrew particles such as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אוֺ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אָז‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַךְ‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אָכֵן‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אוּלָם‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בַּעֲבוּר‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְמַעַן‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לָכֵן‎</span>, etc., were reserved for cases in which special emphasis or distinctness was desired: their frequent use was felt instinctively to be inconsistent with the lightness and grace of movement which the Hebrew ear loved; and thus in [[BDB#AV|AV]], [[BDB#RV|RV]], words like ***or, then, but, notwithstanding, howbeit, so, thus, therefore, that***, constantly appear, where the Hebrew has simply <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְ‎</span>.


**1.** ***And***, connecting both words (see above), and sentences ([[Gen.1.5|Gen 1:5]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְלַחשֶׁךְ קָרָא וג‎</span>). When three, four, or more words follow, the conjunction may connect them all, as [[Gen.7.21|Gen 7:21]]; [[Gen.10.2|Gen 10:2]] (6 t.) [[Gen.24.35|Gen 24:35]] (7 t.) [[Josh.7.24|Josh 7:24]] (10 t.) [[2Sam.17.28|2Sam 17:28]]f.: often however it is prefixed only to the last, as [[Gen.5.32|Gen 5:32]]; [[Gen.10.1|Gen 10:1]]; [[Deut.18.10|Deut 18:10]] etc.; occas. even it connects only the first two, [[1Kgs.8.47|1Kgs 8:47]]; [[Isa.1.13|Isa 1:13]]; [[Ps.45.9|Ps 45:9]]; [[Job.42.9|Job 42:9]]. Remarkably, however, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְ‎</span> as a ***mere*** conjunction is, as a rule, not in classical Hebrew attached directly to ***verbs*** (especially in the perfect), the construction with <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְ‎</span> consecutive (see **2**) being (as in Moabite) preferred: thus <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יָצָא וַיִּבֶן‎</span> is said, not <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יָצָא וּבָנָה‎</span>. **2.** The <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ו‎</span> consecutive (formerly called the Waw 'conversive'); — 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** with the **imperfect** (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וַּ ‎</span> with following daghesh; before <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎א‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וָ‎</span>), as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וַיּאֹמֶר‎</span> properly = ***and he proceeded*** to say, chiefly in contin. of a preceding ***perfect*** tense (so Moabite); 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** with the **perfect** (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְ‎</span>, — in 1 &amp; 2 singular, the tone, with certain exceptions [see [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]<sup>§ 110</sup>], being thrown forward to the ultima), as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְיָָֽשַׁבְתָּ֫‎</span> properly ***so*** [namely, as limited by a verb, or other term, preceding] ***hadst thou sat***, chiefly in continuation of a preceding ***imperfect*** tense, in its various senses of future, jussive, or frequentative. Thefurther analysis of these idioms belongs to the grammar; see on **a** [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]<sup>Ch. vi.</sup>, [[BDB#Ges|Ges]]<sup>«GKC:111»§ 111</sup>, and on **b** [[BDB#Dr|Dr]]<sup>Ch. viii.</sup> [[BDB#Ges|Ges]]<sup>«GKC:112»§ 112</sup>. $\quad$ $\quad$ $\quad$ (***a***) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ו‎</span> consecutive (especially with the imperfect) frequently couples two ***verbs*** in such a manner that the first, indicating the ***general*** character of the action, receives its closer definition in the second: in such cases, the first may often be represented in English by an ***adverb***, as [[Gen.26.18|Gen 26:18]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וַיָּשָׁב וַיַּחְפֹּר‎</span> and he turned ***and*** dug = and he dug ***again***; so often; and similarly with <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הוֺסִיף‎</span> [[Gen.25.1|Gen 25:1]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִהַר‎</span> [[Gen.24.18|Gen 24:18]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הוֺאִיל‎</span> [[Josh.7.7|Josh 7:7]], etc.; more exceptionally, [[Gen.30.27|Gen 30:27]]; [[Esth.8.6|Esth 8:6]], and (not consecutive) [[Gen.47.6|Gen 47:6]]; [[Job.23.3|Job 23:3]]; [[Song.2.3|Song 2:3]]; [[Lam.3.26|Lam 3:26]] (see [[BDB#Ges|Ges]]<sup>«GKC:120»§ 120</sup>, 2 ***a***; compare in Syriac [[BDB#Nö|Nö]]<sup>§ 335-6</sup>.
