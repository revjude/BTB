**1.** in ***direct*** questions: 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** as a simple interrogative, where the answer expected is uncertain, [[Exod.2.7|Exod 2:7]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַאֵלֵךְ‎</span> ***shall I go*** and call thee a nurse ? [[1Sam.23.11|1Sam 23:11]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲיַסְגִּרֻנִי‎</span> ***will*** the men of Keilah deliver me into his hand ? <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲיֵרֵד שָׁאוּל‎</span> ***will*** Saul come down ? [[Job.1.8|Job 1:8]] and frequently. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** often in questions, expressed in a tone of surprise, or put rhetorically, to which a ***negative*** answer is expected (= Latin <abbr title="transliteration"><u>num ?</u></abbr>): [[Gen.4.9|Gen 4:9]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲשֹׁמֵר אָחִי אָנֹכִי‎</span> ***Amos*** I my brother's keeper ? [[Gen.18.17|Gen 18:17]] ***shall*** I hide from Abraham that which I am about to do ? [[Gen.30.2|Gen 30:2]]; [[Gen.50.19|Gen 50:19]]; [[Num.11.23|Num 11:23]] a [[Deut.4.33|Deut 4:33]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲשָׁמַע עַם֗֗֗ ‎</span> ***Did*** a people ever hear the voice of God speaking out of the midst of the fire, . . . and live ? [[Deut.20.19|Deut 20:19]] (read with [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] [[BDB#Tg|ᵑ7]] [[BDB#Syr|ᵑ6]] [[BDB#Vg|ᵑ9]] [[BDB#Ew|Ew]] [[BDB#Ke|Ke]] [[BDB#Di|Di]] etc. <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֶאָדָם‎</span> for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָאָדָם‎</span>), [[2Sam.7.5|2Sam 7:5]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ַהאַתָּה תִּבְנֶהלִּֿי בַיִת‎</span> (altered in [[1Chr.17.4|1Chr 17:4]] to the negative <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לֹא אַתָּה תִּבְנֶה וג‎</span>), [[2Kgs.6.22|2Kgs 6:22]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַאֲשֶׁר שָׁבִיתָ ֗֗֗ ‎</span> those whom thou hast taken captive with thy sword and with thy bow, wilt thou smite ? [[Isa.28.24|Isa 28:24]]; [[Isa.36.12|Isa 36:12]] a [[Isa.57.6|Isa 57:6]]; [[Isa.58.5|Isa 58:5]]; [[Jer.15.12|Jer 15:12]]; [[Jer.16.20|Jer 16:20]]; [[Amos.5.25|Amos 5:25]]; [[Ps.50.13|Ps 50:13]]; [[Job.8.11|Job 8:11]]; [[Job.15.7|Job 15:7]]; [[Job.15.8|Job 15:8]]; [[Job.15.11|Job 15:11]]; [[Job.38.12|Job 38:12]]; [[Job.38.16|Job 38:16]]; [[Job.38.17|Job 38:17]]; [[Job.38.22|Job 38:22]] etc.; before an infinitive absolute ([[BDB#Ew|Ew]]<sup>§ 328 a</sup>) [[Jer.7.9|Jer 7:9]]; [[Job.40.2|Job 40:2]] and probably [[Mic.2.7|Mic 2:7]] (read <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֶאָמוֺר‎</span>: ). After a protasis, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הִנֵההֲ֗֗֗‎</span> [[Num.22.38|Num 22:38]]; [[2Kgs.7.2|2Kgs 7:2]]; [[2Kgs.7.19|2Kgs 7:19]]; [[Jer.32.27|Jer 32:27]]; [[Ezek.17.10|Ezek 17:10]] after <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֵן‎</span> [[Jer.3.1|Jer 3:1]]; [[Hag.2.12|Hag 2:12]]: compare after <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎זוּלַי‎</span> [[Gen.24.5|Gen 24:5]] after <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אִם‎</span> [[Job.14.14|Job 14:14]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אִם יָמוּת גֶּבֶר הֲיִחְיֶה‎</span> a man dieth, shall he live ? Occasionally, one or more words precede <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲ‎</span> (in the same clause) for special emphasis: [[2Sam.7.7|2Sam 7:7]]; [[Job.34.31|Job 34:31]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כִּי אֶלאֵֿל‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֶאָמַר‎</span> for ***to God*** did one ever say. . .? [[Neh.13.27|Neh 13:27]]; [[Jer.22.15|Jer 22:15]] (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲלֹא‎</span>), compare [[Jer.23.26|Jer 23:26]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **c.** it is used in questions which, by seeming to make doubtful what cannot be denied, have the force of an impassioned or indignant affirmation: [[Gen.27.36|Gen 27:36]] ('dubitantis speciem prae se fert Esavus, ut eo acerbius affirmet,' Maurer, cited by [[BDB#Ges|Ges]]), [[1Sam.2.27|1Sam 2:27]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲנִגְלהֹ נִגְלֵיתִי וגי‎</span> ***Did I reveal*** myself to the house of thy father, when they were in Egypt ? etc. (i.e. of course I did, although thy sons, by their actions, appear to belie it), [[1Kgs.16.31|1Kgs 16:31]] (expressing astonishment), [[1Kgs.21.19|1Kgs 21:19]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲרָצַחְתָּ וְגַםיָֿרָ֑שְׁתָּ‎</span> ***Hast thou*** slain, and also taken possession ? [[Jer.31.20|Jer 31:20]]; [[Job.20.4|Job 20:4]]; [[Job.41.1|Job 41:1]] (compare [[BDB#Hi|Hi]]); also [[Gen.16.13|Gen 16:13]], and the phrases <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַרְּאִיתֶם‎</span> ***do ye see ?*** [[1Sam.10.24|1Sam 10:24]]; [[2Kgs.6.32|2Kgs 6:32]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲרָאִיתָ‎</span> ***dost thou see ?*** [[1Kgs.20.13|1Kgs 20:13]]; [[Ezek.8.12|Ezek 8:12]]; [[Ezek.8.15|Ezek 8:15]]; [[Ezek.8.17|Ezek 8:17]] and elsewhere 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **d.** in disjunctive questions, the first question being introduced usually by <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲ‎</span>, the second by <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אִם‎</span> or (more rarely) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְאִם‎</span>: see examples under <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אִם‎</span> **2** (p. 50). The disjunctive question may express a real alternative (as [[Josh.5.13|Josh 5:13]]), or (as especially in poetry) the same thought may be repeated in a different form, in two parallel clauses (e.g. [[Isa.10.15|Isa 10:15]]): in the latter case, the answer ***No*** is usually expected. Only very rarely is the second question introduced by <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲ‎</span> [[Judg.14.15|Judg 14:15]] (where, however, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲלֹם‎</span> ought no doubt to be read, with [[BDB#MSS|MSS]]., [[BDB#Tg|ᵑ7]] [[BDB#Be|Be]] and others, for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲלֹא‎</span>), or <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אוֺ‎</span> [[Judg.18.19|Judg 18:19]]; [[Job.16.3|Job 16:3]]; [[Job.38.28|Job 38:28]]; [[Job.38.31|Job 38:31]].


**2.** In ***indirect*** interrogation, ***whether*** (German ***ob***. . . .?) — 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** singly, after verbs of ***seeing*** [[Gen.8.8|Gen 8:8]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לִרְאוֺת הֲ‎</span> to see ***whether***, [[Gen.18.21|Gen 18:21]]; [[Exod.4.18|Exod 4:18]]; [[Song.6.11|Song 6:11]], ***telling*** [[Gen.43.6|Gen 43:6]], ***trying*** [[Deut.13.4|Deut 13:4]], ***knowing*** [[Judg.3.4|Judg 3:4]]; [[Eccl.3.21|Eccl 3:21]] (read <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַעֹלָה‎</span> and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲיֹרֶדֶת‎</span>: see [[BDB#De|De]] or [[BDB#Wr|Wr]] ***on the passage***.) 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** disjunctively, — usually <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲ ֗֗֗ אִם‎</span>, [[Num.13.18|Num 13:18]] end, [[Num.13.19|Num 13:19]]a (and see the land) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲטוֺבָה הִיא אִם רָעָה ֗֗֗ ‎</span> ***whether*** it be good ***or*** bad, v[[Num.13.19|Num 13:19]] b; v[[Num.13.20|Num 13:20]] v[[Num.13.20|Num 13:20]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲ ֗֗֗ אִם לֹא‎</span> [[Gen.24.21|Gen 24:21]]; [[Gen.27.21|Gen 27:21]]; [[Gen.37.32|Gen 37:32]]; [[Exod.16.4|Exod 16:4]] that I may try it (the people) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲיֵלֵךְ בְּתוֺרָתִי אִם לֹא‎</span> ***whether*** it will walk in my law or not, [[Num.11.23|Num 11:23]]; [[Deut.8.2|Deut 8:2]]; [[Judg.2.22|Judg 2:22]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲ ֗֗֗ הֲ‎</span> [[Num.13.18|Num 13:18]] (by side of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲ ֗֗֗ אִם‎</span>); <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲ ֗֗֗ אוֺ‎</span> [[Eccl.2.19|Eccl 2:19]]; [[Eccl.11.6|Eccl 11:6]]. It is prefixed to other particles, as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַאִם‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַאַף‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲגַם‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲכִי‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲלֹא‎</span>: see  <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אִם‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַף‎</span>,etc. p. 49, etc.
