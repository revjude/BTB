 <span class="bdbLangAramaic">‎כְּעֶ֫נֶת‎</span> and ([[Ezra.4.17|Ezra 4:17]]) contracted <span class="bdbLangAramaic">‎כְּעֶת‎</span> **adverb** ==now== (feminine of <span class="bdbLangAramaic">‎כְּעַן‎</span>; so first [[BDB#SS|SS]] Torrey<sup>JBL 16 (1897), 166 ff.</sup>; now confirmed by Egyptian Aramaic <span class="bdbLangAramaic">‎כענת‎</span> [[BDB#Cowley|Cowley]]<sup>PSBA xxv. 264, 311, 312</sup> ( = RES<sup>492-4</sup> = [[BDB#S-C|S-C]]<sup>M, N, O</sup>); <span class="bdbLangAramaic">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangAramaic">‎וכ‎</span> formerly taken to mean ***and so forth***); — always <span class="bdbLangAramaic">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangAramaic">‎וּכ‎</span>, and always in a letter, introducing the business of the letter, and to be connected with what follows, not, as [[BDB#MT|ᵑ0]], with what precedes: [[Ezra.4.10|Ezra 4:10]] (strike out, as introduced by error from end of v[[Ezra.4.11|Ezra 4:11]]), v[[Ezra.4.11|Ezra 4:11]] (joining to v[[Ezra.4.12|Ezra 4:12]]) ***and now***, let it be known, etc., v[[Ezra.4.17|Ezra 4:17]]f. ***and now***, the letter which, etc., [[Ezra.7.12|Ezra 7:12]]f. ***and now***, I make a decree, etc. (compare <span class="bdbLangAramaic">‎וְעַתָּה‎</span> similarly in letters [[2Kgs.5.6|2Kgs 5:6]]; [[2Kgs.10.2|2Kgs 10:2]], and <span class="bdbLangGreek">καὶ νῦν</span> 2 Macc 1:6).
