**1.** with <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְ‎</span>, only in singular, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְבַד‎</span> properly ***in a state of*** (see below <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְ‎</span>) ***separation, alone, by itself*** (French ***à part***). 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** [[Exod.26.9|Exod 26:9]] (= [[Exod.36.16|Exod 36:16]]) five curtains <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְבַד‎</span> ***by themselves***, and six curtains <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְבַד‎</span> ***by themselves***, [[Judg.7.5|Judg 7:5]] him shalt thou set <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לבד‎</span> ***apart***, <ref ref="Zech 12:12-14" b="38" cBegin="12" vBegin="12" cEnd="12" vEnd="14" onclick="bcv(38,12,12)">Zech 12:12-14</ref>. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** with suffix (89 t.) to express the idea of ***by oneself, alone*** (properly ***in his, thy, my separation***), [[Gen.2.18|Gen 2:18]] it is not good for man to be <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְבַדּוֺ‎</span> ***alone***, [[Gen.21.28|Gen 21:28]] and A. set the seven lambs <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְבַדְּהֶן‎</span> ***by themselves*** (literally in their separation), [[Gen.32.17|Gen 32:17]]; [[Gen.43.32|Gen 43:32]]; [[2Sam.10.8|2Sam 10:8]]; [[Isa.5.8|Isa 5:8]]; [[Gen.42.38|Gen 42:38]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הוּא לְבַדּוֺ‎</span> he ***alone***, [[Exod.18.14|Exod 18:14]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַתָּה לְבַדְּךָ‎</span> thou ***alone***, [[Num.11.14|Num 11:14]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אָנֹכִי לְבַדִּי‎</span> I ***alone***, [[1Kgs.19.10|1Kgs 19:10]]; [[1Kgs.19.14|1Kgs 19:14]]; [[Deut.8.3|Deut 8:3]] not upon bread ***alone***, [[Deut.29.13|Deut 29:13]]; [[2Sam.13.32|2Sam 13:32]]; [[2Sam.18.24|2Sam 18:24]]; [[Isa.44.24|Isa 44:24]]; [[Isa.49.21|Isa 49:21]]; [[Isa.63.3|Isa 63:3]] +; after an oblique case, as a dative [[Exod.22.19|Exod 22:19]]; [[Judg.3.20|Judg 3:20]]; [[Ps.51.6|Ps 51:6]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְךָ לְבַדְּךָ‎</span> against thee ***alone*** have I sinned; a genitive [[Ps.71.16|Ps 71:16]] I will make mention of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎צִדְקָֽתְךָ לְבֶַדּךָ׃‎</span> the righteousness of thee ***alone***. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **c.** as adverb of limitation, [[Isa.26.13|Isa 26:13]] ***only*** through thee do we celebrate thy name, [[Eccl.7.29|Eccl 7:29]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **d.** followed by <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִן‎</span> it becomes a preposition, ***apart from, besides***, [[Exod.12.37|Exod 12:37]]; [[Num.29.39|Num 29:39]]; [[Deut.3.5|Deut 3:5]]; [[Deut.18.8|Deut 18:8]] (read <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִמְּכָרָיו‎</span> with [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] [[BDB#Syr|ᵑ6]] [[BDB#Tg|ᵑ7]] [[BDB#Aq|Aq]] [[BDB#Ew|Ew]] [[BDB#Di|Di]]) [[Judg.8.26|Judg 8:26]]; [[Judg.20.15|Judg 20:15]] and elsewhere (15 t.); once, [[Ezra.1.6|Ezra 1:6]], with <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עַל‎</span> instead of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִן‎</span>. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **e.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִלְּבַד‎</span><sub>33</sub> (probably inverted for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְבַד מִן‎</span> ***besides***) (chiefly [[BDB#P|P]] and late): [[Gen.26.1|Gen 26:1]]; [[Gen.46.26|Gen 46:26]]; [[Lev.9.17|Lev 9:17]]; [[Lev.23.38|Lev 23:38]] (4 t.) With suffix [[Deut.4.35|Deut 4:35]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵין עוֺד מִלְּבַדּוֺ‎</span> there is none else ***besides*** him (compare <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִבַּלְעָדַי‎</span> [[Isa.45.21|Isa 45:21]]).
