**Note.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵת‎</span> expresses closer association than <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עִם‎</span>: hence while <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מֵעִם‎</span> sometimes denotes hardly more than ***from the surroundings*** or ***belongings of***, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מֵאֵת‎</span> expresses ***from close proximity to***. Thus Saul asks, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִי הָלַךְ מֵעִמָּנוּ‎</span> who has gone ***from*** (those) ***about*** us ? but Jacob, speaking of the loss of Joseph, says, [[Gen.44.28|Gen 44:28]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וַיֵּצֵא הָאֶחָד מֵאִתִּי‎</span> and the one is gone ***from with*** me. <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מֵאֵת‎</span> is accordingly preferred to <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מֵעִם‎</span> in the sense of origination or authorship; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מאת‎</span> is not usual in the sense of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מֵעִם‎</span> **c**, nor <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מֵעִם‎</span> in the sense of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מֵאֵת‎</span> 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.**.
