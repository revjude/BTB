 <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎טֶ֫רֶם‎</span><sub>54</sub>, once ([[Ruth.3.14|Ruth 3:14]] [[BDB#Kt|Kt]]) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎טְרוֺם‎</span>, **adverb of time**, ==not yet, ere, before that== (derivation unknown: not found in cognate languages) — construed mostly with the imperfect (with the perfect only [[Gen.24.15|Gen 24:15]] ([[Gen.24.45|Gen 24:45]] imperfect), [[1Sam.3.7|1Sam 3:7]] a (v:b imperfect), and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּטֶרֶם‎</span> [[Ps.90.2|Ps 90:2]]; [[Prov.8.25|Prov 8:25]]): — 

**1.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎טֶרֶם‎</span> in an independent sentence, ***not yet***, [[Gen.2.5|Gen 2:5]] and all the plants of the field <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎טרם יהיה בארץ‎</span> were ***not yet*** in the earth, etc., [[Gen.19.4|Gen 19:4]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎טרם ישׁכבו‎</span> ***not yet*** had they lain down, when etc., [[Gen.24.15|Gen 24:15]]; [[Gen.24.45|Gen 24:45]]; [[Num.11.33|Num 11:33]]; [[Josh.2.8|Josh 2:8]]; [[1Sam.3.3|1Sam 3:3]]; [[1Sam.3.7|1Sam 3:7]] (twice in verse); of present time, [[Exod.9.30|Exod 9:30]]; [[Exod.10.7|Exod 10:7]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הֲטֶרֶם תֵּדַע‎</span> dost thou ***not yet*** know, etc.?; in a subordinate clause, ***ere, before that***, [[Exod.12.34|Exod 12:34]] they took their dough <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎טרם יחמץ‎</span> ***before*** it was leavened, [[Josh.3.1|Josh 3:1]]; [[Ps.119.67|Ps 119:67]] of future time [[Isa.65.24|Isa 65:24]]. More frequently 

**2.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּטֶרֶם‎</span><sub>39</sub>, with the same force: of past time, [[Gen.27.33|Gen 27:33]] I ate of all <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בטרם תבא‎</span> ***before*** thou camest, [[Gen.37.18|Gen 37:18]]; [[Gen.41.50|Gen 41:50]]; [[Judg.14.18|Judg 14:18]]; [[1Sam.2.15|1Sam 2:15]]; [[Jer.1.5|Jer 1:5]]; [[Jer.47.1|Jer 47:1]]; [[Ezek.16.57|Ezek 16:57]]; [[Ps.90.2|Ps 90:2]]; [[Prov.8.25|Prov 8:25]] (both with perfect); more often of present or future, as [[Gen.27.4|Gen 27:4]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בטרם אמות‎</span> ***before*** I die (so [[Gen.45.28|Gen 45:28]], compare [[Ps.39.14|Ps 39:14]]; [[Job.10.21|Job 10:21]]), [[Lev.14.36|Lev 14:36]]; [[Deut.31.21|Deut 31:21]]; [[1Sam.9.13|1Sam 9:13]]; [[2Kgs.2.9|2Kgs 2:9]]; [[Isa.7.16|Isa 7:16]]; [[Isa.42.9|Isa 42:9]]; [[Isa.48.5|Isa 48:5]]; [[Jer.13.16|Jer 13:16]] the imperfect with a frequently force, [[Exod.1.19|Exod 1:19]] ***before*** the midwife ***cometh***, they are wont to bear, [[Ruth.3.14|Ruth 3:14]]; [[Prov.18.13|Prov 18:13]]. Pleon. <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּטֶרֶם לֹא‎</span> [[Zeph.2.2|Zeph 2:2]] (twice in verse). Construed with a substantive in the genitive, [[Isa.17.14|Isa 17:14]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּטֶרֶם בֹּקֶר‎</span> = ***ere*** morning, [[Isa.28.4|Isa 28:4]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּטֶרֶם קַיִץ‎</span>; with an infinitive [[Zeph.2.2|Zeph 2:2]]a (but read here with [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] [[BDB#We|We]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּטֶרֶם לאֹ‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎תֵהְיוּן כְּמוֺץ עֹבֵר‎</span>, without <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יוֺם‎</span>, 'before ye become as chaff passing away'). 

**3.** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִטֶּרֶם‎</span> [[Hag.2.15|Hag 2:15]] (followed by infinitive) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִטֶּרֶם שׂוּם אֶבֶן‎</span> ***from before*** the laying of one stone upon another, etc.
