 <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חָסִיד‎</span> **adjective masculine** ==kind, pious== (so, as denoting active practice of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֶסֶד‎</span>, kindness, [[BDB#Thes|Thes]] [[BDB#MV|MV]] [[BDB#De|De]] and most, compare <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎קָצִיר‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎פָּקִיד‎</span> etc.; &gt; [[BDB#Hup|Hup]] on [[Ps.4.4|Ps 4:4]] [[BDB#RV|RVm]] who explained as passive ***reception*** of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎י‎</span>'s <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֶסֶד‎</span>, compare <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שָֹׂכֹיר‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אָסִיר‎</span> etc.; its use as attribute of God [[Jer.3.12|Jer 3:12]]; [[Ps.145.17|Ps 145:17]], and the context [[Ps.12.1|Ps 12:1]]; [[Mic.7.2|Mic 7:2]] etc., favour active sense) — <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ח‎</span> [[Ps.4.4|Ps 4:4]] + 9 t.; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֲסִידֶ֑ךָ‎</span> [[Deut.33.8|Deut 33:8]]; [[Ps.89.20|Ps 89:20]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֲסִידְי֯ךָ‎</span> [[Ps.16.10|Ps 16:10]] plural <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֲסִידִים‎</span> [[Ps.149.1|Ps 149:1]]; [[Ps.149.5|Ps 149:5]] suffix <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֲסִידָ֑י‎</span> [[Ps.50.5|Ps 50:5]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֲסִידָו֯‎</span> [[1Sam.2.9|1Sam 2:9]] + 15 t. suffixes; — 

**1.** ***kind:*** 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** of man <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎תתחסד‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עם ח‎</span> ***with the kind thou shewest thyself kind*** [[2Sam.22.26|2Sam 22:26]] = [[Ps.18.26|Ps 18:26]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** of wing of ostrich <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אִם אֶבְרָה חֲסִידָה‎</span> [[Job.39.13|Job 39:13]] ***is it a kindly pinion?*** possibly with play on <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֲסִידָה‎</span> feminine noun ***strok*** (is the ostrich kind like the stork?). 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **c.** of God, only [[Jer.3.12|Jer 3:12]]; [[Ps.145.17|Ps 145:17]]. 

**2.** ***pious, godly***, either as exhibition of 'duteous love' toward God ([[BDB#Che|Che]]<sup>OP 378</sup>), or (in view of rarity of such passages as [[Hos.6.4|Hos 6:4]]; [[Hos.6.6|Hos 6:6]]; [[Jer.2.2|Jer 2:2]], and their possible ambiguity) because ***kindness***, as prominent in the godly, comes to imply other attributes, and to be a designation of the godly character, ***piety:*** 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** as adjective — <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎גּוֺי לא חסיד‎</span> ***a nation, not pious***, = ***ungodly*** [[Ps.43.1|Ps 43:1]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** elsewhere as substantive: singular, ***a pious man, the godly*** [[Ps.4.4|Ps 4:4]]; [[Ps.12.2|Ps 12:2]]; [[Ps.32.6|Ps 32:6]]; [[Ps.86.2|Ps 86:2]], \|\| <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יָשָׁר‎</span> [[Mic.7.2|Mic 7:2]] (***thy***) ***pious one***
$\quad$ $\quad$ $\quad$ (***s***) [[Ps.16.10|Ps 16:10]] ([[BDB#Kt|Kt]] plural), [[1Sam.2.9|1Sam 2:9]] ([[BDB#Qr|Qr]] plural) [[Prov.2.8|Prov 2:8]], <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אִישׁ חֲסִידֶ֑ךָ‎</span> ***men of thy pious one*** [[Deut.33.8|Deut 33:8]] (Moses, see [[BDB#Di|Di]]; others, ***the man, thy godly one***, i.e. Levi); plural ***the pious, godly***, those of the people who were faithful, devoted to God's service, only in Psalter and chiefly, if not entirely, in late Psalms [[Ps.149.1|Ps 149:1]]; [[Ps.149.5|Ps 149:5]] ***his pious ones*** [[Ps.30.5|Ps 30:5]]; [[Ps.31.24|Ps 31:24]]; [[Ps.37.28|Ps 37:28]]; [[Ps.85.9|Ps 85:9]]; [[Ps.97.10|Ps 97:10]]; [[Ps.116.15|Ps 116:15]]; [[Ps.148.14|Ps 148:14]]; [[Ps.149.9|Ps 149:9]] ***thy pious ones*** [[Ps.52.11|Ps 52:11]]; [[Ps.79.2|Ps 79:2]]; [[Ps.132.9|Ps 132:9]] = [[2Chr.6.41|2Chr 6:41]]; [[Ps.145.10|Ps 145:10]] ***my pious ones*** [[Ps.50.5|Ps 50:5]], ***her*** (Zion's) ***pious*** [[Ps.132.16|Ps 132:16]]. — (In Maccabean age, <span class="bdbLangGreek">συναγωγὴ Ἀσιδαίων</span> denoted, technically, the party of ***the pious***, who opposed the Hellenization of the Judaea, see 1 Macc 2:42; 7:13; 2 Macc 14:6 and [[BDB#Che|Che]]<sup>OP 48, 56</sup>; so perhaps [[Ps.116.15|Ps 116:15]]; [[Ps.149.1|Ps 149:1]]; [[Ps.149.5|Ps 149:5]]; [[Ps.149.9|Ps 149:9]].)
