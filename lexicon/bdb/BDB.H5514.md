 <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎סִינַי‎</span> **proper name, of a mountain** ==Sinai==; — name of mountain of law-giving in [[BDB#J|J]] and especially [[BDB#P|P]] (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֹרֵב‎</span> in [[BDB#E|E]] and especially [[BDB#D|D]]): [[Judg.5.5|Judg 5:5]]; [[Ps.68.9|Ps 68:9]]; [[Deut.33.2|Deut 33:2]] (poem), also [[Exod.16.1|Exod 16:1]] ([[BDB#P|P]]); usually <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַרסִֿינַי‎</span> (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎סִֿינָ֑י‎</span> [[Lev.7.38|Lev 7:38]] +), rarely [[BDB#J|J]], [[Exod.19.20|Exod 19:20]]; [[Exod.19.23|Exod 19:23]] and perhaps [[Exod.34.2|Exod 34:2]]; [[Exod.34.4|Exod 34:4]], elsewhere mostly [[BDB#P|P]], [[Exod.24.16|Exod 24:16]]; [[Exod.31.18|Exod 31:18]]; [[Exod.34.29|Exod 34:29]]; [[Exod.34.32|Exod 34:32]]; [[Lev.7.38|Lev 7:38]]; [[Lev.25.1|Lev 25:1]]; [[Lev.27.34|Lev 27:34]] and [[Lev.26.46|Lev 26:46]] ([[BDB#H|H]]), [[Num.3.1|Num 3:1]]; [[Num.28.6|Num 28:6]], also [[Neh.9.13|Neh 9:13]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִרְבַּר סִינַי‎</span> (<span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎סִינָ֑י‎</span> [[Num.1.19|Num 1:19]] +), [[Exod.19.1|Exod 19:1]]; [[Exod.19.2|Exod 19:2]]; [[Lev.7.38|Lev 7:38]]; [[Num.1.1|Num 1:1]]; [[Num.1.19|Num 1:19]]; [[Num.3.4|Num 3:4]]; [[Num.3.14|Num 3:14]]; [[Num.9.1|Num 9:1]]; [[Num.9.5|Num 9:5]]; [[Num.10.12|Num 10:12]]; [[Num.26.64|Num 26:64]]; [[Num.33.15|Num 33:15]]; [[Num.33.16|Num 33:16]] (all [[BDB#P|P]]); — in view of these facts, and of [[BDB#E|E]]'s use of  <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֹרֵב‎</span> (q. v.), it is probably that [[Exod.19.11|Exod 19:11]]; [[Exod.19.18|Exod 19:18]] (in [[BDB#E|E]] passages) are from R. — [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] <span class="bdbLangGreek">Σ</span>(<span class="bdbLangGreek">ε</span>)<span class="bdbLangGreek">ινα</span>. Identification dubious; local tradition names ***Jebel Musa***, mountain at southern end of peninsular between the two arms of Red Sea, so [[BDB#Rob|Rob]]<sup>BR i. 90 ff., especially 119-122</sup> compare Stanley<sup>Sinai and Pal. 42 f. and elsewhere</sup>, specifically its northern spur, <abbr title="transliteration"><u>***Ras ‚afƒâfeh***</u></abbr>; but Lepsius<sup>Briefe 345 ff., 416 ff.</sup> [[BDB#Eb|Eb]]<sup>GS especially 392 ff.</sup> and others advocate ***Serbal***, northwest from ***Jebel Musa*** (on tradition, cf [[BDB#Eb|Eb]]<sup>ib. 413 ff.</sup>); see discussion [[BDB#Di|Di]]<sup>[[Exod.19.1|Exod 19:1]]</sup>. Against both is the working of Egyptian mines in the peninsula as late as the 19th dynasty (see [[BDB#Eb|Eb]]<sup>GS 135, 148 ff., 159, etc.</sup>), involving presence of soldiers there. This, and connection with Midian (compare also [[Judg.5.5|Judg 5:5]]), have led to search for Sinai further northeast, near head of Gulf of Akaba, or even in Seir, so [[BDB#Say|Say]]<sup>Monuments 263 ff.</sup>, compare [[BDB#GFM|GFM]]<sup>[[Judg.5.5|Judg 5:5]]; [[Judg.6.1|Judg 6:1]]</sup> and ***Yakut***, cited by Id<sup>179 n. §</sup>, also see Gall<sup>Altisr. Kultst. 12</sup>; on sanctity of the mountain see further [[BDB#RS|RS]]<sup>Sem i. 110 f., 2nd ed. 177 f.</sup> [[BDB#Sm|Sm]]<sup>Rel. Geschichte. 30</sup>.
