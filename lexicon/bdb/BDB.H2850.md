**1. adjective **, of seller of Machpelah to Abraham, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עֶפְרוֺן הַחִתִּי‎</span> [[Gen.23.10|Gen 23:10]]; [[Gen.49.29|Gen 49:29]]; [[Gen.49.30|Gen 49:30]]; [[Gen.50.13|Gen 50:13]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עֶפְרֹן בֶּןצֹֿחַר‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַח‎</span> [[Gen.25.9|Gen 25:9]] (all [[BDB#P|P]]); of fathers of Esau's wives <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּאֵרִי‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַח‎</span> [[Gen.26.34|Gen 26:34]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵילוֺן הַח‎</span> v[[Gen.26.34|Gen 26:34]]; [[Gen.36.2|Gen 36:2]] (all [[BDB#P|P]]); also of warriors of David, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲחִימֶלֶךְ הַח‎</span> [[1Sam.26.6|1Sam 26:6]], and especially <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אוּרִיָּה הַח‎</span> [[2Sam.11.3|2Sam 11:3]]; [[2Sam.11.6|2Sam 11:6]]; [[2Sam.11.17|2Sam 11:17]]; [[2Sam.11.21|2Sam 11:21]]; [[2Sam.11.24|2Sam 11:24]]; [[2Sam.12.9|2Sam 12:9]]; [[2Sam.12.10|2Sam 12:10]]; [[2Sam.23.39|2Sam 23:39]]; [[1Kgs.15.5|1Kgs 15:5]]; [[1Chr.11.41|1Chr 11:41]] feminine singular = substantive, only of (religious) ancestry of Jerusalem [[Ezek.16.3|Ezek 16:3]] ***thy father was the Amorite, and thy mother was a Hittite woman*** <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חִתִּית‎</span>, similarly v[[Ezek.16.45|Ezek 16:45]] feminine plural = substantive of Solomon's foreign wives, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חִתִּיֹּת‎</span> [[1Kgs.11.1|1Kgs 11:1]] ***Hittite women*** (+ <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מוֺאֲבִיּוֺת‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עַמּוֺנִיּוֺת‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲדֹמִיֹּת‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎צֵדְנִיֹּת‎</span>). 

**2. n.** usually 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** collective <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַחִתִּי‎</span> ***the Hittites:*** in lists of Canaanitish peoples [[Gen.15.20|Gen 15:20]] ([[BDB#J|J]][[BDB#E|E]]), [[Exod.3.8|Exod 3:8]]; [[Exod.3.11|Exod 3:11]] (both [[BDB#J|J]]), [[Exod.13.5|Exod 13:5]]; [[Exod.23.23|Exod 23:23]]; [[Exod.23.28|Exod 23:28]]; [[Exod.33.2|Exod 33:2]]; [[Exod.34.11|Exod 34:11]]; [[Num.13.29|Num 13:29]]; [[Josh.3.10|Josh 3:10]] (all [[BDB#J|J]] [[BDB#E|E]]), [[Deut.7.1|Deut 7:1]]; [[Deut.20.17|Deut 20:17]]; [[Josh.9.1|Josh 9:1]]; [[Josh.12.8|Josh 12:8]]; [[Josh.24.11|Josh 24:11]] (all [[BDB#D|D]]); read also for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַחִוִּי‎</span> [[Judg.3.3|Judg 3:3]] (so [[BDB#We|We]] [[BDB#Mey|Mey]] [[BDB#Bu|Bu]]<sup>Urg 350</sup>); in [[Josh.11.3|Josh 11:3]] ([[BDB#D|D]]) strike out <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַחִתִּי‎</span> [[BDB#We|We]] [[BDB#Mey|Mey]] [[BDB#Bu|Bu]]<sup>l.c.</sup>, and read then <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַחִתִּי‎</span> for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַחִוִּי‎</span> v<sup>b</sup> (so [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]], not [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]]<sup>L</sup></lookup>); further [[1Kgs.9.20|1Kgs 9:20]] = [[2Chr.8.7|2Chr 8:7]]; [[Ezra.9.1|Ezra 9:1]]; [[Neh.9.8|Neh 9:8]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** plural <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׃הַחִתִּים‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כֹּל אֶרֶץ הַחִתִּים‎</span> [[Josh.1.4|Josh 1:4]] ([[BDB#D|D]]) nearly = land of Canaanites; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ארץ הַח‎</span> specifically of northern home of Hittites [[Judg.1.26|Judg 1:26]] so also [[2Sam.24.6|2Sam 24:6]] where read <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶרֶץ הַחִתִּים קֵ֫דְשָׁה‎</span> (for [[BDB#MT|ᵑ0]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎תחתים חָדְשִׁי‎</span>, see  <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חָדְשִׁי‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎קֵדֶשׁ‎</span>); <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מַלְכֵי הַחִתִּים‎</span> [[1Kgs.10.29|1Kgs 10:29]] = [[2Chr.1.17|2Chr 1:17]] (both + <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מַלְכֵי אֲרָם‎</span>), [[2Kgs.7.6|2Kgs 7:6]] (+ <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מַלְכֵי מִצְרַיִם‎</span>, both feared by <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲרָם‎</span>). Hence it appears that <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַחִתִּי (םׅ‎</span> had their proper seat in the north — where also they were encountered by Assyrians from time of Tiglath-Pileser I (see [[BDB#Schr|Schr]]<sup></sup> [[BDB#Dl|Dl]]<sup>1. c</sup>.), and by Egyptians from time of Tutmes III (see [[BDB#WMM|W Max Müll.]]<sup>l.c.</sup>) — (compare also [[Judg.3.3|Judg 3:3]]; [[Josh.11.3|Josh 11:3]] above), but that individual Hittites were known in Israel (compare, besides [[1Sam.2.6|1Sam 2:6]]; [[2Sam.11.3|2Sam 11:3]] etc., [[1Kgs.9.20|1Kgs 9:20]] above); that the Hittites were regarded (by [[BDB#J|J]] [[BDB#E|E]] [[BDB#D|D]]) as one of the peoples of Canaan, and that the name even came to be used in more general sense for Canaanites. Only in [[BDB#P|P]] do they appear as having a definite settlement in the south, and are designated by <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּנֵיחֵֿת‎</span> (see  <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎חֵת‎</span>), as well as by the adjective <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַחִתִּי‎</span>.
