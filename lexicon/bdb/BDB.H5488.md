 I. <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎סוּף‎</span> **noun masculine**<sup>[[Jonah.2.6|Jonah 2:6]]</sup> ==reeds, rushes== (collective) (probably loan-word from Egyptian <abbr title="transliteration"><u>***‰wfi***</u></abbr>, ***reeds***, [[BDB#Steind|Steind]]<sup>BAS i. 603</sup> Erman<sup>ZMG xivi (1892), 122</sup>; Semitic according to [[BDB#WMM|WMM]]<sup>As. u.Eur.101</sup>); — 

**1.** ***rushes***, in Nile [[Exod.2.3|Exod 2:3]]; [[Exod.2.5|Exod 2:5]] ([[BDB#E|E]]); <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎קָנֶה וָסוּף‎</span> [[Isa.19.6|Isa 19:6]] (of Egypt). 

**2.** usually in combination <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יַםסֿוּף‎</span> probably = ***sea of rushes or reeds*** ( &gt; ***sea of*** (city) ***Suph***), which Greek included in wider name <span class="bdbLangGreek">θάλασσα Ἐρυθρἀ</span>, ==Red Sea== (compare [[BDB#Di|Di]]<sup>[[Exod.13.18|Exod 13:18]] and</sup> especially [[BDB#WMM|WMM]]<sup>As.u.Eur.42f.</sup>, who explains as name originally given to upper end of Gulf of Suez, extending into Bitter Lakes, shallow and marshy, whence ***reeds*** (probably also reddish colour)); **\*\***compare also Che<sup>EB RED SEA</sup>. — name applied only to arms of Red Sea; most often 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** to ***Gulf of Suez*** [[Exod.10.19|Exod 10:19]]; [[Josh.2.10|Josh 2:10]] (both [[BDB#J|J]]), [[Exod.13.18|Exod 13:18]]; [[Exod.15.4|Exod 15:4]]; [[Exod.15.22|Exod 15:22]]; [[Exod.23.31|Exod 23:31]] (all [[BDB#E|E]]), [[Deut.11.4|Deut 11:4]]; [[Josh.4.23|Josh 4:23]] ([[BDB#D|D]]), [[Num.33.10|Num 33:10]]; [[Num.33.11|Num 33:11]] ([[BDB#P|P]]), elsewhere late [[Neh.9.9|Neh 9:9]]; [[Ps.106.7|Ps 106:7]]; [[Ps.106.9|Ps 106:9]]; [[Ps.106.22|Ps 106:22]]; [[Ps.136.13|Ps 136:13]]; [[Ps.136.15|Ps 136:15]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** sometimes to ***Gulf of Akaba*** [[1Kgs.9.26|1Kgs 9:26]], and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דֶּרֶךְ יַםסֿוּף‎</span> [[Num.21.4|Num 21:4]] ([[BDB#E|E]]), probably also [[Num.14.25|Num 14:25]] ([[BDB#E|E]]), [[Deut.1.40|Deut 1:40]]; [[Deut.2.1|Deut 2:1]] perhaps [[Judg.11.16|Judg 11:16]]; [[Jer.49.21|Jer 49:21]] possibly read <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִיַּםסֿוּף‎</span> for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מוֺל ס‎</span> [[Deut.1.1|Deut 1:1]] (see infor.).
