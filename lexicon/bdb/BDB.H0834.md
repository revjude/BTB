**2.** according to [[BDB#Phi|Phi]]<sup>St. C. 73</sup> Sperling<sup>Nota Rel. im Hebr. 1876, 15-22</sup> for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁל‎</span>, developed from the relative  <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שׁ‎</span> (q. v.) by (1) the prefixing of either a merely prosthetic <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎א‎</span>, or, better, a pronominal <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎א‎</span> (giving rise to <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אש‎</span>, the form of the relative in Phoenician), and (2) the addition of the demonstrative root <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ל‎</span> [found also in  <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵל‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵלָּה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הַלָּזֶה‎</span> (q. v.), <img class="bdbSeriacGlyph" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhKAAcAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAAAAAAALAAAAAAoABwAAAJujI+py+1vAJOwBoBxytQ6rUWhB3Ej6WUXyrbWqXSiCyNw/crbibvb7nvoerlJLDfE6VYlDjA2fB5US2XSBm2ojrbOjXipXmfdLTP85YahVJPb2Z0u5aLte+G1YoPoOx/v92c0Vyb4AWeYlbiYUAAAOw=="> ***he who***, <img class="bdbSeriacGlyph" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhIAASAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAAAAAAALAAAAAAgABIAAAJJjI+pq+DAogwPTupmfXJfbHXcVYmleSLbSGbJyWJMbLCwq655GOP1iuvxfLegMbSwjVKymXCIVB6YyqiraL1WLcTtLwthfsaGAgA7"> ***who*** (plural)]: the main objection to this explanation is the change of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ל‎</span> to <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎ר‎</span>, which is hardly rendered probable by the compare of Syriac <img class="bdbSeriacGlyph" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhJwAVAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAAAAAAALAAAAAAnABUAAAJcjI+py+0PDQBs0hhsnUtjr3Cd+F2hmZBfoyKo1MIOeLwyltr4Hmd6D6FZhDSfroYaDmEg5aXVNHmUSGZR6JJOk8tsLyoCE53OrHcJJVOR3G7aej1Gvbt61Y7P1wsAOw=="> by side of [[BDB#Tg|ᵑ7]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎הָלְכָּא‎</span>. **1** ==seems preferable==, the primitive root having acquired different significations in the different Semitic languages, and having been weakened in Hebrew to a mere particle of relation). A **sign of relation**, bringing the clause introduced by it into relation with an antecedent clause. As a rule <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁר‎</span> is a mere **connecting link**, and requires to be supplemented (see the grammars) by a pronominal affix, or other word, such as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שָׁם‎</span>, defining the nature of the relation more precisely: e.g. [[Gen.1.11|Gen 1:11]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁר זַרֵעוֺבֿוֺ‎</span> literally ***as to which***, its seed is in it = in which is its seed, [[Ps.1.4|Ps 1:4]] like the chaff <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁרתִּֿדְּפֶנּוּ רוּחַ‎</span> ***as to which***, the wind drives it = which the wind drives, etc.; &amp; so <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁרשָׁ֗֗֗ם‎</span> = ***where***, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁרמִ֗֗֗שָּׁם‎</span> = ***whence***, [[Gen.2.11|Gen 2:11]]; [[Gen.3.23|Gen 3:23]]; [[Gen.20.13|Gen 20:13]] etc. Sometimes also (see below) the relation expressed by it is specifically temporal, local causal, etc. More particularly


**1.** it includes its pronominal antecedent, whether in the nominative or oblique cases, as [[Num.22.6|Num 22:6]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וַאֲשֶׁר תָּאֹר יוּאָר‎</span> and ***he whom*** thou cursest is cursed, [[Exod.4.12|Exod 4:12]] and I will teach thee <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁר תְּדַבֵּר‎</span> ***that which*** thou shalt say; and with particles or prepositions, as <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֵת אֲשֶׁר‎</span> (according to the context) ***him who . . ., those who . . ., that which . . .***; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לַאֲשֶׁר‎</span> to ***him who . . .*** [[Gen.43.16|Gen 43:16]], to ***those who . . .*** [[Gen.47.24|Gen 47:24]], to ***that which*** [[Gen.27.8|Gen 27:8]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מֵאֲשֶׁר‎</span> [[Judg.16.30|Judg 16:30]]; [[2Sam.18.18|2Sam 18:18]] ***than those*** whom; [[Lev.27.24|Lev 27:24]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לַאֲשֶׁר‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎קָנָהוּ מֵאִתּוֺ‎</span> ***to him*** from whom he bought it, [[Num.5.7|Num 5:7]]; [[Isa.24.2|Isa 24:2]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כַּאֲשֶׁר נשֶׁא בוֺ‎</span> ***like him*** against whom there is a creditor. 

**2.** instances of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁר‎</span> followed by a pronominal affix, or by <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שָׁם‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎שָֽׁמָּה‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִשָּׁם‎</span>, are so common that the examples cited above will be sufficient. Very rarely there occurs the anomalous construction <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎עִם אֲשֶׁר‎</span> [[Gen.31.32|Gen 31:32]] for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁר עִמּוֺ‎</span> (see [[Gen.44.9|Gen 44:9]]), <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בַּאֲשֶׁר‎</span> [[Isa.47.12|Isa 47:12]] for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁר בָּהֶם‎</span>, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לַאֲשֶׁר‎</span> for <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁרלָ֗֗֗הֶם‎</span> [[Ezek.23.40|Ezek 23:40]]: [[Ps.119.49|Ps 119:49]] see under <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎על אשׁר‎</span>. It is followed by the pronoun in the ***nominative***, in the following cases: —
