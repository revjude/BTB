 <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אָכֵן‎</span> (perhaps from <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַח‎</span>; compare Aramaic <span class="bdbLangAramaic">‎דֵּח‎</span> &amp; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎דִּכֵּן‎</span>) **adverb** with strong asseverative force: 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **a.** ==surely, truly==, especially at beginning of a speech (stronger &amp; more decided than <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אַח‎</span>) [[Gen.28.16|Gen 28:16]]; [[Exod.2.14|Exod 2:14]]; [[1Sam.15.32|1Sam 15:32]]; [[Isa.40.7|Isa 40:7]]; [[Isa.45.15|Isa 45:15]]; [[Jer.3.23|Jer 3:23]] (twice in verse); [[Jer.4.10|Jer 4:10]]; [[Jer.8.8|Jer 8:8]]. In [[1Kgs.11.2|1Kgs 11:2]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אָבֵן‎</span> stands unusually; and <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎פֶּןֿ‎</span> (compare [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] [[BDB#Syr|ᵑ6]] [[BDB#Tg|ᵑ7]]) should probably be read (so [[BDB#Klo|Klo]]). 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **b.** emphasizing a contrast, ***but indeed, but in fact***, especially after <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אָמַרְתִּי‎</span> ***I said*** or ***thought***, expressing the reality, in opposed to what had been wrongly imagined, [[Isa.49.4|Isa 49:4]]b (opposed to v<sup>a</sup>) [[Isa.53.4|Isa 53:4]] (opposed to v[[Isa.53.3|Isa 53:3]] end) [[Jer.3.20|Jer 3:20]] (opposed to the expectation v[[Jer.3.19|Jer 3:19]] b) [[Zeph.3.7|Zeph 3:7]]b [[Ps.31.23|Ps 31:23]]b (opposed to v[[Ps.31.23|Ps 31:23]]a) [[Ps.66.19|Ps 66:19]]; [[Ps.82.7|Ps 82:7]] (opposed to v[[Ps.82.6|Ps 82:6]]) [[Job.32.8|Job 32:8]] (opposed to v[[Job.32.7|Job 32:7]]).
