 <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אוֺפִיר‎</span> **proper name** ==Ophir== — <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎א‎</span> [[1Kgs.10.11|1Kgs 10:11]] +; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אוֺפִ֫ירָה‎</span> [[1Kgs.9.28|1Kgs 9:28]] + <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אוֺפִר‎</span> [[Gen.10.29|Gen 10:29]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֹפִיר‎</span> [[1Kgs.10.11|1Kgs 10:11]] — 

**1. proper name, masculine** ==11th son of Joktan== [[Gen.10.29|Gen 10:29]] ([[BDB#J|J]]) = [[1Chr.1.23|1Chr 1:23]] [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] <span class="bdbLangGreek">Οὐφειρ</span>, <span class="bdbLangGreek">Ὠφειρ</span>, name of an Arabian tribe, see [[Gen.10.30|Gen 10:30]] &amp; [[BDB#Di|Di]]. 

**2. proper name, of a location** (land or city south or southeast from Palestine, exact position unknown, compare below; [[BDB#Sept|ᵐ5]] <span class="bdbLangGreek">Σωφηρα</span>, <span class="bdbLangGreek">Σωφειρα</span>, <span class="bdbLangGreek">Σουφειρ</span>, etc.; [[Job.28.16|Job 28:16]] <span class="bdbLangGreek">Ὠφειρ</span> AC, compare [[1Kgs.22.49|1Kgs 22:49]] [[BDB#A|A]] <span class="bdbLangGreek">Ὠφειρδε</span>, [[BDB#B|B]] omitted) place whither Solomon's ships went from Ezion Geber, bringing thence gold [[1Kgs.9.28|1Kgs 9:28]] — compare [[1Kgs.22.49|1Kgs 22:49]] — [[2Chr.8.18|2Chr 8:18]] gold, almug- (sandal-?) wood &amp; gems [[2Chr.10.11|2Chr 10:11]] (twice in verse), compare [[2Chr.9.10|2Chr 9:10]] probably [[1Kgs.10.22|1Kgs 10:22]] reference to same ships; they came once in three years with gold, silver, ivory, apes &amp; peacocks (all from Ophir ?); [[2Chr.9.21|2Chr 9:21]] makes these ships go to Tarshish (but on Tarshish-ships, i.e. large, sea-going vessels, merchantmen, see [[1Kgs.22.49|1Kgs 22:49]] &amp; below <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎תַּרְשִׁישׁ‎</span>). 

**3.** characteristic of fine gold (in poetry &amp; late) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎זְהַב א‎</span> [[1Chr.29.4|1Chr 29:4]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎׳‎</span><span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כֶּתֶם א‎</span> [[Isa.13.12|Isa 13:12]]; [[Job.28.16|Job 28:16]]; [[Ps.45.10|Ps 45:10]]. 

**4.** hence for fine gold itself [[Job.22.24|Job 22:24]] (\|\| <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בֶּצֶר‎</span>). — (If **2** = **1**, then southern, probably south-eastern, Arabia (compare [[BDB#Di|Di]] [[Gen.10.29|Gen 10:29]]) furnished the gold; and other articles, which point farther east (e.g. to India, toward which the words <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎קוֺפִים‎</span> ***apes*** &amp; <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎תֻּכִּיִּים‎</span> ***peacocks*** seem to lead), were either brought to Ophir by traders, &amp; so found there by Solomon's men, or were found elsewhere by the latter, whose cruise may have taken them beyond Ophir, the name of Ophir alone, as source of gold-supply, being preserved. If (less likely) **1** &amp; **2** are not the same, the only data for determining location of **2** are the articles brought, &amp; one may look toward India, Ceylon or other islands, or even lower Africa. Particular theories have as yet no adequate support; e.g.
