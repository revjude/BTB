$\quad$ $\quad$ **c.** the genitive after <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כל‎</span> is often a relative sentence, introduced by <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אשׁר‎</span>: [[Gen.1.31|Gen 1:31]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎את כל אשׁר עשׂה‎</span> ***the whole*** of what he had made, [[Gen.7.22|Gen 7:22]]; [[Gen.13.1|Gen 13:1]] + very often Sts., with a preposition, <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כל אשׁר‎</span> has the force of ***wheresover, whithersoever***, as [[Josh.1.7|Josh 1:7]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎בְּכֹל אֲשֶׁר תֵּלֵךְ‎</span> ***wheresoever*** thou goest, v[[Josh.1.16|Josh 1:16]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֶלכָּֿלאֲֿשֶׁר‎</span> ***whithersoever*** (see  <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎אֲשֶׁר‎</span> **4 b** <span class="bdbLangGreek">γ</span>). Very rarely in such cases is there ellipse of the rel., as [[Gen.39.4|Gen 39:4]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וְכָליֶֿשׁלֿוֺ נָתַן בְּיָדִי‎</span> (contrast v[[Gen.39.5|Gen 39:5]] v[[Gen.39.8|Gen 39:8]]), [[Exod.9.4|Exod 9:4]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מכללֿבני ישׂראל‎</span>, [[Isa.38.16|Isa 38:16]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎וּלְכָּלבָּֿהֶן חַיֵּי רוּחִי‎</span>, [[Ps.71.18|Ps 71:18]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎לְכָליָֿבוֺא‎</span> ([[Ps.74.3|Ps 74:3]], see **2 a**), [[2Chr.32.31|2Chr 32:31]] peculiarly also in Chronicles ([[BDB#Dr|Dr]]<sup>Intr 505</sup>), [[1Chr.29.3|1Chr 29:3]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎מִכָּלהֲֿכִינוֺתִי‎</span>, [[2Chr.30.18|2Chr 30:18]]f.; [[Ezra.1.6|Ezra 1:6]] compare with <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כֹּל‎</span> (**2 a**) [[1Chr.29.11|1Chr 29:11]] a [[2Chr.30.17|2Chr 30:17]]; [[Ezra.1.5|Ezra 1:5]]. 
$\quad$ $\quad$ **d.** with a ***suffix*** two idiomatic uses of <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כל‎</span> have to be noticed: 
$\quad$ $\quad$ $\quad$ (***a***) <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כל‎</span> is often made more independent and emphatic by being placed with a suffix ***after*** the word which it qualifies, to which it then stands in apposition (compare in Syriac, Arabic, Ethiopic), as [[2Sam.2.9|2Sam 2:9]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎יִשְׂרָאֵל כֻּלּהֹ‎</span>, [[Jer.13.19|Jer 13:19]]; [[Jer.48.31|Jer 48:31]]; [[Isa.9.8|Isa 9:8]]; [[Isa.14.29|Isa 14:29]]; [[Isa.14.31|Isa 14:31]] <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎פלשׁת‎</span> <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כלך‎</span> Philistia, ***all of*** thee ! $\quad$ $\quad$ **e.** Hebrew idiom in certain cases affirms, or denies, of an ***entire*** class, where English idiom affirms, or denies, of an ***individual*** of the class; thus in a comparative or hypothetical sentence <span class="bdbLangHebrew">‎כל‎</span> is = ***any***, and with a negative = ***none:***
