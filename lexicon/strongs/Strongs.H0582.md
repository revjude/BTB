<big><span class="ashebrew">‎אֱנוֹשׁ‎</span></big> **ʼĕnôwsh** <small>*(en-oshe')*</small> ==**human**==  <sup><small>[[TEHMC#H:N-M|H:N-M]]</small></sup> 
<small>from <span class="ashebrew">‎אָנַשׁ‎</span><sup><small>[[H0605]]</small></sup>; properly, a mortal (and thus differing from the more dignified <span class="ashebrew">‎אָדָם‎</span><sup><small>[[H0120]]</small></sup>); hence,</small>

$\quad$ another, [idiom] (blood-) thirsty, certain, chap(-man); divers, fellow, [idiom] in the flower of their age, husband, (certain, mortal) man, people, person, servant, some ([idiom] of them), [phrase] stranger, those, [phrase] their trade. It is often unexpressed in the English versions, especially when used in apposition with another word. Compare <span class="ashebrew">‎אִישׁ‎</span><sup><small>[[H0376]]</small></sup>.
- ***a man*** in general (singly or collectively)

**1)** man, mortal man, person, mankind
$\quad$ **1a)** of an individual
$\quad$ **1b)** men (collective)
$\quad$ **1c)** man, mankind
