<big><span class="asgreek">‎ἡμέρα‎</span></big> **hēméra** <small>*(hay-mer'-ah)*</small> ==**day**==  <sup><small>[[TEGMC#G:N-F|G:N-F]]</small></sup> 
<small>feminine (with <span class="asgreek">ὥρα</span><sup><small>[[G5610]]</small></sup> implied) of a derivative of <greek BETA="H(=MAI" unicode="ἧμαι" translit="hēmai"/> (to sit; akin to the base of <span class="asgreek">ἑδραῖος</span><sup><small>[[G1476]]</small></sup>) meaning tame, i.e. gentle</small>

$\quad$ age, + alway, (mid-)day (by day, (-ly)), + for ever, judgment, (day) time, while, years.
- day, i.e. (literally) the time space between dawn and dark, or the whole 24 hours (but several days were usually reckoned by the Jews as inclusive of the parts of both extremes)
-  figuratively, a period (always defined more or less clearly by the context)
