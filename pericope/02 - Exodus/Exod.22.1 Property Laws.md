[[Exod.22|Exodus 22:1 - Exodus 22:15]]
*Property Laws*

[[Exod.22.1|1]] If a man shall steal an ox, or a sheep, and kill it, or sell it; he shall restore five oxen for an ox, and four sheep for a sheep.

1: 

[[Exod.22.2|2]] If a thief be found breaking up, and be smitten that he die, \[there shall\] no blood \[be shed\] for him.

2: 

[[Exod.22.3|3]] If the sun be risen upon him, \[there shall be\] blood \[shed\] for him; \[for\] he should make full restitution; if he have nothing, then he shall be sold for his theft.

3: 

[[Exod.22.4|4]] If the theft be certainly found in his hand alive, whether it be ox, or ass, or sheep; he shall restore double.

4: 

[[Exod.22.5|5]] If a man shall cause a field or vineyard to be eaten, and shall put in his beast, and shall feed in another man’s field; of the best of his own field, and of the best of his own vineyard, shall he make restitution.

5: 

[[Exod.22.6|6]] If fire break out, and catch in thorns, so that the stacks of corn, or the standing corn, or the field, be consumed \[therewith\]; he that kindled the fire shall surely make restitution.

6: 

[[Exod.22.7|7]] If a man shall deliver unto his neighbour money or stuff to keep, and it be stolen out of the man’s house; if the thief be found, let him pay double.

7: 

[[Exod.22.8|8]] If the thief be not found, then the master of the house shall be brought unto the judges, \[to see\] whether he have put his hand unto his neighbour’s goods.

8: 

[[Exod.22.9|9]] For all manner of trespass, \[whether it be\] for ox, for ass, for sheep, for raiment, \[or\] for any manner of lost thing, which \[another\] challengeth to be his, the cause of both parties shall come before the judges; \[and\] whom the judges shall condemn, he shall pay double unto his neighbour.

9: 

[[Exod.22.10|10]] If a man deliver unto his neighbour an ass, or an ox, or a sheep, or any beast, to keep; and it die, or be hurt, or driven away, no man seeing \[it\]:

10: 

[[Exod.22.11|11]] \[Then\] shall an oath of the LORD be between them both, that he hath not put his hand unto his neighbour’s goods; and the owner of it shall accept \[thereof\], and he shall not make \[it\] good.

11: 

[[Exod.22.12|12]] And if it be stolen from him, he shall make restitution unto the owner thereof.

12: 

[[Exod.22.13|13]] If it be torn in pieces, \[then\] let him bring it \[for\] witness, \[and\] he shall not make good that which was torn.

13: 

[[Exod.22.14|14]] And if a man borrow \[ought\] of his neighbour, and it be hurt, or die, the owner thereof \[being\] not with it, he shall surely make \[it\] good.

14: 

[[Exod.22.15|15]] \[But\] if the owner thereof \[be\] with it, he shall not make \[it\] good: if it \[be\] an hired \[thing\], it came for his hire.

15:
