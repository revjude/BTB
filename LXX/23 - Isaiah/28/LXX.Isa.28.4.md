**LXX Isaiah 28:4** 
<small><sub>**LXX:**</sub></small>
And the flower shall be (the one having fallen off of the hope of the glory) upon \[the\] tip of the high mountain as a forerunner of a fig. The one beholding it, before taking \[it\] into his hand, will want to swallow it down. 
<small><sub>**KJV:**</sub></small>
And the glorious beauty, which [is] on the head of the fat valley, shall be a fading flower, [and] as the hasty fruit before the summer; which [when] he that looketh upon it seeth, while it is yet in his hand he eateth it up.
<small><sub>**BSB:**</sub></small>
The fading flower of his beautiful splendor, set on the summit above the fertile valley, will be like a ripe fig before the summer harvest: Whoever sees it will take it in his hand and swallow it.<small><sub>**MT:**</sub></small>
